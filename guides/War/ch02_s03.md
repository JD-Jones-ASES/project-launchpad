# Study Guide: Smedley Butler and the Origins of "War Is a Racket"

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the life, military career, and rhetorical authority of Major General Smedley Darlington Butler. It explores the historical context of his service and the evolution of his perspectives on military intervention and corporate interests.

---

## I. Biography and Military Profile

### Personal Background
*   **Life Span:** 1881–1940.
*   **Origin:** Born in West Chester, Pennsylvania, to a prominent Quaker family.
*   **Family Influence:** His father, Thomas S. Butler, was a member of the United States House of Representatives for 31 years and served as the chair of the House Naval Affairs Committee.
*   **Early Enlistment:** Despite his Quaker pacifist roots, Butler lied about his age at 16 to enlist in the Marine Corps during the Spanish-American War in 1898.

### Military Record and Decorations
Butler is recognized as one of the most accomplished combat soldiers in United States history. At the time of his retirement in 1931, he was the most decorated Marine in the nation's history.
*   **Rank:** Major General, United States Marine Corps (one of the youngest in history).
*   **Medal of Honor:** One of only nineteen men to receive the Medal of Honor twice.
    *   **First Medal:** Awarded for action at Veracruz, Mexico (1914).
    *   **Second Medal:** Awarded for action at Fort Rivière, Haiti (1915).
*   **Additional Awards:** Marine Corps Brevet Medal, Haitian Médaille Militaire, and the Distinguished Service Medal.

---

## II. Key Military Engagements and Historical Context

Butler’s 33-year career spanned every major theater of American military operations between 1898 and 1931.

### Global Theaters of Operation
| Region | Conflict/Engagement |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Philippines** | The insurrection following the Spanish-American War. |
| **China** | The Boxer Rebellion (1900). |
| **Central America** | Interventions in Honduras (1903), Panama (1903), and Nicaragua (1910). |
| **Mexico** | The occupation of Veracruz (1914). |
| **The Caribbean** | The occupation of Haiti (1915–1934). |
| **France (WWI)** | Command of Camp Pontanezen, the largest American debarkation camp in Europe. |

### The "Banana Wars"
The majority of Butler’s career was spent in the "Banana Wars," a series of military interventions in Latin America and the Caribbean. 
*   **Purpose:** These were not wars declared by Congress or fought for national survival. Instead, they were ordered by the State Department and the Executive Branch, often to protect the commercial interests of American corporations.
*   **Corporate Influence:** Organizations like the United Fruit Company relied on military intervention to protect plantations and railroads.
*   **Butler's Reflection:** He later characterized himself as a "muscle man for Big Business," noting that he enforced commercial interests through military force.

---

## III. Rhetorical Authority and "Ethos"

The power of Butler’s later indictment of war in his pamphlet, *War Is a Racket*, stems from his unique **ethos**—his credibility and authority as a seasoned combat veteran.

*   **The Combatant vs. The Intellectual:** Butler was not a pacifist intellectual or a politician; he was a man who had spent three decades in uniform, mostly in combat.
*   **Firsthand Witness:** His claims that war enriches a few at the expense of the many were not based on speculation but on decades of observation. He witnessed the flow of government contract money into private corporate hands.
*   **The Foundation of Scars:** His authority to denounce war came from his direct experience enforcing the very policies he eventually condemned.

## V. Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

1.  **The Evolution of a Soldier:** Analyze the irony of Smedley Butler’s career. How did his thirty-three years of active duty in American military interventions provide the foundation for his eventual condemnation of war as a "racket"?
2.  **Corporate Interests and Foreign Policy:** Discuss the relationship between American business interests and military intervention during the early twentieth century. Use the "Banana Wars" and Butler's descriptions of his roles in Central America and the Caribbean to support your analysis.
3.  **The Concept of Ethos in Rhetoric:** Using Smedley Butler as a case study, explain how a speaker’s personal history and scars can provide more rhetorical weight than education or eloquence. Why did people "listen" to Butler when he spoke against war?

## VI. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Banana Wars:** A series of U.S. military interventions in Latin America and the Caribbean (1898–1934) aimed at protecting the commercial interests of American corporations.
*   **Ethos:** A rhetorical appeal based on the character, credibility, or authority of the speaker.
*   **Gendarmerie d'Ha:** The Haitian national police force, organized and commanded by U.S. Marine officers during the American occupation of Haiti.
*   **Major General:** The high-ranking military position Butler achieved during his thirty-three years in the Marine Corps.
*   **Muscle Man for Big Business:** A phrase used by Butler to describe his role in enforcing American commercial interests abroad via military force.
*   **Quaker:** A religious group known for a deep tradition of pacifism; the community into which Smedley Butler was born.
*   **United Fruit Company:** A major American corporation whose commercial interests were frequently protected by military interventions in Latin America.
