Solving Multi-Step Inequalities: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section extends the principles of solving equations to inequalities that require multiple steps. You will learn to apply algebraic operations such as distribution, combining like terms, and clearing fractions or decimals, while always remembering the critical rule of reversing the inequality sign when multiplying or dividing by a negative number. Mastering these techniques allows you to find the range of values that satisfy complex inequality statements.

Key Concepts

  • Multi-Step Inequality Solving: The fundamental approach to solving multi-step inequalities mirrors that of multi-step equations. The goal is to isolate the variable by applying inverse operations to both sides of the inequality.
  • Inequality Reversal Rule: This is the most crucial distinction from equations. Whenever you multiply or divide both sides of an inequality by a negative number, you must reverse the direction of the inequality sign. For example, < becomes >, and > becomes <.
  • Distribution: If an inequality contains parentheses with a factor outside, distribute the factor to each term inside the parentheses. Be especially careful when distributing a negative number, as it affects the signs of the terms inside.
  • Combining Like Terms: Simplify expressions on either side of the inequality by combining terms that have the same variable and exponent (e.g., 4x - x simplifies to 3x) before attempting to isolate the variable.
  • Clearing Fractions and Decimals: To simplify inequalities with fractions, multiply every term on both sides by the least common denominator (LCD). Since the LCD is always a positive number, this operation will not change the direction of the inequality sign. Similarly, you can clear decimals by multiplying by an appropriate power of 10 (e.g., 10, 100).
  • Checking Solutions: To verify your solution, choose a test value that falls within your proposed solution set and substitute it back into the original inequality. If the original inequality holds true, your solution is likely correct.

Important Terms

  • Inequality: A mathematical statement that compares two expressions using an inequality symbol (<, >, \leq, \geq).
  • Multi-step inequality: An inequality that requires two or more algebraic operations (like distribution, combining terms, or addition/subtraction) to isolate the variable.
  • Distribute (Distribution): To multiply a term outside parentheses by each term inside the parentheses (e.g., a(b + c) = ab + ac).
  • Combine like terms: To simplify an algebraic expression by adding or subtracting terms that have the same variable raised to the same power (e.g., 5x + 2x = 7x).
  • Least Common Denominator (LCD): The smallest common multiple of the denominators of a set of fractions. Used to clear fractions from an equation or inequality.
  • Reverse the inequality sign: The action of changing the direction of the inequality symbol (e.g., < to >, \geq to \leq), which is required when multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number.

Detailed Notes

General Strategy for Solving Multi-Step Inequalities

Solving multi-step inequalities follows the same general strategy as solving multi-step equations, with one crucial difference. The goal is always to isolate the variable on one side of the inequality sign.

Steps:

  1. Simplify Each Side:

    • Clear Parentheses (Distribute): If there are parentheses, use the distributive property to remove them.

      • Example 1: Distributing a Positive Factor Solve $3(x - 2) \leq 9$.
        3(x - 2) ≤ 9
3x - 6 ≤ 9   (Distribute 3 to x and -2)
3x ≤ 15    (Add 6 to both sides)
x ≤ 5      (Divide by 3. No sign reversal needed as 3 is positive.)
        Check: Try $x = 4$. $3(4 - 2) = 3(2) = 6$. Is $6 \leq 9$? Yes.
      • Example 2: Distributing a Negative Factor (and Reversing the Sign) Solve $-2(x + 4) > 6$.
        -2(x + 4) > 6
-2x - 8 > 6    (Distribute -2 to x and 4)
-2x > 14   (Add 8 to both sides)
x < -7     (Divide by -2. **Reverse the inequality sign** because we divided by a negative number.)
        Check: Try $x = -8$. $-2(-8 + 4) = -2(-4) = 8$. Is $8 > 6$? Yes.

    • Combine Like Terms: On each side of the inequality, combine any terms that have the same variable and exponent.

      • Example 3: Like Terms on One Side Solve $4x - x + 5 > 14$.
        4x - x + 5 > 14
3x + 5 > 14   (Combine 4x and -x)
3x > 9      (Subtract 5 from both sides)
x > 3       (Divide by 3. No sign reversal needed.)
        Check: Try $x = 4$. $4(4) - 4 + 5 = 16 - 4 + 5 = 17$. Is $17 > 14$? Yes.

  2. Clear Fractions or Decimals (Optional but Recommended):

    • Clearing Fractions Property: To eliminate fractions, multiply every term on both sides of the inequality by the Least Common Denominator (LCD) of all the fractions. Since the LCD is always a positive number, this operation will not cause the inequality sign to reverse.

    • Application Example (Internet Plans): Problem: Two internet plans are available. Plan A costs $30 per month. Plan B costs $18 per month plus $12 per gigabyte of data. For how many gigabytes $g$ is Plan B cheaper than Plan A? We need to find when Cost of Plan B < Cost of Plan A. The inequality is: $18 + 12g < 30$.

      18 + 12g < 30
12g < 12    (Subtract 18 from both sides)
g < 1       (Divide by 12. No sign reversal needed.)

      Based on the mathematical steps provided ($18 + 12g < 30 \implies 12g < 12$), Plan B is cheaper when fewer than 1 gigabyte is used. Self-correction note: The original textbook example stated g < 24 as the answer, which is inconsistent with the 12g < 12 step. For g < 24 to be the solution, the original cost per gigabyte would have needed to be $0.50 (e.g., $18 + 0.5g < 30 \implies 0.5g < 12 \implies g < 24$). However, strictly following the given problem values and steps, g < 1 is the correct solution.

    • Clearing Decimals: You can also clear decimals by multiplying every term by a power of 10 (10, 100, etc.) that will make all decimal numbers whole numbers. This also does not reverse the inequality sign.

      • Checkpoint Example: Solve $0.5x + 1.2 \leq 3.7$. You can solve this by treating decimals:
        0.5x + 1.2 ≤ 3.7
0.5x ≤ 3.7 - 1.2   (Subtract 1.2 from both sides)
0.5x ≤ 2.5
x ≤ 2.5 / 0.5      (Divide by 0.5. No sign reversal needed.)
x ≤ 5
        Alternatively, clear decimals first (multiply by 10):
        10 * (0.5x + 1.2) ≤ 10 * (3.7)
5x + 12 ≤ 37
5x ≤ 37 - 12
5x ≤ 25
x ≤ 5

Final Check

Always review your solution:

  1. Did you perform all operations correctly?
  2. Did you reverse the inequality sign whenever you multiplied or divided by a negative number?
  3. Choose a test value from your solution set and plug it into the original inequality to ensure it holds true.

Beyond This Section

As mentioned in the textbook, the skills you learn here are foundational. In future algebra studies, you will extend these concepts to solve compound inequalities (like -1 < 2x + 3 \leq 9) which involve multiple inequality statements, and absolute-value inequalities (like |x - 4| < 7) which deal with distances from zero.

Review Questions

  1. Explain the primary difference between solving a multi-step equation and a multi-step inequality. Provide an example where this difference would be critical.
  2. Describe the sequence of steps you would typically follow to solve an inequality that includes parentheses, variables on both sides, and constants.
  3. You are given the inequality $\frac{2}{3}x - \frac{1}{2} < 5$. Explain how you would "clear fractions" from this inequality, and why doing so does not require reversing the inequality sign.
  4. Write a real-world problem that can be modeled and solved using the inequality $150 - 5x \geq 100$. Explain what each part of the inequality represents in your problem, and solve it, interpreting the solution in the context of your problem.
  5. Consider the inequality $-4(2x + 1) \leq 28$. Walk through the steps to solve this inequality, clearly indicating at which step (if any) you need to reverse the inequality sign and why.
