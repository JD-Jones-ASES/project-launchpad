Solving Two-Step Equations Study Guide

Overview

This section builds upon your knowledge of one-step equations to tackle more complex algebraic problems. You will learn a systematic approach to solve equations that require two inverse operations, focusing on the crucial order of undoing addition/subtraction before multiplication/division. Mastering this strategy is fundamental for advanced algebraic concepts and problem-solving.

Key Concepts

  • Two-Step Equations: These are equations that require exactly two inverse operations to isolate the variable. They typically follow the form ax + b = c.
  • Inverse Operations: Operations that "undo" each other. For example, addition is the inverse of subtraction, and multiplication is the inverse of division.
  • Order of Operations for Solving: When solving two-step equations, you generally reverse the standard order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS). This means you first undo any addition or subtraction, and then undo any multiplication or division.
  • Balancing Equations: To maintain the equality of an equation, any operation performed on one side of the equation must also be performed on the other side. This ensures the equation remains true.
  • Checking Solutions: After finding a potential solution for the variable, it's good practice to substitute that value back into the original equation to verify that it makes the equation true.

Important Terms

  • Two-Step Equation : An algebraic equation that requires two inverse operations to isolate the variable and find its solution.
  • Inverse Operations : Mathematical operations that cancel each other out (e.g., adding 5 and subtracting 5; multiplying by 3 and dividing by 3).
  • Coefficient : The numerical factor that multiplies a variable in an algebraic term (e.g., in 2x, 2 is the coefficient).
  • Constant Term : A term in an algebraic expression that has a fixed numerical value and does not contain a variable (e.g., in 2x + 3 = 11, 3 and 11 are constant terms).
  • Variable : A symbol, typically a letter, used to represent an unknown numerical value in an equation.
  • Solution : The specific value of the variable that makes an equation a true statement.

Detailed Notes

Understanding Two-Step Equations

In Mathematics I, you learned to solve one-step equations like $x + 5 = 12$ (by subtracting 5 from both sides) or $3x = 21$ (by dividing by 3 from both sides). Two-step equations combine these ideas, requiring two such inverse operations to find the value of the variable.

The general form of a common two-step equation is $\mathbf{ax + b = c}$, where $x$ is the variable, $a$ is its coefficient, and $b$ and $c$ are constant terms.

The Strategy for Solving Two-Step Equations

The core strategy for solving equations of the form $\mathbf{ax + b = c}$ involves a specific order of inverse operations:

  1. Undo Addition or Subtraction (the constant term 'b'):

    • If a number is being added to the variable term (+b), subtract that number from both sides of the equation.
    • If a number is being subtracted from the variable term (-b), add that number to both sides of the equation.
    • Purpose: This isolates the term containing the variable (ax).

  2. Undo Multiplication or Division (the coefficient 'a'):

    • If the variable is being multiplied by a number (ax), divide both sides of the equation by that number.
    • If the variable is being divided by a number (x/a), multiply both sides of the equation by that number.
    • Purpose: This isolates the variable x itself, giving you the solution.

  3. Check Your Solution:

    • Substitute the value you found for the variable back into the original equation.
    • Perform the arithmetic. If both sides of the equation are equal, your solution is correct.

Crucial Reminder: Always perform the same operation on both sides of the equation to maintain balance and ensure the equation remains true.

Examples Explained Step-by-Step

Let's walk through the examples provided in the textbook section to solidify this strategy.

Example 1: Basic Two-Step Equation

Solve $2x + 3 = 11$.

  1. Undo Addition/Subtraction: The constant term being added to $2x$ is 3. To undo this, we subtract 3 from both sides. $2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3$ $2x = 8$

  2. Undo Multiplication/Division: Now we have $2x = 8$. The variable $x$ is being multiplied by 2. To undo this, we divide both sides by 2. $\frac{2x}{2} = \frac{8}{2}$ $x = 4$

  3. Check: Substitute $x=4$ into the original equation: $2(4) + 3 = 8 + 3 = 11$ Since $11 = 11$, the solution $x=4$ is correct.

Example 2: Variable Term Has a Fraction Coefficient

Solve $x/4 - 5 = 3$.

  1. Undo Addition/Subtraction: The constant term being subtracted from $x/4$ is 5. To undo this, we add 5 to both sides. $\frac{x}{4} - 5 + 5 = 3 + 5$ $\frac{x}{4} = 8$

  2. Undo Multiplication/Division: Now we have $x/4 = 8$. The variable $x$ is being divided by 4. To undo this, we multiply both sides by 4. $4 \cdot \frac{x}{4} = 8 \cdot 4$ $x = 32$

  3. Check: Substitute $x=32$ into the original equation: $\frac{32}{4} - 5 = 8 - 5 = 3$ Since $3 = 3$, the solution $x=32$ is correct.

Example 3: Negative Coefficient

Solve $-3x + 7 = -8$.

  1. Undo Addition/Subtraction: The constant term being added to $-3x$ is 7. To undo this, we subtract 7 from both sides. $-3x + 7 - 7 = -8 - 7$ $-3x = -15$

  2. Undo Multiplication/Division: Now we have $-3x = -15$. The variable $x$ is being multiplied by -3. To undo this, we divide both sides by -3. $\frac{-3x}{-3} = \frac{-15}{-3}$ $x = 5$ (Remember: a negative divided by a negative is a positive)

  3. Check: Substitute $x=5$ into the original equation: $-3(5) + 7 = -15 + 7 = -8$ Since $-8 = -8$, the solution $x=5$ is correct.

Example 4: A Word Problem

A cell phone plan charges $12 per month plus $0.05 per text message. If the monthly bill is $18.50, how many text messages were sent?

  1. Set up the Equation:

    • Let $t$ represent the number of text messages.
    • The cost for text messages is $0.05t$.
    • The fixed monthly charge is $12.
    • The total bill is $18.50.
    • So, the equation is: $12 + 0.05t = 18.50$

  2. Solve the Equation:

    • Undo Addition/Subtraction: Subtract $12$ from both sides. $12 + 0.05t - 12 = 18.50 - 12$ $0.05t = 6.50$

    • Undo Multiplication/Division: Divide both sides by $0.05$. $\frac{0.05t}{0.05} = \frac{6.50}{0.05}$ $t = 130$

  3. Interpret the Solution: The variable $t$ represents the number of text messages. Therefore, $130$ text messages were sent.

Important Caution: Always remember that any operation you perform on one side of an equation must also be performed on the other side to keep the equation balanced and valid.

Review Questions

  1. What is the fundamental difference between solving a one-step equation and a two-step equation? Why is the strategic order of operations (undoing addition/subtraction before multiplication/division) so crucial for two-step equations?
  2. Explain the concept of "balancing an equation" in your own words. Why is it essential to perform the same operation on both sides of an equation?
  3. Solve the equation: $4x - 10 = 14$. Show each step of your solution and clearly state the inverse operations used.
  4. Solve the equation: $\frac{y}{3} + 7 = 2$. Show each step, including how you handle the fraction, and check your final answer.
  5. A plumber charges a $50 service fee plus $65 per hour for labor. If a customer's total bill was $245, write a two-step equation to represent this situation and then solve it to find out how many hours the plumber worked.
