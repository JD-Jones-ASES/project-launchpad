The Distributive Property with Variables Study Guide

Overview

This section extends your understanding of the distributive property to algebraic expressions that include variables. You will learn how to multiply a factor into an expression within parentheses, including cases where the factor is negative, and then simplify the resulting expression by combining like terms. Mastering this skill is fundamental for solving more complex equations and inequalities in algebra.

Key Concepts

  • The Distributive Property: A fundamental algebraic property that states for any real numbers $a$, $b$, and $c$, the product of $a$ and the sum or difference of $b$ and $c$ is equal to the sum or difference of the products of $a$ and $b$ and $a$ and $c$. That is, $a(b + c) = ab + ac$ and $a(b - c) = ab - ac$.
  • Distributing a Positive Factor: When a positive number or variable is multiplied by an expression in parentheses, multiply that factor by each term inside the parentheses, maintaining the original signs of the terms.
  • Distributing a Negative Factor (Sign): When a negative number or a negative sign (which implies -1) is multiplied by an expression in parentheses, multiply that factor by each term inside the parentheses. This will change the sign of every term within the parentheses. Forgetting to change the sign of all terms is a common mistake.
  • Combining Like Terms: After applying the distributive property, simplify the expression further by grouping and combining terms that have the same variable raised to the same power. This is always the second step in the "Distribute, Then Combine" strategy.
  • Strategy: Distribute, Then Combine: The standard approach for simplifying expressions involving the distributive property is to first distribute any factors into parentheses, and then combine any like terms that result.

Important Terms

  • Distributive Property: The rule $a(b + c) = ab + ac$ and $a(b - c) = ab - ac$, which allows multiplication to be distributed over addition or subtraction.
  • Variable: A symbol, typically a letter (like $x$, $y$, $a$), used to represent an unknown number or a value that can change.
  • Term: A single number, a single variable, or a product of numbers and variables (e.g., $6x$, $15$, $-4x$, $3$). Terms are separated by addition or subtraction signs.
  • Like Terms: Terms that have the exact same variable parts (i.e., the same variables raised to the same powers). Only like terms can be combined (added or subtracted). For example, $6x$ and $2x$ are like terms, but $6x$ and $6x^2$ are not.
  • Factor: A number or expression that is multiplied by another number or expression. In $a(b+c)$, $a$ is a factor.
  • Simplify: To reduce an algebraic expression to its simplest form, usually by performing all possible operations (like distribution) and combining like terms.

Detailed Notes

Introduction to the Distributive Property with Variables

You've likely used the distributive property to multiply a number by a sum of other numbers (e.g., $3(10+2) = 3 \cdot 10 + 3 \cdot 2$). In algebra, we extend this concept to expressions involving variables. The core idea remains the same: whatever is outside the parentheses as a factor must be multiplied by every term inside the parentheses.

The formal statement of the property is: For all real numbers $a$, $b$, and $c$: $$a(b + c) = ab + ac$$ and $$a(b - c) = ab - ac$$

Distributing a Positive Factor

When the factor outside the parentheses is positive, the signs of the terms inside the parentheses remain the same after distribution.

Example: Simplify $3(2x + 5)$.

  1. Identify the factor: The factor is $3$.
  2. Identify the terms inside: The terms are $2x$ and $5$.
  3. Distribute: Multiply $3$ by each term inside. $3(2x + 5) = (3 \cdot 2x) + (3 \cdot 5)$
  4. Simplify the products: $ = 6x + 15$ The simplified expression is $6x + 15$.

Distributing a Negative Factor (Sign)

This is a crucial case where mistakes are common. When a negative sign or a negative number is distributed, it changes the sign of every term inside the parentheses. Remember that a standalone negative sign in front of parentheses implies a factor of $-1$.

Example: Simplify $-(4x - 3)$.

  1. Recognize the factor: The factor is $-1$. (The negative sign outside means $-1$ times the expression.)
  2. Identify the terms inside: The terms are $4x$ and $-3$.
  3. Distribute $-1$: Multiply $-1$ by each term inside. $-(4x - 3) = (-1 \cdot 4x) - (-1 \cdot 3)$ Notice the second term is $-(-1 \cdot 3)$ because the original expression was $4x - 3$, meaning $4x + (-3)$. So distributing $-1$ to $-3$ gives $(-1)(-3) = +3$.
  4. Simplify the products: $ = -4x - (-3)$ $ = -4x + 3$ The simplified expression is $-4x + 3$.

Common Mistake Alert: A frequent error is to only distribute the negative sign to the first term. For example, some students might incorrectly simplify $-(4x - 3)$ to $-4x - 3$. This is wrong because the $-3$ inside the parentheses is also affected by the negative sign, becoming $+3$. Always remember to multiply the negative factor by all terms inside.

Combining Distribute and Combine Like Terms

Often, you'll need to apply the distributive property multiple times within a single expression and then combine any like terms.

Strategy: Distribute, Then Combine

  1. Distribute: Apply the distributive property to remove all parentheses by multiplying each factor by every term inside its respective parentheses.
  2. Combine Like Terms: After distributing, identify any like terms (terms with the same variable and exponent) and combine them by adding or subtracting their coefficients.

Example: Simplify $2(x + 4) + 3(x - 1)$.

  1. Distribute the first factor: $2(x + 4) = 2x + 8$
  2. Distribute the second factor: $3(x - 1) = 3x - 3$
  3. Rewrite the expression with distributed terms: $(2x + 8) + (3x - 3)$
  4. Identify and group like terms: $(2x + 3x) + (8 - 3)$
  5. Combine like terms: $5x + 5$ The simplified expression is $5x + 5$.

Real-World Application: Perimeter

The distributive property is useful in solving word problems involving geometric shapes or other scenarios where expressions need to be simplified.

Example: The length of a rectangle is $(2x + 3)$ cm and the width is $(x - 1)$ cm. Write and simplify an expression for the perimeter. The formula for the perimeter of a rectangle is $P = 2 \cdot \text{length} + 2 \cdot \text{width}$.

  1. Substitute the expressions for length and width: $P = 2(2x + 3) + 2(x - 1)$
  2. Distribute each factor: $P = (2 \cdot 2x + 2 \cdot 3) + (2 \cdot x - 2 \cdot 1)$ $P = 4x + 6 + 2x - 2$
  3. Combine like terms: $P = (4x + 2x) + (6 - 2)$ $P = 6x + 4$ The expression for the perimeter is $(6x + 4)$ cm.

Distributing Fractions

The distributive property works the same way with fractions as it does with integers. You multiply the fraction by each term inside the parentheses.

Example: $1/2(6x + 4)$

  1. Distribute the fraction: $1/2(6x + 4) = (1/2 \cdot 6x) + (1/2 \cdot 4)$
  2. Simplify the products: $ = 3x + 2$

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words why the distributive property is necessary when simplifying expressions like $5(3x - 7)$.
  2. Simplify the expression $7(4y + 2)$. Show each step of your calculation.
  3. Describe the common mistake students make when simplifying expressions such as $-(5x - 8)$. Then, correctly simplify the expression and explain why your answer is different from the common mistake.
  4. Simplify the expression $3(2a + 5) - 4(a - 3)$. Be sure to outline your steps using the "Distribute, Then Combine" strategy.
  5. A triangular garden has sides with lengths $(x + 2)$ meters, $(3x - 1)$ meters, and $(2x + 5)$ meters. Write and simplify an expression for the perimeter of the garden.
