# The Origins of Islam: The Prophet, the Revelation, and the Community

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the foundational period of Islam, focusing on the life of the Prophet Muhammad, the nature of the divine revelations he received, and the establishment of the first Muslim community.

## Key Concepts and Historical Overview

### The Life of Muhammad (c. 570–632)
Muhammad ibn Abdullah was born around 570 in Mecca into the Quraysh tribe, who served as guardians of the Ka'ba. Orphaned at a young age—his father died before his birth and his mother when he was six—he was raised by his uncle, Abu Talib. Before his prophetic mission, Muhammad was a merchant known as **Al-Amin** ("the trustworthy") for his honesty. He married Khadijah, a prosperous widow and his first convert, who supported him during the initial revelations.

### The Revelation and the Quran
In 610, while meditating in a cave on Mount Hira, Muhammad experienced the first of many revelations. The angel **Gabriel (Jibril)** commanded him to "recite" (*iqra’*). These revelations continued for twenty-three years until his death in 632. The word **Quran** is derived from the same root as *iqra’*, meaning "reciting" or "reading aloud."

### The Meccan Period and Persecution
Beginning in 613, Muhammad publicly preached a message that challenged the Meccan elite. His message focused on four core pillars:
1.  **Monotheism:** There is only one God (Allah).
2.  **Equality:** All human beings are equal before God.
3.  **Social Justice:** The wealthy have a moral obligation to the poor.
4.  **Judgment:** A Day of Judgment is coming.

The polytheistic merchants of Mecca responded with persecution, leading some Muslims to flee to Ethiopia in 615 for protection under the Christian king (the Negus).

### The Hijra and the Ummah
The year 622 marks the **Hijra**, the migration of Muhammad and his followers from Mecca to the city of Yathrib (later renamed Medina). This event is so foundational that it serves as the starting point for the Islamic calendar. In Medina, Muhammad transitioned from a preacher to a statesman, drafting the **Constitution of Medina** and establishing the **Ummah**—a spiritual community that transcended traditional tribal and national identities.

### Conflict and the Rededication of the Ka'ba
The transition to Medina was followed by a series of defensive military engagements against Meccan forces, including the battles of **Badr (624)**, **Uhud (625)**, and the **Trench (627)**. In 630, Muhammad returned to Mecca with a large army. The city surrendered peacefully, and Muhammad cleansed the Ka'ba of idols, rededicating it to the one God. Muslims believe the Ka'ba was originally built by Abraham and Ishmael.

## Short-Answer Practice Questions

**1. What was the nickname given to Muhammad during his youth as a merchant, and what does it mean?**
*Answer:* He was called "Al-Amin," which means "the trustworthy."

**2. Who was the first person to believe in Muhammad’s prophetic message?**
*Answer:* His wife, Khadijah.

**3. What does the Arabic word "iqra'" mean, and how does it relate to the name of the Islamic holy book?**
*Answer:* It means "recite" or "read aloud." It share the same linguistic root as the word "Quran."

**4. Why did the Meccan elite perceive Muhammad’s message as a threat?**
*Answer:* His message of monotheism challenged their polytheistic beliefs, and his teachings on social justice and the obligations of the rich to the poor threatened their economic and social status.

**5. What is the significance of the year 622 in the Islamic tradition?**
*Answer:* It is the year of the Hijra (the migration to Medina). It marks the founding of the Muslim community and serves as the first year of the Islamic calendar.

**6. What was the "Constitution of Medina"?**
*Answer:* It was an agreement negotiated by Muhammad that organized the various Muslim, Jewish, and pagan tribes of Medina under a single common framework.

**7. Which three major battles were fought between the community at Medina and the forces of Mecca?**
*Answer:* The Battle of Badr (624), the Battle of Uhud (625), and the Battle of the Trench (627).

**8. To whom do Muslims attribute the original construction of the Ka'ba?**
*Answer:* Abraham and his son Ishmael.

How did the "Constitution of Medina" and the concept of the *Ummah* redefine social and political organization in the 7th-century Arabian Peninsula?

## Glossary of Important Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Al-Amin** | "The trustworthy"; a nickname earned by Muhammad as a young merchant. |
| **Allah** | The Arabic word for God; used by Muslims, as well as Arab Christians and Jews. |
| **Hijra** | The 622 migration of Muhammad and his followers from Mecca to Medina; the starting point of the Islamic calendar. |
| **Islam** | Derived from the root *s-l-m*; means surrender or submission to God. |
| **Jibril** | The Arabic name for the angel Gabriel, who transmitted the divine revelations to Muhammad. |
| **Ka'ba** | A sacred cube-shaped shrine in Mecca; believed to be built by Abraham and Ishmael; the geographical center of Islamic worship. |
| **Muslim** | One who makes *islam*—one who submits to God. |
| **Quran** | The record of divine speech transmitted through Muhammad; literally means "reciting" or "reading aloud." |
| **Ummah** | The community of all Muslim believers, envisioned as a single spiritual family that transcends tribe, race, and nationality. |
