Root Profile: psych (Mind, Soul), path (Feeling, Suffering), hydr (Water), and therm (Heat)

Overview

Chapter 24 surveys four Greek roots that appear throughout scientific and medical vocabulary. Psych comes from psyche (soul, mind). Path comes from pathos (feeling, suffering). Hydr comes from hydor (water). Therm comes from therme (heat).

Root Information

  • Root(s): psych; path; hydr; therm
  • Meaning: psych: mind, soul, spirit | path: feeling, suffering, disease | hydr: water | therm: heat, warmth
  • Origin: Greek psyche (soul, mind); pathos (feeling, suffering); hydor (water); therme (heat)
  • Variants: psycho (in compounds); pathy (as in sympathy); hydro (in compounds); thermo (in compounds)
  • Key Examples: psychology, psyche, psychiatry; empathy, pathology, sympathy, apathy; hydrogen, hydrant, dehydrate; thermal, thermostat, thermometer

Connections to Other Roots

Psych connects to ment (Latin: mind) -- both deal with mental phenomena, but psych carries connotations of soul and spirit from its Greek philosophical origins. Path connects to sens/sent (Latin: to feel) in the domain of feeling and perception. Hydr connects to aqu (Latin: water). Therm connects to calor (Latin: heat).

Detailed Notes

Historical Context

Psyche was both a philosophical concept and a mythological figure -- the beautiful mortal whose name meant 'soul' or 'breath of life.' In Greek philosophy, the psyche encompassed the entire inner life: thoughts, emotions, desires, and the animating principle of a living being. The modern science of psychology (study of the psyche) preserves this broad meaning.

Pathos referred to any experience that happens to a person -- suffering, emotion, or passion. In rhetoric, pathos was one of Aristotle's three modes of persuasion (alongside ethos and logos). The root evolved in two directions in English: emotional feeling (empathy, sympathy) and medical suffering/disease (pathology, pathogen).

Hydor (water) and therme (heat) are physical science roots. The Greeks observed natural hot springs (thermai) and understood water's central role in nature. These roots became foundational in chemistry (hydrogen -- 'water-producer') and physics (thermodynamics -- the science of heat and energy).

How the Roots Appear in English

  • psych-/psycho-: mind, soul (psychology, psychiatry, psychic)
  • -path/-pathy: feeling or suffering (empathy, sympathy, apathy, pathology)
  • hydr-/hydro-: water (hydrogen, hydraulic, dehydrate, hydrant)
  • therm-/thermo-: heat (thermal, thermometer, thermostat, geothermal)
