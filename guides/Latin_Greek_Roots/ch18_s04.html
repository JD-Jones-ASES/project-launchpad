Word Family: mort / mor

Overview

This word family explores seven English words derived from the Latin roots mort (death) and mori (to die). Each entry reveals how the concept of death has been adapted into words covering physical death, metaphorical death, and legal obligations.

Word Breakdown

Mortal

Components: mort (death) + -al (relating to)

Definition: Subject to death; causing death. Every human being is mortal -- bound eventually to die.

Immortal

Components: im- (not) + mort (death) + -al (relating to)

Definition: Living forever; not subject to death. The prefix im- negates mortality, suggesting eternal existence.

Mortify

Components: mort (death) + -ify (to make)

Definition: To cause great embarrassment or shame. Originally meant 'to put to death' or 'to deaden the flesh'; the modern sense of killing one's pride evolved from religious usage.

Mortgage

Components: mort (death) + gage (pledge)

Definition: A loan agreement secured by property. Literally a 'death pledge' -- the agreement 'dies' either when the debt is paid off or when the property is seized.

Moribund

Components: mori (to die) + -bund (about to)

Definition: At the point of death; in terminal decline. Can describe both a dying person and a failing institution or idea.

Post-mortem

Components: post- (after) + mort (death) + -em

Definition: An examination conducted after death. Used both literally (an autopsy) and figuratively (analyzing what went wrong after a project fails).

Mortuary

Components: mort (death) + -uary (place of)

Definition: A place where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation.

Patterns and Observations

Understanding how prefixes and suffixes modify the base root is the key to unlocking unfamiliar vocabulary. Notice how:

  • Prefixes change the direction or scope of the root meaning (e.g., im- means 'not,' post- means 'after').
  • Suffixes change the part of speech or specify the quality (e.g., -al makes an adjective, -ify means 'to make,' -uary means 'place of').
  • The same root can produce nouns, verbs, and adjectives depending on the suffix attached.

Review Questions
