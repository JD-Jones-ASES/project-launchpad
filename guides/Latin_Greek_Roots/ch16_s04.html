Study Guide: Chapter 16, Section 4 – Word Family: From tempor

Overview

This section explores the concept of a word family using the Latin root tempor as a central example. You will learn how tempor, meaning "time," forms the basis for numerous English words, and how understanding this root can unlock the meanings of complex vocabulary. The guide will detail key terms, illustrate the formation of related words, and provide examples of their usage.

Key Concepts

  • Word Family: A group of words that share a common root or base, often also sharing related meanings and originating from the same linguistic source.
  • Root Word (tempor): The basic part of a word that carries the primary meaning. The Latin root tempor means "time."
  • Affixes (Prefixes and Suffixes): Morphemes (meaningful units) added to a root word to modify its meaning or grammatical function.
    • Prefixes: Added to the beginning of a root (e.g., con- in contemporaneous).
    • Suffixes: Added to the end of a root (e.g., -ary in temporary).
  • Etymology: The study of the origin of words and how their meanings have evolved over time. Understanding etymology, especially of root words, is a powerful tool for vocabulary building and comprehension.
  • Meaning Derivation: How the core meaning of "time" from tempor is extended and refined by prefixes and suffixes to create diverse but related words.

Important Terms

  • Root Word: The fundamental part of a word that contains its core meaning, from which other words can be formed.
  • tempor: A Latin root meaning "time" or "season."
  • Word Family: A collection of words derived from a common root, often through the addition of various prefixes and suffixes.
  • Etymology: The historical development of a word, including its origin, changes in form, and changes in meaning.
  • Prefix: A letter or group of letters added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning (e.g., con- meaning "with" or "together").
  • Suffix: A letter or group of letters added to the end of a word to change its meaning or grammatical function (e.g., -ary meaning "relating to").
  • Temporary: Lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent.
  • Contemporary: Existing, occurring, or living at the same time; belonging to or characteristic of the present time.
  • Contemporaneous: Existing or occurring during the same period of time.
  • Temporal: Relating to worldly rather than spiritual affairs; relating to time.
  • Temporize: To avoid making a decision or committing oneself in order to gain time.
  • Extemporaneous: Spoken or done without preparation; impromptu.
  • Tempo: The speed at which a piece of music is played; the pace of an activity or event.

Detailed Notes

1. Understanding Word Families and Root Words

A word family is a collection of words that share a common origin and often a similar core meaning, stemming from a single root word. Learning these roots is a highly effective way to expand your vocabulary because once you understand the root, you can often infer the meaning of many related words, even unfamiliar ones. This section focuses on one such powerful root: tempor.

2. The Latin Root tempor

The Latin root tempor is central to understanding a large family of English words. Its core meaning is "time" or "season." Whenever you encounter tempor within a word, you can anticipate that the word's meaning will in some way relate to time, duration, or a specific period.

3. Words Derived from tempor and Their Meanings

Let's explore some common and significant words that belong to the tempor family:

  • Temporary:

    • Breakdown: tempor (time) + -ary (relating to)
    • Meaning: Lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent. It refers to something existing only for a duration of time.
    • Example: The construction detour is only temporary and will be removed next week.

  • Contemporary:

    • Breakdown: con- (with, together) + tempor (time) + -ary (relating to)
    • Meaning: Existing, occurring, or living at the same time. Also, belonging to or characteristic of the present time (modern).
    • Example: Shakespeare and Queen Elizabeth I were contemporary figures. His artwork has a bold, contemporary style.

  • Contemporaneous:

    • Breakdown: con- (with, together) + tempor (time) + -aneous (of or relating to)
    • Meaning: Existing or occurring during the same period of time. Similar to contemporary but often used to describe events rather than people or objects.
    • Example: The reports indicate that the earthquake and the tsunami were contemporaneous events.

  • Temporal:

    • Breakdown: tempor (time) + -al (relating to)
    • Meaning:
      1. Relating to worldly affairs as opposed to spiritual or eternal ones; secular. This sense refers to things that exist within the confines of time, as opposed to eternal.
      2. Relating to time.
    • Example: The church leader addressed both spiritual and temporal concerns of the community. In physics, the fourth dimension is often referred to as temporal space.

  • Temporize:

    • Breakdown: tempor (time) + -ize (to make, to cause to be)
    • Meaning: To avoid making a definite decision or committing oneself in order to gain time; to delay.
    • Example: Faced with a difficult choice, the politician decided to temporize, hoping for a better solution to emerge.

  • Extemporaneous / Extempore:

    • Breakdown: ex- (out of) + tempor (time) + -aneous (of or relating to) / ex- (out of) + tempore (time, case form)
    • Meaning: Spoken or done without preparation; impromptu; done "out of the moment" or "out of time (for preparation)."
    • Example: Her extemporaneous speech, though unplanned, was eloquent and moving. He delivered an extempore response to the challenging question.

  • Tempo:

    • Breakdown: Direct borrowing from Italian tempo, which itself comes from Latin tempor (time).
    • Meaning: The speed at which a passage of music is or should be played; the pace of an activity or event. Directly relates to the timing and speed of something.
    • Example: The conductor set a brisk tempo for the allegro movement. The project's tempo has accelerated as the deadline approaches.

  • Temporality:

    • Breakdown: tempor (time) + -ality (state or quality of)
    • Meaning: The state or quality of existing within or having relation to time. A philosophical concept.
    • Example: Philosophers often debate the nature of human temporality and our experience of past, present, and future.

4. How Affixes Modify the Root

The power of word families comes from how prefixes and suffixes combine with the root to create nuanced meanings.

  • Prefixes like con- and ex- indicate relationships with time or out of time.
    • con- (together, with) + tempor -> contemporary, contemporaneous (existing with each other in time).
    • ex- (out of) + tempor -> extemporaneous (done out of the usual time for preparation, i.e., at the moment).
  • Suffixes like -ary, -al, -ize, -aneous, -ality change the word's grammatical function (noun, adjective, verb) and add specific qualifiers.
    • -ary (relating to) -> temporary, contemporary (describing a quality of being in time).
    • -al (relating to) -> temporal (describing something as relating to time or the world).
    • -ize (to make, to cause to be) -> temporize (to cause oneself to gain time, to delay).
    • -aneous (of or relating to) -> contemporaneous, extemporaneous (describing the nature of an event or action in time).

By dissecting words into their component parts – prefixes, roots, and suffixes – you can often deduce their meanings and build a stronger foundation for vocabulary acquisition.

Review Questions

  1. What is a word family, and why is understanding root words like tempor an effective strategy for vocabulary building?
  2. List three words from the tempor family and, for each, explain how its meaning is directly related to the concept of "time."
  3. Choose two words from the tempor family that use different prefixes (e.g., con-, ex-). Explain how these prefixes alter the core meaning of "time" in each word.
  4. Distinguish between the meanings of "contemporary" and "temporize." How does the specific suffix used in each word contribute to its distinct meaning?
  5. Imagine you encounter a new word, "supratemporal." Based on your understanding of the tempor root and common prefixes, what might you infer about its meaning? What steps would you take to confirm your inference?
