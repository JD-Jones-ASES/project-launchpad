Important Terms

  • Siege: A military operation in which enemy forces surround a town or building, cutting off essential supplies, with the aim of compelling the surrender of those inside. Metaphorically, a prolonged period of difficulty, pressure, or struggle.
  • Blockade: An act or means of sealing off a place to prevent goods or people from entering or leaving, often associated with a siege.
  • Fortification: A defensive wall or other reinforcement built to strengthen a place against attack.
  • Provisions: Supplies, especially of food, drink, and equipment, as for a journey, expedition, or during a siege.
  • Attrition: The process of gradually reducing the strength or effectiveness of someone or something through sustained attack or pressure.
  • Bed: A piece of furniture for sleeping or resting. Can also metaphorically represent a place of intimacy, illness, recovery, or domesticity.
  • Sanctuary: A place of refuge or safety; a sacred place. This term may become relevant if the "bed" or a specific private space serves as such during the siege.
