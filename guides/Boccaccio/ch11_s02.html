Study Guide: Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day the Tenth

This study guide provides an overview of the thematic and structural significance of Day the Tenth in Giovanni Boccaccio's Decameron. It explores the transition from the chaos of the plague to the pinnacle of human virtue, focusing on the concept of magnanimity as presented in the final stage of the brigata’s journey.

Key Concepts

The Structural Arc of the Decameron

The Decameron follows a deliberate organizational path. The work begins in Day I with the disorder and death caused by the plague. Over the course of ten days, the storytelling moves through various cycles—comic, tragic, and witty—eventually ascending to the noble themes of Day X. This "upward arc" represents a shift from social collapse toward courtesy, order, and virtue.

Magnificenza (Magnanimity)

The central theme of Day X is magnificenza, a term derived from the Latin magnificentia, meaning "great-doing."

  • Classical Roots: The concept is rooted in Aristotle’s Nicomachean Ethics, where megalopsychia (magnanimity) is described as the "crown virtue" of a great-souled individual.
  • Definition: In the context of Day X, it refers to a greatness of soul expressed through generous, active, and selfless deeds.
  • Application: The tales demonstrate this virtue through generosity in love, sacrifices made between enemies, and extreme patience.

Leadership and Tone

Panfilo serves as the king for Day the Tenth. His chosen theme is the most elevated in the entire work: stories of those who have performed generous or magnificent deeds in love or other matters. While the tone is unmistakably elevated and represents the "moral summit" of the work, Boccaccio maintains his characteristic irony and includes tales that are warm and humorous.

The Final Narrative

The storytelling concludes with Dioneo’s tale of Patient Griselda. This story is noted as the most discussed and debated tale in the Decameron, serving as the final word in the company's ten-day sequence.

Short-Answer Practice Questions

1. Who is the reigning king of Day the Tenth?

Answer: Panfilo.

2. What is the specific theme assigned to the stories of Day X?

Answer: Tales of those who have performed some generous or magnificent deed, whether in matters of love or otherwise.

3. According to the text, what does the word "magnificenza" literally mean in its Latin origin?

Answer: "Great-doing" (magnificentia).

4. How does the structural movement of the Decameron reflect a change in social conditions?

Answer: It mirrors a movement from the disorder and plague of the early days toward courtesy and virtue by the final day.

5. Which classical philosopher’s work influenced Boccaccio’s concept of the "great-souled person"?

Answer: Aristotle (Nicomachean Ethics).

6. What was the brigata discussing during their slow-paced walk at the start of Day X?

Answer: They were discussing their future way of life.

7. Which storyteller provides the final tale of the Decameron, and what is the subject?

Answer: Dioneo tells the final tale, which is the story of Patient Griselda.

Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

1. The Evolution of Virtue: Analyze the transition from the themes of earlier days (wit, fortune, and cleverness) to the theme of magnanimity in Day X. Why is "active and selfless" virtue positioned as the conclusion to the brigata’s storytelling journey?

2. Magnificenza as "Great-Doing": Using the definition provided in the text, discuss how the concept of magnanimity transcends simple kindness. How does the shift from "wit" to "magnificenza" change the moral landscape of the Decameron?

3. The Role of the Setting: Examine the introduction to Day X, specifically the description of the sunrise and the company's morning routine. How does the physical environment and the transition of light reflect the "elevated tone" Panfilo intends for the final day?

Glossary of Important Terms

Term Definition
Brigata The company of storytellers (the ladies and their comrades) who fled the plague.
Magnificenza Magnanimity; greatness of soul expressed through generous action.
Megalopsychia The Greek term for "magnanimity" used by Aristotle to describe the "crown virtue."
Moral Summit The description of Day X as the highest point of ethical and virtuous storytelling in the Decameron.
Panfilo The king of Day X who framed the theme of magnanimity.
Vermeil A reddish or flush color; used to describe the "cloudlets in the West" during the sunrise of Day X.
