# Study Guide: The Decameron, Day the First

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the introduction to Day the First of Giovanni Boccaccio’s *The Decameron*. It explores the transition of the "brigata" from the chaos of plague-stricken Florence to the structured retreat in Fiesole, the establishment of social order through storytelling, and the thematic significance of the first day’s narratives.

---

## Key Concepts and Narrative Overview

### The Transition to Order
The narrative begins with ten young people (the *brigata*) fleeing the "plague-stricken" city of Florence. They seek refuge in Fiesole, an ancient Etruscan town north of Florence known for its villas and rural retreats. Their arrival at a beautiful country estate represents a deliberate move from the disordered, chaotic world of the plague to a "civilized order." This order is established through specific rituals: their first meal together, the agreement on rules for storytelling, and the election of a leader.

### The Role of the Queen
Pampinea, the eldest of the women and the individual who originally proposed the retreat, is elected as the first queen. Her role is to govern the company and ensure their time is spent in a healthy and productive manner. She establishes the schedule, choosing the cool of the afternoon for storytelling to avoid the heat of the sun and the "vexation" associated with games like dice or chess.

### The Governance of Storytelling
Day I is unique in its structure. Along with Day IX, it is one of only two days where no specific theme is prescribed. 
*   **Freedom of Subject:** Narrators are granted complete freedom to speak on any topic they wish.
*   **The Overture:** Despite the lack of a theme, the ten tales of Day I serve as an "overture" to the entire work. They introduce foundational themes—such as religious hypocrisy, interfaith wisdom, royal rebuke, and sharp wit—that Boccaccio develops throughout the remaining ninety stories.

## Short-Answer Practice Questions

**1. Where is the country estate located, and what is the historical significance of that area?**
The estate is located in the hills of Fiesole, an ancient Etruscan town overlooking Florence from the north. It is historically associated with villas and rural retreats.

**2. Who is elected as the first queen of the group, and why?**
Pampinea is elected as the first queen. She is the eldest of the women and was the one who initially proposed the retreat from Florence.

**3. Why does Pampinea suggest storytelling instead of playing games like dice or chess?**
She argues that in games of chance, the loser is inevitably vexed, and neither the winner nor the onlookers derive much benefit. In contrast, storytelling provides "solace" and amusement for the entire company.

**4. What is the thematic rule for Day I, and how does it compare to other days in the work?**
There is no prescribed theme for Day I. It and Day IX are the only two days where the narrators have complete freedom to choose their subjects.

**5. Which narrator is chosen to tell the very first story?**
Pamfilo is commanded by the queen to lead off the storytelling.

**6. At what time of day does the storytelling take place, and why?**
The storytelling begins shortly after *none* (mid-afternoon) and continues until the *vesper* hour. This time is chosen because the sun is high and the heat is too intense for other outdoor activities.

## Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

### 1. The Contrast of Settings: Florence vs. Fiesole
Analyze how Boccaccio uses the setting of the Fiesole estate as a foil to the city of Florence. In your essay, discuss the specific elements of "civilized order" the *brigata* establishes and how these elements serve as a psychological and social defense against the chaos of the plague.

### 2. The Function of the "Overture"
Day I is described as an "overture" to the entire *Decameron*. Explore how the freedom of subject matter on the first day allows Boccaccio to introduce the diverse range of human experiences and social critiques found in the rest of the work. Reference the themes of religious hypocrisy and wit mentioned in the text.

### 3. Leadership and Social Utility
Examine Pampinea’s leadership style as the first queen. How does she balance the "pleasure" of the group with their "health" and social harmony? Discuss the significance of her decision to mandate storytelling as a collective activity rather than allowing individual play.

## Glossary of Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Brigata** | The company of ten young people who flee Florence for the countryside. |
| **Fiesole** | An ancient Etruscan town north of Florence; the site of the villa where the stories are told. |
| **None** | A traditional time of day corresponding to the ninth hour after sunrise (approximately 3:00 PM). |
| **Vesper** | The evening hour; the time when the heat abates and the storytelling concludes. |
| **Pampinea** | The eldest woman of the group and the queen of Day I; the architect of the retreat. |
| **Pamfilo** | The first member of the brigata to tell a story under the queen's command. |
| **Cicalas** | Insects (cicadas) mentioned by the queen to emphasize the heat and the stillness of the afternoon. |
| **Villa Palmieri** | The specific location traditionally identified as the setting for the estate in Fiesole. |
