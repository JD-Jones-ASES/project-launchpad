Chapter 5, Section 6: Polynomial Functions and Graphs Study Guide

Overview

This section explores the fundamental connection between the algebraic form of a polynomial function and the visual characteristics of its graph. It covers how to identify x-intercepts (zeros) and understand their behavior based on multiplicity, determine the graph's long-term trend (end behavior) from the leading term, and systematically combine these elements to accurately sketch a polynomial graph without relying on a graphing calculator.

Key Concepts

  • Zeros and x-Intercepts: The values of $x$ for which $P(x)=0$ are called zeros (or roots) of the polynomial. Graphically, these correspond to the points where the polynomial's graph crosses or touches the x-axis. By the Factor Theorem, if $c$ is a zero, then $(x-c)$ is a factor of $P(x)$.
  • Multiplicity and Graph Behavior: The multiplicity of a zero $c$ is the exponent $k$ of the factor $(x-c)^k$ in the polynomial's factored form. If $k$ is odd, the graph crosses the x-axis at $x=c$ (flattening out if $k \ge 3$). If $k$ is even, the graph touches the x-axis at $x=c$ but does not cross it (it "bounces back").
  • End Behavior: This describes what happens to the graph of a polynomial as $x$ approaches very large positive or very large negative values (i.e., as $x \to \infty$ or $x \to -\infty$). The end behavior is solely determined by the polynomial's leading term ($a_n x^n$), considering its degree and the sign of its leading coefficient.
  • Systematic Graph Sketching: A reliable process for sketching polynomial graphs involves determining end behavior, finding and plotting all intercepts (x and y), using multiplicities to guide behavior at x-intercepts, and testing points between zeros to confirm the graph's position above or below the x-axis before drawing a smooth, continuous curve.

Important Terms

  • Zero of a Polynomial (or Root): A value $c$ such that $P(c) = 0$. These are the x-coordinates of the x-intercepts of the graph $y = P(x)$.
  • Multiplicity: If $(x - c)^k$ is a factor of $P(x)$ but $(x-c)^{k+1}$ is not, then $c$ is a zero of multiplicity $k$. This value $k$ determines how the graph behaves at the x-intercept $c$.
  • End Behavior: The direction of the graph of a polynomial function as $x$ approaches positive infinity ($x \to \infty$) and as $x$ approaches negative infinity ($x \to -\infty$).
  • Leading Term: For a polynomial $P(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \dots + a_1 x + a_0$, the leading term is $a_n x^n$, where $a_n \neq 0$. Its degree $n$ and coefficient $a_n$ are crucial for determining end behavior.

Detailed Notes

Zeros and x-Intercepts

A polynomial function $P(x)$ is continuous and has a smooth curve. Its zeros are critical for understanding its graph.

  • A zero (or root) $c$ of a polynomial $P(x)$ is any value for which $P(c) = 0$.
  • By the Factor Theorem, $c$ is a zero of $P(x)$ if and only if $(x - c)$ is a factor of $P(x)$.
  • Graphically, the zeros of $P(x)$ are precisely the x-intercepts of the graph $y = P(x)$. This means the graph touches or crosses the x-axis at these points.

Multiplicity and Graph Behavior at a Zero

The behavior of a polynomial graph at its x-intercepts depends on the multiplicity of each zero.

  • Definition: If $(x - c)^k$ is a factor of $P(x)$, but $(x-c)^{k+1}$ is not, then $c$ is a zero of multiplicity $k$.
  • Rules for Graph Behavior:
    • Odd Multiplicity ($k$ is odd): The graph crosses the x-axis at $x = c$.
      • If $k = 1$, the graph crosses "straight through."
      • If $k \ge 3$, the graph still crosses, but it appears to "flatten out" or become tangent to the x-axis momentarily before crossing.
    • Even Multiplicity ($k$ is even): The graph touches the x-axis at $x = c$ but does not cross it; it "bounces back" from the x-axis. The graph turns around at this intercept.

Example: For $P(x) = x^2(x-3)^3(x+1)$, let's determine the zeros, multiplicities, and graph behavior.

  • Zero $x=0$: The factor is $x^2 = (x-0)^2$. The multiplicity is $k=2$ (even). Therefore, the graph touches (bounces) the x-axis at $x=0$.
  • Zero $x=3$: The factor is $(x-3)^3$. The multiplicity is $k=3$ (odd). Therefore, the graph crosses the x-axis at $x=3$, with a flattened appearance.
  • Zero $x=-1$: The factor is $(x+1) = (x-(-1))^1$. The multiplicity is $k=1$ (odd). Therefore, the graph crosses the x-axis at $x=-1$.

End Behavior

The end behavior describes how the graph looks on the far left and far right sides. It's determined by the polynomial's leading term, $a_n x^n$.

  • Degree $n$ (exponent of the leading term):
    • Even Degree: Both ends of the graph point in the same direction (both up or both down).
    • Odd Degree: The ends of the graph point in opposite directions (one up, one down).
  • Leading Coefficient $a_n$ (coefficient of the leading term):
    • Positive $a_n$: The right end of the graph points up ($y \to \infty$ as $x \to \infty$).
    • Negative $a_n$: The right end of the graph points down ($y \to -\infty$ as $x \to \infty$).

Combining these rules:

Degree ($n$) Leading Coefficient ($a_n$) End Behavior (as $x \to -\infty$, $y \to \dots$; as $x \to \infty$, $y \to \dots$) Description
Even Positive ($a_n > 0$) $y \to \infty$; $y \to \infty$ Both ends up
Even Negative ($a_n < 0$) $y \to -\infty$; $y \to -\infty$ Both ends down
Odd Positive ($a_n > 0$) $y \to -\infty$; $y \to \infty$ Falls left, Rises right
Odd Negative ($a_n < 0$) $y \to \infty$; $y \to -\infty$ Rises left, Falls right

Examples:

  • $f(x) = -2x^4 + 5x^2 - 1$
    • Leading term: $-2x^4$. Degree is 4 (even), leading coefficient is $-2$ (negative).
    • End behavior: Both ends point down (as $x \to \pm \infty$, $y \to -\infty$).
  • $g(x) = 3x^5 - x^3 + 7$
    • Leading term: $3x^5$. Degree is 5 (odd), leading coefficient is $3$ (positive).
    • End behavior: Falls to the left, rises to the right (as $x \to -\infty$, $y \to -\infty$; as $x \to \infty$, $y \to \infty$).

Sketching a Polynomial Graph

Follow these steps to produce a reliable sketch:

  1. Determine the End Behavior: Identify the leading term ($a_n x^n$) and use its degree and coefficient sign to determine if both ends go up/down or if one goes up and one goes down.
  2. Find all Zeros (x-intercepts) and their Multiplicities: Set $P(x) = 0$ and solve for $x$. Factor the polynomial if necessary. Plot these points on the x-axis. Note the multiplicity of each zero.
  3. Determine Behavior at each Zero: Use the multiplicity rule: If $k$ is odd, the graph crosses; if $k$ is even, the graph touches/bounces. Note if crossings are flattened.
  4. Find the y-intercept: Evaluate $P(0)$. Plot this point on the y-axis.
  5. Choose Test Values: Pick an x-value in each interval created by the x-intercepts. Evaluate $P(x)$ at these test values to determine if the graph is above or below the x-axis in that interval. This helps confirm the "flow" between zeros.
  6. Connect Smoothly: Draw a smooth, continuous curve that respects the end behavior, the behavior at each x-intercept, and the position determined by the test values.

Example 1: Sketch $f(x) = x^3 - 4x$. Label all intercepts.

  1. End Behavior: Leading term is $x^3$. Degree 3 (odd), leading coefficient 1 (positive). Falls left, rises right.
  2. Zeros: $f(x) = x(x^2 - 4) = x(x-2)(x+2)$. Zeros are $x=0$, $x=2$, $x=-2$. All have multiplicity 1 (odd). The graph crosses at all three.
  3. y-intercept: $f(0) = (0)^3 - 4(0) = 0$. The y-intercept is $(0,0)$, which is also an x-intercept.
  4. Test Values:
    • For $x < -2$ (e.g., $x=-3$): $f(-3) = (-3)^3 - 4(-3) = -27 + 12 = -15 < 0$. (Below x-axis)
    • For $-2 < x < 0$ (e.g., $x=-1$): $f(-1) = (-1)^3 - 4(-1) = -1 + 4 = 3 > 0$. (Above x-axis)
    • For $0 < x < 2$ (e.g., $x=1$): $f(1) = (1)^3 - 4(1) = 1 - 4 = -3 < 0$. (Below x-axis)
    • For $x > 2$ (e.g., $x=3$): $f(3) = (3)^3 - 4(3) = 27 - 12 = 15 > 0$. (Above x-axis)
  5. Connect Smoothly: Starting from the bottom left, cross at $x=-2$, rise to cross at $x=0$, fall to cross at $x=2$, and then rise to the top right.

Example 2: Sketch $g(x) = (x-1)^2(x+2)$.

  1. End Behavior: The leading term, if expanded, would be $x^2 \cdot x = x^3$. Degree 3 (odd), leading coefficient 1 (positive). Falls left, rises right.
  2. Zeros:
    • $x=1$: multiplicity 2 (even). Graph touches and bounces at $x=1$.
    • $x=-2$: multiplicity 1 (odd). Graph crosses at $x=-2$.
  3. y-intercept: $g(0) = (0-1)^2(0+2) = (-1)^2(2) = 1(2) = 2$. Y-intercept is $(0,2)$.
  4. Test Values:
    • For $x < -2$ (e.g., $x=-3$): $g(-3) = (-3-1)^2(-3+2) = (-4)^2(-1) = 16(-1) = -16 < 0$. (Below x-axis)
    • For $-2 < x < 1$ (e.g., $x=0$): $g(0) = 2 > 0$. (Above x-axis)
    • For $x > 1$ (e.g., $x=2$): $g(2) = (2-1)^2(2+2) = (1)^2(4) = 4 > 0$. (Above x-axis)
  5. Connect Smoothly: Starting from the bottom left, cross the x-axis at $x=-2$, rise up to pass through the y-intercept $(0,2)$, continue rising to touch the x-axis at $x=1$ from above, then bounce back up and continue rising to the top right.

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words what a "zero of a polynomial" is and how it relates to the graph of the polynomial.
  2. Describe how the multiplicity of a zero affects the way a polynomial graph behaves at its x-intercept. Give an example of a zero that crosses the x-axis and one that touches but does not cross.
  3. Without sketching the graph, describe the end behavior of the polynomial $P(x) = -5x^6 + 2x^4 - 10x$. Justify your answer using the rules of end behavior.
  4. Outline the complete step-by-step process for sketching a polynomial graph from its equation, highlighting the purpose of each step.
  5. Consider the polynomial $h(x) = -x^3(x+4)^2$.
    • State all zeros and their multiplicities.
    • Describe the graph's behavior (crosses/touches) at each zero.
    • Determine the end behavior of $h(x)$.
