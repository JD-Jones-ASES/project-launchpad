Understanding Percents: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section introduces the fundamental concept of percents, defining them as ratios that compare a number to 100. It demonstrates how to visualize percents using a 10x10 grid model and provides methods for converting fractions into their percent equivalents, emphasizing the importance of a denominator of 100. The section also highlights that percents can represent quantities less than, equal to, or greater than a whole.

Key Concepts

  • Definition of Percent: A percent is a specific type of ratio that expresses a number as a fraction of 100. The term "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "per hundred."
  • The Percent Symbol (%): The symbol % is universally used to denote "out of 100." For example, 45% directly translates to 45 out of 100 or the fraction 45/100.
  • Percent Grid Model: A 10x10 grid, containing 100 small, equal squares, is a visual tool to represent percents. Each individual small square within the grid represents 1% of the whole.
    • Shading N squares on the grid visually represents N%.
    • 100% means all 100 squares are shaded, representing the entire quantity.
    • 0% means no squares are shaded, representing none of the quantity.
  • Converting Fractions to Percents:
    • If a fraction already has a denominator of 100 (e.g., n/100), then the numerator (n) directly represents the percent (n%).
    • If a fraction does not have a denominator of 100, you must find an equivalent fraction where the denominator is 100. This is typically done by multiplying both the numerator and the denominator by the same factor.
  • Percents Beyond 100%: It's possible for a percent to be greater than 100%. This indicates that the quantity being described is more than the original whole or a starting value. For instance, 250% means two and a half times the original amount.

Important Terms

  • Percent: A ratio that compares a number to 100. It represents a part of a whole, expressed as "per hundred."
  • Ratio: A comparison of two numbers by division. Percents are a specific type of ratio where the second number is always 100.
  • Percent Grid: A 10x10 grid of 100 small squares, used as a visual model where each small square represents 1% of the whole.
  • Equivalent Fraction: Fractions that represent the same value, even though they have different numerators and denominators (e.g., 1/2 is equivalent to 50/100). This concept is crucial for converting fractions to percents when the denominator is not 100.

Detailed Notes

Understanding What a Percent Is

The concept of percent is deeply integrated into our daily lives, appearing in diverse contexts like sales discounts, academic scores, and financial reports. The word "percent" originates from the Latin phrase "per centum," which directly translates to "per hundred."

  • Core Definition: A percent is fundamentally a ratio that compares a specific number to 100. It answers the question, "How many parts out of every 100?"
  • The Percent Symbol: The symbol % is shorthand for "out of 100."
    • For example, 45% literally means 45 out of 100. As a fraction, this is written as 45/100.

Modeling Percents with Grids

A powerful visual tool for understanding percents is the percent grid model.

  • The Grid: This model consists of a 10 x 10 square grid, which is divided into 100 identical small squares.
  • Representing 1%: Each individual small square within this grid represents 1% of the total.
  • Visualizing Percents:
    • To represent 45%, you would shade 45 of the 100 small squares.
    • If all 100 squares are shaded, it shows 100%, signifying the entire quantity or whole.
    • If 0 squares are shaded, it shows 0%, indicating none of the quantity.
  • Quick Interpretations:
    • 100% always means "all" of something.
    • 50% means "half" of something.
    • 0% means "none" of something.

Example: Shading a Percent Grid

To represent 45% on a 10x10 grid, you simply shade 45 of the 100 available small squares.

Example: Writing a Percent from a Model

If a 10x10 grid has 72 shaded squares, then 72% of the grid is shaded, because each square represents 1%.

Converting Fractions to Percents

The ability to convert fractions into percents is a crucial skill. The core idea is to express the fraction with a denominator of 100.

  • Case 1: Denominator is Already 100
    • If you have a fraction n/100, the numerator n is directly the percent. So, n/100 = n%.
  • Case 2: Denominator is Not 100
    • When the denominator is not 100, you need to find an equivalent fraction that does have a denominator of 100.
    • Method: Determine what number you need to multiply the original denominator by to get 100. Then, multiply both the numerator and the denominator by that same number.

Example: Writing a Fraction as a Percent (3/4)

To write 3/4 as a percent:

  1. Identify the denominator: 4.
  2. Determine the factor to reach 100: 4 * 25 = 100.
  3. Multiply both numerator and denominator by 25: (3 * 25) / (4 * 25) = 75 / 100
  4. Since the denominator is now 100, the numerator is the percent: 75%.

Example: Test Score as a Percent (18 out of 20)

Maria answered 18 out of 20 questions correctly. To find her percent score:

  1. Form the fraction: 18/20.
  2. Determine the factor to reach 100: 20 * 5 = 100.
  3. Multiply both numerator and denominator by 5: (18 * 5) / (20 * 5) = 90 / 100
  4. Convert to percent: 90%.

Understanding Percents Greater Than 100%

It's important to recognize that percents are not limited to 0% to 100%. A percent can be greater than 100%.

  • Meaning: A percent greater than 100% signifies a quantity that is larger than the original whole or baseline amount.
  • Example: If a plant grew from 20 cm to 50 cm:
    • The new height (50 cm) compared to the original height (20 cm) is 50/20.
    • Converting 50/20 to a percent: (50 * 5) / (20 * 5) = 250 / 100 = 250%.
    • This means the new height is 250% of the original height, indicating it is 2.5 times the original size.
