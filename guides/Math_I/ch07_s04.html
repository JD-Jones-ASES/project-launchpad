The Percent Equation: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section introduces the foundational Percent Equation, a single formula designed to solve all three common types of percent problems. It demonstrates how to use part = percent * whole to find a part, a percent, or a whole, emphasizing the crucial step of converting percents to decimals (or fractions) before calculation.

Key Concepts

  • The Percent Equation: The core formula part = percent * whole serves as a versatile tool to solve various problems involving percentages.
  • Three Types of Percent Problems:
    • Finding the Part: Calculating a specific portion of a given whole when the percent is known (e.g., "What is 15% of 200?").
    • Finding the Percent: Determining what percentage one number is of another (e.g., "12 is what percent of 48?").
    • Finding the Whole: Identifying the total amount when a part and its corresponding percentage are known (e.g., "30 is 60% of what number?").
  • Percent Conversion: Before performing any calculations using the Percent Equation, the given percentage must be converted into its decimal equivalent (or a fraction).
  • Identifying Part and Whole: Carefully distinguishing between the "part" and the "whole" in a problem statement is critical for correctly setting up the equation. The "whole" often follows the word "of."

Important Terms

  • Part: The specific portion or amount that is being compared to the whole. In the equation part = percent * whole, it is the value represented by part.
  • Percent: A ratio out of 100, indicating how many parts per hundred. In the equation, it must be expressed as a decimal or fraction during calculation.
  • Whole: The total or entire amount to which the part is being compared. It represents the 100% of the quantity.
  • Percent Equation: The mathematical formula part = percent * whole, used to solve any problem involving finding a part, a percent, or a whole.

Detailed Notes

Introduction to The Percent Equation

In previous sections, you might have learned to find a percent of a number. This section unifies all types of percent problems into one simple yet powerful equation: part = percent * whole. This equation allows you to solve for any of the three variables if the other two are known.

The Percent Equation: part = percent × whole

It is essential to remember that when using this equation, the percent value must always be converted to a decimal (or a fraction) before you multiply or divide.

Three Types of Percent Problems and How to Solve Them

  1. Finding the Part: "What is _% of _?"

    • Goal: To find a specific portion of a known total.
    • Setup: The percent and whole are given, and you need to solve for part.
    • Method: Convert the percent to a decimal, then multiply it by the whole.
    • Example: "What is 15% of 200?"
      • Here, percent = 15% and whole = 200.
      • Convert 15% to a decimal: 0.15.
      • part = 0.15 × 200
      • part = 30
      • So, 30 is 15% of 200.

  2. Finding the Percent: "_ is what percent of _?"

    • Goal: To determine what percentage one number is of another.
    • Setup: The part and whole are given, and you need to solve for percent (represented by p).
    • Method: Divide the part by the whole to get a decimal, then convert that decimal to a percent.
    • Equation rearrangement: percent = part / whole
    • Example: "12 is what percent of 48?"
      • Here, part = 12 and whole = 48.
      • p = 12 / 48
      • p = 0.25
      • Convert the decimal to a percent by multiplying by 100: 0.25 × 100% = 25%.
      • So, 12 is 25% of 48.

  3. Finding the Whole: "_ is _% of what number?"

    • Goal: To find the total amount when a part and its corresponding percentage are known.
    • Setup: The part and percent are given, and you need to solve for whole (represented by w).
    • Method: Convert the percent to a decimal, then divide the part by that decimal.
    • Equation rearrangement: whole = part / percent
    • Example: "30 is 60% of what number?"
      • Here, part = 30 and percent = 60%.
      • Convert 60% to a decimal: 0.60.
      • w = 30 / 0.60
      • w = 50
      • So, 30 is 60% of 50.

Practical Application: Survey Results Example

Let's apply the Percent Equation to a real-world scenario.

Problem: In a survey, 78 students said they like science. This is 65% of all the students surveyed. How many students were surveyed in total?

  • Step 1: Read and Understand: We know that 78 students represent a portion of the total, and that portion is 65%. We need to find the total number of students.
  • Step 2: Identify the Variables:
    • part = 78 (the number of students who like science)
    • percent = 65% (the percentage this part represents)
    • whole = unknown (the total number of students surveyed)
  • Step 3: Write the Equation: Using part = percent × whole, substitute the known values.
    • 78 = 0.65 × w (Remember to convert 65% to its decimal form, 0.65)
  • Step 4: Solve for the Unknown: To isolate w, divide both sides of the equation by 0.65.
    • w = 78 / 0.65
    • w = 120
  • Step 5: Check Your Answer: Does 65% of 120 equal 78?
    • 0.65 × 120 = 78 (Yes, it checks out!)

Answer: 120 students were surveyed in total.

Important Considerations and Tips

  • Identify Part vs. Whole Carefully: A common mistake is misidentifying which number is the part and which is the whole. The whole usually follows the word "of" in a problem statement. For example, in "15% of 200," 200 is the whole.
  • Always Convert Percent: Never use the percent number directly in calculations (e.g., don't use 65 for 65%). Always convert it to a decimal (0.65) or a fraction (65/100).
  • Using Fractions: While decimals are often convenient, using fractions can also be helpful, especially for common percentages. For instance, "2/3 of 45" is the same as "66 2/3% of 45."
    • (2/3) × 45 = 30

Percent Equation Summary

  • General Formula: part = percent × whole
  • To find the part: part = (decimal percent) × whole
  • To find the percent: percent = part / whole (result will be a decimal; convert to percent)
  • To find the whole: whole = part / (decimal percent)
  • Key Rule: Always convert the percent to a decimal (or fraction) first!

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words why the single Percent Equation (part = percent * whole) is versatile enough to solve all three types of percent problems.
  2. What is the critical first step you must always take with the percent value before performing any multiplication or division in the Percent Equation? Why is this step necessary?
  3. Describe a strategy for correctly identifying which number in a word problem represents the "part" and which represents the "whole." Provide an example.
  4. A jacket is on sale for $45, which is 25% off its original price. Can you directly use part = 0.25 * whole to find the original price? Why or why not? (Hint: Think about what the "part" and "percent" truly represent in this scenario.)
  5. If you know that 30% of a number is 75, how would you set up the equation to find the original number? Solve it and explain the steps.
