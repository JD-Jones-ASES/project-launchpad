The Percent Equation: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section introduces the fundamental percent equation, part = percent × whole, which serves as a versatile tool for solving all types of percent problems. It demonstrates how to use this single equation to find a part, a percent, or a whole, emphasizing the critical step of converting the percent into a decimal or fraction before performing calculations.

Key Concepts

  • The Percent Equation: The core formula part = percent × whole is the universal relationship used to solve all percent problems. This equation connects three quantities: the part (a portion of the whole), the percent (the rate out of one hundred), and the whole (the total amount).
  • Three Types of Percent Problems: The equation can be rearranged to solve for any of its three components:
    1. Finding the Part: Calculating a certain percentage of a given whole (e.g., "What is 15% of 200?").
    2. Finding the Percent: Determining what percentage one number is of another (e.g., "12 is what percent of 48?").
    3. Finding the Whole: Identifying the total amount when a part and its corresponding percentage are known (e.g., "30 is 60% of what number?").
  • Percent Conversion: Before using the percent equation, it is crucial to convert the percent into its decimal equivalent (e.g., 15% becomes 0.15) or a fraction. Failure to do so will result in incorrect calculations.
  • Identifying "Part" and "Whole": Careful reading of word problems is essential to correctly identify which number represents the part and which represents the whole. A helpful tip is that the whole typically follows the word "of" in a sentence.

Important Terms

  • Part: The quantity that represents a specific portion or fraction of the total amount. In the equation, it's the result when the percent is applied to the whole.
  • Percent: A ratio that expresses a number as a fraction of 100. In calculations, it must be converted to a decimal or fraction form.
  • Whole: The total amount, the entire quantity, or the base from which a part is taken. It's the number that the percent is "of."
  • Percent Equation: The algebraic formula part = percent × whole, used to relate a part to its whole via a percentage.

Detailed Notes

The Universal Percent Equation

The foundation for solving any percent problem is the equation: part = percent × whole

In this equation:

  • part is the amount you are looking for, or the amount given that represents a portion of the total.
  • percent is the rate per hundred. It must always be converted to a decimal (or fraction) before being used in the equation.
  • whole is the total amount or the base quantity.

Type 1: Finding the Part

When you need to find a specific percentage of a number, you are solving for the part.

Formula: part = (decimal percent) × whole

Example: What is 15% of 200?

  1. Identify: percent = 15%, whole = 200. The part is unknown.
  2. Convert: 15% = 0.15
  3. Solve: part = 0.15 × 200 = 30 So, 30 is 15% of 200.

Type 2: Finding the Percent

When you know the part and the whole and need to determine what percentage the part is of the whole, you are solving for the percent.

Formula: percent = part / whole Remember to convert your decimal answer back to a percent by multiplying by 100 and adding the percent symbol.

Example: 12 is what percent of 48?

  1. Identify: part = 12, whole = 48. The percent is unknown.
  2. Solve: percent = 12 / 48 = 0.25
  3. Convert to Percent: 0.25 × 100 = 25% So, 12 is 25% of 48.

Checkpoint: 9 is what percent of 36?

  • percent = 9 / 36 = 0.25 = 25%

Type 3: Finding the Whole

When you know a part and the percent it represents, and you need to find the total amount, you are solving for the whole.

Formula: whole = part / (decimal percent)

Example: 30 is 60% of what number?

  1. Identify: part = 30, percent = 60%. The whole is unknown.
  2. Convert: 60% = 0.60
  3. Solve: whole = 30 / 0.60 = 50 So, 30 is 60% of 50.

Checkpoint: 18 is 40% of what number?

  • whole = 18 / 0.40 = 45

Solving Word Problems: A Step-by-Step Approach

Many real-world problems involve percents. Use a structured approach to solve them effectively.

Example: Survey Results In a survey, 78 students said they like science. This is 65% of all the students surveyed. How many students were surveyed in total?

  1. Step 1: Read. Understand the problem. We know a portion (78 students) and the percentage that portion represents (65%) of the total students surveyed.
  2. Step 2: Identify.
    • The part is 78 (the number of students who like science).
    • The percent is 65%.
    • The whole (total students surveyed) is unknown.
  3. Step 3: Write. Set up the percent equation. First, convert the percent: 65% = 0.65. 78 = 0.65 × w (where w represents the whole)
  4. Step 4: Solve. Isolate w by dividing both sides by 0.65. w = 78 / 0.65 = 120
  5. Step 5: Check. Does 65% of 120 equal 78? 0.65 × 120 = 78. Yes, it checks out. Conclusion: 120 students were surveyed in total.

Important Considerations and Tips

  • Identifying Part vs. Whole: Always read carefully! The whole often follows the word "of" in a sentence (e.g., "15% of 200" means 200 is the whole). The part is usually the number connected to the word "is" when referring to the percent (e.g., "12 is what percent").
  • Always Convert the Percent: Before multiplying or dividing in the equation, convert the percent to a decimal (divide by 100) or a fraction.
  • Using Fractions: For some percents, converting to a fraction might be easier, especially if the percent has a common fractional equivalent (e.g., 66 2/3% is 2/3).
    • Example: "2/3 of 45" is (2/3) × 45 = 30. This is the same as finding 66 2/3% of 45.

Percent Equation Summary

  • To find the part: part = percent × whole
  • To find the percent: percent = part / whole (then convert decimal to percent)
  • To find the whole: whole = part / percent
