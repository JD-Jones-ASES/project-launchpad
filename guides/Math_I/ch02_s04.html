Multiplying and Dividing Integers Study Guide

Overview

This section extends your knowledge of multiplication and division to include positive and negative integers. It focuses on the crucial sign rules that determine whether the result of an operation is positive or negative, and also addresses special cases involving zero. Understanding these rules is fundamental for working with integers in more complex mathematical problems.

Key Concepts

  • Sign Rules for Multiplication and Division: When multiplying or dividing two integers:
    • If the integers have the same sign (both positive or both negative), the result is positive.
    • If the integers have different signs (one positive and one negative), the result is negative.
  • Multiplication by Zero: Any integer multiplied by zero always results in zero.
  • Division by Zero is Undefined: You cannot divide any number by zero; this operation has no mathematical solution.
  • Zero Divided by a Non-Zero Number: Zero divided by any non-zero integer is zero.
  • Multiplication with Multiple Factors: The sign of a product involving three or more factors depends on the count of negative factors: an even number of negative factors yields a positive product, while an odd number yields a negative product.

Important Terms

  • Integer: A whole number (not a fraction or decimal) that can be positive, negative, or zero (e.g., -5, 0, 12).
  • Product: The result obtained when two or more numbers are multiplied together.
  • Quotient: The result obtained when one number is divided by another.
  • Factor: A number that is multiplied by another number to get a product.
  • Undefined: In mathematics, a term used to describe an expression that does not have a meaningful or calculable value, such as any number divided by zero.

Detailed Notes

This section builds upon your existing knowledge of multiplication and division of whole numbers by introducing how to perform these operations with positive and negative integers. The main focus is on the sign rules that govern the outcomes.

I. Sign Rules for Multiplying Integers

The sign of the product (the answer to a multiplication problem) is determined by the signs of the integers you are multiplying.

  1. Multiplying Integers with the Same Sign:

    • Positive $\times$ Positive = Positive: When both factors are positive, the product is positive. This is standard whole number multiplication.
      • Example: $6 \times 7 = 42$
    • Negative $\times$ Negative = Positive: When both factors are negative, the product is positive. This is a crucial rule for integers.
      • Example: $(-4) \times (-9) = 36$
      • Understanding why "negative times negative is positive": Consider the pattern:
        • $3 \times (-1) = -3$
        • $2 \times (-1) = -2$
        • $1 \times (-1) = -1$
        • $0 \times (-1) = 0$
        • Following this pattern, as the first factor decreases by 1, the product increases by 1. Therefore, $(-1) \times (-1)$ must be $0 + 1 = 1$. This pattern helps illustrate why two negative numbers multiplied together result in a positive number.

  2. Multiplying Integers with Different Signs:

    • Positive $\times$ Negative = Negative: When one factor is positive and the other is negative, the product is negative.
      • Example: $7 \times (-2) = -14$
    • Negative $\times$ Positive = Negative: Similarly, when the first factor is negative and the second is positive, the product is negative.
      • Example: $(-5) \times 3 = -15$

  3. Multiplying by Zero:

    • Any integer multiplied by zero always results in zero.
    • For every integer $a$: $a \times 0 = 0$.
    • Example: $15 \times 0 = 0$; $(-23) \times 0 = 0$.

II. Sign Rules for Dividing Integers

The rules for determining the sign of a quotient (the answer to a division problem) are exactly the same as the rules for multiplication.

  1. Dividing Integers with the Same Sign:

    • Positive $\div$ Positive = Positive:
      • Example: $20 \div 5 = 4$
    • Negative $\div$ Negative = Positive:
      • Example: $(-36) \div (-4) = 9$

  2. Dividing Integers with Different Signs:

    • Positive $\div$ Negative = Negative:
      • Example: $42 \div (-7) = -6$
    • Negative $\div$ Positive = Negative:
      • Example: $-24 \div 8 = -3$

  3. Special Cases with Zero in Division:

    • Zero Divided by a Non-Zero Number: If zero is divided by any non-zero integer, the quotient is always zero.
      • Example: $0 \div (-5) = 0$.
    • Division by Zero is Undefined: You cannot divide any number by zero. Attempting to do so yields an undefined result. This is because division can be thought of as repeatedly subtracting the divisor from the dividend. If the divisor is zero, you can never "subtract" enough zeros to reach the dividend (unless the dividend is also zero, which leads to another indeterminate form).
      • Example: $5 \div 0$ has no answer and is undefined.

III. Multiplying Three or More Factors

When you need to multiply more than two integers, you can determine the sign of the final product by counting the number of negative factors:

  • If there is an even number of negative factors in the product, the final product will be positive.

  • If there is an odd number of negative factors in the product, the final product will be negative.

  • Important Note: If any of the factors are zero, the entire product will automatically be zero, regardless of the signs or number of other factors.

  • Example: $(-2) \times (-3) \times (-1)$

    • There are three negative factors (an odd number).
    • Therefore, the product will be negative: $(-2) \times (-3) \times (-1) = (6) \times (-1) = -6$.

IV. Real-World Applications

Integer operations are frequently used to model and solve real-world problems involving quantities that can increase or decrease.

  • Example: A stock price drops $3 each day for 5 days. A drop of $3 can be represented as $-3$. The total change in price is $5 \times (-3) = -15$. This means the stock price decreased by $15 overall.

Review Questions

  1. Explain the sign rules for multiplying two integers. Provide an example for each of the four possible cases (positive $\times$ positive, negative $\times$ negative, positive $\times$ negative, negative $\times$ positive).
  2. How do the sign rules for dividing integers relate to the rules for multiplying integers? Can you give an example of a division problem that results in a positive quotient and one that results in a negative quotient?
  3. What happens when you multiply an integer by zero? What about dividing zero by a non-zero integer? Use examples to illustrate your answers.
  4. Why is division by zero considered "undefined"? Explain the concept in your own words.
  5. Consider the expression $(-4) \times (-2) \times 5 \times (-1) \times (-3)$. Without actually calculating the product, explain how you would determine the sign of the final answer. Then, calculate the product.
