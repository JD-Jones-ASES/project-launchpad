Study Guide: Applications of Percents (Tax, Tip, Discount, Simple Interest)

Overview

This section introduces practical applications of percents in everyday financial scenarios. It covers how to calculate sales tax on purchases, tips for services, discounts on items, and simple interest earned or paid on money. Understanding these concepts is crucial for managing personal finances and making informed consumer decisions.

Key Concepts

  • Sales Tax: An additional amount of money charged by the government on purchases, calculated as a percentage of the item's price. The total cost is the original price plus the sales tax.
  • Tip (Gratuity): An extra amount of money given for a service (e.g., at a restaurant), calculated as a percentage of the bill. The total amount paid is the bill plus the tip.
  • Discount: A reduction in the original price of an item, calculated as a percentage of the original price. The sale price is the original price minus the discount amount. A useful shortcut is to calculate the sale price by multiplying the original price by (100% - discount rate).
  • Simple Interest: Money earned (on deposits) or paid (on loans) on an original amount called the principal. It is calculated using the formula $I = Prt$, where $I$ is the interest, $P$ is the principal, $r$ is the annual interest rate (as a decimal), and $t$ is the time in years.
  • Rate Conversion: When using percent rates in formulas (like for simple interest), always convert the percent to its decimal equivalent by dividing by 100.
  • Time in Years: For simple interest calculations, the time variable ($t$) must always be expressed in years. If time is given in months, convert it to years by dividing by 12.

Important Terms

  • Sales Tax: An extra amount of money charged by the government on purchases, based on a percentage of the item's price.
  • Tax Rate: The percentage used to calculate the sales tax.
  • Tip (Gratuity): An extra amount of money given for a service, usually calculated as a percentage of the bill.
  • Tip Rate: The percentage used to calculate the tip amount.
  • Discount: A reduction in the original price of an item, usually expressed as a percentage.
  • Discount Rate: The percentage used to calculate the discount amount.
  • Original Price: The initial price of an item before any discount is applied.
  • Sale Price: The price of an item after a discount has been applied.
  • Simple Interest: Interest calculated only on the principal amount, without compounding.
  • Principal (P): The initial amount of money deposited, invested, or borrowed.
  • Interest (I): The amount of money earned on an investment or paid for borrowing money.
  • Annual Interest Rate (r): The percentage rate at which interest is charged or earned per year, expressed as a decimal in the simple interest formula.
  • Time (t): The duration, in years, for which the principal is invested or borrowed.

Detailed Notes

Percents are an integral part of financial calculations in daily life. This section focuses on four common applications: sales tax, tips, discounts, and simple interest.

1. Sales Tax

Sales tax is an amount added to the price of goods or services by the government.

  • Formula for Tax Amount: tax amount = tax rate × price
  • Formula for Total Cost: total cost = price + tax amount

Example: A book costs $24.00, and the sales tax rate is 7%.

  1. Calculate the tax amount: tax amount = 0.07 × $24.00 = $1.68
  2. Calculate the total cost: total cost = $24.00 + $1.68 = $25.68

Checkpoint Example: Shoes cost $45.00 with a 6% sales tax.

  1. Tax = 0.06 × $45.00 = $2.70
  2. Total = $45.00 + $2.70 = $47.70

2. Tip (Gratuity)

A tip is extra money given for a service, typically at restaurants or for other service providers.

  • Formula for Tip Amount: tip amount = tip rate × bill
  • Formula for Total Paid: total = bill + tip amount

Example: A restaurant bill is $52.00, and a 15% tip is desired.

  1. Calculate the tip amount: tip amount = 0.15 × $52.00 = $7.80
  2. Calculate the total amount paid: total = $52.00 + $7.80 = $59.80

Checkpoint Example: A lunch bill is $30.00 with a 20% tip.

  1. Tip = 0.20 × $30.00 = $6.00
  2. Total = $30.00 + $6.00 = $36.00

3. Discount

A discount is a reduction in the original price of an item.

  • Formula for Discount Amount: discount amount = discount rate × original price
  • Formula for Sale Price: sale price = original price - discount amount

Example: A backpack has an original price of $55.00 and is 20% off.

  1. Calculate the discount amount: discount amount = 0.20 × $55.00 = $11.00
  2. Calculate the sale price: sale price = $55.00 - $11.00 = $44.00

Shortcut Note: If an item is 20% off, you are paying 100% - 20% = 80% of the original price.

  • Sale price = 0.80 × $55.00 = $44.00 This shortcut can save a step in calculations.

4. Simple Interest

Simple interest is calculated on the principal amount only. It is a straightforward way to calculate the interest on a loan or investment over a period.

  • Formula: $I = Prt$
    • $I$ = Interest earned or paid
    • $P$ = Principal (the original amount of money)
    • $r$ = Annual interest rate (expressed as a decimal)
    • $t$ = Time (in years)

Important Cautions:

  • The rate ($r$) must be converted from a percent to a decimal (e.g., 4% = 0.04).
  • The time ($t$) must be in years. If given in months, divide by 12 (e.g., 6 months = 6/12 = 0.5 years).

Example: Rosa deposits $500 in an account earning 4% simple interest per year for 3 years.

  1. Identify variables:
    • Principal ($P$) = $500
    • Annual Rate ($r$) = 4% = 0.04 (as a decimal)
    • Time ($t$) = 3 years
  2. Apply the formula: I = Prt = $500 × 0.04 × 3
  3. Calculate the interest: I = $60.00
  4. Calculate the total amount in the account (Principal + Interest): Total amount = $500 + $60 = $560

Checkpoint Example: James borrows $1,000 at 5% simple interest for 2 years.

  1. P = $1,000, r = 0.05, t = 2
  2. I = $1,000 × 0.05 × 2 = $100

Summary of Formulas

  • Sales Tax:
    • tax = rate × price
    • total = price + tax
  • Tip:
    • tip = rate × bill
    • total = bill + tip
  • Discount:
    • discount = rate × original price
    • sale price = original price - discount
  • Simple Interest:
    • I = Prt (where P = principal, r = annual rate as decimal, t = time in years)
    • total amount = principal + interest

Review Questions

  1. A new video game costs $59.99. If the sales tax rate is 8.5%, what is the amount of sales tax, and what is the total cost a customer will pay?
  2. You and your friends have a dinner bill of $78.50. You decide to leave a 18% tip. How much is the tip, and what is the total amount you will pay, including the tip?
  3. A jacket originally priced at $120 is on sale for 30% off. Calculate the discount amount and the sale price of the jacket. Explain how you could find the sale price using a single multiplication step.
  4. Maria invests $2,500 in a savings account that pays 3% simple interest annually. How much interest will she earn after 4 years? What will be the total amount in her account at the end of 4 years?
  5. David takes out a small loan of $800 at a 10% annual simple interest rate for 18 months. How much interest will he owe, and what is the total amount he must repay? Remember to adjust the time ($t$) to years.
