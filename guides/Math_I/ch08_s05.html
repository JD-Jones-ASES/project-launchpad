Study Guide: Solving One-Step Equations (Multiplication and Division)

Overview

This section builds on previous knowledge of solving equations by focusing on one-step equations that involve multiplication and division. The core principle remains the same: use the inverse operation to isolate the variable. You will learn and apply the Multiplication and Division Properties of Equality to find solutions, including those with fractional or decimal coefficients, and check your answers.

Key Concepts

  • Inverse Operations: To undo multiplication, use division. To undo division, use multiplication. This is the fundamental strategy for isolating the variable.
  • Multiplication Property of Equality: You can multiply both sides of an equation by the same number without changing the equation's balance or solution. If $a=b$, then $ac=bc$.
  • Division Property of Equality: You can divide both sides of an equation by the same nonzero number without changing the equation's balance or solution. If $a=b$ and $c \neq 0$, then $a/c = b/c$. Dividing by zero is undefined.
  • Solving $ax = b$: To solve an equation where a variable is multiplied by a coefficient (e.g., $6x = 42$), divide both sides of the equation by that coefficient.
  • Solving $x/a = b$: To solve an equation where a variable is divided by a number (e.g., $n/5 = 9$), multiply both sides of the equation by that number.
  • Fractional Coefficients: When the variable is multiplied by a fraction (e.g., $2/3x = 10$), the most efficient way to solve is to multiply both sides by the reciprocal of the fraction.
  • Checking Solutions: Always substitute your solution back into the original equation to verify that it makes the equation true.

Important Terms

  • Multiplication Property of Equality: The rule stating that if two quantities are equal, multiplying both by the same number maintains their equality.
  • Division Property of Equality: The rule stating that if two quantities are equal, dividing both by the same nonzero number maintains their equality.
  • Inverse Operation: An operation that "undoes" another operation (e.g., multiplication is the inverse of division).
  • Coefficient: The numerical factor of a term that contains a variable (e.g., in $6x$, $6$ is the coefficient).
  • Reciprocal: One of a pair of numbers whose product is 1. To find the reciprocal of a fraction, flip the numerator and denominator (e.g., the reciprocal of $2/3$ is $3/2$).

Detailed Notes

The Core Idea: Using Inverse Operations

Just like with addition and subtraction, solving equations involving multiplication and division relies on the idea of inverse operations. To get a variable by itself (isolate it), you need to "undo" whatever operation is being applied to it.

  • If the variable is being multiplied by a number, you divide both sides of the equation by that number.
  • If the variable is being divided by a number, you multiply both sides of the equation by that number.

Properties of Equality

These properties ensure that when you perform an operation on both sides of an equation, the equality remains true, and thus the solution remains correct.

  • Multiplication Property of Equality:

    • If $a = b$, then $ac = bc$.
    • This means you can multiply both sides of an equation by any number $c$.

  • Division Property of Equality:

    • If $a = b$ and $c \neq 0$, then $a/c = b/c$.
    • This means you can divide both sides of an equation by any nonzero number $c$. It's crucial that $c$ is not zero, as division by zero is undefined.

Solving Equations of the Form $ax = b$

In these equations, the variable $x$ is multiplied by a coefficient $a$. Example: Solve $6x = 42$.

  1. Identify the operation: $x$ is being multiplied by $6$.
  2. Apply the inverse operation: To undo multiplication by $6$, we divide both sides by $6$. $6x / 6 = 42 / 6$
  3. Simplify: $x = 7$.
  4. Check your solution: Substitute $7$ back into the original equation: $6(7) = 42$. Since $42 = 42$, the solution is correct.

Solving Equations of the Form $x/a = b$

In these equations, the variable $x$ is divided by a number $a$. Example: Solve $n/5 = 9$.

  1. Identify the operation: $n$ is being divided by $5$.
  2. Apply the inverse operation: To undo division by $5$, we multiply both sides by $5$. $5 \times (n/5) = 5 \times 9$
  3. Simplify: $n = 45$.
  4. Check your solution: Substitute $45$ back into the original equation: $45/5 = 9$. Since $9 = 9$, the solution is correct.

Dealing with Fractional and Decimal Coefficients

  • Fractional Coefficients: When the coefficient of the variable is a fraction, the easiest way to isolate the variable is to multiply by its reciprocal. Example: Solve $2/3x = 10$.

    1. Identify the coefficient: The coefficient is $2/3$.
    2. Find the reciprocal: The reciprocal of $2/3$ is $3/2$.
    3. Multiply both sides by the reciprocal: $(3/2) \times (2/3x) = (3/2) \times 10$ $x = 30/2$
    4. Simplify: $x = 15$.
    5. Check: $2/3(15) = (2 \times 15) / 3 = 30 / 3 = 10$. This is correct.
    • Caution: Remember that multiplying by a fraction's reciprocal is the inverse of multiplying by the fraction. Do not try to "subtract the fraction" – subtraction undoes addition, not multiplication.

  • Decimal Coefficients: When the coefficient is a decimal, you can solve by simply dividing both sides by the decimal. Example: Solve $0.5w = 12.5$.

    1. Identify the operation: $w$ is being multiplied by $0.5$.
    2. Apply the inverse operation: Divide both sides by $0.5$. $0.5w / 0.5 = 12.5 / 0.5$
    3. Simplify: $w = 25$.
    4. Check: $0.5(25) = 12.5$. This is correct.

Solving Word Problems

Many real-world situations can be modeled and solved using one-step multiplication or division equations. Follow a structured approach:

Example: Sharing Equally Four friends share the cost of a gift equally. Each person pays $13.50. What was the total cost of the gift?

  1. Read: Understand the problem. The total cost is divided among 4 friends, and each pays $13.50.
  2. Identify: Assign a variable. Let $c$ = total cost of the gift.
  3. Write: Formulate an equation. Since the total cost ($c$) is divided by 4 friends, and each pays $13.50, the equation is $c/4 = 13.50$.
  4. Solve:
    • To isolate $c$, multiply both sides by $4$.
    • $4 \times (c/4) = 4 \times 13.50$
    • $c = 54$.
  5. Check: Does $54 / 4 = 13.50$? Yes. The total cost of the gift was $54.00.

Summary of Solving One-Step Equations (Multiplication/Division)

  • For $ax = b$: Divide both sides by $a$ ($x = b/a$).
  • For $x/a = b$: Multiply both sides by $a$ ($x = ab$).
  • For fractional coefficients: Multiply by the reciprocal of the coefficient.
  • Always check your solution by substituting it back into the original equation.

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words why we use inverse operations when solving equations. Provide an example using either multiplication or division.
  2. Solve the equation $7x = 91$ and clearly show each step, including checking your answer.
  3. Solve the equation $m/8 = 12$. Describe the property of equality you used to find the solution.
  4. A recipe calls for $3/4$ cup of sugar, which is half the amount needed for a double batch. Let $s$ be the amount of sugar needed for a double batch. Write and solve an equation to find $s$.
  5. What is the key difference in solving an equation like $5x = 20$ compared to solving $x/5 = 20$? Explain why different inverse operations are used.
