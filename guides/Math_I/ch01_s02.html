Study Guide: Order of Operations

Overview

This section establishes the universally accepted rules for evaluating numerical expressions, known as the order of operations. By following a consistent sequence, often remembered by the acronym PEMDAS, mathematicians ensure that everyone arrives at the same answer when calculating expressions involving multiple operations and various grouping symbols.

Key Concepts

  • Purpose of Order of Operations: A set of agreed-upon rules to ensure a single, consistent answer when evaluating any numerical expression. Without it, expressions with multiple operations could yield different results depending on the order in which they are performed.
  • PEMDAS: An acronym representing the standard order of operations:
    • Parentheses (and other grouping symbols)
    • Exponents (not explicitly covered with examples in this section, but part of the standard order)
    • Multiplication and Division (performed from left to right)
    • Addition and Subtraction (performed from left to right)
  • Grouping Symbols: Symbols like parentheses $( ,,)$, brackets $[ ,,]$, and fraction bars indicate which operations must be performed first, overriding the standard PEMDAS order within that group.
  • Innermost Grouping First: When an expression contains nested grouping symbols (e.g., parentheses inside brackets), operations within the innermost group are evaluated first, working outward.
  • Left-to-Right Rule: For operations at the same level (multiplication and division, or addition and subtraction), they are performed in the order they appear from left to right in the expression.
  • Fraction Bar as a Grouping Symbol: A fraction bar implicitly groups both the entire numerator and the entire denominator, meaning operations within each must be completed before performing the division indicated by the bar.

Important Terms

  • Numerical Expression: A combination of numbers and operations (such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) that represents a single numerical value.
  • Grouping Symbols: Special characters used in numerical expressions to indicate which operations should be performed before others. Common examples include parentheses $(,,)$, brackets $[,,]$, and fraction bars.
  • Order of Operations (PEMDAS): A universally accepted set of rules that dictates the sequence in which operations are performed in a numerical expression to ensure a consistent result.

Detailed Notes

The Need for Order of Operations

When an expression like $7 + 3 \times 5$ has multiple operations, the result can differ significantly based on which operation is performed first. Adding first yields $10 \times 5 = 50$, while multiplying first yields $7 + 15 = 22$. To avoid ambiguity and ensure everyone arrives at the same correct answer, mathematicians established a fixed order of operations.

What is a Numerical Expression?

A numerical expression is any combination of numbers and operations (like $+$, $-$, $\times$, $\div$) that ultimately represents a single numerical value. For example, $7 + 3 \times 5$ is a numerical expression.

Grouping Symbols

Grouping symbols are crucial for indicating operations that must be performed first, even if they wouldn't ordinarily be first according to the standard order. The most common grouping symbols are:

  • Parentheses: $(,,)$
  • Brackets: $[,,]$
  • Fraction Bar: The horizontal line in a fraction, like in $\frac{a+b}{c}$. It implicitly groups the entire numerator and the entire denominator as separate expressions that must be evaluated before the division is carried out.

The Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

To evaluate numerical expressions consistently, follow these steps in order:

  1. P — Parentheses (and other grouping symbols): Perform all operations inside parentheses, brackets, or above/below a fraction bar first. If there are nested grouping symbols (e.g., parentheses inside brackets), work from the innermost group outward.
  2. E — Exponents: Evaluate any exponents. (Note: While 'E' is part of PEMDAS, this section does not provide examples involving exponents).
  3. M/D — Multiplication and Division: Perform all multiplication and division operations. These operations are at the same level of precedence, so you must work from left to right across the expression as you encounter them.
  4. A/S — Addition and Subtraction: Perform all addition and subtraction operations. These operations are also at the same level of precedence, so you must work from left to right across the expression as you encounter them.

Examples Applying PEMDAS

Example 1: Two Operations (Multiplication before Addition) Evaluate $7 + 3 \times 5$.

  • Solution: Multiplication comes before addition. $7 + 3 \times 5 = 7 + 15 = 22$.

Example 2: Left-to-Right Rule (Division and Multiplication) Evaluate $24 \div 6 \times 2$.

  • Solution: Division and multiplication are at the same level, so perform them from left to right. $24 \div 6 \times 2 = 4 \times 2 = 8$.

Checkpoint: Evaluate $36 - 4 \times 7$.

  • Answer: Multiply first: $36 - 28 = 8$.

Example 3: Parentheses Evaluate $4 (9 - 3) + 10$.

  • Solution: Perform operations inside parentheses first. $4 (9 - 3) + 10$ $= 4 \times 6 + 10$ (Parentheses first) $= 24 + 10$ (Multiply) $= 34$ (Add)

Example 4: Nested Grouping Symbols Evaluate $2 [14 - (2 + 3)]$.

  • Solution: Work from the innermost grouping outward. $2 [14 - (2 + 3)]$ $= 2 [14 - 5]$ (Inner parentheses first) $= 2 \times 9$ (Brackets next) $= 18$ (Multiply)

Checkpoint: Evaluate $3 (8 + 2) - 5$.

  • Answer: $3 \times 10 - 5 = 30 - 5 = 25$.

Important Notes and Cautions

  • Fraction Bar as Grouping: Remember that a fraction bar acts as a grouping symbol. For example, in $\frac{10 + 6}{4}$, you must evaluate the numerator ($10+6=16$) before dividing by the denominator ($16 \div 4 = 4$).
  • Common Mistake: A frequent error is performing addition or subtraction before multiplication or division when no grouping symbols indicate otherwise. Always remember that multiplication and division take precedence over addition and subtraction (unless parentheses dictate a different order).

Order of Operations Summary

  • Use PEMDAS: Parentheses (and other grouping symbols), Exponents, Multiplication/Division (left to right), Addition/Subtraction (left to right).
  • When dealing with nested grouping symbols, start from the innermost group and work your way outward.
  • A fraction bar implicitly groups the numerator and denominator separately.
  • If two operations are at the same level of precedence (M/D or A/S), perform them from left to right.
