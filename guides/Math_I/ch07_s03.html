Finding a Percent of a Number

Overview

This section introduces a fundamental skill in working with percents: calculating a specific percentage of a given number. You will learn a straightforward two-step process that involves converting the percent into a decimal (or fraction) and then multiplying it by the whole number to find the desired part. This skill is essential for real-world applications like calculating discounts, tips, or understanding proportions.

Key Concepts

  • Converting Percents to Decimals: Before performing any calculations with percents, they must be converted into a decimal (or fraction) form. This is typically done by dividing the percent by 100, which is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the left.
  • "Of" Means Multiply: In percent problems, the word "of" almost always indicates a multiplication operation. For example, "25% of 80" translates to "0.25 multiplied by 80."
  • Formula for Finding a Part: The relationship between a percent, a whole, and a part can be expressed as: Percent × Whole = Part.
  • Percents Greater Than 100%: If the percent you are finding is greater than 100% (e.g., 150%), the resulting "part" will be larger than the original "whole" number. This makes sense as you are finding more than the entire quantity.
  • Common Mistake: A frequent error is to multiply by the percent number directly (e.g., using 25 instead of 0.25 for 25%). Always convert the percent to a decimal or fraction before multiplying.

Important Terms

  • Percent: A ratio that compares a number to 100, meaning "per hundred." Represented by the symbol %.
  • Whole: The total amount or original number from which a part is being taken or calculated.
  • Part: The result obtained when a percent of a whole is calculated; a fractional portion or multiple of the whole.
  • Discount: A reduction in price, often expressed as a percent of the original price.

Detailed Notes

The Core Method: Finding a Percent of a Number

To determine a specific percent of any given number, follow these two crucial steps:

  1. Convert the Percent: Change the percent into its decimal equivalent. To do this, divide the percent by 100. This is the same as moving the decimal point two places to the left.

    • Example: $25% = 0.25$ (25 divided by 100)
    • Example: $8.5% = 0.085$ (8.5 divided by 100)
    • Example: $150% = 1.50$ (150 divided by 100)

  2. Multiply: Once the percent is in decimal form, multiply this decimal by the original number (the whole). The result will be the "part" you are looking for.

    • The general formula is: Decimal Equivalent of Percent × Whole Number = Part
    • Remember, the word "of" in a percent problem signifies multiplication.

Examples in Practice

1. Basic Calculation:

  • Problem: What is $25%$ of $80$?
  • Solution:
    • Convert: $25% = 0.25$
    • Multiply: $0.25 \times 80 = 20$
  • Answer: $25%$ of $80$ is $20$.

2. Percent with a Decimal:

  • Problem: Find $8.5%$ of $200$.
  • Solution:
    • Convert: $8.5% = 0.085$
    • Multiply: $0.085 \times 200 = 17$
  • Answer: $8.5%$ of $200$ is $17$.

3. Percent Greater Than 100:

  • Problem: Find $150%$ of $40$.

  • Solution:

    • Convert: $150% = 1.50$
    • Multiply: $1.50 \times 40 = 60$

  • Observation: Since $150%$ is greater than $100%$, the result ($60$) is greater than the original number ($40$). This makes sense as you're taking more than the full amount.

  • Checkpoint Example: Find $200%$ of $35$.

    • Convert: $200% = 2.00$
    • Multiply: $2.00 \times 35 = 70$

4. Real-World Application (Sale Discount):

  • Problem: A jacket that regularly costs $60 is on sale for $30%$ off. How much is the discount?
  • Solution Steps:
    • Step 1: Read. Understand that the discount is $30%$ of the original price, $60.
    • Step 2: Identify. The percent is $30%$, the whole is $60. You need to find the part, which is the discount amount.
    • Step 3: Write. Set up the problem: discount = 0.30 * 60.
    • Step 4: Solve.
      • Convert: $30% = 0.30$
      • Multiply: $0.30 \times 60 = 18$
    • Answer: The discount is $18.
    • Step 5: Check. The sale price would be $60 - $18 = $42. This seems reasonable, as $18 is roughly one-third of $60.

Important Caution

Always, always, always convert the percent to a decimal (or fraction) before multiplying. A common mistake is to multiply by the numerical value of the percent (e.g., using 25 instead of 0.25 for 25%), which will lead to an incorrect answer that is far too large.

Review Questions

  1. Explain, in your own words, why it's necessary to convert a percent to a decimal before multiplying it by a number. What would happen if you skipped this step?
  2. If you are calculating $75%$ of $120$, what are the two steps you would take? Show your work.
  3. A recipe calls for $120%$ of the usual amount of flour. If the usual amount is 2 cups, how much flour will you use for this recipe? Why is the answer greater than 2 cups?
  4. Describe a real-life scenario, other than a discount, where finding a percent of a number would be a useful skill. How would you apply the steps learned in this section?
  5. What does the word "of" typically signify in a percent problem, and why is it important to remember this when setting up an equation?
