Order of Operations with Integers: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section extends the familiar order of operations (PEMDAS) to include integers. While the fundamental rules remain the same, careful attention to negative signs and the specific handling of negative bases in exponents are crucial for accurately evaluating expressions involving integers.

Key Concepts

  • PEMDAS for Integers: The standard order of operations—Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division (from left to right), and Addition/Subtraction (from left to right)—applies universally to expressions involving integers.
  • Grouping Symbols: Parentheses (), brackets [], and absolute-value bars | | all act as grouping symbols. Operations within the innermost grouping symbols must be simplified first.
  • Absolute Value: When absolute-value bars are present, first simplify the expression inside the bars, then take the absolute value of the result.
  • Exponents with Negative Bases:
    • If a negative number is inside parentheses and raised to an exponent, like (-2)^3, the entire negative number is the base.
    • If a negative sign is outside the base, like -2^3, only the positive number is the base, and the negative sign is applied after evaluating the exponent. This distinction is critical for even vs. odd exponents.
  • Left-to-Right Rule: For operations at the same level (Multiplication/Division or Addition/Subtraction), perform them in the order they appear from left to right.
  • Tracking Signs: Meticulously keeping track of positive and negative signs at every step is the most important "new twist" when applying the order of operations to integers.

Important Terms

  • Order of Operations (PEMDAS): An acronym representing the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (left to right), Addition and Subtraction (left to right).
  • Grouping Symbols: Symbols like parentheses (), brackets [], and absolute-value bars | | that indicate a part of an expression that should be evaluated first.
  • Absolute Value: The distance of a number from zero on the number line, always represented as a non-negative value. For example, |-5| = 5 and |5| = 5.
  • Base (of an exponent): The number or expression that is being multiplied by itself a specified number of times. In x^n, x is the base.
  • Evaluate: To find the numerical value of a mathematical expression.
  • Integer: A whole number (not a fraction) that can be positive, negative, or zero (e.g., -3, 0, 5).

Detailed Notes

The Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

The order of operations is a fundamental rule in mathematics that ensures expressions are evaluated consistently to arrive at a single, correct answer. When working with integers, the rules remain the same, but you must be extra careful with negative signs.

  1. P - Parentheses (and other Grouping Symbols):

    • Always begin by simplifying expressions inside parentheses (), brackets [], braces {}, or absolute-value bars | |.
    • If there are nested grouping symbols (one set inside another), work from the innermost set outward.
    • Example: In 2[10 - 3(1 - 4)], you first simplify (1 - 4) before anything else.
    • Absolute Value as Grouping: Treat absolute-value bars like parentheses. Simplify the expression inside the bars first, then take the absolute value of the result.
      • Example: To evaluate |-8 + 3|, first calculate -8 + 3 = -5, then take |-5| = 5.

  2. E - Exponents:

    • After handling grouping symbols, evaluate all exponents.
    • Crucial Distinction for Negative Bases:
      • (-a)^n: When a negative number is enclosed in parentheses and then raised to a power, the entire negative number is the base.
        • (-2)^3 = (-2)(-2)(-2) = -8 (Odd number of negative factors results in a negative product).
        • (-3)^2 = (-3)(-3) = 9 (Even number of negative factors results in a positive product).
      • -a^n: When there are no parentheses around a negative base, the negative sign is not part of the base. The exponent only applies to the positive number, and the negative sign is applied after the exponent is evaluated.
        • -2^3 = -(2^3) = -(2 \cdot 2 \cdot 2) = -8.
        • -3^2 = -(3^2) = -(3 \cdot 3) = -9.
    • Always pay close attention to whether the negative sign is inside or outside the parentheses when dealing with exponents.

  3. MD - Multiplication and Division (from left to right):

    • After exponents, perform all multiplication and division operations.
    • It's important to remember that these operations have equal precedence. You perform them in the order they appear as you read the expression from left to right.
    • Example: In 12 ÷ 3 × 2, you would first do 12 ÷ 3 = 4, then 4 × 2 = 8.

  4. AS - Addition and Subtraction (from left to right):

    • Finally, perform all addition and subtraction operations.
    • These also have equal precedence and are performed in the order they appear from left to right.
    • Example: In -4 - 12 + 5, you would first do -4 - 12 = -16, then -16 + 5 = -11.

Applying the Rules with Examples

Example: Basic Multi-Operation Evaluate -3 + 4(-2).

  1. Multiplication first: 4(-2) = -8.
  2. Addition: -3 + (-8) = -11.

Example: Nested Grouping Symbols Evaluate 2[10 - 3(1 - 4)].

  1. Innermost Parentheses: (1 - 4) = -3. Expression becomes 2[10 - 3(-3)].
  2. Multiplication inside brackets: 3(-3) = -9. Expression becomes 2[10 - (-9)].
  3. Subtraction inside brackets: 10 - (-9) = 10 + 9 = 19. Expression becomes 2(19).
  4. Multiplication: 2(19) = 38.

Example: Absolute Value Evaluate |-8 + 3| - |-2 - 7|.

  1. Simplify inside first absolute value: -8 + 3 = -5.
  2. Simplify inside second absolute value: -2 - 7 = -9. Expression becomes |-5| - |-9|.
  3. Take absolute values: |-5| = 5 and |-9| = 9. Expression becomes 5 - 9.
  4. Subtract: 5 - 9 = -4.

Example: Word Problem Application (Wind Chill) Air temperature is -4°F, with a 6 mph wind, and sunshine (add 5). Wind chill is: air temperature, subtract 2 for each mph of wind, then add 5 for sunshine.

  1. Formulate Expression: -4 - 2(6) + 5.
  2. Multiplication: 2(6) = 12. Expression becomes -4 - 12 + 5.
  3. Subtraction (left to right): -4 - 12 = -16. Expression becomes -16 + 5.
  4. Addition: -16 + 5 = -11. The wind chill is -11°F.

By following PEMDAS meticulously and paying close attention to the rules for operations with integers, especially signs, you can accurately evaluate any complex expression.

Review Questions

  1. Explain why the order of operations (PEMDAS) is important when evaluating expressions involving integers. What could happen if you don't follow it?
  2. Evaluate the expression: -15 ÷ 3 - 4(-2) + (-6). Show each step of your work.
  3. Differentiate between (-5)^2 and -5^2. Explain why their results are different and provide the value for each.
  4. Evaluate the expression 20 - 3|7 - (10 - 2)|. Describe how you handled the nested grouping symbols and the absolute value.
  5. A submarine descends 100 feet from sea level, then rises 30 feet, and then descends again by 2 times its current depth. Write an expression using integers to represent its final depth relative to sea level, and then evaluate it.
