Chapter 7, Section 2: Fractions, Decimals, and Percents

Overview

This section focuses on the fundamental skill of converting numbers between their three primary forms: fractions, decimals, and percents. Mastering these conversions allows for greater flexibility and understanding when working with quantities, proportions, and real-world applications. The section provides clear, reliable methods for transforming any number from one form to another.

Key Concepts

  • Interchangeable Forms: Fractions, decimals, and percents are simply different ways of representing the same numerical value or proportion.
  • Percent as "Per Hundred": A percent literally means "per hundred" or "out of one hundred." This foundational understanding is key to all percent conversions.
  • Decimal Point Movement Shortcut: Multiplying or dividing by 100 can be quickly accomplished by moving the decimal point two places to the right (for multiplication) or two places to the left (for division).
  • Conversion Pathways:
    • Percent $\leftrightarrow$ Decimal: Direct conversion through multiplication or division by 100.
    • Percent $\leftrightarrow$ Fraction: Percents can be written as fractions over 100, which can then be simplified. Fractions can be converted to decimals first, then to percents.
  • Percents Greater than 100%: A percent greater than 100% represents a value greater than a whole (greater than 1).
  • Percents Less than 1%: A percent less than 1% represents a very small fraction of a whole, and its decimal form will have leading zeros after the decimal point (e.g., 0.5% = 0.005).

Important Terms

  • Fraction: A numerical quantity that is not an integer (a whole number). It represents a part of a whole, typically written as a numerator over a denominator (e.g., $3/5$).
  • Decimal: A number that uses a decimal point to represent a part of a whole, where digits to the right of the decimal point indicate tenths, hundredths, thousandths, and so on (e.g., $0.6$, $1.25$).
  • Percent: A ratio or fraction expressed as parts per one hundred. It is denoted by the symbol "%" (e.g., $60%$, $125%$).
  • Numerator: The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.
  • Denominator: The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts in the whole.
  • Simplify (a fraction): To reduce a fraction to its simplest form by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor.

Detailed Notes

The Relationship Between Fractions, Decimals, and Percents

All three forms—fractions, decimals, and percents—are ways to express the same quantity. Understanding how to convert between them is a vital mathematical skill, allowing you to choose the most appropriate form for a given situation or calculation.

Converting Between Percents and Decimals

Percent to Decimal

To convert a percent to a decimal, you perform one of the following equivalent operations:

  • Divide the percent by 100.
  • Move the decimal point two places to the left. (This is a shortcut for dividing by 100).

Example: Write $35%$ as a decimal.

  • Solution: Move the decimal point two places to the left from its implied position after the 5. $35% = 35.0 \rightarrow 0.35$

Example: Write $125%$ as a decimal.

  • Solution: $125% = 1.25$

Caution for Percents Less Than 1%: When converting a percent less than $1%$ to a decimal, remember to still move the decimal point two places to the left.

  • Example: Convert $0.5%$ to a decimal. $0.5% = 00.5 \rightarrow 0.005$ (not $0.5$)

Decimal to Percent

To convert a decimal to a percent, you perform one of the following equivalent operations:

  • Multiply the decimal by 100.
  • Move the decimal point two places to the right and attach the percent symbol ($%$). (This is a shortcut for multiplying by 100).

Example: Write $0.08$ as a percent.

  • Solution: Move the decimal point two places to the right. $0.08 \rightarrow 8.0 = 8%$

Example: Write $0.455$ as a percent.

  • Solution: $0.455 \rightarrow 45.5 = 45.5%$

Converting Between Percents and Fractions

Percent to Fraction

To convert a percent to a fraction:

  1. Write the percent value over 100. (Remember, percent means "per hundred").
  2. Simplify the resulting fraction to its lowest terms.

Example: Write $60%$ as a fraction.

  • Solution: Write $60$ over $100$ and simplify. $60% = \frac{60}{100} = \frac{6 \div 2}{10 \div 2} = \frac{3}{5}$

Example: Write $125%$ as a mixed number (fraction).

  • Solution: $125% = \frac{125}{100}$. Simplify by dividing both by 25. $\frac{125 \div 25}{100 \div 25} = \frac{5}{4}$. As a mixed number, $\frac{5}{4} = 1\frac{1}{4}$.

Fraction to Percent

To convert a fraction to a percent:

  1. Divide the numerator by the denominator to get a decimal.
  2. Convert the resulting decimal to a percent (by multiplying by 100 or moving the decimal point two places to the right and adding the $%$ symbol).

Example: Write $\frac{3}{5}$ as a percent.

  • Solution:
    1. Divide: $3 \div 5 = 0.6$.
    2. Convert decimal to percent: $0.6 \times 100 = 60%$. Alternatively, move decimal right: $0.6 \rightarrow 60%$.

Example: Write $\frac{7}{8}$ as a percent.

  • Solution:
    1. Divide: $7 \div 8 = 0.875$.
    2. Convert decimal to percent: $0.875 \times 100 = 87.5%$. Alternatively, move decimal right: $0.875 \rightarrow 87.5%$.

Real-World Application: Nutrition Labels

Example: A cereal box states that one serving provides $0.15$ of the recommended daily amount of iron. Write this as a percent.

  • Problem: Convert the decimal $0.15$ to a percent.
  • Solution: To convert a decimal to a percent, multiply by $100$ (or move the decimal point two places to the right). $0.15 \times 100 = 15%$.
  • Check: $15% = 0.15$. The conversion is correct.

Summary of Conversions:

  • Percent $\rightarrow$ Decimal: Divide by $100$ (move decimal two places left).
  • Decimal $\rightarrow$ Percent: Multiply by $100$ (move decimal two places right).
  • Percent $\rightarrow$ Fraction: Write over $100$ and simplify.
  • Fraction $\rightarrow$ Percent: Divide numerator by denominator, then convert the decimal to a percent.
  • A percent greater than $100%$ corresponds to a number greater than $1$.

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words why fractions, decimals, and percents are considered different ways to write the same number. Provide an example to illustrate your point.
  2. Describe the steps to convert $0.725$ into a percent and into a simplified fraction.
  3. A recipe calls for $\frac{3}{4}$ cup of flour. If you wanted to express this quantity as a decimal and as a percent, what would those values be?
  4. You see a discount of $5%$ on a product, and a friend tells you another store offers a discount of $0.05$. Are these the same discount? Why or why not? What if another store offered a discount of $\frac{1}{20}$?
  5. What does a percent like $200%$ signify? How would you write $200%$ as a decimal and as a mixed number?
