Dividing Mixed Numbers Study Guide

Overview

This section explains how to divide mixed numbers by first converting them into improper fractions. Once converted, the division problem transforms into a multiplication problem where you multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction (the divisor), followed by cross-cancellation and simplification.

Key Concepts

  • Convert to Improper Fractions First: Before performing any division or finding a reciprocal, all mixed numbers must be converted into improper fractions. This is a crucial first step to avoid errors.
  • Division as Multiplication by the Reciprocal: Dividing fractions (or improper fractions) is equivalent to multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction (the divisor).
  • Cross-Cancellation: Always look for common factors in the numerators and denominators after rewriting the division as multiplication by the reciprocal. This simplifies calculations and makes the final fraction easier to reduce.
  • Simplification: After multiplying, the resulting improper fraction should be simplified, often by converting it back into a mixed number, unless otherwise specified.
  • Word Problem Context: When solving word problems involving division of mixed numbers, pay close attention to what the question asks. If it asks for "how many full bags/pieces," round down your answer to the nearest whole number.

Important Terms

  • Mixed Number: A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $3,12$).
  • Improper Fraction: A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $72$).
  • Reciprocal: A fraction obtained by inverting another fraction (swapping the numerator and denominator). For example, the reciprocal of $78$ is $87$.
  • Divisor: The number by which another number is divided. In $a \div b$, $b$ is the divisor.
  • Cross Cancel: A technique used in fraction multiplication to simplify before multiplying. If a numerator and a denominator share a common factor, you can divide both by that factor.

Detailed Notes

The process for dividing mixed numbers builds directly on the skills learned for multiplying and dividing proper and improper fractions. The key differentiator is the initial conversion step.

The Steps for Dividing Mixed Numbers

  1. Convert Each Mixed Number to an Improper Fraction: This is the most critical first step. For example, to convert $3,12$ to an improper fraction: multiply the whole number by the denominator ($3 \times 2 = 6$), then add the numerator ($6 + 1 = 7$). Keep the original denominator, so $3,12 = 72$.
  2. Rewrite Division as Multiplication by the Reciprocal of the Divisor: Identify the divisor (the second fraction in the division problem). Find its reciprocal by flipping its numerator and denominator. Then, change the division sign ($\div$) to a multiplication sign ($\times$).
    • Caution: Never try to find the reciprocal of a mixed number directly. Always convert it to an improper fraction before finding the reciprocal. For instance, the reciprocal of $3,12$ is not $2,13$. It's the reciprocal of $72$, which is $27$.
  3. Cross Cancel Common Factors: Before multiplying straight across, look for any common factors between any numerator and any denominator. Divide both by their common factor to simplify the numbers involved. This makes the multiplication easier and the final answer simpler.
  4. Multiply Numerators and Denominators: After cross-cancellation, multiply the remaining numerators together and the remaining denominators together.
  5. Simplify or Convert Back to a Mixed Number: If the resulting fraction is improper, convert it back to a mixed number for the final answer, unless the context requires an improper fraction. Ensure the fractional part is fully simplified.

Examples Breakdown

Example 1: Mixed Number Divided by a Fraction

Problem: Divide $3,12 \div 78$

Solution Steps:

  1. Convert to improper fraction: $3,12 = \frac{(3 \times 2) + 1}{2} = 72$.
  2. Rewrite as multiplication by reciprocal: The divisor is $78$. Its reciprocal is $87$. So, $72 \times 87$.
  3. Cross cancel:
    • The $7$ in the numerator of the first fraction and the $7$ in the denominator of the second fraction can be canceled (divide both by 7). They become $1$.
    • The $2$ in the denominator of the first fraction and the $8$ in the numerator of the second fraction can be canceled (divide both by 2). The $2$ becomes $1$, and the $8$ becomes $4$.
    • The expression becomes $11 \times 41$.
  4. Multiply: $1 \times 4 = 4$ (numerator), $1 \times 1 = 1$ (denominator). This gives $41$.
  5. Simplify: $41 = 4$.

Example 2: Mixed Number Divided by a Mixed Number

Problem: Divide $4,12 \div 1,14$

Solution Steps:

  1. Convert both to improper fractions:
    • $4,12 = \frac{(4 \times 2) + 1}{2} = 92$.
    • $1,14 = \frac{(1 \times 4) + 1}{4} = 54$.
  2. Rewrite as multiplication by reciprocal: The divisor is $54$. Its reciprocal is $45$. So, $92 \times 45$.
  3. Cross cancel:
    • The $2$ in the denominator of the first fraction and the $4$ in the numerator of the second fraction can be canceled (divide both by 2). The $2$ becomes $1$, and the $4$ becomes $2$.
    • The expression becomes $91 \times 25$.
  4. Multiply: $9 \times 2 = 18$ (numerator), $1 \times 5 = 5$ (denominator). This gives $185$.
  5. Simplify to a mixed number: $18 \div 5 = 3$ with a remainder of $3$. So, $185 = 3,35$.

Example 3: Sharing Equally (Word Problem)

Problem: A bag contains $5,14$ pounds of trail mix. It is divided equally into bags that each hold $1,12$ pounds. How many full bags can be made?

Solution Steps:

  1. Understand the problem: This is a division problem: total amount $\div$ amount per bag.
  2. Write the expression: $5,14 \div 1,12$.
  3. Convert to improper fractions:
    • $5,14 = \frac{(5 \times 4) + 1}{4} = 214$.
    • $1,12 = \frac{(1 \times 2) + 1}{2} = 32$.
  4. Rewrite as multiplication by reciprocal: $214 \times 23$.
  5. Cross cancel:
    • The $4$ in the denominator of the first fraction and the $2$ in the numerator of the second fraction can be canceled (divide both by 2). The $4$ becomes $2$, and the $2$ becomes $1$.
    • The $21$ in the numerator of the first fraction and the $3$ in the denominator of the second fraction can be canceled (divide both by 3). The $21$ becomes $7$, and the $3$ becomes $1$.
    • The expression becomes $72 \times 11$.
  6. Multiply: $7 \times 1 = 7$ (numerator), $2 \times 1 = 2$ (denominator). This gives $72$.
  7. Simplify to a mixed number: $7 \div 2 = 3$ with a remainder of $1$. So, $72 = 3,12$.
  8. Answer the specific question: The problem asks for "how many full bags." Since we have $3,12$ bags, only $3$ full bags can be made. The remaining $12$ of a bag represents the leftover trail mix.

Key Point for Word Problems: If a word problem asks for "how many full [items]," always round down the result of your division to the nearest whole number. Even if you have, for example, $3.9$ bags, you only have $3$ full bags.

Review Questions

  1. Explain in your own words why it is essential to convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before taking the reciprocal in a division problem.
  2. Describe the full step-by-step process for dividing $2,23 \div 1,13$, including how you would cross-cancel.
  3. What is the reciprocal of $4,35$? Show your work.
  4. Calculate the following: $6,14 \div 2,12$. Express your answer as a mixed number in simplest form.
  5. A recipe calls for $3,12$ cups of flour, but you only want to make $14$ of the recipe. How much flour do you need? If the problem instead asked, "How many batches of cookies can you make if you have $3,12$ cups of flour and each batch requires $14$ cups?", how would your answer be different?
