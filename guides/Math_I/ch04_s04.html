Study Guide: Dividing Mixed Numbers

Overview

This section focuses on the process of dividing mixed numbers. The core strategy involves converting all mixed numbers into improper fractions first, then applying the standard procedure for dividing fractions: multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second (the divisor). The section emphasizes careful conversion and simplification, especially when interpreting answers in word problems.

Key Concepts

  • Convert to Improper Fractions First: Before performing any division or finding a reciprocal, all mixed numbers must be converted into their equivalent improper fraction form.
  • Division as Multiplication by Reciprocal: The operation of dividing fractions (and thus improper fractions) is transformed into multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the divisor.
  • Cross Cancellation: Look for common factors between numerators and denominators before multiplying to simplify calculations and reduce the final fraction.
  • Simplification: Always simplify the resulting fraction, converting it back to a mixed number if it's an improper fraction, unless a specific form is required (e.g., in word problems).
  • Word Problem Context: When solving word problems, pay attention to whether the question asks for "full" items, which may require rounding down the final answer, or an exact numerical value.

Important Terms

  • Mixed Number: A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $3\frac{1}{2}$).
  • Improper Fraction: A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $\frac{7}{2}$).
  • Reciprocal: A pair of numbers whose product is 1. To find the reciprocal of a fraction, flip the numerator and denominator (e.g., the reciprocal of $\frac{a}{b}$ is $\frac{b}{a}$).
  • Divisor: The number by which another number is divided (in $a \div b$, $b$ is the divisor).
  • Cross Cancel: A simplification technique used in multiplication of fractions where common factors are divided out from a numerator and an opposite denominator before multiplying.
  • Simplify: To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms or convert an improper fraction to a mixed number.

Detailed Notes

The Process for Dividing Mixed Numbers

Dividing mixed numbers combines the skills of converting between mixed numbers and improper fractions, and then applying the rules for dividing fractions. It's a multi-step process that needs to be followed carefully.

To divide mixed numbers, follow these steps:

  1. Convert to Improper Fractions: Change each mixed number in the problem into an improper fraction. This is a crucial first step.

    • Caution: Never try to find the reciprocal of a mixed number directly. Always convert it to an improper fraction first before flipping. There is no shortcut for reciprocating a mixed number.

  2. Rewrite as Multiplication by the Reciprocal: Change the division operation to multiplication. To do this, you must "flip" the divisor (the second fraction) to its reciprocal.

  3. Cross Cancel Common Factors: Look for any common factors between any numerator and any denominator. Divide both by the common factor to simplify the numbers before multiplying. This makes the multiplication easier and often leads to a simplified answer more quickly.

  4. Multiply Numerators and Denominators: Multiply the numerators together and multiply the denominators together.

  5. Simplify and Convert: If the resulting fraction is improper, convert it back to a mixed number. Always ensure your final answer is in its simplest form.

Examples

Let's walk through the examples provided in the section to illustrate these steps.

Example 1: Mixed Number Divided by a Fraction Divide: $3\frac{1}{2} \div \frac{7}{8}$

  • Step 1: Convert to improper fractions. $3\frac{1}{2} = \frac{(3 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{7}{2}$ (The divisor $\frac{7}{8}$ is already a proper fraction, so no conversion needed for it.)

  • Step 2: Rewrite as multiplication by the reciprocal. $\frac{7}{2} \div \frac{7}{8} = \frac{7}{2} \times \frac{8}{7}$

  • Step 3: Cross cancel common factors. We can cancel the $7$s (dividing both by $7$) and cancel $2$ and $8$ (dividing both by $2$). $\frac{\cancel{7}^1}{\cancel{2}^1} \times \frac{\cancel{8}^4}{\cancel{7}^1}$

  • Step 4: Multiply numerators and denominators. $\frac{1 \times 4}{1 \times 1} = \frac{4}{1}$

  • Step 5: Simplify. $\frac{4}{1} = 4$

Example 2: Mixed Number Divided by a Mixed Number Divide: $4\frac{1}{2} \div 1\frac{1}{4}$

  • Step 1: Convert both to improper fractions. $4\frac{1}{2} = \frac{(4 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{9}{2}$ $1\frac{1}{4} = \frac{(1 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{5}{4}$

  • Step 2: Rewrite as multiplication by the reciprocal. $\frac{9}{2} \div \frac{5}{4} = \frac{9}{2} \times \frac{4}{5}$

  • Step 3: Cross cancel common factors. We can cancel $2$ and $4$ (dividing both by $2$). $\frac{9}{\cancel{2}^1} \times \frac{\cancel{4}^2}{5}$

  • Step 4: Multiply numerators and denominators. $\frac{9 \times 2}{1 \times 5} = \frac{18}{5}$

  • Step 5: Simplify and convert to a mixed number. $\frac{18}{5} = 3\frac{3}{5}$ (since $18 \div 5 = 3$ with a remainder of $3$)

Example 3: Word Problem - Sharing Equally A bag contains $5\frac{1}{4}$ pounds of trail mix. It is divided equally into bags that each hold $1\frac{1}{2}$ pounds. How many full bags can be made?

  • Step 1: Understand the problem. We need to divide the total amount of trail mix by the amount each small bag holds. $5\frac{1}{4} \div 1\frac{1}{2}$

  • Step 2: Convert to improper fractions. $5\frac{1}{4} = \frac{(5 \times 4) + 1}{4} = \frac{21}{4}$ $1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{(1 \times 2) + 1}{2} = \frac{3}{2}$

  • Step 3: Rewrite as multiplication by the reciprocal. $\frac{21}{4} \div \frac{3}{2} = \frac{21}{4} \times \frac{2}{3}$

  • Step 4: Cross cancel common factors. We can cancel $4$ and $2$ (dividing both by $2$) and $21$ and $3$ (dividing both by $3$). $\frac{\cancel{21}^7}{\cancel{4}^2} \times \frac{\cancel{2}^1}{\cancel{3}^1}$

  • Step 5: Multiply numerators and denominators. $\frac{7 \times 1}{2 \times 1} = \frac{7}{2}$

  • Step 6: Simplify and interpret the answer. $\frac{7}{2} = 3\frac{1}{2}$

    The question asks "How many full bags can be made?" Since $3\frac{1}{2}$ means $3$ whole bags and half of another, the answer is $3$ full bags. There will be some trail mix remaining.

    • Note: In word problems, always decide whether the answer should be a whole number (often by rounding down if asking for "full" items) or an exact answer based on the context of the question.

Summary of Dividing Mixed Numbers

  • Always convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions first.
  • Change the division operation to multiplication by the reciprocal of the divisor.
  • Utilize cross cancellation to simplify calculations.
  • Multiply numerators and denominators.
  • Simplify the final fraction, converting back to a mixed number if appropriate.
  • For word problems, interpret the answer carefully based on what the question is asking (e.g., "how many full bags").
