Adding and Subtracting Decimals: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Overview

This section explains how to add and subtract decimals, a process very similar to working with whole numbers but with a crucial extra step: aligning the decimal points. You'll learn the step-by-step procedure, including how to handle whole numbers in decimal operations and solve real-world problems involving money.

Key Concepts

  • Aligning Decimal Points: Before adding or subtracting decimals, you must write the numbers vertically and ensure their decimal points are lined up. This aligns digits according to their place value (ones under ones, tenths under tenths, etc.).
  • Annexing Zeros: If numbers have a different number of decimal places, you should add zeros to the end of the decimal with fewer places until all numbers have the same count of digits after the decimal point. This does not change the value of the number but helps maintain proper place value alignment during calculation.
  • Adding/Subtracting Like Whole Numbers: Once decimal points are aligned and zeros are annexed, you perform the addition or subtraction operation exactly as you would with whole numbers, starting from the rightmost column.
  • Placing the Decimal Point in the Answer: The decimal point in your final sum or difference will be placed directly below the decimal points in the numbers you added or subtracted.
  • Whole Numbers and Decimals: When performing operations involving a whole number and a decimal, remember that a whole number can be written with a decimal point and annexed zeros (e.g., $12 = 12.00$).
  • Borrowing and Carrying: The concepts of borrowing in subtraction and carrying in addition apply to decimal operations in the same way they do for whole numbers.

Important Terms

  • Sum: The result obtained when two or more numbers are added together.
  • Difference: The result obtained when one number is subtracted from another.
  • Annex Zeros: To add zeros to the end of a decimal number without changing its value. This is typically done to ensure all numbers have the same number of decimal places before an operation.
  • Place Value: The value of a digit determined by its position within a number (e.g., in 3.45, the '3' is in the ones place, '4' in the tenths place, '5' in the hundredths place).

Detailed Notes

The Fundamental Rule: Line Up Decimal Points

The most important rule when adding or subtracting decimals is to line up the decimal points. This ensures that you are adding or subtracting digits with the same place value (e.g., tens with tens, tenths with tenths, hundredths with hundredths). Failing to do so will lead to incorrect answers.

Step-by-Step Procedure for Adding and Subtracting Decimals

  1. Write the numbers vertically and line up the decimal points. This is the crucial first step to ensure correct place value alignment.
  2. Annex zeros so that all numbers have the same number of decimal places. This makes the columns complete and often prevents errors, especially in subtraction. For example, to add $3.45 + 12.7$, you'd rewrite $12.7$ as $12.70$.
  3. Add or subtract as with whole numbers. Perform the operation column by column, starting from the rightmost digit, just as you would with whole numbers. Remember to carry over digits in addition or borrow in subtraction as needed.
  4. Place the decimal point in the answer directly below the other decimal points. The decimal point in your final result will maintain its aligned position.

Working with Whole Numbers and Decimals

If you need to add or subtract a whole number with a decimal, remember that a whole number can always be written with a decimal point after it, followed by as many zeros as you need. For example, the number $12$ can be written as $12.$ or $12.0$ or $12.00$ to match the number of decimal places in another number.

Examples: Putting the Steps into Practice

Example 1: Adding Decimals

Problem: Find $3.45 + 12.7$.

Solution:

  1. Line up decimal points:
    3.45
+ 12.7
  2. Annex zeros: $12.7$ becomes $12.70$.
    3.45
+ 12.70
-------
  3. Add like whole numbers:
    • Rightmost column (hundredths): $5 + 0 = 5$
    • Next column (tenths): $4 + 7 = 11$. Write down $1$ and carry over $1$ to the ones place.
    • Next column (ones): $3 + 2 + (\text{carried } 1) = 6$
    • Leftmost column (tens): Bring down $1$
  4. Place decimal point: The decimal point goes straight down.
      3.45
+ 12.70
-------
  16.15
    So, $3.45 + 12.7 = 16.15$.

Example 2: Subtracting Decimals with Borrowing

Problem: Find $20.3 - 8.47$.

Solution:

  1. Line up decimal points:
    20.3
- 8.47
  2. Annex zeros: $20.3$ becomes $20.30$.
    20.30
-  8.47
--------
  3. Subtract like whole numbers (with borrowing):
    • Hundredths column: $0 - 7$. You need to borrow. Borrow $1$ from the $3$ in the tenths place, making it $2$. The $0$ becomes $10$. So, $10 - 7 = 3$.
    • Tenths column: $2 - 4$. You need to borrow again. Borrow $1$ from the $0$ in the ones place, but the $0$ has nothing to give. So, borrow from the $2$ in the tens place. The $2$ becomes $1$, the $0$ becomes $10$. Now borrow $1$ from the $10$ in the ones place, making it $9$. The $2$ in the tenths place becomes $12$. So, $12 - 4 = 8$.
    • Ones column: $9 - 8 = 1$.
    • Tens column: Bring down the remaining $1$.
  4. Place decimal point:
      20.30
-  8.47
--------
  11.83
    So, $20.3 - 8.47 = 11.83$.

Example 3: Adding Multiple Decimals

Problem: Find $4.2 + 0.85 + 13.007$.

Solution:

  1. Line up decimal points:
      4.2
  0.85
+ 13.007
  2. Annex zeros (to match three decimal places for all numbers):
      4.200
  0.850
+ 13.007
--------
  3. Add like whole numbers column by column.
  4. Place decimal point:
      4.200
  0.850
+ 13.007
--------
  18.057
    So, $4.2 + 0.85 + 13.007 = 18.057$.

Example 4: Making Change — A Money Word Problem

Problem: Mia buys a notebook for $3.79 and a pen for $1.54. She pays with a $10 bill. How much change does she receive?

Solution: This problem requires two steps: first, find the total cost of her items, then subtract that total from the amount she paid.

  1. Find the total cost of items (addition):

      3.79  (notebook)
+ 1.54  (pen)
--------
  5.33

    The total cost is $5.33.

  2. Calculate the change (subtraction): Mia paid with a $10 bill. We can write $10$ as $10.00$ to line up with the total cost.

      10.00 (amount paid)
-  5.33 (total cost)
--------
   4.67

    Mia receives $4.67 in change.

Important Caution!

Always line up the decimal points, not the last digits. If you were to add $3.45 + 12.7$ by lining up the $5$ and $7$, you would incorrectly add hundredths to tenths, leading to a wrong answer.

Summary Checklist for Adding and Subtracting Decimals:

  • Line up the decimal points before adding or subtracting.
  • Annex zeros so all numbers have the same number of decimal places.
  • Add or subtract as with whole numbers; bring the decimal point straight down into the answer.
  • (Optional but recommended) Use estimation to quickly check that your answer is reasonable.

Review Questions

  2. What is the purpose of "annexing zeros" in decimal addition and subtraction? Provide an example where annexing zeros would be particularly helpful.
  3. Describe the steps you would take to solve a problem like $15 - 3.48$. How do you treat the whole number in this operation?
  4. Imagine you are at a grocery store. You buy items costing $2.55, $0.89, and $4.10. If you pay with a $20 bill, how much change should you expect? Show your work.
  5. A student calculated $7.2 + 1.85$ and got $8.35$. What common mistake did they likely make, and how would you explain the correct approach to them?
