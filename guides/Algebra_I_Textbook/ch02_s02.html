Study Guide: Multi-Step Equations

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the methods and strategies required to solve multi-step algebraic equations. By following structured procedures for simplification and isolation, complex equations can be reduced to manageable steps.

Core Concepts and Strategies

The General Strategy for Solving Multi-Step Equations

Solving multi-step equations involves a systematic approach to "undoing" operations in reverse order to isolate the variable. The process generally follows these four steps:

  1. Simplify Each Side: Apply the distributive property where necessary and combine like terms to reduce the complexity of the expression on both sides of the equal sign.
  2. Collect Variable Terms: Use addition or subtraction to move all terms containing the variable to one side of the equation.
  3. Collect Constant Terms: Use addition or subtraction to move all constant terms (numbers without variables) to the opposite side of the equation.
  4. Isolate the Variable: Use multiplication or division to solve for the variable.
  5. Verification: Always check the final answer by substituting the value back into the original equation to ensure both sides remain equal.

Special Procedures for Distribution

When an equation requires distribution, specifically when a negative number is involved, careful attention must be paid to signs.

  • Sign Caution: Distributing a negative number changes the sign of every term inside the parentheses. For example, in the expression $-3(x - 2)$, the result is $-3x + 6$ because the product of two negative numbers is positive.

Optional Techniques: Clearing Fractions and Decimals

While multi-step equations can be solved using fractions and decimals directly, "clearing" them often simplifies the arithmetic and reduces the likelihood of errors.

Technique Method Goal
Clearing Fractions Multiply every term on both sides of the equation by the Least Common Denominator (LCD). Produces an equivalent equation with integer coefficients.
Clearing Decimals Multiply every term on both sides by a power of 10 (e.g., 10 for tenths, 100 for hundredths). Converts decimal coefficients into integers.

Short-Answer Practice Quiz

Instructions: Solve the following equations using the strategies outlined in the text. Show your work for each step.

  1. Basic Multi-Step: Solve $5n - 3 = 27$.
  2. Inverse Operations: Solve $3x + 7 = 22$.
  3. Distribution: Solve $2(x - 4) + 3 = 11$.
  4. Clearing Fractions: What is the first step to solve $\frac{x}{2} + \frac{x}{3} = 10$, and what is the resulting equation?
  5. Clearing Decimals: Solve $0.2x + 0.5 = 1.3$ by first clearing the decimals.

Answer Key

  1. Add 3 to both sides ($5n = 30$); divide by 5. Answer: $n = 6$.
  2. Subtract 7 from both sides ($3x = 15$); divide by 3. Answer: $x = 5$.
  3. Distribute the 2 ($2x - 8 + 3 = 11$); combine terms ($2x - 5 = 11$); add 5 ($2x = 16$); divide by 2. Answer: $x = 8$.
  4. Multiply every term by the LCD, which is 6. Resulting Equation: $3x + 2x = 60$.
  5. Multiply by 10 ($2x + 5 = 13$); subtract 5 ($2x = 8$); divide by 2. Answer: $x = 4$.

  2. The Utility of Clearing Fractions and Decimals: The text notes that clearing fractions and decimals is an optional technique. Compare the potential benefits of clearing these values versus solving the equation in its original form. Under what circumstances might clearing be the preferred strategy?
  3. Common Pitfalls in Distribution: Analyze the mathematical risks associated with distributing negative numbers. Provide a hypothetical example of an error a student might make and explain the rule that prevents this mistake.

Glossary of Key Terms

  • Clearing Decimals: The process of multiplying an equation by a power of 10 (10, 100, 1000, etc.) to eliminate decimal points and work with integers.
  • Clearing Fractions: The process of multiplying every term in an equation by the Least Common Denominator (LCD) to eliminate denominators.
  • Combine Like Terms: The process of adding or subtracting terms that have the same variable and exponent to simplify an expression.
  • Distribute: To multiply a single term by each term inside a set of parentheses.
  • Integer Coefficients: Numbers (coefficients) in an equation that are whole numbers or their negatives, rather than fractions or decimals.
  • Inverse Operations: Opposite operations that "undo" each other, such as addition and subtraction or multiplication and division.
  • Isolate the Variable: The goal of solving an equation, where the variable is positioned alone on one side of the equal sign with a coefficient of 1.
  • Least Common Denominator (LCD): The smallest multiple shared by the denominators of two or more fractions.
  • Substitution: The process of replacing a variable with a specific value to check if that value makes the equation true.
