Study Guide: Factoring General Trinomials

This study guide focuses on the methodology for factoring trinomials of the form $ax^2 + bx + c$ where the leading coefficient ($a$) is not equal to 1. While these expressions require more steps than simpler trinomials, they follow a consistent logical process known as the $ac$ method.

Key Concepts and Methodology

Understanding the General Form

A general trinomial is expressed as $ax^2 + bx + c$. In this section, the focus is on instances where $a \neq 1$ and $a \neq 0$. The goal of factoring is to find a way to split the expression into two binomial factors.

The $ac$ Method

The $ac$ method is a systematic approach to factoring these trinomials. The process involves four primary steps:

  1. Compute the Product: Multiply the leading coefficient ($a$) by the constant term ($c$) to find the $ac$ product.
  2. Find the Factors: Identify two integers ($m$ and $n$) that satisfy two conditions:
    • Their product equals $ac$ ($m \cdot n = ac$).
    • Their sum equals the middle coefficient ($m + n = b$).
  3. Rewrite the Expression: Replace the middle term ($bx$) with the two terms $mx + nx$.
  4. Factor by Grouping: Divide the resulting four-term polynomial into two groups and factor out common binomials.

Prime Trinomials over Integers

If it is impossible to find a pair of integers $m$ and $n$ that satisfy both conditions ($m \cdot n = ac$ and $m + n = b$), the trinomial is considered prime over the integers. This means it cannot be factored using integer coefficients.

Strategic Pre-Step: The Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Before initiating the $ac$ method, it is essential to check for a Greatest Common Factor (GCF) across all terms. Factoring out the GCF first simplifies the remaining coefficients, making the $ac$ method easier to execute.

Glossary of Important Terms

  • $ac$ Method: A technique for factoring trinomials by finding two integers whose product is $ac$ and whose sum is $b$, then factoring by grouping.
  • Coefficient: The numerical factor of a term (e.g., $a$ and $b$ in $ax^2 + bx$).
  • Constant Term: The term in a polynomial that does not contain a variable (represented by $c$).
  • Factor by Grouping: A method of factoring polynomials with four terms by grouping them into pairs and factoring out the GCF of each pair.
  • Greatest Common Factor (GCF): The highest factor that is common to all terms in an expression.
  • Leading Coefficient: The coefficient of the term with the highest degree (represented by $a$).
  • Prime over the Integers: A polynomial that cannot be written as a product of polynomials with integer coefficients.
  • Trinomial: A polynomial consisting of three terms.
