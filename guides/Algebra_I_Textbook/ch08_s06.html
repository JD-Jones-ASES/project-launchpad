Study Guide: Applications of Quadratic Equations and Functions

This study guide focuses on the practical application of quadratic equations to real-world scenarios, specifically regarding projectile motion, area optimization, and revenue management. By understanding the properties of quadratic functions—particularly the vertex—one can solve complex problems involving maximum and minimum values.

Key Concepts and Formulas

1. The Role of the Vertex in Optimization

For any quadratic function modeled by $f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c$, the vertex is the critical point for finding optimal values:

  • Maximum Value: If $a < 0$, the parabola opens downward, and the vertex represents the maximum value.
  • Minimum Value: If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upward, and the vertex represents the minimum value.
  • Coordinates: The $x$-coordinate of the vertex ($x = \frac{-b}{2a}$) indicates where the optimal value occurs, while the $y$-coordinate ($f(x)$) indicates what the optimal value is.

2. Projectile Motion Model

The height of an object launched vertically is modeled by the function: $$h(t) = -16t^2 + v_0 t + h_0$$

Variable Description
$h(t)$ Height of the object at time $t$ (in feet)
$t$ Time elapsed since launch (in seconds)
$v_0$ Initial upward velocity (in feet per second)
$h_0$ Initial height from which the object is launched (in feet)
$-16$ Coefficient representing half the acceleration due to gravity ($32 \text{ ft/s}^2$)

3. Area and Revenue Optimization

  • Area Optimization: Often involves using a perimeter constraint to express area as a quadratic function of a single variable ($A = \text{length} \times \text{width}$).
  • Revenue Optimization: Revenue is calculated as $\text{Price} \times \text{Quantity}$. When price changes affect the quantity sold, the resulting revenue function is typically quadratic.

Short-Answer Practice Questions

1. Projectile Identification: In the height function $h(t) = -16t^2 + 32t + 48$, what is the initial velocity and the initial height of the object?

2. Finding the Peak: Using the function $h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t$, at what time $t$ does the object reach its maximum height, and what is that height?

3. Impact Time: A ball's height is modeled by $h(t) = -16t^2 + 32t + 48$. Show the steps to determine when the ball hits the ground.

4. Geometric Constraints: A rancher uses $120$ feet of fencing to create a rectangular pen against a barn wall. If $x$ represents the two sides perpendicular to the barn, write the equation for the side parallel to the barn ($y$).

5. Revenue Calculation: If a store sells $200$ shirts at $$15$ each, and every $$1$ increase in price ($x$) results in $10$ fewer sales, what is the binomial expression for the "Number sold per week"?

Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

1. The Physics of the Quadratic Model: Explain the components of the projectile motion formula $h(t) = -16t^2 + v_0 t + h_0$. Specifically, discuss why the leading coefficient is $-16$ and how the sign of the leading coefficient determines the physical path (trajectory) and the existence of a maximum height rather than a minimum height.

2. Interpreting Optimization in Business: Analyze the process of maximizing revenue using quadratic functions. How does a business use the $x$-coordinate and the $y$-coordinate of the vertex to make pricing decisions? In your explanation, distinguish between the "number of price increases" and the "final optimal price."

Glossary of Key Terms

  • $h_0$ (Initial Height): The starting vertical position of an object before it is launched.
  • $v_0$ (Initial Velocity): The starting upward speed of an object at $t = 0$.
  • Optimization: The process of finding the best possible solution, such as the maximum area or maximum revenue, within given constraints.
  • Projectile Motion: The motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the acceleration of gravity.
  • Revenue: The total income generated by the sale of goods or services, calculated as the product of price and quantity sold.
  • Vertex: The highest or lowest point on a parabola; in applications, it represents the point where the dependent variable is maximized or minimized.
  • Vertex Formula ($x = \frac{-b}{2a}$): The algebraic method used to find the $x$-value (or $t$-value) of the vertex in a quadratic function.
