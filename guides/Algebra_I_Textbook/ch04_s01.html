Study Guide: Fundamentals of the Coordinate Plane

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the coordinate plane, including its structure, the methods for plotting points, calculations for distance and midpoints, and a glossary of essential terms. This material serves as the foundation for algebraic graphing.

Key Concepts and Principles

The Structure of the Coordinate Plane

The coordinate plane is a two-dimensional surface formed by the intersection of two perpendicular number lines. This system allows every point on the surface to be described using a pair of numbers.

  • Axes: The horizontal number line is known as the $x$-axis, and the vertical number line is the $y$-axis.
  • The Origin: The point where the two axes intersect is called the origin, represented by the coordinates $(0, 0)$.
  • Quadrants: The axes divide the plane into four distinct regions called quadrants. These are numbered I, II, III, and IV, moving in a counter-clockwise direction starting from the upper right.

Ordered Pairs and Plotting

An ordered pair $(x, y)$ identifies the precise location of a point.

  • $x$-coordinate: The first number indicates the horizontal distance from the origin.
  • $y$-coordinate: The second number indicates the vertical distance from the origin.

To plot a point $(a, b)$:

  1. Start at the origin $(0, 0)$.
  2. Move $a$ units horizontally: Right if $a$ is positive ($a > 0$), or left if $a$ is negative ($a < 0$).
  3. Move $b$ units vertically: Up if $b$ is positive ($b > 0$), or down if $b$ is negative ($b < 0$).

The Midpoint Formula

The midpoint is the point located exactly halfway between the two endpoints of a line segment. To find the midpoint $M$ of a segment with endpoints $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, average the coordinates:

$$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right)$$

Short-Answer Practice Quiz

1. Identify the intersection point of the $x$-axis and the $y$-axis and provide its coordinates. Answer: The intersection is the origin, located at $(0, 0)$.

2. In which quadrant would a point with a negative $x$-coordinate and a positive $y$-coordinate be located? Answer: Quadrant II.

3. If a point has an $x$-coordinate of 0, where is it located on the coordinate plane? Answer: The point lies on the $y$-axis.

4. Calculate the distance between the points $(-3, 4)$ and $(-3, -5)$. Answer: Since the $x$-coordinates are the same, the points are on a vertical line. Distance = $|-5 - 4| = |-9| = 9$.

5. Find the midpoint of a segment with endpoints $(-2, 5)$ and $(6, 1)$. Answer: Using the midpoint formula: $(\frac{-2 + 6}{2}, \frac{5 + 1}{2}) = (\frac{4}{2}, \frac{6}{2}) = (2, 3)$.

6. If a point is located in Quadrant III, what are the signs of its $x$ and $y$ coordinates? Answer: Both coordinates are negative.

Glossary of Important Terms

  • Coordinate Plane: A two-dimensional surface formed by two perpendicular number lines (axes) intersecting at their zero points.
  • Distance (Horizontal/Vertical): The length between two points on the same axis line, calculated as the absolute value of the difference between their non-matching coordinates.
  • Midpoint: The point located exactly halfway between the endpoints of a segment.
  • Ordered Pair: A pair of numbers $(x, y)$ used to locate a point on the coordinate plane, where $x$ is the horizontal coordinate and $y$ is the vertical coordinate.
  • Origin: The center of the coordinate plane $(0, 0)$, where the $x$-axis and $y$-axis intersect.
  • Quadrant: One of the four regions of the coordinate plane divided by the axes, numbered I through IV counter-clockwise.
  • X-axis: The horizontal number line in the coordinate plane.
  • X-coordinate: The first number in an ordered pair, representing the horizontal distance from the origin.
  • Y-axis: The vertical number line in the coordinate plane.
  • Y-coordinate: The second number in an ordered pair, representing the vertical distance from the origin.
