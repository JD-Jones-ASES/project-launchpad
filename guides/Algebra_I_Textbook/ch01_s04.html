Study Guide: Adding and Subtracting Real Numbers

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the rules and applications for adding and subtracting signed real numbers. By utilizing the number line as a conceptual foundation, these properties allow for the systematic simplification of mathematical expressions involving both positive and negative values.

Key Concepts and Rules

Addition of Real Numbers

The process for adding signed numbers depends entirely on whether the signs of the addends are identical or different.

Scenario Rule Example
Adding Numbers with the Same Sign Add their absolute values and keep the common sign. $(-3) + (-5) = -8$
Adding Numbers with Different Signs Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value. Keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value. $(-7) + 4 = -3$

The Subtraction Rule

Subtraction is defined by its relationship to addition. To subtract a number, one must add its opposite. This is expressed through the formula: $a - b = a + (-b)$

Simplifying Complex Expressions

When faced with an expression containing multiple operations, the most effective method for simplification involves:

  1. Rewriting: Convert all subtraction operations into the addition of opposites.
  2. Grouping: Collect all positive numbers and all negative numbers together.
  3. Combining: Sum the grouped numbers and then apply the rule for adding numbers with different signs to reach the final result.

Example of Simplification: $-4 + 7 - 3 + (-2) - (-5)$

  • Step 1 (Rewrite): $-4 + 7 + (-3) + (-2) + 5$
  • Step 2 (Group): $(-4 + (-3) + (-2)) + (7 + 5)$
  • Step 3 (Combine): $-9 + 12 = 3$

Real-World Applications

Signed-number arithmetic is essential for calculating changes in various contexts:

  • Temperature: Measuring fluctuations, such as a drop of $8^\circ$ from an initial $3^\circ$ ($3 - 8 = -5^\circ$).
  • Elevation: Determining position relative to sea level. For instance, descending $200$ feet from an elevation of $150$ feet results in $-50$ feet (or $50$ feet below sea level).
  • Business: Calculating profit and loss to determine the financial standing of an enterprise.

Short-Answer Practice Quiz

Instructions: Simplify the following expressions using the rules for adding and subtracting real numbers.

  1. What is the result of $(-6) + (-11)$?
  2. Simplify the expression $15 + (-9)$.
  3. Calculate the sum of $(-4) + 10$.
  4. Apply the subtraction rule to solve $7 - 12$.
  5. What is the result of $-3 - 8$?
  6. Simplify the expression $-5 - (-9)$.
  7. Find the value of $(-8) + (-3)$.
  8. Solve the expression $-6 - (-14)$.

Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

  1. The Relationship Between Addition and Subtraction: Explain the conceptual and mathematical link between addition and subtraction within the set of real numbers. Why is it possible to treat every subtraction problem as an addition problem, and how does this simplify the process of evaluating complex expressions?
  2. The Role of Absolute Value in Signed Arithmetic: Discuss how the concept of absolute value serves as the foundation for the rules of adding and subtracting numbers with different signs. Why must we compare the "magnitude" (absolute value) of numbers rather than just their signed values when performing these operations?
  3. Practicality of Signed Numbers: Using the examples of temperature, elevation, or business, describe a scenario where failing to use signed numbers would make it difficult to communicate or calculate information accurately. How do negative numbers provide clarity in these contexts?

Glossary of Key Terms

  • Absolute Value: The distance of a number from zero on a number line, regardless of its sign.
  • Addends: The numbers being added together in an addition problem.
  • Different Signs: A term describing a pair of numbers where one is positive and the other is negative.
  • Opposite: The number that, when added to a given number, results in a sum of zero (e.g., the opposite of $b$ is $-b$).
  • Real Numbers: The set of numbers that includes all rational and irrational numbers, which can be represented on a continuous number line.
  • Same Sign: A term describing a pair of numbers that are either both positive or both negative.
  • Signed Numbers: Numbers that are explicitly identified as being positive or negative.
  • Subtraction Rule: The mathematical principle stating that subtracting a number is equivalent to adding its opposite.
