# Study Guide: The Pythagorean Theorem and Distance Formula

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the mathematical relationship between the sides of right triangles and the methods for calculating distance and midpoints within a coordinate plane.

---

### Key Concepts and Formulas

#### 1. The Pythagorean Theorem
The Pythagorean Theorem is a fundamental principle in geometry that relates the lengths of the three sides of a right triangle.

*   **The Formula:** In a right triangle with legs of lengths $a$ and $b$ and a hypotenuse of length $c$, the relationship is expressed as:
    $$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$
*   **The Hypotenuse:** This is always the side opposite the right angle and is the longest side of the triangle.
*   **The Converse:** The relationship also works in reverse. If the side lengths of a triangle satisfy the equation $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, then the triangle is confirmed to be a right triangle.

#### 2. The Distance Formula
The distance formula is used to find the length of a segment connecting any two points in the coordinate plane. It is derived directly from the Pythagorean Theorem by treating the distance between two points as the hypotenuse of a right triangle formed by horizontal and vertical segments.

*   **Derivation:** Given two points $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$:
    *   The horizontal leg length is $(x_2 - x_1)$.
    *   The vertical leg length is $(y_2 - y_1)$.
*   **The Formula:** The distance $d$ between points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is:
    $$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

#### 3. The Midpoint Formula
The midpoint of a segment is the point located exactly halfway between the two endpoints.

*   **The Formula:** The midpoint $M$ of the segment joining $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$ is calculated by averaging the x-coordinates and the y-coordinates:
    $$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right)$$

### Short-Answer Practice Questions

| Question | Answer |
| :--- | :--- |
| **1.** A right triangle has legs of 5 and 12. What is the length of the hypotenuse? | **13** (Calculated as $\sqrt{5^2 + 12^2} = \sqrt{169}$) |
| **2.** If a right triangle has a hypotenuse of 10 and one leg of 6, what is the length of the other leg? | **8** (Calculated as $\sqrt{10^2 - 6^2} = \sqrt{64}$) |
| **3.** What is the distance between the points $(-2, 3)$ and $(4, -1)$? | **$2\sqrt{13}$** (Calculated as $\sqrt{6^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{52}$) |
| **4.** Find the distance between $(1, 5)$ and $(7, -3)$. | **10** (Calculated as $\sqrt{6^2 + (-8)^2} = \sqrt{100}$) |
| **5.** What is the midpoint of the segment joining $(1, 5)$ and $(7, -3)$? | **$(4, 1)$** |
| **6.** If a triangle has sides $a, b,$ and $c$, and $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$, what type of triangle is it? | **A right triangle** |

### Glossary of Important Terms

| Term | Definition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Coordinate Plane** | A two-dimensional surface formed by the intersection of a horizontal x-axis and a vertical y-axis. |
| **Converse** | In mathematics, a theorem that reverses the hypothesis and conclusion of a known theorem (e.g., using $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ to prove a triangle is a right triangle). |
| **Hypotenuse** | The longest side of a right triangle, located directly opposite the right angle. |
| **Legs** | The two shorter sides of a right triangle that meet to form the right angle. |
| **Midpoint** | The point on a line segment that is equidistant from both endpoints. |
| **Pythagorean Theorem** | The geometric principle stating that the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$). |
| **Right Triangle** | A triangle that contains one $90$-degree angle. |
