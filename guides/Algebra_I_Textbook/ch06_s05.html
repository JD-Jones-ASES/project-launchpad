Study Guide: Multiplying Polynomials

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of multiplying polynomials, ranging from simple monomials to multi-term expressions. The material covers foundational properties, specific techniques such as FOIL, and the rules governing the degree of the resulting products.

I. Fundamental Concepts

The Distributive Property

The primary technique for multiplying polynomials is the distributive property. Unlike polynomial addition or subtraction, which focuses on combining like terms, multiplication requires each term of the first polynomial to be multiplied by every term of the second polynomial. Once the multiplication is complete, the resulting terms are simplified by combining like terms.

Closure and Degrees

Two critical rules govern the results of polynomial multiplication:

  1. Closure: Polynomials are closed under multiplication. This means that the product of any two polynomials will always result in another polynomial.
  2. Sum of Degrees: The degree of the resulting product is equal to the sum of the degrees of the individual factors.
    • Example: A degree-2 polynomial multiplied by a degree-3 polynomial will always result in a degree-5 polynomial.

II. Multiplication Techniques

1. Monomial and Polynomial

To multiply a monomial by a polynomial, the monomial must be distributed to every term within the polynomial.

Mathematical Rule: For any monomial $m$ and polynomial $p_1 + p_2 + \dots + p_k$: $$m(p_1 + p_2 + \dots + p_k) = mp_1 + mp_2 + \dots + mp_k$$

Example:

  • Expression: $-3x^2(4x^3 - 2x + 5)$
  • Distribution: $(-3x^2)(4x^3) + (-3x^2)(-2x) + (-3x^2)(5)$
  • Result: $-12x^5 + 6x^3 - 15x^2$

2. Two Binomials (FOIL Method)

When multiplying two binomials, the distributive property is applied twice. The mnemonic FOIL serves as a guide for these four specific multiplications:

Letter Meaning Description
F First Multiply the first terms of each binomial.
O Outer Multiply the outermost terms of the expression.
I Inner Multiply the innermost terms of the expression.
L Last Multiply the last terms of each binomial.

Example:

  • Expression: $(2x + 3)(x - 5)$
  • FOIL Process: $(2x)(x) + (2x)(-5) + (3)(x) + (3)(-5)$
  • Simplification: $2x^2 - 10x + 3x - 15$
  • Final Product: $2x^2 - 7x - 15$

3. Larger Polynomials

The FOIL method is restricted to binomials. For larger polynomials, such as a binomial multiplied by a trinomial, one must distribute each term of the first polynomial to every term of the second.

Example:

  • Expression: $(x + 3)(x^2 - 2x + 4)$
  • Distribution: $x(x^2 - 2x + 4) + 3(x^2 - 2x + 4)$
  • Expansion: $x^3 - 2x^2 + 4x + 3x^2 - 6x + 12$
  • Final Product: $x^3 + x^2 - 2x + 12$

III. Short-Answer Practice Quiz

  1. True or False: Polynomials are closed under multiplication.
  2. Degree Calculation: If a degree-4 polynomial is multiplied by a degree-2 polynomial, what is the degree of the product?
  3. Monomial Distribution: Simplify $-3x^2(4x^3 - 2x + 5)$.
  4. FOIL Application: Multiply $(x + 4)(x - 6)$.
  5. FOIL Application: Multiply $(2x + 3)(x - 5)$.
  6. Multi-term Distribution: Multiply $(2x - 1)(x^2 + 3x - 2)$.
  7. Conceptual Step: What is the final step required after distributing terms in polynomial multiplication?
  8. Mnemonic Definition: In the FOIL method, what do the "O" and "I" represent?

IV. Essay Prompts for Deeper Exploration

  1. The Limits of FOIL: Explain why the FOIL method is considered a specialized version of the distributive property. In your discussion, explain why FOIL cannot be used to multiply a binomial by a trinomial and describe the general method that must be used instead.
  2. The Relationship Between Factor Degrees and Product Degrees: Discuss the rule regarding the degree of a product of polynomials. Why is it mathematically significant that the degree of the product equals the sum of the degrees of the factors? Provide an example involving a monomial and a trinomial to illustrate your point.
  3. Procedural Comparison: Contrast the processes of adding polynomials and multiplying polynomials. Focus on the use of the distributive property and the rules for combining like terms in each operation.

V. Glossary of Important Terms

Term Definition
Binomial A polynomial consisting of exactly two terms.
Closure A property stating that an operation on members of a set (such as polynomials) always results in a member of that same set.
Distributive Property The process of multiplying each term of one polynomial by every term of another polynomial.
FOIL A mnemonic for multiplying two binomials: First, Outer, Inner, Last.
Like Terms Terms within a polynomial that have the same variables raised to the same powers; these are combined after the distribution phase of multiplication.
Monomial A polynomial consisting of a single term.
Polynomial Degree The highest power of the variable in the polynomial; in multiplication, the product's degree is the sum of the degrees of the factors.
Trinomial A polynomial consisting of three terms.
