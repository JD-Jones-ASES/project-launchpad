# Study Guide: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

This study guide explores the geometric and algebraic relationships between lines in a coordinate plane, specifically focusing on how to identify and construct equations for parallel and perpendicular lines using their slopes.

## Key Concepts and Properties

In a two-dimensional plane, lines that do not occupy the same space will either cross at exactly one point or never cross at all. These relationships are defined by their intersections and their slopes.

### 1. Parallel Lines
Lines that exist in the same plane but never intersect are defined as **parallel**. 
*   **The Slope Property:** Two non-vertical lines are parallel if and only if they have the same slope ($m_1 = m_2$).
*   **Vertical Lines:** All vertical lines are considered parallel to one another.

### 2. Perpendicular Lines
Lines that intersect specifically at right angles (90 degrees) are defined as **perpendicular**.
*   **The Slope Property:** Two non-vertical lines are perpendicular if and only if their slopes are negative reciprocals of each other. Algebraically, the product of their slopes must be $-1$:
    *   $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$
    *   $m_2 = \frac{-1}{m_1}$
*   **Horizontal and Vertical Lines:** A horizontal line and a vertical line are always perpendicular to each other.

---

## Classification of Line Pairs

To determine the relationship between two lines, compare their slopes ($m$).

| Relationship | Slope Comparison | Algebraic Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Parallel** | Slopes are identical | $m_1 = m_2$ |
| **Perpendicular** | Slopes are negative reciprocals | $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$ |
| **Neither** | Slopes are not equal and not negative reciprocals | $m_1 \neq m_2$ and $m_1 \cdot m_2 \neq -1$ |

## Short-Answer Practice Quiz

**Instructions:** Use the principles of slopes to answer the following questions based on the provided data.

1.  **Identify Relationship:** If Line A has the equation $y = 2x + 5$ and Line B has the equation $y = 2x - 3$, what is their relationship?
2.  **Identify Relationship:** If Line A has a slope of $3$ and Line B has a slope of $-13$, and the product of their slopes is calculated as $-1$, how are these lines classified?
3.  **Identify Relationship:** Lines with slopes of $2$ and $-2$ are compared. Are they parallel, perpendicular, or neither?
4.  **Equation Construction (Parallel):** What is the slope-intercept form of a line that is parallel to $y = -3x + 2$ and passes through the point $(4, 1)$?
5.  **Equation Construction (Perpendicular):** What is the equation of a line perpendicular to $y = 25x - 1$ passing through $(2, -3)$? (Note: Use the negative reciprocal slope $-52$).
6.  **Checkpoint Calculation:** Find the equation of the line parallel to $y = 12x + 3$ that passes through the point $(-4, 5)$.

---

## Answer Key

1.  **Parallel.** Both lines have an identical slope of $m = 2$.
2.  **Perpendicular.** Because the product of the slopes $m_1 \cdot m_2 = -1$.
3.  **Neither.** The slopes are not equal (not parallel) and their product ($2 \cdot -2 = -4$) is not $-1$ (not perpendicular).
4.  **$y = -3x + 13$.** (Calculated using point-slope form: $y - 1 = -3(x - 4)$).
5.  **$y = -52x + 2$.** (Calculated using the negative reciprocal slope $-52$ and point $(2, -3)$).
6.  **$y = 12x + 7$.** (Using slope $12$ and point $(-4, 5)$: $y - 5 = 12(x + 4)$).

## Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Negative Reciprocal:** A value that, when multiplied by a given number, results in a product of $-1$. In coordinate geometry, these represent the slopes of perpendicular lines.
*   **Parallel:** Lines in the same plane that maintain a constant distance and never meet; they possess identical slopes.
*   **Perpendicular:** Lines that cross at a 90-degree (right) angle; their slopes are negative reciprocals of one another.
*   **Point-Slope Form:** An algebraic format (derived from the slope formula) used to find the equation of a line when one point and the slope are known.
*   **Slope ($m$):** A measure of the steepness and direction of a line.
*   **Slope-Intercept Form:** The equation of a straight line in the form $y = mx + b$, where $m$ is the slope and $b$ is the y-intercept.
