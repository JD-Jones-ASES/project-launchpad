# Study Guide: Factoring Polynomials Completely and Solving Equations

This study guide provides a comprehensive overview of the strategies and properties required to factor polynomials fully and use those factors to solve algebraic equations. The material is based on the principles of identifying prime factors and applying the Zero Product Property.

---

## I. Core Concepts in Factoring

### Factoring Completely
A polynomial is considered **factored completely** when every resulting factor is **prime**. A prime factor is one that cannot be factored further using integers. The process of factoring completely often requires multiple steps and the application of different techniques in a specific sequence.

### The Systematic Factoring Strategy
To ensure a polynomial is factored thoroughly, the following hierarchical strategy should be applied:

| Step | Action | Details |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1** | **Factor out the GCF** | Always identify the Greatest Common Factor first. If the leading coefficient is negative, factor out a negative GCF. |
| **2** | **Count the Terms** | The number of terms determines the next technique to use: |
| | *Two Terms* | Check for a **Difference of Squares** ($a^2 - b^2$). Note: A **Sum of Squares** ($a^2 + b^2$) does not factor. |
| | *Three Terms* | Factor as a trinomial. Check for a perfect square trinomial or use standard trinomial factoring. |
| | *Four+ Terms* | Attempt **factoring by grouping**. |
| **3** | **Factor Again** | Examine every resulting factor to see if it can be factored further. |
| **4** | **Check** | Multiply all final factors together to verify they recover the original polynomial. |

## II. Solving Equations via Factoring

Factoring serves as a primary tool for solving equations. This is made possible by a specific mathematical property regarding products that equal zero.

### The Zero Product Property
The **Zero Product Property** states that if the product of two or more factors is zero, then at least one of those factors must be zero.
*   **Property:** If $A \cdot B = 0$, then $A = 0$ or $B = 0$ (or both).

### Application in Problem Solving
By factoring a polynomial equation set to zero, you can isolate the individual factors and solve for the variable by setting each factor to zero independently. This converts a complex polynomial equation into simpler linear equations.

## III. Short-Answer Practice Questions

**1. What is the definition of a polynomial that is "factored completely"?**
*Answer:* A polynomial is factored completely when every factor is prime, meaning it cannot be factored any further using integers.

**2. According to the factoring strategy, what is the very first step you should take regardless of the number of terms?**
*Answer:* You must first factor out the Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

**3. If you have a binomial in the form $a^2 + b^2$, can it be factored?**
*Answer:* No. While a difference of squares ($a^2 - b^2$) can be factored, a sum of squares ($a^2 + b^2$) does not factor.

**4. When factoring out a GCF, what special rule applies to the leading coefficient?**
*Answer:* If the leading coefficient of the polynomial is negative, you should factor out a negative GCF.

**5. Factor the expression $2x^3 - 50x$ completely.**
*Answer:* $2x(x + 5)(x - 5)$. (First factor out the GCF $2x$ to get $2x(x^2 - 25)$, then factor the difference of squares).

**6. Solve the equation $x^2 - 5x - 14 = 0$ using the Zero Product Property.**
*Answer:* $x = 7$ or $x = -2$. (Factors are $(x - 7)(x + 2) = 0$).

**7. Solve the equation $5x^2 - 20 = 0$.**
*Answer:* $x = 2$ or $x = -2$. (Factored form is $5(x + 2)(x - 2) = 0$).

## V. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Difference of Squares:** A specific binomial pattern ($a^2 - b^2$) that factors into $(a + b)(a - b)$.
*   **Factoring by Grouping:** A technique used for polynomials with four or more terms where terms are grouped to find common factors within the sub-groups.
*   **Greatest Common Factor (GCF):** The highest expression that divides exactly into all terms of a polynomial.
*   **Prime Factor:** A factor that cannot be broken down into further factors using integer coefficients.
*   **Trinomial:** A polynomial consisting of three terms.
*   **Zero Product Property:** A property stating that if the product of multiple factors is zero, then at least one factor must be equal to zero.
