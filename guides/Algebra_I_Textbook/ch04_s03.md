# Study Guide: Slope and Linear Relationships

This study guide explores the fundamental algebraic concept of slope as presented in Chapter 4, Section 3. Slope is a critical numerical value used to measure the steepness and direction of a line, representing how fast $y$-values change in relation to $x$-values.

---

## I. Key Concepts and Formulas

### Definition of Slope
Slope is the measure of the steepness of a non-vertical line. It is defined as the ratio of the vertical change (the "rise") to the horizontal change (the "run") between any two distinct points on a line. In mathematical notation, slope is commonly represented by the letter $m$.

$$\text{Slope } (m) = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{\text{change in } y}{\text{change in } x}$$

### The Slope Formula
To find the slope of a line passing through two specific points, $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, use the following formula:

$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}, \text{ where } x_1 \neq x_2$$

**Important Note on Order:** The result remains the same regardless of which point is designated as $(x_1, y_1)$ or $(x_2, y_2)$, provided that the coordinates are subtracted in the same order in both the numerator and the denominator.

### Slope as a Rate of Change
Beyond geometry, slope serves as a rate of change in real-world applications. It describes how one quantity changes relative to another. In these contexts, slope often carries specific units such as:
*   Dollars per hour
*   Miles per gallon
*   Degrees per minute
*   Inches per hour (e.g., the rate at which a candle burns)

## II. The Four Types of Slope

The value of $m$ reveals the orientation of the line on a coordinate plane:

| Slope Type | Visual Description | Mathematical Characteristic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Positive Slope** | The line rises as it moves from left to right. | $m > 0$ |
| **Negative Slope** | The line falls as it moves from left to right. | $m < 0$ |
| **Zero Slope** | The line is horizontal (flat). | $m = 0$ (The numerator is 0) |
| **Undefined Slope** | The line is vertical. | The denominator is 0 (Division by zero) |

**Caution:** It is essential to distinguish between zero slope and undefined slope. A horizontal line has a slope of $0$ because there is no "rise." A vertical line has an undefined slope because there is no "run," and division by zero is mathematically undefined.

## III. Short-Answer Practice Questions

**1. Calculate the slope of the line passing through the points $(1, 3)$ and $(4, 9)$.**
*Answer:* $m = \frac{9 - 3}{4 - 1} = \frac{6}{3} = 2$.

**2. What is the slope of a line passing through $(2, 5)$ and $(7, 5)$? Identify the type of line.**
*Answer:* $m = \frac{5 - 5}{7 - 2} = \frac{0}{5} = 0$. This is a horizontal line.

**3. Determine the slope for the points $(3, -1)$ and $(3, 4)$. Why is it described this way?**
*Answer:* $m = \frac{4 - (-1)}{3 - 3} = \frac{5}{0}$. The slope is undefined because the horizontal change (run) is zero, and division by zero is not possible.

**4. If a 12-inch candle burns down to 6 inches over 3 hours, what is the slope of the line representing its height over time?**
*Answer:* Using points $(0, 12)$ and $(3, 6)$, $m = \frac{6 - 12}{3 - 0} = \frac{-6}{3} = -2$.

**5. Find the slope of the line through $(-2, 7)$ and $(4, -5)$.**
*Answer:* $m = \frac{-5 - 7}{4 - (-2)} = \frac{-12}{6} = -2$.

## V. Glossary of Important Terms

*   **Change in $x$ (Run):** The horizontal distance between two points on a line.
*   **Change in $y$ (Rise):** The vertical distance between two points on a line.
*   **Horizontal Line:** A flat line with a slope of $0$, where all points share the same $y$-coordinate.
*   **Linear Relationship:** A relationship between two variables that, when graphed, forms a straight line.
*   **$m$:** The standard algebraic symbol used to denote the slope of a line.
*   **Rate of Change:** A comparison of how much one quantity changes relative to another; in a linear context, this is the slope.
*   **Slope:** The ratio of the rise to the run; a measure of the steepness of a line.
*   **Undefined Slope:** The slope of a vertical line where the horizontal change ($x_2 - x_1$) is zero.
*   **Vertical Line:** A line that goes straight up and down, possessing an undefined slope because all points share the same $x$-coordinate.
