Study Guide: Factoring Trinomials with Leading Coefficient 1

This study guide focuses on the algebraic process of factoring trinomials of the form $x^2 + bx + c$. Factoring is the inverse of multiplying binomials; while multiplication combines factors to create a product, factoring breaks a trinomial down into the two binomials that produced it.

Key Concepts and Methodology

The Factoring Property

To factor a trinomial where the leading coefficient is 1 (the coefficient of $x^2$ is an invisible 1), the goal is to identify two integers, $p$ and $q$, that satisfy two specific conditions:

  1. Sum Condition: $p + q = b$ (the coefficient of the middle term).
  2. Product Condition: $p \cdot q = c$ (the constant term).

If these integers exist, the trinomial $x^2 + bx + c$ can be written as the product of two binomials: $(x + p)(x + q)$.

Sign Analysis for Factoring

The signs of the integers $p$ and $q$ are determined by the signs of the middle term ($b$) and the constant term ($c$). Understanding these patterns streamlines the process of finding the correct factors.

Sign of $c$ (Constant) Sign of $b$ (Middle Term) Signs of $p$ and $q$
Positive (+) Positive (+) Both $p$ and $q$ are positive.
Positive (+) Negative (-) Both $p$ and $q$ are negative.
Negative (-) Positive (+) $p$ and $q$ have different signs; the positive number has the larger absolute value.
Negative (-) Negative (-) $p$ and $q$ have different signs; the negative number has the larger absolute value.

Prime Trinomials

It is important to note that not every trinomial can be factored using integers. If there is no pair of integers that satisfies both the sum and product conditions, the trinomial is considered prime (or irreducible over the integers).

  • Example: $x^2 + 3x + 5$ is prime because no two integers multiply to 5 and add to 3.

Verification via FOIL

To ensure accuracy, always check the factored result by multiplying the binomials using the FOIL (First, Outer, Inner, Last) method. The resulting expansion must exactly match the original trinomial.

Short-Answer Practice Quiz

Instructions: Factor the following trinomials into two binomials. If a trinomial cannot be factored, state "Prime."

  1. Factor: $x^2 + 7x + 12$
  2. Factor: $x^2 - 9x + 20$
  3. Factor: $x^2 + 11x + 24$
  4. Factor: $x^2 + 2x - 15$
  5. Factor: $x^2 - 3x - 18$
  6. Factor: $x^2 + 3x + 5$

Answer Key

  1. $(x + 3)(x + 4)$ (Factors of 12 that add to 7: 3 and 4)
  2. $(x - 4)(x - 5)$ (Factors of 20 that add to -9: -4 and -5)
  3. $(x + 3)(x + 8)$ (Factors of 24 that add to 11: 3 and 8)
  4. $(x - 3)(x + 5)$ (Factors of -15 that add to 2: -3 and 5)
  5. $(x - 6)(x + 3)$ (Factors of -18 that add to -3: -6 and 3)
  6. Prime (No integer factors of 5 add to 3)

Essay Questions for Deeper Exploration

  1. The Relationship Between Multiplication and Factoring: Explain how the process of factoring $x^2 + bx + c$ relates to the FOIL method of multiplication. Describe why the constant term $c$ is the product of $p$ and $q$, while the middle coefficient $b$ is the sum of $p$ and $q$. Use the expansion of $(x + p)(x + q)$ to support your explanation.

  2. Predicting Factor Signs: Describe the logical reasoning used to determine the signs of $p$ and $q$ when the constant term $c$ is negative. Why must the factors have different signs in this scenario, and how does the sign of the middle term $b$ dictate which factor has the larger absolute value?

  3. The Concept of Irreducibility: Define what it means for a trinomial to be "prime" or "irreducible over the integers." Provide an example of a prime trinomial other than $x^2 + 3x + 5$ and explain why it cannot be factored using the integer method.

Glossary of Important Terms

  • Binomial: An algebraic expression consisting of two terms, such as $(x + 3)$.
  • Coefficient: A numerical or constant quantity placed before and multiplying the variable in an algebraic expression (e.g., in $8x$, 8 is the coefficient).
  • Constant Term: The term in a polynomial that does not contain a variable (the $c$ in $x^2 + bx + c$).
  • Factoring: The process of breaking down a mathematical expression into a product of simpler expressions.
  • FOIL Method: A mnemonic for multiplying two binomials: First, Outer, Inner, Last.
  • Leading Coefficient: The coefficient of the term with the highest exponent. In this section, the leading coefficient of $x^2$ is 1.
  • Prime (Irreducible): A polynomial that cannot be factored into polynomials of lower degree with integer coefficients.
  • Trinomial: An algebraic expression consisting of three terms, such as $x^2 + 8x + 15$.
