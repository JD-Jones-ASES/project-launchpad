Chapter 5, Section 1: The Math: Working with Time and Rates Study Guide

Overview

This section introduces the fundamental mathematical skills required to calculate various forms of employee compensation. It covers how to convert time into decimal hours, compute gross pay for hourly, salaried, commission-based, and piecework employees, and finally, how to determine net pay by subtracting common payroll deductions. The goal is to equip learners with the tools to understand how wages are calculated before any money is taken out, and what remains afterwards.

Key Concepts

  • Decimal Hours: A standardized way to express time for calculation, converting minutes into a decimal fraction of an hour (minutes / 60).
  • Gross Pay: The total amount of money an employee earns before any deductions are subtracted.
  • Overtime Pay: Federal law mandates that most hourly workers receive 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for any hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek.
  • Salary: A fixed amount of pay for a set period (typically annually), divided across regular pay periods regardless of specific hours worked.
  • Commission: Pay based directly on the volume of sales an individual generates.
  • Graduated Commission: A commission structure where different rates apply to different levels (tiers) of sales, often increasing the rate as sales volume grows.
  • Piecework: Pay based on the number of units produced or tasks completed by an employee.
  • Payroll Deductions: Mandatory or voluntary amounts subtracted from gross pay, such as taxes (FICA, federal, state), health insurance, or retirement contributions.
  • Net Pay: The final amount of money an employee receives after all deductions have been subtracted from their gross pay, also known as "take-home pay."

Important Terms

Decimal Hours : Time expressed as a decimal number, converting minutes into fractions of an hour (e.g., 30 minutes = 0.50 hours).

Gross Pay : The total earnings of an employee before any deductions are taken out.

Overtime Pay : Payment at a rate of 1.5 times the regular hourly rate for hours worked beyond 40 in a standard workweek, as mandated by federal law for most hourly workers.

Salary : A fixed amount of compensation paid to an employee over a specific period (e.g., annually), which is then divided into equal payments for each pay period.

Commission : Compensation calculated as a percentage of the total sales an employee makes.

Graduated Commission : A commission structure where the percentage rate applied to sales increases as the total sales reach higher specified tiers or thresholds.

Piecework : A compensation method where an employee is paid a set amount for each unit of work they produce or task they complete.

Net Pay : The amount of money an employee takes home after all mandatory and voluntary deductions have been subtracted from their gross pay. Also referred to as "take-home pay."

FICA : An acronym for the Federal Insurance Contributions Act, which funds Social Security and Medicare through payroll taxes. The rate is typically 7.65% of gross pay for employees.

Detailed Notes

The Basics of Payroll Math

Payroll calculations fundamentally involve multiplying a rate by a quantity to determine earnings, and then subtracting various deductions. This section lays the groundwork for understanding how these calculations are performed for different payment structures.

Working with Time

Converting Minutes to Decimal Hours Many payroll systems require time to be expressed in decimal hours for accurate calculations. To convert minutes to a decimal:

  • Divide the number of minutes by 60 (since there are 60 minutes in an hour).
  • Add this decimal value to the full hours.

Formula: Decimal hours = hours + (minutes / 60)

Examples:

  • 7 hours 30 minutes = 7 + (30 / 60) = 7 + 0.50 = 7.50 hours
  • 6 hours 45 minutes = 6 + (45 / 60) = 6 + 0.75 = 6.75 hours
  • 8 hours 10 minutes = 8 + (10 / 60) = 8 + 0.166... ≈ 8.17 hours (rounded to the nearest hundredth)

Common Conversions to Remember:

  • 15 min = 0.25 hr
  • 30 min = 0.50 hr
  • 45 min = 0.75 hr
  • 20 min ≈ 0.33 hr
  • 10 min ≈ 0.17 hr

Adding Time from a Time Card When calculating total hours from a time card, first determine the hours worked for each day, convert them to decimal hours, and then sum them up for the week.

  • Example: If someone works from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM, that is 8 hours and 30 minutes, or 8.50 decimal hours.
  • To find total hours for a week, sum the daily decimal hours.

Gross Pay Calculations

1. Hourly Pay For hourly workers, gross pay is calculated by multiplying the number of hours worked by the hourly rate.

Formula: Gross pay = Hours worked × Hourly rate

Overtime Pay:

  • Most hourly workers are entitled to overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular rate for hours worked over 40 in a single workweek.

  • To calculate gross pay with overtime:

    1. Calculate regular pay: 40 hours × Regular rate.
    2. Calculate overtime hours: Total hours worked - 40.
    3. Calculate overtime rate: 1.5 × Regular rate.
    4. Calculate overtime pay: Overtime hours × Overtime rate.
    5. Add regular pay and overtime pay for total gross pay.

    Formula: Gross pay = (40 × Regular rate) + (Overtime hours × 1.5 × Regular rate)

  • Example: Jasmine earns $18.00/hour and worked 46 hours.

    • Regular pay: 40 hours × $18.00/hr = $720.00
    • Overtime hours: 46 - 40 = 6 hours
    • Overtime rate: 1.5 × $18.00/hr = $27.00/hr
    • Overtime pay: 6 hours × $27.00/hr = $162.00
    • Total Gross Pay: $720.00 + $162.00 = $882.00

2. Salary A salary is a fixed annual amount. To find the pay per period, divide the annual salary by the number of pay periods in a year.

Formula: Pay per period = Annual salary / Number of pay periods per year

Common Pay Periods:

  • Weekly: 52 pay periods per year

  • Biweekly: 26 pay periods per year (every two weeks)

  • Semimonthly: 24 pay periods per year (twice a month, e.g., 1st and 15th)

  • Monthly: 12 pay periods per year

  • Example: Tran earns an annual salary of $47,000.00.

    • Monthly pay: $47,000.00 / 12 ≈ $3,916.67
    • Biweekly pay: $47,000.00 / 26 ≈ $1,807.69

3. Commission Commission pay is based on a percentage of sales.

Formula: Commission = Sales × Commission rate (as a decimal)

Graduated Commission: This system uses different commission rates for different sales levels (tiers).

  • Calculate commission for each tier separately.

  • Sum the commissions from all tiers to get the total.

  • Example (Straight Commission): Maria sells $85,000.00 at a 4% commission.

    • Commission = $85,000.00 × 0.04 = $3,400.00

  • Example (Graduated Commission): Derek's sales are $32,000.00 with tiers:

    • 5% on first $10,000
    • 8% on sales from $10,000.01 to $25,000
    • 11% on sales above $25,000
    • Tier 1: $10,000 × 0.05 = $500.00
    • Tier 2: ($25,000 - $10,000) × 0.08 = $15,000 × 0.08 = $1,200.00
    • Tier 3: ($32,000 - $25,000) × 0.11 = $7,000 × 0.11 = $770.00
    • Total Commission: $500.00 + $1,200.00 + $770.00 = $2,470.00

4. Piecework Piecework pay is determined by the number of units produced and the rate per unit.

Formula: Piecework pay = Number of units × Rate per unit

  • Example: Angela assembles 215 boards at $2.75 per board.
    • Gross Pay = 215 × $2.75 = $591.25

Payroll Deductions and Net Pay

Net Pay Net pay is what's left after all deductions are subtracted from gross pay.

Formula: Net pay = Gross pay - Total deductions

Common Payroll Deductions:

  • FICA (Social Security + Medicare): Typically 7.65% of gross pay.
  • Federal Income Tax: Varies significantly based on income, filing status, and allowances. A given percentage (e.g., 12%) will be provided for problems.
  • State Income Tax: Varies by state; a given percentage (e.g., 3-5%) will be provided.
  • Other Deductions: Health insurance premiums, retirement contributions, union dues, etc. These can be fixed dollar amounts or percentages.

Important Caution: When calculating percentage-based deductions, always apply the percentage to the full gross pay, not to the amount remaining after other deductions. Each deduction is calculated independently from the gross amount.

  • Example: Kevin's gross pay is $2,400.00. Deductions: FICA 7.65%, Federal tax 12%, State tax 4%, Health insurance $85.00.
    • FICA: $2,400.00 × 0.0765 = $183.60
    • Federal tax: $2,400.00 × 0.12 = $288.00
    • State tax: $2,400.00 × 0.04 = $96.00
    • Health insurance: $85.00
    • Total Deductions: $183.60 + $288.00 + $96.00 + $85.00 = $652.60
    • Net Pay: $2,400.00 - $652.60 = $1,747.40

Review Questions

  1. Time Conversion: A shift supervisor worked from 6:45 AM to 3:15 PM, with a 30-minute unpaid lunch break. How many decimal hours did the supervisor work that day?
  2. Hourly Pay with Overtime: Sarah earns $25.00 per hour. Last week she worked 48 hours. Calculate her total gross pay, assuming overtime is paid at 1.5 times the regular rate for hours over 40.
  3. Salary Pay Period: Robert has an annual salary of $62,000.00. If he is paid semimonthly, what is his gross pay per pay period?
  4. Graduated Commission: Emily works on a graduated commission structure: 7% on the first $20,000 in sales, and 10% on sales above $20,000. If her total sales for the month were $35,000, what is her total commission?
  5. Net Pay Calculation: David's gross pay for the week is $1,500.00. His deductions include FICA at 7.65%, federal income tax at 15%, state income tax at 5%, and a retirement contribution of $50.00. Calculate his total net pay.
