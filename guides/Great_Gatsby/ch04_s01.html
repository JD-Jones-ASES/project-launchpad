Study Guide: Chapter 3: The Novel

Key Concepts

  • Nick receives a rare formal invitation to one of Gatsby's Saturday night parties — most guests simply arrive uninvited
  • The party is an elaborate spectacle: orchestras, hundreds of guests, crates of lemons and oranges, elaborate food, and colored lights transforming the garden
  • Guests trade wild, contradictory rumors about Gatsby: that he killed a man, that he was a German spy, that he is a nephew of Kaiser Wilhelm
  • The owl-eyed man in the library is astonished to find Gatsby's books are real, comparing Gatsby to the theatrical producer David Belasco
  • Nick finally meets Gatsby without recognizing him; Gatsby's most distinctive feature is his extraordinary, reassuring smile and his formal phrase "old sport"
  • Gatsby summons Jordan Baker for a private conversation; its contents are not revealed, only that she found it "simply amazing"
  • Nick develops a relationship with Jordan Baker but discovers she is "incurably dishonest," having moved a golf ball during a tournament

Summary

Chapter 3 opens with Nick's description of the preparations and spectacle of Gatsby's parties: five crates of oranges and lemons delivered every Friday, a corps of caterers, hundreds of feet of colored lights, and a full orchestra. Guests arrive without invitation and depart without ever meeting their host. Nick, one of the few formally invited, arrives after seven and wanders uncomfortably through the crowd until he finds Jordan Baker. They circulate through the party, hearing competing rumors — that Gatsby killed a man, that he was a German spy, that he is connected to European royalty. In the library, a stout, drunk man with enormous owl-eyed spectacles marvels that the books are genuinely real, though their pages have never been cut, and compares Gatsby to theatrical producer David Belasco.

Nick finally meets Gatsby by chance at a table, not realizing who he is until Gatsby introduces himself. The encounter is brief but striking: Gatsby has an extraordinary smile that seems to concentrate entirely on whoever he addresses, and he speaks with an elaborate, careful formality, calling Nick "old sport." Gatsby is called away by phone calls from Chicago and Philadelphia. Later he summons Jordan Baker for a private conversation. Jordan emerges astonished but sworn to secrecy.

The chapter closes with Nick reflecting on his daily life in New York — his bond work, his appreciation for the city's excitement — and his growing relationship with Jordan Baker. He discovers that Jordan is incurably dishonest, citing a golf tournament scandal and a borrowed car left in the rain with the top down. Nick concludes by asserting his own honesty as a "cardinal virtue."

Key Terms

  • Prodigality: Reckless extravagance or wastefulness in the use of resources; lavish generosity — used to describe how laughter spills at Gatsby's party
  • Innuendo: An indirect or subtle reference, especially one made in a suggestive way; part of the party's social atmosphere
  • Belasco: David Belasco (1853–1931), a Broadway producer famous for hyper-realistic stage sets; the owl-eyed man's comparison suggests Gatsby's library is an impressive theatrical production
  • Old sport: Gatsby's characteristic address, meant to sound casually aristocratic; its repeated use signals his carefully constructed persona
  • Erroneous: Wrong or incorrect; based on false information — used to describe the rumors circulating about Gatsby at the party

Review Questions

  1. How do most guests arrive at Gatsby's parties, and how does Nick's arrival differ from theirs?
  2. What are two of the rumors guests trade about Gatsby's identity or past, and what do these rumors suggest about how he is perceived?
  3. What does the owl-eyed man find surprising about Gatsby's library, and what does his comparison to Belasco imply?
  4. Describe Gatsby's smile as Nick experiences it. What is unusual or unsettling about it?
  5. What does Nick discover about Jordan Baker after their house party in Warwick, and how does it affect his view of her?
