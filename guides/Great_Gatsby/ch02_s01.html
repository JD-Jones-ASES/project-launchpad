Study Guide: Chapter 1: The Novel

Key Concepts

  • Nick Carraway is the first-person narrator, a Yale-educated Midwesterner who moves to West Egg, Long Island to work in the bond business
  • West Egg (new money, less fashionable) and East Egg (old money, established wealth) are separated by a "courtesy bay"
  • Gatsby's mansion is a colossal imitation of a French hotel, located next door to Nick's modest bungalow
  • Nick visits his cousin Daisy and her husband Tom Buchanan in East Egg, where he also meets Jordan Baker
  • Tom has a mistress in New York; a phone call from her disrupts the dinner party
  • The chapter ends with Nick glimpsing Gatsby alone on his lawn, arms outstretched toward a single green light across the bay

Summary

Nick Carraway opens the novel by reflecting on advice from his father: to reserve judgment of others, because not everyone has had the same advantages. He frames himself as a tolerant, nonjudgmental observer — though the reader soon notices him rendering verdicts on nearly everyone he meets. After the Great War, Nick finds the Midwest too settled and heads East to learn the bond business, renting a small bungalow in West Egg for eighty dollars a month, next to Gatsby's enormous mansion.

Nick drives to fashionable East Egg to dine with his second cousin Daisy and her husband Tom Buchanan, a wealthy former football star from their shared New Haven days. At the Buchanan home — an elaborate Georgian Colonial mansion — Nick also meets Jordan Baker, a professional golfer. Over dinner, Tom makes racist remarks drawn from pseudoscientific books, and a phone call from Tom's mistress in New York strains the evening. Daisy confides to Nick a brittle cynicism about her life, saying she hoped her infant daughter would grow up to be "a beautiful little fool."

Returning home, Nick finds the evening unsettling. He spots his mysterious neighbor standing alone on his lawn in the dark, arms stretched toward a tiny green light at the end of a distant dock across the bay. Nick decides not to call out to him, and when he looks back, Gatsby has vanished into the night.

Key Terms

  • West Egg: The less fashionable of the two peninsulas on Long Island, home to the newly rich, including Nick and Gatsby
  • East Egg: The more fashionable peninsula, home to families with established wealth and social pedigree, including Tom and Daisy Buchanan
  • Green light: A small light at the end of Daisy's dock across the bay, toward which Gatsby reaches in the novel's final image of Chapter 1; associated with longing and unattainable desire
  • Bond business: The financial profession Nick has moved East to pursue, buying and selling investment securities
  • Privy: Having secret or private knowledge of something; admitted to a confidence

Review Questions

  1. Why does Nick say he came East, and where does he settle when he arrives on Long Island?
  2. How does Fitzgerald physically describe Tom Buchanan, and what does the description suggest about his character?
  3. What does Jordan Baker reveal to Nick about Tom during the dinner party?
  4. What is Daisy's "family secret" about her daughter, and what does it reveal about how she views her own life?
  5. What does Gatsby appear to be doing at the end of the chapter, and what object does Nick notice across the water?
