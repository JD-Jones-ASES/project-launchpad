Study Guide: Sonnet LXXXIV

Overview

Sonnet LXXXIV opens with an elegant logical puzzle: what is the highest praise that can be given to the Fair Youth? The speaker's answer is the tautology "you alone are you" — that the youth is uniquely himself, his own standard of excellence, with no equal anywhere by which he could be measured. This leads to an argument about the best poetic strategy for writing about such a subject, culminating in a surprising gentle rebuke of the youth himself.

Meaning and Argument

The octave argues that the youth is so absolutely unique that his own confines hold the entire store of excellence that would be needed to find his equal — and since no such equal exists, the greatest praise is simply the recognition of his absolute singularity. The sestet proposes a poetic strategy: the best poet is not the most inventive but the most accurate copier, reproducing faithfully what nature has already written in the youth. Such a "counterpart" (faithful copy) will make the poet's wit famous and his style admired everywhere. The couplet turns unexpectedly: "you to your beauteous blessings add a curse, / Being fond on praise, which makes your praises worse." The youth's own vanity — his craving for compliment — is the flaw that degrades the quality of his praise.

Poetic Devices

The tautological praise ("you alone are you") is a philosophical device called self-predication, which paradoxically becomes the greatest possible compliment: the youth is the only category into which he fits. "Immured" (walled in) applies the language of confinement to the excellence within the youth, giving his singularity a spatial metaphor. The couplet's volta — turning from universal praise to personal criticism — demonstrates Shakespeare's characteristic refusal of unqualified flattery.

Themes

The themes are absolute uniqueness as a form of perfection, the ethics of faithful representation versus inventive flattery, and vanity as a self-undermining flaw. LXXXIV's critique of the youth's fondness for praise is one of the more honest and complex moments in the sequence.
