Study Guide: Sonnet CLIV — The Anacreontic Coda (Part 2)

Summary and Meaning

Sonnet CLIV is the twin and variant of CLIII, the closing poem of the entire 1609 Quarto sequence. It retells the same Anacreontic myth with slightly expanded focus: Cupid sleeps, a band of chaste nymphs passes by, and the fairest votary quenches his heart-inflaming brand in a cool well, transforming the water into a perpetual healing bath. The speaker, enslaved to his mistress ("my mistress' thrall"), comes to the bath for cure — and fails. The final couplet, "Love's fire heats water, water cools not love," is the sequence's epitaphic last word: love's power operates in one direction only, transforming everything it touches while remaining itself unquenchable.

Key Poetic Devices

  • "General of hot desire": Cupid as military commander — bathos in being disarmed by a sleeping and a virgin hand
  • "Legions of true hearts": the brand has warmed countless lovers — universalizing the speaker's condition across time
  • Epigrammatic couplet: "Love's fire heats water, water cools not love" — compressed paradox as final summation, one of Shakespeare's most memorable closing lines
  • Chiasmus / antithesis: fire/water, heats/cools — the couplet's structure mirrors its logic of irreversibility

The Anacreontic Coda: Final Themes

Love's incurability as the sequence's conclusion: The closing epigram declares that love cannot be remedied by any external means — the sequence's emotional truth rendered as timeless law. Moral struggle resolved — or not: The coda does not resolve the moral anguish of sin and shame catalogued across Chapter 13; it reframes it as inevitable. Love's fire is not condemned as sinful here but presented as a force beyond moral adjudication. The Anacreontic register: The lightness and elegance of the classical myth form a tonal contrast with the raw self-indictment of CLII, suggesting that the sequence ends not in despair but in a rueful, clear-eyed acknowledgment of desire's sovereignty. The final paradox — water cannot cool love — answers every prior attempt at reason, medicine, and spiritual discipline with the same inescapable truth.
