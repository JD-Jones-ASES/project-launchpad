Study Guide: Sonnet CXXXIII — Triple Enslavement

Overview

Sonnet CXXXIII introduces the most complex dramatic situation in the Dark Lady sequence: the speaker discovers that his closest friend has also been enslaved by the mistress. She has captured both men — and in doing so has stolen the speaker from himself, his friend from him, and effectively blocked all three parties from genuine freedom. The sonnet is driven by anguished logic that the couplet finally dismisses with resigned self-awareness.

Key Themes

Triangular Imprisonment. The mistress has not merely captured the speaker's heart; she has captured his dearest companion (widely identified as the Fair Youth). Now the speaker is "forsaken" of all three: his own self, his friend, and any real access to the mistress herself. The geometry of the situation is mathematically cruel.

Loss of Self Through Love. "Me from myself thy cruel eye hath taken" is one of the sequence's most striking lines. The speaker has been so thoroughly captured by passion that he is no longer self-possessed. Love has alienated him from his own identity — a condition that renders even his logical arguments powerless (the couplet's conclusion).

Legal Metaphors of Slavery. The speaker reaches for legal imagery — prison, bail, ward, keeper — to describe his and his friend's situation. This language of property and custody underscores the dehumanizing aspect of his passion: to be enslaved by love is to lose the legal status of a free person.

Literary Devices

  • Legal metaphor: prison, ward, bail, guard, rigor
  • Paradox: his being pent in her means all his logic belongs to her to dismiss
  • Anaphora: "Of him, myself, and thee" — the three-fold loss enumerated
  • Irony: the elaborate argument of lines 11-14 demolished by the couplet's two lines

Significance

CXXXIII bridges the Dark Lady and Fair Youth sequences, showing how the speaker's two great loves have collided in a triangle that harms everyone. The sonnet's legal ingenuity and its final resignation capture the helplessness of all-consuming obsession.
