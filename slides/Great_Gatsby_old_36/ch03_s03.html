The Nature of Gatsby's Guests

Uninvited Access

Most guests were not formally invited; they "went there" after hearing about the parties, ending up at Gatsby's door through various means.

Anonymous Behavior

Guests are described as conducting themselves "according to the rules of behavior associated with amusement parks," implying a lack of formality or personal responsibility. Many never meet Gatsby.

Motivations

Some guests, particularly young Englishmen, are perceived as "selling something" (bonds, insurance, automobiles), drawn by the "easy money in the vicinity."

Fleeting Connections

Introductions are "forgotten on the spot," and women meet "who never knew each other's names," emphasizing the superficiality of the interactions.

Nick Carraway's Experience

Unique Invitation

Nick is one of the few genuinely invited guests, receiving a formal note from Gatsby via chauffeur.

Initial Discomfort

He feels "ill at ease" and "purposeless and alone" amidst the unfamiliar crowd, highlighting his difference from the other guests.

Search for Gatsby

Nick attempts to find his host but is met with confused stares and denials of knowledge, underscoring Gatsby's elusive nature.

Encounter with Jordan Baker

Nick finds refuge by attaching himself to Jordan, whom he met previously. They wander through the party, observing the scene.

Sitting with Strangers

They join a table with two "girls in yellow" and three anonymous men, illustrating the casual and often impersonal social dynamics.
