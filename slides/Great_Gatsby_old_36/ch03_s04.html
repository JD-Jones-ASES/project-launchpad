Important Terms (1/3)

Echolalia

(Figurative use) The repetitive or meaningless repetition of another person's spoken words, here used to describe the murmuring, indistinct chatter of the party guests.

Jovial condescension

A cheerful or hearty attitude that also implies a patronizing or superior air, as shown by the orchestra leader.

Fraternal hilarity

Friendly, boisterous, and unrestrained merriment among a group, typically men, but here applied to the mixed-gender party guests.

Puppyish, convivial

Playfully affectionate and friendly, describing the guests' increasingly uninhibited interactions.

Vinous sleep

A deep sleep induced by excessive consumption of wine or alcohol.

Important Terms (2/3)

Rent asunder

Torn apart or violently separated, referring to the arguments breaking up Jordan's party and other couples.

Flank attacks

(Figurative use) Indirect or roundabout aggressive approaches; here, a wife's sudden, insistent confrontations with her husband.

Indignant

Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment.

Apparition

A ghost-like figure or phantom; here, used to describe the disoriented, drunken second driver emerging from the wrecked car.

Crescendo

The loudest point reached in a gradually increasing sound; here, the climax of the car horns' blaring.

Wafer of a moon
