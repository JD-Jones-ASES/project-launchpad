Important Terms (1/2)

Pasquinade

(As used in the text) A satire or lampoon, often public and abusive. Nick initially feared Wilson's story would be turned into a racy one, ridiculing Gatsby.

Adventitious Authority

Authority or influence that is accidental or acquired incidentally, rather than inherent or by right. Used to describe the detective's voice that shaped the early, false newspaper reports.

Deranged by Grief

The official explanation for George Wilson's actions, concocted by Catherine's testimony to simplify the murder-suicide case and protect reputations, particularly Myrtle's.

Superfluous

Unnecessary; more than what is needed or useful. Nick's request for Wolfshiem to come seemed superfluous because he assumed Wolfshiem would naturally respond to the news.

Ulster

A long, loose overcoat made of heavy material, often with a cape. What Gatsby's father, Henry C. Gatz, was wearing upon his arrival.
