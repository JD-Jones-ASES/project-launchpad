Important Terms (1/2)

Nick Carraway

The narrator of *The Great Gatsby*, a Yale-educated bond salesman from the Midwest who moves to West Egg, Long Island.

"Reserving Judgment"

Nick's stated philosophy, learned from his father, which claims he avoids making quick evaluations of others. This is immediately challenged by his own narrative.

West Egg

The less fashionable of the two wealthy Long Island peninsulas, home to "new money," including Nick and Jay Gatsby.

East Egg

The more fashionable and established of the two wealthy Long Island peninsulas, home to "old money," including Daisy and Tom Buchanan.

Jay Gatsby

Nick's mysterious, immensely wealthy neighbor in West Egg, known for his lavish mansion and parties.

Important Terms (2/2)

Daisy Buchanan

Nick's second cousin, a beautiful and charming woman living in East Egg with her husband, Tom. Her voice is particularly captivating.

Tom Buchanan

Daisy's immensely wealthy husband, a former Yale football star described as powerful, arrogant, and physically imposing.

Jordan Baker

A languid, cynical professional golfer whom Nick meets at the Buchanan's dinner party.

Supercilious

Behaving or looking as though one thinks one is superior to others; a term used to describe Tom Buchanan's manner.

Indirect Characterization

The process by which the personality of a fictitious character is revealed through the character's speech, actions, appearance, etc., rather than through direct statements by the narrator.
