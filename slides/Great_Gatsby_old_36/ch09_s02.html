Important Terms (1/2)

Nick Carraway

The narrator, Gatsby's neighbor, and the only character who takes responsibility for Gatsby's funeral. He embodies genuine loyalty in a world of superficiality.

Jay Gatsby

The enigmatic millionaire, whose murder precipitates the events of this section. His death exposes the true nature of his relationships and the emptiness of his social world.

Henry C. Gatz

Gatsby's elderly, impoverished father from Minnesota. His arrival offers a poignant connection to Gatsby's humble origins and provides insight into his son's early ambitions.

Meyer Wolfsheim

Gatsby's business associate, who refuses to attend the funeral, citing his inability to "get mixed up in it." His refusal underscores the shady nature of his and Gatsby's business dealings.

Important Terms (2/2)

Catherine

Myrtle Wilson's sister. Her false testimony at the inquest denies Myrtle's affair with Tom and her connection to Gatsby, helping to simplify the official narrative of the car accident.

Inquest

A legal inquiry, typically held to establish the facts surrounding a death. Catherine's testimony here is crucial in shaping the public understanding of the events.
