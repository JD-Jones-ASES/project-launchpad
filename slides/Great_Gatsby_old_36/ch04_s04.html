Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Gatsby's Mysterious Business

Gatsby's interactions with Wolfshiem hint at the illicit nature of his wealth and connections, further establishing his enigmatic background.

Meyer Wolfshiem as a Criminal Figure

Wolfshiem is revealed as the man who fixed the 1919 World Series, solidifying his role as a prominent figure in the criminal underworld and illuminating the source of Gatsby's "business connections."

The Corrupting Influence of Wealth

The ease with which Wolfshiem discusses his criminal acts and the luxury he enjoys highlights the pervasive corruption that could exist beneath the glittering surface of the Jazz Age.

Early Signs of Marital Discord

The flashback shows that Daisy's marriage to Tom was fraught with emotional turmoil and infidelity from its very beginning, setting a precedent for their dysfunctional relationship.

Key Concepts (2/2)

The Unreliability of Reputation

Daisy's ability to maintain an "absolutely perfect reputation" despite moving in a "fast crowd" and her husband's blatant infidelity underscores the superficiality of social appearances.

Sauterne

A sweet white wine, in this context, used by Daisy to get drunk before her wedding to Tom.

Santa Barbara

A city in California where Tom and Daisy stayed after their wedding, and where Tom's first recorded act of infidelity occurred.
