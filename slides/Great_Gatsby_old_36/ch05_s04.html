Important Terms (1/2)

Vestige (A trace or remnant)

Used to describe how "every vestige of embarrassment was gone" from Gatsby and Daisy, indicating a complete shift in their emotional state.

Ecstatic (Feeling or expressing overwhelming happiness)

Describes Gatsby's joyful reaction to the sun, illustrating his elation at the success of the reunion.

Inconceivable (Not capable of being imagined or grasped mentally; unbelievable)

Refers to the "inconceivable pitch of intensity" of Gatsby's long-held dream, emphasizing the profound nature of his longing.

Colossal (Extremely large or great; immense)

Gatsby uses this word to describe the previous "colossal significance" of the green light, highlighting how important it was to his dream.

Mr. Klipspringer (The "Boarder")

A peculiar character who seems to live at Gatsby's mansion indefinitely, discovered doing exercises. He represents the transient, often parasitic, nature of some of Gatsby's "guests."

Important Terms (2/2)

Dan Cody

An elderly man in a yachting costume whose photograph is displayed in Gatsby's study. Gatsby reveals he was his "best friend years ago," hinting at a crucial mentor figure from his past.

The Green Light

A symbol Gatsby associated with Daisy and his dream of reuniting with her. In this section, its symbolic power diminishes upon the physical realization of his dream.

Marie Antoinette music-rooms/Restoration salons

Architectural and decorative styles Gatsby uses in his mansion, suggesting his attempt to mimic the grandeur and aristocratic past of old money, albeit in a somewhat theatrical or purchased way.

"The Love Nest" / "Ain't We Got Fun"

Songs played by Klipspringer. Their lyrics, often about simple happiness or getting by, create an ironic contrast with Gatsby's extravagant lifestyle and the complex emotions of the reunion.
