The Fundamental Test of Strength

Self-Sufficiency

A prince is strong if he possesses the men and money necessary to field an army and face any attacker in a pitched battle. Such a prince controls his own destiny.

The German Free Cities Model

Autonomy

They owed allegiance to the emperor but governed themselves.

Preparedness

They maintained their own militias and regular military training.

Wealth

They were trading centers capable of funding significant defenses.

Civic Engagement

The citizens were armed participants in their own defense, creating a state that was extremely difficult to conquer.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What foundational question does Machiavelli ask about principalities in Chapter X, which determines how a prince should govern?

Whether a prince can defend himself with his own resources, or if he depends on the assistance of others.

According to Machiavelli, what defines a prince who can 'support himself by his own resources'?

A prince who can, by abundance of men or money, raise a sufficient army to join battle against any attacker in the open field.

What defines a prince who 'has always need of the assistance of others'?

A prince who cannot face the enemy in the open field and is forced to defend himself by sheltering behind city walls.

What is Machiavelli's primary advice for princes who must depend on others for defense, regarding their territory?

To provision and fortify their towns well, and on no account to attempt defending the open country.

What two key conditions make a prince and his city resilient against attack, according to Machiavelli?

A prince who has a strong, well-fortified city AND has not made himself odious (hated) by his people.
