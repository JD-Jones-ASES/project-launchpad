Important Terms (1/2)

Fortune

In Machiavelli's context, Fortune refers to the unpredictable, uncontrollable forces of chance, fate, or external circumstances that influence human events and can elevate or depose rulers.

Free Will

The human capacity to make choices and direct one's own actions, even when faced with powerful external forces. Machiavelli argues it accounts for roughly half of human affairs.

Spirit of the Times

The prevailing conditions, trends, opportunities, or demands of a specific period or situation. A prince's success hinges on conforming their methods to this spirit.

Impetuous

Acting quickly, spontaneously, and sometimes rashly, driven by sudden emotion or instinct. Machiavelli contrasts this with cautious behavior.

Circumspect

Careful, cautious, and unwilling to take risks; characterized by prudence and forethought.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is virtù in Machiavelli's philosophy?

Human skill, energy, and decisiveness in political action.

What is fortuna in Machiavelli's philosophy?

Fortune, luck, or uncontrollable circumstances that affect political outcomes.

What is the central philosophical tension addressed in Chapter XXV of The Prince?

The relationship between virtù (human skill and agency) and fortuna (circumstances beyond human control).

What belief about fortune was widespread in Machiavelli's era, which he partially acknowledges?

The belief that fortune governs everything and human effort makes no difference. Machiavelli concedes fortune is powerful but not omnipotent.

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to Machiavelli, what was a key reason for the Italian princes' failure against foreign invasion?

They were like people who had never built embankments before the flood, meaning they lacked foresight, preparation, and virtù to manage fortuna.

How does Machiavelli balance virtù and fortuna in his philosophy?

He occupies a middle ground, stating that fortune governs roughly half of human affairs and virtù governs the other half, rejecting both fatalism and ideal...

What qualities enable a prince to better master fortune's domain?

Being bold, prepared, and adaptable, which allows a prince to maximize his chances, though fortune cannot be fully controlled.

Can a prince fully control fortuna according to Machiavelli?

No, fortune cannot be fully controlled. The best a prince can do is maximize his chances by developing virtù and building strong political foundations.

What is Machiavelli's second key argument concerning how a prince should approach fortune?

Fortune favors bold action over cautious action.

Who does Machiavelli cite as an example of a successful bold prince?

Pope Julius II, who was consistently successful despite his reckless boldness because his actions matched the demands of his moment.
