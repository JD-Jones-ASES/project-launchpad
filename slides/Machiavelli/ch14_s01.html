Auxiliaries and the Principle of Military Self-Reliance

Study Slides

The Specific Danger of Auxiliaries

Definition

Auxiliaries are complete military units borrowed from another ruler, operating under the command of that ruler's captains.

Loyalty

Unlike mercenaries, who fight for money and have no master, auxiliaries are loyal to the prince who dispatched them.

The "Win-Win" Trap

The Risk of Loss

If auxiliaries lose, the host prince is defeated and weakened, leaving them vulnerable to the "benefactor" who may seize the opportunity to take control.

Political Self-Reliance

Fundamental Lever of Power

Military security is the primary lever of political power. To outsource it is to surrender sovereignty.

Conditional Goodwill

Relying on the help of others subjects a prince to the conditional and changing goodwill of benefactors.

Modern Equivalents

In contemporary contexts, "own arms" extends beyond soldiers to include an independent industrial base, domestic food supplies, and control over critical technology and energy.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are 'auxiliaries' in Machiavelli's military classification?

Auxiliaries are forces of another ruler called in to aid and defend a prince.

Why are auxiliaries always disadvantageous, according to Machiavelli?

If a prince loses with auxiliaries, he is undone; if he wins, he becomes their captive, losing his independence and control.

What military strategy does a wise prince always adopt regarding foreign arms?

A wise prince always avoids foreign arms (mercenaries and auxiliaries) and turns to his own, willing to lose with them rather than conquer with others.

What constitutes a 'real victory' for Machiavelli?

A real victory is one gained with one's own arms, not with the arms of others.
