Q: According to the text, why do princes who rise by fortune have difficulty maintaining their states?

> They lack the knowledge of how to command (having lived as private citizens), they do not have loyal or faithful forces, and their states lack "foundations and correspondencies" to withstand political "storms."

Q: What was the purpose of the "court of judgment" established in Romagna?

> Borgia set up a court under an "excellent president" with advocates for every city to replace the excessive authority of Ramiro d’Orco, ensuring the government did not become "odious" to the people.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is Cesare Borgia's significance to Machiavelli in "The Prince"?

He is Machiavelli's foremost example of a capable new prince who rose through others' arms but ultimately displayed exceptional ability.

What is Machiavelli's initial skepticism regarding princes who rise by others' fortune?

He believes their foundations are insecure, dependent on the continued goodwill of those who elevated them.

Why does Machiavelli consider Cesare Borgia an extraordinary exception to his general skepticism about princes who rise on others' fortune?

Borgia recognized his opportunity, understood what he needed to do, and executed a remarkably coherent and ruthless strategy to consolidate his power.

Who was Cesare Borgia's father, and how did his position aid Borgia's early rise to power?

His father was Cardinal Rodrigo Borgia, who became Pope Alexander VI. As Pope, he used the Church's resources (money, influence, spiritual sanctions) to carv...

Before pursuing military and political ambitions, what ecclesiastical role did Cesare Borgia hold?

He was originally created a cardinal before being released from clerical orders in 1498.

What personal qualities and methods characterized Cesare Borgia as a military commander and ruler?

He was known for physical prowess, intelligence, and an utter lack of scruple, using military force, negotiation, and murder to conquer petty lordships.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What specific aspect of Borgia's strategy did Machiavelli most admire?

His methodical, long-range approach, planning for his survival and power after his father's death, rather than just seizing opportunities.

What was Cesare Borgia's first strategic step to secure his future power base, independent of his father?

What was Cesare Borgia's second strategic step to secure his future power base?

He worked to ensure that any future pope would be either friendly to his interests or too weak to harm him.

What was Cesare Borgia's third crucial strategic step to build a lasting foundation for his rule?

He built his own loyal military force, owing allegiance to him rather than to his father or hired commanders.

What territory did Cesare Borgia conquer, which was known for its chaos and petty lordships?

The Romagna, a region in central Italy.

Who was Remirro de Orco, and what was his initial role under Cesare Borgia?

He was a "cruel and capable man" appointed by Borgia as governor of the Romagna to brutally suppress chaos and impose order.
