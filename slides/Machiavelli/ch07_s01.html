Powers and Principalities (Machiavelli)

Chapter 7, Section 1

New Principalities and the Exercise of Virtù

Study Slides

The Interaction of Virtù and Fortuna

Fortuna (Opportunity)

The specific historical circumstances, crises, or moments of instability that make a population willing to accept a new leader.

Virtù (Ability/Talent)

The exceptional personal qualities required to recognize that opportunity and give it lasting political form.

The Crisis as Raw Material

Moses

found the Israelites enslaved.

Cyrus

found the Persians discontented.

Romulus

found Rome in chaos.

Theseus

found the Athenians scattered.

The Difficulty of the "New Order"

Active Enemies

Those who benefited from the old system will fight the reformer with ferocity.

Timid Supporters

Those who might benefit from the new system offer only lukewarm support because they are uncertain of the outcome and fear the established powers.

The Unarmed Prophet: Girolamo Savonarola

Achievement

Expelled the Medici and instituted moral and religious reforms.

Failure

When his support collapsed following excommunication by Pope Alexander VI, he had no army to protect his position.

Outcome

He was arrested, tortured, and executed in the same square where he had held his "Bonfires of the Vanities."

Key Concepts (1/2)

What central concept of princely quality does Machiavelli articulate most fully in Chapter VI of The Prince?

Machiavelli articulates his fullest vision of virtù, exemplified by the great founders of states.

What provocative observation does Machiavelli make about the success of armed versus unarmed prophets?

He observes that armed prophets succeed, and unarmed ones fail, without exception.

How does the prince's 'ability' impact the difficulty of keeping an entirely new principality?

The more ability the prince possesses, the less difficulty will be found in keeping the new principality.

What two primary factors does Machiavelli identify as necessary for a private citizen to become a prince?

Ability (virtù) or fortune (fortuna).

Between ability and fortune, which one does Machiavelli claim makes a prince strongest in the long run?

He who has relied least on fortune is established the strongest.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What does Machiavelli mean by fortuna in the context of political outcomes?

Fortuna refers to the unpredictable forces—luck, circumstance, or timing—that influence political events. Machiavelli often personifies it as a woman.

Name four key historical or legendary figures Machiavelli presents as exemplary founders of new states through their own ability.

Moses, Cyrus the Great, Romulus, and Theseus.

What 'opportunity' was essential for Moses to demonstrate his ability?

He needed to find the people of Israel enslaved and oppressed in Egypt, making them ready to follow him for deliverance.
