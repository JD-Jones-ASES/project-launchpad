The Two Factions: Nobles vs. The People

The Nobles

Driven by the desire to rule and oppress the common people.

The People

Driven by the desire to avoid being ruled or oppressed by the nobles.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Absolute Order

A system of government where the prince exercises total authority personally, rather than through magistrates or shared civic structures.

Civil Principality

A state where a leading citizen becomes the ruler through the favor and support of his fellow citizens.

Magistrates

Citizens appointed to administrative positions who carry out the orders of the government; Machiavelli warns that a prince’s reliance on them can be a source of weakness.

Nobles (Grandi)

The elite faction of a city characterized by their ambition to rule and oppress the common people.

People (Popolo)

The general populace characterized by their desire to avoid oppression and live without being ruled by the nobility.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pusillanimity

A lack of courage or spirit; a quality Machiavelli attributes to certain nobles who do not bind themselves to a prince’s fortune.

Shrewdness (Astuia)

A combination of intelligence and political craftiness used to navigate the competing interests of the nobles and the people.
