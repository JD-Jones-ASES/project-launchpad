The Prince and the Art of War: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

War as the Prince’s Sole Profession

Credibility

Coercive power depends on a credible threat of force.

Control

A prince ignorant of military matters loses the respect of his soldiers and becomes a puppet for his generals or allies.

Strategic Soundness

Understanding military constraints prevents strategic miscalculations.

Deterrence

The perception of military weakness invites aggression from rivals.

According to Machiavelli, what is the sole art a prince ought to study?

War and its rules and discipline.

Beyond upholding those born as princes, how can mastery of the art of war benefit an individual?

It can enable men to rise from a private station to the rank of prince.

Which historical figure does Machiavelli cite as an example of someone who rose to power through martial skill?

Francesco Sforza, who became Duke of Milan from a private person through his martial prowess.

What contrasting example does Machiavelli use to illustrate the consequences of avoiding the hardships of arms?

Francesco Sforza's sons, who, by avoiding military hardships, fell from dukes to private persons.
