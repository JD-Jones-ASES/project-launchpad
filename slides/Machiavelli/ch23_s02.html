Powers and Principalities (Machiavelli)

Chapter 23, Section 2

Machiavelli on Choosing Ministers (The Prince, Chapter XXII)

Study Slides

The Reflection of Intelligence

The Wise Prince

Recognized by his ability to identify capable and faithful servants.

The Failed Prince

Judged poorly because his "prime error" was the initial selection of inadequate or untrustworthy advisors.

The Taxonomy of Intellects

Rank. Description

Classification

First Rank. Those who comprehend things by themselves, without external aid.

Excellent

Second Rank. Those who appreciate and understand what others have comprehended.

Good

Third Rank. Those who neither comprehend by themselves nor through the showing of others.

Useless

The Litmus Test for Ministers

• Thinks more of their own interests than the prince’s.

• Seeks their own profit in every action.

• Focuses on matters unrelated to the state or the prince's concerns.

The Maintenance of Loyalty

Rewards

The prince should honor and enrich the servant, doing them kindnesses and sharing both honors and cares.

Interdependence

The servant must see that they "cannot stand alone."

Strategic Satisfaction

The servant should be given enough riches and honors so they do not desire more, but enough "cares" (responsibilities/worries) that they dread any change in the political status quo.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Antonio da Venafro

The servant to Pandolfo Petrucci; cited as an example of an excellent choice of minister that reflected well on his master’s intelligence.

Cares

Responsibilities or burdens of state. Machiavelli suggests sharing these with a servant so the servant "dreads" any change that might disrupt their current position.

Discrimination

The ability to judge or perceive the difference between good and bad qualities in others.

Faithful

The quality of being loyal and reliable; along with "capability," it is the primary requirement for a minister.

Honours

Titles, status, or public recognition bestowed by the prince upon a servant to ensure their satisfaction and loyalty.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pandolfo Petrucci

The Prince of Siena, used as an example of a leader who demonstrated wisdom through his choice of an able advisor.

Prime Error

The fundamental mistake of choosing poor advisors, which immediately damages the public perception of a prince’s understanding.
