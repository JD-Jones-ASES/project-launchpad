Alexander the Great and the Kingdom of Darius

The Structure of Darius's State

It was similar to that of the Turk—highly centralized.

The Conquest

Alexander had to defeat Darius in the field and then kill him to secure the state.

The Result

Because the state was centralized, it remained secure after Darius's death. The only difficulties Alexander’s successors faced were those they provoked among themselves due to their own ambitions.

The Romans in Spain, France, and Greece

The Cause of Rebellion

These regions were filled with many small principalities (similar to the French model).

The Duration of Insecurity

As long as the "memory" of the old principalities endured, Roman possession remained insecure.

Final Security

The Romans only became secure possessors through the "long continuance" of their empire, which eventually caused the memory of the former lords to pass away.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Antiquity of Blood

The status of nobility derived from long-standing hereditary lineage rather than being granted by a current ruler.

Barons

Lords who possess their own subjects and states, holding their dignity through inheritance rather than the prince's favor.

Bondmen/Slaves

Terms used to describe the ministers of a centralized prince (like the Turk) to highlight their total dependence on the ruler’s will and lack of independent authority.

Darius

Darius III, the last Achaemenid king of Persia, whose empire's centralized structure allowed it to be held by Alexander the Great once Darius was defeated and killed.

Malcontents

Dissatisfied members of the nobility who are willing to assist a foreign invader in hopes of changing the current political order.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Sanjaks

Administrative divisions within the Ottoman Empire (the Turk) used by the prince to manage his territory through appointed administrators.

Uniformity in the Subject State

The degree to which a state is centrally organized under a single authority; high uniformity makes a state harder to enter but easier to keep.
