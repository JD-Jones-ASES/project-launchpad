Study Slides

The Paradox of Clemency and Cruelty

Effective Cruelty

Ineffective Mercy

The Florentine people are used as a counter-example; their desire to avoid a reputation for cruelty led them to allow the destruction of Pistoia through factional violence.

The Superiority of Fear over Love

Human Nature

Men are generally ungrateful, fickle, false, cowardly, and covetous.

The Fragility of Love

Love is maintained by a "link of obligation" which men break whenever it serves their advantage.

The Stability of Fear

Fear is maintained by a "dread of punishment" which is a constant and reliable deterrent.

Avoiding Hatred

The Priority of Property

Machiavelli notes that men forget the death of a father more quickly than the loss of their inheritance (patrimony).

Military Leadership and Discipline

Hannibal

His "inhuman cruelty" and "boundless valour" kept his diverse army united and free from dissension, even in bad fortune.

Scipio

Despite his virtues, his "too great forbearance" led to army rebellion in Spain. His "easy nature" was criticized by the Senate as a corrupting influence on military discipline.

Quiz Question 1

According to Machiavelli, why was Cesare Borgia actually 'more merciful' than the Florentine people?

A. His use of cruelty successfully restored peace and unity to the Romagna.

B. He offered public apologies and reparations for his initial acts of violence.

C. He only targeted the ruling elite while sparing the common citizens.

D. His cruelty was purely performative and did not involve actual loss of life.

Hint: Think about the ultimate outcome of Borgia's administration compared to the fate of Pistoia.

Quiz Question 2

Why does Machiavelli assert that it is 'much safer to be feared than loved'?

A. Love is an obligation that men break for their own advantage, while fear is maintained by a constant dread of punishment.

B. Fear creates a deep sense of respect and moral duty that love cannot inspire.

C. People are naturally inclined to obey those they dislike more than those they admire.

D. Loved leaders are perceived as weak and are therefore more likely to be assassinated.

Hint: Consider the difference between a voluntary 'link of obligation' and an involuntary 'dread of punishment.'

Quiz Question 3

What does Machiavelli identify as the primary cause of hatred that a prince must avoid?

A. Interfering with the property and the women of his subjects.

B. Executing political dissidents without a public trial.

C. Raising taxes to fund foreign military campaigns.

D. Demonstrating cowardice or indecision in times of war.

Hint: The author suggests that men have a longer memory for material loss than for personal loss.

Quiz Question 4

D. His shared cultural heritage with the majority of his troops.

Hint: Look for the specific 'terrible' trait that Machiavelli claims short-sighted writers wrongly condemn.
