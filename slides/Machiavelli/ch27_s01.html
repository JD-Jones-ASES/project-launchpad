Important Terms (1/2)

The Prince

Niccolò Machiavelli's influential 16th-century political treatise, offering advice to rulers on how to acquire, maintain, and wield political power.

Chapter XXVI (The Exhortation)

The final chapter of *The Prince*, which dramatically shifts in tone to a passionate appeal for the liberation and unification of Italy.

Virtù

A key Machiavellian concept referring to a prince's strength, skill, courage, foresight, and ability to act decisively and effectively in the political sphere, often against the dictates of fortune...

Fortune

Refers to luck, fate, or external circumstances that are beyond a prince's direct control. Machiavelli discusses how a prince can attempt to manage or mitigate the effects of fortune through virtù.

Propitious

Favorable, opportune; indicating a good chance of success. Machiavelli describes the state of Italy as "propitious" for a new prince.

Important Terms (2/2)

Armed Prophet

A leader who combines religious or ideological vision with the military capacity to enforce that vision. Moses is presented as the primary example.

Allegorical Figure

A symbolic representation of an abstract concept, often personified (e.g., a figure representing a nation, as in Philipp Veit's painting).
