Chapter 1, Section 2

Strategies for Reading Machiavelli’s *The Prince*

Study Slides

Reading as Argument

Thesis-Driven Chapters

Each chapter is designed to advance a specific thesis regarding how politics works.

Evidence-Based Claims

Every argument is backed by specific evidence. Readers should constantly ask: What is the central argument? Does the provided evidence logically support the claim?

The Method of Historical Case Study

Inductive Reasoning

He reasons from specific examples to establish general rules of governance.

Key Examples

He frequently cites figures such as Cesare Borgia, Moses, Ferdinand of Aragon, and various Roman emperors.

Critical Evaluation

Readers must assess whether Machiavelli’s interpretation of these historical figures is accurate, fair, and if a single case is sufficient to prove a broader rule.

Analyzing the "Significant Silence"

Omissions

There is a near-total absence of appeals to God, justice, natural law, or traditional morality.

Focus on Effectiveness

By silencing ethics and theology, Machiavelli signals that his project is grounded entirely in the practical effectiveness of political action.

Engaging with Provocations

Critical Inquiry

Readers should test his assertions against their own knowledge of leadership—for example, his claim that it is better to be feared than loved, or that an unarmed prophet is doomed to failure.

The W. K. Marriott Translation

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What was Machiavelli’s assumption about his intended audience?

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Abstract Principles

Theoretical concepts or philosophical rules not necessarily derived from specific, real-world examples.

Archaic

Language or vocabulary that is old-fashioned and no longer in common usage (addressed in the Marriott translation via footnotes).

Case Study

A process or record of research in which detailed consideration is given to the development of a particular person, group, or situation over a period of time; Machiavelli's primary method.

Edwardian English

The style of English used during the reign of King Edward VII (early 20th century), characterized in the Marriott translation as clear and accessible.

Everyman's Library

The series in which the standard 1908 Marriott translation of *The Prince* was published.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Natural Law

A body of unchanging moral principles regarded as a basis for all human conduct; notably absent from Machiavelli's arguments.

Political Realist

A term applied to Machiavelli, describing his focus on how politics actually works (effectiveness) rather than how it should work (ethics).

Provocation

An action or statement intended to examine, challenge, or stimulate a critical response.

Theology

The study of religious faith, practice, and experience; one of the traditional foundations of political thought that Machiavelli ignores.

Thesis

A statement or theory that is put forward as a premise to be maintained or proved.
