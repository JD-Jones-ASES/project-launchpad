Powers and Principalities (Machiavelli)

Chapter 17, Section 2

Machiavelli on Liberality and Meanness

Study Slides

The Strategic Value of Meanness

Sufficiency of Revenue

His economy ensures he has enough funds for governance.

Defense and Enterprise

He can defend the state and engage in major projects without placing extra burdens on the populace.

Broad Liberality

By not taking from the "numberless" (the general population), he is effectively liberal toward them, while being "mean" only toward the "few" to whom he does not give.

Contextual Liberality

Scenario. Recommendation

Reasoning

A Prince in Fact. Be sparing/frugal.

Extravagance leads to the destruction of the government.

In a Way to Become a Prince. Be considered liberal.

It is necessary to gain pre-eminence and support while ascending to power.

Spending One's Own/Subjects' Wealth. Be very sparing.

Spending these resources leads to poverty and resentment.

Spending the Wealth of Others. Be a "ready giver."

Using wealth from pillage, sack, and extortion (others' money) adds to reputation without personal cost.

Historical Illustrations

Pope Julius II

Used a reputation for liberality to attain the papacy but abandoned it to fund his wars through "long thriftiness" rather than extraordinary taxes.

The King of Spain

Achieved numerous successful enterprises specifically because he was not reputed to be liberal.

Julius Caesar

Used liberality to reach the highest positions in Rome. However, Machiavelli notes that had Caesar survived and not moderated his expenses, he would have destroyed his government.

Cyrus and Alexander

These leaders were able to be generous because they squandered the wealth of conquered territories ("others' property") rather than their own.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Liberality

Generosity; the quality of being open-handed or spending freely.

Meanness

Frugality or parsimony; a reluctance to spend money (often used by Machiavelli as a strategic tool).

Magnificence

The state of being grand, extravagant, or lavish in appearance and spending.

Odious

Arousing or deserving of hatred or strong dislike.

Parsimony

Extreme unwillingness to spend money or use resources; thriftiness.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Rapacious

Aggressively greedy or grasping; in this context, forced to seize wealth from subjects through high taxes or confiscation.

Reproach

An expression of disapproval or disappointment; a cause for shame.
