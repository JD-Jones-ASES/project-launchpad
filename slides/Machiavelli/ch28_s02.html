Machiavelli's Intellectual Descendants: The Italian Realist School

Foundation

Three centuries after Niccolò Machiavelli, Italian thinkers Gaetano Mosca, Vilfredo Pareto, and Robert Michels built upon his core insights.

Shared Commitment

Like Machiavelli, they focused on studying politics "as it actually works," not as idealists claim it should.

Core Conclusion

Power is consistently held and exercised by a small, organized minority over a larger, disorganized majority.

Significance

Their work marked the first systematic, academic development of Machiavelli's ideas, transforming his practical advice for princes into a general theory of political power.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who are the three main thinkers collectively known as the Italian Realist School or the Elite Theory School?

Gaetano Mosca, Vilfredo Pareto, and Robert Michels.

What core belief did the Italian Realist School share with Machiavelli regarding political power?

How did the Italian Realist School transform Machiavelli's ideas?

They developed his practical handbook for princes into a systematic academic theory of political power and its structural conditions.

Which Italian Realist thinker authored The Ruling Class?

Gaetano Mosca.

What is Gaetano Mosca's central argument about governance, regardless of a society's formal constitution?

Every society is governed not by the majority but by a small, organized, coherent minority (the 'political class' or 'ruling class').

According to Mosca, why does a minority always govern society?

Because small groups can coordinate more easily and effectively than large, disorganized ones, giving them an organizational advantage in controlling power.

What was a key difference between Mosca's work and Machiavelli's in terms of focus?

Machiavelli wrote about individual princes, while Mosca focused on the structural conditions that make elite rule inevitable in any society.

Vilfredo Pareto.

What does Pareto's 'circulation of elites' theory propose?
