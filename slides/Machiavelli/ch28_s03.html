From Political Theory to International Relations

Evolution of Realism

The Machiavellian tradition, initially focused on individual princes and domestic elites, significantly evolved in the 20th century.

New Context

Two World Wars, the rise of totalitarianism, and the Cold War between the United States and the Soviet Union necessitated new analytical frameworks.

Shift in Application

Political thinkers began applying Machiavellian insights to the **relations between nation-states** within an **anarchic international system**.

Dominant Framework

This tradition, known as **international relations realism**, became the prevailing approach to understanding world politics in the 20th century and maintains substantial influence today.

Hans Morgenthau: Politics Among Nations

Founding Text

Hans Morgenthau's *Politics Among Nations* (1948) is considered the seminal work of modern realist international relations theory.

Core Argument: Pursuit of Power

Morgenthau argued that the fundamental driver in international politics is the **pursuit of power**.

State Motivation

States, like Machiavelli's princes, are primarily motivated by survival and the desire to maximize their power relative to others, not by ideology, morality, or international law.
