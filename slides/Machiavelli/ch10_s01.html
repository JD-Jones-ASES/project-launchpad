Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Civil Principality

A state where a private citizen becomes prince through the favor and support of his fellow citizens.

Grandi (The Great)

The noble or elite class characterized by an ambitious desire to command and oppress others.

Popolo (The People)

The general body of citizens who seek security and freedom from oppression rather than political power.

Humors

The two opposing natural inclinations or drives within a city (the desire to oppress vs. the desire not to be oppressed).

Political Legitimacy

The practical reality that a ruler’s power is most durable when it has the consent or acquiescence of the majority of the population.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Machiavelli, what is a "civil principality"?

A principality where a leading citizen becomes prince by the favor of fellow citizens, not by wickedness or any intolerable violence.

What two distinct parties does Machiavelli identify in all cities as the source of political conflict?

The people and the nobles.

What are the fundamental desires of the people and the nobles, respectively, that drive political outcomes?

The people desire not to be ruled nor oppressed by the nobles; the nobles wish to rule and oppress the people.

What are the three possible results that arise from the opposing desires of the people and the nobles?

A principality, self-government, or anarchy.

Why do the nobles sometimes create a prince from among themselves?

To gain a shadow under which they can give vent to their ambitions, particularly when they cannot withstand the people.

Why do the people sometimes create a prince from among themselves?

To be defended by his authority, particularly when they cannot resist the nobles.

How does Machiavelli describe the position of a prince who reaches sovereignty by popular favor?

He finds himself alone, with few around him who are not prepared to obey him.

Which group is easier to satisfy: the nobles or the people, and what is the reason?

The people are easier to satisfy because their object is more righteous: they only desire not to be oppressed, while the nobles wish to oppress.

What is the worst a prince can expect from a hostile people, and how does this compare to hostile nobles?

From a hostile people, the worst is abandonment. From hostile nobles, he fears not only abandonment but also that they will rise against him.
