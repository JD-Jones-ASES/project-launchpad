The "Real Truth" vs. The Ideal

The Risk of Virtue

A man who attempts to act with perfect virtue in all circumstances will inevitably be destroyed, as he is surrounded by many who are not virtuous.

The Necessity of "Doing Wrong"

To maintain power and "hold his own," a prince must learn how to be "not good" and know when to apply this knowledge based on necessity.

The Qualities of a Prince (Praise vs. Blame)

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Avaricious

In the Tuscan context, one who seeks to acquire wealth or possessions through robbery.

Effectual Truth

The reality of how people live and act, as opposed to how they "ought" to live; the practical outcome of political actions.

Hinge

A metaphorical description of Chapter XV, indicating its role as the central turning point where the text moves from theory to application.

Human Conditions

The inherent limitations and realities of human nature that prevent a leader from being perfectly virtuous.

Miserly

A term used by Machiavelli to describe someone who is overly frugal with their own resources.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Preservation

The act of maintaining power and keeping the state secure; the ultimate goal of the prince.

Prudent

Acting with care and thought for the future; specifically, a prince's ability to navigate between virtues and vices to maintain his state.

Rapacious

Aggressively greedy or predatory; the opposite of being generous.

Reproach

Blame or disapproval directed at a prince for his conduct or character.
