Powers and Principalities (Machiavelli)

Chapter 9, Section 2

Principality Through Wickedness

Study Slides

The Strategic Use of Cruelty

Properly Used Cruelty

These are injuries applied at a single stroke, deemed necessary for one’s security, and not persisted in. They are eventually turned toward the advantage of the subjects.

Badly Employed Cruelty

These are injuries that may be few at first but multiply over time rather than decreasing. Rulers who follow this path find it impossible to maintain their position.

Benefit and Injury Timing

Injuries

Should be done all at once so they are "tasted less" and therefore offend less.

Benefits

Should be granted "little by little" so that their "flavour" lasts longer and the subjects remain grateful.

Case Studies in Wickedness

Feature. Agathocles of Syracuse (Ancient)

Oliverotto da Fermo (Modern)

Origins. Son of a potter; rose from a "low and abject position."

Orphan; raised by his uncle, Giovanni Fogliani.

Rise to Power. Rose through military ranks to become Praetor of Syracuse.

Trained under Pagolo and Vitellozzo Vitelli; became a top soldier.

Method of Seizure. Assembled the senate and people; had soldiers kill all senators and the rich.

Invited his uncle and city chiefs to a banquet; had them slaughtered in a private room.

Outcome. Successfully defended Syracuse from Carthaginians; held the princedom without civil commotion.

Seized Fermo and became formidable to neighbors; strengthened himself with new ordinances.

End of Reign. Lived long and secure; never conspired against by his own citizens.

Overreached by Cesare Borgia; strangled one year after his crime.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Agathocles

A Sicilian who rose from the humble origins of a potter's son to become the King of Syracuse through military merit and extreme cruelty.

Amilcar the Carthaginian

The commander with whom Agathocles reached an understanding before seizing power in Syracuse.

Cesare Borgia

The figure who eventually "overreached" and executed Oliverotto da Fermo at Sinigaglia.

Giovanni Fogliani

The uncle and guardian of Oliverotto da Fermo who was murdered by his nephew during a banquet.

Glory

The lasting historical prestige and moral standing that the text argues cannot be achieved through wicked methods, even if those methods result in an "empire."

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Oliverotto da Fermo

A professional soldier who seized the city of Fermo by murdering his uncle and the city's leading citizens.

Praetor

The high military and administrative rank Agathocles reached in Syracuse before seizing the princedom.

Properly Used Severities

Cruel acts performed "at one blow" for the sake of security and then transitioned into benefits for the subjects.

Virtù (implied as "Genius" or "Talent")

The quality of mind and body that allows a ruler to overcome fortune, which the text distinguishes from mere "wickedness."
