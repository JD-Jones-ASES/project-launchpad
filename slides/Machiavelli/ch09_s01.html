The Utility of Cruelty

Type of Cruelty. Characteristics

Outcome

Well-Used. Committed all at once at the beginning; used only for security; followed by a shift to benevolence.

Stability; psychological safety for the prince; gradual distribution of benefits to subjects.

Badly-Used. Starts small and escalates over time; requires constant repetition of violence.

Accumulation of resentment; multiplication of enemies; a precarious and unstable state.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Badly-Used Cruelty

Violence that starts small and increases over time, preventing stability and breeding constant resentment.

Dirty Hands

A philosophical problem where a leader must do something morally wrong to achieve a greater political good or security.

Fortuna

The acquisition of power through luck or the assistance/arms of others.

Glory

A level of political achievement that earns lasting respect and admiration; distinct from mere power or empire.

Virtù

A quality of political genius and leadership that includes effectiveness, planning, and the ability to inspire admiration.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Well-Used Cruelty

Violent acts committed all at once at the start of a reign to secure power, followed by a transition to benevolent governance.

Wickedness

The acquisition of power through outright crime, treachery, or mass murder.
