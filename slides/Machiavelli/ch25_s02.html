Key Concepts (1/2)

What is Machiavelli's main verdict regarding why Italian princes lost their states?

They lost their states not due to bad luck or fortune, but because they were inadequate and violated fundamental principles of good governance.

According to Machiavelli, what was the primary issue with the armies of Italian princes?

Why did Italian princes lack popular loyalty, according to Machiavelli?

They failed to win the genuine support of their people, who therefore had little stake in their rulers' survival and offered minimal resistance to invaders.

What critical error did Italian princes make regarding preparedness for adversity?

They failed to prepare during times of peace, living as if good times would continue indefinitely and making no provision for potential attacks.

What common excuse did defeated Italian princes offer for their losses?

They blamed fortune, claiming the enemy was too strong, the timing was unlucky, or circumstances conspired against them.
