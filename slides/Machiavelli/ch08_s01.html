Case Study: The Remirro de Orco Episode

The Problem

The Romagna was a territory of petty lordships characterized by chaos, private wars, and cruelty.

The Instrument

Borgia appointed Remirro de Orco, a "cruel and capable man," as governor. Remirro used brutal efficiency to restore order and pacify the region.

The Dilemma

While order was restored, the population grew to hate Remirro for his cruelty.

The Resolution

To satisfy the people and deflect the blame for the necessary violence, Borgia had Remirro arrested and executed. His body was displayed in the public square of Cesena, cut in two.

The Result

Machiavelli notes the spectacle left the people "satisfied and stupefied." It effectively removed the instrument of cruelty while allowing Borgia to claim credit for restoring both order and justice.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Alexander VI (Rodrigo Borgia)

The Spanish-born Pope (1492–1503) who used the wealth and influence of the Holy See to advance his family’s interests, specifically those of his son, Cesare.

Cesare Borgia (Duke Valentino)

The illegitimate son of Alexander VI; a cardinal turned military commander who conquered the Romagna and is used by Machiavelli as the model of a capable new prince.

Fortuna

A term used to describe luck or the unpredictable concatenation of events (such as Borgia's ill-timed illness) that can undermine even the most methodical political planning.

Julius II

The successor to the papacy after Alexander VI; an enemy of Borgia whose election Borgia failed to prevent, leading to his ultimate political collapse.

Remirro de Orco

The cruel governor appointed by Borgia to pacify the Romagna; he was eventually executed by Borgia to shift the blame for the region's harsh pacification away from the Prince.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

The Romagna

A territory in central Italy consisting of petty lordships that Borgia conquered and attempted to consolidate into a singular state.

Satisfied and Stupefied

The phrase Machiavelli uses to describe the public’s psychological state following the spectacle of Remirro de Orco’s execution—combining a sense of justice served with a fear of the Prince’s power.
