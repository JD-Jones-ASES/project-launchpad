The Pre-Expansion Era

Glossary of Key Terms and Figures (1/2)

Alexander VI

The Pope who first demonstrated the effectiveness of using money and arms to expand the Church's temporal power.

Capacity (Virtù)

One of the two methods (alongside fortune) by which a principality is initially acquired.

Duke Valentino

The agent and instrument used by Alexander VI to carry out military and territorial expansions.

Ecclesiastical Principality

A state held by the Church, unique because it is maintained by religious tradition rather than military or political effort.

Julius II

A successor to Alexander VI who further strengthened the Church, gained Bologna, and worked to drive the French out of Italy.

Glossary of Key Terms and Figures (2/2)

Leo X

The Pope at the time of the text's conclusion, noted for inheriting a powerful pontificate and expected to enhance it through virtue.

Orsini and Colonnesi

The two primary rival factions of Roman barons whose constant conflict historically kept the Papacy weak.

Temporal Power

The secular, political, and military power of the Church, as opposed to its spiritual authority.

Venetians

A major Italian power that, along with the Roman barons, served to restrain the territorial ambitions of the Pope prior to the reign of Alexander VI.
