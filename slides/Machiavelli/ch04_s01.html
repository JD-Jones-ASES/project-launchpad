Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Balance of Power

The distribution of political and military strength among multiple states to prevent any single power from becoming dominant.

Colonization

The practice of sending a prince’s own subjects to reside in a newly annexed territory to ensure loyalty and reduce the cost of control.

Garrison

A body of troops stationed in a fortress or town to defend it; Machiavelli views these as expensive and a source of resentment.

Hereditary Principality

A state ruled by a prince whose family has been in power for a long time; described as easier to govern than mixed principalities.

Italian Wars (1494–1559)

A series of conflicts for control of Italy involving major European powers and Italian city-states, marking the end of the balanced Italian political system.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Louis XII

King of France (1498–1515) used by Machiavelli as a primary example of a ruler who failed to maintain a mixed principality due to strategic errors.

Mixed Principality

A territory newly annexed to a prince’s existing state, often possessing different cultural and linguistic traditions.

Temporal Authority

The worldly, political power held by a religious figure, such as the Pope’s control over lands and armies.
