III. Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: What was the primary grievance of the Bonus Expeditionary Force?

The veterans were demanding the early payment of cash bonuses promised by the World War Adjusted Compensation Act of 1924, which were not scheduled to be paid until 1945.

Q: Which future U.S. Presidents were involved in the clearing of the Bonus Army camp?

Major Dwight Eisenhower (as an aide to MacArthur) and Major George Patton (commanding the cavalry) were both involved in the military action against the veterans.

Q: Why did the conspirators of the Business Plot specifically target Smedley Butler?

They chose him because of his fame and his significant influence among veterans, believing he could mobilize them into a private army to march on Washington.

III. Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: How did the McCormack-Dickstein Committee respond to Butler’s testimony?

The committee investigated the claims and confirmed in its final report that Butler's testimony was credible and that a plot had indeed been discussed.

Q: What is the defining characteristic of the tone in *War Is a Racket*?

The tone is characterized by anger, directness, and a sense of personal betrayal. It is written as a witness's testimony rather than a dispassionate or scholarly analysis.

V. Glossary of Important Terms

Bonus Expeditionary Force

A group of tens of thousands of WWI veterans and their families who marched on Washington in 1932 to demand early payment of military bonuses.

Business Plot (The Wall Street Putsch)

A 1933 conspiracy alleged by Smedley Butler involving wealthy financiers who intended to overthrow the U.S. government and install a fascist regime.

McCormack-Dickstein Committee

The congressional body that investigated Butler’s claims regarding the Business Plot.

Pamphlet

A short, unbound publication (typically under 100 pages) focused on a single argument; known for its direct and urgent political rhetoric.

World War Adjusted Compensation Act (1924)

Federal legislation that granted veterans certificates for cash bonuses, with a deferred payment date of 1945.
