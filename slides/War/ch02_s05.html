The Rhetoric Examined

Study Slides

Key Terms (1/2)

Vernacular

— Ordinary, everyday language as opposed to formal academic prose.

Ethos

— Credibility; for Butler, derived from 33 years of combat service.

Logos

— Appeal to reason and evidence; Butler uses documented profit data.

Pathos

— Appeal to emotion; Butler uses physical specificity and anaphora.

Anaphora

— The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses to create rhythmic emphasis.

Key Terms (2/2)

Superlative

— The grammatical form expressing the highest degree (oldest, most profitable, most vicious).
