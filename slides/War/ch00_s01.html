VI. Glossary of Terms (1/3)

Claim

The central point or assertion a speaker is trying to prove.

Counterargument

A challenge or objection to the speaker's position that a strong writer will acknowledge and address.

Ethos

A rhetorical appeal based on the speaker's character, credibility, or authority.

Evidence

Specific, verifiable facts or reasoning provided to support a claim.

Logos

A rhetorical appeal based on logic, evidence, and facts.

VI. Glossary of Terms (2/3)

Omission

The act of leaving out specific facts or perspectives; a critical element for identifying a speaker's focus and bias.

Pathos

A rhetorical appeal to the audience’s emotions.

Primary Source

An original firsthand account or document from a specific historical period.

Rhetoric

The art and study of effective communication and persuasion.

Secondary Source

A document that describes or analyzes primary sources, such as a textbook.

VI. Glossary of Terms (3/3)

Warrant

The unstated assumption or logical bridge that connects evidence to a claim.
