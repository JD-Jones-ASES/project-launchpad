Chapter 4, Section 2

From Diagnosis to Prescription – Smedley Butler’s War Is a Racket

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Summary

Proposal. Description

Intended Goal

Conscription of Capital. Before drafting men, the government must conscript industry and labor. All participants (executives, politicians, generals) are limited to $30 a month.

To remove the profit motive from warfare.

Limited Plebiscite. The decision to enter war is moved from Congress to a direct vote by those of military age who pass a physical exam.

To ensure the decision is made only by those who will bear the consequences.

Defensive Restriction. Naval vessels must stay within 200 miles of the coast; aircraft within 500 miles; the army must never leave American territory.

To make offensive warfare physically and legally impossible.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Capital

The nation's industry, finance, and labor resources. Butler argued this should be conscripted alongside men.

Liberty Bonds

Government securities sold to the American public to finance World War I; Butler highlights them as a tool of soldier exploitation.

Limited Plebiscite

A direct vote on a specific issue (in this case, war) where the voting pool is restricted to a specific group—specifically those who would be eligible to fight.

Peroration

The concluding part of a speech or argument, intended to inspire enthusiasm or action through heightened emotion.

Prescription

In a persuasive context, the specific solution or plan of action proposed to fix a diagnosed problem.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

The Living Dead

Butler’s evocative term for veterans whose bodies or minds were permanently broken by the trauma of combat.
