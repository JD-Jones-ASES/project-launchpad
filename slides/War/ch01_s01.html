Key Concepts (1/2)

When and where was Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr. born?

Boston, March 8, 1841.

What college did Holmes attend, and when did he enroll?

Harvard College, enrolling in 1857.

Which military unit did Holmes join at the start of the Civil War?

The Twentieth Massachusetts Volunteer Infantry, as a first lieutenant.

Where was Holmes wounded for the first time, and what happened?

Ball's Bluff, Virginia (October 21, 1861): shot through the chest; he nearly died.

Where was Holmes wounded for the second time?

Antietam, Maryland (September 17, 1862): shot through the neck.

Why is September 17, 1862 significant in American history?

It was the bloodiest single day in American history (Battle of Antietam).

Key Concepts (2/2)

How did Holmes' father respond to the Antietam wound?

He traveled frantically from Boston to find his wounded son and later recounted the journey in a famous essay.

Where and how was Holmes wounded a third time?

Fredericksburg, Virginia (May 3, 1863, during the Chancellorsville campaign): struck in the heel by a shell fragment.

What rank did Holmes hold when he mustered out of the army?

Brevet colonel, in 1864.

What was Holmes' landmark legal work, and what did it argue?

The Common Law (1881): argued that law is a living body of rules shaped by experience and social needs, not fixed logical propositions.

When was Holmes appointed to the Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, and how long did he serve?

1882; he served twenty years, the last three as Chief Justice.

Who appointed Holmes to the U.S. Supreme Court, and when?

President Theodore Roosevelt in 1902.
