War: A Debate in Primary Sources

Chapter 5, Section 3

The Philosophical Clash Over War

Study Slides

Core Perspectives on War

Glorification (Holmes)

War is a necessary crucible for human character.

Condemnation (Butler)

War is an exploitative racket that serves economic interests.

Middle Ground (Wilson)

War is a terrible but occasionally necessary instrument of policy.

Holmes’ Core Claim: War as a Forge of Character

Virtues Cultivated

Courage, self-sacrifice, discipline, and "faith without certainty."

The Necessity of Battle

Without the test of combat, civilization grows "soft" and loses the qualities that make it worth defending.

Ethos

Holmes’ authority came from his direct experience as a soldier in the Civil War, having been wounded at Antietam and Ball’s Bluff.

Butler’s Core Claim: War as a Machine of Destruction

The Beneficiaries

War enriches a small class of profiteers—corporations selling ammunition, uniforms, and supplies—who never see combat.

The Human Cost

War destroys bodies, minds, and families while the many pay for the profits of the few.

The Purpose of War

Butler concluded that the entire enterprise of war is a scheme where economic interests treat conflict as a business opportunity.

Comparative Analysis: The Question of Scale

Feature. Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr.

Smedley Butler

Primary Focus. The Individual Experience

The Systemic Function

View of the Soldier. A display of moral courage and virtue.

An exploited participant in a scheme.

Outcome of War. Transformation and strengthening of the soul.

Destruction of the body and enrichment of the elite.

Key Metaphor. The Crucible

The Racket

Glossary of Key Terms

Crucible

Literally, a container for melting metals at high temperatures; figuratively, a severe test or trial that transforms or reveals the nature of what passes through it.

Ethos

Personal authority or credibility based on one's character or experience.

Pacifist

A person who believes that war and violence are unjustifiable and refuses to participate in armed conflict.

Profiteer

An individual or corporation that makes an excessive or unfair profit, particularly during times of national hardship like war.

Racket

A fraudulent scheme or dishonest enterprise, often used by Butler to describe war as a business opportunity for the few at the expense of the many.
