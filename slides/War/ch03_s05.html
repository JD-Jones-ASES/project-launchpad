Woodrow Wilson and the Road to World War I

Quiz Question 1

How did the British blockade of Germany differ from German submarine warfare in its diplomatic impact on the United States?

A. The British blockade primarily targeted civilian resources without causing American fatalities, whereas German U-boats killed American citizens.

B. The British blockade was considered legal under international law, while unrestricted submarine warfare was a total violation of maritime codes.

C. Wilson successfully used the British blockade to justify a military alliance with the Allied powers early in the conflict.

D. The British blockade only affected munitions, while German submarines targeted all merchant vessels regardless of cargo.

Hint: Consider the tangible consequences for American lives rather than just the economic impact.

Quiz Question 2

What was the primary strategic calculation made by German generals in early $1917$ regarding the resumption of unrestricted submarine warfare?

A. They believed they could starve Britain into submission before the United States could effectively mobilize for war.

B. They intended to provoke the United States into a war to divert American resources away from the Western Front.

C. They assumed the United States would remain neutral due to the significant financial debt owed to American banks by the Allies.

D. They sought to protect German merchant vessels from the British Royal Navy's blockade in the North Sea.

Hint: Think about the timing of the decision and the intended effect on the island nation of Britain.

Quiz Question 3

In what way did the Zimmermann Telegram transform the American public's perception of the conflict?

A. It shifted the perception of the war from an abstract maritime dispute to a direct territorial threat against the American mainland.

B. It proved that the British had been manipulating American intelligence to force a declaration of war.

C. It confirmed that Mexico had already begun military operations against Texas, New Mexico, and Arizona.

D. It highlighted the economic necessity of protecting American trade routes from German-Mexican naval coalitions.

Hint: Reflect on how the location of the proposed conflict changed the stakes for the average citizen.

Quiz Question 4

Which detail regarding the $Lusitania$ incident illustrates the complexity of 'selective facts' mentioned in the text?

A. The British denied the ship was carrying munitions, a claim that was later proven false by investigations.

B. Germany admitted the attack was a mistake caused by poor visibility and offered immediate reparations.

C. The United States government suppressed evidence that there were no Americans on board to justify entering the war.

D. International law at the time explicitly allowed submarines to sink passenger liners if they were in a declared war zone.

Hint: Look for a discrepancy between the public narrative at the time and the findings of later investigations.

Quiz Question 5

What was the significance of the Sussex Pledge of May $1916$?

A. It was a temporary commitment by Germany to provide warnings before sinking merchant ships.

B. It officially established a formal military alliance between the United States and Great Britain.

C. It marked the moment Wilson decided that neutrality was no longer a viable path for the nation.

D. It was a British promise to end the naval blockade of Germany in exchange for American financial support.

Hint: Recall the conditions under which submarines were traditionally required to operate before attacking.

Quiz Question 6

Why did critics argue that American neutrality was 'neutral in name only' by $1917$?

A. American industry and banks were heavily invested in the success of the Allies through trade and loans.

B. Wilson had already signed a secret treaty with Britain to enter the war if the stalemate on the Western Front continued.

C. The United States had begun sending volunteer troops to the Western Front under the guise of 'armed neutrality.'

D. The German government had officially blacklisted all American firms from trading with European nations.

Hint: Focus on the financial and industrial relationships that developed during the early years of the war.
