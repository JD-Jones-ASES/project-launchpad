Crucible / Forge

Holmes' central metaphor for what war does — it tests and reveals character under extreme pressure.

Gilded Age

The era of American history Holmes was responding to — a period of material prosperity and, in his view, moral softness.

Butler: War as a Machine of Destruction

Racket

Butler's central claim — war is a scheme in which the few profit and the many pay.

Systemic exploitation

The war system uses genuine soldier courage for economic ends that benefit corporations, not the soldiers themselves.

Can Both Be Right?

Holmes focuses on persons

For the individual soldier in the moment of decision, the virtues he describes are real. Courage is real. Self-sacrifice is real.

Butler focuses on systems

Whatever happens to the individual soldier's soul, the machinery of war serves economic interests. Corporations profit. Politicians gain power.

Key Concepts for Review

Individual vs. systemic analysis

The difference between asking "what does war do to a person?" and "what does war do to a society or economy?"

Ethos and lived experience

Both Holmes and Butler derive their authority from direct combat experience, making their disagreement philosophically honest even if irresolvable.
