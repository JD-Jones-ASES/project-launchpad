Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Audience Analysis

The practice of identifying who a speaker is addressing and evaluating how those listeners shape the structure and tone of the argument.

Bearing Witness

A rhetorical mode, specifically attributed to Remarque, where the author provides testimony of an experience to allow the reader to form their own judgment rather than providing an explicit thesis.

Explicit Argument

A rhetorical approach that uses a clear thesis and marshals specific evidence to persuade an audience.

Implicit Argument

An argument that is not stated directly but is understood through narrative, testimony, or the presentation of an experience.

Legalistic Phrasing

The use of careful, precise, and formal language often found in presidential or official documents to convey gravity and avoid the appearance of recklessness.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Oration

A formal speech intended for a ceremonial occasion, often characterized by classical allusions and a performance-oriented style.

Rhetorical Situation Analysis

The study of the interconnected circumstances of a text’s creation, specifically its audience, occasion, and purpose.

The Deserter

A 1917 anti-war cartoon by Boardman Robinson depicting a firing squad taking aim at Christ, published the same year Wilson asked for a declaration of war.
