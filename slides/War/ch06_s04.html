Key Terms (1/2)

Logos

The use of logic and evidence to support a claim; the backbone of an argument.

Inductive generalization

Reasoning from specific observations to a general principle.

Empirical analysis

Drawing conclusions from data that can, in principle, be independently verified.

Deductive reasoning

Drawing a conclusion from premises; if premises are true and the structure is valid, the conclusion follows.

Experiential testimony

Evidence based on first-hand sensory experience; powerful but unable to resolve policy disputes on its own.

Key Terms (2/2)

Correlation vs. causation

The logical error of concluding that because two things occur together, one caused the other.
