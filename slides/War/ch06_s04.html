Persuasion vs. Manipulation

Emotional Manipulation

Occurs when a speaker uses emotion to bypass rational judgment, forcing the audience to stop thinking critically. This involves distorting facts or using emotion as a total substitute for evidence.

Legitimate Persuasion

Acknowledges that emotions are a necessary part of human reasoning. It uses pathos to illuminate a genuine truth and ensures the emotional reaction is proportional to the evidence provided.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Elevation

An emotional register aimed at making an audience feel inspired or lifted toward noble, transcendent concerns; often used in ceremonial oratory.

Empathic Horror

The emotional effect produced by unmediated descriptions of suffering, intended to make the reader feel as though they are experiencing a nightmare.

Emotional Manipulation

The use of pathos to bypass critical thinking and rational judgment, often by substituting emotion for evidence.

Outrage

A specific emotional architecture designed to provoke anger at a perceived injustice or "con game."

Pathos

The rhetorical appeal to the audience’s emotions; the element of a speech or text that answers "Why should I care?"

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Solemn Resolve

The emotional state Wilson seeks to induce, characterized by a heavy heart but a firm commitment to a necessary, albeit painful, duty.

Tragic Hero Logic

The emotional framework where an individual acknowledges the suffering their actions will cause but proceeds because they believe it is morally required.

Unmediated Experience

A rhetorical strategy of "showing" raw reality without imposing an explicit interpretation, argument, or call to action.
