Key Terms

Gilded Age

The late nineteenth-century era of rapid industrial growth and concentrated wealth in America

Decoration Day

The original name for Memorial Day

Grand Army of the Republic

The major veterans' organization for Union soldiers after the Civil War

Periodic sentence

Holmes' characteristic sentence style — long, accumulating clauses that delay the main point for maximum impact

Key Concepts (1/2)

What year was Holmes' 'The Soldier's Faith' delivered, and how many years had passed since Appomattox?

1895; thirty years had passed since Lee's surrender.

What was Memorial Day originally called, and how did it begin?

Decoration Day; it began after the Civil War as a day to honor Union dead by decorating their graves with flowers.

What veterans' organization helped organize Memorial Day ceremonies in the 1890s?

The Grand Army of the Republic.

On what exact date did Holmes deliver 'The Soldier's Faith'?

May 30, 1895.

Who organized the gathering at which Holmes spoke?

The Harvard graduating class.

Who made up Holmes' audience for the speech?

Harvard students and faculty, Boston civic leaders, and surviving veterans of the Civil War.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What term describes the era of American history in 1895?

The Gilded Age.

Name three industrialists Holmes mentions as symbols of the new wealth.

Andrew Carnegie, John D. Rockefeller, and Cornelius Vanderbilt.

What shift in national values did Holmes observe in Gilded Age America?

A shift from honor to profit, and from duty to comfort; commerce had replaced martial virtue as the ruling value.

What did Holmes fear a society devoted entirely to material comfort would lose?

The capacity for sacrifice and the willingness to risk everything for something larger than oneself.

Is 'The Soldier's Faith' primarily a nostalgic tribute to fallen soldiers?

No — it is also a carefully constructed argument that martial virtues are indispensable to a healthy civilization.

What rhetorical technique does the text note Holmes uses extensively in the speech?

The periodic sentence — long, rolling sentences that build toward emotional climaxes, delaying the main point until the end.
