Key Terms (1/2)

Ethos

The credibility, character, and authority a speaker brings to an argument.

Appeal to authority

Drawing persuasive force from credentials, experience, or position rather than logic alone — legitimate when relevant, problematic when it substitutes for evidence.

Ethos of the volunteer

Authority from chosen sacrifice.

Ethos of the insider

Authority from insider betrayal of an institution.

Ethos of the statesman

Authority from positional power combined with reluctance.

Key Terms (2/2)

Ethos of the witness

Authority from representative, ordinary experience.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is ethos in rhetoric?

The credibility, character, and authority a speaker brings to the podium — the answer to the silent question every audience asks: 'Why should I listen to you?'
