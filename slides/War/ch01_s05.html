Study Slides

The Periodic Sentence

• Stacked conditional clauses: "if you have... if you have... if you have..."

• Each clause adds a new sensory detail of combat, escalating the stakes

• From watching the enemy to feeling the burst of shell to slipping on a dead man's body

• The sentence does not resolve until the final clause: "you know that there is such a thing as the faith I spoke of."

The Three Classical Appeals

Key Terms

Periodic sentence

A sentence whose main clause is withheld until the end, building suspense and momentum

Victorian grand style

Late nineteenth-century oratorical tradition marked by complexity, elevation, and literary allusion

Ethos / Pathos / Logos

The three classical appeals (credibility, emotion, logic)

Elegy

A mournful, meditative poem or speech honoring the dead
