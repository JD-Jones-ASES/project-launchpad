What was Wilson's initial position when war broke out in Europe in August 1914?

He declared American neutrality, believing the European conflict was a quarrel among empires that America had no business joining.

On what slogan did Wilson win re-election in 1916, and what does it reveal about his public position on the war?

'He Kept Us Out of War.' It shows Wilson ran successfully as a peace candidate just months before asking Congress to declare war.

How many people died when the Lusitania was sunk in May 1915, including how many Americans?

1,198 people died in total, including 128 Americans.

How much did American banks lend to Britain and France by 1917, and which firm led those loans?

Over $2 billion, led by J.P. Morgan & Company. American factories were also supplying the Allies with munitions, food, and raw materials on a vast scale.
