Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Why did President Wilson call Congress into an extraordinary session on April 2, 1917?

Wilson called the session because serious choices of policy regarding the war had to be made immediately—responsibilities he felt were neither right nor constitutionally permissible for him to handle alone.

Q: What promise did the German government break regarding submarine warfare?

Q: Why does Wilson argue that property damage is secondary to the loss of life?

Wilson states that while the loss of property is immense and serious, "property can be paid for; the lives of peaceful and innocent people cannot be."

Q: What was Wilson’s critique of "armed neutrality"?

Q: How does Wilson describe the recent political changes in Russia?

He describes them as "wonderful and heartening," claiming Russia has always been "democratic at heart" and has now shaken off its autocracy to become a "fit partner for a League of Honor."

Q: What specific evidence does Wilson provide of the German government's hostile intent toward the U.S. before the formal declaration of war?

He cites the filling of American communities and government offices with spies, criminal intrigues against national unity and industry, and the intercepted note to the German Minister at Mexico City.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: How does Wilson propose to fund the war effort?

He suggests that the war should be sustained as much as possible by "well conceived taxation" rather than entirely on borrowed money, in order to protect the people from the "hardships and evils" of inflation.
