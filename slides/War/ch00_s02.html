I. Profiles of the Four Voices

Figure. Background & Experience

Core Argument on War

Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr.. Civil War veteran (Union Army); US Supreme Court Jurist; known as "The Great Dissenter."

War is a "crucible" that develops virtues—courage and self-sacrifice—that a commercial society cannot produce.

Smedley Darlington Butler. Career Marine; two-time Medal of Honor recipient; exposed the "Business Plot" of 1933.

War is a "racket" where insiders profit while soldiers suffer. The profit motive must be removed from warfare.

Woodrow Wilson. Scholar and 28th US President; progressive reformer; champion of the League of Nations.

War is a necessary tool to protect human rights and make the world "safe for democracy."

Erich Maria Remarque. WWI German Army veteran; author of *All Quiet on the Western Front*; target of Nazi censorship.

War is a physical and psychological horror that defies noble abstractions; it is best understood through the lived reality of the soldier.

V. Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Business Plot (1933)

An alleged conspiracy by wealthy industrialists to overthrow the United States government, exposed by Smedley Butler.

Crucible

A severe test or trial. Holmes uses this term to describe how war "forges" human virtues like courage and devotion.

League of Nations

An international organization championed by Woodrow Wilson intended to promote democracy and international cooperation.

Racket

A fraudulent scheme or business. Butler uses this term to describe the military-industrial system as a way for insiders to make a profit.

The Soldier’s Faith

A term used by Holmes to describe the highest expression of human character, characterized by devotion to a cause greater than oneself.

V. Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Trench Warfare

A form of combat characterized by the "mud, terror, and senseless death" described by Remarque in his depictions of the Western Front.

War for Democracy

The central justification used by Woodrow Wilson to explain American intervention in World War I, asserting that American power is necessary to ensure democratic governance globally.
