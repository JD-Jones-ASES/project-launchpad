Erich Maria Remarque and the Reality of Trench Warfare

Study Slides

The Role of "Chance"

The Indifference of Fate

A soldier can be killed in a "bomb-proof" dugout or survive hours of bombardment in the open.

The "Cage"

The front is described as a cage where soldiers must fearfully await whatever happens, living in a "network of arching shells."

Statistical Survival

As the text notes, "No soldier outlives a thousand chances," yet every soldier must trust their luck to remain sane.

The Limits of Rhetorical Frameworks

Oliver Wendell Holmes Jr.

Views war as a "school of virtue" and a test of "soldier’s faith."

Woodrow Wilson

Frames war as a necessary struggle to make the "world safe for democracy."

Smedley Butler

Analyzes war as a "racket" where ordinary men die for corporate profit.

Q: How did the Nazi party respond to Remarque’s work in 1933?

The Nazis burned Remarque's books and revoked his German citizenship, eventually forcing him into exile in Switzerland and the United States. They viewed his "truth-telling" about the war as a crime against the state.

Q: What do the new coffins stacked against the schoolhouse signify to the soldiers?

The soldiers recognize that the high volume of brand-new coffins is "preparation for the offensive." Despite the dark jests of some men, they realize the coffins are intended for them.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: What triggered the "fit" of the recruit in the dugout?

The recruit suffered from claustrophobia, triggered by the intense, sustained bombardment while confined in a deep dugout. The feeling of "suffocating" led to a psychological breakdown where he attempted to rush out into the deadly shelling.
