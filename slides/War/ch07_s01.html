Perspectives on the Purpose and Reality of War

Study Slides

The Four Primary Voices

Voice. Key Work/Perspective

Primary Focus

Oliver Wendell Holmes. Martial Virtue

A moral argument celebrating honor, sacrifice, and the virtues of the soldier.

Smedley Butler. Economic Indictment

An economic argument focusing on profiteering and those who benefit financially from conflict.

Woodrow Wilson. Democratic Necessity

A hybrid argument (moral and economic) focused on the necessity of war to preserve or spread democracy.

Erich Maria Remarque. Human Reality

The "voice from the trench" that reports the physical and psychological cost to the individual soldier.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: How many Americans are commemorated on the Vietnam Veterans Memorial?

Over 58,000 Americans who died in the conflict have their names etched into the memorial’s black granite.

Q: According to the text, why is it not a "failure of argument" that different authors see war differently?

It is not a failure because war is too immense and terrible to be captured by any single frame of analysis; each author identifies a different aspect of the truth.

Q: What is the primary difference between the arguments of Holmes and Butler?

Holmes makes a moral argument centered on martial virtue and sacrifice, whereas Butler makes an economic argument centered on the indictment of war profiteering.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What is the "voice of the soldier in the trench" according to the text?

It is the voice that does not argue for a cause but simply reports the reality of what it sees—the direct human cost of the conflict.

Q: How does Remarque describe the impact of war on his generation?

He describes his generation as no longer being "youth," but rather as individuals fleeing from themselves and their lives, forced to "shoot to pieces" a world they had just begun to love at age eighteen.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Economic Argument

An argument based on calculations of cost and benefit, or an analysis of who profits financially from a specific action.

Frame of Analysis

A specific perspective or set of criteria used to understand and evaluate a complex subject.

Martial Virtue

The qualities of bravery, honor, and self-sacrifice associated with military service and combat.

Moral Argument

An argument grounded in claims about right and wrong, or justice and injustice, rather than financial or practical considerations.

Omitted Evidence

Information or data points that are left out of an argument, either intentionally or unintentionally, which might otherwise challenge the speaker's conclusion.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Vietnam Veterans Memorial

A monument in Washington, D.C., made of polished black granite, honoring the Americans who died in the Vietnam War.
