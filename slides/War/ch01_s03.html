Key Concepts (1/2)

With what image does Holmes open the speech?

A blind man playing a flute on Washington Street — hoping his music reaches someone in the passing crowd.

What is the quote Holmes cites about Vanderbilt to illustrate the worship of wealth?

'Where Vanderbilt sits, there is the head of the table. I teach my son to be rich.'

What does Holmes say has replaced honor as the measure of a man?

Wealth; commerce has become the great power and the aspiration of the world.

What does Holmes mean by 'cosmopolitanism' as he uses the term?

A rootless, self-seeking search for a place where the most enjoyment may be had at the least cost — not genuine global community.

What does Holmes say the modern age has concluded about evil?

That evil means pain, and there is a growing revolt against pain in all its forms.

How has science affected religion, according to Holmes?

It has shaken established religion and reduced the world to 'one vast network of vibrations,' fading the divine meaning from life.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is Holmes' central claim about the soldier's faith?

'The faith is true and adorable which leads a soldier to throw away his life in obedience to a blindly accepted duty, in a cause which he little understands.'

What is 'spherical case-shot'?

A type of artillery ammunition (shrapnel shell) that exploded in the air, scattering metal balls over a wide area.

What happened at the dead angle of Spotsylvania?

Soldiers fought on two sides of an earthwork for twenty-four hours; in the morning the dead and dying lay piled six deep.

What ancient work does Holmes quote with the words 'Let him who may, gain honor ere death. That is best for a warrior when he is dead'?

Beowulf.

Whose voice does Holmes quote with the lines beginning 'Not of the sunlight, / Not of the moonlight'?

Tennyson, in the voice of the dying Merlin ('Follow the Gleam').

What did Holmes mean by 'the divine folly of honor'?

The irrational but noble quality that makes civilization worthwhile — the willingness to prize honor over material comfort or survival.
