Key Terms

Propaganda

Communication designed to influence attitudes and behavior rather than to inform or reason; subordinates truth to effect.

False dilemma

Presenting only two options when more actually exist; creates urgency by foreclosing alternatives.

Straw man

Misrepresenting an opponent's position to make it easier to attack.

Persuasion vs. propaganda spectrum

The recognition that the distinction is a matter of degree and intent, not a clean binary division.

Zimmermann Telegram

The real German diplomatic communication offering Mexico an alliance against the United States — one of the genuine crises underlying Wilson's argument.
