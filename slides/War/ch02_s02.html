Key Terms

World War Adjusted Compensation Act (1924)

— Law promising WWI veterans a cash bonus, not payable until 1945.

Bonus Expeditionary Force

— The organization of WWI veterans who marched on Washington in 1932 to demand early bonus payment.

Business Plot

— The alleged 1933 conspiracy by wealthy businessmen to install a fascist-style government using Butler as figurehead.

McCormack-Dickstein Committee

— The congressional committee that investigated Butler's testimony; it confirmed the credibility of his claims.

Pamphlet

— A short, unbound publication devoted to a single argument, rewarding brevity and urgency over scholarly qualification.

Key Concepts (1/2)

When did Butler retire, and what transformation followed within a few years?

He retired in 1931; within a few years he transformed from celebrated warrior to outspoken antiwar crusader.

What was the World War Adjusted Compensation Act of 1924?

A law promising World War I veterans a cash bonus, but the money would not be paid until 1945.

What was the Bonus Expeditionary Force?

The name adopted by tens of thousands of WWI veterans and their families who marched on Washington in 1932 to demand early payment of their promised bonus.

Who commanded the forces that dispersed the Bonus Army, and who assisted?

General Douglas MacArthur commanded, with Major Dwight Eisenhower as his aide and Major George Patton commanding the cavalry.

Why did the conspirators choose Butler specifically?

Because of his fame and influence among veterans — they believed he could mobilize 500,000 veterans into a private army to seize control of Washington.

What did Butler do when approached by the Business Plot conspirators?

He reported the conspiracy to Congress — exactly what the plotters did not expect.

What was the McCormack-Dickstein Committee's finding?

It confirmed that Butler's testimony was credible, but the affair was quickly buried and no one was prosecuted.

Why did major newspapers largely dismiss the Business Plot story?

Many of them were owned by the same class of businessmen Butler accused.
