Chapter 6, Section 3

Pathos: Where Does Each Writer Go for Emotion?

Study Slides

Key Terms (1/2)

Pathos

The rhetorical appeal to emotion; the element of argument that answers "Why should I care?"

Elevation

An emotional register aimed at inspiring reverence and aspiration toward something noble.

Empathic horror

The emotional state produced by Remarque's unmediated depictions — the feeling of being inside a nightmare.

Emotional manipulation

Using pathos to bypass rational judgment, substituting emotion for evidence.

Solemn resolve

The emotional state Wilson seeks — acknowledging suffering while insisting duty demands action.

Key Terms (2/2)

Righteous anger

Butler's target emotion — fury at injustice that motivates political action rather than passive sympathy.
