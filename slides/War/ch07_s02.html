Perspectives on War—Racket or Crusade?

Study Slides

The Four Perspectives on War

Author. Core Argument

Perspective Summary

Oliver Wendell Holmes. War ennobles.

War is a "crucible of character." The willingness to die for a cause greater than oneself represents the highest human virtue.

Smedley Butler. War is a racket.

War is an economic scheme organized by the powerful to enrich themselves while ordinary people pay with their lives.

Woodrow Wilson. War is a moral necessity.

War is sometimes required to defend democracy and civilization against autocracy and external threats.

Erich Maria Remarque. War destroys.

The reality of industrial warfare (shelling and death) mocks every abstraction or argument offered in its defense.

Levels of Abstraction (Framing)

Personal Experience (Holmes)

Focuses on the impact of war on the individual soul and personal virtue.

Economic System (Butler)

Focuses on the financial motivations—who pays for the war and who profits from it.

Political Necessity (Wilson)

Focuses on the macro-level implications for democracy, security, and international order.

The Principles of Synthesis

Simultaneous Truths

Acknowledging that multiple, seemingly contradictory viewpoints can be true at once (e.g., war can ennoble a soldier while simultaneously enriching a profiteer).

Critical Evaluation

Using rhetorical tools (ethos, pathos, and logos) to distinguish evidence from mere assertion.

Identifying Omissions

Asking "What has been left out?" to determine what a specific frame is concealing.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Abstraction

A conceptual or theoretical way of framing an issue, often removed from concrete, physical realities.

Autocracy

A system of government by one person with absolute power; the opposite of the democracy Wilson sought to defend.

Crucible

A situation of severe trial, or in Holmes's context, a place where different elements interact to produce something new and virtuous.

Ethos

A rhetorical appeal based on the character or credibility of the speaker.

Logos

A rhetorical appeal based on logic, reason, and evidence.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pathos

A rhetorical appeal to the emotions of the audience.

Racket

A fraudulent or dishonest scheme or business, typically one that involves bribery or intimidation; the term Butler used to describe war.

Synthesis

The process of combining multiple perspectives or arguments into a single, coherent analysis that accounts for the strengths of each side.

Unrestricted Submarine Warfare

A type of naval warfare used by Germany that Wilson cited as a threat to international order and American security.

Zimmermann Telegram

A specific historical document cited by Wilson as evidence of a threat to the United States, justifying the necessity of war.
