Smedley Butler and the Origins of "War Is a Racket"

Study Slides

I. Biography and Military Profile (1/2)

Life Span

1881–1940.

Origin

Born in West Chester, Pennsylvania, to a prominent Quaker family.

Family Influence

His father, Thomas S. Butler, was a member of the United States House of Representatives for 31 years and served as the chair of the House Naval Affairs Committee.

Early Enlistment

Despite his Quaker pacifist roots, Butler lied about his age at 16 to enlist in the Marine Corps during the Spanish-American War in 1898.

Rank

Major General, United States Marine Corps (one of the youngest in history).

I. Biography and Military Profile (2/2)

Medal of Honor

One of only nineteen men to receive the Medal of Honor twice.

Additional Awards

Marine Corps Brevet Medal, Haitian Médaille Militaire, and the Distinguished Service Medal.

The Combatant vs. The Intellectual

Butler was not a pacifist intellectual or a politician; he was a man who had spent three decades in uniform, mostly in combat.

VI. Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Banana Wars

A series of U.S. military interventions in Latin America and the Caribbean (1898–1934) aimed at protecting the commercial interests of American corporations.

Ethos

A rhetorical appeal based on the character, credibility, or authority of the speaker.

Gendarmerie d'Ha

The Haitian national police force, organized and commanded by U.S. Marine officers during the American occupation of Haiti.

Major General

The high-ranking military position Butler achieved during his thirty-three years in the Marine Corps.

Muscle Man for Big Business

A phrase used by Butler to describe his role in enforcing American commercial interests abroad via military force.

VI. Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Quaker

A religious group known for a deep tradition of pacifism; the community into which Smedley Butler was born.

United Fruit Company

A major American corporation whose commercial interests were frequently protected by military interventions in Latin America.
