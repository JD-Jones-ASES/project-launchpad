Key Concepts (2/2)

What were typical peacetime vs. wartime profit percentages, according to Butler?

Normal profits: 6, 8, 10, sometimes 12%. Wartime profits: 20, 60, 100, 300, even 1,800% — 'the sky is the limit.'

What were du Pont's average annual earnings before and during the war?

1910–1914: $6,000,000/year. 1914–1918: $58,000,000/year — an increase of more than 950%.

What were Bethlehem Steel's average annual earnings before and during the war?

1910–1914: $6,000,000/year. 1914–1918: $49,000,000/year.

What were United States Steel's average annual earnings before and during the war?

1910–1914: $105,000,000/year. 1914–1918: $240,000,000/year.

What was International Nickel Company's profit increase during the war?

From $4,000,000/year to $73,000,000/year — an increase of more than 1,700%.
