Variables and Algebraic Expressions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Variables

Letters or symbols that stand for unknown or changeable numbers. They are crucial for representing quantities whose values can vary.

Algebraic Expressions

Combinations of numbers, variables, and mathematical operations (like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) that do not include an equal sign.

Terms

The individual components within an algebraic expression that are separated by addition (+) or subtraction (-) signs. A term can be a number, a variable, or a product of numbers and variables.

Coefficients

The numerical factor that is multiplied by a variable in a term. If a variable appears alone (e.g., `x`), its coefficient is understood to be 1.

Constants

Terms in an algebraic expression that consist only of a number and do not contain any variables. Their value remains fixed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Expression vs. Equation

An expression is a mathematical phrase without an equal sign, while an equation is a mathematical sentence that includes an equal sign, indicating that two expressions are equivalent.

Translating Words to Algebra

The ability to convert verbal descriptions of situations into algebraic expressions is a core skill in problem-solving.

Important Terms (1/2)

Variable

A letter or symbol (e.g., $x, y, n, t$) that represents an unknown or changeable numerical value.

Algebraic Expression

A mathematical phrase formed by combining numbers, variables, and operation symbols (e.g., $3x + 5$, $7y$).

Term

A single number, a single variable, or a product of numbers and variables within an expression. Terms are separated by addition or subtraction signs.

Coefficient

The numerical factor that directly multiplies a variable in a term (e.g., in $5x$, the coefficient is $5$).

Constant

A term in an algebraic expression that consists only of a number and does not contain a variable (e.g., in $5x + 3$, the constant is $3$).

Important Terms (2/2)

Expression

A mathematical phrase that does not contain an equal sign (e.g., $2x + 7$).

Equation

A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal by using an equal sign (e.g., $2x + 7 = 15$).

What is a Variable?

• A variable is a placeholder for a number.

• It's called "variable" because the value it represents can vary or change depending on the situation.

• Common variables used are $x, y, n, t$, but any letter or symbol can be used.

Understanding Algebraic Expressions

• An algebraic expression is built by combining numbers, variables, and mathematical operations (+, -, $\times$, $\div$).

• Examples: $3x + 5$, $n4 - 2$, $7y$.

• Notice that algebraic expressions do not have an equal sign.

Components of an Algebraic Expression

• The coefficient of the variable term ($4n$) is $4$.

Important Conventions and Cautions

Order of Multiplication

When a number is multiplied by a variable, the number (coefficient) is conventionally written first. Write $5x$, not $x5$. Both have the same value, but $5x$ is standard.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary difference between arithmetic and algebra regarding the types of symbols used?

Arithmetic primarily works with numbers, while algebra also incorporates letters (variables) to represent unknown or changeable numerical values.

Define 'variable' in the context of algebra.

A variable is a letter or symbol that represents an unknown or changeable numerical value.

Give four common letters used as variables in algebra.

$x$, $y$, $n$, and $t$.

What is an algebraic expression?

An algebraic expression is a combination of numbers, variables, and operations (such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division).

Provide two examples of an algebraic expression.

$3x + 5$ and $7y$.

How is a 'term' defined within an algebraic expression?

A term is a single number, a single variable, or a number multiplied by one or more variables.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How are terms typically separated within an algebraic expression?

Terms in an expression are separated by + or - signs.

Identify the terms in the expression $5x + 3$.

The terms are $5x$ and $3$.

What is a 'coefficient' in an algebraic term?

A coefficient is the numerical factor multiplied by a variable in a term.

What is the coefficient in the term $5x$?

The coefficient is $5$.

If a variable term like 'x' has no number written in front of it, what is its understood coefficient?

The coefficient is understood to be $1$.

Define 'constant' in the context of an algebraic expression.

A constant is a term that has no variable, meaning its value never changes.
