Chapter 8, Section 1: Variables and Algebraic Expressions Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Variables

Letters (like $x, y, n, t$) or symbols that stand for unknown or changeable numerical values. In algebra, these variables allow us to represent general relationships.

Algebraic Expressions

Combinations of numbers, variables, and mathematical operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division). They do not contain an equal sign.

Terms

Coefficients

The numerical factor that multiplies a variable in a term. For example, in $5x$, $5$ is the coefficient. If a variable stands alone (like $x$), its coefficient is understood to be $1$.

Constants

Terms in an algebraic expression that consist of only a number and do not contain any variables. Their value remains fixed.

Writing Algebraic Expressions

The ability to translate verbal descriptions or word problems into symbolic algebraic forms using variables and operations.

Important Terms

Variable

A letter or symbol that represents an unknown or changeable numerical value.

Algebraic Expression

A mathematical phrase combining numbers, variables, and operations, without an equal sign.

Term

A single number, a single variable, or a product of numbers and variables within an expression, separated by addition or subtraction signs.

Coefficient

The numerical factor that is multiplied by a variable in a term.

Constant

A term in an algebraic expression that consists of only a number and does not contain a variable.
