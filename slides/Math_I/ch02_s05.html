Key Concepts

Order of Operations (PEMDAS) for Integers

The rules for evaluating expressions remain the same for integers as for whole numbers.

Tracking Signs

A critical aspect when working with integers, as a single sign error can lead to a completely wrong answer. Always pay close attention to whether a number is positive or negative.

Absolute-Value Bars

Important Terms

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

A universally accepted set of rules that dictates the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed to ensure a consistent result. The acronym stands for Parentheses, Exponents, Mul...

Integers

The set of whole numbers (0, 1, 2, 3, ...) and their opposites (-1, -2, -3, ...). They include positive numbers, negative numbers, and zero.

Base (of an exponent)

In an expression like $a^n$, 'a' is the base. It is the number or variable that is multiplied by itself 'n' times.

A measure that describes how cold the weather feels to humans, combining the actual air temperature with the cooling effect of wind.

Exponents with Negative Bases: A Crucial Distinction

`(-a)^n` (Parentheses around the negative base)

The entire value `(-a)` is the base that is multiplied by itself `n` times.

`-a^n` (No parentheses around the negative base)

Only the number `a` is the base. The exponent applies only to `a`, and the negative sign is applied after the exponentiation. It's equivalent to `-(a^n)`.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the fundamental rules for the order of operations in mathematics?

PEMDAS: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division (left to right), Addition/Subtraction (left to right).

How do the order of operations rules apply when working with integers?

The same PEMDAS rules apply, but you must keep careful track of signs at every step.

What does the 'P' in PEMDAS stand for, and what does it include?

'P' stands for Parentheses, which includes all grouping symbols like brackets and braces.

What does the 'E' in PEMDAS stand for?

'E' stands for Exponents.

What operations are covered by 'MD' in PEMDAS, and how are they performed?

'MD' covers Multiplication and Division. These operations are performed from left to right.

What operations are covered by 'AS' in PEMDAS, and how are they performed?

'AS' covers Addition and Subtraction. These operations are performed from left to right.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the context of order of operations, how do absolute-value bars function?

Absolute-value bars act as grouping symbols. You must simplify the expression inside the bars first, then take the absolute value.

Evaluate the expression: -3 + 4 (-2).

Multiply first: -3 + (-8). Then add: -11.

Evaluate the expression: 5 - 6 (-3).

Multiply first: 5 - (-18). Then subtract (add the opposite): 5 + 18 = 23.

How should you approach an expression that contains nested grouping symbols?

Work from the innermost grouping symbols outward, applying PEMDAS at each level.

Evaluate the expression: 2[10 - 3(1 - 4)].

2[10 - 3(-3)] = 2[10 - (-9)] = 2[10 + 9] = 2[19] = 38.

Evaluate the expression: -2[4 + 3(-5)].

-2[4 + (-15)] = -2[-11] = 22.
