Chapter 1, Section 1: Place Value, Rounding, and Estimation Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Place Value System

Our number system uses ten digits (0-9) where the position of each digit gives it a specific value. Each place value is ten times greater than the place to its immediate right.

Number Forms

Whole numbers can be expressed in three primary ways: Standard Form (digits), Expanded Form (sum of place values), and Word Form (written out in words).

Rounding

This process replaces an exact number with a simpler, nearby number, providing an approximation. It's done by identifying a "rounding place" and adjusting based on the digit to its right.

Estimation

The act of finding an approximate answer, often used for quick mental checks or when an exact answer isn't necessary. Rounding is a common method for estimation.

Front-End Estimation

A specific estimation technique that involves using only the leftmost (leading) digit of each number and replacing all other digits with zeros before performing an operation.

Important Terms (1/2)

Place Value

The value a digit holds based on its position within a number (e.g., in 47, the 7 is in the ones place, and the 4 is in the tens place).

Standard Form

The usual way of writing a number using digits (e.g., 5,304).

Expanded Form

A way of writing a number that shows the sum of the values of its individual digits (e.g., 5,000 + 300 + 4 for 5,304).

Word Form

A way of writing a number using words (e.g., five thousand three hundred four).

Rounding

The process of approximating a number by replacing it with a nearby number that is easier to work with, usually ending in zeros.

Important Terms (2/2)

Estimation

The process of finding an approximate answer to a calculation rather than an exact one.

Front-End Estimation

An estimation method where you only consider the leading (leftmost) digit of each number, replacing all other digits with zeros, and then perform the operation.

Standard, Expanded, and Word Form

Standard Form

This is the common numerical representation, such as $5,304$.

Expanded Form

This breaks down the number into the sum of the values of its digits. For $5,304$, the expanded form is $5,000 + 300 + 4$.

Word Form

This writes the number out entirely in words. For $5,304$, it is "five thousand three hundred four."
