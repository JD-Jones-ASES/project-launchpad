Dividing Mixed Numbers Study Guide

Key Concepts

Convert to Improper Fractions First

Before performing any division or finding a reciprocal, all mixed numbers *must* be converted into improper fractions. This is a crucial first step to avoid errors.

Division as Multiplication by the Reciprocal

Dividing fractions (or improper fractions) is equivalent to multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second fraction (the divisor).

Important Terms

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $3\,12$).

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $72$).

Reciprocal

A fraction obtained by inverting another fraction (swapping the numerator and denominator). For example, the reciprocal of $78$ is $87$.

Divisor

The number by which another number is divided. In $a \div b$, $b$ is the divisor.

Cross Cancel

A technique used in fraction multiplication to simplify before multiplying. If a numerator and a denominator share a common factor, you can divide both by that factor.
