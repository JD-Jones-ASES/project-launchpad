Dividing Mixed Numbers

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Convert to Improper Fractions First

Before performing any division or finding a reciprocal, all mixed numbers must be converted into their equivalent improper fraction form.

Division as Multiplication by Reciprocal

The operation of dividing fractions (and thus improper fractions) is transformed into multiplying the first fraction by the reciprocal of the divisor.

Cross Cancellation

Look for common factors between numerators and denominators before multiplying to simplify calculations and reduce the final fraction.

Simplification

Always simplify the resulting fraction, converting it back to a mixed number if it's an improper fraction, unless a specific form is required (e.g., in word problems).

Word Problem Context

When solving word problems, pay attention to whether the question asks for "full" items, which may require rounding down the final answer, or an exact numerical value.

Important Terms (1/2)

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $3\frac{1}{2}$).

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $\frac{7}{2}$).

Reciprocal

A pair of numbers whose product is 1. To find the reciprocal of a fraction, flip the numerator and denominator (e.g., the reciprocal of $\frac{a}{b}$ is $\frac{b}{a}$).

Divisor

The number by which another number is divided (in $a \div b$, $b$ is the divisor).

Cross Cancel

A simplification technique used in multiplication of fractions where common factors are divided out from a numerator and an opposite denominator before multiplying.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simplify

To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms or convert an improper fraction to a mixed number.

Summary of Dividing Mixed Numbers

• Always convert all mixed numbers to improper fractions first.

• Change the division operation to multiplication by the reciprocal of the divisor.

• Utilize cross cancellation to simplify calculations.

• Multiply numerators and denominators.

• Simplify the final fraction, converting back to a mixed number if appropriate.

• For word problems, interpret the answer carefully based on what the question is asking (e.g., "how many full bags").

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the very first step when dividing mixed numbers?

Convert each mixed number to an improper fraction.

After converting mixed numbers to improper fractions, what is the next step in the division process?

Rewrite the division as multiplication by the reciprocal of the divisor.

List the complete sequence of steps for dividing mixed numbers.

1. Convert each mixed number to an improper fraction. 2. Rewrite division as multiplication by the reciprocal of the divisor. 3. Cross cancel common factors....

What crucial mistake should you avoid when dealing with reciprocals of mixed numbers?

Do not try to find the reciprocal of a mixed number directly. Always convert to an improper fraction first, then find the reciprocal.

Convert the mixed number $3\,12$ to an improper fraction.

$72$

If you are dividing $3\,12 \div 78$, what multiplication problem would you set up after the initial conversion?

$72 \times 87$

Key Concepts (2/2)

Solve: $3\,12 \div 78$

$3\,12 \div 78 = 72 \div 78 = 72 \times 87 = 172 \times 817 = 82 = 4$

Convert both $4\,12$ and $1\,14$ to improper fractions.

$4\,12 = 92$, $1\,14 = 54$

To divide $4\,12 \div 1\,14$, what multiplication problem would you set up after converting both numbers?

$92 \times 45$

Solve: $4\,12 \div 1\,14$

$4\,12 \div 1\,14 = 92 \div 54 = 92 \times 45 = 912 \times 245 = 185 = 3\,35$

Convert $2\,23$ and $1\,13$ to improper fractions.

$2\,23 = 83$, $1\,13 = 43$

Solve: $2\,23 \div 1\,13$

$83 \div 43 = 83 \times 34 = 2412 = 2$
