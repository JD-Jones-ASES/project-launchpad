Adding/Subtracting Like Whole Numbers

Once decimal points are aligned and zeros are annexed, you perform the addition or subtraction operation exactly as you would with whole numbers, starting from the rightmost column.

Placing the Decimal Point in the Answer

The decimal point in your final sum or difference will be placed directly below the decimal points in the numbers you added or subtracted.

Whole Numbers and Decimals

When performing operations involving a whole number and a decimal, remember that a whole number can be written with a decimal point and annexed zeros (e.g., $12 = 12.00$).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Borrowing and Carrying

The concepts of borrowing in subtraction and carrying in addition apply to decimal operations in the same way they do for whole numbers.

Important Terms

Sum

The result obtained when two or more numbers are added together.

Difference

The result obtained when one number is subtracted from another.

Annex Zeros

To add zeros to the end of a decimal number without changing its value. This is typically done to ensure all numbers have the same number of decimal places before an operation.

Place Value

The value of a digit determined by its position within a number (e.g., in 3.45, the '3' is in the ones place, '4' in the tenths place, '5' in the hundredths place).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of "sum"?

The sum is the result of adding two or more numbers.

What is the definition of "difference"?

The difference is the result of subtracting one number from another.

What is the crucial first step when adding or subtracting decimals?

You must line up the decimal points so that digits in the same place value are aligned.

When subtracting decimals, what should you do if the numbers have a different number of decimal places?

Annex zeros as needed so both numbers have the same number of decimal places.

What is the first step in the general process for adding or subtracting decimals?

Write the numbers vertically and line up the decimal points.

What is the second step in the general process for adding or subtracting decimals?

Annex zeros so that all numbers have the same number of decimal places.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the third step in the general process for adding or subtracting decimals?

Add or subtract as with whole numbers.

What is the fourth step in the general process for adding or subtracting decimals?

Place the decimal point in the answer directly below the other decimal points.

How do you prepare a whole number (e.g., 12) for addition or subtraction with decimals?

Place a decimal point after the whole number and annex zeros (e.g., 12 = 12.00).

Explain how to find the sum of 3.45 + 12.7.

Line up decimal points (3.45 + 12.70), annex a zero to 12.7, then add column by column to get 16.15.

Explain how to find the difference of 20.3 - 8.47, including preparing the numbers.

Line up decimal points, annex a zero to 20.3 (making it 20.30), then subtract column by column, borrowing as needed, to get 11.83.
