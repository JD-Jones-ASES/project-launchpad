Adding/Subtracting as Whole Numbers

Once the decimal points are aligned and zeros are annexed, you can perform the addition or subtraction just as you would with whole numbers, carrying over or borrowing as necessary.

Placing the Decimal Point in the Answer

The decimal point in your final answer will be placed directly below the aligned decimal points in the numbers you added or subtracted.

Handling Whole Numbers

When a whole number is part of a decimal operation, imagine a decimal point at the end of the whole number (e.g., 12 becomes 12.00) and then proceed with aligning and annexing zeros.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Real-World Applications

These skills are vital for practical situations like managing money, calculating distances, and understanding measurements.

Estimation

It's good practice to estimate your answer before or after performing the exact calculation to check if your result is reasonable.

Important Terms

Sum

The result obtained when two or more numbers are added together.

Difference

The result obtained when one number is subtracted from another number.

Annex Zeros

To add zeros to the end of a decimal number to ensure all numbers have the same number of decimal places for easier calculation.

Special Considerations

Whole Numbers and Decimals

Adding Multiple Decimals

The same rules apply when adding more than two decimal numbers. Line up all the decimal points, annex zeros to ensure all numbers have the same number of decimal places, and then add column by column.

Common Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them

Misaligning Decimal Points

This is the most common mistake. *Always* line up the decimal points, not the last digits. Forgetting this will lead to incorrect place value calculations.
