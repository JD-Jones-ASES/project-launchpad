Key Concepts (1/2)

Factors and Multiples

Understanding factors (numbers that divide evenly) and multiples (products of a number and any whole number) is foundational to GCF and LCM.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest factor shared by two or more numbers. It is used when you need to divide numbers into the largest possible equal parts, such as simplifying fractions.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Important Distinction

GCF is always less than or equal to the smallest of the given numbers. LCM is always greater than or equal to the largest of the given numbers.

Important Terms

Factor

A whole number that divides another whole number evenly, with no remainder.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest factor that two or more whole numbers share. It is sometimes denoted as `(a,b)` in the textbook, but more commonly as `GCF(a,b)` or `gcd(a,b)`.

Multiple

The product of a whole number and any other whole number (including 0).

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The smallest positive number that is a multiple of two or more whole numbers. It is sometimes denoted as `(a,b)` in the textbook, but more commonly as `LCM(a,b)` or `lcm(a,b)`.

Prime Factorization

The process of expressing a composite number as a product of its prime factors. This method is highly efficient for finding GCF and LCM, especially for larger numbers.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a 'factor' of a whole number?

A whole number that divides it evenly (with no remainder).

Define 'Greatest Common Factor (GCF)' of two or more whole numbers.

The largest factor that all of the numbers share. It is often written as (a,b).

What is a 'multiple' of a whole number?

The product of that number and any whole number (0, 1, 2, 3, ...).

Define 'Least Common Multiple (LCM)' of two or more whole numbers.

The smallest positive number that is a multiple of each number. It is often written as (a,b).

Before adding, subtracting, or simplifying fractions, what two tools are essential?

The greatest common factor (GCF) and the least common multiple (LCM).

What are the steps to find the GCF by listing factors?

1. List all factors of each number. 2. Circle the factors that appear in every list. 3. The greatest circled factor is the GCF.

Find the GCF of 24 and 36 by listing factors.

Factors of 24: {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24}. Factors of 36: {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36}. Common factors: {1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12}. GCF(24,36) = 12.

Find the LCM of 8 and 12 by listing multiples.

Multiples of 8: {8, 16, 24, 32, 40, 48, ...}. Multiples of 12: {12, 24, 36, 48, ...}. The smallest positive number in both lists is 24. So, LCM(8,12) = 24.

Find the GCF of 60 and 90 using prime factorization.

Prime factorization: 60 = 2^2 3 5, and 90 = 2 3^2 5. Shared primes with smallest exponents: 2^1, 3^1, 5^1. GCF(60,90) = 2 3 5 = 30.
