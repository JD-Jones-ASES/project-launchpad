Perimeter and Area of Polygons

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Perimeter

The total distance around the boundary of a two-dimensional shape. It's found by adding the lengths of all its sides. For regular polygons (where all sides are equal), a simpler formula can be used.

Area

The measure of the amount of surface enclosed within a two-dimensional shape. It quantifies how much space the figure occupies on a flat plane.

Important Considerations: Units

Perimeter

is a one-dimensional measurement, so its units are linear (e.g., centimeters, feet, meters).

Area

is a two-dimensional measurement, so its units are square units (e.g., square centimeters, square feet, square meters, denoted as cm², ft², m²).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the perimeter of a figure tell you?

The perimeter of a figure tells you the distance around it.

What does the area of a figure tell you?

The area of a figure tells you how much surface it covers.

Define the perimeter of a polygon.

The perimeter of a polygon is the sum of the lengths of all its sides.

What is the formula for the perimeter of a regular polygon?

For a regular polygon, the perimeter is $P = ns$, where $n$ is the number of sides and $s$ is the side length.

What is the area formula for a rectangle?

The area of a rectangle is $A = lw$ (length × width).

What is the area formula for a parallelogram?

The area of a parallelogram is $A = bh$ (base × height).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the area formula for a triangle?

The area of a triangle is $A = \frac{1}{2}bh$ (half of base × height).

What is the area formula for a trapezoid?

The area of a trapezoid is $A = \frac{1}{2}(b_1+b_2)h$ (half of the sum of the parallel bases × height).

In all polygon area formulas, what does 'h' represent?

'h' represents the perpendicular height, which is measured at a right angle to the base, not a slant side.

Find the perimeter of a rectangle with length $12$ cm and width $7$ cm.

$P = 2l + 2w = 2(12) + 2(7) = 24 + 14 = 38$ cm.

Calculate the area of a triangle with base $10$ in. and height $6$ in.

$A = \frac{1}{2}bh = \frac{1}{2}(10)(6) = 30$ in.$^2$.

Find the area of a parallelogram with base $9$ m and height $4$ m.

$A = bh = 9 \times 4 = 36$ m$^2$.
