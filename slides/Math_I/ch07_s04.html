Important Terms

Part

The specific portion or amount that is being compared to the whole. In the equation `part = percent × whole`, it is the result of applying the percent to the whole.

Percent

A ratio that compares a number to 100. In the percent equation, it must be converted to a decimal (or fraction) before calculation.

Whole

The total amount, or the entire quantity, from which the "part" is taken. It represents 100% of the quantity.

Percent Equation

The mathematical formula `part = percent × whole` which establishes the relationship between these three components and allows for solving various percent problems.

Important Considerations:

Identifying Part vs. Whole

Always read carefully! The "whole" usually follows the word "of" in problem statements. For example, in "15% *of* 200", 200 is the whole. In "12 is what percent *of* 48", 48 is the whole.

Percent Equation Summary

Basic Form

`part = percent × whole`

To find the percent (`p`)

`p = part / whole` (remember to convert the decimal result to a percent)

To find the whole (`w`)

`w = part / percent`

Crucial Step

Always convert the percent to a decimal (or fraction) *before* doing any multiplication or division.
