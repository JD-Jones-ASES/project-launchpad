Important Terms

Part

The quantity that represents a specific portion or fraction of the total amount. In the equation, it's the result when the percent is applied to the whole.

Percent

A ratio that expresses a number as a fraction of 100. In calculations, it must be converted to a decimal or fraction form.

Whole

The total amount, the entire quantity, or the base from which a part is taken. It's the number that the percent is "of."

Percent Equation

The algebraic formula `part = percent × whole`, used to relate a part to its whole via a percentage.

The Universal Percent Equation

• `part` is the amount you are looking for, or the amount given that represents a portion of the total.

• `percent` is the rate per hundred. It must always be converted to a decimal (or fraction) before being used in the equation.

• `whole` is the total amount or the base quantity.

Always Convert the Percent

Before multiplying or dividing in the equation, convert the percent to a decimal (divide by 100) or a fraction.

Using Fractions

For some percents, converting to a fraction might be easier, especially if the percent has a common fractional equivalent (e.g., 66 2/3% is 2/3).

Percent Equation Summary

To find the `part`

`part = percent × whole`

To find the `percent`

`percent = part / whole` (then convert decimal to percent)

To find the `whole`

`whole = part / percent`

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the general form of the Percent Equation?

part = percent × whole

What does 'part' represent in the Percent Equation?

'Part' is a specific amount or a portion of the 'whole'.

What does 'percent' represent in the Percent Equation?

'Percent' is the rate, usually expressed as a decimal or fraction in the equation, that relates the part to the whole.

What does 'whole' represent in the Percent Equation?

'Whole' is the total amount or the base value to which the percent is applied.

Before performing calculations with the Percent Equation, what must you always do with the percent value?

Always convert the percent to a decimal (or fraction) first.

What is the process for finding the 'part' using the Percent Equation?

Multiply the decimal form of the percent by the whole (part = percent × whole).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Find the part: What is 15% of 200?

part = 0.15 × 200 = 30

How do you find the 'percent' if you know the part and the whole?

Divide the part by the whole (percent = part / whole), then convert the decimal result to a percent.

Find the percent: 12 is what percent of 48?

percent = 12 / 48 = 0.25 = 25%

Find the percent: 9 is what percent of 36?

percent = 9 / 36 = 0.25 = 25%

How do you find the 'whole' if you know the part and the percent?

Divide the part by the decimal form of the percent (whole = part / percent).

Find the whole: 30 is 60% of what number?

whole = 30 / 0.60 = 50
