Important Terms (1/2)

Common Denominator

A denominator that is shared by two or more fractions, allowing for direct comparison of their numerators.

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common denominator for a set of fractions; it is the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of their denominators.

Cross Multiplication

A method to compare two fractions ($\frac{a}{b}$ and $\frac{c}{d}$) by comparing the products $a \times d$ and $b \times c$.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts taken or considered from the whole.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Important Terms (2/2)

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The smallest positive integer that is a multiple of two or more given integers. Used to find the LCD.
