Important Terms (1/2)

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest positive common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. It is used to create equivalent fractions that can be easily compared.

Least Common Multiple (LCM)

The smallest positive integer that is a multiple of two or more integers. The LCD is the LCM of the denominators.

Common Denominator

A common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. While any common denominator works for comparison, the LCD is usually preferred for simplicity.

Cross Multiplication

A method used to compare two fractions or solve proportions by multiplying the numerator of one fraction by the denominator of the other.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered out of the whole.

Important Terms (2/2)

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Why is it difficult to compare fractions like 3/8 and 5/12 by sight?

It is hard to compare them by sight because they have different denominators, meaning the parts are not of the same size.

What are the two reliable methods presented for comparing and ordering fractions?

The two reliable methods are using common denominators and using cross multiplication.

What does LCD stand for in the context of comparing fractions?

LCD stands for Least Common Denominator.

How is the Least Common Denominator (LCD) of two fractions determined?

The LCD is the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of their denominators.

If you compare fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$ using cross multiplication, and $a \times d > b \times c$, what can you conclude?

If $a \times d > b \times c$, then the first fraction is greater: $a/b > c/d$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

If you compare fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$ using cross multiplication, and $a \times d < b \times c$, what can you conclude?

If $a \times d < b \times c$, then the first fraction is smaller: $a/b < c/d$.

If you compare fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$ using cross multiplication, and $a \times d = b \times c$, what can you conclude?

If $a \times d = b \times c$, then the fractions are equal: $a/b = c/d$.

Compare $3/8$ and $5/12$ using the common denominator method.

LCD(8, 12) = 24. $3/8 = 9/24$ and $5/12 = 10/24$. Since $9 < 10$, $3/8 < 5/12$.

Compare $4/7$ and $3/5$ using cross multiplication.

Compute $4 \times 5 = 20$ and $7 \times 3 = 21$. Since $20 < 21$, $4/7 < 3/5$.

How do you compare two mixed numbers, such as $3 \, 2/5$ and $3 \, 3/8$?

What is the general strategy for ordering three or more fractions from least to greatest or greatest to least?

Find the LCD for all fractions, rewrite each fraction with the LCD, and then order them by comparing their new numerators.
