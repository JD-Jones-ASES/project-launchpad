Chapter 2, Section 4

Multiplying and Dividing Integers

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Sign Rule for Same Signs (Multiplication & Division)

When multiplying or dividing two integers with the same sign (both positive or both negative), the result is always positive.

Sign Rule for Different Signs (Multiplication & Division)

When multiplying or dividing two integers with different signs (one positive and one negative), the result is always negative.

Multiplication by Zero

The product of any integer and zero is always zero.

Division by Zero

Division by zero is undefined; it is an operation that has no valid mathematical result.

Zero Divided by a Nonzero Number

When zero is divided by any nonzero integer, the quotient is zero.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Multiplying Multiple Integers

For a product involving three or more factors, count the number of negative factors. An even count results in a positive product, while an odd count results in a negative product.

Important Terms

Integers

The set of whole numbers and their opposites (e.g., ..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...).

Product

The result of a multiplication operation.

Factors

The numbers that are multiplied together to get a product.

Quotient

The result of a division operation.

Undefined

A term used to describe a mathematical expression or operation that does not have a meaningful or valid numerical result, such as division by zero.

Multiplying Integers

Q: Multiplying Integers with the Same Signs

When both integers in a multiplication problem have the same sign (both positive or both negative), their product is always positive.

Q: Multiplying Integers with Different Signs

When the two integers in a multiplication problem have different signs (one positive and one negative), their product is always negative.

Q: Multiplying by Zero

Any integer multiplied by zero always results in zero.

Dividing Integers

Same Signs

If the dividend and divisor have the same sign, the quotient is positive.

Different Signs

If the dividend and divisor have different signs, the quotient is negative.

Multiplying Three or More Factors

Even number of negative factors

The product will be positive.

Odd number of negative factors

The product will be negative.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the sign of the product when multiplying two integers with the same sign?

Positive. For example, $(+)(+) = +$ and $(-)(-) = +$.

What is the sign of the product when multiplying two integers with different signs?

Negative. For example, $(+)(-) = -$ and $(-)(+) = -$

State the sign rule for dividing two integers with the same sign.

The quotient of two integers with the same sign is positive.

State the sign rule for dividing two integers with different signs.

The quotient of two integers with different signs is negative.

What is the result when any integer is multiplied by zero?

Zero. For any integer $a$, $a \times 0 = 0$.

What happens if you attempt to divide any number by zero?

Division by zero is undefined. You cannot divide any number by zero.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Calculate: $(-4) \times (-9)$

$36$ (Both factors are negative, so the product is positive).

Calculate: $7 \times (-2)$

$-14$ (The factors have different signs, so the product is negative).

Calculate: $(-36) \div (-4)$

$9$ (Both numbers are negative, so the quotient is positive).

Calculate: $42 \div (-7)$

$-6$ (The numbers have different signs, so the quotient is negative).

Calculate: $0 \div (-5)$

$0$ (Zero divided by any nonzero number is zero).

Explain the general relationship between the sign rules for multiplying and dividing integers.

The sign rules for division are exactly the same as for multiplication.
