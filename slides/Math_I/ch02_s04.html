Key Concepts

Sign Rules for Multiplication and Division

When multiplying or dividing two integers:

Multiplication by Zero

Any integer multiplied by zero always results in zero.

Division by Zero is Undefined

You cannot divide any number by zero; this operation has no mathematical solution.

Zero Divided by a Non-Zero Number

Zero divided by any non-zero integer is zero.

Important Terms

Integer

A whole number (not a fraction or decimal) that can be positive, negative, or zero (e.g., -5, 0, 12).

Product

The result obtained when two or more numbers are multiplied together.

Quotient

The result obtained when one number is divided by another.

Factor

A number that is multiplied by another number to get a product.

Undefined

In mathematics, a term used to describe an expression that does not have a meaningful or calculable value, such as any number divided by zero.

Multiplying Three or More Factors

Important Note

If any of the factors are zero, the entire product will automatically be zero, regardless of the signs or number of other factors.

Real-World Applications

Example

A stock price drops $3 each day for 5 days. A drop of $3 can be represented as $-3$. The total change in price is $5 \times (-3) = -15$. This means the stock price decreased by $15 overall.
