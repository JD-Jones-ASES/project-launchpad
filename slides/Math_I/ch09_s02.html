Classifying Triangles and Quadrilaterals Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Triangle Classification by Sides

Triangles can be categorized as equilateral (all three sides equal), isosceles (at least two sides equal), or scalene (no sides equal).

Triangle Classification by Angles

Triangles can be categorized as acute (all three angles less than 90°), right (one angle exactly 90°), or obtuse (one angle greater than 90°).

Triangle Angle Sum Property

The sum of the interior angles of any triangle always equals 180°.

Quadrilateral Definition

A polygon with four sides.

Quadrilateral Angle Sum Property

The sum of the interior angles of any quadrilateral always equals 360°.

Angle Limitations in Triangles

A triangle can have at most one right angle or at most one obtuse angle, never two of either, due to the 180° angle sum property.

Important Terms (1/3)

Equilateral triangle

A triangle with all three sides equal in length.

Isosceles triangle

A triangle with at least two sides equal in length.

Scalene triangle

A triangle with no sides equal in length.

Acute triangle

A triangle in which all three interior angles are less than 90°.

Right triangle

A triangle with exactly one interior angle measuring 90°.

Important Terms (2/3)

Obtuse triangle

A triangle with exactly one interior angle measuring greater than 90°.

Quadrilateral

A polygon with four sides.

Parallelogram

A quadrilateral where opposite sides are parallel and equal in length.

Rectangle

A parallelogram with four right (90°) angles.

Rhombus

A parallelogram with all four sides equal in length.

Important Terms (3/3)

Square

A quadrilateral that is both a rectangle and a rhombus, meaning it has four right angles and four equal sides.

Trapezoid

A quadrilateral with exactly one pair of parallel sides.
