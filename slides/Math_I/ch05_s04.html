Dividing by Powers of Ten

There is a simple shortcut for dividing by 10, 100, or 1,000: move the decimal point to the left by one place for each zero in the power of ten.

Checking Your Work

Division can always be checked by multiplying the quotient by the divisor; the result should equal the original dividend.

Annexing Zeros

Sometimes, you may need to add zeros to the end of the dividend (after the decimal point) to continue the division process and get a more precise quotient.

Important Terms

Quotient

The answer or result obtained from a division problem. For example, in $6.4 \div 2 = 3.2$, the quotient is $3.2$.

Dividend

The number that is being divided in a division problem. In $6.4 \div 2$, the dividend is $6.4$.

Divisor

The number by which you are dividing in a division problem. In $6.4 \div 2$, the divisor is $2$.

Summary of Dividing Decimals

Dividing by a Whole Number

Place the decimal point in the quotient directly above the decimal point in the dividend, then divide as usual.

Dividing by a Decimal

Move the decimal point in both the divisor and dividend to the right until the divisor is a whole number. Then, place the decimal point in the quotient and divide.

Dividing by Powers of 10

Move the decimal point to the left by one place for each zero in the power of ten.

Always Check Your Answer

Multiply your quotient by the original divisor to confirm it equals the original dividend.
