Multiplication Rule for Fractions

To multiply two fractions, you simply multiply their numerators together and multiply their denominators together. The result is a new fraction formed by these products.

Important Terms

Cross Canceling

A method used to simplify fractions before multiplying by dividing a numerator from one fraction and a denominator from the other fraction by a common factor.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Simplest Form

A fraction is in simplest form (or lowest terms) when its numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Common Factor

A number that divides exactly into two or more other numbers.

The Power of Cross Canceling

• `5` and `10` share a common factor of `5`. (`5÷5=1`, `10÷5=2`)

• `3` and `6` share a common factor of `3`. (`3÷3=1`, `6÷3=2`)

• After canceling, you have `1/2 1/2`.

• Multiplying gives `(1×1) / (2×2) = 1/4`.

Applying Fraction Multiplication: Word Problems

• This is asking for `2/7` of `7/8`. So, `2/7 7/8`.

• Cross cancel `7` with `7` (`7÷7=1`).

• Cross cancel `2` with `8` (`2÷2=1`, `8÷2=4`).

• Multiply: `1/1 1/4 = 1/4`.

• You used `1/4` meter of ribbon.

In what significant way is multiplying fractions simpler than adding or subtracting them?

You do not need a common denominator when multiplying fractions.

What are the benefits of using cross canceling?

It keeps the numbers small during multiplication and saves you from simplifying a larger fraction at the end.

State the general property for multiplying any two fractions, $a/b$ and $c/d$.

For any fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$ (with $b eq 0$ and $d eq 0$), $a/b \times c/d = (a \times c) / (b \times d)$.

What are the two primary steps for multiplying fractions, followed by a final step?

Multiply the numerators, multiply the denominators. Then, simplify the result if possible.

Which property of multiplication applies to the factors within the fraction's numerators and denominators?

The commutative property of multiplication applies, meaning you can multiply the numerators and denominators in any order.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Multiply $2/3 \times 4/5$. Write the answer in simplest form.

$2/3 \times 4/5 = (2 \times 4) / (3 \times 5) = 8/15$. It is already in simplest form.

Multiply $3/8 \times 4/9$ using cross canceling.

Divide 3 and 9 by 3 (becomes 1 and 3). Divide 4 and 8 by 4 (becomes 1 and 2). Then, $1/2 \times 1/3 = 1/6$.

How do you correctly represent a whole number when multiplying it by a fraction?

Always write the whole number as a fraction over 1 (e.g., $5 = 5/1$). Do not put the whole number in a denominator by mistake.

Multiply $5 \times 2/3$.

$5/1 \times 2/3 = (5 \times 2) / (1 \times 3) = 10/3$. As a mixed number, this is $3\,1/3$.

A rectangular garden measures $3/4$ yard long and $2/3$ yard wide. What is the area of the garden?

Area = Length \times Width. $3/4 \times 2/3$. Cross cancel the 3s (1,1) and the 2 with the 4 (1,2). Result: $1/2 \times 1/1 = 1/2$ square yard.

A ribbon is $7/8$ meter long. You use $2/7$ of it. How many meters of ribbon did you use?

You used $2/7 \times 7/8$ meters. Cross cancel the 7s (1,1) and the 2 with the 8 (1,4). Result: $1/1 \times 1/4 = 1/4$ meter.
