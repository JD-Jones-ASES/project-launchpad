No Common Denominator Needed

A significant advantage of multiplying fractions is that you do not need to find a common denominator, simplifying the initial setup compared to addition or subtraction.

Multiplying with Whole Numbers

When multiplying a whole number by a fraction, always write the whole number as a fraction over 1 (e.g., $5 = \frac{5}{1}$). This allows you to apply the standard fraction multiplication rule.

Simplifying the Answer

After multiplying, always simplify the resulting fraction to its simplest form by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor.

Using Cross Canceling for Simpler Calculations

Smaller Numbers

By dividing out common factors early, you work with smaller numbers, which makes the multiplication step much easier.

Less Simplification Later

Often, after cross canceling, the resulting fraction is already in simplest form, or requires very little further simplification.
