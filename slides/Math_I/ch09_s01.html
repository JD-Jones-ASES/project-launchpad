Angles and Their Vertices

An angle is formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint, called the vertex. Angles are measured in degrees.

Types of Angles

Angles are classified by their degree measure:

Complementary Angles

Two angles whose measures add up to exactly $90^\circ$.

Supplementary Angles

Two angles whose measures add up to exactly $180^\circ$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Vertical Angles

When two lines intersect, the angles opposite each other are called vertical angles. A key property is that vertical angles always have equal measures.

Important Terms (1/3)

Point

An exact location in space, having no size. Named with a capital letter (e.g., point $A$).

Line

Extends forever in both directions. Named by two points on the line (e.g., $\overleftrightarrow{AB}$).

Line Segment (Segment)

A part of a line consisting of two endpoints and all points between them. Named by its two endpoints (e.g., $\overline{AB}$).

Ray

Starts at one endpoint and extends forever in one direction. Named by its endpoint first, then another point on the ray (e.g., $\overrightarrow{AB}$, starting at $A$).

Plane

A flat surface that extends forever in all directions.

Important Terms (2/3)

Angle

Formed by two rays sharing a common endpoint.

Vertex

The common endpoint where the two rays of an angle meet.

Acute Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ such that $0^\circ < m < 90^\circ$.

Right Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ equal to $90^\circ$.

Obtuse Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ such that $90^\circ < m < 180^\circ$.

Important Terms (3/3)

Straight Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ equal to $180^\circ$.

Complementary Angles

Two angles whose measures sum to $90^\circ$.

Supplementary Angles

Two angles whose measures sum to $180^\circ$.

Vertical Angles

A pair of opposite angles formed by the intersection of two lines. They are always equal in measure.

Protractor

A tool used to measure angles in degrees.

Tools and Memory Aids

Protractor

A semicircle tool used to measure angles. To use it, place the center mark on the angle's vertex and one ray along the baseline, then read the degree where the other ray crosses the scale.

Caution

Complementary and supplementary angles can be easily confused.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a point in geometry?

An exact location in space with no size, named with a capital letter.

Define a line in geometry.

A geometric figure that extends forever in both directions.

What is a line segment?

The part of a line between two endpoints.

How is a ray defined?

A part of a line that starts at one point and extends forever in one direction.

In geometry, what is a plane?

A flat surface that extends forever in all directions.

How is an angle formed?

By two rays that share a common endpoint called the vertex.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the common endpoint of the two rays forming an angle called?

The vertex.

What unit is used to measure angles?

Degrees (°).

Define an acute angle.

An angle whose measure is greater than 0° but less than 90° (0 < m < 90).

What defines a right angle?

An angle whose measure is exactly 90°. It is often marked with a small square.

Describe an obtuse angle.

An angle whose measure is greater than 90° but less than 180° (90 < m < 180).

What is a straight angle?

An angle whose measure is exactly 180°, forming a straight line.
