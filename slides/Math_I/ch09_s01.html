Important Terms (1/3)

Point

An exact location in space, having no size. Named with a capital letter.

Line

A straight path that extends infinitely in both directions. Represented by two points on the line (e.g., $AB$) with arrows on both ends.

Line Segment (Segment)

A part of a line with two distinct endpoints. Represented by its endpoints (e.g., $AB$).

Ray

A part of a line that starts at a specific point (the endpoint) and extends infinitely in one direction. Represented by its endpoint and another point on the ray (e.g., $AB$, starting at $A$).

Plane

A flat, two-dimensional surface that extends infinitely in all directions.

Important Terms (2/3)

Angle

A geometric figure formed by two rays that share a common endpoint.

Vertex

The common endpoint where two rays meet to form an angle.

Degree ($^\circ$)

The unit of measurement for angles.

Acute Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ such that $0^\circ < m < 90^\circ$.

Right Angle

An angle with a measure $m = 90^\circ$. It is often indicated by a small square symbol at its vertex.

Important Terms (3/3)

Obtuse Angle

An angle with a measure $m$ such that $90^\circ < m < 180^\circ$.

Straight Angle

An angle with a measure $m = 180^\circ$. It forms a straight line.

Complementary Angles

Two angles whose measures sum to $90^\circ$.

Supplementary Angles

Two angles whose measures sum to $180^\circ$.

Vertical Angles

A pair of non-adjacent angles formed by the intersection of two lines. Vertical angles are always equal in measure.
