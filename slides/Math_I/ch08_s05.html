Solving One-Step Equations (Multiplication and Division)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Inverse Operations

To solve for a variable, perform the operation that "undoes" what is being done to the variable. Multiplication is the inverse of division, and division is the inverse of multiplication.

Multiplication Property of Equality

If two expressions are equal ($a = b$), then multiplying both by the same number ($c$) keeps them equal ($ac = bc$). This allows you to eliminate division from one side of an equation.

Solving $ax = b$

To solve an equation where a variable `x` is multiplied by a coefficient `a`, divide both sides of the equation by `a`.

Solving $x/a = b$

To solve an equation where a variable `x` is divided by a number `a`, multiply both sides of the equation by `a`.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Fractional Coefficients

When a variable is multiplied by a fraction, it's generally most efficient to multiply both sides of the equation by the reciprocal of that fraction.

Checking Solutions

Always substitute your calculated solution back into the original equation to ensure it makes the equation true.

Important Terms

Inverse Operation

An operation that reverses the effect of another operation. For example, multiplication is the inverse of division.

Coefficient

A numerical factor multiplying a variable in an algebraic term. In `6x`, `6` is the coefficient.

Reciprocal

One of a pair of numbers whose product is 1. To find the reciprocal of a fraction, simply flip it (e.g., the reciprocal of $2/3$ is $3/2$).

Multiplication Property of Equality

A rule stating that if you multiply both sides of an equation by the same number, the equality remains true.

Division Property of Equality

A rule stating that if you divide both sides of an equation by the same nonzero number, the equality remains true.
