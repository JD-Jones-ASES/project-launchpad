Standard Strategy for Comparison

To compare or order rational numbers in different forms, the most effective method is to convert them all into the same form.

Preferred Form (Decimals)

Converting all numbers to decimals is often the fastest and easiest way to compare them because decimal points can be aligned, and digits compared place by place.

Comparing Positive Numbers

Once converted to decimals, positive rational numbers are compared just like whole numbers, from left to right, digit by digit.

What is a Rational Number?

Fractions

Like $1/2$, $3/4$, or $-5/8$.

Terminating Decimals

Decimals that end, like $0.5$ (which is $1/2$) or $0.75$ (which is $3/4$).

Repeating Decimals

Decimals that have a pattern of digits that repeats infinitely, like $0.333...$ (which is $1/3$) or $0.142857142857...$ (which is $1/7$).

Integers

Whole numbers and their opposites, like $3$, $-7$, or $0$. These can be written as fractions (e.g., $3 = 3/1$).

Summary for Comparing and Ordering Rational Numbers

Convert to Decimals

Always convert all rational numbers to decimal form first. This is usually the easiest approach.

Compare Positive Decimals

Line up decimal points and compare digits from left to right, just like whole numbers.

Compare Negative Decimals

Be extra careful!

Use a Number Line

A number line can be a powerful visual aid for understanding the relative positions and order of both positive and negative rational numbers.
