Applications

Important Terms

Proportion

An equation that states two ratios are equal, typically written in the form $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$.

Cross Products Property

A property of proportions that states if $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $a \cdot d = b \cdot c$. This provides a method for verifying proportions and solving for unknown values.

What is a proportion?

State the Cross Products Property.

If a/b = c/d (with b ≠ 0, d ≠ 0), then the cross products are equal: a d = b c.

How can you use cross products to determine if two ratios, a/b and c/d, form a proportion?

Calculate the cross products (a d and b c). If they are equal, then the ratios form a proportion.

What is the underlying mathematical operation that cross multiplication shortcuts?

Cross multiplication is a shortcut for multiplying both sides of an equation by both denominators to clear fractions.

Under what specific condition does cross multiplication work?

Cross multiplication only works when there is a single fraction on each side of the equals sign.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Do the ratios 3/4 and 9/12 form a proportion?

Yes. The cross products are 3 12 = 36 and 4 9 = 36. Since 36 = 36, they form a proportion.

Do the ratios 5/8 and 15/20 form a proportion?

No. The cross products are 5 20 = 100 and 8 15 = 120. Since 100 ≠ 120, they do not form a proportion.

Outline the two main steps to solve a proportion using cross multiplication.

First, cross multiply the terms. Second, divide both sides by the coefficient of the unknown variable to solve for it.

Solve the proportion: n/6 = 10/15.

n 15 = 6 10 => 15n = 60 => n = 60/15 => n = 4.

Solve the proportion: 8/n = 12/9.

8 9 = n 12 => 72 = 12n => n = 72/12 => n = 6.

A machine fills 5 bottles in 2 minutes. Set up a proportion to find out how many bottles (n) it fills in 14 minutes.

5 bottles / 2 minutes = n bottles / 14 minutes (or 2 minutes / 5 bottles = 14 minutes / n bottles, as long as units correspond).
