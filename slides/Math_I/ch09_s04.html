Key Concepts (1/2)

Circle Definition

A circle is a set of all points in a plane that are equidistant from a central point. This consistent distance defines its shape.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circle to any point on its edge. It's a fundamental measurement for defining a circle's size.

Diameter ($d$)

The distance across a circle, passing directly through its center. It's always twice the length of the radius ($d = 2r$, or $r = d/2$).

Circumference ($C$)

The perimeter of a circle, or the distance around its edge. It can be calculated using the formulas $C = \pi d$ or $C = 2\pi r$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Area ($A$)

The amount of two-dimensional space enclosed within a circle. It is calculated using the formula $A = \pi r^2$.

Order of Operations for Area

When calculating the area, it's crucial to square the radius ($r^2$) *before* multiplying by $\pi$. Forgetting this order is a common mistake.

Choosing Pi Approximation

Use $\pi \approx 22/7$ often when the radius or diameter is a multiple of 7, as it can simplify calculations. Otherwise, $\pi \approx 3.14$ is generally preferred.

Important Terms (1/2)

Circle

A closed two-dimensional shape where all points on the boundary are equidistant from a central point.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circle to any point on its circumference.

Diameter ($d$)

The distance across a circle, passing through its center. It is equal to two times the radius ($d = 2r$).

Pi ($\pi$)

An irrational mathematical constant, approximately $3.14159...$, used in calculations involving circles. It represents the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter.

Circumference ($C$)

The total distance around the outside of a circle; its perimeter.
