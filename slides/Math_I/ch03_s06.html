Adding and Subtracting Mixed Numbers Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Separating Whole and Fraction Parts

The primary approach for both addition and subtraction of mixed numbers is to operate on the whole number components and the fraction components independently.

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

Before adding or subtracting the fraction parts, they must have a common denominator. The LCD is the most efficient choice.

Simplifying Fractions

The final step in both addition and subtraction is to simplify the resulting fraction part to its lowest terms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Alternative Method

Mixed numbers can be converted into improper fractions, added or subtracted, and then the result can be converted back into a mixed number.

Important Terms

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 2 1/2).

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions; essential for adding or subtracting fractions.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/4).

Borrowing (Regrouping)

Simplify

To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms by dividing both the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor.
