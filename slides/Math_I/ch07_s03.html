Finding a Percent of a Number

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Converting Percents to Decimals

Before performing any calculation, a percentage must be converted into its decimal equivalent. This is done by dividing the percent value by 100 (e.g., $25\% = 0.25$).

Multiplication to Find the Part

Once the percent is in decimal form, multiply it by the "whole" number to find the "part" (e.g., $0.25 \times 80 = 20$).

"Of" Means Multiply

In percent problems, the word "of" is a strong indicator that multiplication is the operation to be performed.

Percents Greater Than 100%

If the percent is greater than $100\%$, the resulting "part" will be larger than the original "whole" number. This means you are finding more than the original amount.

Common Mistake: Forgetting to Convert

A frequent error is to multiply the percent value directly without converting it to a decimal first. Always remember to convert $25\%$ to $0.25$ before multiplying.

Important Terms

Percent

A ratio that compares a number to $100$. It means "per hundred" or "out of $100$." Represented by the symbol $\%$.

Whole

The total or original amount or number from which a part is being taken or calculated.

Part

The portion of the whole that corresponds to the given percent.

Discount

A reduction in price, often expressed as a percentage off the original price.
