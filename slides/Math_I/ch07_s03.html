Finding a Percent of a Number

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Converting Percent to Decimal

Before any calculation, a percent must be converted into its decimal equivalent. This is done by dividing the percent by 100, which is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the left.

The Meaning of "Of"

In percentage problems, the word "of" almost always signifies multiplication. When you see "what is X% of Y," it means X% multiplied by Y.

Formula

The relationship can be expressed as: Percent × Whole = Part. Here, 'percent' is in decimal form, 'whole' is the original number, and 'part' is the resulting percentage amount.

Percents Greater Than 100%

If the percent you are finding is greater than 100% (e.g., 150%), the resulting "part" will be larger than the original "whole" number.

Common Mistake

A frequent error is to multiply by the percent number directly (e.g., using "25" instead of "0.25" for 25%). Always convert the percent to a decimal first.

Important Terms

Percent

A ratio or a fraction out of 100. It literally means "per hundred." (e.g., 25% means 25 out of 100).

Whole

The original, total amount or number that you are taking a percentage of.

Part

The amount that results from taking a certain percent of the whole. This is the value you are usually trying to find.

Decimal

A number system that uses powers of 10 and a decimal point to represent fractional parts. It's the standard format for performing calculations with percents.

Discount

A reduction in price, often expressed as a percentage off the original price.

Summary of Finding a Percent of a Number

• Convert the percent to a decimal (divide by 100).

• Multiply: decimal × whole = part.

• If the percent is greater than 100%, the part will be greater than the whole.

• The word "of" indicates multiplication.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary goal when you 'find a percent of a number'?

To calculate a specific part or portion of a given whole number.

What are the two main steps to find a percent of a number?

1. Convert the percent to a decimal (or fraction). 2. Multiply the decimal (or fraction) by the number.

Provide the mathematical formula for finding a percent of a number, using the terms 'percent', 'whole', and 'part'.

percent × whole = part

In a percent problem, what mathematical operation does the word 'of' typically signify?

Multiplication.

How do you convert 25% to a decimal?

Divide 25 by 100, which results in 0.25 (or move the decimal point two places to the left).

Calculate 25% of 80.

Convert 25% to 0.25. Then, 0.25 × 80 = 20.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the first step to find 8.5% of 200?

Convert 8.5% to a decimal by dividing by 100, which gives 0.085.

Find 8.5% of 200.

Convert 8.5% to 0.085. Multiply 0.085 × 200 = 17.

When finding a percent of a number, what effect does a percent greater than 100% have on the result ('part') compared to the original number ('whole')?

If the percent is greater than 100%, the calculated 'part' will be greater than the original 'whole' number.

Calculate 150% of 40.

Convert 150% to 1.50. Then, 1.50 × 40 = 60.

If a jacket costs $60 and is on sale for 30% off, how much is the discount?

Convert 30% to 0.30. Multiply 0.30 × $60 = $18. The discount is $18.
