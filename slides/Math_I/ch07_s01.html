Understanding Percents: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of Percent

A percent is a ratio that compares a number to $100$. It signifies "out of $100$".

Percent Symbol (%)

The symbol $\%$ directly means "per hundred". For instance, $45\%$ means $45$ out of $100$.

Converting Fractions to Percents

Percent Ranges

While often associated with parts of a whole (0% to 100%), percents can also represent values less than $1\%$ or greater than $100\%$, especially in contexts of growth or comparison.

Important Terms

Percent

A ratio that compares a number to $100$. The word comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "per hundred." The symbol is $\%$.

Percent Grid

A visual model, typically a $10 \times 10$ grid of $100$ squares, used to represent percents where each small square represents $1\%$.

Definition

A percent is fundamentally a ratio that compares a specific number to $100$.

Introduction to Percents (2/2)

Symbol

The symbol "$\%$" is used to denote a percent, and it means "out of $100$."

Modeling Percents with Grids

Construction

A percent grid is typically a $10 \times 10$ square, meaning it is divided into $100$ smaller, identical squares.

Representation

Each individual small square within the grid represents $1\%$ of the total grid.

Visualizing Percents

Examples of Grid Modeling:

Shading a Percent Grid

To represent $45\%$, you would shade $45$ out of the $100$ squares on the grid.

Writing a Percent from a Model

If a $10 \times 10$ grid has $72$ squares shaded, then $72\%$ of the grid is shaded because each square represents $1\%$.

Checkpoint

If $30$ out of $100$ squares are shaded, this represents $30\%$.

Writing Fractions as Percents

Fractions with a Denominator of 100

If a fraction already has a denominator of $100$, the numerator *is* the percent.

Fractions with Other Denominators

If a fraction does not have a denominator of $100$, you must find an *equivalent fraction* that does.
