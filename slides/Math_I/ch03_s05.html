Key Concepts (1/2)

Common Denominators are Essential

You can only add or subtract fractions if they represent pieces of the same size, meaning they must have the same denominator. Think of the denominator as the "name" of the fraction's pieces.

Adding/Subtracting with Like Denominators

When fractions already share the same denominator, simply add or subtract their numerators and keep the denominator the same.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Never Add or Subtract Denominators

A critical rule to remember is that denominators are never added or subtracted. Doing so changes the fundamental size of the pieces, leading to an incorrect answer.

Important Terms (1/2)

Fraction

A number representing a part of a whole, written as a numerator over a denominator (e.g., 2/3).

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, indicating how many parts of the whole are being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts into which the whole has been divided.

Like Denominators

Denominators that are the same.

Unlike Denominators

Denominators that are different.

Important Terms (2/2)

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. It's the smallest number that all denominators divide into evenly.

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that represent the same value, even though they have different numerators and denominators (e.g., 1/2 and 2/4).

Simplify (Reduce) a Fraction

To express a fraction in its lowest terms, where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 7/5).

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., 1 2/5).

Adding and Subtracting Fractions with Like Denominators

• For addition: `a/c + b/c = (a + b)/c` (where `c` is not 0)

• For subtraction: `a/c - b/c = (a - b)/c` (where `c` is not 0)

• The denominators are already the same (10).

• Add the numerators: `3 + 1 = 4`.

• Keep the denominator: `4/10`.

• Simplify: `4/10` can be divided by 2 (common factor of 4 and 10) to get `2/5`.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the fundamental requirement for adding or subtracting fractions?

Fractions must have the same denominator (like denominators).

Why is it necessary for fractions to have the same denominator before adding or subtracting them?

Think of the denominator as the 'name' of the pieces. You can only combine or separate pieces that are the same size.

State the rule for adding fractions with like denominators.

Add the numerators and keep the denominator. (e.g., a/c + b/c = (a+b)/c)

State the rule for subtracting fractions with like denominators.

Subtract the numerators and keep the denominator. (e.g., a/c - b/c = (a-b)/c)

What is an essential final step after adding or subtracting any fractions?

Always simplify the result if possible.
