Chapter 8, Section 2: Evaluating Expressions

Study Slides

Key Concepts

What is Evaluating?

Evaluating an algebraic expression means finding its numerical value. This is done by replacing each variable with a specific number.

Substitution

The first step in evaluating is to substitute (replace) each variable in the expression with its given numerical value.

Order of Operations

After substitution, you must simplify the resulting numerical expression by strictly following the order of operations (PEMDAS/BODMAS) to get the correct final value.

Parentheses for Clarity and Correctness

Always use parentheses around the number you are substituting, particularly when the number is negative or when a variable is raised to a power. This prevents ambiguity and common errors.

Formulas are Expressions

Many real-world formulas are algebraic expressions that can be evaluated to find a specific quantity when given values for its variables.

Important Terms

Evaluate

To find the numerical value of an algebraic expression by substituting given numbers for the variables and then simplifying using the order of operations.

Substitute

To replace a variable in an expression with a specific numerical value.

Examples of Evaluating Expressions (1/2)

Step 1 (Original Expression)

$3x + 5$

Step 2 (Substitute)

Replace $x$ with $4$. Using parentheses, this becomes $3(4) + 5$.

Step 3 (Simplify)

Step 1 (Original Expression)

$2n^2 - 5$

Step 2 (Substitute)

Replace $n$ with $-3$. It's essential to write this as $2(-3)^2 - 5$.

Examples of Evaluating Expressions (2/2)

Step 3 (Simplify)

Step 1 (Original Formula)

$A = lw$

Step 2 (Substitute)

Replace $l$ with $9$ and $w$ with $4$. This becomes $A = (9)(4)$.

Step 3 (Simplify)

Perform the multiplication: $9 \times 4 = 36$.

The Importance of Parentheses

Correct

$(-5)^2 = (-5) \times (-5) = 25$

Incorrect (Common Error)

$-5^2 = -(5 \times 5) = -25$ (Here, only the 5 is squared, then the negative is applied.)
