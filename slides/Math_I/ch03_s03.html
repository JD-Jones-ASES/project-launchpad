Important Terms (1/2)

Proper Fraction

A fraction with a numerator smaller than its denominator, indicating a value between 0 and 1.

Improper Fraction

A fraction with a numerator greater than or equal to its denominator, indicating a value of 1 or greater.

Mixed Number

A number that consists of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $2 \frac{3}{4}$).

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts that make up one whole.

Important Terms (2/2)

Quotient

The result obtained when one number is divided by another (the whole number part in improper to mixed conversion).

Remainder

The amount left over after a division when the dividend is not perfectly divisible by the divisor (the new numerator in improper to mixed conversion).

Real-World Applications

Leftover Pizza Example

If there are $11/4$ pizzas left, converting it to $2 \frac{3}{4}$ pizzas makes it much clearer to understand that you have two whole pizzas and three-quarters of another.

Recipe Example

A recipe calling for $13/6$ cups of flour can be understood more easily as $2 \frac{1}{6}$ cups.

Important Notes and Cautions

Whole Numbers as Improper Fractions

Any whole number can be expressed as an improper fraction (e.g., $5 = 5/1$). This is useful when you need to perform operations with fractions that require a common denominator.

Summary of Key Conversions

Proper Fraction

Numerator < Denominator.

Improper Fraction

Numerator $\ge$ Denominator.

Mixed Number

A whole part and a fraction part.

Improper to Mixed

Divide (Numerator $\div$ Denominator); Quotient = Whole Part, Remainder = New Numerator, Denominator stays the same.

Mixed to Improper

(Whole Part $\times$ Denominator + Numerator) / Denominator.
