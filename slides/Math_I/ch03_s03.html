Mixed Numbers and Improper Fractions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Proper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is smaller than the denominator. Its value is always between 0 and 1. Examples: $3/5$, $7/8$.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator. Its value is 1 or greater. Examples: $7/4$, $11/3$, $5/5$. These fractions indicate that you have one or more whole units.

Important Terms

Proper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator (the top number) is less than its denominator (the bottom number). Its value is always between $0$ and $1$, representing a part of a whole.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to its denominator. Its value is $1$ or greater, indicating that it represents one whole or more.

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction. It is a way to express quantities greater than one in a more readable and often more understandable format.

Converting Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers (1/2)

Step 1

Divide 17 by 5.

Step 2

The quotient, 3, is the whole-number part.

Step 3

The remainder, 2, is the new numerator.

Step 4

The original denominator, 5, remains the same.

Result

$17/5 = 3\,2/5$.

Converting Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers (2/2)

Check

To ensure correctness, you can convert the mixed number back: $5 \times 3 + 2 = 15 + 2 = 17$, so $17/5$ is correct.

Converting Mixed Numbers to Improper Fractions

Step 1

Multiply the whole number (3) by the denominator (7): $3 \times 7 = 21$.

Step 2

Add the numerator (2) to the product: $21 + 2 = 23$.

Step 3

Write this result (23) over the original denominator (7).

Result

$3\,2/7 = 23/7$.

Caution

A common error is to forget to add the numerator after multiplying. For example, incorrectly writing $3\,2/7$ as $21/7$ instead of the correct $23/7$. Always remember to add the existing numerator!

Plotting on a Number Line

• The whole-number part is $2$, which tells us the point is between $2$ and $3$.

• Divide the segment between $2$ and $3$ into $4$ equal parts (because the denominator is 4).

• Count $3$ parts past $2$ (because the numerator is 3).

• The point will be three-fourths of the way from $2$ to $3$.

Real-World Applications

Leftover Pizza

If you have $11/4$ pizzas left, converting it to $2\,3/4$ pizzas makes it much clearer that you have two whole pizzas and three-quarters of another.

Recipes

A recipe calling for $13/6$ cups of flour is easier to measure and understand as $2\,1/6$ cups.

Summary of Key Conversions

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are numbers like 7/4 called, where the numerator is greater than the denominator?

These are called improper fractions.

Define a proper fraction.

A proper fraction has a numerator that is less than its denominator. Its value is between 0 and 1.

Give three examples of proper fractions.

3/5, 7/8, 1/100 (or any fraction where the numerator is smaller than the denominator).

What defines an improper fraction?

An improper fraction has a numerator that is greater than or equal to its denominator. Its value is 1 or greater.

Provide three examples of improper fractions.

7/4, 11/3, 5/5 (or any fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator).

Describe what a mixed number combines.

A mixed number combines a whole-number part and a proper-fraction part.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How can the mixed number 2 3/4 be expressed as a sum?

2 3/4 means 2 + 3/4.

What is the first step when converting an improper fraction to a mixed number?

Divide the numerator by the denominator.

When converting an improper fraction (a/b) to a mixed number (q r/b), what do 'q' and 'r' represent?

'q' is the quotient (whole-number part), and 'r' is the remainder (numerator of the fraction part).

What happens to the denominator when converting an improper fraction to a mixed number?

The denominator stays the same.

Convert the improper fraction 17/5 to a mixed number.

17/5 = 3 2/5 (Divide 17 by 5: quotient = 3, remainder = 2).

What is the initial step for converting a mixed number to an improper fraction?

Multiply the whole number by the denominator.
