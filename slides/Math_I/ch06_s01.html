Chapter 6, Section 1: Ratios and Equivalent Ratios Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Ratio Definition

A ratio is a mathematical tool used to compare two quantities by division. It helps us understand the relationship between different amounts.

Forms of Ratios

Ratios can be expressed in three primary ways: word form (e.g., "a to b"), colon form (e.g., "a : b"), and fraction form (e.g., "a/b").

Order of Terms

The sequence of the quantities in a ratio is crucial. "a to b" is generally different from "b to a" unless both quantities are equal.

Equivalent Ratios

These are ratios that represent the same comparison or relationship, even if the numbers look different (e.g., 2:3 and 4:6).

Finding Equivalent Ratios

To create an equivalent ratio, you must multiply or divide both terms of the original ratio by the same non-zero number. This maintains the proportional relationship.

Important Terms

Ratio

A comparison of two quantities by division.

Terms of a Ratio

The two quantities ($a$ and $b$) being compared in a ratio $a$ to $b$, $a:b$, or $a/b$.

Equivalent Ratios

Ratios that express the same comparison or relationship between quantities.

Simplest Form (of a ratio)

A ratio where the two terms have no common factors other than 1.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest positive integer that divides evenly into two or more numbers. (Used when simplifying ratios).

The Importance of Order

Caution

Always pay attention to the wording. "The ratio of boys to girls" is different from "the ratio of girls to boys."

Ratios vs. Fractions

• While a ratio can be written in fraction form, a ratio is not always the same as a fraction.

• A fraction typically names part of a whole (e.g., 1/2 of a pizza).

• A ratio compares any two quantities, which do not necessarily have to be parts of the same whole (e.g., 5 marbles to 8 marbles, or 3 cups of flour to 2 cups of milk).

Understanding Equivalent Ratios

• For example, $2:3$ and $4:6$ are equivalent ratios. If you have 2 apples for every 3 oranges, it's the same relationship as having 4 apples for every 6 oranges.

Key Concepts (2/2)

When is a ratio in its simplest form?

A ratio is in simplest form when its two terms have no common factor other than 1.

How does a ratio differ from a fraction, even though it can be written in fraction form?

A fraction names part of a whole, while a ratio compares any two quantities, which do not necessarily need to be parts of the same whole.

A bag contains 5 red marbles and 8 blue marbles. Write the ratio of red marbles to blue marbles in all three forms.

Word form: 5 to 8; Colon form: 5 : 8; Fraction form: $\frac{5}{8}$.

Write two ratios equivalent to $3 : 5$.

Examples: $6 : 10$ (multiplying by 2), $9 : 15$ (multiplying by 3), $12 : 20$ (multiplying by 4). Any two of these or similar would be correct.

Simplify the ratio $18 : 24$.

The GCF of 18 and 24 is 6. Dividing both terms by 6 gives $3 : 4$.

Simplify the ratio $20 : 35$.

The GCF of 20 and 35 is 5. Dividing both terms by 5 gives $4 : 7$.
