Chapter 7, Section 2: Fractions, Decimals, and Percents

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Interchangeable Forms

Fractions, decimals, and percents are simply different ways of representing the same numerical value or proportion.

Percent as "Per Hundred"

A percent literally means "per hundred" or "out of one hundred." This foundational understanding is key to all percent conversions.

Decimal Point Movement Shortcut

Multiplying or dividing by 100 can be quickly accomplished by moving the decimal point two places to the right (for multiplication) or two places to the left (for division).

Conversion Pathways

Percents Greater than 100%

A percent greater than 100% represents a value greater than a whole (greater than 1).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Percents Less than 1%

A percent less than 1% represents a very small fraction of a whole, and its decimal form will have leading zeros after the decimal point (e.g., 0.5% = 0.005).

Important Terms (1/2)

Fraction

A numerical quantity that is not an integer (a whole number). It represents a part of a whole, typically written as a numerator over a denominator (e.g., $3/5$).

Decimal

A number that uses a decimal point to represent a part of a whole, where digits to the right of the decimal point indicate tenths, hundredths, thousandths, and so on (e.g., $0.6$, $1.25$).

Percent

A ratio or fraction expressed as parts per one hundred. It is denoted by the symbol "%" (e.g., $60\%$, $125\%$).

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts in the whole.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simplify (a fraction)

To reduce a fraction to its simplest form by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor.

Summary of Conversions:

Percent $\rightarrow$ Decimal

Divide by $100$ (move decimal two places left).

Decimal $\rightarrow$ Percent

Multiply by $100$ (move decimal two places right).

Percent $\rightarrow$ Fraction

Write over $100$ and simplify.

Fraction $\rightarrow$ Percent

Divide numerator by denominator, then convert the decimal to a percent.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are three different ways to write the same number in mathematics?

Fractions, decimals, and percents.

What is the fast, reliable method for converting a percent to a decimal?

Divide the percent by 100, or move the decimal point two places to the left.

Convert 35% to a decimal.

0.35

What is the fast, reliable method for converting a decimal to a percent?

Multiply the decimal by 100 and attach the % symbol, or move the decimal point two places to the right and attach the % symbol.

Convert 0.08 to a percent.

8%

Explain the shortcut 'move the decimal point' for percent-decimal conversions.

It's a shortcut for either dividing by 100 (moving left, making the number smaller) or multiplying by 100 (moving right, making the number larger).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Convert 0.455 to a percent.

45.5%

How do you convert a percent to a fraction?

Write the percent value over 100 and then simplify the resulting fraction.

Convert 60% to a simplified fraction.

60/100 = 3/5

How do you convert a fraction to a percent?

First, divide the numerator by the denominator to get a decimal. Then, convert that decimal to a percent.

Convert 3/5 to a percent.

3 ÷ 5 = 0.6; 0.6 = 60%.

Convert 7/8 to a percent.

7 ÷ 8 = 0.875; 0.875 = 87.5%.
