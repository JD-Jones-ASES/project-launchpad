Key Concepts (1/2)

Three Representations of the Same Number

Fractions, decimals, and percents are different notations for expressing the same numerical value.

Percent to Decimal Conversion

To convert a percent to a decimal, divide the percent by 100, which is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the left.

Decimal to Percent Conversion

To convert a decimal to a percent, multiply the decimal by 100, which is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the right, and then add the percent symbol (%).

Percent to Fraction Conversion

To convert a percent to a fraction, write the percent value as the numerator over a denominator of 100, then simplify the resulting fraction to its lowest terms.

When converting a percent less than 1% (e.g., 0.5%) to a decimal, you still move the decimal point two places to the left. This results in a very small decimal (e.g., 0.005).

Important Terms (1/2)

Fraction

A way of representing a part of a whole, expressed as a ratio of two numbers (numerator over denominator).

Decimal

A number that contains a decimal point, representing a fractional part using powers of ten.

Percent (%)

A ratio that compares a number to 100, meaning "per hundred" or "out of one hundred."

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, indicating how many parts of the whole are being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts that make up the whole.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simplify (a fraction)

To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common divisor.

Mixed Number

A number consisting of an integer and a proper fraction, representing a value greater than one.

Caution for Percents Less Than 1%

Rule

When converting a percent less than $1\%$ (e.g., $0.5\%$) to a decimal, you still move the decimal point two places to the left.

Common Mistake to Avoid

A common error is to move the decimal only one place or forget to add leading zeros.

Example

$0.5\% = 0.005$ (not $0.5$).

Summary of Conversions

Percent $\rightarrow$ Decimal

Divide by $100$ (move decimal two places left).

Decimal $\rightarrow$ Percent

Multiply by $100$ (move decimal two places right), attach $\%$.

Percent $\rightarrow$ Fraction

Write over $100$ and simplify.

Fraction $\rightarrow$ Percent

Divide to get a decimal, then convert the decimal to a percent.
