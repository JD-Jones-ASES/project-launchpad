Important Terms

Reciprocal

(Also called multiplicative inverse) For a fraction $a/b$, its reciprocal is $b/a$. The product of a number and its reciprocal is always $1$.

Multiplicative Inverse

Another term for reciprocal.

Understanding the Reciprocal

Definition

The reciprocal (or multiplicative inverse) of a fraction $a/b$ is $b/a$. Essentially, you "flip" the fraction. This applies as long as $a eq 0$ and $b eq 0$.

Examples

Property

A number multiplied by its reciprocal always equals $1$. For instance, $(2/3) \times (3/2) = (2 \times 3) / (3 \times 2) = 6/6 = 1$.

Important Caution

Zero does not have a reciprocal. This is because you cannot flip $0/1$ to get $1/0$, as division by zero is undefined.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the reciprocal of a fraction?

The reciprocal (also called the multiplicative inverse) of a fraction a/b is b/a, where a ≠ 0 and b ≠ 0. You "flip" the fraction.

What is the reciprocal of 2/3?

The reciprocal of 2/3 is 3/2.

What is the reciprocal of the whole number 5?

The reciprocal of 5 is 1/5, because 5 can be written as 5/1.

What is the product of a number and its reciprocal?

A number times its reciprocal always equals 1. For example, (a/b) × (b/a) = 1.

Does zero have a reciprocal? Explain why or why not.

No, zero does not have a reciprocal. You cannot flip 0/1 to get 1/0 because division by zero is undefined.

What is the general rule for dividing fractions a/b by c/d?

For any fractions a/b and c/d (with b ≠ 0, c ≠ 0, and d ≠ 0): (a/b) ÷ (c/d) = (a/b) × (d/c). To divide by a fraction, multiply by its reciprocal.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What mnemonic helps students remember the rule for dividing fractions?

Many students remember this rule as "keep, change, flip": keep the first fraction, change division to multiplication, and flip the second fraction.

How do you set up the division 3/5 ÷ 2/7?

You set it up as a multiplication by the reciprocal of the second fraction: 3/5 × 7/2.

How do you divide a whole number by a fraction (e.g., 4 ÷ 2/3)?

First, write the whole number as a fraction over 1 (e.g., 4 = 4/1). Then, multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction: (4/1) × (3/2).

How do you divide a fraction by a whole number (e.g., 5/8 ÷ 3)?

First, write the whole number as a fraction over 1 (e.g., 3 = 3/1). Then, multiply by the reciprocal of the whole number: (5/8) × (1/3).

When dividing a fraction by a whole number, which number should you flip?

You should flip the whole number (write its reciprocal), not the fraction you started with.

A piece of rope is 3/4 meter long and is cut into pieces that are each 1/8 meter long. Set up the division problem.

The division problem is 3/4 ÷ 1/8.
