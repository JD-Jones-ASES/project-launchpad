Properties of Addition and Multiplication

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Commutative Property

This property states that the **order** in which you add numbers or multiply numbers does not change the final sum or product. For example, `3 + 5` is the same as `5 + 3`.

Associative Property

This property indicates that the way you **group** numbers (using parentheses) in an addition or multiplication problem does not affect the sum or product. For example, `(2 + 3) + 4` is the same as...

Identity Property of Addition

When you add `0` to any number, the sum is that same number. `0` is known as the **additive identity**.

Identity Property of Multiplication

When you multiply any number by `1`, the product is that same number. `1` is known as the **multiplicative identity**.

Zero Property of Multiplication

The product of any number and `0` is always `0`.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Application in Mental Math

These properties are powerful tools for performing calculations mentally by allowing you to reorder and regroup numbers to create "round" numbers (like 10, 100, etc.) that are easier to work with.

Limitations

It is crucial to remember that these properties **do not apply** to subtraction or division. The order and grouping in subtraction and division matter significantly.

Important Terms (1/3)

Commutative Property of Addition

Changing the order of the numbers being added (addends) does not change the sum.

Commutative Property of Multiplication

Changing the order of the numbers being multiplied (factors) does not change the product.

Associative Property of Addition

Changing the grouping of the numbers being added (addends) does not change the sum.

Associative Property of Multiplication

Changing the grouping of the numbers being multiplied (factors) does not change the product.

Identity Property of Addition

The sum of any number and `0` is that number.

Important Terms (2/3)

Additive Identity

The number `0`, because adding it to any number does not change the number's identity.

Identity Property of Multiplication

The product of any number and `1` is that number.

Multiplicative Identity

The number `1`, because multiplying it by any number does not change the number's identity.

Zero Property of Multiplication

The product of any number and `0` is `0`.

Addends

The numbers that are being added together.

Important Terms (3/3)

Factors

The numbers that are being multiplied together.

Sum

The result of an addition operation.

Product

The result of a multiplication operation.
