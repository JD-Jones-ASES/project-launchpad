Key Concepts

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that name the same amount or represent the same part of a whole. For example, $1/2$ and $3/6$ are equivalent.

Simplest Form (Lowest Terms)

A fraction is in simplest form when its numerator and denominator share no common factor other than $1$. This means their greatest common factor (GCF) is $1$. For example, $3/5$ is in simplest form...

Building Equivalent Fractions

To create an equivalent fraction, multiply both the numerator and the denominator by the same non-zero number. This operation does not change the value of the fraction.

Simplifying Fractions

To simplify a fraction, divide both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF). This process reduces the fraction to its unique simplest form.

Determining Equivalence

Two fractions are equivalent if they simplify to the same simplest form, or if their cross products (numerator of the first multiplied by denominator of the second, and vice-versa) are equal.

Important Terms

Equivalent Fractions

Two or more fractions that represent the exact same quantity or proportion, even if they have different numerators and denominators.

Simplest Form (Lowest Terms)

The form of a fraction where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest positive integer that divides exactly into two or more numbers without leaving a remainder. Used to simplify fractions.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts the whole is divided into.

Summary of Key Operations

To build an equivalent fraction

Multiply the numerator and denominator by the same nonzero number.

To simplify a fraction to its simplest form

Divide the numerator and denominator by their GCF.

To determine if two fractions are equivalent

Simplify both to their simplest form and compare, or use cross-multiplication.
