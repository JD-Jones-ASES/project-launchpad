Equivalent Fractions and Simplifying: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that name the same amount or represent the same part of a whole. For example, $\frac{1}{2}$ and $\frac{2}{4}$ are equivalent.

Simplest Form (or Lowest Terms)

A fraction is in simplest form when its numerator and denominator share no common factors other than 1. This means their Greatest Common Factor (GCF) is 1.

Building Equivalent Fractions

You can create an equivalent fraction by multiplying both the numerator and the denominator by the same non-zero number. This operation does not change the value of the fraction.

Simplifying Fractions

To simplify a fraction, divide both the numerator and the denominator by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF). This process reduces the fraction to its simplest form.

Important Terms (1/2)

Equivalent Fractions

Fractions that represent the same value or quantity, even though they have different numerators and denominators.

Simplest Form (or Lowest Terms)

The form of a fraction where the numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts in the whole.

Common Factor

A number that divides exactly into two or more other numbers.

Important Terms (2/2)

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest number that is a common factor of two or more numbers.

Cross-Multiply

A method used to compare or determine the equivalence of two fractions by multiplying the numerator of one fraction by the denominator of the other.

• Multiply by 3: $\frac{3 \times 3}{5 \times 3} = \frac{9}{15}$

• Multiply by 4: $\frac{3 \times 4}{5 \times 4} = \frac{12}{20}$

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are "equivalent fractions"?

Fractions that represent the same part of a whole. For example, $12$ and $36$.

Are $23$ and $812$ equivalent? Explain why.

Yes, they are equivalent because they both name the same amount (both simplify to $23$).

What does it mean for a fraction to be in "simplest form" (or "lowest terms")?

A fraction is in simplest form when its numerator and denominator have no common factor other than $1$. This means $(numerator,denominator) = 1$.

Is the fraction $35$ in simplest form? Why or why not?

Yes, because the greatest common factor (GCF) of $3$ and $5$ is $1$.

Is the fraction $410$ in simplest form? Why or why not?

No, because the numerator $4$ and denominator $10$ have a common factor of $2$ (i.e., $(4,10)=2$).

State the rule for building equivalent fractions.

Multiplying the numerator and denominator of a fraction by the same nonzero number produces an equivalent fraction: $ab = a nb n$, where $n ≠ 0$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Give an example of building two fractions equivalent to $35$.

$35 = 3 × 25 × 2 = 610$ and $35 = 3 × 35 × 3 = 915$.

State the rule for simplifying a fraction to its simplest form.

Dividing the numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF) gives the simplest form: $ab = a ÷ (a,b)b ÷ (a,b)$.

Describe the steps to simplify $2436$ using the GCF.

1. Find the GCF of $24$ and $36$, which is $12$. 2. Divide both the numerator and denominator by $12$: $24 ÷ 1236 ÷ 12 = 23$.

Simplify the fraction $1827$.

The GCF of $18$ and $27$ is $9$. Dividing both by $9$ results in $23$.
