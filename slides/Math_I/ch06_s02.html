Understanding Unit Rates

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities measured in different units. Unlike simple ratios, rates deal with distinct types of measurements (e.g., miles and hours, dollars and pounds).

Unit Rate

Finding a Unit Rate

To convert any rate into a unit rate, divide both the first and second quantities by the value of the second quantity. This process ensures the denominator becomes 1.

"Per"

The word "per" is a strong indicator of a unit rate, meaning "for each" or "for every 1." For example, "miles per hour" means "miles for every 1 hour."

Applications of Unit Rates

Key Concepts (2/2)

Consistency in Comparison

When comparing two unit rates, it's crucial that both rates use the same unit in their denominators to ensure an accurate and fair comparison.

Important Terms

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities measured in different units.

Unit Rate

A rate in which the second quantity (the denominator) is $1$.

Per

A word often used in unit rates, meaning "for each" or "for every $1$."

Better Buy

Refers to identifying which option offers a lower unit price, making it more cost-effective.
