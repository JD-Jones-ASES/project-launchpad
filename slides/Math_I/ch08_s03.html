Translating Words to Algebraic Expressions: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Algebra as a Language

Algebra provides a way to express real-world situations and relationships using mathematical symbols, just as different spoken languages convey ideas.

Keywords for Operations

Specific words and phrases consistently indicate which mathematical operation (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) should be used in an algebraic expression.

Order of Operations for Subtraction

Phrases like "less than" and "subtracted from" require careful attention to the order of the terms, as the order is reversed from how they appear in the English phrase. For example, "5 less than n"...

Translating Multi-Operation Phrases

Complex phrases often involve more than one operation. These can be broken down and translated piece by piece before being combined into a full algebraic expression.

Forming Equations from Word Problems

Once an expression is written, it can often be set equal to a known value to form an equation, which can then be solved to find an unknown quantity.

Important Terms

Algebraic Expression

A mathematical phrase that combines numbers, variables, and operation symbols (like +, -, *, /) but does not contain an equality sign. Examples: `n + 15`, `6n`, `2n + 7`.

Variable

A symbol, usually a letter (e.g., `n`, `t`), used to represent an unknown number or quantity in an algebraic expression or equation.

Operation

A mathematical process such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division.

Equation

A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal, connected by an equals sign (=). Example: `8.50t = 51`.
