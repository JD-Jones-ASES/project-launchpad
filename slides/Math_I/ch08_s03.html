Translating Words to Algebraic Expressions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Algebra as a Language

Just like translating between spoken languages, algebra allows us to translate word phrases into symbolic mathematical expressions.

Keywords for Operations

Specific words or phrases consistently indicate addition, subtraction, multiplication, or division. Identifying these is crucial for correct translation.

Translating Multiple Operations

Complex word phrases can be broken down into smaller parts, translated individually, and then combined into a single algebraic expression.

Forming Equations

An algebraic expression can be set equal to a known value to create an equation, which can then be solved to find an unknown quantity.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Problem-Solving Strategy

A structured approach (Read, Identify, Write, Solve, Check) helps in converting word problems into solvable algebraic equations.

Important Terms

Algebraic Expression

A mathematical phrase that contains numbers, variables, and operation symbols, but no equality sign. Examples: `n + 15`, `6n`, `2n + 7`.

Variable

A symbol, usually a letter (like `n` or `t`), used to represent an unknown number or quantity in an expression or equation.

Equation

A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal. It contains an equality sign (`=`). Example: `8.50t = 51`.

Keywords for Operations

Addition (+)

Multiplication (×, , or implied by juxtaposition)

Division (÷, /)

Subtraction (-)

Translating Expressions with Multiple Operations

Example

"7 more than twice a number `n`."

Summary of Translating Words to Expressions

• Always identify the keywords that indicate each operation (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).

• Translate the phrase piece by piece, building up the expression.

• Be especially careful with "less than" and "subtracted from" phrases, as they reverse the order of the terms in the expression.

• Once you have an expression, you can often use it to form an equation by setting it equal to a known value given in the problem.

What algebraic operation do keywords like 'times' or 'product' signal?

Multiplication.

What algebraic operation do keywords like 'divided by' or 'quotient' signal?

What algebraic operation do keywords like 'less than' or 'subtracted from' signal?

Subtraction.

What critical rule must be remembered when translating phrases using 'less than' or 'subtracted from'?
