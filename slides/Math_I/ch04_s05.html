Mixed Numbers

For most operations, especially multiplication and division, it is often necessary and easier to convert mixed numbers into improper fractions before applying the order of operations.

Left-to-Right Rule

For operations at the same level (Multiplication/Division or Addition/Subtraction), always perform them from left to right within the expression.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Step-by-Step Simplification

It is good practice to simplify expressions at each step to keep the numbers manageable and reduce the chances of errors.

Important Terms (1/2)

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

An acronym (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division, Addition and Subtraction) dictating the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed to ensure a single, correct re...

Fractions

Numbers representing parts of a whole, written as a numerator over a denominator.

Mixed Numbers

Numbers composed of a whole number and a fraction (e.g., $2\,12$).

Grouping Symbols

Symbols like parentheses ( ), brackets [ ], braces { }, and fraction bars that indicate which parts of an expression should be evaluated first.

Complex Fraction

A fraction in which the numerator, denominator, or both contain fractions themselves.

Important Terms (2/2)

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts in the whole.

Grouping Symbols: Parentheses and Fraction Bars

Reduce fractions to their lowest terms at each step when possible to keep numbers smaller and computations easier.
