To keep calculations manageable and reduce the chance of error, it is good practice to simplify fractions to their lowest terms whenever possible during intermediate steps.

Important Terms (1/2)

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

An acronym (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication, Division, Addition, Subtraction) representing the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed to ensure a single, consistent re...

Fractions

Numbers that represent a part of a whole, expressed as a ratio of two integers, a numerator over a denominator (e.g., $1/2$).

Mixed Numbers

Numbers consisting of an integer and a proper fraction (e.g., $2 \frac{1}{2}$).

Grouping Symbols

Mathematical symbols used to indicate that the operations within them should be performed first. Common examples include parentheses `()`, brackets `[]`, braces `{}`, and fraction bars.

Complex Fraction

A fraction where the numerator, denominator, or both contain fractions. It represents division between the simplified numerator and denominator.

Important Terms (2/2)

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $5/2$).

Summary of Best Practices

• Always follow PEMDAS rigorously.

• Treat fraction bars as implicit grouping symbols.

• Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before performing multiplication or division, and often for addition/subtraction.

• Work left-to-right for operations of the same precedence (M/D, A/S).

• Simplify fractions as you go to avoid large numbers and make calculations easier.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What general rule applies to the order of operations with fractions?

The same order of operations (PEMDAS) used for whole numbers and integers applies to fractions and mixed numbers.

What does the acronym PEMDAS stand for in the context of order of operations?

Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (left to right), Addition and Subtraction (left to right).

In the order of operations, what is the highest priority grouping symbol, and what other symbols fall into this category?

Parentheses are the highest priority grouping symbols. Other grouping symbols include brackets, braces, and fraction bars.

When performing multiplication and division in an expression, how is their order determined?

Multiplication and Division are performed from left to right as they appear in the expression.

When performing addition and subtraction in an expression, how is their order determined?

Addition and Subtraction are performed from left to right as they appear in the expression.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is an important step to take when an expression involves mixed numbers?

Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before performing operations, especially multiplication and division.

What is a good strategy to keep arithmetic manageable when solving multi-step fraction computations?

Simplify fractions to their lowest terms at each step whenever possible.

Evaluate the first step in the expression: 1/2 + 3/4 2/3.

Multiply 3/4 2/3 first, as multiplication comes before addition.

Evaluate the first step in the expression: (5/6 - 1/3) 3/5.

Work inside the parentheses first, subtracting 1/3 from 5/6.

What common mistake should be avoided when simplifying complex fractions?
