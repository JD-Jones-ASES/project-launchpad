Key Concepts (1/2)

Sales Tax

An additional amount of money charged by the government on purchases, calculated as a percentage of the item's price. The total cost is the original price plus the sales tax.

Tip (Gratuity)

An extra amount of money given for a service (e.g., at a restaurant), calculated as a percentage of the bill. The total amount paid is the bill plus the tip.

Time in Years

For simple interest calculations, the time variable ($t$) must always be expressed in years. If time is given in months, convert it to years by dividing by 12.

Important Terms (1/3)

Sales Tax

An extra amount of money charged by the government on purchases, based on a percentage of the item's price.

Tax Rate

The percentage used to calculate the sales tax.

Tip (Gratuity)

An extra amount of money given for a service, usually calculated as a percentage of the bill.

Tip Rate

The percentage used to calculate the tip amount.

Discount

A reduction in the original price of an item, usually expressed as a percentage.

Important Terms (2/3)

Discount Rate

The percentage used to calculate the discount amount.

Original Price

The initial price of an item before any discount is applied.

Sale Price

The price of an item after a discount has been applied.

Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the principal amount, without compounding.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money deposited, invested, or borrowed.

Important Terms (3/3)

Interest (I)

The amount of money earned on an investment or paid for borrowing money.

Annual Interest Rate (r)

The percentage rate at which interest is charged or earned per year, expressed as a decimal in the simple interest formula.

Time (t)

The duration, in years, for which the principal is invested or borrowed.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is sales tax?

Sales tax is an extra amount of money charged by the government on purchases, calculated as a percent of the purchase price.

What is the formula to calculate the sales tax amount?

tax amount = tax rate × price

How do you calculate the total cost of an item including sales tax?

total cost = purchase price + tax amount

A pair of shoes costs $45.00 with a 6% sales tax rate. What is the total cost?

Tax = 0.06 × $45.00 = $2.70. Total cost = $45.00 + $2.70 = $47.70.

What is a 'tip' or 'gratuity'?

A tip (or gratuity) is an extra amount of money given for a service, calculated as a percent of the bill.

What is the formula to calculate the tip amount?

tip amount = tip rate × bill

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you find the total amount paid after adding a tip?

total amount = bill + tip amount

A lunch bill is $30.00. You leave a 20% tip. What is the total amount paid?

Tip = 0.20 × $30.00 = $6.00. Total amount = $30.00 + $6.00 = $36.00.

What is a discount?

A discount is a reduction in the original price of an item.

What is the formula to calculate the discount amount?

discount amount = discount rate × original price

How do you calculate the sale price of an item after a discount?

sale price = original price - discount amount

A backpack has an original price of $55.00 and is on sale for 20% off. What is the sale price?

Discount amount = 0.20 × $55.00 = $11.00. Sale price = $55.00 - $11.00 = $44.00.
