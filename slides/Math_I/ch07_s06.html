Applications of Percents: Tax, Tip, Discount, and Simple Interest

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Sales Tax

An additional government charge added to the price of goods and services, calculated as a percentage (tax rate) of the purchase price. The total cost includes the original price plus the tax amount.

Tip (Gratuity)

An extra amount of money given for a service (e.g., at a restaurant), calculated as a percentage (tip rate) of the service bill. The total paid is the bill plus the tip.

Time Units

For simple interest, the time (`t`) must always be expressed in years. If given in months, divide by 12 to convert it to years.

Important Terms (1/2)

Sales Tax

An amount of money charged by the government on purchases, calculated as a percent of the purchase price.

Tip (Gratuity)

An extra amount of money given for a service, calculated as a percent of the bill.

Discount

A reduction in the original price of an item.

Simple Interest

Money earned (or paid) on an original amount, called the principal.

Principal (P)

The original amount of money deposited or borrowed.

Important Terms (2/2)

Annual Interest Rate (r)

The percentage rate at which interest is earned or charged per year, expressed as a decimal in the `I = Prt` formula.

Time (t)

The duration, in years, over which interest is calculated in the `I = Prt` formula.

Sales Tax

Calculation

The tax amount is found by multiplying the purchase price by the sales tax rate (expressed as a decimal).

Total Cost

To find the total cost, you add the calculated tax amount to the original purchase price.

Tip (Gratuity)

Calculation

The tip amount is found by multiplying the service bill by the tip rate (expressed as a decimal).

Total Paid

To find the total amount paid, you add the calculated tip amount to the original bill.

Discount

Calculation

The discount amount is found by multiplying the original price by the discount rate (expressed as a decimal).

Sale Price

To find the sale price, you subtract the calculated discount amount from the original price.

Shortcut

If an item is, for example, 20% off, you are paying 80% of the original price. So, you can directly calculate the sale price as `sale price = (1 - discount rate) × original price`.

Simple Interest

Formula

`I = Prt`

Total Amount

To find the total amount in an account or the total amount to repay, add the interest to the principal.

Rate Conversion

Always convert the annual interest rate from a percent to a decimal before using it in the formula. (e.g., 4% = 0.04).

Time Units

The time `t` must be in years. If the time is given in months, divide the number of months by 12 to convert it to years (e.g., 6 months = 6/12 = 0.5 years).
