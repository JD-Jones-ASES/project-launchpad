Important Terms

Tenths

The first digit to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/10$ or $0.1$.

Hundredths

The second digit to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/100$ or $0.01$.

Thousandths

The third digit to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/1000$ or $0.001$.

Understanding Decimal Place Value

Tenths

This is the first place to the right of the decimal point. It represents fractions with a denominator of 10. For example, $0.1$ is one-tenth.

Hundredths

This is the second place to the right of the decimal point. It represents fractions with a denominator of 100. For example, $0.01$ is one-hundredth.

Thousandths

This is the third place to the right of the decimal point. It represents fractions with a denominator of 1000. For example, $0.001$ is one-thousandth.
