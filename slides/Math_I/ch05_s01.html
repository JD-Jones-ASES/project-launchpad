Important Terms (1/2)

Decimal

A number that uses a decimal point to represent values less than one. The digits to the right of the decimal point represent fractional parts.

Decimal Point

The symbol used to separate the whole-number part from the fractional (decimal) part of a number.

Tenths

The first place value to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/10$ or $0.1$.

Hundredths

The second place value to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/100$ or $0.01$.

Thousandths

The third place value to the right of the decimal point, representing $1/1000$ or $0.001$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Standard Form

The common way of writing a decimal number using digits and a decimal point.

Expanded Form

A way to write a decimal by showing the sum of the value of each digit.

Word Form

A way to write a decimal using words, where "and" signifies the decimal point.

Annex Zeros

The process of writing extra zeros at the end of a decimal number without changing its value, often done to help with comparison or arithmetic.

Understanding Decimal Place Value

The Decimal Point

This crucial symbol separates the whole number part (to its left) from the fractional or decimal part (to its right).

Place Values to the Right

Pattern

Each place value to the right of the decimal point is one-tenth the value of the place immediately to its left.

Naming Convention

Notice that decimal place values end in "-ths" (e.g., tenths, hundredths), which distinguishes them from whole number places (e.g., tens, hundreds).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Name the three forms in which a decimal can be written.

Standard form, expanded form, and word form.

In the word form of a decimal, what word is used to represent the decimal point?

The word 'and' marks the decimal point.

Write $4.037$ in expanded form.

$4 + 0.0 + 0.03 + 0.007$.

Write $4.037$ in word form.

Four and thirty-seven thousandths.

What is the place value of the digit 8 in the number $12.583$?

Hundredths ($8/100$ or $0.08$).

Write $0.269$ in expanded form and word form.

Expanded form: $0.2 + 0.06 + 0.009$. Word form: two hundred sixty-nine thousandths.
