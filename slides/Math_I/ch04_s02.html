Key Concepts (2/2)

Whole Numbers as Fractions

When multiplying a whole number by a mixed number, remember to write the whole number as a fraction with a denominator of 1 (e.g., $6 = \frac{6}{1}$) to facilitate multiplication with other fractions.

Important Terms (1/2)

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a fraction (e.g., $2\frac{1}{4}$).

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $\frac{9}{4}$).

Cross-Cancellation

A technique used in fraction multiplication where common factors are divided out from a numerator and a denominator *before* multiplying, simplifying the calculation.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, indicating how many parts are being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts in the whole.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simplify (a fraction)

To reduce a fraction to its lowest terms by dividing both the numerator and the denominator by their greatest common factor.
