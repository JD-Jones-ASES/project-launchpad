Key Concepts (1/2)

Convert to Improper Fractions First

The foundational rule for multiplying mixed numbers is to always convert each mixed number into an improper fraction before you begin the multiplication process.

Do Not Multiply Parts Separately

A common mistake is to multiply the whole number parts and the fraction parts of mixed numbers independently. This yields an incorrect result. For example, $2\,12 \times 3\,13 eq 6\,16$.

Important Terms (1/2)

Mixed Number

A number consisting of a whole number and a proper fraction (e.g., $2\,13$).

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., $94$).

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, indicating how many parts of the whole are being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts that make up one whole.

Cross Cancel

A technique used in multiplying fractions where common factors are divided out from a numerator and a denominator before the actual multiplication, simplifying the calculation.

Important Terms (2/2)

Proper Fraction

A fraction where the numerator is smaller than the denominator (e.g., $23$).

Summary of Multiplying Mixed Numbers

• Always convert mixed numbers to improper fractions before multiplying.

• Cross cancel common factors to keep numbers small and simplify calculations.

• Multiply numerators and denominators.

• Simplify the resulting improper fraction and convert it back to a mixed number when appropriate for the final answer.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is cross-canceling common factors useful when multiplying fractions or improper fractions?

Cross-canceling helps to keep the numbers small, making the multiplication step easier and reducing the amount of simplification needed at the end.

When multiplying a whole number by a mixed number, how should the whole number be written to facilitate multiplication?

Write the whole number as a fraction with a denominator of 1 (e.g., 6 becomes 6/1).

To begin multiplying 2 1/4 × 2/3, what is the first calculation you would perform?

Convert 2 1/4 to an improper fraction: 2 1/4 = (2 × 4 + 1)/4 = 9/4.

Given the multiplication problem 9/4 × 2/3, what common factors can be cross-canceled?

9 and 3 share a common factor of 3. 2 and 4 share a common factor of 2.

How would you prepare 1 2/3 × 2 1/4 for multiplication?

Convert both mixed numbers to improper fractions: 1 2/3 = 5/3 and 2 1/4 = 9/4.
