PEMDAS

An acronym representing the standard order of operations:

Grouping Symbols

Symbols like parentheses $( \,\,)$, brackets $[ \,\,]$, and fraction bars indicate which operations must be performed first, overriding the standard PEMDAS order within that group.

Innermost Grouping First

When an expression contains nested grouping symbols (e.g., parentheses inside brackets), operations within the innermost group are evaluated first, working outward.

Left-to-Right Rule

For operations at the same level (multiplication and division, or addition and subtraction), they are performed in the order they appear from left to right in the expression.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Fraction Bar as a Grouping Symbol

A fraction bar implicitly groups both the entire numerator and the entire denominator, meaning operations within each must be completed before performing the division indicated by the bar.

Important Terms

Numerical Expression

A combination of numbers and operations (such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division) that represents a single numerical value.

Grouping Symbols

Special characters used in numerical expressions to indicate which operations should be performed before others. Common examples include parentheses $(\,\,)$, brackets $[\,\,]$, and fraction bars.

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

A universally accepted set of rules that dictates the sequence in which operations are performed in a numerical expression to ensure a consistent result.

Important Notes and Cautions

Fraction Bar as Grouping

Remember that a fraction bar acts as a grouping symbol. For example, in $\frac{10 + 6}{4}$, you must evaluate the numerator ($10+6=16$) before dividing by the denominator ($16 \div 4 = 4$).
