Limitations

It is crucial to remember that the Distributive Property *only* works with multiplication over addition or subtraction. It does *not* apply to division within parentheses.

Important Terms

Distributive Property

A mathematical property that states multiplying a number by a sum or difference is the same as multiplying the number by each term in the sum or difference and then adding or subtracting the products.

Factor

A number or expression that is multiplied by another number or expression. In the expression $a(b+c)$, $a$ is a factor, and $(b+c)$ is also a factor.

Term

A single number, variable, or the product of numbers and variables, separated by addition or subtraction signs in an expression. For example, in $ab + ac$, $ab$ and $ac$ are terms.

Common Factor

A factor that is shared by two or more terms in an expression. For instance, in $7 \times 48 + 7 \times 52$, the number 7 is a common factor.
