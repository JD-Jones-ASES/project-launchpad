Chapter 5, Section 5

Converting Between Fractions and Decimals

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Fractions and Decimals Represent the Same Values

Any value that can be expressed as a fraction can also be expressed as a decimal, and vice-versa, for terminating and repeating decimals.

Converting a Fraction to a Decimal

To change a fraction to its decimal form, perform division: divide the numerator by the denominator.

Terminating Decimals

These are decimals that have a finite number of digits after the decimal point, meaning the division process ends with a remainder of zero.

Repeating Decimals

These are decimals where one or more digits after the decimal point repeat endlessly. A bar notation is used above the repeating digit(s) to indicate this.

Converting a Terminating Decimal to a Fraction

This involves using the decimal's place value to determine the denominator (a power of 10) and then simplifying the resulting fraction to its lowest terms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Rational Numbers

Every fraction, and consequently every terminating or repeating decimal, represents a rational number, which means it can be written as a ratio of two integers.

Important Terms (1/2)

Terminating Decimal

A decimal that ends; it has a finite number of digits after the decimal point.

Repeating Decimal

A decimal in which one or more digits repeat endlessly after the decimal point. A bar is placed over the repeating digits to denote this.

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts of the whole.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, representing the total number of equal parts that make up the whole.

Place Value

The value of a digit based on its position in a number (e.g., in 0.45, the 4 is in the tenths place, and the 5 is in the hundredths place).

Important Terms (2/2)

Lowest Terms (Simplify)

A fraction is in lowest terms when its numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1.

Terminating Decimals

Process

Divide the numerator by the denominator. If the division eventually yields a remainder of 0, the decimal terminates.

Example

Convert $\frac{3}{8}$ to a decimal.

Repeating Decimals

Process

Divide the numerator by the denominator. If a remainder repeats, the corresponding digit(s) in the quotient will also repeat.

Notation

A bar is placed over the digit(s) that repeat.

Example

Convert $\frac{1}{3}$ to a decimal.

Converting a Terminating Decimal to a Fraction

Example

Checkpoint Example

Real-World Application

Example

A recipe calls for $\frac{2}{5}$ cup of sugar. Your measuring cup is marked in decimals. How much sugar do you measure?
