Converting Between Fractions and Decimals: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Equivalence of Fractions and Decimals

Fractions and decimals are two different notations that can represent the exact same quantity or value.

Terminating Decimals

These are decimals that have a finite number of digits after the decimal point, meaning their division process ends with a remainder of zero.

Repeating Decimals

These are decimals where one or more digits after the decimal point repeat endlessly in a pattern. A bar notation is used to indicate the repeating digits.

Converting Fractions to Decimals

To transform a fraction into its decimal equivalent, you perform division: the numerator is divided by the denominator. The result will be either a terminating or a repeating decimal.

Converting Decimals to Fractions

To convert a terminating decimal into a fraction, you use its place value to determine the denominator (a power of 10). The resulting fraction is then simplified to its lowest terms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Rational Numbers

Every fraction (and therefore every terminating or repeating decimal) represents a rational number, meaning it can be expressed as a ratio of two integers.

Important Terms (1/2)

Terminating Decimal

A decimal that has a finite number of digits after the decimal point. The division process ends with a zero remainder.

Repeating Decimal

A decimal in which one or more digits repeat endlessly after the decimal point. A bar ($\bar{\phantom{0}}$) is placed over the repeating digit(s).

Numerator

The top number in a fraction, representing the number of parts being considered.

Denominator

The bottom number in a fraction, indicating the total number of equal parts into which the whole is divided.

Lowest Terms (Simplifying a Fraction)

A fraction is in lowest terms when its numerator and denominator have no common factors other than 1. This often involves dividing both by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

Important Terms (2/2)

Place Value

The value of a digit based on its position in a number (e.g., in 0.45, the 4 is in the tenths place, and the 5 is in the hundredths place).

Summary of Conversions

Fraction to Decimal

Divide the numerator by the denominator. The result will be either a decimal that ends (terminating) or one with digits that repeat forever (repeating, indicated by a bar).

Decimal to Fraction

Use place value to write the decimal as a fraction with a power of 10 in the denominator, then simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.

What is a terminating decimal?

A decimal that ends, meaning it has a finite number of digits after the decimal point. For example, 0.375.

How do you convert a fraction to a decimal?

Divide the numerator by the denominator.

What are the two possible types of decimals that result from converting a fraction?

Either a terminating decimal or a repeating decimal.

0.375

What is the first step to convert a terminating decimal to a fraction?

Read the decimal using its place value, which determines the denominator (e.g., 'forty-five hundredths' means 100 is the denominator).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the second step to convert a terminating decimal to a fraction?

Write the fraction with the appropriate power of 10 in the denominator (e.g., 0.45 becomes 45/100).

What is the final step to convert a terminating decimal to a fraction?

Simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.
