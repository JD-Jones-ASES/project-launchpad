Important Terms

Correlation

A statistical relationship between two variables, where a change in one is associated with a change in the other. It indicates a connection but not necessarily a cause.

Causation

A relationship where one event (the cause) directly leads to another event (the effect).

Confounding Variable (Lurking Variable)

A factor that affects both variables being studied, creating a spurious (false) correlation that can be mistaken for causation.

Post Hoc Fallacy (Post hoc, ergo propter hoc)

A logical fallacy where one assumes that because event B occurred after event A, A must have caused B.
