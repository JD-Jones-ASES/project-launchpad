Important Terms

Vagueness

When a word or phrase has no clear boundary or cutoff point, resulting in a fuzzy range of possible meanings rather than distinct ones.

Ambiguity

The characteristic of a word or phrase having two or more distinct, separate meanings, leading to potential confusion about which meaning is intended.

Loaded Language

Language that employs words with strong positive or negative connotations to manipulate emotions and opinions, making a statement appear more favorable or unfavorable than it might be objectively.

Connotation

The emotional associations or cultural implications that a word carries beyond its literal meaning.

Denotation

The literal, dictionary definition of a word, devoid of any emotional or cultural associations.
