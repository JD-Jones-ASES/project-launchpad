Introduction to New Logic Challenges

Sudoku requires math

This is false. The numbers (1-4 or 1-9) are simply symbols that could be replaced by colors or letters; their mathematical value is irrelevant to solving the puzzle.

Lateral thinking riddles have hidden constraints

Students often get stuck by assuming extra rules or details not explicitly stated. Remember to only use the information given.

Multi-step logic chains can be skipped

It's crucial to solve these puzzles one step at a time, as each answer is vital for the next part of the chain.

Logic Puzzle Strategies — A Summary (1/2)

Read all clues first

Get a full understanding before diving in.

Use elimination

Systematically rule out possibilities.

Assume and check

If stuck, make a temporary assumption and follow its logical consequences; if a contradiction arises, the assumption was false.

Question your assumptions

Especially for lateral thinking, be aware of what you *might* be assuming.

Work one step at a time

Maintain a methodical approach for multi-step problems.

Logic Puzzle Strategies — A Summary (2/2)

Try a different starting point

If one line of reasoning gets blocked, switch gears.
