Important Terms

Hypothesis

A proposed, testable explanation for an observation. It is an educated guess that can be verified or disproven through investigation.

Falsifiable

A characteristic of a scientific claim indicating that it can be disproven by evidence or a possible test result. If a claim cannot conceivably be proven wrong, it is not considered scientific.

Variable

Any factor, trait, or condition that can exist in differing amounts or types in an experiment or situation.

The Hypothesis–Test Cycle (1/2)

Observation

Maya notices plants on the left side of her classroom are dying, while those on the right are healthy.

Hypothesis

The left-side plants are not getting enough sunlight (due to no windows on that wall).

Prediction

If a dying plant is moved to the right side (near a window), it should become healthier.

Test

Maya moves one left-side plant to the right side and observes it for two weeks.

Result

The moved plant improves, growing new leaves.

Conclusion

The hypothesis is supported, suggesting sunlight was the crucial factor.

What is a 'variable' in a scientific test or experiment?

Anything in a situation that can change or differ.

What is an 'independent variable'?

The variable that you intentionally change or manipulate on purpose in an experiment to see its effect.

What is a 'dependent variable'?

The variable that you measure or observe to see what happens or how it responds to changes in the independent variable.
