What Logic Is (and Isn't): A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Logic Defined

The study of good reasoning. It gives us tools to figure out whether an argument makes sense — whether the reasons someone gives actually support what they're trying to prove.

Logic Is Not Math

Logic and math are cousins, but you don't need to be good at math to be good at logic. Only basic counting is needed for this book.

Logic Is Not Debate

Debate is about persuading people. Logic is about figuring out if reasoning actually works, whether or not anyone is persuaded. You can win a debate with a terrible argument if you're charming enough.

Logic Is Not About Opinions

The Big Idea

Logic is about the structure of reasoning, not any particular topic. It works everywhere — science, history, sports, video games, or lunch.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Logic as a Ruler

A ruler doesn't care what you're measuring; it just helps you measure accurately. Logic is like a ruler for your thinking.
