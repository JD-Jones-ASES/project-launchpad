Important Terms

Analogy

A comparison between two things that are alike in some ways. In an argument by analogy, we reason that because two things are similar in known ways, they are probably similar in some further way.

Relevant Similarity

A way in which two things are alike that actually matters for the conclusion being drawn.

Irrelevant Similarity

A shared feature that has nothing to do with the conclusion.

Strong vs. Weak Analogies

Strong Analogy

When the similarities between the compared items are relevant and numerous, and there are few significant differences that undermine the conclusion.

Weak Analogy

When the similarities are irrelevant, superficial, or outweighed by important differences related to the conclusion.

Relevant vs. Irrelevant Similarities

Relevant Similarity

A similarity that logically connects to or supports the conclusion. It *matters* for the inference being made.

Irrelevant Similarity

A similarity that does not logically connect to the conclusion. It's a shared feature, but it doesn't help establish the likelihood of the inferred property.
