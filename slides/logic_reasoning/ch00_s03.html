Superpower #1: Argument Detective (Chapters 1–4)

• Identify the parts of any argument (premises and conclusion)

• Evaluate whether the reasons actually support the claim

• Determine if an argument holds together or falls apart

Superpower #2: Fallacy Finder (Chapters 5–6)

• Recognize tricks like "Everyone's doing it!" (bandwagon) and personal attacks

• See through bad arguments that sound persuasive

• Identify when people use these tricks on purpose or accidentally

Superpower #3: Puzzle Master (Chapters 7–9)

• Solve grid challenges and truth-teller-and-liar problems

• Reason carefully about evidence (Chapters 7–8)

• Compete in a logic puzzle championship (Chapter 9)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the three 'superpowers' the logic book promises?

1) Argument Detective — take apart arguments. 2) Fallacy Finder — recognize reasoning tricks. 3) Puzzle Master — solve logic puzzles.

What does the Argument Detective superpower let you do?

Take apart any argument, spot the reasons (premises) and the main claim (conclusion), and know whether it holds together or falls apart.

Which chapters develop the Argument Detective skill?

Chapters 1 through 4.

What does the Fallacy Finder superpower let you do?

Recognize the sneaky tricks people use — sometimes on purpose, sometimes without realizing it — to make bad arguments sound good.

Which chapters are the Fallacy Finder training ground?

Chapters 5 and 6.

What does the Puzzle Master superpower let you do?

Solve brain-bending puzzles — grid challenges, truth-teller-and-liar problems, and riddles.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the grand finale in Chapter 9?

A logic puzzle championship.

What are the five parts of the book?

I: Foundations (Ch 1-2), II: Deductive Reasoning (Ch 3-4), III: Spotting Bad Thinking (Ch 5-6), IV: Inductive Reasoning (Ch 7-8), V: Finale (Ch 9).

What does Part I: Foundations cover?

What arguments are, how words matter, and how to build clear reasoning. (Chapters 1-2)

What does Part II: Deductive Reasoning cover?

Syllogisms, Venn diagrams, truth tables, and 'If...Then' logic. (Chapters 3-4)

What does Part III: Spotting Bad Thinking cover?

Logical fallacies and cognitive biases — the tricks that fool smart people. (Chapters 5-6)

What does Part IV: Inductive Reasoning cover?

Patterns, analogies, evidence evaluation, and probability. (Chapters 7-8)
