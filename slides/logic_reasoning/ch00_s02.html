Logic in Your Daily Life: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Arguments Are Everywhere

Every time you try to convince someone of something, you are making an argument. Every time someone tries to convince you, they are making one too.

Arguments with Friends and Family

Everyday conversations are full of arguments — about movie choices, allowances, getting a pet. Logic helps you figure out whether the reasons are strong enough.

Advertising Tricks

Companies use persuasive-sounding claims that don't always make logical sense. Examples include appeals to unnamed experts ("nine out of ten dentists"), celebrity endorsements, and bandwagon claims...

Social Media Claims

Logic gives you tools to ask the right questions instead of just believing everything you read online.

School Rules and Arguments

Even disputes about rules involve logic. A student's argument for using phones and a teacher's counter-argument about distraction and test scores are both arguments that logic can evaluate.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Logic in the Courtroom

The entire justice system runs on logical reasoning. Prosecutors build arguments, defense lawyers identify holes in reasoning, and judges and juries decide who makes the better case.

Advertising and Social Media

Appeal to authority

"Nine out of ten dentists recommend..." (Who are they? What does the tenth think?)

Celebrity endorsement

"This celebrity wears our sneakers, so you should too." (Fame does not equal shoe expertise.)

Bandwagon appeal

"Everyone is switching to our phone plan!" (Everyone used to think the Earth was flat.)
