The Barber Paradox — Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Barber Paradox

A concrete illustration of Russell's Paradox — a barber who shaves all and only those who do not shave themselves cannot consistently exist

Structural Parallel to Russell's Paradox

The barber corresponds exactly to the set R; "shaving yourself" corresponds to "containing yourself as a member"

Impossibility as Resolution

The correct response is not to find who shaves the barber, but to conclude that no such barber can exist

Self-Referential Rules

Rules that apply to themselves create contradictions when the rule's subject must simultaneously satisfy and violate the rule

Analogical Clarity

The Barber Paradox makes abstract set-theoretic contradiction tangible by translating it into a familiar scenario

Key Concepts (2/2)

Restricting Definitions

The remedy for both the Barber and Russell's Paradox is to restrict what kinds of definitions (or rules) are permissible

Important Terms

Barber Paradox

The logical puzzle of a barber who shaves all and only those who do not shave themselves — the barber cannot consistently shave himself or refrain from doing so, so no such barber can exist

Russell's Paradox

The formal set-theoretic paradox the Barber Paradox illustrates — whether the set of all non-self-containing sets contains itself

Self-defeating rule

A rule whose application to the rule-maker itself produces contradiction — the barber's rule is self-defeating when directed at the barber

Naive set theory

The pre-paradox system that permitted any definable collection to count as a set, analogous to the assumption that a barber with the stated rule could exist

Premise rejection

In both the barber and Russell cases, the resolution is to deny that the defined entity can exist; for set theory, this required abandoning Unrestricted Comprehension

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the Barber Paradox?

A popular illustration of Russell's Paradox that translates an abstract set-theoretic problem into a concrete, everyday scenario.

What is the strict rule of the barber in the Barber Paradox?

He shaves all and only those residents who do not shave themselves.

What is the central question that reveals the contradiction in the Barber Paradox?

Who shaves the barber?

According to the barber's rule, what happens if a resident shaves themselves?

The barber leaves them alone.

According to the barber's rule, what happens if a resident does NOT shave themselves?

The barber shaves them.

What is the contradiction if one assumes the barber shaves himself?

If he shaves himself, he is someone who shaves himself. By his rule, he does not shave people who shave themselves, so he cannot shave himself.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the contradiction if one assumes the barber does NOT shave himself?

If he does not shave himself, he is someone who does not shave himself. By his rule, he must shave all such people, so he must shave himself.

What is the ultimate conclusion drawn from the Barber Paradox regarding the barber's existence?

The rule is self-contradictory, meaning no such barber with that rule can exist.

In the analogy between the Barber Paradox and Russell's Paradox, what does the barber represent?

The barber represents the set R — the set of all sets that do not contain themselves.

In the context of Russell's Paradox, what concept does 'shaving yourself' correspond to?

'Containing yourself as a member.'

How does the barber's rule relate to Russell's Paradox?

The barber's rule corresponds to the defining property of the Russell set R.

What is the straightforward solution to the Barber Paradox?

We reject the premise, concluding that the scenario is impossible and the barber does not exist.
