Important Terms (1/2)

Zeno of Elea (c. 490–c. 430 BC)

Ancient Greek philosopher and student of Parmenides, known for his paradoxes concerning motion and infinity. Aristotle credited him as the inventor of dialectic.

Parmenides

Zeno's teacher, a pre-Socratic Greek philosopher who argued that reality is a single, unchanging, eternal, and indivisible whole, and that motion and change are illusions of the senses.

Achilles and the Tortoise Paradox

One of Zeno's most famous paradoxes, illustrating the supposed impossibility of a faster runner overtaking a slower one with a head start due to the infinite divisibility of space and time.
