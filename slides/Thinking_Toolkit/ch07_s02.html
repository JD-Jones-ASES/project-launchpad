Key Concepts (1/2)

Who identified the Peak-End Rule and when?

Daniel Kahneman and collaborators in the early 1990s.

What fundamental idea does the Peak-End Rule reveal about how we remember experiences?

Remembered experience systematically diverges from experienced experience.

Define the Peak-End Rule.

We judge past experiences not by their total or average quality, but by how they felt at their most intense moment (the peak) and how they felt at the end.

According to the Peak-End Rule, how would most people remember a two-week vacation with 12 wonderful days followed by 2 miserable days?

As a pretty bad vacation, because the miserable ending 'sticks' disproportionately.

Describe the two trials in Kahneman's cold water experiment used to demonstrate the Peak-End Rule.

Trial 1: Immerse hand in 14C water for 60 seconds. Trial 2: Immerse hand in 14C water for 60 seconds, then 15C water for an additional 30 seconds.

In the cold water experiment, which trial involved objectively more pain?

The second trial, because it contained every moment of pain from the first trial plus 30 extra seconds of discomfort.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which trial did a majority of subjects prefer to repeat in the cold water experiment, and why?

The longer, second trial (Trial 2), because it ended slightly better (less painful), demonstrating the power of the 'end'.

The result of the cold water experiment violates what common-sense understanding of pain?

That more total pain should be worse, not better.

What metaphor does the text use to describe how we process past experiences, rather than being 'rational accountants'?

We are 'editors,' compressing experiences into a handful of vivid moments, especially the peak and the ending.

Define 'duration neglect' as it relates to memory.

The phenomenon where the actual length of an experience has remarkably little effect on how it is remembered, provided its peaks and endings are similar.
