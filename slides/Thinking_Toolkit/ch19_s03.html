Important Terms

Streetlight Effect (Revisited)

An organizational and institutional phenomenon where entities systematically measure, study, and manage what is easiest to quantify, regardless of its true importance.

Measurable Proxy

An outcome or data point that is easy to count or track, and is used as a substitute for a more meaningful but harder-to-measure result.

Institutional Measurement Bias

A structural tendency within organizations to prioritize easily quantifiable metrics, leading to entrenched practices and a reluctance to question established measures.

Sophisticated Ignorance

The state of appearing data-driven and rigorous due to precise and well-collected metrics, but being fundamentally ignorant of what truly matters because the wrong things are being measured.
