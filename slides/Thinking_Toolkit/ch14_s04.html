The Ultimatum Game

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Ultimatum Game Setup

A two-player game where a "proposer" divides a sum of money, and a "responder" can either accept (both keep their shares) or reject (both get nothing).

Rational Economic Prediction

Traditional theory suggests the proposer should offer the smallest possible amount, and the responder should accept any positive offer, as "something is better than nothing."

Actual Experimental Results

Contrary to theory, proposers typically offer 40-50% of the total, and responders frequently reject offers below 30%, often rejecting offers below 20% most of the time.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Homo Economicus is an Idealization

The Ultimatum Game shows that the perfectly rational, purely self-interested agent of economic theory does not accurately describe real human behavior.

Costly Enforcement of Norms (Altruistic Punishment)

People are willing to incur personal costs to punish unfairness, which is "irrational" in a narrow sense but crucial for maintaining cooperative social norms.

Anticipation of Fairness Shapes Offers

Proposers offer fair splits not out of pure generosity, but because they anticipate that unfair offers will be rejected, demonstrating that the threat of punishment incentivizes fair behavior.

Important Terms (1/3)

Ultimatum Game

An experimental game designed to study human decision-making regarding fairness, cooperation, and self-interest.

Proposer (Player One)

The player in the Ultimatum Game who decides how to divide a sum of money.

Responder (Player Two)

The player in the Ultimatum Game who can either accept or reject the proposer's offer.

Rational and Self-Interested (Economic Theory)

A theoretical model of human behavior where individuals make decisions solely to maximize their own material gain.

Fairness Norms

Socially accepted standards or expectations about what constitutes a just or equitable division or treatment.

Important Terms (2/3)

Negative Reciprocity

The tendency to respond to a perceived negative action with another negative action, often as a form of punishment.

Reputation and Signaling

Behaviors that communicate information about an individual's character or trustworthiness, potentially influencing future interactions.

Anterior Insula

A region of the brain associated with emotional processing, particularly disgust and negative emotions, activated by unfair offers.

Market Integration

The degree to which a society participates in market exchange and commercial transactions.

Homo Economicus

A theoretical construct in economics representing a hypothetical human being who is perfectly rational and purely self-interested.

Important Terms (3/3)

Altruistic Punishment

The act of punishing someone else at a cost to oneself, often done to enforce social norms and promote cooperation.

The Ultimatum Game: A Core Experiment

Origin

First described by Werner G\"uth, Rolf Schmittberger, and Bernd Schwarze in 1982.

Simplicity and Replication

A simple yet powerful experiment, it is one of the most widely replicated results in experimental economics, yielding consistent findings across diverse settings.
