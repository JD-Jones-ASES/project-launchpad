Important Terms (1/2)

Cobra Effect

A term describing when an incentive designed to solve a problem inadvertently causes the problem to become worse.

Perverse Incentive

A reward or penalty structure that encourages undesirable behavior or outcomes, often the opposite of what was intended.

Proxy

A measurable indicator or substitute used to represent a desired goal or outcome. Policies often target proxies because the actual goal is difficult to measure directly.

Welfare Cliffs

A situation in social benefit programs where a small increase in income leads to a sudden and significant loss of benefits, effectively penalizing work and discouraging earning more.

The Origin Story: British India and the Cobra Bounty

Problem

The British colonial government in Delhi wanted to reduce the number of venomous cobras.

Solution

They offered a bounty for every dead cobra.

Initial Outcome

People killed cobras, collected rewards, and the cobra population initially declined.

Unexpected Outcome (The Cobra Effect)

Another Example: French Colonial Hanoi and the Rat Tail Bounty

Problem

French colonial administration in Hanoi was troubled by a large rat population in the sewers.

Solution

Offered a bounty for rat tails (specifically tails, to avoid piles of dead rats).

Unexpected Outcome

The Broader Phenomenon: Perverse Incentives

Definition

A perverse incentive is one that produces an outcome opposite to its intended effect.

Mechanism

Modern Examples of Perverse Incentives

Healthcare

Hospitals penalized for high patient mortality rates may avoid admitting or treating the sickest patients, improving their statistics but potentially worsening overall healthcare outcomes.

Education

Schools evaluated solely on standardized test scores might narrow their curriculum to focus only on tested material, sacrificing broader education for better numbers.

Software Development

Developers measured by lines of code written may produce unnecessarily verbose or complex code, rather than efficient or elegant solutions.

Caution and Antidote

Not All Incentives Fail

The Cobra Effect does not mean all incentive systems are flawed. Most well-designed systems work.

Specific Failure Mode

The Cobra Effect highlights a specific failure: when the metric (proxy) can be achieved or "gamed" *more cheaply* than the actual goal can be genuinely achieved.

Connections to Other Concepts

Goodhart's Law

The general principle that "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." This is closely related to the Cobra Effect's mechanism of proxies being gamed.

Incentives

A broader framework by figures like Charlie Munger, emphasizing how incentive structures profoundly drive human behavior.

The Law of Unintended Consequences

A general principle acknowledging that actions, especially policy interventions, often have unforeseen and undesirable outcomes. The Cobra Effect is a specific, vivid example of this law in action.
