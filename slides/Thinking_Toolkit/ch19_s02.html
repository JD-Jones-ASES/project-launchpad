Important Terms (1/2)

Goodhart's Law

An economic and management principle formulated by Charles Goodhart, stating that "when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure."

Measure

A quantitative indicator or metric used to assess a particular aspect of a system or reality (e.g., crime rate, test score, average handle time).

Target

A specific goal or benchmark set for a measure, often with rewards or punishments attached, intended to drive improvement or achieve a desired outcome.

Statistical Regularities

Observable patterns or relationships in data that are consistent and predictable, often forming the basis for initial measures before they are used as targets.

Optimizing for the Number

The behavioral strategy of adjusting actions primarily to improve a specific numerical metric, often at the expense of other considerations or the actual underlying goal.

Important Terms (2/2)

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

Quantifiable measures used to evaluate the success of an organization, employee, or specific activity in meeting objectives. They are particularly susceptible to Goodhart's Law.
