Key Concepts (1/2)

Classical Utilitarianism

A moral theory that posits the right action is the one that maximizes overall happiness or utility, summing up the pleasure or well-being across all individuals.

The Utility Monster

A hypothetical creature that gains disproportionately massive amounts of pleasure from resources compared to ordinary people. It serves as a device to expose a perceived flaw in utilitarianism.

Consequentialism

A broad class of ethical theories that judge the morality of an action based solely on its outcomes or consequences. Utilitarianism is a form of consequentialism.

Rights-Based Ethics

A moral framework that emphasizes the inherent rights of individuals, asserting that some actions are inherently wrong, regardless of the consequences, because they violate fundamental entitlements.
