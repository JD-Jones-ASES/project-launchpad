Origin and Biological Foundation

Predator-Prey

Cheetahs evolve to run faster, so gazelles must also evolve greater speed to escape.

Host-Parasite

Parasites develop new ways to evade immune systems, prompting hosts to evolve new immune responses to detect and fight them off.

Bacteria-Antibiotics

Bacteria develop resistance to existing antibiotics, necessitating the development of new antibiotics.

Competitive Markets

Academic Credentials and Credential Inflation

The Paradox: Individual Rationality, Collective Exhaustion

• No single company is wrong to invest in R&D to improve its products.

• No single student is wrong to pursue an advanced degree to enhance their career prospects.

Real-World Examples

Corporate R&D

Technology companies' continuous investment in innovation, often just to match competitors' advances.

Military Arms Races

The Cold War arms buildup, where both superpowers amassed vast arsenals, with neither ultimately becoming "safer" due to the other's equivalent escalation.

Credential Inflation

The rising educational bar for competitive advantage in the job market, where more education is required for the same relative position.

Connections to Other Concepts

The Tragedy of the Commons

Where individual rationality in using a shared resource leads to its depletion for all.

The Cobra Effect

Where an attempted solution to a problem inadvertently makes the problem worse.

The Pyrrhic Victory

Winning a conflict at such a high cost that it amounts to a defeat.

Why did the French administration in Hanoi offer bounties for rat tails instead of whole rats?

They wanted to avoid piles of dead rats delivered to their offices, but this proxy enabled people to collect bounties without fully eliminating the rats.
