Who originated the 'Chesterton's Fence' principle and in which notable work?

G. K. Chesterton, in his 1929 book 'The Thing,' specifically within the chapter titled 'The Drift from Domesticity.'

According to Chesterton, what is the mistaken approach a 'certain kind of reformer' takes when encountering an unexplained 'fence'?

To assume there is 'no reason for it' because its purpose isn't immediately obvious, and therefore to advocate for its removal without investigation.

Is Chesterton's Fence an argument against all change or reform?

No, it is specifically an argument against ignorant change. It encourages informed reform based on understanding, rather than prohibiting change altogether.
