Important Terms (1/2)

Lindy Effect

A heuristic or statistical phenomenon where the future life expectancy of some non-perishable things (like technologies, ideas, or organizations) is proportional to their current age.

Perishable things

Items or entities that naturally degrade, age, and decay over time, reducing their future lifespan as they get older (e.g., humans, cars, food).

Non-perishable things

Nassim Nicholas Taleb

A scholar and author who popularized and expanded the concept of the Lindy Effect, particularly in his book *Antifragile*.
