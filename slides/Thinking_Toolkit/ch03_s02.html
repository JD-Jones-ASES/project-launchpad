Assumed Constraints

Real Constraints

Actual, unavoidable limitations based on laws of physics, legal requirements, resource availability, or other objective factors that must be respected when solving a problem.

Reframing the Problem

The act of redefining a problem by questioning its underlying assumptions and perceived boundaries. This often involves stepping outside the conventional framework to find entirely new approaches.

The Risk of "Cutting the Knot"

While powerful, rejecting constraints carries the risk of ignoring valid limitations that exist for good reasons, potentially leading to new, unforeseen problems or an inadequate solution.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the legend of the Gordian Knot?

An oracle declared that whoever untied an impossibly complex knot on an ox-cart in Gordium would rule all of Asia. Many failed until Alexander the Great arri...

Who solved the Gordian Knot and how, according to the most popular version of the legend?

Alexander the Great solved it by drawing his sword and slicing the knot in half.

Where was the Gordian Knot located?

In the ancient city of Gordium, in what is now central Turkey.

What profound insight about problem-solving does the story of the Gordian Knot capture?

Sometimes the constraints of the puzzle are themselves the obstacle, and the problem needs to be reframed by rejecting assumed constraints.

What fundamental mistake did everyone trying to untie the Gordian Knot make before Alexander?

They accepted, without question, the assumed constraint that the rules of the problem required untying, even though the oracle only said “loosen the knot.”

Define "assumed constraints" in problem-solving.

Inherited habits, conventional wisdom, or unexamined premises that narrow the solution space without justification, often mistaken for real constraints.
