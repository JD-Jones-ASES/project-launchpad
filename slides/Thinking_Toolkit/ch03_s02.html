Willpower as a Finite Resource (Ego Depletion)

The Concept

Psychologists propose that self-control and decision-making abilities draw from a limited pool of mental energy. This pool depletes with use, a concept known as ego depletion.

While some recent research has debated the magnitude of ego depletion effects, the practical observation remains: difficult decisions become harder as the day progresses for most individuals.

Do the Hardest Thing First

Core Principle

The task you are most dreading is typically the most important one.

Strategy

Tackle it when your energy and self-discipline are strongest.

Benefit

Once the "frog" is handled, everything else feels easier by comparison, both practically and psychologically.

Purpose

This heuristic is not about suffering, but about the strategic and efficient allocation of your finite willpower and mental resources.

Real-World Applications

Academic Work

Students who start study sessions with their most challenging subject consistently perform better. "Warming up" with easier material often leads to the harder subject being neglected as time runs out.
