Important Terms (1/2)

Curse of Knowledge

A cognitive bias that occurs when an individual, communicating with other individuals, unknowingly assumes the others have the background to understand.

Elizabeth Newton's Tapping Experiment

A classic 1990 experiment demonstrating the Curse of Knowledge, where participants tapping song rhythms overestimated listeners' ability to identify the songs.

Dunning-Kruger Effect

(Related concept) A cognitive bias in which people with low ability at a task overestimate their ability. The Curse of Knowledge is its "mirror image," focusing on experts misjudging others.

Novice

A person new to or inexperienced in a field or situation.

Expert

A person who has a comprehensive and authoritative knowledge of or skill in a particular area.

Important Terms (2/2)

User Experience (UX) Research

(From real-world application) The study of how users interact with a product or service, often employed to identify and mitigate issues arising from the Curse of Knowledge in design.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who identified the Dunning-Kruger effect and in what publication?

David Dunning and Justin Kruger, in their 1999 paper 'Unskilled and Unaware of It,' published in the Journal of Personality and Social Psychology.

What types of tasks were used in Dunning and Kruger's 1999 study to test participants?

Logical reasoning, grammar, and humor tests.

How did the lowest-performing participants (bottom quartile) in Dunning and Kruger's study estimate their performance?

They dramatically overestimated their performance, believing they performed better than roughly two-thirds of their peers.

How did the highest-performing participants (top quartile) in Dunning and Kruger's study estimate their performance?

They consistently underestimated their performance.
