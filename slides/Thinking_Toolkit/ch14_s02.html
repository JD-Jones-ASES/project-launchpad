Important Terms (1/2)

Stag Hunt

A parable and game theory model illustrating a coordination problem where cooperation is risky but rewarding, while individual action is safe but less rewarding.

Game Theory

The mathematical study of strategic interactions between rational decision-makers.

Coordination Problem

A situation where individuals benefit from taking the same action but face uncertainty about what others will do.

Trust

The belief that another party will act in a predictable, reliable, and honest way, particularly in situations of vulnerability.

Nash Equilibrium

A concept in game theory where no player can improve their outcome by changing their strategy, assuming all other players keep their strategies constant.

Important Terms (2/2)

Prisoner's Dilemma

A classic game theory model where two individuals acting in their own self-interest ultimately produce a worse outcome for both than if they had cooperated. Defection is the dominant strategy.

Dominant Strategy

A strategy that yields the best outcome for a player regardless of what strategies other players choose.

Assurance

Providing credible signals or guarantees to other players that one will cooperate, thereby reducing their fear of defection.

Commitment

A public or private declaration of intent to pursue a specific course of action, often used to build trust and encourage reciprocal cooperation.

The Parable Explained

Hunt a Stag

This is a large, highly rewarding prize, but neither hunter can catch it alone. It requires both to cooperate and commit to the hunt.

Hunt a Hare

This is a smaller prize, but it is guaranteed. Each hunter can catch a hare independently, regardless of what the other hunter chooses to do.

Leadership

often plays a crucial role in Stag Hunt scenarios by being the first to visibly commit to the cooperative strategy, thereby providing the necessary assurance for others to follow.

Connections to Other Concepts

The Prisoner's Dilemma

Provides a contrast where defection is the dominant strategy rather than merely a safe alternative.

The Two Generals' Problem

Illustrates how coordination can fail due to unreliable communication channels, leading to uncertainty similar to the Stag Hunt's trust issues.

The Game of Chicken

Another game structure with different strategic dynamics involving risk-taking and brinkmanship, but also with multiple equilibria and the potential for mutually undesirable outcomes.
