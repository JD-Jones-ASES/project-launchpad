Important Terms

Sunk Cost

An investment that has already been made and cannot be recovered.

Sunk-Cost Fallacy

The cognitive bias to continue an undertaking because of past investments (time, money, effort) instead of future prospects.

Concorde Fallacy

A historical example and specific term for the sunk-cost fallacy, referencing the continued funding of the Concorde jet despite its economic non-viability.

Loss Aversion

The psychological phenomenon where the emotional impact of a loss is perceived as greater than the emotional impact of an equivalent gain.

Differentiating the Fallacy from Rational Persistence

Not All Continuation is a Fallacy

It's important to distinguish between succumbing to the sunk-cost fallacy and genuinely persisting with a project based on its future merits.

The Crucial Distinction

The difference lies in the *reason* for continuing.

Challenge of Self-Examination

Making this distinction requires honest self-examination and critical thinking, which is precisely why the sunk-cost fallacy is so difficult to escape.
