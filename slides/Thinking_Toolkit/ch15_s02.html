The Dollar Auction: A Paradox of Rationality

Origin

Designed by economist Martin Shubik in 1971, first published in the Journal of Conflict Resolution.

Purpose

To demonstrate how intelligent people can behave foolishly, engaging in irrational escalation.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of Martin Shubik's "Dollar Auction" game?

To demonstrate the phenomenon of irrational escalation, where intelligent people behave foolishly, leading to collectively insane outcomes.

Who designed the Dollar Auction and when was it first published?

Economist Martin Shubik designed it in 1971, and it was first published in the Journal of Conflict Resolution.

What is the unique rule in the Dollar Auction that leads to paradoxical outcomes?

The highest bidder wins the dollar, but the second-highest bidder must also pay their bid and receives nothing in return.

Describe the typical initial perception players have when the Dollar Auction begins.

It appears to be a wonderful opportunity for a significant return, as early bids are much lower than the dollar's value.

What is the ultimate outcome observed in classroom demonstrations of the Dollar Auction?

Bids frequently exceed the value of the dollar, sometimes going past $2.00, $5.00, or even $20 for a single dollar bill.
