Definition

Examples of Salient Positive Qualities

Attractiveness, eloquence, fame, warmth, or a strong initial good impression.

Impact

We fail to evaluate traits independently; instead, one positive impression "spills over" and biases our perceptions across the board.

The Dangerous Combination with Confirmation Bias

Reinforcement

The halo and horn effects become particularly potent and dangerous when combined with confirmation bias.

Selective Perception

Once an initial halo or horn is established, confirmation bias leads us to selectively notice and interpret information that aligns with our initial impression.

Self-Sustaining Filter

The initial impression effectively acts as a filter, admitting only evidence that confirms itself, making the bias hard to dislodge.

Mitigating the Halo and Horn Effects: Structured Evaluation

The Antidote

The most effective way to counter these biases is through **structured evaluation**.

Principle

This approach involves assessing each dimension or quality separately and independently, ideally without knowledge of how other traits were rated or what the overall impression might be.
