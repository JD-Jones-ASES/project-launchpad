PoSiWiD — The Purpose of a System Is What It Does

Study Slides

Key Concepts

PoSiWiD

— "The Purpose of a System Is What It Does": judge systems by their actual outputs, not their stated intentions or mission statements

Stated purpose vs. actual purpose

— The gap between what a system claims to do and what it reliably produces; PoSiWiD says the latter is the truth

De facto purpose

— The real, functional purpose revealed by consistent outputs regardless of stated goals

Diagnostic tool

— PoSiWiD is a tool for analysis, not a cynical conclusion; it directs attention to measurable results rather than aspirational language

Important Terms

Stafford Beer

British cyberneticist and management theorist (1926–2002), father of management cybernetics; coined PoSiWiD in a 2001 address

Viable System Model

Beer's framework for understanding how organizations maintain identity and adapt to their environments; PoSiWiD is one tool within this framework

Management cybernetics

The application of cybernetics — the science of communication and control in complex systems — to organizational management

Recidivism

The tendency of formerly incarcerated people to reoffend; high recidivism rates suggest that a prison system's outputs differ from its stated rehabilitative purpose

Persistent divergence

The signal that PoSiWiD is most useful to identify — not a single failure, but a consistent pattern of outputs that contradict stated goals

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the acronym PoSiWiD stand for?

The Purpose of a System Is What It Does.

Who coined the term PoSiWiD and what was his field?

Stafford Beer, a British cyberneticist and pioneer of management cybernetics.

What is the core principle of PoSiWiD regarding a system's purpose?

A system's true purpose is revealed by what it actually does and reliably produces, not by its stated intentions, mission statements, or designers' beliefs.

According to PoSiWiD, what is considered the 'truth' when assessing a system's purpose?

The system's actual outputs are the truth; the intentions are commentary.

PoSiWiD would conclude that the system's de facto purpose is profit generation, with healthcare serving as a byproduct, regardless of stated goals.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does PoSiWiD view an education system that sorts students by socioeconomic background more reliably than it cultivates understanding?

PoSiWiD identifies sorting students into social tiers as the system's de facto purpose, despite any stated educational goals.

Is PoSiWiD meant to be a cynical conclusion or a diagnostic tool?

It is intended as a diagnostic tool, helping to strip away comfortable stories about institutions and confront their actual behavior.
