Consequences: Instability and Detachment

Loss of Anchor

When prices are driven by fundamental value, there's an eventual anchor: the company's actual performance will justify (or fail to justify) its stock price.

Dissolving Anchor

When prices are driven by "expectations about expectations," this anchor dissolves. Prices can become completely detached from underlying value for extended periods.

The "Greater Fool" Theory

This dynamic thrives on the belief that "I know it's overpriced, but I think someone else (a 'greater fool') will pay even more."

Core Principle

In all these cases, the "fundamental" quality of the item or idea matters less than the social dynamics of belief about belief.

Real-World Examples

Dot-com Bubble (Late 1990s)

Many investors openly acknowledged that technology stocks were overvalued but continued buying, expecting prices to rise further, demonstrating the beauty contest dynamic.

Social Media Virality

Creators strategize to produce content they think will "go viral" by predicting audience engagement, which is influenced by what others are engaging with.

Fashion

Trends emerge and spread as people adopt styles they believe others will deem fashionable, rather than purely on personal aesthetic preference.
