Important Terms (1/2)

Eureka Moment

The sudden, unexpected realization or discovery of a solution to a problem, often accompanied by a feeling of excitement ("I have found it!").

Insight

A sudden and deep understanding of a complex problem or situation, often appearing fully formed.

Preparation

The initial stage of creative problem-solving involving conscious, deliberate effort, research, and engagement with the problem.

Incubation

The stage where the problem is set aside, allowing the unconscious mind to continue processing it, often leading to later breakthroughs.

Illumination

The "aha!" moment, where the solution to a problem suddenly becomes clear after a period of incubation.

Important Terms (2/2)

Verification

The final stage of creative insight where the newly discovered solution or idea is rigorously tested and evaluated for its correctness and practicality.

Assumed Constraints

Limitations or rules that individuals unconsciously impose on a problem that are not explicitly stated or inherent to the problem itself.

Real-World Applications

Scientific Breakthroughs

Einstein's theory of special relativity was the culmination of a decade of preparation regarding light and motion, leading to the insight that the speed of light is constant.

Product Design

The iPhone emerged from Apple's deep understanding of existing mobile constraints and recognizing that many (like physical keyboards or styluses) were assumed, not necessary.

Everyday Problem-Solving

A student struggling with a math problem may find the solution after a walk (incubation), but only if they first put in the effort to understand the problem (preparation).
