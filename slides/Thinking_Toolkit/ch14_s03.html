The core dynamic where seemingly rational choices by individuals, when aggregated, lead to an irrational and destructive outcome for the group. Solutions require changing the incentive structure.

Important Terms (1/3)

Tragedy of the Commons

A term coined by Garrett Hardin describing the depletion of a shared resource due to individual self-interest.

Common-pool resource

A resource system that is difficult to exclude potential users from, but where each user's consumption reduces the availability for others (e.g., fisheries, forests, atmosphere).

Rivalrous resource

A resource whose consumption by one person prevents its consumption by another.

Public good

A good or service that is both non-rivalrous (one person's use does not diminish another's) and non-excludable (people cannot be prevented from using it) – distinct from a common-pool resource.

Individual rationality

The decision-making process where an individual acts in a way that maximizes their own personal benefit, often assuming others will do the same.

Important Terms (2/3)

Collective ruin

The destructive outcome for a group or shared resource resulting from multiple individuals acting in their own short-term self-interest.

Garrett Hardin

The ecologist who popularized the "Tragedy of the Commons" concept in his 1968 *Science* article.

Real-World Examples

Overfishing

Oceans are shared resources. Each fishing fleet aims to maximize its catch, leading to the depletion and collapse of global fish stocks.

Carbon Emissions

The atmosphere's capacity to absorb greenhouse gases is a global commons. Each country benefits from industrial activities that produce emissions, while the climate costs are shared globally.

Antibiotic Resistance

The effectiveness of antibiotics is a shared resource. Overuse and misuse by individuals contribute to the evolution of drug-resistant bacteria, harming everyone in the long run.

Traffic Congestion

Roads are a shared resource. Each additional driver gains convenience but imposes a small delay cost on all other drivers, leading to widespread gridlock.
