The Origin of the Paradox

Plutarch's Account

The paradox originates with the Greek philosopher Plutarch in the 1st century AD, documented in his *Life of Theseus*.

The Scenario

Athenians preserved the ship Theseus sailed to Crete. Over time, as planks rotted, they were systematically replaced with new timber. Eventually, every single original plank was replaced.

The Core Question

Plutarch asked: Is it still the "Ship of Theseus" after all its original parts have been replaced? This question explores what fundamentally constitutes the identity of an object across time.

Arguments for "Same" (Continuity)

The "Yes" Argument

Those who argue it is still the same ship appeal to the concept of **continuity**.

Explanation

The ship maintained an unbroken chain of existence; it was never destroyed, only repaired and maintained. Its continuous presence and function define its identity.

Real-World Analogy

This is the common-sense view many apply to themselves – though every cell in your body is replaced over years, you still perceive yourself as the same person.

Arguments for "Different" (Material Identity)

The "No" Argument

Those who argue it is a different ship appeal to **material identity**.

Explanation

An object's identity is fundamentally tied to its original parts. Once the original parts are gone, the original thing is gone too.

Real-World Analogy

If every single component of a car is replaced with different components, it might seem illogical to still call it the "same" car.

Hobbes's Twist: The Reassembled Ship

The Added Complexity

Thomas Hobbes later proposed an additional layer to the paradox.

The Scenario

Imagine someone collects all the discarded original planks and uses them to reassemble a second ship.

The New Question

Now there are *two* ships. Which one is the "real" Ship of Theseus?

Modern Relevance: Digital Identity (Real-World Application)

The Question

If a mind is perfectly uploaded to a computer (a copy of all neural connections) and the biological brain is destroyed, is the "upload" still *you*?

Conflict

The upload has memories, personality, and thought patterns (similar to form/function), but it lacks **continuity** with the biological existence. It's a copy, not a direct continuation.

Challenge

The puzzle makes us question if continuity is merely a "prejudice" attached to biological systems. It helps frame these complex questions precisely.
