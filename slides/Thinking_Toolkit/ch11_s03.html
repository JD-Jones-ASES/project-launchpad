The Trolley Problem

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Trolley Problem (Original)

A scenario where one must choose between diverting a runaway trolley to kill one person and saving five, or doing nothing and allowing five to die. Most people choose to divert.

Doctrine of Double Effect

A moral principle stating that it is permissible to perform an action that has both a good intended effect and a foreseen, but unintended, bad side effect, provided the good effect is proportionate...

Footbridge Variant

A variation where one must actively push a large man off a footbridge to stop a trolley and save five, resulting in his death. Most people recoil, despite the identical arithmetic outcome.

Transplant Surgeon Variant

A scenario where a surgeon could kill one healthy person to harvest organs and save five dying patients. This highlights the concept of individual rights.

Consequentialism

An ethical theory that judges the morality of an action based solely on its outcomes or consequences. The "best outcome" (e.g., saving more lives) is considered the most moral.
