Important Terms (1/2)

Conway's Law

An observation, widely treated as a fundamental principle of organizational design, stating that the structure of a system will inevitably reflect the communication structure of the organization th...

Communication Structures

The established patterns, pathways, and hierarchies through which information, decisions, and interactions flow among individuals and teams within an organization.

System Architecture

The fundamental organization of a system, encompassing its components, their relationships to each other and to the environment, and the principles guiding its design and evolution.

Intra-team Coordination

Communication and collaboration that occurs within a single team, generally characterized by shared context, goals, and ease of interaction.

Inter-team Coordination

Communication and collaboration required between different teams, often more challenging due to differing priorities, schedules, vocabularies, and formal handoff processes.

The Powerful Implication: Organizational Structure as a Design Decision

For Modular and Flexible Products

Organizations aiming for highly modular, flexible, and independently deployable systems should organize into small, autonomous teams.

For Tightly Integrated Products

Conversely, organizations desiring tightly integrated products might structure themselves around shared functions or components to encourage greater cross-functional collaboration.
