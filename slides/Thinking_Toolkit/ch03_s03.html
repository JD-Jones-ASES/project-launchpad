Assumed Constraints

Unspoken or unexamined beliefs, habits, conventional wisdom, or inherited premises that narrow the range of possible solutions to a problem without actual justification. They are often the real obs...

Real Constraints

Actual, unavoidable limitations based on laws of physics, legal requirements, resource availability, or other objective factors that must be respected when solving a problem.

Reframing the Problem

The act of redefining a problem by questioning its underlying assumptions and perceived boundaries. This often involves stepping outside the conventional framework to find entirely new approaches.

The Risk of "Cutting the Knot"

While powerful, rejecting constraints carries the risk of ignoring valid limitations that exist for good reasons, potentially leading to new, unforeseen problems or an inadequate solution.

Important Terms (1/2)

Gordian Knot

An ancient, complex knot tied to an ox-cart, which an oracle declared would be untied only by the future ruler of Asia. It symbolizes an exceptionally difficult or intricate problem.

Oracle

A priest or priestess acting as a medium through whom advice or prophecy was sought from the gods in ancient Greece. In the context of the story, it's the source of the prophecy.

Solution Space

The set of all possible solutions to a problem. Assumed constraints often artificially limit this space.
