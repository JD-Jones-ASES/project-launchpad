Important Terms (1/2)

Campbell's Law

A principle stating that the more any quantitative social indicator is used for social decision-making, the more it will be subject to corruption pressures and distort the processes it monitors.

Social Indicator

A statistical measure used to track social conditions, trends, or outcomes, often used in policy-making and program evaluation (e.g., test scores, wait times, publication counts).

Corruption Pressures

The forces and incentives, often linked to high stakes (funding, job security, reputation), that lead individuals or organizations to manipulate or distort social indicators.

Goodhart's Law

A related principle stating that when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure; primarily describes a statistical phenomenon.

Program Evaluation

The systematic assessment of the design, implementation, or results of a program, often relying on social indicators, a field in which Donald Campbell was a key figure.

Dashboard
