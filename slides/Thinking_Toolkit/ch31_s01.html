The Eisenhower Matrix — Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Eisenhower Matrix

A two-by-two decision grid that sorts tasks by urgency and importance, revealing where time is actually spent versus where it should be spent.

Urgency vs. Importance

These are distinct dimensions. Something urgent demands immediate attention; something important contributes to meaningful long-term goals. They frequently do not coincide.

Quadrant 2 as the Key Insight

False Urgency

Urgency creates a psychological sense of importance that is often unwarranted; many urgent tasks (phone calls, routine emails, others' minor emergencies) advance no meaningful goal.

Crises as Quadrant 2 Failures

Many Quadrant 1 crises — health emergencies, competitive failures, last-minute deadline crunches — are the downstream consequences of neglected Quadrant 2 work.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Scheduled Protection

The practical discipline the matrix demands: block time for Quadrant 2 work before it becomes urgent, and protect that time as non-negotiable.

Important Terms (1/2)

Urgent

A task that demands immediate attention or has a pressing deadline; urgency is a property of timing, not of value.

Important

A task that contributes meaningfully to significant long-term goals, values, or outcomes; importance is a property of consequence, not of timing.

Quadrant 1 (Urgent and Important)

Crises, hard deadlines, genuine emergencies — tasks that must be handled immediately.

Quadrant 2 (Important but Not Urgent)

Long-term planning, relationship-building, health maintenance, education, strategic thinking — the most valuable category and the one most easily crowded out.

Quadrant 3 (Urgent but Not Important)

Interruptions that feel pressing but do not advance meaningful goals — most phone calls, many meetings, others' minor requests.

Important Terms (2/2)

Quadrant 4 (Neither Urgent Nor Important)

Time-wasters — activities that provide momentary relief or pleasure but contribute nothing to meaningful goals.

Forcing Function

An external constraint (such as a deadline) that compels action; Quadrant 2 work lacks a forcing function, which is why it is perpetually postponed.
