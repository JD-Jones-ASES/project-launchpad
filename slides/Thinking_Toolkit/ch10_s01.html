Important Terms (1/2)

Euthyphro Dilemma

A philosophical quandary, originating from Plato's dialogue *Euthyphro*, which explores whether moral acts are good because they are commanded by God, or commanded by God because they are good.

Socrates

An influential ancient Greek philosopher (c. 470–399 BC) known for his method of questioning (Socratic method) to probe fundamental concepts and expose contradictions.

Euthyphro

A character in Plato's dialogue who is a self-proclaimed expert on piety and prosecuting his own father for manslaughter, believing it to be the pious thing to do.

Piety

In the context of the Euthyphro dialogue, this term refers to righteousness, holiness, or actions deemed morally right, particularly in relation to religious duty and the gods.

Divine Command Theory

An ethical theory that posits an action's moral status (right or wrong) is determined by God's commands.

Important Terms (2/2)

Arbitrary

Based on random choice or personal whim, rather than any reason or system; lacking intrinsic reason or justification.

Divine Sovereignty

The concept of God's supreme power, authority, and independence over all things, including the creation and definition of morality.
