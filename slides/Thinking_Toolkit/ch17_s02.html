Important Terms (1/2)

Byzantine Generals Problem

A problem illustrating the difficulty of reaching consensus among distributed parties when some may be deceptive.

Loyal General

A participant in the Byzantine Generals Problem who honestly follows the agreed-upon plan and sends truthful messages.

Traitorous General (Traitor)

A participant who actively tries to prevent consensus by sending conflicting or false messages to different loyal participants.

Consensus

A state where all honest and operational participants in a distributed system agree on a common decision or value.

Byzantine Failure

An arbitrary and potentially malicious failure mode where a system component sends inconsistent information to different parts of the system.

Important Terms (2/2)

Crash Failure

A simple failure mode where a system component merely stops working, making it easier to detect and handle than a Byzantine failure.

Distributed System

A system whose components are located on different networked computers, which communicate and coordinate their actions by passing messages.

Connections to Related Concepts

A simpler problem that focuses on the impossibility of guaranteed coordination when messages might be lost, setting the stage for the added complexity of traitors.

The Prisoner's Dilemma

Explores the logic of trust and defection in game theory, offering insights into why individuals might choose self-interested actions even when cooperation would yield a better collective outcome.
