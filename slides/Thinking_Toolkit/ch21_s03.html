Defensive Design / Poka-Yoke

These terms describe the application of the engineering Murphy's Law. It's the practice of "mistake-proofing" designs to make errors impossible, or at least non-catastrophic.

Designer's Responsibility

The designer's role is to assume incompetent use and design systems that are robust enough to withstand such use, rather than relying on perfect user competence.

Statistical Reality of Complex Systems

In systems with many components, the probability of at least one part failing is very high, even if individual parts have high reliability. This "arithmetic," not bad luck, necessitates redundancy.

What is Betteridge's Law of Headlines?

Betteridge's Law states that any headline ending in a question mark can be answered by the word 'no'.

Who articulated Betteridge's Law and when?

Ian Betteridge, a British technology journalist, articulated the principle in a 2009 blog post.

How is Betteridge's Law useful as a quick filter for readers?

It helps readers evaluate sensational claims quickly, promoting skepticism and inoculating against common forms of journalistic overclaiming.

What should be your default assumption when you encounter a question-mark headline?

Your default assumption should be skepticism, as the publication is almost certainly overselling the evidence.
