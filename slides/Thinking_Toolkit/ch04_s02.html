Everyday Utility

This heuristic works reasonably well in many daily situations. If an animal looks like a duck, walks like a duck, and quacks like a duck, it's highly probable that it is, in fact, a duck.

Predictable Failures

The problem arises when resemblance and actual probability diverge. The heuristic ignores crucial statistical information, particularly base rates.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the Conjunction Fallacy?

The error of judging a conjunction of two events (e.g., A and B) as more probable than one of the constituent events (e.g., A) alone.

When applying the representativeness heuristic, what does the mind substitute for actual probability?

Resemblance to a stereotype or a mental portrait of a typical member of a category.

What critical statistical information does the representativeness heuristic often cause us to ignore?

Actual statistical base rates, sample sizes, and the laws of probability.

What common idiom is analogous to judging likelihood using the representativeness heuristic?
