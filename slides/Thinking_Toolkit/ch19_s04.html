Sturgeon's Law in the Internet Age

Increased Relevance

The proliferation of content online (social media, self-publishing, open-access journals) has democratized production dramatically.

Content Explosion

This has led to an enormous increase in the *absolute quantity* of both "crud" and excellence. The *percentage* (90/10) may remain similar.

Shift in Challenge

The primary challenge has moved from **scarcity** (not enough good material) to **curation** (too much material, requiring effective methods to find the good stuff).

Connections to Other Concepts

Base Rate Neglect

The tendency to ignore prior probabilities or general information in favor of specific, vivid information.

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms one's pre-existing beliefs or hypotheses.

The Availability Heuristic

The mental shortcut where people rely on immediate examples that come to mind. Vivid bad examples might seem more prevalent than quiet good ones.
