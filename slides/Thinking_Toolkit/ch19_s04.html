Connections to Other Concepts

Base Rate Neglect

The tendency to ignore prior probabilities or general information in favor of specific, vivid information.

Confirmation Bias

The tendency to search for, interpret, favor, and recall information in a way that confirms one's pre-existing beliefs or hypotheses.

The Availability Heuristic

The mental shortcut where people rely on immediate examples that come to mind. Vivid bad examples might seem more prevalent than quiet good ones.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who was Theodore Sturgeon, and what was his primary profession?

Theodore Sturgeon (1918–1985) was an American science fiction and horror author.

What observation did Theodore Sturgeon make in 1957 to defend science fiction?

He observed that 'ninety percent of science fiction is crud, but ninety percent of everything is crud.'

What is the core statement of Sturgeon's Law?

Ninety percent of everything—movies, novels, academic papers, etc.—is mediocre or worse.

According to Sturgeon's Law, what is the significance of the 'ninety percent crud' observation?

It's not a crisis but the base rate, a normal distribution of quality across all domains. It sets expectations.

How does Sturgeon's Law serve as a 'calibration tool'?

It sets correct expectations, helping you allocate attention wisely by understanding that most content will be forgettable, and only a small percentage will ...

What common rhetorical trick does Sturgeon's Law help defend against?

Dismissing an entire category or field by selectively pointing to its worst examples (e.g., 'Modern art is nonsense — look at this blank canvas').

Key Concepts (2/2)

Does pointing to the brilliant ten percent redeem the mediocre ninety percent, according to Sturgeon's Law?

No. The existence of excellent examples (e.g., Shakespeare) does not mean that most content in that field is good.

What type of reasoning failure is common when judging a field by its extremes (worst or best examples)?

Failures of base-rate reasoning.

How has the relevance of Sturgeon's Law changed in the age of the internet?

It has become more relevant due to the explosion of content and democratized production, which increases both the absolute quantity of 'crud' and excellence.

What challenge has shifted in the internet age regarding Sturgeon's Law?

The challenge has shifted from scarcity (not enough good material) to curation (too much material, needing to find the good stuff).

What is the practical skill needed in a 'noisy world' where ninety percent of content is mediocre?

Developing filters such as taste, expertise, trusted curators, and critical judgment to find the 'signal' (the good ten percent).

What was Sturgeon's Law originally called?

Sturgeon's Revelation.
