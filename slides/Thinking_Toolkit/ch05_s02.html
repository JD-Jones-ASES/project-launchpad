"Labor Leads to Love"

This core principle summarizes that the effort we put into creating something makes us attach more value and affection to it.

Mitigation

To counteract the bias of overvaluing one's own creations, it is crucial to seek objective, outside evaluation from individuals who were not involved in the creation process.

IKEA Effect

The cognitive bias where people place a disproportionately high value on objects they have partially or fully created.

The researchers credited with formally naming and experimentally demonstrating the IKEA Effect in their 2012 paper published in the *Journal of Consumer Psychology*.

Connections to Other Cognitive Biases

Dunning-Kruger Effect

Overestimating one's own competence, similar to overvaluing one's creations.

Confirmation Bias

Actively seeking evidence that confirms the goodness of one's own creations while ignoring contradictory evidence.

Sunk-Cost Fallacy

Continuing to invest in a project due to past investments (time, effort) rather than current objective value, which can amplify the IKEA Effect, especially in professional projects.
