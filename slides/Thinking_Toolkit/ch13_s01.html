Important Terms (1/2)

Brain in a Vat (BIV)

The hypothetical scenario where a disembodied brain is sustained in a nutrient solution and hooked up to a computer that generates all its sensory input, creating a perfectly simulated reality.

Evil Demon

In Descartes's Meditations on First Philosophy, a hypothetical, supremely powerful and cunning deceiver responsible for creating a complete illusion of reality.

Cogito

Short for Cogito ergo sum; the realization of one's own existence as a thinking being, derived from the act of doubting.

Meditations on First Philosophy (1641)

Ren\'e Descartes's seminal philosophical work where he develops his method of systematic doubt and the evil demon hypothesis.

Reason, Truth and History (1981)

Hilary Putnam's book where he presents his self-refutation argument against the brain-in-a-vat hypothesis.

Who introduced the "Experience Machine" thought experiment and in what work?

Robert Nozick in his 1974 book, Anarchy, State, and Utopia.

What is the fundamental concept behind the Experience Machine?

It is a hypothetical machine that can provide any desired experience, indistinguishable from reality, making users believe they are living a real life withou...

What philosophical view does the Experience Machine primarily challenge?

Hedonism, the view that pleasure or positive subjective experience is the only thing that is intrinsically good.

According to Nozick, if hedonism were correct, what should be people's reaction to the Experience Machine?

People should find the machine irresistible, as it offers a lifetime of perfect, tailor-made experiences without possibility of pain or disappointment.

What is the typical response of most people when confronted with the choice to plug into the Experience Machine?

Most people say they would not plug in.

What is Nozick's first reason why people typically refuse the Experience Machine?

We want to do things, not merely experience doing them; we value genuine accomplishment over just the feeling of accomplishment.
