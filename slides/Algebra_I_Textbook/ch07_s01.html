Greatest Common Factor and Factoring by Grouping

Study Slides

Finding the Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Coefficients

The GCF of 12 and 18 is 6.

Variables

For $x$, the smallest exponent is 2 ($x^2$). For $y$, the smallest exponent is 2 ($y^2$).

Result

The GCF is $6x^2y^2$.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What algebraic process is defined as writing a product as a multiplication of simpler expressions?

Factoring

Factoring is considered the reverse process of which algebraic operation?

Multiplication

Why is factoring considered one of the most important skills in algebra?

It allows for solving equations, simplifying expressions, and analyzing functions.

What is the first step to take in any factoring problem?

Look for a greatest common factor (GCF).

Term: Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of Monomials

The product of the GCF of numerical coefficients and the highest power of each variable shared by every monomial.

When finding the GCF of monomials, which power of a shared variable is used?

The smallest exponent that appears among the monomials.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the three steps to find the GCF of a set of monomials?

Find the GCF of coefficients, find the smallest exponent for shared variables, and multiply those results.

What is the GCF of the numerical coefficients $12$ and $18$?

$6$

Between the monomials $12x^3y^2$ and $18x^2y^5$, what is the GCF of the $x$ variables?

$x^2$

Between the monomials $12x^3y^2$ and $18x^2y^5$, what is the GCF of the $y$ variables?

$y^2$

What is the GCF of $12x^3y^2$ and $18x^2y^5$?

$6x^2y^2$

What are the three steps to factor a polynomial by removing the GCF?

Find the GCF of all terms, divide each term by the GCF, and write the expression as $GCF(remaining\ polynomial)$.
