The Structure of the Coordinate Plane

Axes

The horizontal number line is known as the $x$-axis, and the vertical number line is the $y$-axis.

The Origin

The point where the two axes intersect is called the origin, represented by the coordinates $(0, 0)$.

Quadrants

The axes divide the plane into four distinct regions called quadrants. These are numbered I, II, III, and IV, moving in a counter-clockwise direction starting from the upper right.

Ordered Pairs and Plotting

$x$-coordinate

The first number indicates the horizontal distance from the origin.

$y$-coordinate

The second number indicates the vertical distance from the origin.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Coordinate Plane

A two-dimensional surface formed by two perpendicular number lines (axes) intersecting at their zero points.

Distance (Horizontal/Vertical)

The length between two points on the same axis line, calculated as the absolute value of the difference between their non-matching coordinates.

Midpoint

The point located exactly halfway between the endpoints of a segment.

Ordered Pair

A pair of numbers $(x, y)$ used to locate a point on the coordinate plane, where $x$ is the horizontal coordinate and $y$ is the vertical coordinate.

Origin

The center of the coordinate plane $(0, 0)$, where the $x$-axis and $y$-axis intersect.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Quadrant

One of the four regions of the coordinate plane divided by the axes, numbered I through IV counter-clockwise.

X-axis

The horizontal number line in the coordinate plane.

X-coordinate

The first number in an ordered pair, representing the horizontal distance from the origin.

Y-axis

The vertical number line in the coordinate plane.

Y-coordinate

The second number in an ordered pair, representing the vertical distance from the origin.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the term for the surface formed by two perpendicular number lines intersecting at their zero points?

The coordinate plane

In the coordinate plane, what is the specific name given to the horizontal number line?

The $x$-axis

In the coordinate plane, what is the specific name given to the vertical number line?

The $y$-axis

What is the name of the point $(0, 0)$ where the $x$-axis and $y$-axis intersect?

The origin

The axes divide the coordinate plane into four regions known as _____.

Quadrants

In which direction are the quadrants of the coordinate plane numbered?

Counter-clockwise

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which quadrant is located in the upper right section of the coordinate plane?

Quadrant I

Term: Ordered Pair

Definition: A pair of numbers $(x, y)$ that gives the specific position of a point in the coordinate plane.

In the ordered pair $(x, y)$, what does the first number $x$ represent?

The $x$-coordinate (horizontal distance from the origin)

In the ordered pair $(x, y)$, what does the second number $y$ represent?

The $y$-coordinate (vertical distance from the origin)

To plot a point $(a, b)$, what is the required starting location in the coordinate plane?

The origin

When plotting $(a, b)$, if $a > 0$, in which horizontal direction should you move?

Right
