"And" Inequalities (Intersections)

Graphical Representation

The solution is represented by the intersection—the area where the two individual solution sets overlap.

Notation

The intersection of two sets $A$ and $B$ is denoted as $A \cap B$.

Compact Form

"And" inequalities can often be written in a condensed format. For example, $x > 1$ and $x \le 5$ is written as $1 < x \le 5$.

"Or" Inequalities (Unions)

Graphical Representation

The solution is the union—the combination of all elements found in either of the solution sets.

Notation

The union of two sets is denoted by the symbol $\cup$.

Solving Methodology

Unlike "and" inequalities, "or" inequalities must be solved as two entirely separate problems. The final solution is expressed by joining the two resulting sets.

Glossary of Important Terms

Compound Inequality

A mathematical sentence formed by joining two inequalities with the word "and" or the word "or."

Intersection of Sets

Written as $A \cap B$, this is the set of all elements that belong to both set $A$ and set $B$.

Union of Sets

Written as $A \cup B$, this is the set of all elements that belong to at least one of the two sets.

Interval Notation

A method of writing solution sets using parentheses `()` for exclusive boundaries and brackets `[]` for inclusive boundaries.

No Solution ($\emptyset$)

A result that occurs in an "and" inequality when there is no numerical value that can satisfy both conditions at the same time.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a compound inequality?

A sentence formed by joining two inequalities with the word "and" or the word "or."

Under what condition is a compound inequality joined by "and" considered true?

It is true only when both individual parts are true.

Graphically, what does the solution of an "and" compound inequality represent?

The overlap or intersection of the two individual solution sets.

How can the compound inequality $x > 1$ and $x \le 5$ be expressed in compact notation?

$1 < x \le 5$

Term: Intersection of Sets

Definition: The set of elements that belong to both set $A$ and set $B$, written as $A \cap B$.

When solving a three-part inequality like $-3 < 2x + 1 \le 7$, what is the primary goal?

To isolate the variable $x$ in the middle part of the inequality.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the first step in solving the inequality $-3 < 2x + 1 \le 7$?

Subtract $1$ from all three parts of the inequality.

After subtracting $1$ from all parts of $-3 < 2x + 1 \le 7$, what is the resulting inequality?

$-4 < 2x \le 6$

What operation is used to isolate $x$ in the inequality $-4 < 2x \le 6$?

Divide all three parts of the inequality by $2$.

What is the final inequality solution for $-3 < 2x + 1 \le 7$?

$-2 < x \le 3$

Convert the inequality $-2 < x \le 3$ into interval notation.

$(-2, 3]$

When is a compound inequality joined by the word "or" considered true?

It is true when at least one part of the inequality is true.
