Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

$ac$ Method

A technique for factoring trinomials by finding two integers whose product is $ac$ and whose sum is $b$, then factoring by grouping.

Coefficient

The numerical factor of a term (e.g., $a$ and $b$ in $ax^2 + bx$).

Constant Term

The term in a polynomial that does not contain a variable (represented by $c$).

Factor by Grouping

A method of factoring polynomials with four terms by grouping them into pairs and factoring out the GCF of each pair.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The highest factor that is common to all terms in an expression.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Leading Coefficient

The coefficient of the term with the highest degree (represented by $a$).

Prime over the Integers

A polynomial that cannot be written as a product of polynomials with integer coefficients.

Trinomial

A polynomial consisting of three terms.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the general form of the trinomials discussed in Chapter 7, Section 3?

$ax^2 + bx + c$ where $a eq 1$

Which method is used to factor trinomials of the form $ax^2 + bx + c$?

The $ac$ method

In the $ac$ method, what is the first calculation performed?

The product $ac$

In the $ac$ method, what is the required product for the integers $m$ and $n$?

$m \cdot n = ac$

In the $ac$ method, what is the required sum for the integers $m$ and $n$?

$m + n = b$

The $ac$ method: Compute $ac$. Find two integers $m, n$ such that $m \cdot n = ac$ and $m + n = b$. Rewrite $bx$ as _____. Factor by grouping.

$mx + nx$

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which factoring technique is applied after rewriting the middle term in the $ac$ method?

Factor by grouping

When is a trinomial considered 'prime over the integers' in the context of the $ac$ method?

When no pair of integers $m$ and $n$ satisfy the product and sum requirements.

What is the very first step to take before applying the $ac$ method to a trinomial?

Check for and factor out a Greatest Common Factor (GCF).

What is the advantage of removing the GCF before using the $ac$ method?

It makes the remaining coefficients smaller and the factoring process easier.

In the example $2x^2 + 7x + 3$, what is the value of $a$?

$2$

In the example $2x^2 + 7x + 3$, what is the value of $c$?

$3$
