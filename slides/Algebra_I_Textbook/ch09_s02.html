Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Rational expressions operate using the same four arithmetic rules as ______.

numerical fractions

What is the key initial step recommended before performing operations with rational expressions?

Factor all numerators and denominators completely.

What is the final step in the general process of operating with rational expressions after factoring?

Simplify the expression by dividing out common factors.

According to the property for multiplying rational expressions, $\frac{P}{Q} \cdot \frac{R}{S} =$ ______.

$\frac{PR}{QS}$

In the multiplication property $\frac{P}{Q} \cdot \frac{R}{S} = \frac{PR}{QS}$, what are the two required conditions for $Q$ and $S$?

$Q e 0$ and $S e 0$

Step 1 in multiplying rational expressions is to ______.

factor all numerators and denominators completely

Key Concepts (2/2)

Step 2 in multiplying rational expressions is to ______ factors common to any numerator and any denominator.

divide out

What is the final step in the procedure for multiplying rational expressions?

Multiply the remaining factors.

When multiplying $\frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 5} \cdot \frac{x + 5}{x + 2}$, what is the factored form of the first numerator?

$(x+2)(x-2)$

In the example $\frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 5} \cdot \frac{x + 5}{x + 2}$, which factor is common to the first denominator and the second numerator?

$(x+5)$

The product of $\frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 5} \cdot \frac{x + 5}{x + 2}$ simplifies to which expression?

$x - 2$

What are the excluded values for $x$ in the simplified product $\frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 5} \cdot \frac{x + 5}{x + 2} = x - 2$?

$x e -5$ and $x e -2$
