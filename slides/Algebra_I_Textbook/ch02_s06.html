Percent Applications and Simple Interest

Study Slides

Understanding Percents

Example

$45\%$ is equivalent to $\frac{45}{100}$ or $0.45$.

Finding a Part

Procedure

To find $35\%$ of $80$, convert $35\%$ to $0.35$ and multiply: $0.35 \times 80 = 28$.

Finding a Percent

Procedure

To find what percent $12$ is of $50$, divide $12$ by $50$ to get $0.24$. Convert $0.24$ to $24\%$.

Percent Increase and Decrease

Scenario. Calculation Steps

Example

Percent Decrease. $\frac{\text{Original Price} - \text{Sale Price}}{\text{Original Price}}$

A $\$60$ jacket on sale for $\$45$: $\frac{15}{60} = 0.25$ or $25\%$

Percent Increase. $\frac{\text{New Price} - \text{Original Price}}{\text{Original Price}}$

A $\$40$ shirt now costing $\$52$: $\frac{12}{40} = 0.30$ or $30\%$

The Simple Interest Formula

$I$ (Interest)

The amount of interest earned or charged.

$P$ (Principal)

The starting amount of money.

$r$ (Annual Interest Rate)

The rate expressed as a decimal (e.g., $4\%$ becomes $0.04$).

$t$ (Time)

The duration of the investment or loan in years.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the literal meaning of the term "percent"?

Per hundred.

What is the decimal representation of $45\%$?

$0.45$

What is the fraction representation of $45\%$?

$\frac{45}{100}$

What must be done to a percent before using it in an algebraic equation?

Convert it to a decimal or a fraction.

State the standard Percent Equation using the terms part, percent, and whole.

$part = percent \cdot whole$

In the percent equation $part = pw$, what does the variable $p$ represent?

The percent written as a decimal.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the percent equation $part = pw$, what does the variable $w$ represent?

The whole.

What is the first step in finding a part, such as "What is $35\%$ of $80$"?

Write the percent as a decimal ($0.35$).

What is the result of $35\%$ of $80$?

$28$

What is $18\%$ of $250$?

$45$

When solving for the percent $p$ in $12 = p \cdot 50$, what is the result of the division $\frac{12}{50}$?

$0.24$

What is the final step after finding the decimal value $0.24$ in a "finding the percent" problem?

Convert the decimal back to a percent ($24\%$).
