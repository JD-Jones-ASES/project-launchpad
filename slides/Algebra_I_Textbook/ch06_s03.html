Principles and Applications of Scientific Notation

Study Slides

Defining the Notation

The Coefficient ($a$)

The factor $a$ must be a number greater than or equal to 1 and less than 10 ($1 \le a < 10$).

The Power of 10

The exponent $n$ must be an integer.

45,000,000. $4.5 \times 10^7$

Left (7 places)

0.00032. $3.2 \times 10^{-4}$

From Scientific Notation to Standard Form

• If $n > 0$, move the decimal point $n$ places to the right.

• If $n < 0$, move the decimal point $n$ places to the left.

Arithmetic Operations

Glossary of Key Terms

Coefficient

The factor $a$ in the scientific notation expression $a \times 10^n$; it must be a number where $1 \le a < 10$.

Integer

A whole number that can be positive, negative, or zero; used as the exponent $n$ in scientific notation.

Power of 10

The portion of the scientific notation expression consisting of the base 10 raised to an integer exponent ($10^n$).

Scientific Notation

A compact method of writing numbers as the product of a coefficient and a power of 10.

Standard Form

The traditional way of writing numbers (e.g., 93,000,000) without the use of exponents.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In scientific notation, what is the required range for the coefficient $a$?

The coefficient $a$ must satisfy the condition $1 \le a < 10$.

What type of number must the exponent $n$ be in the scientific notation form $a \times 10^n$?

The exponent $n$ must be an integer.

Term: Coefficient

Definition: The factor $a$ in the scientific notation expression $a \times 10^n$.

In the form $a \times 10^n$, what is the term used for the component $10^n$?

The power of 10.

What are two primary reasons for using scientific notation in science and engineering?

It provides a compact way to express very large or very small numbers while reducing errors from writing many zeros.

When converting a standard number to scientific notation, where should the decimal point be placed to determine the coefficient $a$?

The decimal point must be moved so that exactly one nonzero digit is to its left.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In standard to scientific conversion, if the decimal point is moved to the left, what is the sign of the exponent $n$?

The exponent $n$ is positive.

In standard to scientific conversion, if the decimal point is moved to the right, what is the sign of the exponent $n$?

The exponent $n$ is negative.

How is the value of the exponent $n$ determined when converting from standard form to scientific notation?

The value of $n$ is the count of how many places the decimal point was moved.

Write the number $45,000,000$ in scientific notation.

$4.5 \times 10^7$

How many places must the decimal point move to convert $45,000,000$ to scientific notation?

Seven places to the left.

Write the number $0.00032$ in scientific notation.

$3.2 \times 10^{-4}$
