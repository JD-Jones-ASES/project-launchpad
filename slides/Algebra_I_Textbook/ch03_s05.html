Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Absolute Value

A mathematical expression representing the distance of a number from zero, which is always non-negative ($\geq 0$).

"And" Compound Inequality

A mathematical statement where the solution must satisfy two conditions simultaneously, resulting in a single interval between two values.

GreatOR

A memory aid indicating that "greater than" absolute value inequalities translate into "or" compound inequalities.

Interval Notation

A method of writing solution sets using brackets $[ ]$ for inclusive values ($\leq, \geq$) and parentheses $( )$ for exclusive values $(<, >)$.

Isolation

The process of using algebraic operations to ensure the absolute value expression is the only term on one side of the inequality.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Less thAND

A memory aid indicating that "less than" absolute value inequalities translate into "and" compound inequalities.

"Or" Compound Inequality

A mathematical statement where the solution satisfies at least one of two conditions, often resulting in two separate rays pointing in opposite directions.
