Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the standard form of a quadratic equation?

$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$

In the standard form $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, what types of numbers must $a$, $b$, and $c$ be?

Real numbers

In the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, what is the necessary constraint on the coefficient $a$?

$a eq 0$

What is the highest power of the variable in a quadratic equation?

2

According to the Zero Product Property, if $ab = 0$, then what must be true of $a$ or $b$?

$a = 0$ or $b = 0$ (or both)

What property states that if the product of two factors is zero, at least one factor must be zero?

Zero Product Property

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the first step in solving a quadratic equation by factoring?

Write the equation in standard form ($ax^2 + bx + c = 0$).

After writing a quadratic equation in standard form, what is the next step in the factoring method?

Factor the left side completely.

Once a quadratic equation is factored as $(x - r_1)(x - r_2) = 0$, what property is applied next?

Zero Product Property

What is the final step in the process of solving a quadratic equation to ensure the answers are correct?

Check each solution in the original equation.

What is the factored form of the quadratic equation $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0$?

$(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0$

What are the solutions to the equation $(x - 2)(x - 3) = 0$?

$x = 2$ and $x = 3$
