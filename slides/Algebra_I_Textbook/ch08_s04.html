The Discriminant in Quadratic Equations

Study Slides

The Three Cases of the Discriminant

Discriminant Value ($\Delta$). Number of Real Solutions

Nature of Solutions

Positive ($\Delta > 0$). Two solutions

Distinct real numbers. If $\Delta$ is a perfect square, solutions are rational.

Zero ($\Delta = 0$). One solution

Exactly one real solution.

Negative ($\Delta < 0$). Zero solutions

No real solutions exist.

Choosing an Efficient Solution Method

Factoring

Use this method when the discriminant is a perfect square and the equation factors easily over integers.

Square Root Property

Best used when there is no linear term ($b = 0$) or when the equation is structured as $(ax + b)^2 = k$.

Quadratic Formula

A universal method that always works, especially useful when factoring is difficult or solutions involve radicals.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Discriminant

The expression $b^2 - 4ac$ used to determine the nature of the roots of a quadratic equation.

Linear Term

The part of the quadratic equation representing $bx$; if $b=0$, the equation has no linear term.

Perfect Square

A number that is the square of an integer (e.g., $1, 4, 9, 16, 25, 36, 49$). A perfect square discriminant indicates rational solutions.

Quadratic Formula

The formula $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}$, used to find the solutions of any quadratic equation.

Rational Solutions

Solutions that can be expressed as a fraction of two integers. These occur when the discriminant is a perfect square.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Real Solutions

Values of $x$ that satisfy the equation and are part of the set of real numbers. The discriminant indicates if zero, one, or two such values exist.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In the context of quadratic equations, what is the term for the expression $b^2 - 4ac$?

Discriminant

For the quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$, what is the formula for the discriminant?

$b^2 - 4ac$

What condition must be met by the coefficient $a$ in the equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ to use the discriminant?

$a eq 0$

Where is the discriminant located in the quadratic formula?

Under the radical sign

What does the value of the discriminant determine regarding the solutions of a quadratic equation?

The number and type of solutions

If the discriminant is positive ($b^2 - 4ac > 0$), how many real solutions does the equation have?

Two real solutions

Key Concepts (2/2)

If the discriminant equals zero ($b^2 - 4ac = 0$), how many real solutions exist?

Exactly one real solution

If the discriminant is negative ($b^2 - 4ac < 0$), how many real solutions are there?

No real solutions

When the discriminant is a positive perfect square, what specific type of real numbers are the solutions?

Rational numbers

A quadratic equation $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ can be solved by factoring over the integers if the discriminant is a _____.

Positive perfect square

What is the value of the discriminant for the equation $x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0$?

$0$

How many real solutions does the equation $x^2 - 6x + 9 = 0$ have?

Exactly one real solution
