Adding and Subtracting Real Numbers

Study Slides

Addition of Real Numbers

Scenario. Rule

Example

Adding Numbers with the Same Sign. Add their absolute values and keep the common sign.

$(-3) + (-5) = -8$

Adding Numbers with Different Signs. Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value. Keep the sign of the number with the larger absolute value.

$(-7) + 4 = -3$

Simplifying Complex Expressions

Step 1 (Rewrite)

$-4 + 7 + (-3) + (-2) + 5$

Step 2 (Group)

$(-4 + (-3) + (-2)) + (7 + 5)$

Step 3 (Combine)

$-9 + 12 = 3$

Real-World Applications

Temperature

Measuring fluctuations, such as a drop of $8^\circ$ from an initial $3^\circ$ ($3 - 8 = -5^\circ$).

Elevation

Determining position relative to sea level. For instance, descending $200$ feet from an elevation of $150$ feet results in $-50$ feet (or $50$ feet below sea level).

Business

Calculating profit and loss to determine the financial standing of an enterprise.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Absolute Value

The distance of a number from zero on a number line, regardless of its sign.

Addends

The numbers being added together in an addition problem.

Different Signs

A term describing a pair of numbers where one is positive and the other is negative.

Opposite

The number that, when added to a given number, results in a sum of zero (e.g., the opposite of $b$ is $-b$).

Real Numbers

The set of numbers that includes all rational and irrational numbers, which can be represented on a continuous number line.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Same Sign

A term describing a pair of numbers that are either both positive or both negative.

Signed Numbers

Numbers that are explicitly identified as being positive or negative.

Subtraction Rule

The mathematical principle stating that subtracting a number is equivalent to adding its opposite.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the rule for adding two real numbers that have the same sign?

Add their absolute values and keep the common sign.

When adding two numbers with different signs, how do you determine the numerical part of the result?

Subtract the smaller absolute value from the larger absolute value.

In the addition of two numbers with different signs, which sign is kept for the final result?

The sign of the number with the larger absolute value.

What is the primary rule for subtracting one real number from another?

Add the opposite of the number being subtracted.

According to the subtraction rule, what is the equivalent addition expression for $ a - b $?

$ a + (-b) $

To add two numbers with the same sign, you must add their _____ and keep the common sign.

absolute values
