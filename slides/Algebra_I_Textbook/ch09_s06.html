Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

State the Pythagorean Theorem.

In a right triangle with legs of lengths $a$ and $b$ and hypotenuse of length $c$, $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

In a right triangle, define the hypotenuse.

The hypotenuse is the side opposite the right angle and is always the longest side.

What is the converse of the Pythagorean Theorem?

If $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ for the sides of a triangle, then the triangle is a right triangle.

In the Pythagorean Theorem ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$), which variables represent the legs and which represents the hypotenuse?

$a$ and $b$ represent the lengths of the legs, and $c$ represents the length of the hypotenuse.

A right triangle has legs of lengths 5 and 12. Find the length of the hypotenuse.

Using $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$: $5^2 + 12^2 = c^2 \ 25 + 144 = c^2 \ 169 = c^2 \ c = \sqrt{169} = 13$.

A right triangle has a hypotenuse of length 10 and one leg of length 6. Find the length of the other leg.

Using $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$: $6^2 + b^2 = 10^2 \ 36 + b^2 = 100 \ b^2 = 64 \ b = \sqrt{64} = 8$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How is the Distance Formula derived?

The Distance Formula is derived by applying the Pythagorean Theorem to a right triangle formed by the two points in the coordinate plane.

When deriving the Distance Formula for points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, what are the lengths of the horizontal and vertical legs of the right triangle formed?

The horizontal leg has length $|x_2 - x_1|$ and the vertical leg has length $|y_2 - y_1|$.

State the Distance Formula for two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$.

The distance $d$ between two points is $d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$.

Find the distance between the points $(-2, 3)$ and $(4, -1)$.

$d = \sqrt{(4 - (-2))^2 + (-1 - 3)^2} = \sqrt{(6)^2 + (-4)^2} = \sqrt{36 + 16} = \sqrt{52} = 2\sqrt{13}$.

State the Midpoint Formula for a segment joining $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$.

The midpoint $M$ is $M = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \; \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$.

Find the midpoint between the points $(1, 5)$ and $(7, -3)$.

$M = \left(\frac{1 + 7}{2}, \; \frac{5 + (-3)}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{8}{2}, \; \frac{2}{2}\right) = (4, 1)$.

Quiz Question 4

If the midpoint of a line segment is $(3, -2)$ and one endpoint is $(1, 4)$, what are the coordinates of the other endpoint?

A. $(5, -8)$

B. $(2, 1)$

C. $(4, -6)$

D. $(7, -10)$

Hint: The midpoint is the average of the endpoints; set up an equation for $x$ and $y$ separately to solve for the unknown.

Quiz Question 5

A right triangle has a hypotenuse of length $10\sqrt{5}$ and one leg of length $6\sqrt{5}$. Find the length of the other leg.

A. $8\sqrt{5}$

B. $4\sqrt{5}$

C. $160$

D. $4\sqrt{10}$

Hint: Square both given side lengths, subtract the squared leg from the squared hypotenuse, and then take the square root.

D. $(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2})$
