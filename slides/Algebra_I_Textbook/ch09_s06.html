Quiz Question 1

Which of the following sets of side lengths does NOT form a right triangle?

A. $9, 12, 16$

B. $8, 15, 17$

C. $7, 24, 25$

D. $\sqrt{7}, 3, 4$

Hint: Check if the sum of the squares of the two shorter sides equals the square of the longest side.

Quiz Question 2

What is the distance between the points $(-7, 4)$ and $(5, -1)$ in the coordinate plane?

A. $13$

B. $\sqrt{13}$

C. $17$

D. $\sqrt{37}$

Hint: Remember to subtract the $x$-coordinates and $y$-coordinates carefully, noting the double negatives.

Quiz Question 3

Calculate the distance between the points $(1, 2, 3)$ and $(3, 4, 2)$ in a three-dimensional coordinate system.

A. $3$

B. $\sqrt{5}$

C. $9$

D. $\sqrt{33}$

Hint: Extend the two-dimensional distance formula by including the squared difference of the $z$-coordinates.

Quiz Question 4

If the midpoint of a line segment is $(3, -2)$ and one endpoint is $(1, 4)$, what are the coordinates of the other endpoint?

A. $(5, -8)$

B. $(2, 1)$

C. $(4, -6)$

D. $(7, -10)$

Hint: The midpoint is the average of the endpoints; set up an equation for $x$ and $y$ separately to solve for the unknown.

Quiz Question 5

A right triangle has a hypotenuse of length $10\sqrt{5}$ and one leg of length $6\sqrt{5}$. Find the length of the other leg.

A. $8\sqrt{5}$

B. $4\sqrt{5}$

C. $160$

D. $4\sqrt{10}$

Hint: Square both given side lengths, subtract the squared leg from the squared hypotenuse, and then take the square root.

Quiz Question 6

When deriving the distance formula between $A(x_1, y_1)$ and $B(x_2, y_2)$, a right triangle is formed by a horizontal and a vertical segment. At what point do these segments meet to form the right...

A. $(x_2, y_1)$

B. $(x_1, y_2)$

C. $(x_1 + x_2, y_1 + y_2)$

D. $(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2})$

Hint: The right angle is formed where the $x$-coordinate of one point meets the $y$-coordinate of the other.
