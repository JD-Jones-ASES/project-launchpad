Zero and Negative Exponents

Study Slides

Core Properties of Exponents

Property. Definition

Mathematical Expression

Zero Exponent. Any nonzero base raised to the zero power equals 1.

$a^0 = 1$

Negative Exponent. A nonzero base raised to a negative exponent is the reciprocal of the base raised to the corresponding positive exponent.

$a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$ or $\frac{1}{a^{-n}} = a^n$

Quotient of Powers. To divide powers with the same base, keep the base and subtract the exponents.

$\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$

Power of a Quotient. To raise a quotient to a power, raise both the numerator and the denominator to that power.

$(\frac{a}{b})^n = \frac{a^n}{b^n}$

Logical Justifications

Resulting in Zero

$\frac{a^3}{a^3} = a^{3-3} = a^0 = 1$

Resulting in Negative

$\frac{a^2}{a^5} = a^{2-5} = a^{-3} = \frac{1}{a^3}$

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Base

The number or variable that is being raised to a power.

Exponent

The number that indicates how many times the base is used as a factor.

Negative Exponent

An exponent less than zero which indicates the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive version of that exponent.

Nonzero Real Number

Any real number (positive or negative rational or irrational number) except for zero.

Power of a Quotient

A rule stating that a fraction raised to a power is equal to the numerator raised to that power divided by the denominator raised to that power.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Quotient of Powers

A rule stating that when dividing two powers with the same base, the exponents should be subtracted.

Reciprocal

The result of dividing 1 by a given number; for a term $a^n$, the reciprocal is $\frac{1}{a^n}$.

Zero Exponent

An exponent of zero which, when applied to any nonzero base, results in a value of 1.
