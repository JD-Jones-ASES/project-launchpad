Glossary of Key Terms

Contradiction

A mathematical statement that is false and contains no variables (e.g., $6 = 10$). In a system of equations, it indicates there is no solution because the lines are parallel.

Identity

A mathematical statement that is always true (e.g., $18 = 18$). In a system of equations, it indicates that the two equations describe the same line, resulting in infinitely many solutions.

Substitution Method

An algebraic technique used to solve systems of equations by isolating one variable and replacing it in the second equation to reduce the system to a single variable.

System of Equations

A set of two or more equations with the same variables that are solved simultaneously to find common solutions.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the main advantage of the substitution method over graphing when solving a system of equations?

The substitution method produces exact solutions, whereas reading coordinates from a graph can be imprecise.

What is the first step in solving a system of two equations by substitution?

Solve one equation for one variable (choose the variable that is easiest to isolate).

What is the second step in the substitution method?

Substitute the expression from Step 1 into the other equation.

What is the third step in the substitution method?

Solve the resulting equation in one variable.

What is the fourth step in the substitution method?

Substitute the value from Step 3 back into the equation from Step 1 to find the other variable.

What is the final step in the substitution method?

Check the solution in both original equations.

Key Concepts (2/2)

After performing the substitution step, what type of equation should you expect to solve next?

An equation with only one variable.

For the system y = 3x - 5 and 2x + y = 10, what expression would you substitute for 'y' in the second equation?

3x - 5

Using substitution for the system y = 3x - 5 and 2x + y = 10, what is the value of x?

x = 3

After finding x=3 for the system y = 3x - 5 and 2x + y = 10, what is the value of y?

y = 4

What is the solution to the system y = 3x - 5 and 2x + y = 10?

(3, 4)

When solving a system by substitution, what algebraic outcome indicates that the system has no solution?

The appearance of a contradiction, which is a false statement (e.g., 6 = 10) that does not contain a variable.
