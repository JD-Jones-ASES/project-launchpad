Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the slope-intercept form of a linear equation?

$y = mx + b$

In the equation $y = mx + b$, what does the variable $m$ represent?

The slope of the line.

In the equation $y = mx + b$, what does the variable $b$ represent?

The $y$-intercept.

Define the $y$-intercept of a line.

The $y$-coordinate of the point where the line crosses the $y$-axis.

Why is slope-intercept form considered the most common form for linear equations?

The slope and $y$-intercept can be read directly from the equation.

What is the equation of a line with a slope of $3$ and a $y$-intercept of $-4$?

$y = 3x - 4$

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the point-slope form of a linear equation?

$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$

In the point-slope form $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$, what does $(x_1, y_1)$ represent?

Any specific point on the line.

When is the point-slope form particularly useful?

When the slope and a point are known, but the point is not the $y$-intercept.

What is the first step to write the equation of a line with slope $-2$ passing through $(3, 1)$?

Substitute the values into the point-slope form: $y - 1 = -2(x - 3)$.

Convert the point-slope equation $y - 1 = -2(x - 3)$ into slope-intercept form.

$y = -2x + 7$

Write the point-slope form of a line with slope $4$ that passes through $(-1, 2)$.

$y - 2 = 4(x - (-1))$
