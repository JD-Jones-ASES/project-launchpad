Properties of Square Roots

Product Property of Square Roots

For $a \ge 0$ and $b \ge 0$, $\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$.

Quotient Property of Square Roots

For $a \ge 0$ and $b > 0$, $\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of the principal square root of $a$ ($a \ge 0$)?

The non-negative number $b$ such that $b^2 = a$.

Which symbol is used to denote a radical sign?

$\sqrt{x}$

What is the term for the expression located under a radical sign?

Radicand

In the expression $\sqrt{3x + 7}$, what is the radicand?

$3x + 7$

Why must a radicand be non-negative when working within the set of real numbers?

The square root of a negative number is not a real number.

The Product Property of Square Roots states that $\sqrt{ab} = \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b}$ under what conditions for $a$ and $b$?

$a \ge 0$ and $b \ge 0$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What formula defines the Quotient Property of Square Roots?

$\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$

In the Quotient Property $\sqrt{\frac{a}{b}} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$, why must $b$ be strictly greater than $0$?

To ensure the denominator is not zero.

What is the first requirement for a radical expression to be in simplest form?

The radicand must have no perfect square factor other than $1$.

What is the second requirement for a radical expression to be in simplest form regarding fractions?

The radicand must contain no fractions.

What is the third requirement for a radical expression to be in simplest form regarding denominators?

No radical sign may appear in a denominator.

To simplify a radical, what should be factored out from the radicand?

The largest perfect square factor.
