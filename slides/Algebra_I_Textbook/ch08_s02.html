Solving Quadratic Equations by Square Roots

Study Slides

The Square Root Property

The Rule

If $x^2 = k$, then $x = \sqrt{k}$ or $x = -\sqrt{k}$. This is commonly written as $x = \pm\sqrt{k}$.

Solutions based on the value of $k$

Glossary of Important Terms

Quiz Question 1

If the equation $x^2 = k$ has no real solutions, what must be true about the value of $k$?

A. $k < 0$

B. $k = 0$

C. $k > 0$

D. $k$ is not a perfect square

Hint: Consider the property of squares regarding negative numbers in the real number system.

Quiz Question 2

Solve the quadratic equation $x^2 = 72$ and express the answer in simplified radical form.

A. $x = \pm 6\sqrt{2}$

B. $x = 6\sqrt{2}$

C. $x = \pm 2\sqrt{6}$

D. $x = \pm 36\sqrt{2}$

Hint: Look for the largest perfect square that divides into $72$ and remember the $\pm$ symbol.

Quiz Question 3

Which of the following expressions is NOT in simplified radical form?

A. $\sqrt{\frac{3}{5}}$

B. $2\sqrt{11}$

C. $5\sqrt{3}$

D. $\frac{\sqrt{7}}{2}$

Hint: Review the three specific criteria listed for simplified radical form in the text.

Quiz Question 4

Solve the equation $(x - 5)^2 = 18$ for $x$.

A. $x = 5 \pm 3\sqrt{2}$

B. $x = -5 \pm 3\sqrt{2}$

C. $x = 5 \pm 9\sqrt{2}$

D. $x = 23$ or $x = -13$

Hint: Start by taking the square root of both sides and then isolate the variable.

Quiz Question 5

Solve the quadratic equation $3x^2 - 150 = 0$.

A. $x = \pm 5\sqrt{2}$

B. $x = \pm 50$

C. $x = \pm 10\sqrt{3}$

D. No real solution

Hint: Isolate the squared term first before applying the Square Root Property.

Quiz Question 6

What are the solutions to the equation $(2x + 1)^2 = 49$?

A. $x = 3$ and $x = -4$

B. $x = \pm 3$

C. $x = 4$ and $x = -3$

D. $x = \pm 7$

Hint: The square root of $49$ yields two separate linear equations to solve.
