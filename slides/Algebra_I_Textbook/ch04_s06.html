Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Correlation

The general direction or relationship followed by the points in a scatter plot (positive, negative, or none).

Correlation Coefficient ($r$)

A precise numerical measure of how closely data follows a linear trend (introduced in advanced courses).

Extrapolation

The process of estimating a value outside the range of observed data points.

Interpolation

The process of estimating a value within the range of observed data points.

Line of Best Fit

Another name for a trend line; a straight line that represents the general direction of data on a scatter plot.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Negative Correlation

A relationship where $y$ decreases as $x$ increases, shown by points trending downward.

Positive Correlation

A relationship where $y$ increases as $x$ increases, shown by points trending upward.

Scatter Plot

A coordinate graph where each point $(x, y)$ represents an observation of two related variables.

Trend Line

A straight line drawn through data points on a scatter plot to model the linear pattern of the data.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What type of graph uses points in a coordinate plane to represent two related variables?

A scatter plot

In a scatter plot, what does each individual point $(x, y)$ represent?

One observation of two related variables

Term: Positive Correlation

Definition: A relationship where $y$ tends to increase as $x$ increases.

How do points trend visually in a positive correlation?

They trend upward from left to right.

Term: Negative Correlation

Definition: A relationship where $y$ tends to decrease as $x$ increases.

How do points trend visually in a negative correlation?

They trend downward from left to right.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is indicated when points are scattered on a plot with no obvious upward or downward trend?

No correlation

What is a straight line drawn through data points to follow their general direction called?

A trend line (or line of best fit)

According to the general rule, how should a trend line be positioned relative to the data points?

It should have roughly the same number of points above it as below it.

What is the primary purpose of a trend line in a scatter plot?

To describe the data pattern with a linear equation.

Formula: Slope ($m$) of a trend line passing through $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$

$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

When using two points to write a trend line equation, which linear form is often used first after finding the slope?

Point-slope form
