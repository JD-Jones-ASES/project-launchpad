Study Slides

The Role of the Vertex in Optimization

Maximum Value

If $a < 0$, the parabola opens downward, and the vertex represents the maximum value.

Minimum Value

If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upward, and the vertex represents the minimum value.

Coordinates

The $x$-coordinate of the vertex ($x = \frac{-b}{2a}$) indicates **where** the optimal value occurs, while the $y$-coordinate ($f(x)$) indicates **what** the optimal value is.

Area and Revenue Optimization

Area Optimization

Often involves using a perimeter constraint to express area as a quadratic function of a single variable ($A = \text{length} \times \text{width}$).

Revenue Optimization

Revenue is calculated as $\text{Price} \times \text{Quantity}$. When price changes affect the quantity sold, the resulting revenue function is typically quadratic.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

$h_0$ (Initial Height)

The starting vertical position of an object before it is launched.

$v_0$ (Initial Velocity)

The starting upward speed of an object at $t = 0$.

Optimization

The process of finding the best possible solution, such as the maximum area or maximum revenue, within given constraints.

Projectile Motion

The motion of an object thrown or projected into the air, subject only to the acceleration of gravity.

Revenue

The total income generated by the sale of goods or services, calculated as the product of price and quantity sold.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Vertex

The highest or lowest point on a parabola; in applications, it represents the point where the dependent variable is maximized or minimized.

Vertex Formula ($x = \frac{-b}{2a}$)

The algebraic method used to find the $x$-value (or $t$-value) of the vertex in a quadratic function.
