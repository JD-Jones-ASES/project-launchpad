Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a rational equation?

An equation that contains one or more rational expressions.

What is the primary strategy for clearing fractions when solving a rational equation?

Multiply every term on both sides of the equation by the least common denominator (LCD).

Multiplying a rational equation by its LCD transforms it into what type of equation?

A polynomial equation.

What is the first step in the procedure for solving a rational equation?

Find the LCD of all rational expressions in the equation.

What is the second step in the procedure for solving a rational equation?

Multiply every term on both sides by the LCD.

After solving the resulting polynomial equation, what must you do with each potential solution?

Check each solution in the original rational equation.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Under what specific condition must a solution to a rational equation be discarded?

When the solution makes any denominator in the original equation equal to zero.

In the equation $\frac{3}{x} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{x}$, what is the least common denominator?

$4x$

When solving $\frac{3}{x} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{x}$ by multiplying every term by $4x$, what is the resulting polynomial equation?

$12 + x = 20$

What is the solution to the rational equation $\frac{3}{x} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{5}{x}$?

$x = 8$

Define: Extraneous solution

A value that appears when solving a transformed equation but does not satisfy the original equation.

Why do extraneous solutions sometimes occur when solving rational equations?

Multiplying both sides by an expression containing a variable can introduce values that make a denominator zero.
