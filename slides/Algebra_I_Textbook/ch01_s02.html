The Real Number System

The Hierarchy of Real Numbers

Set Name. Description

Examples

Natural Numbers. Also known as counting numbers.

$1, 2, 3, 4, 5, \dots$

Whole Numbers. Natural numbers plus the number zero.

$0, 1, 2, 3, \dots$

Integers. All whole numbers and their opposites (negative versions).

$\dots, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \dots$

Rational Numbers. Numbers that can be written as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ (where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b eq 0$). Their decimal forms either terminate or repeat.

$0.75$ (terminates), $0.333\dots$ (repeats)

Irrational Numbers. Real numbers that cannot be expressed as a fraction of integers. Their decimal forms never terminate and never repeat.

$\sqrt{2} \approx 1.41421$, $\pi \approx 3.14159$

Real Numbers. The complete set consisting of all rational and irrational numbers.

All numbers listed above.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the alternative name for the set of natural numbers?

Counting numbers

The set of numbers $\{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, \dots\}$ is known as the _____ numbers.

Natural

How do the whole numbers differ from the natural numbers?

The whole numbers include zero, whereas natural numbers do not.

The set containing the natural numbers and zero is called the _____ numbers.

Whole

Which set of numbers includes all whole numbers and their opposites?

Integers

List three examples of integers that are not whole numbers.

$-1, -2, -3$ (or any negative whole numbers)

Key Concepts (2/2)

Term: Rational Number

Definition: Any number that can be written as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b e 0$.

In decimal form, what are the two possible behaviors of a rational number?

It either terminates or repeats.

Is the decimal $0.75$ a rational or irrational number?

Rational (it terminates).

Is the decimal $0.333\dots$ a rational or irrational number?

Rational (it repeats).

What is the primary characteristic of an irrational number's decimal form?

It neither terminates nor repeats.

Provide two common examples of irrational numbers.

$\sqrt{2}$ and $\pi$
