Adding and Subtracting Radicals

Requirement

Only like radicals can be combined.

The Process

Multiplying Radical Expressions

The Product Property

This property allows for the multiplication of radicands.

The Distributive Property

When multiplying a single radical by a binomial expression containing radicals, distribute the term outside the parentheses to both terms inside.

Simplification

After multiplying, always simplify the resulting radicals to ensure the expression is in its final form.

Conjugates and Rationalizing Denominators

Binomial Denominators

When a denominator is a binomial containing a radical (e.g., $a + b\sqrt{c}$), you must multiply both the numerator and the denominator by the conjugate.

The Conjugate Rule

The conjugate of $a + b\sqrt{c}$ is $a - b\sqrt{c}$.

Short-Answer Practice Quiz (1/2)

Q: Under what specific condition can two radical expressions be added or subtracted?

> Answer: They must be "like radicals," meaning they have the exact same radicand.

Q: What should be the first step if you are asked to combine radicals that do not have the same radicand?

> Answer: You must simplify each radical first to see if they can be converted into like radicals.

Q: Define the conjugate of the expression $3 + \sqrt{5}$.

> Answer: $3 - \sqrt{5}$.

Short-Answer Practice Quiz (2/2)

Q: When multiplying a radical by a binomial, which algebraic property is typically used?

> Answer: The distributive property.

Q: What is the result of multiplying $(a + b\sqrt{c})$ by its conjugate $(a - b\sqrt{c})$?

> Answer: $a^2 - b^2c$.

Q: Complete the following simplification: $3\sqrt{12} + 5\sqrt{27} - 2\sqrt{48}$.

> Answer: $13\sqrt{3}$. (Calculated by: $6\sqrt{3} + 15\sqrt{3} - 8\sqrt{3}$).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What condition must be met to add or subtract radical expressions?

The expressions must have the same radicand.

What is the term for radical expressions that have the same radicand?

Like radicals.

Like radicals are conceptually similar to what algebraic concept?

Like terms.

Simplify the expression $3\sqrt{5} + 7\sqrt{5}$.

$10\sqrt{5}$.

Why is it often necessary to simplify radicals before adding or subtracting them?

To determine if they can be converted into like radicals.

What is the first step in simplifying the expression $3\sqrt{12} + 5\sqrt{27} - 2\sqrt{48}$?

Simplify each individual radical term.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Simplify the radical term $3\sqrt{12}$.

$6\sqrt{3}$.

Simplify the radical term $5\sqrt{27}$.

$15\sqrt{3}$.

Simplify the radical term $2\sqrt{48}$.

$8\sqrt{3}$.

After simplifying, how is $6\sqrt{3} + 15\sqrt{3} - 8\sqrt{3}$ combined?

By combining the coefficients: $(6 + 15 - 8)\sqrt{3}$.

What are the two primary mathematical properties used to multiply radical expressions?

The product property of square roots and the distributive property.

State the product property of square roots used during multiplication.

$\sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{ab}$.
