Factoring Polynomials Completely and Solving Equations

Study Slides

The Systematic Factoring Strategy

1. Factor out the GCF

Always identify the Greatest Common Factor first. If the leading coefficient is negative, factor out a negative GCF.

2. Count the Terms

The number of terms determines the next technique to use:

3. Factor Again

Examine every resulting factor to see if it can be factored further.

4. Check

Multiply all final factors together to verify they recover the original polynomial.

The Zero Product Property

Property

If $A \cdot B = 0$, then $A = 0$ or $B = 0$ (or both).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Difference of Squares

A specific binomial pattern ($a^2 - b^2$) that factors into $(a + b)(a - b)$.

Factoring by Grouping

A technique used for polynomials with four or more terms where terms are grouped to find common factors within the sub-groups.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The highest expression that divides exactly into all terms of a polynomial.

Prime Factor

A factor that cannot be broken down into further factors using integer coefficients.

Trinomial

A polynomial consisting of three terms.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Zero Product Property

A property stating that if the product of multiple factors is zero, then at least one factor must be equal to zero.
