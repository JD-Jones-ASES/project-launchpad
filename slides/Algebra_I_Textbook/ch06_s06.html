Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Binomial

A polynomial consisting of two terms.

Difference of Squares

A mathematical pattern where the product of the sum and difference of two terms results in the square of the first term minus the square of the second term: $(a + b)(a - b) = a^2 - b^2$.

FOIL

A mnemonic for multiplying two binomials by calculating the product of the First, Outer, Inner, and Last terms.

Perfect Square Trinomial

The three-term polynomial result of squaring a binomial, following the patterns $a^2 + 2ab + b^2$ or $a^2 - 2ab + b^2$.

Special Products

Specific multiplication patterns (like squaring binomials or multiplying sums and differences) that follow predictable algebraic rules.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Square of a Binomial

The result of multiplying a binomial by itself; it always produces three terms: the square of the first term, twice the product of the two terms, and the square of the last term.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Why is it beneficial to memorize special product patterns in algebra?

It saves time when multiplying binomials and helps with factoring polynomials in later chapters.

Formula: Square of a Binomial Sum

$(a + b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2$

Formula: Square of a Binomial Difference

$(a - b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2$

How many terms are produced by the square of a binomial?

Three terms.

In the expansion of $(a + b)^2$, what is the first term?

The square of the first term ($a^2$).

In the expansion of $(a + b)^2$, what is the middle term?

Twice the product of the two terms ($2ab$).

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the expansion of $(a + b)^2$, what is the last term?

The square of the last term ($b^2$).

The common algebraic error $(a + b)^2 = a^2 + b^2$ fails to include which specific term?

The middle term $2ab$.

Show the FOIL expansion steps for $(a + b)(a + b)$.

$a^2 + ab + ab + b^2$

Calculate $(x + 5)^2$ using the special product pattern.

$x^2 + 10x + 25$

Using a numerical example like $(3 + 5)^2$, why is the result not $3^2 + 5^2$?

Because $64 e 34$.

When expanding $(3x + 4)^2$, what is the value of the term $a^2$?

$9x^2$
