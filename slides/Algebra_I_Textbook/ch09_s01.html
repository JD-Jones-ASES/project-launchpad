Simplifying Rational Expressions

Study Slides

Important Caution: Factors vs. Terms

Terms

are separated by addition or subtraction (e.g., $x$ and $5$ in the expression $x+5$).

Factors

are expressions multiplied together (e.g., $(x+5)$ and $(x+3)$).

Quiz Question 1

Which of the following expressions does NOT meet the definition of a rational expression as defined in the text?

A. $\frac{x^2 - 5x + 6}{x + 1}$

B. $\frac{\sqrt{x} + 4}{x^2 - 9}$

C. $\frac{12}{x^2 + 1}$

D. $x - 5$

Hint: Recall that the numerator and denominator must both be polynomials, which excludes roots of variables.

Quiz Question 2

What are the excluded values for the rational expression $\frac{x^2 - 1}{x^2 - 3x + 2}$?

A. $x \ne 2$ and $x \ne 1$

B. $x \ne 2$

C. $x \ne 1$ and $x \ne -1$

D. $x \ne -2$ and $x \ne -1$

Hint: To find excluded values, you must factor the denominator and find what makes it equal to zero, regardless of the numerator.

Quiz Question 3

Simplify the expression $\frac{x^2 - 4x + 4}{x^2 - 4}$.

A. $\frac{x - 2}{x + 2}$, where $x \ne 2, -2$

B. $\frac{x + 2}{x - 2}$, where $x \ne 2, -2$

C. $x + 1$, where $x \ne 2, -2$

D. $\frac{x - 2}{x + 2}$, where $x \ne -2$

Hint: Try factoring the numerator as a perfect square trinomial and the denominator as a difference of squares.

Quiz Question 4

Why is the expression $\frac{x + 7}{x + 14}$ already in simplest form?

A. Because $x$ and $7$ are terms, not factors, and the numerator and denominator share no common factors.

B. Because the denominator is exactly double the numerator.

C. Because it simplifies to $\frac{1}{2}$ by dividing the $x$'s and the constants.

D. Because the expression has no excluded values.

Hint: Recall the caution mentioned in the text regarding the difference between canceling factors versus canceling terms.

Quiz Question 5

Which set of values represents the excluded values for the expression $\frac{3x - 15}{2x^2 - 10x}$?

A. $x \ne 0, 5$

B. $x \ne 5$

C. $x \ne 0, -5$

D. $x \ne 5, \frac{1}{3}$

Hint: Check if there is a Greatest Common Factor (GCF) that can be factored out of the denominator first.

Quiz Question 6

Simplify the rational expression $\frac{2x^2 - 50}{x^2 + 10x + 25}$.

A. $\frac{2(x - 5)}{x + 5}$, where $x \ne -5$

B. $2(x - 5)$, where $x \ne -5$

C. $\frac{2(x + 5)}{x - 5}$, where $x \ne 5$

D. $\frac{x - 5}{x + 5}$, where $x \ne -5$

Hint: First, factor out the GCF of 2 in the numerator, then apply the difference of squares and perfect square trinomial formulas.
