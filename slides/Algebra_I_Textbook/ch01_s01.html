Variables and Expressions

Study Slides

Components of Algebraic Expressions

Variables

Symbols, typically letters like $x$, $y$, $n$, or $t$, used to represent one or more numbers.

Constants

Quantities that do not change in value, such as $3$, $-7$, or $12$.

Terms

The individual parts of an expression separated by addition or subtraction signs. For example, in the expression $3x + 5$, the terms are $3x$ and $5$.

Coefficients

The numerical factor multiplied by a variable. In the term $3x$, the coefficient is $3$.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Algebraic Expression

A mathematical phrase combining variables, constants, and operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation).

Coefficient

The numerical factor that is multiplied by a variable in a term.

Constant

A fixed quantity that does not change its value.

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

The specific sequence (Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction) used to simplify mathematical expressions.

Term

A part of an algebraic expression that is separated from other parts by addition or subtraction signs.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Variable

A letter or symbol used to represent one or more numerical values.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the fundamental difference between algebra and arithmetic?

Algebra uses letters to represent numbers and describe general quantities, while arithmetic works with specific numbers.

Term: Variable

Definition: A letter or symbol that represents one or more numbers. Example: $x$, $y$, $n$, or $t$.

Term: Constant

Definition: A quantity whose value does not change. Example: $3$, $-7$, or $12$.

What is an algebraic expression?

A combination of variables, constants, and operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation.

Which mathematical operations can be part of an algebraic expression?

Addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, and exponentiation.

In an algebraic expression, what are the parts separated by addition or subtraction signs called?

Terms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Identify the terms in the algebraic expression $3x + 5$.

The terms are $3x$ and $5$.

Term: Coefficient

Definition: The numerical factor multiplied by the variable(s) in a term.

What is the coefficient in the term $3x$?

The coefficient is $3$.

What is the numerical coefficient of a variable written alone, such as $x$?

The coefficient is $1$.

What is the coefficient in the algebraic term $-y$?

The coefficient is $-1$.
