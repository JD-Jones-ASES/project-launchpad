Adding and Subtracting Polynomials

Study Slides

Definitions of Terms

Monomial

A monomial is a single mathematical unit that can be a number, a variable, or the product of a number and one or more variables. Crucially, these variables must be raised to **whole-number exponent...

Polynomial

A polynomial is either a single monomial or the sum of multiple monomials.

Terms

Each individual monomial within a polynomial is referred to as a "term."

Like Terms

These are terms that share the exact same variable(s) raised to the exact same power(s). Only like terms can be combined during addition or subtraction.

Determining Degree

Degree of a Monomial

Calculated by finding the sum of the exponents of all its variables.

Degree of a Polynomial

This is determined by the highest degree of any single term within the polynomial.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Binomial

A polynomial consisting of exactly two terms.

Closure

A property stating that an operation on members of a set (like polynomials) always produces a member of that same set.

Cubic

A polynomial of degree 3.

Degree of a Monomial

The sum of the exponents of the variables in the monomial.

Degree of a Polynomial

The highest degree of any term within the polynomial.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Linear

A polynomial of degree 1.

Monomial

A number, variable, or product of numbers and variables with whole-number exponents.

Polynomial

A monomial or a sum of monomials.

Quadratic

A polynomial of degree 2.

Standard Form

A format where polynomial terms are ordered from the highest degree to the lowest degree.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Term

An individual monomial that forms part of a polynomial sum.

Trinomial

A polynomial consisting of exactly three terms.
