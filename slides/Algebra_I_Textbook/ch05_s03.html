Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the alternative name for the elimination method of solving systems?

The addition method.

Under what structural condition is the elimination method considered especially efficient?

It is efficient when neither equation is already solved for a variable.

In the elimination method, both equations should first be written in _____ form.

Standard form ($Ax + By = C$).

What is the primary goal of multiplying equations by constants in the elimination method?

To make the coefficients of one variable opposites.

What mathematical operation is performed on the two equations to cause one variable to drop out?

Addition (or subtraction).

After adding two equations and eliminating a variable, what is the next step in the procedure?

Solve the resulting equation for the remaining variable.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Once the value of one variable is found, how is the value of the other variable determined?

Substitute the value back into either original equation.

Where should the final solution to a system be checked to ensure accuracy?

In both original equations.

In the system $3x + 2y = 16$ and $3x - 2y = 8$, adding the equations results in the equation _____.

$6x = 24$.

In the system $3x + 2y = 16$ and $3x - 2y = 8$, the solved value for $x$ is _____.

$4$.

For the system $3x + 2y = 16$ and $3x - 2y = 8$, the solved value for $y$ is _____.

$2$.

System: $2x + 5y = 1$ and $3x + 2y = -4$. To eliminate $x$, what should the first equation be multiplied by?

$3$.
