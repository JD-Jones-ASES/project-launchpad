The Four Types of Slope

Slope Type. Visual Description

Mathematical Characteristic

Positive Slope. The line rises as it moves from left to right.

$m > 0$

Negative Slope. The line falls as it moves from left to right.

$m < 0$

Zero Slope. The line is horizontal (flat).

$m = 0$ (The numerator is 0)

Undefined Slope. The line is vertical.

The denominator is 0 (Division by zero)

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Change in $x$ (Run)

The horizontal distance between two points on a line.

Change in $y$ (Rise)

The vertical distance between two points on a line.

Horizontal Line

A flat line with a slope of $0$, where all points share the same $y$-coordinate.

Linear Relationship

A relationship between two variables that, when graphed, forms a straight line.

$m$

The standard algebraic symbol used to denote the slope of a line.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Rate of Change

A comparison of how much one quantity changes relative to another; in a linear context, this is the slope.

Slope

The ratio of the rise to the run; a measure of the steepness of a line.

Undefined Slope

The slope of a vertical line where the horizontal change ($x_2 - x_1$) is zero.

Vertical Line

A line that goes straight up and down, possessing an undefined slope because all points share the same $x$-coordinate.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What property of a line does the slope measure in algebra?

Steepness

Slope describes the rate at which $y$-values change relative to what other values?

$x$-values

The slope of a non-vertical line is the ratio of the _____ change to the horizontal change.

vertical

What specific term refers to the vertical change between two points on a line?

Rise

What specific term refers to the horizontal change between two points on a line?

Run

Term: Slope

Definition: The ratio of the rise to the run between any two points on a non-vertical line.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which lowercase letter is most commonly used to denote slope in algebra?

$m$

Formula: Slope ($m$)

$m = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{\text{change in } y}{\text{change in } x}$

Provide the coordinate-based formula for the slope of a line passing through $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$.

$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$

What condition regarding $x_1$ and $x_2$ must be true for the slope formula to be defined?

$x_1 eq x_2$

How does the choice of which point is designated as $(x_1, y_1)$ versus $(x_2, y_2)$ affect the resulting slope?

It does not change the result, provided the subtraction order remains consistent for both coordinates.

What is the slope of a line that rises from left to right?

Positive slope
