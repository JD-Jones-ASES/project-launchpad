Applications of Systems of Equations

Study Slides

Mixture Problems (Coins)

Condition 1

The sum of the number of items equals the total quantity (e.g., $n + d = 20$).

Condition 2

The sum of the values of the items equals the total monetary value, often converted to cents to simplify calculations (e.g., $5n + 10d = 135$).

Distance-Rate-Time Problems

Opposite Directions

When two objects move in opposite directions from the same point, their individual distances are added together to find the total distance between them.

Equation Structure

$(Rate_1 \times Time) + (Rate_2 \times Time) = Total Distance$.

Cost and Break-Even Problems

Equation Structure

Total Cost ($T$) = Setup Fee + (Rate per unit $\times$ Number of units).

The Break-Even Point

The solution is found by setting the two cost equations equal to one another ($T_1 = T_2$) to solve for the number of units.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the first step in the general strategy for solving a system from a word problem?

Read the problem carefully and identify the two unknowns.

After identifying the two unknowns in a word problem, what action should be taken next?

Assign a variable to each unknown.

How many relationships (conditions) must be found to connect two unknowns in a system of equations?

Two relationships.

Once two relationships are identified in a word problem, what is the next step in the strategy?

Write each relationship as an equation.

Which two algebraic methods are suggested for solving a system of equations derived from a word problem?

Substitution or elimination.

What is the final step in the strategy for solving word problems using systems?

Check that the answer makes sense in the context of the problem.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the 'Mixture Problem' example, what do the variables $n$ and $d$ represent?

$n$ is the number of nickels and $d$ is the number of dimes.

For a jar containing 20 total coins (nickels $n$ and dimes $d$), what is the 'total number' equation?

$n + d = 20$

How many cents are in $\$1.35$?

$135$ cents

In a system of equations for coins, what does the expression $5n$ represent if $n$ is the number of nickels?

The total value of the nickels in cents.

In a system of equations for coins, what does the expression $10d$ represent if $d$ is the number of dimes?

The total value of the dimes in cents.

For a collection of nickels ($n$) and dimes ($d$) worth $135$ cents, what is the 'total value' equation?

$5n + 10d = 135$
