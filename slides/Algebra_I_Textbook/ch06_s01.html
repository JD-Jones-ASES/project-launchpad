Properties of Exponents

Study Slides

Fundamental Concepts and Definitions

Base ($a$)

The factor that is being repeated.

Exponent ($n$)

The number of times the base appears as a factor.

Product of Powers

Property

For every nonzero real number $a$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$: $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$

Rationale

Writing out the factors shows that $m$ factors of $a$ multiplied by $n$ factors of $a$ results in a total of $m+n$ factors.

Power of a Power

Property

For every nonzero real number $a$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$: $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$

Rationale

The expression $(a^m)^n$ means that $a^m$ is used as a factor $n$ times. Adding the exponent $m$ to itself $n$ times results in $m \cdot n$.

Power of a Product

Property

For all nonzero real numbers $a$ and $b$ and every positive integer $n$: $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$

Summary Table of Operations

Operation. Rule

Example

Multiplying Powers. Add Exponents

$x^3 \cdot x^5 = x^{3+5} = x^8$

Power to a Power. Multiply Exponents

$(x^4)^3 = x^{4 \cdot 3} = x^{12}$

Power of a Product. Distribute Power

$(3x)^4 = 3^4 \cdot x^4 = 81x^4$

Critical Cautions and Distinctions

Mismatched Bases

The Product of Powers rule requires the bases to be identical. For example, $x^3 y^5$ cannot be simplified because $x$ and $y$ are different bases.

Confusing Addition and Multiplication

Negative Bases in Products

When raising a negative product to a power, ensure the sign is handled correctly. For example, $(-2x^3)^4 = (-2)^4 \cdot (x^3)^4 = 16x^{12}$.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Base

The number or variable in an exponential expression that is used as a factor. In $a^n$, $a$ is the base.

Exponent

The number in an exponential expression that indicates how many times the base is used as a factor. In $a^n$, $n$ is the exponent.

Power

An expression representing the product of a base and an exponent, or the result of raising a base to an exponent.

Product of Powers

A rule stating that when multiplying two expressions with the same base, the exponents are added.

Power of a Power

A rule stating that when an exponential expression is raised to another power, the exponents are multiplied.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Power of a Product

A rule stating that when a product is raised to a power, each factor in the product is raised to that power individually.

Simplify

The process of rewriting an expression in a more efficient or concise form using mathematical properties.
