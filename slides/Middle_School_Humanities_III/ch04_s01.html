Important Terms (1/2)

Enlightenment

An influential intellectual and philosophical movement in 18th-century Europe emphasizing reason, individualism, and skepticism of tradition and religious dogma.

Divine Right of Kings

A political doctrine asserting that monarchs derive their authority directly from God and are not accountable to any earthly authority, often used to justify absolute rule.

Glorious Revolution (1688)

A bloodless revolution in England that established the supremacy of Parliament over the monarch and significantly influenced Locke's political writings.

Auto-da-Fé

A ritual of public penance of convicted heretics or apostates, especially in the Spanish Inquisition, often involving torture and execution by burning. Voltaire critiques this in *Candide*.
