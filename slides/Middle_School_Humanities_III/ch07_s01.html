Important Terms (1/2)

Western Front

The main theater of war during World War I in Western Europe, characterized by trench warfare and immense casualties. Both Jünger and Remarque served here.

Pour le Mérite

Germany's highest military honor for bravery, awarded to Ernst Jünger.

Armistice

An agreement made by opposing sides in a war to stop fighting; a truce. Wilfred Owen died one week before the Armistice that ended World War I on November 11, 1918.

Primary Sources

Original materials from the time period under study, such as diaries, letters, photographs, and literary works like memoirs or novels, offering direct insights into historical events and perspectives.

Memoir

A historical account or biography written from personal knowledge or special sources, often based on diaries or recollections. The Storm of Steel is a memoir.

Endurance

Idealism

The practice of forming or pursuing ideals, especially unrealistically. Remarque's novel portrays the destruction of the initial idealism of young recruits.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What event triggered the start of World War I?

The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne, and his wife Sophie on June 28, 1914, in Sarajevo.

Who was Gavrilo Princip?

A nineteen-year-old Bosnian nationalist who assassinated Archduke Franz Ferdinand and his wife, an act that sparked World War I.

Define the 'alliance system' as it existed in Europe prior to World War I.

A network of treaties where countries promised to defend one another if attacked. It converted a regional crisis into a world war.

Name the two main alliance blocs in Europe in 1914 and their member countries.

The Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy) and the Triple Entente (France, Russia, Britain).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the initial expectation regarding the duration of the war in August 1914, and what was the reality?

Most soldiers expected the war to be over by Christmas, but it lasted for four years and three months.

Describe Germany's initial war strategy for the Western Front.

The Schlieffen Plan, which called for a rapid six-week knockout of France through Belgium, then a turn east to face Russia.

What was the outcome of the Schlieffen Plan?

It nearly worked, bringing German armies close to Paris, but was halted by French and British forces at the First Battle of the Marne in September 1914.
