Important Terms (1/4)

Alliance system

Network of military defense treaties dividing Europe into Triple Alliance (Germany, Austria-Hungary, Italy) and Triple Entente (France, Russia, Britain); converted a regional assassination into a w...

Archduke Franz Ferdinand

Heir to the Austro-Hungarian throne; assassinated in Sarajevo on June 28, 1914 by Gavrilo Princip; triggering event of WWI.

Gavrilo Princip

Bosnian nationalist who assassinated Archduke Franz Ferdinand; 19 years old; his pistol shot set in motion the chain of events leading to WWI.

Schlieffen Plan

Germany's pre-war strategy: knock out France through Belgium in six weeks, then turn east against Russia; failed at the First Battle of the Marne (September 1914).

Trench warfare

Opposing armies dug into parallel trench systems; machine guns, barbed wire, and artillery made attacking across No Man's Land catastrophically costly; stalemated the Western Front for four years.

Important Terms (2/4)

No Man's Land

The strip of cratered, barbed-wire ground between opposing trenches; soldiers crossed it in attacks at enormous cost.

Battle of the Somme (1916)

British offensive in northern France; first day (July 1, 1916): 57,000 British casualties including 20,000 dead; bloodiest single day in British military history; over one million total casualties.

Battle of Verdun (1916)

German attack on French fortress; intended to "bleed France white"; lasted 10 months; approximately 700,000 dead; symbol of the war's senseless consumption.

Gallipoli (1915-1916)

Allied attempt to knock Ottoman Empire out of war by forcing the Dardanelles; championed by Winston Churchill; catastrophic failure; nearly 500,000 casualties.

Lusitania

British ocean liner sunk by German submarine in 1915, killing 1,198 including 128 Americans; contributed to US public opinion shifting toward entering the war.

Important Terms (3/4)

Zimmermann Telegram

German diplomatic message (intercepted by Britain) proposing Mexico attack the US in exchange for recovering Texas, New Mexico, and Arizona; helped push Wilson toward asking Congress for war declar...

Armistice

The formal agreement to stop fighting, signed November 11, 1918 at 11:00 AM; not yet a peace treaty but ended the killing; commemorated as Remembrance Day and Veterans Day.

Fourteen Points

Wilson's peace proposal (January 1918): open diplomacy, freedom of seas, arms reduction, national self-determination, League of Nations; mostly rejected or weakened at Paris.

Treaty of Versailles (1919)

Peace treaty ending WWI; signed June 28, 1919; war guilt clause, 132 billion gold marks reparations, territorial losses, military limits; Germany not invited to negotiate.

War guilt clause

Article 231 forcing Germany to accept full responsibility for the war; basis for reparations; considered a lie by most Germans; rallying cry for nationalists.

Important Terms (4/4)

Reparations

Financial payments demanded from Germany; set at 132 billion gold marks; contributed to German economic instability through the 1920s and early 1930s.

League of Nations

International peace organization established by the Treaty of Versailles; collective security principle; no army; US never joined; failed to stop aggressors in the 1930s.

Self-determination

Wilson's principle that peoples of a common nationality should govern themselves; in practice, Europe's intermixed populations made clean national boundaries impossible.
