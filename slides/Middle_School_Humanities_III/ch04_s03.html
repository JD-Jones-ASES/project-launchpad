Important Terms (1/2)

Philosophes

French word for "philosophers," used to describe the leading thinkers of the Enlightenment across Europe.

_Cogito, ergo sum_

Latin for "I think, therefore I am," René Descartes's famous philosophical proposition, asserting the undeniable existence of a thinking self.

State of Nature

An imagined hypothetical condition of humanity before the formation of governments or societies, used by philosophers like Locke to theorize about natural rights and the origins of political author...

_L'infâme_

French for "the infamous thing," Voltaire's term for religious intolerance, superstition, and the persecution of those with differing beliefs, which he vehemently opposed.

Divine Right of Kings

The traditional doctrine that a monarch's authority comes directly from God, not from the consent of the people, which Locke's ideas directly challenged.

Important Terms (2/2)

Checks and Balances

A system closely related to the separation of powers, where each branch of government has specific powers to limit the actions of the other branches, ensuring no single branch becomes too powerful.
