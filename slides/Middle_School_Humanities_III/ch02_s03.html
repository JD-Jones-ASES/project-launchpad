Viceroyalty

A large colonial territory governed by a **viceroy**, an official who ruled on behalf of the Spanish crown with almost royal authority. Examples include New Spain (Mexico) and Peru (South America).

Philip II (reigned 1556–1598)

King of Spain during its Golden Age, a staunch defender of Catholicism and leader of the Counter-Reformation. His reign saw Spain's greatest global power but also planted seeds of future decline.

Important Terms (3/4)

Escorial

A vast royal palace, monastery, and mausoleum built by Philip II near Madrid, symbolizing the power and religious devotion of the Spanish Habsburg monarchy.

Battle of Lepanto (1571)

A decisive naval battle in which a Spanish-led Holy League fleet crushed the Ottoman fleet off the coast of Greece, marking a major Christian victory against Ottoman expansion in the Mediterranean.

Counter-Reformation

The Catholic Church's internal reform movement and response to the Protestant Reformation in the 16th and 17th centuries, supported vigorously by Spain under Philip II.

Valladolid Debate (1550–1551)

A formal legal and theological debate convened by King Charles I of Spain to discuss the moral legitimacy of Spain's conquest of the Americas and the treatment of its indigenous inhabitants.

Juan Ginés de Sepúlveda

A Spanish humanist scholar and theologian who, during the Valladolid Debate, argued that indigenous peoples were "natural slaves" and could be justly conquered and ruled by the Spanish.

Important Terms (4/4)

Spanish Armada (1588)

A large Spanish fleet sent by Philip II to invade England, which was defeated by the English navy and severe storms. This defeat was a major strategic and psychological blow to Spain.

Moriscos

Descendants of Muslims who converted to Christianity after the Reconquista but were later expelled from Spain in 1609. Their expulsion caused significant economic and demographic loss.

Peace of Westphalia (1648)

A series of treaties that ended the Thirty Years' War, which significantly weakened Spain's power and marked the rise of France as the dominant European power.

War of Spanish Succession (1701–1714)

A major European conflict fought over the succession to the Spanish throne after the death of the last Habsburg king of Spain without an heir.
