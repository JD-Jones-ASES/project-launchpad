Important Terms (1/3)

Reconquista

The centuries-long Christian campaign to retake the Iberian Peninsula from Muslim rulers; completed 1492 with the fall of Granada; shaped Spanish national identity.

Alhambra Decree (1492)

The expulsion of Spain's Jewish population, issued the same year as Columbus's voyage.

Treaty of Tordesillas (1494)

The agreement dividing newly discovered lands between Spain and Portugal; Spain received everything west of a line through the Atlantic.

Viceroyalty

A large colonial territory governed by a viceroy on behalf of the Spanish crown; the two great viceroyalties were New Spain and Peru.

Encomienda

The Spanish colonial labor system granting colonists (encomenderos) the labor of indigenous people in exchange for Christianizing them; in practice, forced labor working people to death.

Important Terms (2/3)

Bartolome de las Casas

Dominican friar and former encomendero who became the great defender of indigenous peoples; wrote A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies (1542); his arguments produced the New Laws of 1542.

New Laws of 1542

Laws attempting to reform the worst encomienda abuses, produced partly by Las Casas's advocacy; had mixed success.

Valladolid Debate (1550-1551)

Formal theological inquiry into whether Spain's conquest of the Americas was morally just; Sepulveda argued natural slavery, Las Casas argued universal human dignity; no verdict issued.

Counter-Reformation

The Catholic Church's reform movement responding to Protestantism; key institutions: Council of Trent, Jesuits, renewed Inquisition; Spain under Philip II was its political champion.

Battle of Lepanto (1571)

The Spanish-led Catholic naval coalition that destroyed the Ottoman fleet; Cervantes fought there and was wounded.

Important Terms (3/3)

Moriscos

Descendants of Muslim converts to Christianity expelled by Philip III in 1609; roughly 300,000 skilled workers lost to Spain's economy.

Treaty of Utrecht (1713)

Peace settlement ending the War of Spanish Succession; installed a French Bourbon as king of Spain but stripped Spain of its European territories; ended Spanish hegemony.
