Describe the living conditions in the urban slums that developed during the Industrial Revolution.

Workers lived packed into slums without clean water, sanitation, or fresh air, leading to repeated cholera epidemics caused by sewage-contaminated water.

What was the 'social question' that dominated European political thought from the 1840s through the early twentieth century?

The debate over what a just society owes to its workers, whether the market alone can solve poverty, and if the state has an obligation to intervene.
