The Plantation Economy (1/2)

Destination for Enslaved Africans

Most were sent to plantations in the Americas.

Driven by Cash Crops

Sugar was particularly significant, but tobacco and cotton were also key.

Intensive Labor

Sugar cultivation, especially in the Caribbean, required brutal, intensive labor in harsh tropical conditions.

High Death Rates

Enslaved workers died rapidly from overwork, disease, and mistreatment.

Economic Calculation

It was often considered cheaper by planters to work an enslaved person to death and purchase a new one than to maintain a healthy workforce.
