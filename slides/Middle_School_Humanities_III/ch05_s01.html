Reading the Map of Empire

Maps are not neutral

They are created with specific purposes, by specific people, and from particular viewpoints.

European colonial maps

Documents of Power and Evidence

Colonial maps explicitly demonstrate the power dynamics and the undeniable scale of European expansion and conquest.

The Triangle Trade

Economic Engine

The primary driver of Atlantic expansion and colonization.

A Continuous Circuit

Connected Europe, West Africa, and the Americas.

Legs of the Trade

Profitability

Each leg of the triangle was profitable, with enslaved people being the central commodity.

Scale of the Slave Trade (1500-1867)

The Plantation Economy (1/2)

Destination for Enslaved Africans

Most were sent to plantations in the Americas.

Driven by Cash Crops

Sugar was particularly significant, but tobacco and cotton were also key.

Intensive Labor

Sugar cultivation, especially in the Caribbean, required brutal, intensive labor in harsh tropical conditions.

High Death Rates

Enslaved workers died rapidly from overwork, disease, and mistreatment.

Economic Calculation

It was often considered cheaper by planters to work an enslaved person to death and purchase a new one than to maintain a healthy workforce.
