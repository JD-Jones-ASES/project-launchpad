Background

The Sack of Magdeburg (May 1631)

The Question Both Texts Ask

Grotius's Answer

Law and reason – norms established by human rationality can bind even nations at war.

Schiller's Answer

Memory and moral confrontation – only by honestly remembering and confronting the horrors of war (the ash, the dead, the weeping general) can a civilization learn to prefer peace.

Legacy

Key Concepts (1/2)

What historical event serves as the backdrop for the writings of Hugo Grotius and Friedrich Schiller discussed in this section?

The Thirty Years' War (1618-1648).

What fundamental difference in perspective do Grotius and Schiller represent in understanding a historical moment?

Grotius represents experiencing a historical moment (writing during the war), while Schiller represents interpreting it (looking back 150 years later).

Who was Hugo Grotius, and when did he write his seminal work, The Rights of War and Peace?

What urgent question did Grotius address in The Rights of War and Peace concerning warfare?

He asked if there are any rules that bind nations, even when they are engaged in war.

What was the 'explosive' implication of Grotius's argument that natural law binds nations?

It meant that international law could apply universally to all nations, and no sovereign could claim to be above it.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Name at least three specific acts that Grotius argued were forbidden even during wartime.

Killing innocent civilians, using poison, breaking treaties, and enslaving captives without legal cause.

Who was Friedrich Schiller, and in what era did he write his History of the Thirty Years' War?

Friedrich Schiller (1759–1805) was a German writer and Enlightenment figure who wrote his History of the Thirty Years' War in the 1790s.

What was Schiller's Enlightenment belief regarding the purpose of studying history?

He believed history had a moral purpose, teaching nations and individuals how to live better by studying the past.

Which specific atrocity of the Thirty Years' War did Schiller vividly recount in his history?

The Sack of Magdeburg in May 1631, where Imperial and League troops stormed the Protestant city.
