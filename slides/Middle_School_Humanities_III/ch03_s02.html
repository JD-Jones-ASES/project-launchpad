Schiller: History as Moral Witness

Background

The Sack of Magdeburg (May 1631)

The Question Both Texts Ask

Grotius's Answer

Law and reason – norms established by human rationality can bind even nations at war.

Schiller's Answer

Memory and moral confrontation – only by honestly remembering and confronting the horrors of war (the ash, the dead, the weeping general) can a civilization learn to prefer peace.
