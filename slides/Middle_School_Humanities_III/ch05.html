Important Terms (1/3)

Middle Passage

The sea journey enslaved Africans were forced to make from West Africa across the Atlantic; the middle leg of the triangle trade; approximately 1.8 million died of approximately 12 million transpor...

Triangle trade

The three-leg commercial circuit: European goods to Africa, enslaved people to the Americas, American products back to Europe.

Plantation economy

Large estates producing a single cash crop for export using enslaved labor; made small numbers of landowners wealthy while reducing millions to property.

East India Company

Private English trading corporation founded 1600; evolved into a governing power with its own army that controlled most of India before the British crown took direct control in 1858.

Sepoy Mutiny

The 1857-1858 Indian uprising against East India Company rule; resulted in the British crown assuming direct imperial control of India.

Important Terms (2/3)

Haitian Revolution (1791-1804)

The uprising of enslaved people in French Saint-Domingue; led by Toussaint Louverture; founded Haiti as the first Black republic and the only successful slave revolt in history.

Toussaint Louverture

Formerly enslaved military commander who led the Haitian Revolution to victory against French, British, and Napoleonic forces.

Abolitionism

The movement to legally end the slave trade and slavery; grounded in both Christian teaching (imago Dei) and Enlightenment natural rights.

William Wilberforce

Evangelical Christian MP who led the British Parliamentary campaign against the slave trade for nearly twenty years; slave trade abolished 1807; Emancipation Act 1833.

Emancipation

Being set free from slavery; the British Emancipation Act (1833) freed approximately 800,000 people in British colonies.
