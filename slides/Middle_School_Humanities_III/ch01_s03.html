Important Terms (1/2)

Elizabethan Settlement

The religious compromise established by Elizabeth I in 1559–1563, making England officially Protestant (Church of England) while keeping many Catholic forms of worship to achieve peace.

Divine Right of Kings

The belief that a monarch's authority comes directly from God and cannot be limited by any human institution, including Parliament.

Petition of Right

Commonwealth

The republican government established in England after the execution of Charles I in 1649, existing as a state without a king, governed by Parliament and a Council of State until 1653.

Protectorate

The government of England from 1653 to 1659, headed by Oliver Cromwell as "Lord Protector," where Cromwell held near-kingly powers but refused the crown.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What fundamental shift in authority occurred in 17th-century England regarding knowledge?

Scholars began challenging the ultimate authority of old figures like Aristotle, just as Parliament challenged the authority of kings.

Who was considered the ultimate authority for knowledge about the natural world in medieval Europe?

Aristotle and the scholars who commented on his works.

Who was Francis Bacon, and what was his primary contribution to the new way of knowing?

A lawyer, philosopher, and statesman who articulated why the old Aristotelian method was flawed and proposed the inductive method as its replacement.

What did Francis Bacon call the mental habits and prejudices that distorted observation?

Idols
