Important Terms (1/3)

Enlightenment

Intellectual movement (c. 1680-1800) applying human reason to improve government, society, religion, and morality; centered in France, England, Scotland, and German states.

Philosophes

French word for "philosophers"; nickname for Enlightenment thinkers across Europe who used reason to question tradition and challenge authority.

Method of Doubt

Descartes's technique of doubting everything until reaching the undoubtable; produced "I think, therefore I am" (cogito ergo sum).

Cogito ergo sum

Descartes's Latin phrase ("I think, therefore I am"); the foundation of his philosophy; shifted focus from external authority to the individual thinking mind.

Deism

Important Terms (2/3)

Natural Rights

Rights belonging to every human by nature, not granted by governments; Locke's core rights: life, liberty, property; roots in Christian natural law tradition.

State of Nature

Locke's philosophical thought experiment imagining life before government; establishes that rights exist before and independent of any state.

Social Contract

The agreement by which people consent to be governed in exchange for protection of their rights; if government violates rights, the people may replace it; directly influenced the Declaration of Ind...

General Will

Rousseau's concept of what the community truly needs, not the sum of individual desires; used by Robespierre to justify the Terror.

Separation of Powers

Montesquieu's remedy for tyranny: dividing government into legislative, executive, and judicial branches that check each other; foundation of the US Constitution.

L'infame
