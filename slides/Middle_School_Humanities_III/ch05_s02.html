Important Terms (1/3)

Abolition

The movement to end slavery and the slave trade.

Enlightenment

An 18th-century intellectual and philosophical movement that emphasized reason, individualism, and human rights.

Natural Rights

Fundamental rights that are believed to be inherent to all human beings and not dependent on government or cultural norms.

Imago Dei

A Latin theological term meaning "image of God," referring to the belief that human beings are created in God's likeness and therefore possess inherent dignity.

Middle Passage

The sea journey undertaken by slave ships from West Africa to the West Indies, notorious for its horrific conditions and high mortality rates.

Important Terms (2/3)

Encomienda System

Conquistadors

Spanish conquerors of Mexico and Peru in the 16th century.

Council of Valladolid (1550-51)

A significant debate in Spanish history between Bartolomé de Las Casas and Juan Ginés de Sepúlveda regarding the treatment of indigenous peoples in the Americas.

Polemic

A strong verbal or written attack on someone or something; a controversial argument.

Dispossession

The action of depriving someone of land, property, or other possessions.

Important Terms (3/3)

Ethnographic Mode

A style of writing or description that provides detailed accounts of the customs, social life, and culture of a particular people or group.

Introduction: Three Witnesses

Olaudah Equiano

A survivor of the Middle Passage who used Enlightenment principles and literary skill to advocate for abolition.

Bartolomé de las Casas

A Spanish priest who condemned the conquest and enslavement of indigenous peoples in the Americas based on Christian theology.

William Shakespeare

Through his play *The Tempest*, he explored themes of dispossession and power dynamics, reflecting on early colonial encounters.
