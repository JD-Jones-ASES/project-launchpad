How to Read Nietzsche

Study Slides

The Aphoristic Style

Purpose

The aphorism honors the "messiness" of reality.

Reader’s Role

Because aphorisms are isolated perspectives or provocations, the reader must do the active work of connecting them and holding contradictory ideas in tension.

Methodological Provocation

Performance

Dramatizing a position to expose its flaws.

Sincerity

Expressing a genuine, if uncomfortable, position.

Doors vs. Walls

Some provocations are "doors" inviting deeper insight, while others are "walls" representing positions the reader must argue against.

Short-Answer Practice Questions

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Aphorism

A short, self-contained section of writing. Nietzsche uses these to provide individual "perspectives" or "tests" rather than a continuous argument.

Intellectual Dishonesty

Nietzsche’s term for the pretense that the world can be fully captured by a logical, non-contradictory philosophical system.

Perspectivalism

The philosophical concept that there is no single absolute truth or viewpoint; rather, the world must be understood through multiple, sometimes conflicting, angles.

Philologist

Historically, a scholar of language and classical texts. Nietzsche suggests readers must adopt the "delicate eyes" of a philologist to read his work properly.

Provocateur

A role Nietzsche adopts to unsettle readers, using "monstrous" or shocking claims to force them to question their own assumptions.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Rumination (*Wiederkäuen*)

Literally "chewing the cud." The process of reading slowly, repeatedly, and deeply, as opposed to consuming information quickly.
