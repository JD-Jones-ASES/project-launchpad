Important Terms (1/2)

Aphorism

A short, pithy, and often self-contained statement expressing a general truth or observation; Nietzsche's primary literary form.

Systematic Philosophy

A philosophical approach that aims to create a comprehensive, logically consistent, and interconnected framework to explain reality. Nietzsche rejected this.

Perspectival

The characteristic of being dependent on a particular perspective or viewpoint, implying that no single, objective view fully captures reality.

Provocation

In Nietzsche's context, an intentional statement or idea designed to disturb, challenge, or unsettle the reader's assumptions to stimulate genuine thought.

Wiederkäuen

A German term literally meaning "chewing the cud," used metaphorically by Nietzsche to describe the slow, deliberate, and repeated process of reading and contemplating his texts.

Important Terms (2/2)

Classical Philology

The academic study of ancient Greek and Latin languages, literatures, and cultures, which was Nietzsche's initial academic profession.

Arthur Schopenhauer (1788–1860)

A German philosopher whose work, *The World as Will and Representation*, profoundly influenced Nietzsche with its concept of a blind, irrational "Will" underlying existence.

Richard Wagner (1813–1883)

A famous German composer and conductor, known for his operas and his vision of a comprehensive artwork (Gesamtkunstwerk), who became a significant friend and intellectual influence on Nietzsche.

The Will (Schopenhauerian)

Schopenhauer's concept of a fundamental, irrational, and purposeless striving force that constitutes the underlying reality of the world and drives all phenomena, including human desires.
