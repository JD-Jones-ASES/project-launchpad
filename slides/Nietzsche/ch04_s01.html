Guilt, Conscience, and Punishment

Study Slides

The Inefficacy of Punishment

External vs. Internal

Punishment is an external imposition acting upon the body. It teaches individuals how to avoid being caught in the future, but it does not teach them that they *deserved* to suffer.

The Source of Guilt

True guilt (bad conscience) is an internal process caused by the internalization of instincts, not by external disciplinary measures.

Bad Conscience as "Pregnancy"

Internalization of Instinct

When human instincts of aggression are prevented from being expressed outwardly, they turn inward.
