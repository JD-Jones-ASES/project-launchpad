Negative vs. Positive Freedom

Type of Freedom. Description

Nietzschean Context

Negative Freedom. "Freedom from" authority, church, and tradition.

Achieved by the "freethinker"; necessary but incomplete.

Positive Freedom. "Freedom for" the creation of new values and ways of life.

The hallmark of the genuine free spirit; moves beyond nihilism.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Aristocratic (Spiritual)

A hierarchy of rank based on intellectual strength and independence rather than hereditary birth or race.

Freedom For (Positive Freedom)

The capacity to create new values and new possibilities of life.

Freedom From (Negative Freedom)

The liberation from external authorities like the church, tradition, or inherited moral codes.

Freethinker

Nietzsche’s derogatory term for shallow rationalists who replace religious dogma with an uncritical belief in scientific truth.

Nihilist

Someone who concludes that there are no values or meaning and remains in that state of rejection.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pathos of Distance

The feeling of superiority or separation that a higher "type" of person feels toward the common mass.

The Three Metamorphoses

The spiritual evolution from the Camel (burden-bearer) to the Lion (destroyer) to the Child (creator).

Truth (Value of)

The meta-question asked by the free spirit regarding why humanity values "truth" as an absolute in the first place.
