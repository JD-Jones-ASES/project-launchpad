Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the single most important interpretive key to understanding Nietzsche's The Antichrist?

The sharp distinction between the person of Jesus and the religion of Christianity.

Why is it easy for readers to miss Nietzsche's distinction between Jesus and Christianity?

His rhetoric is so ferocious that it often appears as an attack on everything associated with the tradition.

How does Nietzsche describe the political or social nature of Jesus?

He calls Jesus a "holy anarchist."

What was Jesus' stance toward evil according to Nietzsche's interpretation?

He refused to resist evil.

How did Nietzsche's Jesus view the "kingdom of God"?

As a present way of experiencing the world rather than a future reward.

Why does Nietzsche find Jesus' character sympathetic?

Jesus is not a theologian and does not trade in doctrines or creeds.

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to Nietzsche, how did Jesus communicate his message?

He simply lived a certain way through his practice.

When did Jesus' specific practice end according to Nietzsche's claim in AC 39?

It died with him on the cross.

What is the implication of Nietzsche's claim that "the only genuine Christian died on the cross"?

Every subsequent Christian has practiced something other than what Jesus taught.

What is Nietzsche's primary target when he attacks the religion built in Jesus' name?

The theology of Paul and the institutional church.

According to Nietzsche, how does institutional Christianity relate to Jesus' practice?

It is a betrayal of his practice rather than a continuation of it.

What did the apostle Paul do to Jesus' "way of living"?

He turned it into a system of belief.
