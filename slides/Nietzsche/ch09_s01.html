Comparative Analysis: Nietzsche and Kant

Feature. Kant’s Categorical Imperative

Nietzsche’s Eternal Recurrence

Primary Question. Could the principle of my action become a universal law for everyone?

Could I will the infinite, identical repetition of my own life?

Orientation. Social: Focused on duty, fairness, and consistency with others.

Personal: Focused on self-affirmation and creative intensity.

Assumption. Morality must be universal; what is right for one is right for all.

Morality is individual; the standard is the depth and honesty of one's own life.

Goal. To determine the moral validity of a specific action or maxim.

To determine if a life is "worthy of eternity."

Glossary of Key Terms

Amor Fati

Literally "love of fate." The state of being where one does not wish anything to be different, embracing every aspect of life, including suffering.

Categorical Imperative

Kant’s moral test requiring individuals to act only according to principles that they could will to become universal laws.

GS 341

A reference to passage 341 in The Gay Science, where Nietzsche introduces the demon that proposes the eternal recurrence.

Nachlass

A German term meaning "that which is left behind." In Nietzsche scholarship, it refers to his massive volume of unpublished notebooks, letters, and fragments.

The Demon

The figure in Nietzsche’s thought experiment who offers the hypothesis of the eternal recurrence to test the listener's response.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the literal translation of the German term "Nachlass"?

That which is left behind.

In scholarly terms, what does Nietzsche's "Nachlass" comprise?

His unpublished notebooks, letters, and fragments.

Which individual is noted for the editorial manipulation of Nietzsche's Nachlass?

Elisabeth Förster-Nietzsche.

The notebook arguments for eternal recurrence as a physical theory assume that time is infinite and matter is _____.

Finite

According to the physical theory in Nietzsche's notebooks, what must eventually happen to every possible configuration of atoms?

They must recur.

In which specific published works does Nietzsche present eternal recurrence as an existential test?

GS 341 and BGE 56.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the core hypothetical question posed by the published versions of eternal recurrence?

"What if your life recurred?"

What figure appears in GS 341 to offer the hypothesis of eternal recurrence?

A demon.

Scholars Alexander Nehamas and Maudemarie Clark argue that eternal recurrence should not be treated as a _____ theory.

Physical

To what other philosopher's "evil demon" thought experiment is Nietzsche's demon compared?

René Descartes.

Nietzsche's eternal recurrence experiment is valuable because it reveals what an individual truly _____.

Values

While a doctor might ask how you would spend a single remaining year of life, Nietzsche asks how you would feel if time were _____.

Infinite
