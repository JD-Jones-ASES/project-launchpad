The Rigor of "No Regrets"

Modern Version

A philosophy of impulse that avoids reflection.

Nietzschean Version

A rigorous self-examination where one faces every wound, failure, and moment of shame, and affirms them as necessary components of the whole of one's life.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Amor Fati

Latin for "love of fate"; the attitude of not only accepting but embracing one's life and the history of the world as necessary.

Ascetic

A person who practices severe self-discipline and abstention from all forms of indulgence, often for spiritual reasons; associated by Nietzsche with "nay-saying" and life-denial.

Logos

The Stoic concept of a rational, divine order that governs the universe.

Nirvana

In Buddhism, the state of liberation from the cycle of suffering and rebirth; Nietzsche viewed it as a form of world-denial.

Pessimism (Schopenhauerian)

The belief that existence is fundamentally suffering and that the best course of action is the denial of the will to live.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Samsara

The Buddhist cycle of birth, death, and rebirth, characterized as a "wheel of suffering."

The Greatest Weight

Nietzsche’s term for the burden of the eternal recurrence, which makes every moment and decision matter by making them eternal.

Will (Schopenhauerian)

A blind, irrational force that drives all of existence and is the source of endless unsatisfied desire and suffering.
