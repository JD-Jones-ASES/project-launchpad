Friedrich Nietzsche

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Christendom

A term used by Kierkegaard to describe the institutional, state-sponsored, and bourgeois version of Christianity that he believed betrayed the radical nature of the faith.

Death of God Theology

A mid-20th-century theological movement asserting that traditional theism is no longer culturally viable and that faith must be lived in a secularized context.

Ground of Being

Paul Tillich’s redefinition of God, moving away from a traditional "supernatural being" toward a more philosophical concept.

Historical Jesus

The figure of Jesus as he actually lived, as distinguished from the "Christ of faith" or the doctrines established by the church.

Kerygma

The preached message of the New Testament; Rudolf Bultmann argued this was the essential element of Christianity, regardless of historical facts about Jesus.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Leap of Faith

Kierkegaard’s concept of a necessary, irrational, and terrifying individual commitment to God that defies the comforts of institutional religion.

Religionless Christianity

A concept proposed by Dietrich Bonhoeffer regarding the practice of faith in a world where traditional religious structures have lost their meaning.

The "Idot"

A reference to Dostoevsky's novel of the same name, used by Nietzsche to characterize Jesus as a figure of simple, mystical innocence.
