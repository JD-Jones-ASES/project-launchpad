Quiz Question 1

In the context of 'The Gay Science' aphorism 341, why does Nietzsche specifically place the thought experiment of eternal recurrence after the declaration that 'God is dead'?

A. To provide a scientific alternative to religious accounts of the afterlife.

B. To serve as a test of whether an individual can confront the resulting void without nihilism.

C. To demonstrate that moral values are still objective even without a divine lawgiver.

D. To console the 'madman' by suggesting that life continues in a different form.

Hint: Consider the relationship between the loss of external meaning and the need for internal affirmation.

Quiz Question 2

Which of the following best describes the condition the demon imposes during the thought experiment in GS 341?

A. The soul will be reincarnated into different lives based on previous karma.

B. Life will repeat, but with the possibility of minor improvements in each cycle.

C. Every moment of your current life will repeat identically, infinitely, and without change.

D. Only the most significant triumphs and failures are preserved in the eternal cycle.

Hint: Look for the term that emphasizes the lack of any variation or 'second chances.'

Quiz Question 3

Nietzsche contrasts the 'Yes-sayer' ($Ja-sagender$) with the 'Nay-sayer' ($Nein-sagender$). According to the text, which group does he associate with the latter?

A. Egoists and practitioners of $amor\ fati$.

B. Ancient Stoic philosophers like Marcus Aurelius.

C. Christianity, pessimism, and nihilism.

D. Scientific materialists and atheists.

Hint: Identify the traditions Nietzsche believes view existence as something to be redeemed or escaped.

Quiz Question 4

In 'Beyond Good and Evil' 56, Nietzsche uses the musical term 'da capo' to describe a specific human ideal. What does this reveal about his philosophy?

A. That life should be viewed as a scripted performance with a fixed ending.

B. That the 'vivacious man' insatiably desires the repetition of the 'play' exactly as it was and is.

C. That one should strive to change the 'melody' of their life in the next cycle.

D. That suffering is merely a 'discord' that will be resolved in the final movement.

Hint: Think about the literal musical instruction 'da capo' and what it asks the performer to do.

Quiz Question 5

How does Nietzsche’s concept of $amor\ fati$ differ from the traditional Stoic acceptance of fate?

A. Nietzsche argues that fate can be changed through the power of the will.

B. Stoics demanded love of fate, whereas Nietzsche only counsels patient endurance.

C. Nietzsche demands that one not only accept fate but perceive the necessary characters in things as beautiful.

D. Nietzsche believes fate is an illusion, while Stoics believed it was a physical law.

Hint: Consider the difference between 'resignation to what must be' and 'active love for what must be.'

Quiz Question 6

Nietzsche's phrase 'circulus vitiosus deus' in BGE 56 is used to describe which concept?

A. The idea that God is a logic error in traditional theology.

B. The world eternally repeating itself and willing this repetition.

C. The moral cycle of sin and redemption in Christian doctrine.

D. The repetitive nature of philosophical arguments throughout history.

Hint: Translate the Latin components 'circulus' and 'deus' to find the connection to the eternal return.
