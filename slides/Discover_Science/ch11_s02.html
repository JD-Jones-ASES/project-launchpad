Vertebrates and Invertebrates: A Study Guide

Key Concepts

Backbone as a Classifier

The presence or absence of a backbone (spine) is the primary characteristic used to divide the entire animal kingdom into two main groups.

Endoskeleton Functions

Provides shape and support, protects soft internal organs, and works with muscles for movement.

Important Terms (1/2)

Vertebrate

An animal that has a backbone (spine) inside its body.

Backbone (Spine)

A column of small bones running along the back of an animal. It provides support for the body and protects the delicate spinal cord and nerves.

Endoskeleton

An internal skeleton, typically made of bone or cartilage, found inside an animal's body (characteristic of vertebrates).

Invertebrate

An animal that does not have a backbone.

Exoskeleton

A hard, protective outer covering that supports and protects the body of some invertebrates.

Important Terms (2/2)

Molting

The process by which an animal sheds its old exoskeleton to grow a new, larger one, allowing for growth.

Invertebrates: Animals without a Backbone

No Skeleton

Some invertebrates, such as earthworms and jellyfish, are entirely soft-bodied and lack any hard skeletal structure.
