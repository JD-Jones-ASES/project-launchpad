Where Resources Come From

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Earth's Layers as Resource Providers

The atmosphere, hydrosphere, and lithosphere each offer unique and vital natural resources necessary for life and human activities.

Resource Extraction Methods

Humans employ specific techniques like mining, drilling, logging, and farming to retrieve resources from their natural state.

Transformation from Raw to Finished Product

Natural resources rarely appear as finished goods; they undergo complex processing steps, often involving significant energy and other resources, to become usable products.

Responsible Resource Management

While resources are essential for human existence, their use must be managed carefully and wisely to mitigate environmental damage.

Important Terms (1/3)

Atmosphere

The layer of gases surrounding Earth, providing resources like oxygen, nitrogen, and wind energy.

Hydrosphere

All the water on Earth, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and underground water, providing fresh water, food, and minerals.

Lithosphere

Earth's solid outer layer, consisting of land and rock, which provides soil, minerals, metals, and fossil fuels.

Mining

The process of digging into the Earth to remove valuable rocks, minerals, metals, or fossil fuels.

Surface mining

A type of mining where soil and rock are removed from the top to reach resources located near the surface, creating large open pits.

Important Terms (2/3)

Underground mining

A type of mining where tunnels and shafts are dug deep into the Earth to access resources far below the surface.

Drilling

The process of boring deep wells into the Earth to extract liquids or gases, primarily oil and natural gas.

Logging

The act of cutting down trees to harvest wood for various uses like building, paper, and fuel.

Farming

The practice of cultivating land, growing crops, and raising livestock for food and other useful products (e.g., cotton, rubber).

Ore

A naturally occurring rock or mineral from which a valuable metal or other mineral can be extracted, often containing impurities.

Important Terms (3/3)

Smelt

To extract metal from its ore by heating it in a furnace, often with a chemical reducing agent.

Landfills

Large areas where trash and waste materials are buried underground for disposal.

Erosion

The process by which soil and rock are removed from the Earth's surface by natural processes such as wind or water, often exacerbated by the removal of plant cover.
