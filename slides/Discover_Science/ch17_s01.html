What Is Weather?

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of Weather

Weather is the condition of the atmosphere (the blanket of air around Earth) at a particular location and moment. It describes what the air is doing right now.

Dynamic Nature of Weather

Weather is not static; it changes constantly, often within hours or days. This ever-changing nature is a defining characteristic.

Important Terms (1/2)

Weather

The condition of the atmosphere in a particular place at a particular time.

Atmosphere

The blanket of air that surrounds Earth.

Temperature

A measure of how hot or cold the air is.

Wind

Moving air; described by its speed and the direction from which it is blowing.

Clouds

Visible masses of tiny water droplets or ice crystals suspended in the atmosphere.

Important Terms (2/2)

Precipitation

Any form of water—liquid or solid—that falls from the atmosphere to Earth's surface (e.g., rain, snow, sleet, hail).

Water Cycle

The continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth, involving processes like evaporation, condensation, and precipitation.

Evaporation

The process by which liquid water changes into water vapor (a gas) and rises into the atmosphere.

Condensation

The process by which water vapor in the air cools and changes back into liquid water droplets or ice crystals, forming clouds.

Why Does Weather Matter?

What We Wear

Weather dictates our clothing choices. We bundle up in coats and hats on cold, windy days and opt for light clothing like shorts and t-shirts on hot, sunny days.

What We Eat (and Grow)

Farmers rely heavily on favorable weather conditions to grow crops. Too little rain can lead to a drought, causing crops to fail, while too much rain can result in destructive flooding.

How We Travel

Weather significantly affects transportation. Snow and ice make roads treacherous and can delay or cancel flights. Dense fog drastically reduces visibility, making driving dangerous.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of weather?

Weather is the condition of the atmosphere in a particular place at a particular time.

How does the atmosphere relate to weather?

The atmosphere is the blanket of air that surrounds Earth, and weather describes what the atmosphere is doing right now.

Does weather stay the same or does it change?

Weather changes all the time, often within hours or days. It describes what is happening right now or in the next few days.

Name the four main things scientists look at to describe weather.

Temperature, Wind, Clouds, and Precipitation.

What does 'Temperature' describe in terms of weather?

How hot or cold the air is.

What does 'Wind' describe in terms of weather?

If the air is moving, how fast it's moving, and in which direction.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What do 'Clouds' describe in terms of weather?

The kinds of clouds in the sky and how much of the sky they cover.

What does 'Precipitation' describe in terms of weather?

Water falling from the sky as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

How is the water cycle connected to weather?

The water cycle is the 'engine' that drives much of our weather, especially cloud formation and precipitation.

What is the first step in the water cycle that leads to weather events?

The Sun heats water on Earth's surface, causing some of it to evaporate into the air as water vapor.

Describe how clouds form as part of the water cycle.

Water vapor rises into the atmosphere, cools, and condenses into tiny droplets, forming clouds.

When do these cloud droplets become precipitation?

When the droplets grow heavy enough, they fall as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.
