Atoms Bond to Make Molecules and Compounds Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Chemical Bonds Hold Atoms Together

A chemical bond is an invisible but strong force that connects two or more atoms, forming stable groups.

Molecules are Groups of Bonded Atoms

A molecule is formed when two or more atoms, regardless of whether they are the same or different kinds, are held together by chemical bonds.

Compounds are Molecules of Different Atoms

A compound is a specific type of molecule that consists of two or more *different kinds* of atoms bonded together.

The Molecule-Compound Relationship

All compounds are molecules, but not all molecules are compounds. For example, oxygen gas (O2) is a molecule but not a compound, while water (H2O) is both a molecule and a compound.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Models Represent Molecules

Scientists use simple models, often with colored circles representing different atoms and lines representing chemical bonds, to visualize how atoms are connected in molecules.

Important Terms

Chemical bond

A strong, invisible force that holds two or more atoms together to form a molecule or compound.

Molecule

A group of two or more atoms (which can be the same or different kinds) that are chemically bonded together.

Compound

A substance made of two or more *different kinds* of atoms that are chemically bonded together. All compounds are molecules, but not all molecules are compounds.
