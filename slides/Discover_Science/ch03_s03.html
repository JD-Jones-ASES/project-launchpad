Chapter 3, Section 3: Properties of Matter

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of Property

A characteristic of matter that can be observed or measured. Properties are essential for describing and identifying different types of matter.

Common Properties of Matter

Using Properties for Identification

By observing and comparing a combination of properties, we can narrow down the possibilities for an unknown object or substance, helping to identify it.

Scientific Application of Properties

Scientists, like geologists, systematically test and record properties (e.g., mineral hardness, color, streak) to accurately identify substances.

Properties Across States of Matter

Properties can be used to describe solids, liquids, and gases, although some properties (like shape or hardness) apply differently depending on the state.

Important Terms

Property

A characteristic of matter that you can observe or measure, such as color, shape, size, texture, smell, or hardness.

Common Properties We Use (1/2)

Color

This is one of the most obvious properties. Is an object red, blue, green, or perhaps clear?

Shape

Does it have a distinct form? Is it round, square, flat, or does it have an irregular, undefined shape?

Size

How large or small is it? We can describe its length, width, height, or simply say "big" or "small."

Texture

This describes how an object feels when you touch it. Is it smooth like glass, rough like sandpaper, bumpy like a strawberry, or soft like a pillow?

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a 'property' in the context of matter?

A characteristic of matter that you can observe or measure.

How do properties help us understand and identify matter?

They help us describe and distinguish between different types of matter.

Name three common properties of matter discussed in the text.

Color, shape, size, texture, smell, and hardness (any three).

What property would you describe if you mentioned something was 'red, blue, or clear'?

Color.

If an object is described as 'round, flat, or irregular,' which property is being referred to?

Shape.

What information does the property 'size' convey about an object?

How big or small it is, including its length, width, or height.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you determine the 'texture' of an object?

By how it feels to the touch, such as smooth, rough, bumpy, or soft.

What crucial safety precaution must be taken when investigating the 'smell' of a substance?

Never smell unknown chemicals directly.

What does the property 'hardness' indicate about an object?

Whether it is hard like a rock or soft like a pillow, and if it can be scratched.

Imagine you are handed a mystery object in a bag. What specific properties could you use to begin identifying it without seeing it?

Texture, shape, size, and hardness.

How do scientists, such as geologists, use properties to identify substances like minerals?

They test and observe specific properties like hardness, color, and streak (the color of its powder).

When describing a strawberry as 'red, small, bumpy, and sweet-smelling,' what are you listing?

Its properties.
