CHAPTER 26, SECTION 4: HOW ADAPTATIONS HAPPEN

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Natural Selection

The fundamental process by which organisms with beneficial traits for survival and reproduction are more likely to pass those traits on, leading to populations changing over long periods.

Variation

The natural differences that exist among individuals within any group of plants or animals, such as slight differences in fur thickness or running speed.

Struggle to Survive

All living things face daily challenges like finding food, avoiding predators, and enduring harsh weather, meaning not all individuals will survive to reproduce.

Helpful Traits

Variations that give an individual an advantage in their environment, making them more likely to survive and grow.

Passing on Traits

Organisms that survive are more likely to reproduce, and their offspring often inherit the helpful traits, ensuring they continue in the population.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Fossils

Preserved remains or traces of ancient living things, providing concrete evidence of how life on Earth has changed over millions of years.

Extinction

The complete disappearance of a species from Earth, often occurring when environmental changes happen too rapidly for species to adapt.

Types of Fossils:

Body fossils

Actual remains of an organism, such as bones, teeth, or shells, that have been mineralized and turned to rock.

Trace fossils

Evidence of an organism's activity, like footprints, burrows, or nests, preserved in rock.

Plant fossils

Impressions of leaves, bark, or seeds pressed into rock, showing their ancient forms.

Insects in amber

Small insects or other creatures trapped in sticky tree sap that hardened into amber, preserving them in remarkable detail.

What Fossils Reveal:

Past Diversity

Fossils show that Earth was once home to many creatures vastly different from those alive today, such as dinosaurs, giant dragonflies, and enormous sharks.

Causes of Extinction:

Rapid Environmental Change

The most famous example is the asteroid impact 66 million years ago, which caused drastic climate changes leading to the extinction of dinosaurs and many other species.

Habitat Destruction

As human populations grow, natural habitats are often destroyed for development, agriculture, or resources, leaving species without places to live and find food.

Pollution

Contamination of air, water, and soil can harm and kill organisms, reducing their numbers and leading to extinction.

Climate Change

Modern climate change, driven partly by human activities, is altering environments too quickly for many species to adapt, posing a significant threat of increased extinction rates today.
