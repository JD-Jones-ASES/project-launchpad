Parts of a Plant

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Specialized Plant Parts

Plants are composed of distinct parts, each performing specific functions essential for life.

Roots

Anchor the plant in the soil, absorb water and nutrients, and can store food.

Stem

Provides support to hold the plant upright and acts as a transport system for water, nutrients, and sugars throughout the plant.

Leaves

The primary sites for photosynthesis, where sunlight energy is captured to make food (sugar) for the plant, largely due to chlorophyll.

Flowers

Reproductive organs that produce seeds through a process called pollination, often attracting pollinators with color and scent.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Fruit

Develops from the flower after pollination, serving to protect the seeds and aid in their dispersal.

Seeds

Contain a tiny embryonic plant and stored food, allowing for the propagation of new plants.

Scientific vs. Common Definitions

The scientific classification of plant parts (e.g., fruit vs. vegetable) can differ from common culinary usage.

Important Terms (1/2)

Roots

The parts of a plant that grow underground, holding the plant in place and absorbing water and nutrients.

Stem

The main stalk of a plant that supports it upright and transports substances between roots and leaves.

Leaves

Flat, usually green parts of a plant that capture sunlight and make food (sugar).

Chlorophyll (KLOR-oh-fill)

The special green substance in leaves that captures energy from sunlight.

Flowers

The colorful, reproductive parts of a plant that produce seeds.

Important Terms (2/2)

Pollen

Tiny grains produced by flowers that are essential for plant reproduction.

Pollination

The process where pollen is transferred from one flower to another, leading to seed formation.

Fruit

The part of a plant that forms around the seeds and protects them, developing from a pollinated flower.

Seeds

Tiny packages containing a baby plant and a supply of food to help it grow.
