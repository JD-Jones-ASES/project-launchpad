Important Terms (1/7)

Life cycle

The series of stages a living thing goes through from birth to death.

Germination

When a seed begins to sprout and grow into a new plant.

Metamorphosis

A dramatic change in body form during an organism's life cycle (e.g., tadpole to frog, caterpillar to butterfly).

Adaptation

A feature or behavior that helps an organism survive in its environment.

Structural adaptation

A physical feature that helps survival (e.g., thick fur, sharp claws, cactus spines).

Important Terms (2/7)

Behavioral adaptation

An action or behavior that helps survival (e.g., migration, hibernation, camouflage).

Natural selection

The process by which organisms with helpful traits survive longer and have more offspring, so those traits become more common.

Fossil

Evidence of an organism that lived long ago, preserved in rock or other material.

Extinction

When every member of a species has died and no more exist.

Natural resource

Something from nature that people use to meet their needs.

Important Terms (3/7)

Renewable resource

A resource that can be replaced or regrown within a human lifetime (e.g., sunlight, wind, trees, water).

Nonrenewable resource

A resource that takes millions of years to form and cannot be replaced once used up (e.g., coal, oil, natural gas, metals).

Fossil fuel

A fuel formed from the remains of ancient organisms buried and changed over millions of years (coal, oil, natural gas).

Conservation

The careful use and protection of natural resources.

Endangered species

A type of living thing that is in danger of becoming extinct.

Important Terms (4/7)

Chemical bond

A force that holds atoms together in a molecule or compound.

Molecule

Two or more atoms bonded together.

Compound

A molecule made of two or more different types of atoms bonded together (e.g., H2O, CO2, NaCl).

Chemical formula

A shorthand way to show which atoms are in a molecule and how many of each (e.g., H2O means 2 hydrogen and 1 oxygen).

Conservation of matter

The principle that matter is never created or destroyed in a chemical reaction — atoms just rearrange.

Important Terms (5/7)

Electric charge

A property of matter; can be positive or negative.

Static electricity

A buildup of electric charge on an object.

Current electricity

A steady flow of electric charges through a conductor.

Circuit

A complete path through which electricity can flow.

Conductor

A material that allows electricity to flow through it easily (e.g., metals like copper and aluminum).

Important Terms (6/7)

Insulator

A material that does not allow electricity to flow through it easily (e.g., rubber, plastic, wood).

Magnet

An object that produces a magnetic field and attracts certain metals (iron, nickel, cobalt).

Magnetic field

The invisible area around a magnet where its force can be felt.

Electromagnet

A magnet created by running electric current through a coil of wire.

Generator

A machine that converts motion into electricity using magnets and wire coils.

Important Terms (7/7)

Electric motor

A machine that converts electricity into motion.
