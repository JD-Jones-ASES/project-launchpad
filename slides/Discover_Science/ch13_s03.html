Key Concepts (2/2)

Elements Grouped by Properties

Elements with similar chemical behaviors or properties are grouped together in columns on the periodic table, making it easier to study their characteristics.

Important Terms

Element

A pure substance made of only one kind of atom. It cannot be broken down into simpler substances.

Chemical symbol

A one- or two-letter abbreviation for an element. The first letter is always capitalized.

Periodic table

A chart that organizes all known elements by their properties. Elements with similar properties are grouped together.

Atomic number

The number that identifies each element. It tells how many protons are in the center of each atom of that element.

What Are Elements?

Definition

An element is a pure substance that is made of only one specific kind of atom. This means all atoms in a sample of an element are identical.

Indivisible

Elements cannot be broken down into simpler substances using ordinary chemical methods. They are fundamental building blocks of matter.

Examples

How Many Elements Are There?

Total Discovered

Scientists have identified approximately 118 different elements.

Universal Building Blocks

Remarkably, these 118 elements combine in various ways to form everything in the entire universe—stars, planets, living organisms, water, and sand.

Common Elements

While there are 118 elements, you typically encounter only about 20-30 of them regularly in daily life.
