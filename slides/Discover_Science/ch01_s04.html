Observing Living Things

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Power of Observation

Direct observation is a fundamental scientific method for learning about living things and their behaviors.

Techniques for Good Observation

Being quiet and patient, using all senses (safely), recording observations in a field journal (including drawings), looking closely with tools like magnifying glasses, and searching for life in div...

Classification of Objects

Objects found in nature can be categorized as living, nonliving, or once-living, helping to organize observations.

Characteristics of Life

Observing living things often reveals evidence of their fundamental characteristics, such as movement, response to environment, or growth.

The Scientific Impact of Observation

As demonstrated by Jane Goodall, careful and patient observation can lead to groundbreaking discoveries, challenging existing beliefs and revealing complex aspects of animal life.

Important Terms (1/2)

Observer

Someone who watches carefully, especially to notice details.

Field Journal

A notebook used by scientists and naturalists to record observations, drawings, and notes while in the field.

Classify

To arrange things into groups based on shared characteristics.

Magnifying Glass

A lens that produces an enlarged image of an object, used for seeing fine details.

Living

Possessing the characteristics of life, such as growth, reproduction, and response to stimuli.

Important Terms (2/2)

Nonliving

Lacking the characteristics of life; never having been alive (e.g., a rock, water).

Once-living

Items that were once part of a living organism but are no longer alive (e.g., a fallen leaf, a log, a seashell).
