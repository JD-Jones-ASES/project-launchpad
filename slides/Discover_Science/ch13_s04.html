Key Concepts (1/2)

Molecules are Everywhere

Many common substances, from the water we drink to the air we breathe, are made of specific molecules.

Chemical Formula

A shorthand notation using element symbols and numbers to show the types and quantities of atoms in a molecule. For example, H₂O means two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.

Molecular Structure and Bonding

Atoms bond together to form molecules, and the arrangement and type of bonds determine the molecule's shape and properties (e.g., water molecules are bent, carbon dioxide is straight).

Important Terms (1/2)

Chemical Formula

A symbolic way to represent a molecule, showing the elements it contains and the number of atoms of each element.

Atom

The smallest unit of an element that retains the chemical identity of that element.

Molecule

A group of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds.

Element

A pure substance consisting only of atoms that all have the same numbers of protons in their nuclei.

Photosynthesis

The process used by plants to convert light energy into chemical energy, using carbon dioxide and water to create food and release oxygen.

Important Terms (2/2)

Periodic Table

A chart that organizes all known chemical elements by their atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties.

Water: H₂O

Formula

H₂O

Composition

Ubiquity

Water is incredibly common and essential for life. Trillions of these tiny H₂O molecules make up every drop of water.

Oxygen Gas: O₂

Formula

O₂

Composition

Oxygen gas, which we breathe, is not individual oxygen atoms. Instead, it's made of molecules where two oxygen (O) atoms are bonded together. The '2' indicates two oxygen atoms per molecule.

Importance

When you inhale, billions of O₂ molecules enter your lungs. Your body uses this oxygen to release energy from the food you eat, a process vital for survival.

Carbon Dioxide: CO₂

Formula

CO₂

Composition

Each carbon dioxide molecule consists of one carbon (C) atom bonded to two oxygen (O) atoms.

Reading a Chemical Formula

Letters

Indicate the types of atoms involved (e.g., Na for Sodium, Cl for Chlorine).

Numbers (Subscripts)

Indicate how many atoms of each type are in the molecule. If there is no number after an element symbol, it means there is only one atom of that element.

To understand the arrangement of atoms in real molecules and how they are bonded.

Key Learnings

Dmitri Mendeleev: Father of the Periodic Table

Background

A Russian chemist (1834–1907) who made a groundbreaking contribution to chemistry.

His Work

In 1869, Mendeleev developed the first widely accepted version of the periodic table. He organized the then-known 60 elements by writing their properties on cards and arranging them like a puzzle.

Key Insight

He noticed a repeating pattern in element properties when arranged by weight. Crucially, he left gaps in his table for elements not yet discovered, and he even predicted their properties.

Impact

When these predicted elements were later discovered and matched his predictions, the scientific community recognized the immense importance of his periodic table. Today, it is an indispensable tool...

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a chemical formula?

A short way to write a molecule using element symbols and numbers, telling how many of each atom are in the molecule.

What is the chemical formula for water, and what does it tell you about its composition?

The chemical formula for water is H2O. It tells you there are two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom.

In the chemical formula H2O, what does the 'H' stand for and how many atoms are there?

'H' stands for hydrogen, and the small '2' indicates there are two hydrogen atoms.

In the chemical formula H2O, what does the 'O' stand for and how many atoms are there?

'O' stands for oxygen. Since there is no number after it, there is just one oxygen atom.

What is the chemical formula for the oxygen gas we breathe?

O2.

How many oxygen atoms are in a molecule of oxygen gas (O2)?

Two oxygen atoms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is oxygen gas (O2) vital for human survival?

Our bodies use oxygen to release energy from the food we eat.

What is the chemical formula for carbon dioxide?

CO2.

Describe the composition of a carbon dioxide molecule (CO2).

A carbon dioxide molecule is made of one carbon atom bonded to two oxygen atoms.

What are two common occurrences or uses of carbon dioxide (CO2) in everyday life?

It is the gas we breathe out, and it gives soda its fizz (the bubbles in soda pop).

How do plants use carbon dioxide?

Plants use carbon dioxide from the air to make their food through a process called photosynthesis.

When reading a chemical formula, what does it mean if there is no small number written after an element symbol?

It means there is just one atom of that element in the molecule.
