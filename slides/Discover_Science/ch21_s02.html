Chapter 21, Section 2: Producers, Consumers, and Decomposers Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Energy Origin

Almost all energy in an ecosystem originates from the Sun, but not all organisms can use sunlight directly.

Three Main Roles

Living things are grouped into producers, consumers, and decomposers based on how they get energy.

Producers (Food Makers)

Organisms that make their own food, primarily through photosynthesis, forming the base of every ecosystem.

Photosynthesis

The process by which producers use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to create sugar (food) and release oxygen.

Consumers (Food Eaters)

Organisms that cannot make their own food and must eat other organisms to get energy, divided into herbivores, carnivores, and omnivores.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Decomposers (Nature's Recyclers)

Organisms that break down dead plants, animals, and waste, returning essential nutrients to the soil for producers to reuse.

Interconnected Cycle

Producers, consumers, and decomposers are linked in a vital cycle where energy flows from the Sun through organisms, and nutrients are continuously recycled within the ecosystem.

Important Terms (1/2)

Producer

A living thing that makes its own food, usually by using energy from sunlight (e.g., plants, algae).

Photosynthesis

The process by which plants and algae use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to make sugar (food) and release oxygen.

Consumer

A living thing that cannot make its own food and must eat other organisms to get energy (e.g., animals).

Herbivore

An animal that eats only plants (e.g., rabbits, deer).

Carnivore

An animal that eats only other animals (e.g., hawks, wolves).

Important Terms (2/2)

Omnivore

An animal that eats both plants and animals (e.g., bears, humans).

Decomposer

A living thing that breaks down dead plants and animals and returns nutrients to the soil (e.g., fungi, bacteria, earthworms).

Nutrients

Substances that decomposers return to the soil from dead organic material, which plant roots then absorb.

Decomposers: Nature's Recyclers

Definition

Decomposers are living things that break down dead plants, dead animals, and animal waste.

Function

They are the "clean-up crew" of an ecosystem. They break organic material into simpler substances called **nutrients**.

Recycling Role

These nutrients are returned to the soil, where plant roots can absorb and reuse them. This creates a continuous cycle of nutrient availability.

How the Three Roles Work Together

Interconnected Cycle

Producers, consumers, and decomposers are all linked in a continuous loop.

Energy Flow

Energy flows in one direction: from the Sun, through producers, and then to consumers.

Nutrient Cycling

Nutrients are constantly cycled: from the soil to producers, then to consumers, and finally back to the soil via decomposers.
