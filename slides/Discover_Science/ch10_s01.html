Important Terms (1/4)

Photosynthesis

The process by which green plants use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water.

Igneous rocks

Rocks formed from the cooling and solidification of molten magma or lava.

Sedimentary rocks

Rocks formed from the accumulation and compaction of sediments (fragments of other rocks, minerals, or organic matter) over time.

Rock cycle

The continuous process by which rocks are created, changed, destroyed, and reformed, involving processes like weathering, erosion, deposition, melting, and cooling.

Minerals

Naturally occurring inorganic solids with a definite chemical composition and crystalline structure, essential nutrients for plants.

Important Terms (2/4)

Dissolve

To break down and spread out evenly in a liquid, forming a solution.

Solution

A homogeneous mixture formed when one substance (the solute) dissolves in another (the solvent).

Solvent

The substance that dissolves another substance (usually a liquid). In the garden, water is the primary solvent for minerals.

Solute

The substance that is dissolved in a solvent to form a solution (e.g., minerals in water).

Xylem

A plant tissue that transports water and dissolved mineral nutrients from the roots to the rest of the plant.

Important Terms (3/4)

Fertilizer

A chemical or natural substance added to soil or land to increase its fertility and promote plant growth.

Concentrated solution

A solution containing a large amount of solute relative to the amount of solvent.

Length

A measure of distance or how long something is, often measured in **centimeters (cm)** using a **ruler**.

Mass

A measure of the amount of matter in an object, often measured in **grams (g)** using a **balance**.

Volume

A measure of the amount of space an object or substance occupies, often measured in **milliliters (mL)** using a **graduated cylinder**.

Important Terms (4/4)

Temperature

A measure of the degree of hotness or coldness of a substance or environment, often measured in **degrees Celsius (°C)** using a **thermometer**.

Chemistry: Solutions Feed the Garden

Dissolving Minerals

When water enters the soil, it interacts with the minerals. Many of these minerals **dissolve** in the water.

Solutions in Action

Nutrient Absorption

Plant roots are designed to absorb this mineral-rich solution. The dissolved nutrients are then transported throughout the plant, primarily through the **xylem**, reaching every leaf and stem.

Importance of Water

Without water to dissolve the minerals, plants cannot access these essential nutrients, even if the soil is rich in them.
