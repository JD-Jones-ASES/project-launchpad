Chemical Formulas as Recipes: Understanding Molecules and Reactions

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Chemical Formulas as Molecular Recipes

A chemical formula provides specific instructions for building a molecule, listing the elements and the exact number of each atom required.

Reading Element Symbols and Subscripts

Counting Atoms in Molecules

By reading the element symbols and their subscripts, you can determine the exact number of each type of atom and the total number of atoms in any given molecule.

Conservation of Atoms in Chemical Reactions

During a chemical reaction, atoms are rearranged to form new molecules, but the total number of each type of atom remains constant before and after the reaction. No atoms are created or destroyed.

Important Terms (1/2)

Chemical Formula

A combination of element symbols and subscript numbers that shows which atoms are in a molecule and how many of each.

Subscript

A small number written to the lower right of an element symbol. It tells how many of that atom are in the molecule.

Element Symbol

A one or two-letter abbreviation for a chemical element (e.g., H for hydrogen, O for oxygen, Na for sodium).

Molecule

Two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds, represented by a chemical formula.

Coefficient

A large number written in front of a chemical formula in an equation, indicating the number of molecules of that substance involved in the reaction.

Important Terms (2/2)

Chemical Reaction

A process that involves rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance, as opposed to a change in physical form or a nuclear reaction.

Conservation of Atoms/Matter

The scientific law stating that matter cannot be created or destroyed in an isolated system. In chemical reactions, this means the total number of each type of atom remains constant.

Atoms in Chemical Reactions: Conservation

Hydrogen (H)

The `2 H₂` means two molecules of hydrogen gas. Each H₂ molecule has 2 hydrogen atoms. So, `2 molecules * 2 atoms/molecule = 4 hydrogen atoms`.

Oxygen (O)

The `O₂` means one molecule of oxygen gas. Each O₂ molecule has 2 oxygen atoms. So, `1 molecule * 2 atoms/molecule = 2 oxygen atoms`.

Hydrogen (H)

The `2 H₂O` means two molecules of water. Each H₂O molecule has 2 hydrogen atoms. So, `2 molecules * 2 atoms/molecule = 4 hydrogen atoms`.

Oxygen (O)

The `2 H₂O` means two molecules of water. Each H₂O molecule has 1 oxygen atom (the subscript for O is implicit 1). So, `2 molecules * 1 atom/molecule = 2 oxygen atoms`.
