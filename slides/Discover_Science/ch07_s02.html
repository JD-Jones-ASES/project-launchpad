Key Concepts

Rock Classification by Formation

Rocks are grouped into igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic based *how they formed*, not just their current appearance.

Igneous Rocks: Formed from Melted Rock

These rocks originate from the cooling and hardening of molten rock.

Sedimentary Rocks: Formed from Layers of Sediment

These rocks are created when pieces of other rocks, sand, mud, or shells (called sediment) are deposited, compacted by weight, and then cemented together over time.

Metamorphic Rocks: Formed by Transformation

These rocks are existing igneous, sedimentary, or even other metamorphic rocks that have been changed ("metamorphosed") by intense heat, pressure, or both, deep within the Earth.

Distinct Characteristics

Each rock type has specific visual clues (e.g., crystals, layers, fossils, wavy bands) that help in identification.

Important Terms (1/2)

Igneous rock

A type of rock that forms when hot, melted rock (magma or lava) cools and hardens.

Magma

Hot, melted rock that is found deep *underground* within the Earth.

Lava

Hot, melted rock that flows *out of a volcano* onto the Earth's surface.

Sedimentary rock

A type of rock that forms when small pieces of rock, sand, mud, or shells (sediment) are pressed and cemented together over time.

Sediment

Tiny bits of rock, sand, mud, or shells that settle and accumulate, often carried by wind or water.

Important Terms (2/2)

Fossils

The preserved remains or traces of ancient living things, almost exclusively found in sedimentary rocks.

Metamorphic rock

A type of rock that has been changed from its original form by intense heat, pressure, or both deep inside the Earth.

Spotting Rocks in the Real World

Marble

(metamorphic) might appear in smooth, swirling-colored building facades or countertops.

Sandstone

(sedimentary) might be seen in buildings or pavers with a rough, sandy texture.

Granite

(igneous) is common in countertops and building exteriors, recognizable by its visible, interlocked mineral specks.
