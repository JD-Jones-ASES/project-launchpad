Forms of Energy

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Energy's Many Forms

Energy is a single concept, but it manifests in different ways, much like different flavors of ice cream are all still ice cream. Each form of energy does something specific.

Heat Energy (Thermal Energy)

This is the energy that moves from warmer objects to cooler objects, causing a sensation of warmth or heat. Heat transfer always occurs in this direction.

Light Energy

This is the form of energy that we can see, enabling us to perceive our surroundings. It travels extremely fast and in straight lines from its sources.

Sound Energy

This type of energy is created by vibrations. When an object vibrates, it sends waves through a medium (like air) that our ears detect and our brain interprets as sound.

Kinetic Energy

This is the energy an object possesses purely because it is in motion. The amount of kinetic energy an object has depends on both its speed and its mass.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Potential Energy

This is stored energy that an object has due to its position or condition, meaning it has the *potential* to do work or be converted into another form of energy.

Important Terms (1/2)

Heat Energy (Thermal Energy)

Energy that flows from warmer objects to cooler objects, making things feel hot or warm.

Light Energy

Energy that you can see, which travels in straight lines and allows us to see the world.

Sound Energy

Energy produced when an object vibrates, sending waves through the air (or other materials) that your ears can detect.

Vibration

A rapid back-and-forth or side-to-side motion that is necessary to produce sound energy.

Kinetic Energy

The energy an object has because it is moving. Derived from the Greek word meaning "to move."

Important Terms (2/2)

Potential Energy

Energy that is stored in an object because of its position or condition, waiting to be used or released.

Gravitational Potential Energy

A type of potential energy stored in an object due to its height above the ground.

Elastic Potential Energy

A type of potential energy stored in an object that is stretched or compressed, like a rubber band or spring.

Chemical Energy

A form of potential energy stored in the bonds of chemical compounds (like in food or batteries) that can be released during chemical reactions.

Kinetic Energy — The Energy of Motion

Speed Matters

The faster an object moves, the more kinetic energy it has. A car driving at 100 km/h has more kinetic energy than the same car driving at 50 km/h.
