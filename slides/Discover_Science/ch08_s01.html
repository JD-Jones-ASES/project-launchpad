Chapter 8, Section 1: What Is a Mixture?

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of a Mixture

A mixture is formed when two or more substances are combined, but each individual substance keeps its own unique properties.

Retention of Properties

In a mixture, the ingredients do not undergo any chemical change; they simply exist together in the same space, maintaining their original characteristics (like taste, color, or texture).

Ubiquity of Mixtures

Mixtures are incredibly common and can be found everywhere around us, from the food we eat to the environment we live in.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a mixture?

A mixture is two or more substances combined together, where each substance keeps its own properties.

What happens to the individual ingredients when they are combined in a mixture?

In a mixture, the ingredients do not turn into a different substance; they just sit together in the same place.

How can you tell if something is a mixture by observation?

You can still see, taste, or pick out the separate parts of a mixture.

Yes, in a mixture, every ingredient keeps its own properties, such as color, shape, and texture.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does a mixture differ from a chemical change regarding the properties of its parts?

In a mixture, parts keep their properties. In a chemical change, parts do not keep their properties because new substances are formed.

Is a new substance formed in a mixture? What about in a chemical change?

No new substance is formed in a mixture. Yes, a new substance is formed in a chemical change.

Are the components of a mixture generally easy to separate?

Yes, the components of a mixture are usually easy to separate.
