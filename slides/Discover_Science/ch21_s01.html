Key Concepts

Ecosystem Definition

An ecosystem is comprised of all the living (biotic) and nonliving (abiotic) things in a specific area, functioning together as a connected system where each part depends on others.

Interdependence

All parts of an ecosystem are interconnected. Living things rely on other living things and on nonliving things for survival, and in turn, living things can modify their nonliving surroundings.

Important Terms

Ecosystem

All the living things (biotic factors) and nonliving things (abiotic factors) in an area, working together as a system.

Biotic factor

Any living part of an ecosystem, including plants, animals, fungi, and bacteria.

Abiotic factor

Any nonliving part of an ecosystem, such as water, sunlight, air, soil, and temperature.

Universal Rules

Despite their unique characteristics, all ecosystems operate under fundamental principles: energy flows through them, and living things depend on each other and their nonliving surroundings.
