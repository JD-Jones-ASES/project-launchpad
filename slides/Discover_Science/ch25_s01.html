Important Terms (1/5)

Ecosystem

A community of living organisms interacting with their nonliving environment.

Food Web

A system of interlocking and interdependent food chains, illustrating who eats whom in an ecosystem.

Producers

Organisms (like plants, trees, mosses) that make their own food, usually through photosynthesis, forming the base of a food web.

Photosynthesis

The chemical process by which green plants and some other organisms use sunlight to synthesize foods from carbon dioxide and water, releasing oxygen as a byproduct.

Herbivores (Primary Consumers)

Organisms that primarily eat plants (e.g., squirrels, deer).

Important Terms (2/5)

Carnivores (Secondary Consumers)

Organisms that primarily eat other animals (e.g., hawks, foxes).

Decomposers

Organisms (like mushrooms, earthworms, bacteria) that break down dead organic matter, returning nutrients to the soil.

Nutrients

Substances that provide nourishment essential for the maintenance of life and growth.

Topsoil

The uppermost layer of soil, rich in organic matter and minerals, vital for plant growth.

Humus

Decomposed organic material (like leaves, bark) found in topsoil, which improves soil fertility.

Important Terms (3/5)

Subsoil

The layer of soil beneath the topsoil, typically less fertile.

Bedrock

The solid rock underlying loose deposits such as soil or alluvium.

Erosion

The process by which the surface of the Earth is worn away by the action of water, wind, ice, or gravity.

Weathering

The process of breaking down rocks, soil, and minerals through contact with the Earth's atmosphere, biota, and waters.

Carbon Dioxide (CO2)

A gas absorbed by plants during photosynthesis and released by animals during respiration.

Important Terms (4/5)

Water (H2O)

An essential compound for all known forms of life, absorbed by plants and released during respiration.

Glucose (C6H12O6)

A simple sugar that is an important energy source for living organisms, produced during photosynthesis.

Oxygen (O2)

A gas released by plants during photosynthesis and used by animals for respiration.

pH

A measure of the acidity or alkalinity of a solution, affecting which plants can grow in soil.

Light Absorption

The process by which light energy is taken in by a substance, such as a leaf for photosynthesis.

Important Terms (5/5)

Light Reflection

The process by which light bounces off a surface, such as green light reflecting off leaves.

Sound Waves

Vibrations that travel through a medium (like air or water) and can be heard.

Canopy

The uppermost layer of branches and foliage of trees in a forest, which intercepts most of the sunlight.
