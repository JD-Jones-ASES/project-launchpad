Important Terms (1/4)

Skeletal System

Provides shape, support, and protection for the body's organs.

Muscular System

Enables movement by pulling on bones.

Digestive System

Breaks down food into nutrients the body can use.

Nervous System

Sends and receives signals throughout the body, coordinating actions and senses.

Evaporation

The process by which a liquid changes to a gas, taking heat energy with it (e.g., sweat cooling the body).

Important Terms (2/4)

Shivering

Rapid, involuntary muscle contractions that generate heat to warm the body.

Chemical Change

A process that results in the formation of new substances with different properties (e.g., digestion).

Digestion

The process of breaking down food into molecules the body can absorb and use.

Glucose

A simple sugar that is a primary source of energy for the body's cells.

Law of Conservation of Matter

States that matter is neither created nor destroyed in any chemical reaction or physical transformation.

Important Terms (3/4)

Simple Machines

Basic mechanical devices that change the direction or magnitude of a force. Examples include levers, wedges, and pulleys.

Lever

A simple machine consisting of a rigid bar that pivots on a fixed point called a fulcrum.

Fulcrum

The fixed point around which a lever pivots.

Wedge

A simple machine that is thick at one end and tapers to a thin edge, used to separate or hold objects.

Pulleys

Simple machines consisting of a wheel over which a rope or cable runs, used to change the direction or magnitude of a force.

Important Terms (4/4)

Tendons

Strong, fibrous connective tissues that attach muscles to bones.

Physics: Your Body Is Full of Simple Machines

Lever Systems

Many bones and joints function as levers.

Wedges

Your front teeth (incisors) are designed as wedges. Their sharp, thin shape allows them to cut and split food, similar to how an axe splits wood.

Pulleys

Certain tendons in your body act like pulleys, changing the direction of the force generated by your muscles, allowing for efficient movement around joints.
