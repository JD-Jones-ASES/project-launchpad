Key Concepts (1/2)

Three States of Matter

Matter commonly exists in three main physical forms: solid, liquid, and gas. These states can be observed in a single substance, such as water (ice, liquid water, steam).

Solids

Characterized by a definite shape and a definite volume. Their particles are tightly packed and vibrate in fixed positions, preventing them from changing shape or volume easily.

Liquids

Possess a definite volume but no definite shape, meaning they take the shape of their container. Their particles are close but can slide past one another, allowing for flow.

Gases

Have neither a definite shape nor a definite volume, expanding to fill whatever container they are in. Their particles are widely spaced and move rapidly and randomly.

Key Concepts (2/2)

State Changes

Substances can transition between these states, a common phenomenon in everyday life (e.g., freezing, boiling, condensation).

Important Terms (1/2)

Matter

Anything that has mass and takes up space.

State of Matter

One of the distinct forms in which matter can exist, primarily characterized by how its particles are arranged and move.

Solid

A state of matter with a definite shape and a definite volume.

Liquid

A state of matter with a definite volume but no definite shape; it takes the shape of its container.

Gas

A state of matter with no definite shape and no definite volume; it spreads out to fill whatever container it is in.

Important Terms (2/2)

The amount of space that a substance or object occupies.

Shape

The external form or outline of an object.

Particles

The tiny, fundamental units (atoms or molecules) that make up all matter.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many main forms, or states, does matter come in?

Matter comes in three main forms or states.

Can the same substance exist in all three states of matter? Provide an example.

Yes, the same substance can exist in all three states. Water is a common example, existing as ice (solid), liquid water, and steam (gas).

What is a solid state of matter?

A solid is a state of matter with a definite shape and a definite volume.

Describe the shape and volume characteristics of a solid.

A solid has a definite shape (it keeps its shape no matter the container) and a definite volume.

How are particles arranged and how do they move in a solid?

In a solid, particles are packed tightly together and mostly vibrate in place.

What is a liquid state of matter?

A liquid is a state of matter with a definite volume but no definite shape. It takes the shape of its container.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe the shape and volume characteristics of a liquid.

A liquid has a definite volume but takes the shape of its container, meaning it has no definite shape.

How are particles arranged and how do they move in a liquid?

In a liquid, particles are close together but can slide past one another.

What is a gas state of matter?

A gas is a state of matter with no definite shape and no definite volume. It spreads out to fill whatever container it is in.

Describe the shape and volume characteristics of a gas.

A gas has no definite shape (it fills its container) and no definite volume (it spreads out to fill the entire container).

How are particles arranged and how do they move in a gas?

In a gas, particles are spread out and move very fast and randomly.

Compare the shape of a solid, liquid, and gas.

A solid has a definite shape; a liquid takes the shape of its container; a gas fills the entire container.
