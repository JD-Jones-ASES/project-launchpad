Important Terms (1/2)

Electromagnet

A temporary magnet created by wrapping a coil of wire around a piece of iron (or other ferromagnetic material) and running an electric current through the wire. Its magnetic field can be turned on ...

Electromagnetic Induction

The process by which an electric current is produced in a conductor when it is exposed to a changing magnetic field, typically by moving a magnet near a coil of wire or vice versa.

Generator

A machine that uses magnets and coils of wire to convert mechanical motion energy (e.g., from flowing water, steam, or wind) into electrical energy through electromagnetic induction.

Electric Motor

A device that uses electricity and magnets to create spinning or rotational motion, converting electrical energy into mechanical energy.

Turbine

A machine that uses the flow of a fluid (like water, steam, or air) to spin a rotor, often connected to a generator to produce electricity.

Important Terms (2/2)

Conductor

A material that allows electric current to flow through it easily (e.g., metals, water).

Electric Motors: The Reverse of a Generator

How a Motor Works

Electric current is sent through coils of wire within a magnetic field. The magnetic field exerts a force on the current-carrying wires, causing them to rotate and spin a central shaft.
