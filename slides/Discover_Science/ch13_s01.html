Important Terms

Atom

The smallest particle of matter that still has the properties of that kind of matter. Atoms are the fundamental building blocks of everything.

Matter

Anything that has mass and takes up space. All matter is composed of atoms.

Solid

A state of matter where atoms are packed closely together and vibrate in fixed positions, giving the substance a definite shape and volume.

Liquid

A state of matter where atoms are close together but can slide past one another, allowing the substance to take the shape of its container while maintaining a definite volume.

Gas

A state of matter where atoms are far apart and move freely and rapidly, allowing the substance to expand to fill any container and lacking a definite shape or volume.

Atoms Are Always Moving

Solids

In a solid, like your desk, atoms are packed very close together. They are held in relatively fixed positions, but they are constantly vibrating back and forth.

Liquids

In a liquid, like water, atoms are still close together, but they have enough energy to slide past one another. This allows liquids to flow and take the shape of their container.

Gases

In a gas, like the air around you, atoms are much farther apart and move very rapidly, zooming around freely and spreading out to fill any available space.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is an atom?

An atom is the smallest particle of matter that still has the properties of that kind of matter.

What is the fundamental role of atoms in the physical world?

Atoms are the basic building blocks of everything that exists.

Can atoms be seen with the naked eye or a standard classroom microscope?

No, atoms are incredibly small and cannot be seen with the naked eye, a magnifying glass, or a regular microscope.

How small are atoms, using the orange/Earth analogy?

If an orange were blown up to the size of Earth, the atoms inside it would be about the size of blueberries.

Provide an example that demonstrates the enormous number of atoms around us.

A single glass of water holds more atoms than there are glasses of water in all the Earth's oceans.

What common things are made of atoms?

Everything around you, including your desk, chair, books, air, and even your own body, is made of atoms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Are all atoms identical?

No, there are different kinds of atoms, each with its own special properties (e.g., atoms in iron differ from atoms in gold).

Approximately how many different kinds of atoms have scientists identified?

Scientists have found about 118 different kinds of atoms in the entire universe.

Are atoms stationary or constantly in motion?

In a liquid, atoms are able to slide past one another.
