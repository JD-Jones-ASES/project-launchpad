Key Concepts

Light Reflection for Seeing

We see most objects because light from a source (like the Sun or a lamp) bounces off them and enters our eyes. Without a light source, there is no reflection, and therefore no vision.

White Light is a Spectrum

White light, such as sunlight, is actually a mixture of all the colors of the rainbow.

Light as Energy

Light is a form of energy. When objects absorb light, they also absorb this energy, which can be converted into heat, making dark-colored objects feel warmer in the sun than light-colored ones.

Important Terms

Prism

A clear, triangular piece of glass or plastic that separates white light into a band of colors called a spectrum by bending different colors by different amounts.

Spectrum

The band of colors (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Indigo, Violet) that appears when white light is separated, for example, by a prism or water droplets.

Absorb

To take in light energy. When an object absorbs light, it soaks up that light energy instead of reflecting it, often converting it into heat.

Reflect

To bounce back light. The color an object appears to be is the color of light it reflects.

Refraction

The bending of light as it passes from one transparent medium (like air) into another (like water or glass) at an angle. This is the principle behind prisms and rainbows.

How We See Things

The Necessity of Light

We cannot see in total darkness because vision requires a light source.

The Process of Seeing (5 Steps)

Common Misconception

It is a common misconception that our eyes send out light to "scan" objects. This is incorrect; our eyes receive light that has reflected off objects.

White Light and the Rainbow

Composition of Sunlight

Sunlight, which appears white, is actually a mixture of many different colors of light.

Why Objects Have Color

Absorption vs. Reflection

An object's color is determined by which colors of light it absorbs and which it reflects when white light hits it.

Examples

Key Principle

The color you *see* is the color the object *reflects*. The colors you *don't see* are the colors the object *absorbs*.

Light Is a Form of Energy

Energy Transfer

Light carries energy. The Sun's light energy travels vast distances to Earth, warming our planet and enabling processes like photosynthesis in plants.

Absorption and Heat

When an object absorbs light, it absorbs light energy. This energy is often converted into heat.

Dark vs. Light Colors

Dark-colored objects absorb more light energy (and thus convert more to heat) than light-colored objects. This is why a black shirt feels warmer in the sun than a white shirt.

"Try This: Refraction and Rainbows" Activity

Purpose

To demonstrate how light bends (refracts) when passing through different materials and how water can create a spectrum (rainbow).

Part 1 (Refraction)

Shining a flashlight through a glass of water shows how light bends as it enters and exits the water, and may even reveal faint colors. A pencil in water appears bent due to refraction.

Part 2 (Make a Rainbow)

Spraying a fine mist of water into the air with the Sun behind you on a sunny day allows you to create and observe your own mini-rainbow as water droplets split the sunlight.
