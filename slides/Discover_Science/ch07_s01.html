Key Concepts (1/2)

Rocks are Earth's Outer Layer

Rocks form the entire outer surface of our planet, found everywhere from beneath cities to ocean floors and mountaintops.

Definition of a Rock

A rock is a solid, natural material made of one or more minerals. Although non-living, rocks are vital components of Earth.

Definition of a Mineral

A mineral is a natural, nonliving solid that possesses a specific chemical makeup and a characteristic crystal shape. Minerals are the basic "ingredients" of rocks.

Properties of Minerals

Every mineral has unique physical and chemical properties (e.g., hardness, luster, color, crystal shape), and over 5,000 different types exist, though only about 30 are common.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Examples of Minerals

Common minerals include Quartz, Feldspar, Mica, and Calcite, each with identifiable characteristics.

Composition of Rocks

When observed closely, rocks like granite can be seen to contain different colored specks, which are individual minerals (e.g., feldspar, quartz, mica).

Important Terms (1/2)

Rock

A solid, natural material made of one or more minerals; constitutes the entire outer layer of Earth.

Mineral

A natural, nonliving solid that has a specific chemical makeup and a crystal shape; the fundamental building blocks of rocks.

Quartz

A very common mineral, often clear like glass or found in various colors (pink, purple, smoky gray).

Feldspar

The most abundant mineral in Earth's crust, typically appearing white or pink.

Mica

A shiny, flat mineral that can be peeled apart into thin sheets.

Important Terms (2/2)

Calcite

A white or clear mineral found in limestone and marble, known for fizzing when exposed to vinegar.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a rock?

A solid, natural material made of one or more minerals.

Are rocks considered living organisms?

No, rocks are not alive; they do not grow, eat, or breathe.

What vital role do rocks play for Earth?

They make up the entire outer layer of Earth.

What common food item is used as an analogy to explain how minerals relate to a rock?

A chocolate chip cookie, where its ingredients (flour, sugar, butter, chocolate chips) are like minerals in a rock.

Define the term 'mineral.'

A natural, nonliving solid that has a specific chemical makeup and a crystal shape.

List three key characteristics that define a mineral.

Minerals are natural, nonliving solids, have a specific chemical makeup, and possess a crystal shape.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe some of the varying physical properties that different minerals can exhibit.

Minerals can be hard or soft, shiny or dull, and come in various colors.

Approximately how many different minerals have scientists discovered on Earth, and how many are commonly found?

Over 5,000 different minerals have been discovered, but only about 30 are common.

What are some common characteristics of Quartz?

Quartz is one of the most common minerals, and it can be clear like glass or found in colors such as pink, purple, or smoky gray.

Which mineral is the most abundant in Earth's crust, and what are its usual colors?

Feldspar is the most abundant mineral, and it is usually white or pink.

How can you easily identify Mica?

Mica is shiny and flat, and you can peel it apart in thin sheets, almost like pages of a tiny book.

Which mineral is common in limestone and marble, and what unique test can identify it?

Calcite is found in limestone and marble, and it fizzes when you put vinegar on it.
