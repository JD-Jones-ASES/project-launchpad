Important Terms (1/2)

Poles (of a magnet)

The two ends of a magnet where the magnetic force is strongest. Every magnet has a north pole and a south pole.

North pole (N)

One of the two distinct poles of a magnet, often labeled 'N'. It repels other north poles and attracts south poles.

South pole (S)

One of the two distinct poles of a magnet, often labeled 'S'. It repels other south poles and attracts north poles.

Magnetic field

The invisible area around a magnet where its force can attract or repel other magnets or magnetic materials.

Magnetic material

A material that is attracted to a magnet, such as iron, nickel, and cobalt.

Important Terms (2/2)

Compass

An instrument containing a small, freely rotating magnet (the needle) that aligns itself with Earth's magnetic field, used for navigation.

Attract and Repel — The Fundamental Rule

Opposite poles attract

If you bring a north pole close to a south pole, they will pull towards each other and snap together.

Like poles repel

If you bring two north poles together, or two south poles together, they will push away from each other.

What Is Magnetic?

Iron

Very common in nails, paper clips, and is the main component of steel.

Nickel

Found in some coins and certain types of jewelry.

Cobalt

Used in some tools, specialized alloys, and batteries.
