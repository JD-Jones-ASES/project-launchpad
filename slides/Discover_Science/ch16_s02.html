Chapter 16, Section 2: The Skeletal System

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Skeletal System Definition

The complete framework of bones inside your body that provides shape, support, and enables movement.

Bone Count and Development

Adults have 206 bones, while babies are born with around 270. Many smaller bones fuse together as a person grows.

Three Main Jobs of the Skeleton

Major Bones

Key bones include the skull, spine (vertebrae), ribs, pelvis, femur (longest and strongest), and tibia.

Joints

Places where two or more bones meet, allowing for various types of movement.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Bones as Living Tissue

Bones are not lifeless; they contain blood vessels, receive nutrients, constantly rebuild themselves, and bone marrow inside them produces blood cells.

Bone Health

Bones need calcium (found in dairy, leafy greens) to remain strong and healthy.

Smallest Bone

The stirrup bone (stapes) in the ear is the smallest, crucial for hearing.

Important Terms (1/3)

Skeletal System

The framework of bones inside your body.

Skeleton

The complete set of bones in your body that gives you shape and support.

Femur

The longest and strongest bone in the human body, located in the upper leg (thigh bone).

Stirrup bone (Stapes)

The smallest bone in the human body, located deep inside the ear, crucial for hearing.

Skull

The bony framework of the head, protecting the brain.

Important Terms (2/3)

Spine (Backbone)

A column of 33 small bones (vertebrae) that protects the spinal cord and allows bending and twisting.

Vertebrae

The individual small bones that make up the spine.

Ribs

Twelve pairs of curved bones that form a cage around the heart and lungs.

Pelvis

The large bone at the hips that supports the upper body and connects to the legs.

Tibia

The shinbone, a major bone in the lower leg.

Important Terms (3/3)

Joint

A place where two or more bones meet, allowing movement.

Hinge joint

A type of joint that allows movement in only one plane, like a door hinge (e.g., knee, elbow).

Ball-and-socket joint

A type of joint that allows for a wide range of motion in many directions, including rotation (e.g., shoulder, hip).

Bone marrow

A soft, spongy material found in the center of many bones that produces new blood cells for the body.

Calcium

An essential mineral that helps keep bones healthy and strong.

A Look at Your Major Bones (1/2)

Skull

Located in the head, it protects the brain and is made of several bones fused together.

Spine (Backbone)

A flexible column made of 33 small bones called vertebrae. It protects the spinal cord and allows the body to bend and twist.

Ribs

Twelve pairs of bones that curve around the chest, forming a protective cage for the heart and lungs.

Pelvis

The large bone located at the hips. It supports the upper body and forms the connection point for the legs.

Femur

The thigh bone, found in the upper leg. It is the longest and strongest bone in the body.

A Look at Your Major Bones (2/2)

Tibia

The shinbone, located in the lower leg.

Joints: Where Bones Meet

Joints

are the places where two or more bones come together.

Bones Are Alive!

Calcium

is a vital mineral for bone health. Foods rich in calcium include milk, cheese, yogurt, and leafy green vegetables.
