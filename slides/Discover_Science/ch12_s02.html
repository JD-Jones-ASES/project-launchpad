Chapter 12, Section 2: The Water Cycle

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Continuous Movement

The water cycle is a never-ending loop where water constantly moves and changes forms without a beginning or an end.

States of Water

Water transforms between liquid (water), gas (water vapor), and sometimes solid (ice) states throughout the cycle.

Four Main Stages

The cycle is typically broken down into four key stages: Evaporation, Condensation, Precipitation, and Collection.

Sun's Role

The Sun's energy is the primary driver of the water cycle, providing the heat needed for evaporation.

Conservation of Water

Water is neither created nor destroyed during the cycle; it only changes location and form, meaning the same water has been recycling for billions of years.

Important Terms (1/2)

Water Cycle

The continuous movement of water from Earth's surface to the atmosphere and back again.

Evaporation

The process where liquid water changes into water vapor (an invisible gas), primarily due to heat from the Sun.

Water Vapor

The gaseous form of water, which is invisible and rises into the atmosphere.

Condensation

The process where water vapor (a gas) cools and changes back into tiny liquid water droplets, often forming clouds.

Precipitation

Any form of water that falls from clouds to Earth's surface, including rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

Important Terms (2/2)

Collection

The process where precipitation gathers in oceans, lakes, rivers, or soaks into the ground to become groundwater.

Groundwater

Water that has seeped into the ground and is stored in underground aquifers.
