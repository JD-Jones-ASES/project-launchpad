Unit 1: What Makes Something Alive? - Characteristics of Life and Habitats

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Five Characteristics of Life

All living things grow and develop, reproduce, need energy, respond to their environment, and are made of cells. If something lacks even one of these, it is not considered alive.

Habitats Provide Essentials

A habitat is the natural home of a living thing, supplying four vital needs: food, water, shelter, and space. Different environments on Earth support unique types of life.

Observation as a Scientific Tool

Careful, patient observation using one's senses and recording findings (like in a field journal) is essential for understanding the natural world and identifying the characteristics of living things.

Important Terms

Living thing

Something that grows, reproduces, needs energy, responds to its environment, and is made of cells.

Reproduce

To make new living things of the same kind.

Nonliving thing

Something that was never alive.

Once-living thing

Something that was alive in the past but is no longer alive.

Habitat

The natural home or environment of a living thing, where it finds everything it needs to survive.

Living, Nonliving, and Once-Living

Living Things

These are objects that currently display all five characteristics of life. Examples include trees, birds, fish, insects, mushrooms, and humans.

Nonliving Things

These are objects that have never been alive and never will be. They do not grow, reproduce, need energy, respond, or contain cells. Examples include rocks, water, spoons, and clouds.

Where Living Things Live: Habitats

Forest

Characterized by tall trees, rich soil, and shade. Can be warm (tropical rainforests) or cold (northern evergreen forests). Home to deer, owls, squirrels, ferns, and many insects.

Desert

Very dry with extreme temperature shifts (hot days, cold nights). Organisms like cacti, lizards, and scorpions have adaptations to survive with minimal water.

Ocean

The largest habitat, covering most of Earth with salty water. Home to fish, whales, dolphins, jellyfish, coral, and seaweed.

Freshwater

Includes lakes, rivers, ponds, and streams, which contain unsalty water. Home to frogs, turtles, fish, and water lilies.

Grassland

Wide open areas covered primarily with grasses. Gets more rain than deserts but less than forests. Home to bison, prairie dogs, and hawks.

Observing Living Things

Be quiet and patient

Animals are often shy and will reveal more if you are still and quiet.

Use your senses

Notice what you see, hear, and smell (but never taste anything in nature without adult supervision).

Write it down

Keep a "field journal" to record observations, descriptions, and drawings. This helps organize thoughts and notice patterns.

Look closely

A magnifying glass can reveal tiny details on plants, insects, or bark.

Look everywhere

Living things can be found on the ground, in the air, on trees, under rocks – explore all areas.
