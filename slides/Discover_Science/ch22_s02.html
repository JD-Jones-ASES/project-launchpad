Key Concepts (1/2)

What do scientists call the distinct layers found in soil?

Soil horizons, or more simply, soil layers.

Name the three main soil layers from top to bottom.

Topsoil, subsoil, and bedrock.

Define topsoil.

The top layer of soil, dark in color and rich in humus and nutrients, where most plants grow their roots.

What gives topsoil its characteristic dark brown or black color?

The presence of a high amount of humus.

Why is topsoil considered the most important layer for life?

It is rich in nutrients, humus, and is where seeds sprout, roots spread, and earthworms tunnel, supporting most plant growth.

What is the typical thickness of topsoil?

Usually a few inches to about a foot thick.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Define subsoil.

The layer of soil below the topsoil, lighter in color, with more rock fragments and less organic matter.

Describe the typical appearance of subsoil.

It is lighter in color, often reddish, yellowish, or tan.

How does the organic matter content of subsoil compare to topsoil?

Subsoil contains fewer decomposed organisms and therefore less organic matter than topsoil.

What happens to water and dissolved minerals as they pass through topsoil and reach the subsoil?

Water filters down and collects in the subsoil, carrying dissolved minerals with it.

Define bedrock.

The solid layer of unbroken rock beneath all the soil.

Is bedrock considered soil? Explain.

No, bedrock is not soil; it is the original solid rock from which the soil above it formed through weathering.
