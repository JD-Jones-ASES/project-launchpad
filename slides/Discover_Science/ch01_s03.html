Key Concepts

Definition of Habitat

A habitat is the natural home or environment where a living thing resides, providing all the necessary resources for its survival and growth.

Diversity of Habitats

Earth hosts a wide variety of habitats, each with unique characteristics and supporting different types of life. Examples include forests, deserts, oceans, freshwater systems, and grasslands.

Suitability and Adaptation

Living things are specifically adapted to thrive in their particular habitats. An organism's survival depends on finding a habitat that perfectly matches its needs and adaptations.

Impact of Habitat Change

When a habitat undergoes significant changes (e.g., destruction, pollution), the living things that depend on it may face severe challenges, including displacement or extinction.

Important Terms (1/2)

Habitat

The natural home or environment of a living thing, providing everything it needs to survive, including food, water, shelter, and space.

Food

Resources that provide energy and nutrients for an organism to grow, repair, and carry out life functions.

Water

An essential liquid needed by all known forms of life for hydration and various biological processes.

Shelter

A place or structure that provides protection from weather, predators, and other environmental dangers.

Space

The physical area or volume an organism requires to live, grow, find resources, and reproduce comfortably.

Important Terms (2/2)

Forest

A large area covered chiefly with trees and undergrowth, characterized by shade, rich soil, and varying temperatures.

Desert

A barren or desolate area, especially one with very little rainfall, making it difficult for most plant and animal life to survive. Known for hot days and cold nights.

Ocean

A very large expanse of salty water that covers most of the Earth's surface, supporting a vast array of marine life. It is Earth's largest habitat.

Freshwater

Water that is not salty, found in lakes, rivers, ponds, and streams, supporting different aquatic life than oceans.

Grassland

A large open area of country covered with grass, especially one used for grazing. Receives moderate rainfall, more than deserts but less than forests.
