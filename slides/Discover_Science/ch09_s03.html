Understanding Volume (1/2)

What is Volume?

Volume is the amount of space an object takes up. It tells you how big an object is in terms of the room it occupies.

Volume vs. Mass

Volume is different from mass. A balloon full of air has a large volume (takes up a lot of space) but very little mass. A small marble has a small volume but can have more mass than you might expect.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is mass?

Mass is the amount of matter (stuff) in an object, measured in grams (g) or kilograms (kg).

Is an object's mass the same as its size?

No, mass is not the same as size. A big beach ball has very little mass, while a small rock can have a lot of mass.

What is a gram (g), and what is a common object with a mass of about 1 g?

A gram (g) is a very small amount of mass. A paperclip has a mass of about 1 g.

What is a kilogram (kg), and how many grams are in 1 kilogram?

A kilogram (kg) is 1000 grams. A liter bottle of water has a mass of about 1 kg.

How is the mass of an object typically measured using a balance?

Place the object on one side and add known weights to the other side until both sides are level. The total of the weights is the object's mass.

If you crumple a sheet of paper, how does its mass compare to the flat sheet of paper?

A crumpled sheet of paper and a flat sheet of paper have the same mass, because the amount of matter has not changed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is volume?

Volume is the amount of space that something takes up, measured in milliliters (mL) or liters (L).

What is a milliliter (mL), and what is a common example of this volume?

A milliliter (mL) is a tiny amount of liquid. One drop of water from a medicine dropper is about 1 mL.

What is a liter (L), and how many milliliters are in 1 liter?

A liter (L) is 1000 milliliters. A large water bottle holds about 1 L.

What is a graduated cylinder used for?

A graduated cylinder is a tall, narrow container with measurement lines printed on the side, used to measure the volume of a liquid.

What are the steps to accurately measure the volume of a liquid using a graduated cylinder?

Pour the liquid into the cylinder, set it on a flat surface, crouch down to eye level, and read the number at the bottom of the curved surface (meniscus).
