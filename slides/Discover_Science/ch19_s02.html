Important Terms (1/2)

Lever

A stiff bar that rests on a fulcrum and is used to lift or move things.

Fulcrum

The fixed point on which a lever turns or pivots.

Wheel and Axle

A simple machine made of a large wheel connected to a smaller rod (axle). Turning the wheel turns the axle, often with increased force.

Pulley

A wheel with a groove for a rope, used to lift loads, change the direction of a force, or reduce the force needed.

Inclined Plane

A flat, sloped surface (a ramp) that allows you to move objects up or down using less force over a longer distance.

Important Terms (2/2)

Wedge

A simple machine shaped like two inclined planes joined back to back, forming a sharp edge, used to split or push things apart.

Screw

An inclined plane wrapped in a spiral around a rod, used to hold things together or to lift. The spiral ridge is called the thread.

Thread

The spiral ridge that winds around the shaft of a screw or bolt.

The Lever

Definition

A lever is a rigid bar that pivots around a fixed point.

Fulcrum

The fixed point around which the lever rotates is called the fulcrum.

How it Works

Levers multiply force. The key principle is that the farther the applied force is from the fulcrum, the less force is required to move a load.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many types of simple machines do scientists group all simple machines into?

Six types.

What is the primary function of a simple machine?

Each simple machine changes force in its own special way (e.g., changes direction, reduces force needed).

Define a lever.

A stiff bar that turns on a fixed point.

What is the fixed point on which a lever turns called?

The fulcrum.

Explain the relationship between distance from the fulcrum and the force needed when using a lever.

The farther you are from the fulcrum, the less force you need to push down.

Name three common examples of levers.

Seesaw, crowbar, bottle opener, hammer (pulling nails), baseball bat, broom.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe the function of a wheel and axle.

A simple machine made of a large wheel connected to a smaller rod (axle). Turning the wheel turns the axle with more force.

How does the size of the wheel relative to the axle affect the force required?

The bigger the wheel compared to the axle, the less force you need to turn it.

Give two examples of a wheel and axle.

Doorknob, bicycle wheel, steering wheel, screwdriver handle, rolling pin.

What is a pulley?

A wheel with a groove around the outside for a rope, chain, or cable, used to lift loads or change the direction of a force.

How does a simple pulley typically make lifting a load feel easier?

It changes the direction of your force, allowing you to pull down (often using your body weight) instead of pushing up.

Why do construction cranes use multiple pulleys?

Multiple pulleys working together reduce the force needed to lift very heavy loads.
