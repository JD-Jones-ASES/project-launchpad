Cyclical Nature of Life Cycles

Life cycles are best understood as circles, not straight lines. Reproduction at the end of one cycle leads directly to the beginning of a new cycle, demonstrating that life is continuous.

Variation in Life Cycle Lengths

The time it takes for an organism to complete its life cycle varies greatly, from a single day for some insects to thousands of years for certain plants.

Plant-Specific Life Cycles

While following the universal pattern, plants have distinct stages: seed, sprout (germination), seedling, mature plant, flower, and finally fruit and seeds, which restarts the cycle.

Important Terms

Life Cycle

The complete series of stages a living thing goes through from birth, to growth, to reproduction, to death.

Germination

The process by which a seed begins to sprout and grow into a new plant.

Reproduction

The biological process by which new individual organisms (offspring) are produced from their parents.

Offspring

The young or descendants of a living organism.

Nutrients

Substances that an organism needs for growth, metabolism, and other bodily functions.
