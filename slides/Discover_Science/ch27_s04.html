Important Terms (1/2)

Conservation

Using natural resources wisely and carefully so they last as long as possible and cause as little harm as possible.

Reduce

To use less of something; the most effective conservation strategy by preventing resource extraction and waste creation.

Reuse

To use an item again, either for its original purpose or a new one, avoiding the need for a new product.

Recycle

To break down used materials and turn them into new products, saving raw materials and energy.

Endangered species

Important Terms (2/2)

Greenhouse effect

The natural process where certain gases in the Earth's atmosphere trap heat from the sun, warming the planet.

Impact

Protecting Habitats and Wildlife

Threats to Habitats

Deforestation, wetland drainage, and ocean pollution destroy the homes of countless organisms.

Endangered Species

When habitats are destroyed, species can become **endangered**, meaning their populations are so low that they face the risk of disappearing forever.

Ecological Impact

Protecting habitats safeguards entire ecosystems, including the intricate food chains and webs that sustain life.

What Kids Can Do for Conservation

Practical Actions

Activity: Trash Audit

Goal

To sort classroom or household trash into categories (Recyclable, Reusable, Compostable, True Trash) and quantify what could be conserved.

Process

Students wear gloves, sort trash, count items in each category, and calculate the fraction of waste that could have been diverted.

Key Discovery

Most trash audits reveal that more than half of discarded items could have been reduced, reused, or recycled, highlighting the potential for significant waste reduction.
