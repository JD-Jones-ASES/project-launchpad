Key Concepts

Defining Characteristics of Animals

Animals are unique among living things because they possess three key traits: they move on their own, they eat other organisms for energy, and they have senses to detect their surroundings.

Animals as Consumers

Unlike plants that produce their own food, animals must consume (eat) other living things to acquire the energy necessary for survival and growth.

Importance of Senses

Animal senses (sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch) are crucial for finding food, recognizing danger, and communicating with others.

Humans Are Animals

Based on these defining characteristics, humans are classified as animals, specifically belonging to the mammal group.

Animal Diversity and Classification

Scientists have identified millions of animal species, and they organize these diverse creatures into groups based on shared characteristics to better understand them.

A. Animals Move on Their Own

Definition

Unlike plants, which are typically rooted in one place, animals are capable of locomotion – moving from one place to another independently.

Examples

Birds fly, snakes slither, fish swim, and even simpler animals like sea sponges can move water through their bodies.

B. Animals Eat Other Organisms for Energy (Consumers)

Definition

Animals cannot produce their own food using sunlight (like plants do through photosynthesis). Instead, they must obtain energy by consuming other living things.

Food Sources

Animals' diets can include plants (herbivores), other animals (carnivores), or both (omnivores).

Scientific Term

Animals are called **consumers** because they consume (eat) food to get the energy they need to live, grow, and reproduce.

Animals Have Senses

Definition

Animals possess specialized organs and systems that allow them to detect and interpret information from their surrounding environment.

Types of Senses

These include common senses like sight, hearing, smell, taste, and touch, though the specific senses can vary greatly among different animal species.

Examples

Hawks use keen eyesight to spot prey from above; dogs have an exceptional sense of smell; even simple animals like earthworms can sense light and vibrations in their environment.

Humans belong to the group of animals known as mammals, alongside creatures like dogs, cats, and whales.

How Many Animals Are There?

Vast Diversity

Scientists have discovered and named approximately 1.5 million animal species to date.

Undiscovered Species

It is estimated that millions more animal species may exist but have not yet been discovered, particularly in hard-to-reach environments like the deep ocean and dense tropical rainforests.
