Chapter 11, Section 3

The Five Groups of Vertebrates

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Vertebrate Classification

Vertebrates are animals with backbones, sorted into five distinct groups: Fish, Amphibians, Reptiles, Birds, and Mammals.

Unique Group Characteristics

Each vertebrate group possesses specialized traits that define it, such as body covering, habitat, breathing apparatus, and reproductive method.

Fish: Aquatic Pioneers

The earliest vertebrates, adapted for life exclusively in water, using gills for oxygen and fins for movement, covered in scales, and are cold-blooded.

Amphibians: The "Double Life"

Begin life in water with gills and transition to land with lungs, requiring moist skin and environments, and are cold-blooded.

Reptiles: Terrestrial Adapters

Among the first vertebrates to live fully on land, characterized by dry, scaly skin, lungs for breathing air, and laying leathery eggs on land; they are cold-blooded.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Birds: Feathered Flyers (and more)

The only animals with feathers, possessing wings and beaks, laying hard-shelled eggs, and notably being warm-blooded.

Mammals: Hair, Milk, and Live Birth

Characterized by having hair or fur, feeding their young with milk, and mostly giving birth to live young; they are warm-blooded.

Important Terms (1/2)

Fish

A cold-blooded vertebrate that lives in water, breathes through gills, and has fins and scales.

Gills

Organs that allow fish to take in oxygen from water.

Fins

Appendages that help fish steer, balance, and swim.

Scales

Thin, overlapping plates that protect a fish's body.

Amphibian

A cold-blooded vertebrate that usually begins life in water with gills and later develops lungs to live on land.

Important Terms (2/2)

Reptile

A cold-blooded vertebrate with dry, scaly skin that breathes air with lungs and usually lays eggs on land.

Bird

A warm-blooded vertebrate that has feathers, wings, a beak, and lays hard-shelled eggs.

Mammal

A warm-blooded vertebrate that has hair or fur and feeds its young with milk.

Warm-blooded

Describes an animal whose body temperature stays about the same, no matter the temperature outside.

Cold-blooded

Describes an animal whose body temperature changes with the temperature of its surroundings.

Fish

Definition

Cold-blooded vertebrates that live exclusively in water, breathe using gills, and are equipped with fins and scales.

Evolutionary Significance

Fish were the first vertebrates to appear on Earth, millions of years ago, showcasing adaptations perfect for aquatic life.

Key Characteristics

Examples

Salmon, clownfish, catfish, great white shark.

Amphibians

Definition

Cold-blooded vertebrates known for their "double life," starting in water with gills and later developing lungs to live on land.

"Double Life" Concept

The term "amphibian" means "double life" in Greek, perfectly describing their transition from an aquatic larval stage (e.g., tadpole) to a terrestrial adult stage.

Key Characteristics

Examples

Frogs, toads, salamanders, newts.

Reptiles

Definition

Cold-blooded vertebrates characterized by dry, scaly skin, breathing air with lungs throughout their lives, and typically laying eggs on land.

Terrestrial Adaptation

Reptiles were pioneers in living their entire lives on land, thanks to adaptations like their dry, scaly skin, which prevents water loss.

Key Characteristics

Examples

Turtles, lizards, snakes, crocodiles, alligators.

Birds

Definition

Warm-blooded vertebrates uniquely distinguished by having feathers, wings, a beak, and laying hard-shelled eggs.

Feathers

Birds are the only animals with feathers, which serve multiple vital functions: insulation for warmth, aid in flight, and often provide vibrant coloration.

Key Characteristics

Examples

Robin, eagle, penguin, hummingbird, owl.

Mammals

Definition

Warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by having hair or fur and feeding their young with milk.

Familiarity

This group includes humans and many other commonly known animals.

Key Characteristics

Examples

Dogs, cats, elephants, bats, whales, humans.
