Key Concepts (1/2)

Ubiquity of Chemicals

Many common household items are simple chemical compounds with a variety of practical uses.

Salt (Sodium Chloride - NaCl)

An essential compound used for seasoning, food preservation (by drawing out water), melting ice (by lowering freezing point), and as a raw material for other chemicals.

Baking Soda (Sodium Bicarbonate - NaHCO₃)

A mild base (pH ~9) used in baking (produces CO₂ gas with acid to make dough rise), cleaning (mild abrasive), deodorizing (absorbs smells), and as an antacid.

Vinegar (Acetic Acid)

A mild acid (pH ~2-3, typically 5% acetic acid in water) used in cooking (tangy flavor), cleaning (dissolves minerals and grease), and food preservation (pickling).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Chlorine

A powerful element used as a disinfectant in small, controlled amounts. It kills germs in swimming pools, drinking water, and is a key component in household bleach (sodium hypochlorite).

Chemical Safety

Strong chemicals like bleach require careful handling, good ventilation, and should never be mixed with other cleaners to avoid dangerous reactions.

pH Indicators

Substances that change color depending on whether they are in an acidic, basic, or neutral solution. Red cabbage juice is a natural pH indicator.

Acids and Bases in Biology

The balance of acids and bases is important in the body, as seen with toothpaste (a mild base) neutralizing acids produced by mouth bacteria to protect tooth enamel.

Sodium Chloride (NaCl)

The chemical name for baking soda.

Acetic Acid

The active ingredient in vinegar, a mild acid.

Important Terms (2/3)

Sodium Hypochlorite

The active compound in household bleach, derived from chlorine.

pH Indicator

A substance that changes color in the presence of an acid or a base, indicating the pH level.

Enamel

The hard, outer protective coating of teeth, which can be dissolved by acids.

Fluoride

A chemical added to toothpaste that strengthens tooth enamel.

Neutralize

To cause an acid and a base to react, forming water and a salt, thereby reducing their acidic or basic properties.

Important Terms (3/3)

Abrasive

A substance capable of cleaning or polishing by rubbing or grinding.

Freezing Point

The temperature at which a liquid turns into a solid. Salt lowers the freezing point of water.

Antacid

A substance that neutralizes stomach acid, often used to relieve indigestion.

Science Life: Toothpaste: A Base That Protects Your Teeth

Problem

Bacteria in your mouth produce acids from sugary foods, which attack and dissolve tooth enamel, leading to cavities.

Solution

Toothpaste is a mild base (pH 8-9). When you brush, it **neutralizes** these harmful acids, protecting your teeth.

Additional Protection

Toothpaste also contains **fluoride**, a chemical that strengthens tooth enamel, making it more resistant to acid attack. Brushing regularly therefore neutralizes acids and fortifies your enamel.
