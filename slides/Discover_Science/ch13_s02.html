Atoms Join Together: Molecules - Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Atoms are Building Blocks

Everything around us is made of tiny particles called atoms, which are the fundamental units of matter.

Atoms Join to Form Molecules

Most atoms do not exist independently; they connect with other atoms to create larger structures called molecules.

Molecules Define Substances

A molecule is the smallest piece of a specific substance that retains all the characteristics and properties of that substance. For example, a single water molecule still *is* water.

Bonds are Connections

Atoms are held together within a molecule by strong connections known as bonds. These bonds are what "snap" atoms together.

Arrangement Matters

The same types of atoms can combine in different ways to form entirely different molecules with distinct properties, similar to how the same LEGO bricks can build various objects.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Properties Change When Atoms Bond

When atoms bond to form a molecule, the new molecule often has properties that are completely different from the individual atoms it's made of (e.g., hydrogen gas + oxygen gas = liquid water).

Important Terms

Atom

The smallest piece of an element that retains the chemical identity of that element; the fundamental building block of all matter.

Molecule

A particle made of two or more atoms joined together by chemical bonds. It is the smallest unit of a substance that still acts like that substance.

Bond

A strong connection or attractive force that holds two or more atoms together within a molecule.

Oxygen Gas (O₂)

The air you breathe contains oxygen gas, which is made up of two oxygen atoms bonded together.

Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

This gas, which we exhale and plants use, consists of one carbon (C) atom bonded to two oxygen (O) atoms.
