Chemical Changes

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of Chemical Change

A fundamental alteration in matter that results in the production of entirely new substances, each possessing distinct properties different from the original materials.

Atom Rearrangement

Irreversibility

Chemical changes are generally difficult or impossible to reverse, meaning the new substances cannot easily be turned back into the original ones.

Reactants and Products

The starting materials in a chemical change are called **reactants**, and the new substances formed are called **products**.

Distinguishing Physical vs. Chemical Change

Key differences include whether new substances form, if the change is easily reversible, and if atoms rearrange.

Important Terms

Chemical Change (Chemical Reaction)

A change in matter that produces one or more new substances with different properties.

Atoms

The basic building blocks of matter; they rearrange during a chemical change.

Molecules

Groups of two or more atoms held together by chemical bonds; new molecules are formed during a chemical change.

Reactants

The original substances present at the beginning of a chemical change.

Products

The new substances that are formed as a result of a chemical change.

Examples of Chemical Changes

Burning Wood

When wood burns, it combines with oxygen from the air. This reaction produces ash, smoke, carbon dioxide gas (CO2), and water vapor (H2O). None of these products can be transformed back into wood.

Physical Change vs. Chemical Change

Feature. Physical Change

Chemical Change

What happens?. The substance remains the same; only its size, shape, or state changes.

One or more *new* substances form with different properties.

Can you reverse it?. Usually yes (e.g., melting ice, dissolving salt).

Usually no (e.g., burning wood, cooking an egg).

Do atoms rearrange?. No. Atoms stay bonded in the same way.

Yes. Atoms break old bonds and form new bonds.

Examples. Cutting, melting, dissolving, folding, breaking.

Burning, rusting, cooking, baking.
