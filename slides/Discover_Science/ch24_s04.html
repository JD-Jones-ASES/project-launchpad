While fast compared to everyday objects, sound travels much slower than light, with its speed dependent on the medium it passes through.

Important Terms (1/2)

Light Speed

The speed at which light waves travel, approximately 300,000 kilometers per second in a vacuum. It is the fastest speed in the universe.

Sound Speed

The speed at which sound waves travel, which varies depending on the medium. In air, it's about 340 meters per second.

Medium (for sound)

The material through which sound waves travel (e.g., air, water, steel). The density and elasticity of the medium affect sound's speed.

Lightning

A giant electrical spark in the sky that simultaneously produces light and sound.

Thunder

The sound produced by lightning, caused by the rapid expansion of air heated by the electrical discharge.

Important Terms (2/2)

Echo

A sound that is reflected off a surface and heard again after a short delay.

Scientific Method

A systematic approach to inquiry that involves observation, forming hypotheses, conducting experiments to test hypotheses, and drawing conclusions; famously advocated by Ibn al-Haytham.

Optics

The branch of physics that studies the behavior and properties of light, including its interactions with matter and the construction of instruments that detect or use light.

Famous Scientist: Ibn al-Haytham (Alhazen)

Pioneer of Optics

Born around 965 in Iraq, Ibn al-Haytham is often celebrated as "the father of optics" due to his groundbreaking work on light and vision.

Challenging Old Ideas

Before his time, many believed that our eyes emitted invisible beams to see objects. Ibn al-Haytham disproved this through rigorous experimentation.

Correct Theory of Vision

He demonstrated that we see because light from objects enters our eyes after reflecting off them – a fundamental principle we now take for granted.

"Book of Optics"

His most famous work detailed how light reflects off mirrors, how lenses bend light, and the mechanics of the human eye.
