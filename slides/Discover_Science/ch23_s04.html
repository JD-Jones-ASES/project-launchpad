Important Terms

Toxic/Poisonous

A substance that can cause serious harm, illness, or death if swallowed, breathed in, or absorbed through the skin.

Corrosive

A substance capable of eating through or destroying materials, including skin and clothing, through a chemical reaction. Strong acids and bases are often corrosive.

Chemical Safety Symbols

Flame Symbol

Indicates the product is highly flammable and catches fire easily.

Skull and Crossbones

Signifies the product is toxic (poisonous).

Exclamation Mark

Warns that the product may cause irritation to the skin, eyes, or lungs.

Corrosion Symbol

Indicates the product is corrosive, meaning it can burn skin, eyes, or other tissues, and can eat through materials.

Environment Symbol

Shows that the product can be harmful to aquatic life, plants, or other living organisms if it contaminates natural environments.

Why Reading Labels Matters (1/2)

Product Identification and Use

What the product is and its intended purpose.

Safe Usage Directions

How to use the product correctly and safely.

Warning Symbols and Signal Words

Visual symbols and words like "Caution," "Warning," or "Danger" indicate the level of hazard. "Danger" usually signifies the highest risk.

First Aid Instructions

What steps to take if the product comes into contact with skin or eyes, or is swallowed.

Ingredients

A list of the chemicals contained within the product.

Why Reading Labels Matters (2/2)

Storage Information

Guidelines on how to store the product safely (e.g., "keep in a cool place," "keep away from children").

Putting It All Together (Unit Recap) (1/2)

Ubiquity of Chemicals

Everything, including water, air, and our bodies, is made of chemicals.

Acids and Bases

Acids have a pH below 7 (e.g., lemons, vinegar), and bases have a pH above 7 (e.g., soap).

pH Scale

Measures acidity/basicity from 0 to 14, with 7 being neutral.

Common Household Chemicals

Many common chemicals like salt, baking soda, vinegar, water, and chlorine have various important uses.

Neutralization

A reaction between an acid and a base that results in a substance closer to neutral pH.
