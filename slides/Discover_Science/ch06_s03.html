What Do Plants Need? Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Environmental Dependence

Plants cannot move to find resources; they rely on their immediate environment for everything they need to live and grow.

Sunlight as Energy

Sunlight provides the energy for photosynthesis, the process by which plants make their own food (sugar). Plants exhibit *phototropism*, growing towards light.

Water's Dual Role

Water is a key ingredient in photosynthesis and is also essential for maintaining plant cell structure, keeping plants firm and upright.

Air and Gas Exchange

Plants take in carbon dioxide (CO2) from the air for photosynthesis and release oxygen (O2), which is vital for animal life.

Nutrients from Soil (or Water)

Soil provides essential mineral *nutrients* like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, which are like vitamins, supporting healthy plant development. Plants do *not* "eat" soil.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Hydroponics

Plants can grow without traditional soil using hydroponics, a method where they receive dissolved minerals in water, demonstrating that the critical needs can be met through alternative means.

Important Terms (1/3)

Sunlight

Energy from the Sun that plants use to make food.

Phototropism

The growth response of a plant toward a light source, meaning "turning toward light."

Water

An essential liquid absorbed by roots, used in photosynthesis, and crucial for maintaining plant cell structure.

Carbon Dioxide (CO2)

A gas from the air that plants take in through their stomata to use in photosynthesis.

Oxygen (O2)

A gas released by plants during photosynthesis, essential for respiration in animals and humans.

Important Terms (2/3)

Stomata

Tiny pores, primarily on the underside of leaves, through which plants exchange gases with the atmosphere.

Nutrients

Substances that a living thing needs to grow and stay healthy; for plants, these are often minerals dissolved in soil water.

Nitrogen

A key mineral nutrient essential for leaf growth and overall plant vigor.

Phosphorus

A key mineral nutrient important for root development, flowering, and fruiting.

Potassium

A key mineral nutrient that supports overall plant health, water regulation, and disease resistance.

Essential for Life

Soil and Nutrients

More Than Just Dirt

Soil is a complex mixture, not just "dirt." It contains broken-down rock particles, water, air, and decaying organic matter (bits of dead plants and animals).

Nutrients (Minerals)

Dissolved in the water within the soil are essential *nutrients* (minerals). These are substances that plants need in small amounts to grow and stay healthy.

Key Minerals

Important mineral nutrients include nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium.

Can Plants Grow Without Soil?

Hydroponics

Yes! This method, called *hydroponics*, involves growing plants in water that has dissolved minerals added to it.

Meeting All Needs

Hydroponic systems still provide all four essential requirements: water (with dissolved nutrients), air (often aerated into the water), light, and the necessary minerals.

Practical Applications

Hydroponics is used by farmers globally to grow crops like lettuce, tomatoes, and herbs in controlled environments like greenhouses, especially in areas where soil quality is poor or space is limited.
