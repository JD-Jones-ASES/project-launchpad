Sound Originates from Vibrations

Every sound, from a whisper to thunder, begins with an object vibrating (moving back and forth rapidly).

Important Terms (1/2)

Vibration

A quick back-and-forth movement. This is the fundamental cause of all sound.

Sound

A form of energy caused by vibrations that travel through a material (a medium) such as air, water, or a solid.

Sound wave

A vibration that moves through air, water, or another material, carrying sound energy from one place to another.

Medium

The material (solid, liquid, or gas) through which sound waves travel. Sound cannot travel without a medium.

Pitch

How high or low a sound is. Pitch depends on how fast an object vibrates (the frequency of vibration).

Important Terms (2/2)

Volume

How loud or soft a sound is. Volume depends on how much an object vibrates (the amplitude or size of the vibration).

The Necessity of a Medium

Water

also acts as a medium. Whales, for instance, communicate over vast distances underwater.

Solids

are also excellent mediums for sound. If you place your ear against a table, you can clearly hear a tap from the other end because the sound travels efficiently through the wood.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Explain how sound waves are ultimately perceived by your brain.

When sound waves reach your ear, tiny parts inside your ear vibrate. Your ear then sends a signal to your brain, which interprets what you are hearing.

Can sound travel through empty space? Why or why not?

No, sound cannot travel through empty space. It needs a material, called a medium, for its vibrations to move through.

What is a 'medium' in the context of sound waves?

A medium is the material (solid, liquid, or gas) through which sound waves travel. Sound cannot travel without a medium.
