States of Matter

Matter commonly exists in three primary states, each with distinct characteristics:

Property

A property is an observable or measurable characteristic of matter. For example, color, hardness, density, boiling point, and flammability are all properties.

Physics (Unit 4)

Force

A force is simply a push or a pull. Forces are what cause objects to start moving, stop moving, speed up, slow down, or change direction.

Gravity

A ubiquitous force, gravity is the natural phenomenon by which all things with mass are brought toward one another. On Earth, it's the force that pulls objects down towards the planet's center.

Motion

When an object changes its position over a period of time, it is said to be in motion.

Speed

Speed is a measure of how fast an object is moving. It's calculated by dividing the distance an object travels by the amount of time it takes to travel that distance (Speed = Distance / Time).

Friction

Friction is a force that arises when two surfaces rub against each other. It always acts in the opposite direction of motion, tending to slow down or prevent movement.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the five key characteristics of a living thing?

A living thing grows, reproduces, needs energy, responds to its environment, and is made of cells.

How do you distinguish between a nonliving thing and a once-living thing?

A nonliving thing was never alive, while a once-living thing was alive in the past but is no longer.

What does it mean for a living thing to "reproduce"?

To make new living things of the same kind.

Define "habitat."

The natural home of a living thing.

What is the Earth's rocky outer layer called?

The lithosphere.

Which of Earth's spheres includes all the water on Earth?

The hydrosphere.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe the atmosphere.

The layer of air around Earth.

What is a continent, and how many are there?

A very large area of land; there are seven continents.

What is an ocean, and how many are typically identified?

A huge body of salt water; there are five oceans.

Define "matter."

Anything that has mass and takes up space.

What is mass, and in what units is it typically measured?

Mass is the amount of matter in an object, measured in grams.

What is volume, and what units are used to measure it?

Volume is the amount of space something takes up, measured in milliliters (mL) or liters (L).
