Important Terms (1/2)

Ecosystem

A community of living organisms (biotic components) interacting with their non-living environment (abiotic components).

Habitat

The natural home or environment of an animal, plant, or other organism, providing food, water, shelter, and space.

Niche

The specific role an organism plays within its ecosystem, including its feeding habits, interactions, and where it lives.

Invasive Species

A plant or animal that is introduced to a new ecosystem where it does not naturally belong and often causes harm to native organisms, often by outcompeting them or disrupting the food web.

Pesticide

A chemical substance used to kill pests, such as insects, weeds, or fungi.

Important Terms (2/2)

Food Chain

A sequence showing how energy is transferred from one living organism to another by feeding.

Overuse of Resources

Harvesting natural resources faster than they can regenerate (e.g., overfishing, deforestation) depletes ecosystems and can lead to the collapse of populations or entire resource systems.

Protecting Ecosystems

Protecting Habitats

Establishing national parks, wildlife refuges, and nature reserves safeguards critical ecosystems from development, allowing species to thrive.

Reducing Pollution

Individual and collective actions like reducing plastic use, recycling, proper chemical disposal, and supporting cleaner industries help prevent harmful substances from entering ecosystems.

Removing Invasive Species

Scientists and volunteers actively work to identify and remove invasive plants and animals to restore native ecological balance.

Planting Native Species

Choosing to grow plants indigenous to a specific area provides essential food and shelter for local wildlife, supporting native biodiversity.

Learning and Sharing

Educating ourselves and others about ecological principles fosters greater environmental awareness and encourages responsible decision-making.
