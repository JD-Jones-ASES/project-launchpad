Important Terms (1/2)

Genetics

The scientific study of heredity, which is how traits are passed from parents to their offspring.

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

The complex molecule that contains the genetic instructions for the development, functioning, growth, and reproduction of all known organisms.

Plate Tectonics

The scientific theory explaining that the Earth's outer layer (the lithosphere) is divided into several large, rigid plates that are constantly moving.

Atmosphere

The blanket of gases that surrounds Earth, vital for weather and climate.

Periodic Table

A tabular display of the chemical elements, organized by atomic number, electron configuration, and recurring chemical properties.

Important Terms (2/2)

Atomic Structure

The composition of an atom, including its central nucleus (containing protons and neutrons) and orbiting electrons.

Chemical Bonds

The attractive forces that hold atoms together in molecules and compounds.

Newton's Laws of Motion

Three fundamental laws of classical mechanics that describe the relationship between a body and the forces acting upon it, and its motion in response to those forces.

Waves

Disturbances that transfer energy through a medium or through space without transferring matter. Examples include sound waves, light waves, and water waves.

Nuclear Energy

The energy released from the nucleus of an atom, either through fission (splitting) or fusion (combining).

Earth's Interior and Volcanoes

Physics: Going Deeper

Newton's Laws of Motion

You will study Sir Isaac Newton's three fundamental **laws of motion** in greater mathematical detail, understanding how they describe why and how objects move.

Waves

You will explore different types of **waves** – not just sound and light, but also other forms – and learn about how they carry energy without carrying matter.

Electricity

Your understanding of electricity will expand to include more complex circuits, such as **series and parallel circuits**, and how they work.

Nuclear Energy

You will also be introduced to **nuclear energy**, learning about the incredible amount of energy stored inside the nucleus of an atom.
