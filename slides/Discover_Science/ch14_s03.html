Key Concepts

Energy Transformation

The process by which energy changes from one form (e.g., electrical, chemical, potential) into one or more other forms (e.g., light, heat, kinetic, sound). This happens constantly in our daily lives.

Law of Conservation of Energy

The formal scientific law that encapsulates the idea that the total amount of energy in an isolated system remains constant; it can only change from one form to another.

Important Terms

Energy transformation

When energy changes from one form to another.

Energy chain

A diagram or sequence showing multiple energy transformations occurring in a series, from an energy source to final observable forms.

Conservation of energy

The scientific rule that energy cannot be created or destroyed—it can only change from one form to another.

Law of Conservation of Energy

The fundamental scientific law stating that energy can neither be created nor destroyed, but can only be changed from one form to another.

Applying the Law (Bicycle Example)

At the top of the hill

The bicycle (and rider) possesses **potential energy** due to its height.

Riding downhill

As it descends, this potential energy is transformed into **kinetic energy**, causing the bicycle to speed up. Some energy is also lost as heat due to air resistance and friction in the moving parts.
