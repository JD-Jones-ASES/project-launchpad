Dissolving vs. Melting

These are two distinct processes:

Important Terms (1/2)

Solution

A special kind of mixture in which one substance dissolves evenly into another, making the particles too small to see.

Dissolve

To break apart and spread evenly throughout a liquid so that the pieces are too small to see.

Solute

The substance that dissolves in a solution.

Solvent

The substance that does the dissolving in a solution.

Universal Solvent

A term often used to describe water due to its ability to dissolve more substances than any other common liquid.

Important Terms (2/2)

Melting

The process by which a solid changes into a liquid when it absorbs enough heat.

Not Everything Dissolves

Sand in Water

If you try stirring sand into water, no matter how long you stir, the sand will simply settle to the bottom. Sand does not dissolve in water; it forms a suspension, not a solution.

Oil in Water

Similarly, oil does not dissolve in water. When mixed, oil will separate and float on top of the water, forming distinct layers. This is an example of two immiscible (non-mixing) liquids.
