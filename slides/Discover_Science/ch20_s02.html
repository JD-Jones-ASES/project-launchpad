Important Terms (1/8)

Body system

A group of organs that work together to do a specific job for the body.

Cell

The smallest living unit of the body; the basic building block of all living things.

Tissue

A group of similar cells working together.

Organ

A body part made of different tissues that does a specific job (e.g., the heart, the stomach).

Skeletal system

The system of 206 bones that supports, protects, and helps move the body.

Important Terms (2/8)

Joint

A place where two bones meet, allowing movement.

Muscular system

The system of more than 600 muscles that move the body.

Voluntary muscle

A muscle you can control on purpose (e.g., arm muscles).

Involuntary muscle

A muscle that works automatically without your thinking about it (e.g., the heart).

Digestive system

The system that breaks down food into nutrients the body can use.

Important Terms (3/8)

Nutrition

Eating the right foods to keep your body healthy and strong.

Weather

The condition of the atmosphere at a particular time and place — temperature, wind, clouds, and precipitation.

Temperature

A measure of how hot or cold something is, measured in degrees Celsius (°C).

Wind

Moving air, caused by uneven heating of Earth's surface.

Precipitation

Water that falls from clouds as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

Important Terms (4/8)

Anemometer

An instrument that measures wind speed.

Rain gauge

An instrument that measures how much rain has fallen.

Meteorologist

A scientist who studies and forecasts weather.

Cumulus

Puffy, white clouds that usually mean fair weather.

Stratus

Flat, gray clouds that form in layers and may bring drizzle.

Important Terms (5/8)

Cirrus

Thin, wispy clouds made of ice crystals high in the sky.

Climate

The average weather pattern of a place over many years.

Climate zone

A large area of Earth with a similar climate pattern (tropical, temperate, or polar).

Physical change

A change in which the substance stays the same, even though it may look different (e.g., cutting, melting, dissolving).

Chemical change

A change in which one or more new substances are formed (e.g., burning, rusting, cooking).

Important Terms (6/8)

Chemical reaction

Another name for a chemical change — the process in which atoms rearrange to form new molecules.

Reactants

The substances you start with before a chemical change.

Products

The new substances formed after a chemical change.

Clues of chemical change

Signs that a chemical change may have occurred: color change, gas bubbles, heat or light, new smell, hard to reverse.

Conservation of matter

The idea that matter is never created or destroyed in a chemical reaction — the atoms just rearrange.

Important Terms (7/8)

Work

Using a force to move an object over a distance (Work = Force × Distance).

Simple machine

A device with few or no moving parts that makes work easier by changing the amount or direction of force.

Lever

A bar that turns on a fixed point (the fulcrum) to lift or move a load.

Fulcrum

The fixed point on which a lever turns.

Wheel and axle

A wheel attached to a rod (axle); turning the wheel turns the axle with greater force.

Important Terms (8/8)

Pulley

A wheel with a rope or chain that changes the direction of a force.

Inclined plane

A flat surface set at an angle to the ground; a ramp.

Wedge

Two inclined planes joined together to form a sharp edge, used to split or cut.

Screw

An inclined plane wrapped around a cylinder, used to hold things together or lift.

Compound machine

A machine made of two or more simple machines working together (e.g., scissors, bicycle).
