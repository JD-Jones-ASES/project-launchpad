Light: Straight Lines, Reflection, and Refraction

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Light as Energy and Vision

Light is a form of energy that travels, allowing our eyes to perceive the world around us. Without light, we would see nothing.

Straight-Line Propagation (Rays)

Light travels in perfectly straight paths, which scientists call rays. The formation of shadows provides direct evidence of light's straight-line travel, as light cannot bend around opaque objects.

Reflection

This is the process where light bounces off a surface.

Refraction

This is the bending of light as it passes from one material into another (e.g., from air to water).

How Mirrors Work

Mirrors are constructed with a very smooth glass front protecting a thin, shiny metal layer (like aluminum or silver), which is the actual reflective surface.

Important Terms

Light

A form of energy that travels in straight lines and makes it possible for us to see things.

Ray

A narrow beam of light that travels in a straight line.

Reflection

The bouncing of light off a surface. Smooth, shiny surfaces reflect light in an orderly way, creating clear images.

Refraction

The bending of light as it passes from one material into another, such as from air into water or glass.

Light Travels in Straight Lines

Principle

One of the most important properties of light is that it travels in straight paths.

Rays

Scientists use the term "ray" to describe a narrow, straight beam of light.

How Mirrors Work (ScienceLife)

Construction

A typical mirror consists of a very smooth piece of glass coated on one side with a thin layer of highly reflective metal, usually aluminum or silver.

Function of Layers

Applications

Mirrors can be very large, such as the eight-meter-wide mirrors found in powerful telescopes, which are used to collect light from distant celestial objects.

Mnemonic for Reflection vs. Refraction (Teacher Note)

Reflection

Think of the 'L' in "reflection" and "looking" – you **look** in a mirror.

Refraction

Think of the 'A' in "refraction" and "angle change" – light bends at a new **angle**.
