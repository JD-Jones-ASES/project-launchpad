Review: Atoms and Elements

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Atoms as the Fundamental Building Blocks

Atoms are incredibly tiny particles that constitute all matter—solids, liquids, and gases. Everything we see, touch, and smell is made of atoms.

Elements as Pure Substances

An element is a substance that contains only one specific kind of atom. There are approximately 118 known elements, each unique.

Chemical Symbols for Elements

Each element has a unique one- or two-letter abbreviation called a chemical symbol. These symbols are universally recognized by scientists, facilitating global communication.

The Periodic Table as an Organizational Tool

All known elements are systematically arranged on a chart called the periodic table. This table provides essential information about each element, including its name, symbol, and atomic number.

Important Terms

Atom

The smallest particle of matter that still retains the chemical identity of an element. Atoms are too small to be seen with a regular microscope.

Matter

Anything that has mass and takes up space. All matter is made of atoms.

Element

A pure substance made of only one kind of atom. Examples include gold, oxygen, and carbon.

Chemical Symbol

A standardized one- or two-letter abbreviation for an element, used universally by scientists. The first letter is always capitalized, and the second (if present) is lowercase.

Periodic Table

A comprehensive chart that organizes all known elements based on their properties, displaying their name, symbol, and atomic number in individual squares.

The Periodic Table

What it Is

The periodic table is a highly organized chart that displays all 118 known elements. It's not just a random list; elements are arranged in a specific way that reflects their properties.

Analogy

Think of the periodic table as a "giant address book for atoms." Just like an address book helps you find people, the periodic table helps scientists find information about elements.

What is the smallest particle of matter?

An atom.

Describe the size of an atom.

Atoms are so tiny that you cannot see them, even with a powerful microscope; millions could fit on the tip of a sharpened pencil.

What does everything you can see, touch, and smell, including solids, liquids, and gases, consist of?

Atoms.

Approximately how many different kinds of atoms are there?

About 118.

Define an element.

An element is a pure substance made of only one kind of atom.

Give three examples of elements mentioned in the text.

Gold, oxygen, carbon, iron, hydrogen, sodium, or chlorine (any three).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the rules for writing the chemical symbol for an element?

The first letter is always a capital letter. If there is a second letter, it is always lowercase.

Why do scientists around the world use the same chemical symbols for elements?

So that chemists globally can read and understand each other's work.

What is the chemical symbol for Hydrogen, and where is it commonly found?

H; In water; it is the most common element in the universe.

What is the chemical symbol for Oxygen, and where is it commonly found?

O; In the air you breathe.

What is the chemical symbol for Carbon, and where is it commonly found?

C; In pencil lead, diamonds, and all living things.

What is the chemical symbol for Iron, and where is it commonly found?

Fe; In nails, bridges, and your blood.
