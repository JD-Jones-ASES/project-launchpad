You Are a Scientist: Discovering the Scientist Within

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Science as an Innate Human Trait

The act of asking "Why?", observing closely, and making predictions are natural, everyday scientific behaviors, not exclusive to professional scientists.

Everyday Scientific Method

The process of noticing something interesting, forming a question, making a prediction, and testing it is the scientific method in action, accessible to everyone.

Diversity of Scientific Inquiry

Science as a "Way of Seeing"

Beyond a school subject, science is a perspective that encourages observation, questioning, and understanding the natural phenomena of the world around us.

Empowerment for Lifelong Learning

The section aims to empower readers to embrace their inner scientist, utilizing tools, vocabulary, and thinking skills to continue asking questions and seeking answers throughout their lives.

Important Terms (1/2)

Scientist

According to this section, anyone who asks "Why?", notices interesting things, looks closely, makes predictions, and tests them; not limited to those with specific degrees or lab settings.

Scientific Method

A systematic approach to understanding the world, involving observation, asking questions, forming hypotheses (predictions), testing them through experiments or further observation, and drawing con...

Curiosity

A strong desire to know or learn something, identified as the starting point for many scientific discoveries.

Observation

The act of carefully watching or noticing something, a fundamental step in scientific inquiry.

Prediction

A forecast or statement about what will happen in the future, often based on existing knowledge or hypotheses, which can then be tested.

Important Terms (2/2)

Experiment

A scientific procedure undertaken to make a discovery, test a hypothesis, or demonstrate a known fact, though it can be as simple as testing an everyday prediction.

The Scientist Within All of Us

Asking "Why?"

This fundamental question drives scientific inquiry.

Noticing and Observing

Paying attention to details and looking more closely at interesting phenomena.

Predicting and Testing

Forming an idea about what might happen and then seeing if it holds true, which is the scientific method in its simplest form.

Inspiration from Diverse Scientists (1/5)

Jane Goodall

Started with **curiosity**, patiently observing chimpanzees for years.

Marie Tharp

Started with **determination**, mapping the ocean floor despite skepticism.

Marie Curie

Started with **hard work**, discovering new elements against significant obstacles.

Isaac Newton

Started with **wonder**, asking a profound question about a falling apple that transformed physics.

George Washington Carver

Started with **creativity**, developing hundreds of uses for peanuts.

Inspiration from Diverse Scientists (2/5)

Florence Bascom

Started with **courage**, becoming the first woman to earn a Ph.D. in geology in the U.S.

Alice Ball

Started with **brilliance**, developing a treatment for leprosy at a young age.

Anders Celsius

Started with **precision**, creating the temperature scale we use today.

Charles Henry Turner

Started with **patience**, demonstrating learning and memory in insects.

Dmitri Mendeleev

Started with **patterns**, organizing elements into the periodic table and predicting undiscovered ones.

Inspiration from Diverse Scientists (3/5)

James Joule

Started with **careful measurement**, proving energy conservation.

Daniel Hale Williams

Started with **bravery**, performing one of the first successful heart surgeries.

Tetsuya "Ted" Fujita

Started with **dedication**, studying tornadoes to save lives.

Antoine Lavoisier

Started with **weighing things**, discovering matter conservation.

Archimedes

Started with **thinking**, figuring out the principle of the lever.

Inspiration from Diverse Scientists (4/5)

Rachel Carson

Started with **love for nature**, warning the world about environmental pesticides.

Wangari Maathai

Started with **one seedling**, planting millions of trees to protect communities and soil.

Percy Julian

Started with **chemistry**, turning soybeans into life-saving medicines.

Ibn al-Haytham

Started with **experiments**, proving how vision works.

Mary Anning

Started with a **hammer and curiosity**, digging fossils as a child.

Inspiration from Diverse Scientists (5/5)

Eunice Newton Foote

Started with a **simple experiment**, showing CO2 traps heat long before others.

Linus Pauling

Started with **questions about shapes**, explaining atomic bonding.

Michael Faraday

Started as a **bookbinder's apprentice who loved to read**, discovering how to make electricity from magnetism.

Science as a Way of Seeing

Observing natural phenomena

Like watching a sunset and wondering why the sky changes color.

Questioning everyday occurrences

Such as asking why ice cream melts faster on a hot day.

Feeling awe and wonder

Looking at the stars and being amazed by the universe's vastness.
