Chapter 26, Section 2: Different Life Cycles Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Universal Life Cycles

All living organisms undergo a life cycle, but the specific stages and forms differ significantly between species.

Mammal Life Cycle (Gradual Growth)

Characterized by direct birth, maternal milk feeding, steady growth to adult size, and eventual reproduction, with young resembling miniature adults. Humans are an example of mammals.

Metamorphosis

A dramatic biological process where an animal undergoes a distinct and often rapid change in its body structure, habitat, and behavior as it develops from a young form to an adult.

Frog Metamorphosis

A classic example of metamorphosis, involving a transition from an aquatic, gill-breathing tadpole to a semi-aquatic, lung-breathing adult frog.

Butterfly Complete Metamorphosis

A four-stage life cycle (egg, larva, pupa, adult) where the animal's body form completely transforms, often involving a non-feeding pupal stage.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Plant Life Cycles

Flowering plants follow a cycle starting from a seed, through germination and seedling growth, maturation, flowering, pollination, and finally producing fruit and new seeds.

Widespread Metamorphosis

Many insects, including beetles, flies, ants, bees, ladybugs, and dragonflies, also undergo various forms of metamorphosis.

Important Terms (1/3)

Mammals

A class of animals characterized by having fur or hair, being warm-blooded, and feeding their young milk. Most mammals give birth to live young.

Life Cycle

The series of changes an organism undergoes from the beginning of its life until its reproduction and subsequent death.

Metamorphosis

A major biological process involving a distinct change in body form that some animals (like frogs and insects) go through as they develop from a young, immature form to an adult.

Tadpole

The aquatic larval stage of an amphibian, such as a frog, characterized by a tail, gills for breathing, and lacking legs.

Froglet

A young frog that has developed all four legs and begun to breathe air with lungs, but still possesses a short stub of a tail.

Important Terms (2/3)

Complete Metamorphosis

A type of metamorphosis that includes four distinct stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult, with significant differences in form between each stage.

Larva

The immature form of an insect or other invertebrate, often worm-like and distinct from the adult form (e.g., a caterpillar is a butterfly larva).

Caterpillar

The larval stage of a butterfly or moth, primarily focused on eating and growing.

Pupa

The non-feeding, often immobile stage in complete metamorphosis, during which the larval body transforms into the adult body inside a protective casing.

Chrysalis

The specific hard, protective casing that forms around a butterfly pupa.

Important Terms (3/3)

Germination

The process by which a seed sprouts and begins to grow into a plant.

Seedling

A young plant that has recently emerged from a germinated seed and is developing its first true leaves.

Photosynthesis

The process used by green plants and some other organisms to convert light energy into chemical energy, creating food from carbon dioxide and water.
