CHAPTER 1, SECTION 2: LIVING, NONLIVING, AND ONCE-LIVING

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Three Categories of Existence

Everything in the world can be categorized as either living, nonliving, or once-living based on its relationship to the characteristics of life.

Living Things

These are organisms that currently exhibit all five characteristics of life. This means they grow, reproduce, need energy, respond to their environment, and are made of cells, *right now*.

Nonliving Things

These are objects or substances that have never exhibited the characteristics of life and never will. They do not grow, reproduce, need energy, respond, or have cells at any point.

Once-Living Things

This category includes objects or materials that were part of a living organism in the past but are no longer alive. They once possessed all the characteristics of life but do so no longer.

The Key Question for Classification

To differentiate between nonliving and once-living, ask: "Was it *ever* alive?" If yes, it's once-living. If no, it's nonliving.

Important Terms

Living Thing

An organism that exhibits all five characteristics of life *at the present moment*.

Nonliving Thing

An object or substance that has *never* been alive and does not exhibit any characteristics of life.

Once-Living Thing

An object or material that was alive in the past (part of a living organism) but is no longer alive.

Living Things (1/2)

Trees

They grow, reproduce, make their own food (energy), respond to sunlight, and are made of cells.

Birds

They fly, eat (energy), reproduce, react to dangers, and are multicellular.

Fish

They swim, consume food, lay eggs, react to water conditions, and are made of cells.

Insects

They move, feed, reproduce, respond to threats, and are composed of cells.

Mushrooms

They grow, reproduce by spores, obtain energy from decomposing matter, respond to moisture, and are multicellular.

Living Things (2/2)

You

As a human, you grow, reproduce, eat, respond to your surroundings, and are made of trillions of cells.

Nonliving Things

A rock

It's an inert mineral compound, never grown, reproduced, or had cells.

A glass of water

Essential for life, but water itself is a chemical compound, not an organism.

A spoon

An inanimate object made of metal, never alive.

A cloud

A collection of water droplets or ice crystals, a meteorological phenomenon, not a living entity.

Once-Living Things

A fallen leaf

While part of a living tree, it was alive. Once it falls and dries up, it ceases to perform life functions, but its origin is from a living plant.

A seashell

This structure was created by a living sea creature (like a snail or clam) to house itself. When the creature dies, the shell remains, a testament to its once-living maker.

A wooden table

Wood is harvested from trees. The tree was alive, and the wood, even after being processed, retains its "once-living" status because of its origin.

A feather

A feather is shed by a living bird. It was part of the bird's living structure.

Fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas)

These energy sources formed over millions of years from the remains of ancient organisms (plants and animals) that were once alive.

Sorting Challenge Application (1/2)

Butterfly

This creature clearly exhibits all characteristics of life (flies, eats, reproduces, responds, made of cells). It is **Living**.

Water

A fundamental chemical compound, never alive. It is **Nonliving**.

Piece of bread

Bread is made from wheat, a plant that was once alive. It is **Once-Living**.

Flower in a pot

Actively growing, needs water and sunlight, reproduces, responds, made of cells. It is **Living**.

Rock

An inorganic material, never alive. It is **Nonliving**.

Sorting Challenge Application (2/2)

Leather shoe

Leather comes from the hide of an animal (e.g., a cow), which was once alive. It is **Once-Living**.

Puppy

A young animal that grows, eats, plays (responds), will reproduce, and is made of cells. It is **Living**.
