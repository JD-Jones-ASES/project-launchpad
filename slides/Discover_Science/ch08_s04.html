Important Terms (1/2)

Concentration

The amount of solute dissolved in a given amount of solvent.

Solute

The substance that is dissolved in a solvent to form a solution (e.g., sugar in water, orange powder in water).

Solvent

The substance, usually a liquid, that dissolves the solute to form a solution (e.g., water in orange juice).

Solution

A homogeneous mixture formed when a solute dissolves completely in a solvent.

Dilute

Describes a solution with a low concentration of solute.

Important Terms (2/2)

Concentrated

Describes a solution with a high concentration of solute.

Magnetism

A property of certain materials (like iron) that allows them to be attracted by a magnet; used as a separation method.

Filtering

A separation method that uses a filter (like a coffee filter) to trap solid particles while allowing liquid to pass through.

Evaporation

The process by which a liquid changes into a gas or vapor, often used to separate a dissolved solid from a liquid.

Hansen's disease (Leprosy)

A chronic infectious disease that affects the skin, nerves, and respiratory tract, for which chaulmoogra oil was a primary treatment.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is concentration in chemistry?

Concentration is how much solute is dissolved in a certain amount of solvent.

Differentiate between a concentrated solution and a dilute solution.

A concentrated solution has a lot of solute dissolved in the solvent, while a dilute solution has only a little solute dissolved.

Describe a dilute solution using the orange juice example.

A dilute orange juice solution is made by mixing one spoonful of orange powder (solute) into a cup of water (solvent), resulting in a pale color and mild taste.

How can you visually observe a difference in concentration?

You can observe concentration by adding varying amounts of food coloring to water; more drops lead to a darker color, indicating higher concentration.

How can you feel a difference in concentration?

You can feel concentration, for example, with bubble soap in a bath; more soap (higher concentration) creates thicker, foamy bubbles.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do cooks apply the concept of concentration in the kitchen?

Cooks adjust concentration to achieve desired tastes, such as adding a teaspoon of salt for mild flavor versus a tablespoon for a much saltier taste.

What is the approximate salt concentration of ocean water and why is it significant?

Ocean water has a salt concentration of about 35 grams per liter, which is why it tastes so salty and cannot be consumed.

What four ingredients make up the 'Super Mixture' in the activity?

The Super Mixture consists of sand, salt, iron filings, and water.

What three separation methods are used in the 'Super Mixture Challenge' activity?

The three separation methods used are magnetism, filtering, and evaporation.

Which separation method is used first in the Super Mixture Challenge, and what does it separate?

Magnetism is used first to separate the iron filings from the mixture.

Explain why magnetism is an effective method for separating iron filings from the Super Mixture.

Iron is magnetic, while sand, salt, and water are not, allowing the magnet to attract and remove only the iron filings.
