Staying Healthy: Fueling, Moving, and Resting Your Body

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Holistic Health

Your body is an incredible system that relies on a combination of good nutrition, regular exercise, and adequate sleep to stay healthy and strong.

The Power of Exercise

Moving your body regularly strengthens not just your muscles, but also your bones, heart, and even helps your digestive system work more efficiently.

Rest and Repair (Sleep)

Sleep is not just downtime; it's a vital period when your body grows, repairs itself, your brain processes information, and your immune system gets stronger.

Taking Charge of Your Health

You have the power to make daily choices that positively impact your body's performance and long-term well-being.

Important Terms

Nutrition

The process of eating and using food to keep your body healthy and strong. It's how your body gets the fuel and materials it needs.

Balanced diet

Eating a variety of foods from all the main food groups (fruits, vegetables, grains, protein, dairy) so your body gets all the nutrients it needs to thrive.

Exercise

Moving your body in ways that make your heart beat faster and your muscles work harder. It helps strengthen your entire body.

Sleep

A natural period of rest during which the body grows, repairs itself, the brain organizes new information, and the immune system recharges.

Nutrition: Fuel for Your Body

Fruits and Vegetables

Grains (e.g., bread, rice, pasta, oatmeal)

Protein (e.g., meat, fish, beans, eggs, nuts)

Dairy (e.g., milk, cheese, yogurt)

Water

Exercise: Keeping Your Systems Strong

Bones

Activities like running, jumping, and playing (where your feet hit the ground) make your bones stronger and denser.

Muscles

Regular use makes your muscles grow bigger and stronger, increasing your endurance.

Heart

Your heart is a muscle, and exercise strengthens it, allowing it to pump blood more efficiently with less effort.

Digestive System

Being active helps food move smoothly through your intestines, improving digestion.

Sleep: Your Body's Repair Time

Growth

Your bones grow during sleep, which is why growing children need more sleep than adults.

Repair

Muscles repair tiny tears that occur during normal daily activities.

Brain Function

Your brain organizes everything you learned during the day, moving important information into long-term memory.

Immune System

Your body's defense system against germs gets stronger when you get enough sleep.
