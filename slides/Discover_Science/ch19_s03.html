Key Concepts (1/2)

Ubiquity of Simple Machines

Simple machines are not just theoretical concepts but are found everywhere in everyday objects, from kitchen tools to playground equipment and even parts of the human body.

Simple Machines at Home

Common household items like knives, jar lids, faucet handles, brooms, ramps, light switches, window blinds, drawer handles, and screws are all examples of simple machines.

Simple Machines on the Playground

Playgrounds are rich environments for observing simple machines, including seesaws (levers), slides (inclined planes), flagpole ropes (pulleys), merry-go-rounds (wheel and axles), and bolts (screws).

Key Concepts (1/2)

Where can simple machines be found in everyday life?

Simple machines are found everywhere, including homes, schools, playgrounds, and even inside the human body.

What type of simple machine is a kitchen knife, and how does it work?

A kitchen knife is a wedge; it pushes food apart to cut it.

How does a jar lid utilize a simple machine?

A jar lid is a screw; you twist it along spiral threads to open or close.

Identify the simple machine found in a faucet handle.

A faucet handle is a wheel and axle; turning the handle opens the valve inside.

What simple machine does a broom represent when you sweep?

A broom acts as a lever, with your hand near the middle serving as the fulcrum.

What simple machine is a ramp leading into a garage?

A ramp is an inclined plane.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What type of simple machine are light switches?

Light switches are levers.

What simple machine is commonly used in window blinds?

Window blinds use pulleys.

How do drawer handles function as simple machines?

Drawer handles work like wheel-and-axle systems.

What simple machine is essential for holding furniture together?

Screws hold furniture together.

Name the simple machine found in a seesaw on a playground.

A seesaw is a lever.

What simple machine is a playground slide?

A playground slide is an inclined plane.
