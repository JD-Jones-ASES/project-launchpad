What Makes Something Alive?

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of a Living Thing

A living thing is any entity that exhibits all five fundamental characteristics: it grows, reproduces, needs energy, responds to its environment, and is made of cells.

The Five Characteristics of Life

These are the universal traits present in every organism, from the smallest bacterium to the largest whale. If something lacks even one of these traits, it is not considered alive.

Growth and Development

All living things increase in size (grow) and undergo changes over their lifespan (develop).

Reproduction

Living things create new individuals of their own kind, ensuring the continuation of their species.

Need for Energy

Every living organism requires energy to fuel its life processes, such as growing, moving, and maintaining its body.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Response to Environment

Living things react to stimuli and changes in their surroundings, allowing them to adapt and survive.

Cellular Composition

All living things are composed of one or more cells, which are the basic structural and functional units of life.

Important Terms (1/2)

Living Thing

An organism or entity that displays all five characteristics of life: growth and development, reproduction, energy utilization, response to environment, and cellular composition.

Reproduce

The biological process by which living organisms create new individuals of the same species.

Energy

The capacity to do work; living things acquire and use energy to power all their biological processes.

Environment

The surroundings or conditions in which a person, animal, or plant lives or operates; living things constantly interact with and respond to their environment.

Cell

The basic structural, functional, and biological unit of all known organisms. Cells are often microscopic and are the fundamental building blocks of life.

Important Terms (2/2)

Develop

To change or grow over time, often becoming more complex or mature.
