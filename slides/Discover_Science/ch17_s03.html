Clouds Tell a Story: Your Guide to Weather Prediction

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Cloud Formation

Clouds are formed when warm, moist air rises, cools, and its water vapor condenses into billions of tiny water droplets or ice crystals that float in the air.

Three Main Cloud Types

Clouds are primarily classified by their shape and height:

Cloud Altitude

Each main cloud type forms at a distinct range of heights in the atmosphere: Stratus are low, Cumulus are low to middle, and Cirrus are high.

Clouds as Weather Indicators

The appearance and type of clouds are natural signs that provide clues about current weather and potential future weather changes, allowing for basic weather prediction.

Important Terms (1/2)

Condenses

The process by which water vapor (an invisible gas) changes into liquid water droplets or ice crystals, forming clouds. This happens when air cools.

Cumulus cloud

A big, white, puffy cloud with a flat bottom that typically appears in fair weather. They look like cotton balls.

Cumulonimbus cloud

A very tall, towering storm cloud that develops from cumulus clouds and brings thunderstorms, heavy rain, and sometimes hail.

Stratus cloud

A flat, gray cloud that forms in layers and often covers the whole sky like a blanket, associated with gloomy weather, light rain, or drizzle.

Fog

A stratus cloud that forms at ground level, reducing visibility.

Important Terms (2/2)

Cirrus cloud

A thin, wispy cloud made of ice crystals that forms very high in the sky, often looking like feathery white streaks.

Altitude

The height of an object or point in relation to sea level or ground level. In meteorology, it refers to how high a cloud forms in the atmosphere.

Cumulus Clouds

Appearance

These are the classic "puffy" clouds, resembling giant cotton balls or scoops of ice cream. They are typically white with distinct, flat bases.

Formation

Cumulus clouds commonly form on warm, sunny afternoons when the sun heats the ground, causing warm air to rise in distinct parcels.

Weather Indication

Stratus Clouds

Appearance

Stratus clouds are flat, gray, and spread out in layers across the sky, often covering it completely like a dull blanket. They lack distinct features.

Formation

They form when a large, stable mass of air cools slowly and uniformly over a wide area, rather than rapidly rising in columns.

Weather Indication

Fog

A unique manifestation of a stratus cloud is fog, which forms when a stratus cloud is located directly at ground level. Walking through fog means you are literally immersed in a cloud.

Cirrus Clouds

Appearance

Cirrus clouds are the highest and appear as thin, wispy, feathery streaks across the sky, often described as looking like painted brush strokes.
