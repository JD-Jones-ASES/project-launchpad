The amount of three-dimensional space an object occupies.

Important Terms (1/3)

Mass

The amount of matter in an object.

Gram (g)

The standard unit for measuring mass.

Balance

A scientific tool used to measure the mass of an object by comparing it to known weights.

Volume

The amount of space something takes up.

Milliliter (mL)

A common unit for measuring liquid volume, equal to one-thousandth of a liter.

Important Terms (2/3)

Liter (L)

A standard unit for measuring volume, often used for larger quantities of liquids.

Graduated Cylinder

A tall, cylindrical laboratory tool with markings (graduations) used for precisely measuring the volume of liquids.

Meniscus

The curve formed at the surface of a liquid in a container. For water and many other liquids, the lowest point of this curve is used for accurate volume readings.

Water Displacement

A method used to determine the volume of an irregularly shaped solid by measuring the volume of water it displaces when submerged.

Polonium

One of the new elements discovered by Marie Curie.

Important Terms (3/3)

Radium

The other new element discovered by Marie Curie, known for its radioactivity.

Pitchblende

A mineral from which Marie Curie isolated polonium and radium through careful measurement and study.

Measuring Mass

Unit of Measurement

Mass is measured in **grams (g)**. Larger masses are often measured in kilograms (kg), where 1 kg = 1000 g.

Tool

A **balance** is used to measure mass. It works by comparing the object's mass to known standard weights until both sides are level, indicating equal mass.

Measuring Volume

Units of Measurement

Volume is measured in **milliliters (mL)** or **liters (L)**. (1 L = 1000 mL).

Tool

A **graduated cylinder** is the primary tool for measuring volume, especially for liquids.
