Connection to Atoms and Molecules

Building on prior knowledge (Unit 13), chemicals are fundamentally made of specific atoms joined together to form molecules.

Connection to Chemical Changes

Many everyday uses of chemicals involve chemical changes (reactions) that create new substances (Unit 18), such as baking soda reacting with vinegar or soap breaking down grease.

Important Terms

Chemical

A substance that has a definite composition—a specific set of atoms arranged in a specific way. Examples include water (H₂O), salt (NaCl), and oxygen (O₂).

Understanding What a Chemical Is

Challenging Misconceptions

Many people mistakenly associate the word "chemical" with something harmful, artificial, or unnatural. This section directly addresses and corrects this common misunderstanding.

The True Definition

A chemical is simply any substance that has a definite, specific makeup or composition. This means it consists of a particular arrangement of atoms.
