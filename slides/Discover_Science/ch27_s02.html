Nonrenewable Resources Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Nonrenewable Resources

These are natural resources that form over millions of years and cannot be replenished once used up at the rate humans consume them.

Fossil Fuels as Primary Nonrenewable Energy

Coal, oil (petroleum), and natural gas are the most significant nonrenewable resources, formed from ancient organic matter buried under heat and pressure for millions of years. They are crucial for...

Formation of Fossil Fuels

Coal originated from ancient swamp plants, while oil and natural gas came from tiny marine organisms, all transformed over vast geological timescales.

Minerals and Metals

Many valuable metals (like iron, copper, aluminum, gold) are extracted from Earth's crust. They are nonrenewable because their formation through geological processes also takes millions of years.

Recycling Metals

Unlike fossil fuels, metals can be melted down and reused, offering a way to conserve resources and reduce the need for new mining.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Rapid Depletion

Humanity is consuming nonrenewable resources, especially fossil fuels, far faster than Earth can naturally create them, leading to concerns about scarcity within decades.

Importance of Alternatives

Addressing resource depletion requires shifting towards renewable energy sources (solar, wind, water) and practicing conservation, waste reduction, and recycling.

Important Terms (1/2)

Nonrenewable Resource

A natural resource that takes millions of years to form, meaning it cannot be replaced once used up at the current rate of consumption.

Fossil Fuel

A fuel (coal, oil, or natural gas) that formed from the remains of ancient plants and animals buried deep underground for millions of years.

Coal

A hard, black rock formed from ancient swamp plants that is mined from the ground and burned for heat and electricity.

Oil (Petroleum)

A thick, dark liquid formed mainly from tiny sea organisms, found trapped in rock layers, used to produce gasoline, diesel, and plastics.

Natural Gas

A clean-burning gaseous fossil fuel formed from ancient sea organisms, used for cooking, heating, and generating electricity.

Important Terms (2/2)

Minerals

Naturally occurring inorganic solids found in Earth's crust, often containing useful elements or metals.

Metals

Elements typically found in minerals (e.g., iron, copper, aluminum, gold) that are strong, conductive, and malleable, used in construction, electronics, and various products.

Recycling

The process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects, which is particularly effective for conserving nonrenewable metals.

Natural gas also formed from ancient sea organisms, similar to oil. It is lighter than oil and typically rises to sit above it in underground rock layers.

List some common uses for coal.

Generating electricity (as a main fuel) and providing heat for homes and industry.

What are some key products and uses derived from oil (petroleum)?

Gasoline, diesel fuel, plastics, and various other chemical products.

What are the primary uses of natural gas?
