When to Use a Tally Chart

Real-Time Counting

When you are collecting data as it happens (e.g., counting types of birds you see in your backyard, recording answers to a quick survey on the spot).

Small Number of Categories

They work best when you have a limited number of distinct groups or choices (typically 10 or fewer).

Quick, Rough Counts

When you need a fast way to get a general idea of frequencies, rather than highly precise calculations or complex statistical analysis.
