The Ice Cream and Drowning Mystery

Observation

Researchers observed a strong positive correlation between ice cream sales and drowning incidents; both peaked in July and were low in January.

Initial Question

Does eating ice cream cause people to drown? The correlation might suggest a link.

The Explanation

No, ice cream does not cause drowning. The underlying factor is a **lurking variable**: hot weather.

Historical Example: John Snow and Cholera

The Problem

In 1854, a cholera epidemic plagued London, with most scientists believing it spread through "bad air" (miasma theory).

Snow's Approach

Dr. John Snow mapped every cholera death as a dot on a street map. This created a geographic scatter plot, showing a clear cluster of deaths around a specific water pump on Broad Street.

The Discovery

Snow didn't just find a correlation (deaths clustered around the pump). He investigated further and identified the **mechanism**: contaminated water from that specific pump.
