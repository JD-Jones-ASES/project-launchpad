Correlation and Trend Lines

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Correlation

Describes whether two variables on a scatter plot tend to move together. It indicates the direction and strength of the relationship.

Positive Correlation

Occurs when both variables tend to increase together. On a scatter plot, the dots generally slope upward from left to right.

Negative Correlation

Occurs when one variable tends to increase as the other decreases. On a scatter plot, the dots generally slope downward from left to right.

No Correlation

Occurs when there is no apparent relationship or pattern between the two variables; the dots appear randomly scattered.

Strong Correlation

Indicated when the data points cluster tightly around a clear diagonal direction, making the trend easy to see.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Weak Correlation

Indicated when the data points are more spread out, making the trend visible but less distinct amidst the scatter.

Trend Line (Line of Best Fit)

A straight line drawn through the center of a scatter plot's data pattern. Its purpose is to summarize the overall direction and strength of the relationship, allowing for estimations.

Estimation

Using a trend line to predict a typical y-value for a given x-value. It's important to remember these are estimates, not guarantees, as individual data points vary.

Important Terms (1/2)

Correlation

A statistical measure that describes the extent to which two variables move in relation to each other.

Positive Correlation

A relationship between two variables in which both variables move in the same direction (e.g., as one increases, the other also increases).

Negative Correlation

A relationship between two variables in which they move in opposite directions (e.g., as one increases, the other decreases).

No Correlation

A situation where there is no discernible linear relationship or pattern between two variables.

Strong Correlation

A relationship where data points on a scatter plot are tightly clustered, indicating a clear and consistent trend.

Important Terms (2/2)

Weak Correlation

A relationship where data points on a scatter plot are more scattered, indicating a less clear or consistent trend, even if a general direction is present.

Trend Line (Line of Best Fit)

A straight line drawn through the middle of the data points on a scatter plot to visually represent the general direction of the relationship between two variables.

Important Note: The Trend Line Is Not Perfect

Individual Variation

Real data points will always scatter above and below the trend line. A student who studies 6 hours might score 80 (below the line), and one who studies 2 hours might score 88 (above the line).
