Too Many Slices

When a pie chart contains a large number of categories, especially if they are similar in size, it becomes cluttered and nearly impossible to read or compare effectively.

Importance of 2D Charts

Flat, two-dimensional pie charts are essential for accurate representation, as they avoid the visual distortions inherent in 3D effects.

Key Concepts (2/2)

When to Use Bar Charts

For datasets with many categories or categories with very similar values, horizontal bar charts are a superior alternative to pie charts, offering clearer comparisons and rankings.

Visual Perception vs. Labels

Readers often form impressions from the visual size of slices before or instead of reading percentage labels. Misleading visual cues can therefore override correct numerical information.

Trick #1: The 3D Pie Chart

What it is

Adding a visual three-dimensional effect by tilting the circular chart and giving it depth (like a coin on its side).

Why it's misleading

The Fix

Always use flat, two-dimensional pie charts. Avoid 3D effects entirely to ensure accurate visual representation.

Trick #2: Exploded Slices

What it is

Separating one or more segments from the main body of the pie chart.

How it can be misleading

When a slice is exploded, it visually stands out, which can make it appear more important, larger, or more significant than its actual proportion in the data.

Legitimate Use

An exploded slice can be used legitimately to draw attention to a *specific* category that is the focus of discussion, as long as it doesn't visually exaggerate its size or importance beyond the da...

The Fix

Use exploded slices sparingly and only when the intent is to highlight, not to distort. Ensure the visual emphasis doesn't imply a larger proportion than is true.

Trick #3: Slices That Don't Add to 100%

What it is

A pie chart where the sum of all slice percentages is either more or less than exactly 100%.

Why it's misleading

The Fix

Always verify that the percentages for all slices in a pie chart sum to precisely 100%. If they don't, the chart is flawed and should not be used as a part-to-whole visualization.

What it is
