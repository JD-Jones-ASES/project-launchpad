Horizontal Bar Charts

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of Horizontal Bar Chart

A bar chart where bars extend horizontally from left to right. Categories are listed on the vertical (y-axis), while values are shown on the horizontal (x-axis).

When to Use Horizontal Bars

These charts are preferred in three main scenarios:

Axis Role Reversal

Reading Bar Lengths

To find a bar's value, trace vertically down from the right end of the bar to the value axis at the bottom.

Ranking Charts

A specific type of horizontal bar chart where categories are ordered from longest to shortest (or shortest to longest), making it easy to identify rankings (e.g., first, second, third).

Important Terms

Category Axis (y-axis)

In a horizontal bar chart, this is the vertical axis on the left side, which lists the names or labels of the categories being compared.

Value Axis (x-axis)

In a horizontal bar chart, this is the horizontal axis along the bottom (or sometimes top), which displays the numerical range or values that the bars represent.

Ranked Bar Chart

A horizontal bar chart where the categories are intentionally ordered (e.g., from longest bar to shortest bar, or vice-versa) to highlight a clear ranking among the items.

Understanding the Axes

• The y-axis (the vertical axis on the left side) displays the categories. These are the descriptive labels like "Nile River" or "Amazon River."

• The x-axis (the horizontal axis along the bottom) displays the values. This axis always has numbers on it, representing the quantity, length, or other numerical measure for each category.

Name one situation where a horizontal bar chart is preferable to a vertical bar chart.

When categories have long names (e.g., 'Mississippi River'). Horizontal charts provide more room for labels on the left side, preventing squeezing and tilting.

Name a second situation where a horizontal bar chart is preferable to a vertical bar chart.

When there are many categories (e.g., 8, 10, or more bars). Horizontal charts spread out more naturally on a page, reducing crowding.

Name a third situation where a horizontal bar chart is preferable to a vertical bar chart.

When displaying ranking data (from largest to smallest or vice-versa). Horizontal charts make ranking easy to see, as eyes naturally read down the list.

What is the first step in reading any bar chart, including a horizontal one?

Read the title to understand what is being compared.

Key Concepts (2/2)

On which axis do you find the numerical values in a horizontal bar chart?

The value axis runs along the bottom (x-axis), and it has numbers on it.

Where are the categories listed in a horizontal bar chart?

The category axis runs along the left side (y-axis), with each category label sitting next to its bar.

How do you determine the specific value represented by a horizontal bar?

Trace vertically down from the right end of the bar straight to the value axis on the bottom. The number below the bar tip is its value.

How can you quickly compare two different bars in a horizontal bar chart?

The longest bar has the highest value, and the shortest bar has the lowest value. You can also trace their ends to the value axis to find their exact numeric...

What is a common misconception students have when reading a horizontal bar chart regarding the axes?

Students may forget that categories go on the y-axis (left side) and values go on the x-axis (bottom), mistakenly looking for values on the y-axis.

How can students avoid incorrectly reading a horizontal bar's value from its right end?

Practice tracing vertically down from the bar tip to the value axis on the bottom. Remind them to 'find the axis with numbers on it'.
