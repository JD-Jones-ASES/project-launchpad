Important Terms (1/2)

Bubble Chart

An extension of a scatter plot that displays three numerical variables, with two encoded by the x- and y-axes and the third by the size (area) of each plotted circle (bubble).

Scatter Plot

A graph that displays the relationship between two numerical variables, where each data point is plotted as a single dot based on its x and y values.

Bubble Size

In a bubble chart, the visual representation of the third numerical variable, which should be proportional to the data value by area.

X-axis

The horizontal axis on a chart, typically representing the independent variable.

Y-axis

The vertical axis on a chart, typically representing the dependent variable.

Important Terms (2/2)

Correlation

A statistical measure that expresses the extent to which two variables are linearly related (meaning they change together at a constant rate).

Outlier

A data point that differs significantly from other observations, potentially indicating variability in a measurement or an experimental error.

GDP per capita

Gross Domestic Product per person; a measure of a country's average economic output and income.

Life Expectancy

The average period that a person may expect to live.

Population

The total number of people inhabiting a country, region, or area.
