Misleading Bar Charts

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Misleading Bar Charts

Bar charts that distort visual perceptions of data, making differences appear larger or smaller than they actually are.

Truncated Y-Axis

A common technique where the value axis (y-axis) does not start at zero, visually exaggerating even small differences between bars.

Inconsistent Bar Widths

When bars in the same chart have different widths, causing the eye to perceive wider bars as representing a greater magnitude or importance, regardless of their height.

Cherry-Picked Categories

The practice of selectively presenting only data categories that support a particular viewpoint while omitting others that might tell a different or contradictory story.

Critical Data Literacy

The ability to analyze and evaluate data visualizations with skepticism, identifying potential misrepresentations to form accurate conclusions.

Important Terms

Truncated Axis (or Truncated Y-Axis)

A value axis in a chart that does not begin at zero. This technique visually amplifies minor differences between data points by focusing on a narrower range of values.
