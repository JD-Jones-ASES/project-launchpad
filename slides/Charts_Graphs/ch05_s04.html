Key Concepts

Data with a Natural Midpoint

Diverging bar charts are best suited for data that has a clear central point, such as "Neutral" in a survey, a zero value for financial changes, or a dividing line between two opposing categories.

What Makes a Bar Chart "Diverging"?

Opinion Data

Agree vs. Disagree, Positive vs. Negative sentiment.

Quantitative Data

Above Zero vs. Below Zero, Increase vs. Decrease.

When to Use Diverging Bar Charts

Survey Results

Especially those using Likert scales (e.g., "Strongly Agree," "Agree," "Neutral," "Disagree," "Strongly Disagree").

Performance Metrics

Showing positive vs. negative changes over time or against a target.

Comparative Analysis

Visualizing two opposing aspects of a single subject.
