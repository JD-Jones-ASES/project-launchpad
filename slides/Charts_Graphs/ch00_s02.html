The Vocabulary of Charts: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Universal Chart Vocabulary

Most charts share the same basic parts. Learning these nine terms once equips you to read any chart you encounter.

Title

The name of the chart that answers "What am I looking at?"

Axis (Axes)

The two reference lines framing the chart. The x-axis runs horizontally (usually categories or time), and the y-axis runs vertically (usually values/amounts).

Label

Text naming something on the chart — axis labels name what each axis measures, and data labels show exact values.

Legend (Key)

A small box explaining what colors, patterns, or symbols represent.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Scale

The range of numbers on an axis and the spacing between them, helping you estimate values.

Data Point

One piece of information plotted on a chart (a bar, a dot, a slice, etc.).

Category

One of the groups being compared or counted, usually along the x-axis.

Value

The number a data point represents — the "how much" answer.

Source

A note saying where the data came from, so readers can verify the information.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the title of a chart?

The name of the chart that tells you what the chart is about. A good title answers the question: 'What am I looking at?'

What is an axis (plural: axes)?

One of the two reference lines that form the frame of most charts. The x-axis runs horizontally (left to right); the y-axis runs vertically (up and down).

What does the x-axis usually show?

Categories or time. It runs left to right (horizontal).

What does the y-axis usually show?

Values (amounts). It runs up and down (vertical).

What is a label on a chart?

A piece of text that names something on the chart. Axis labels tell you what each axis measures; data labels show exact values of specific bars, points, or s...

What is a legend (key)?

A small box that explains what colors, patterns, or symbols in the chart represent.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the scale on a chart?

The range of numbers shown on an axis and the spacing between them. It helps you estimate values from the chart.

What is a data point?

One piece of information plotted on a chart. On a bar chart, each bar is a data point. On a scatter plot, each dot is a data point.

What is a category on a chart?

One of the groups being compared or counted. Categories are usually listed along the x-axis.

What is a value on a chart?

The number that represents a data point — the 'how much' or 'how many' answer. On a bar chart, it is the height of the bar.

What is the source on a chart?

A note at the bottom of the chart that says where the data came from. A good chart always cites its source so readers can check the information.

What are the nine terms in the chart-reading toolkit?

Title, axes, labels, legend, scale, data points, categories, values, and source.
