Histograms vs. Box Plots: A Comparative Study and Introduction to Stem-and-Leaf Plots

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Histograms for Shape

Histograms excel at revealing the detailed shape of a data distribution, showing where data clusters, gaps, and multiple peaks (bimodal distributions) occur.

Box Plots for Comparison

Box plots provide a compact summary of the five-number summary (minimum, Q1, median, Q3, maximum) and outliers, making them ideal for comparing the spread and central tendency of two or more groups...

The Bin-Size Problem

The appearance and interpretation of a histogram can drastically change based on the chosen bin size. Too few bins hide detail, while too many create a noisy, spiky appearance.

Choosing the Right Display

Neither display is inherently "better"; the choice depends on the specific question being asked and what aspects of the data you want to emphasize or compare.

Stem-and-Leaf Plots

These plots offer a hybrid approach for small datasets, displaying both the overall shape of the distribution (like a histogram) and every individual data value (like a list).

Important Terms (1/2)

Histogram

A graphical display of data using bars of different heights. Each bar groups numbers into ranges (bins), and taller bars show that more data falls into that range. It reveals the shape, spread, and...

Box Plot (Box-and-Whisker Plot)

A standardized way of displaying the distribution of data based on the five-number summary: minimum, first quartile (Q1), median, third quartile (Q3), and maximum. It also clearly marks outliers.

Bin Size

The width of the intervals or ranges used to group data in a histogram. An appropriate bin size is crucial for accurately representing the data's distribution.

Stem-and-Leaf Plot

A data display method that organizes numerical data by splitting each value into a "stem" (usually the leading digit or digits) and a "leaf" (usually the final digit).

Stem

In a stem-and-leaf plot, the leading digit(s) of a data value. All values with the same stem are grouped together in a row.

Important Terms (2/2)

Leaf

In a stem-and-leaf plot, the final digit of a data value, listed horizontally next to its corresponding stem.
