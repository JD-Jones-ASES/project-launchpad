Important Terms (1/2)

Infographic (Information Graphic)

A visual display that combines charts, icons, images, and text into a single design to communicate data or ideas quickly and clearly.

Headline

The large-text title of an infographic that states its main message or key takeaway.

Charts and Graphs

Visual data elements (e.g., bar charts, pie charts, line graphs) used to present statistical evidence.

Icons

Simple, small images used to represent concepts or categories, making information easier to grasp.

Key Numbers

Large, bold statistics extracted from the data to attract attention and highlight important facts.

Important Terms (2/2)

Explanatory Text

Short paragraphs or captions that provide context, definitions, or additional details about the data and visuals.

Data Source

A reference (usually at the bottom) indicating where the information or statistics presented in the infographic originated, crucial for verifying credibility.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is an infographic?

An infographic (information graphic) is a visual display that combines charts, icons, images, and text into a single design to communicate data or ideas quic...

What is the primary goal of a well-made infographic?

To tell a story at a glance, allowing readers to understand the main message in a few seconds and then explore deeper details if they choose.

List the six main building blocks, or typical components, of an infographic.

Headline, Charts and graphs, Icons, Key numbers, Explanatory text, and Data source.

What is the function of the headline in an infographic?

It is the bold, large-text title that announces the main message. It's always the first thing a reader should see.

What role do charts and graphs play within an infographic?

They are the data visualization elements (like bar, pie, or line charts) that provide the evidence supporting the infographic's headline and main message.

Why are icons used in infographics?

Icons are simple, small images that make the infographic look friendly, help readers grasp concepts quickly, and aid in visual scanning.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are 'key numbers' in an infographic?

Key numbers are large, bold statistics pulled out of the data to grab attention and highlight important facts.

What is the purpose of explanatory text in an infographic?

Explanatory text consists of short paragraphs or captions that explain what the charts and numbers mean, usually written in simple language.

Why is the data source an 'extremely important' component of an infographic?

It tells you where the data came from, which is crucial for evaluating whether the infographic is trustworthy and reliable.

What is the very first step when reading an infographic, according to the textbook?

Read the headline first to understand the main message the designer wants you to take away.

After reading the headline, what should you do next when analyzing an infographic?

Scan the layout to understand how the infographic is organized (e.g., top-to-bottom, left-to-right, separate sections).

Beyond scanning and identifying elements, what are two critical steps for evaluating the reliability of an infographic?

Find the data source to check its credibility and check the claims to ensure numbers in the text match charts and visuals match the data.
