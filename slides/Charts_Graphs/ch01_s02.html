Frequency Tables Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Frequency

The exact number of times a specific item or category appears in a collection of data. It's a raw count, not a percentage.

Frequency Table

An organized display of data that lists categories and their corresponding frequencies. It often includes a column for tally marks as an intermediate step.

Purpose of Frequency Tables

To transform raw, potentially messy tally chart data into a clean, easy-to-read format for analysis and sharing.

Reading a Frequency Table

Involves identifying categories, their counts (frequencies), the most common category (mode), the least common category, and checking the total count for accuracy.

Relationship to Tally Charts

Tally charts are used for real-time data collection, while frequency tables are used to summarize and present that collected data clearly.

Important Terms

Frequency

The number of times a particular event, item, or category occurs within a dataset. For example, if 15 students chose "pizza" as their favorite food, the frequency of "pizza" is 15.

Frequency Table

Category

A distinct group or classification of data that is being counted. In a survey of favorite ice cream flavors, "Chocolate," "Vanilla," and "Strawberry" would be categories.

Mode

The category or value that appears most frequently in a dataset; in a frequency table, this corresponds to the category with the highest frequency count.

Dataset

A collection of related data points or observations.
