Key Biographical Overview (1/2)

Birth

384 BC in **Stagira**, a Greek colonial city on the Chalcidice peninsula. Due to his birthplace, he is frequently referred to as **“the Stagirite.”**

Tutoring Alexander

In 343 BC, King Philip II of Macedon hired Aristotle to tutor his son, the future **Alexander the Great**. This relationship lasted approximately three years.

Key Biographical Overview (2/2)

Founding the Lyceum (335 BC)

Upon returning to Athens, Aristotle established his own school, the **Lyceum**. It became a massive research center where Aristotle and his associates studied everything from city constitutions to ...

The Peripatetic School

Followers of Aristotle were known as **Peripatetics**, a name derived from the Greek word *peripatein* ("to walk about"), reflecting Aristotle’s habit of walking while lecturing.

Departure from Athens

After Alexander the Great died in 323 BC, anti-Macedonian sentiment rose in Athens. Aristotle, being closely associated with the Macedonian court, was threatened.

"Sin Against Philosophy"

Aristotle fled to Chalcis on the island of Euboea to prevent Athens from "sinning twice against philosophy," a reference to the city's execution of Socrates in 399 BC.

Death

Aristotle died in Chalcis in 322 BC at the age of 62.
