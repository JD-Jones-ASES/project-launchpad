Friendship and the Contemplative Life in Aristotle’s Ethics

Study Slides

The Philosophical Importance of Friendship

Friendship as Virtue

It is a stable disposition to relate to others correctly.

Social Necessity

Aristotle asserts that "no one would choose to live without friends, even if he had all other goods."

Character Illumination

Relationships reveal an individual's values and the kind of person they are becoming.

The Three Types of Friendship

Type. Basis of Relationship

Duration/Stability

Utility. Mutual benefit or what each party provides.

Contingent; dissolves when utility ceases.

Pleasure. Enjoyment or the feelings the relationship brings.

Contingent; dissolves when the pleasure fades.

Character (Perfect). Goodness of character; valuing the other for who they are.

Stable; character is enduring.

Civic Friendship and the State

Civic Friendship

A healthy community requires goodwill among citizens and a shared investment in one another's welfare.

Family Analogies

Political structures reflect family relationships:

Institutional Love

Good government is essentially a form of love made institutional.

The Contemplative Life (Bios Theoretikos)

Highest Part of Man

It exercises the intellect.

Self-Sufficiency (Autarkes)

It requires fewer external goods than the active life.

Continuity

It can be pursued more uninterruptedly than social activities.

Stability

The pleasures of philosophy are the most enduring.

Divinity

It is the most godlike activity.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Autarkes (Self-Sufficiency)

A mark of the highest good; the quality of requiring few external goods or others to achieve happiness.

Bios Theoretikos

The contemplative life; a life devoted to intellectual inquiry and theoria.

Civic Friendship

The goodwill and shared investment in mutual welfare that binds citizens together in a healthy political community.

Ordo Amoris

A concept (later explored by Augustine) regarding the ordered love of all things according to their proper rank; relevant to Aristotle's hierarchy of self-love and friendship.

Philia

Generally translated as friendship; a stable disposition to relate to others and a necessary component of the virtuous life.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Proper Self-Love

Love for the highest part of oneself (reason and virtue), which motivates noble actions and benefits others.

Teleios (Perfect)

Referring to the highest form of friendship based on character, which is complete and enduring.

Theoria

Intellectual contemplation; the active pursuit of truth through reasoning.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle, friendship is not just a personal matter but a stable _____ to relate to others in the right way.

disposition

Aristotle argues that friendship is a philosophical topic because it is a _____, meaning it has better and worse forms.

virtue

Why does Aristotle consider friendship a 'social necessity'?

Because no one would choose to live without friends, even if they had all other goods.

According to the text, what does friendship reveal about a person?

What the person values and the kind of character they are developing.

In a utility friendship, what do the parties value in one another?

What the other person provides (benefits or services).

What group of people are utility friendships most common among?

Business partners, colleagues, and acquaintances.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Under what condition does a utility friendship typically dissolve?

When the utility or benefit provided by the other person ceases.

What is the primary value shared between parties in a pleasure friendship?

The enjoyment or pleasure the relationship brings.

Pleasure friendships are noted by Aristotle to be particularly common among which demographic?

The young.

What causes a pleasure friendship to end?

The fading of the pleasure derived from the relationship.

In a character friendship, what is valued in the other person?

Their goodness of character or who they are as a person.

Why is character friendship considered stable compared to utility or pleasure friendships?

The character of a good person does not change with circumstances.
