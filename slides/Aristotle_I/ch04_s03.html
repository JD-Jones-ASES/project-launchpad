Aristotle’s Ethics: Friendship, Self-Love, and the Contemplative Life

Study Slides

The Three Types of Friendship

Type of Friendship. Primary Motive

Characteristics

Utility. Profit / Advantage

Common among the old or those seeking profit; based on what one can gain from the other.

Pleasure. Enjoyment / Passion

Common among the young; based on immediate gratification or pleasant company.

Perfect (Virtue). Goodness / Character

Based on mutual recognition of virtue; friends wish the good for the other for the other’s sake.

The Relationship Between Friendship and the State

Monarchy (Kingship)

Analogous to the Father-Son relationship, where the ruler acts for the benefit of the ruled. Its corruption is Despotism (ruling for the ruler's benefit).

Aristocracy

Analogous to the Husband-Wife relationship, where rule is based on excellence and assigned to its proper sphere. Its corruption is Oligarchy.

Timocracy

Analogous to the relationship between Brothers, where equality is the primary principle. Its corruption is Democracy.

Theoria: The Supreme Happiness

• It is the activity of our highest principle (the intellect).

• It is the most continuous and pleasant activity.

• It is the most self-sufficient (autarkes), requiring fewer external goods than practical virtues like courage or justice.

• It is "godlike," as it is the only activity we can reasonably attribute to the divine.

A "social friendship" where a community agrees on points of common interest and expediency, such as the choice of magistrates or alliances.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to the outset of Nicomachean Ethics Book VIII, what is the value of friendship even for a person who possesses all other goods?

No one would choose to live without friends.

Why do those in positions of authority or wealth have a special need for friends according to Aristotle?

They need objects for doing kindnesses and a means to preserve their slippery prosperity.

In what specific way does friendship benefit the young according to Aristotle?

It helps keep them from committing errors.

What is the primary benefit of friendship for those in the prime of life?

It enables them to better devise and carry out noble deeds.

How does Aristotle characterize the relationship between friendship and justice in social communities?

Where people are friends, justice is not required; but just people still need friendship.

Term: Philia

Definition: A broad Greek term for friendship covering personal bonds, familial affection, civic ties, and general goodwill.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the three distinct objects of affection that serve as motives for friendship?

The good, the pleasurable, and the useful.

According to Aristotle, why is it 'ridiculous' to say one feels friendship for inanimate objects like wine?

There is no reciprocation of affection or wishing well for the object's own sake.

What three conditions must be met for a relationship to be formally called 'Friendship'?

Mutually known kindly feelings, wishing one another good, and a motive of utility, pleasure, or goodness.

Why are friendships based on utility considered 'accidental' or fragile?

They dissolve as soon as the parties cease to be useful to one another.

In which age group is friendship based on utility most commonly found?

The old.

What is the primary motive for friendship among the young according to Aristotle?

Pleasure.
