The Eastern Preservation

Aristotle's works survived in the Greek East (Byzantium) and were translated into Arabic during the intellectual flowering of the Islamic world in the ninth and tenth centuries.

The Arabic Contribution

Scholars in Baghdad and Muslim Spain—specifically Al-Farabi, Avicenna, and Averroes—preserved, commented upon, and expanded Aristotelian philosophy.

The Conflict of Worldviews

The Eternity of the World

Aristotle argued the world was eternal, contradicting the Christian doctrine of creation.

The Nature of God

Aristotle described a God indifferent to the world, focused only on self-contemplation.

The Sufficiency of Reason

Aristotle suggested that human goods and virtues could be achieved through unaided reason, potentially rendering divine grace unnecessary.

The Thomistic Synthesis

Partnership, Not Rivalry

Aquinas argued that since God is the source of both natural reason and divine revelation, they cannot contradict.

Levels of Truth

Philosophy operates by the light of natural reason (the domain of the accessible), while theology operates by the light of divine revelation (the domain of truths surpassing reason).

The Principle of Perfection

Summarized by the phrase gratia non tollit naturam, sed perficit—"Grace does not destroy nature, but perfects it."

Short-Answer Practice Questions

Al-Farabi (c. 872–950)

The first great Arabic commentator on Aristotle's political philosophy.

Avicenna (Ibn Sina, 980–1037)

A Persian polymath who synthesized Aristotle with Neoplatonism in his Book of Healing.

Averroes (Ibn Rushd, 1126–1198)

Known as "The Commentator," he provided meticulous line-by-line commentaries that became standard references for Latin scholars.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In what year did Aristotle die?

322 BC

What was the name of the school Aristotle founded in Athens?

The Lyceum

What was the primary reason most of Aristotle's works were lost to Western Europe in the fifth and sixth centuries?

The knowledge of Greek was largely lost following the fragmentation of the Western Roman Empire.

Which Roman statesman and philosopher preserved a handful of Aristotle's logical texts for the Latin West before his execution in 524?

Boethius

Name the two logical texts by Aristotle that survived in the Latin West during the early Middle Ages.

The Categories and On Interpretation

Through which cultural and intellectual flowering were Aristotle's works preserved and translated into Arabic in the ninth and tenth centuries?

The Islamic world (specifically the courts of Baghdad and Muslim Spain)

Key Concepts (2/2)

Who is known as the “Second Teacher” in the Arabic tradition for his synthesis of Aristotle and Plato?

Al-Farabi

Concept: The Book of Healing (Kitab al-Shifa)

Definition: A comprehensive philosophical encyclopedia by Avicenna that drew heavily on Aristotle and Neoplatonism.

Which Andalusian philosopher was known simply as “The Commentator” in the Latin West?

Averroes (Ibn Rushd)

The chain of transmission for Aristotle's recovery in Western Europe followed which sequence of languages?

Greek to Arabic to Latin

During which century did Latin translations of Aristotle's works begin flooding into Western Europe from Arabic sources?

The twelfth century

What Aristotelian doctrine regarding the origin of the universe contradicted the Christian teaching of creation?

The eternity of the world
