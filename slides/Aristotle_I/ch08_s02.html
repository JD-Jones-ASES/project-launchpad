Q: Why is "hamartia" often mistranslated as a "moral flaw"?

While it can be a moral failing, *hamartia* literally means "missing the mark" or an error in judgment. It often refers to an ignorance of facts or a failure of practical wisdom in a specific situation rather than a deep-seated character defect.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: Which of the three means of persuasion did Aristotle consider the most powerful, and why?

Aristotle considered *ethos* (character) the most powerful. This is because persuasion operates most effectively when the speaker is perceived as trustworthy, knowledgeable, and genuinely interested in the audience's well-being.

Q: How does the *Rhetoric* connect to Aristotle’s definition of the "political animal"?

Political life is governed by speech and persuasion. Since humans are defined by *logos* (rational speech), the ability to construct and evaluate arguments is a necessary skill for citizens to participate in assemblies, courts, and leadership.
