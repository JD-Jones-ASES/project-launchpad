The Structure of Virtue: The Mean

Distributive Justice

Focus

The division of shared communal goods, such as honors, public offices, and civic benefits.

Principle

Proportionality. Goods are distributed based on a person's contribution, worth, or merit.

Distinction

This is not modern "redistributive justice" (taking from some to give to others); rather, it is the initial allocation of benefits to those who have earned a stake in the community.

Corrective Justice

Focus

Restoring fairness after a wrong has been committed or a contract has been breached.

Principle

Arithmetic. The goal is to restore the balance by stripping the wrongdoer of their gain and compensating the victim for their loss.

Key Feature

In corrective justice, the social standing or merit of the individuals involved is irrelevant; the law treats the parties as equals to rectify the specific harm.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Corrective Justice

A form of justice concerned with rectifying transactions or harms arithmetically, ensuring that gains and losses are balanced regardless of the parties' merit.

Distributive Justice

The proportionate allocation of communal goods (offices, honors, benefits) based on the merit or contribution of the recipients.

Nomos

Law, custom, or convention; the established rules of a specific community.

Phronesis

Practical wisdom; the intellectual virtue required to identify the mean in specific situations.

Physis

Nature; the inherent character of a thing that holds true independently of cultural or legal arrangements.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Proairesis

Deliberate choice; the combination of rational deliberation and the will that characterizes a responsible moral agent.

The Mean

The virtuous intermediate state between the vices of excess and deficiency, relative to the situation and the person.

Voluntary Action

An act that originates within the agent who has knowledge of the particular circumstances involved.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What two elements must be balanced when studying Aristotle's approach to virtue and justice?

One must hold together a systematic theory and a careful attention to the specific features of individual cases.

Why is the discussion of voluntary and involuntary action considered the foundation of the study of virtue?

Character cannot be evaluated unless it is clear when an agent is genuinely responsible for their actions.

How does Aristotle distinguish between two people performing the same outward act of cheating?

He evaluates the degree to which the action was a product of settled choice versus impulse or panic.

The Greek term for a deliberate choice involving both rational deliberation and a genuine act of will is $proairesis$.

True

What is the function of $proairesis$ in Aristotle's ethics?

It distinguishes a morally responsible agent from someone acting solely from impulse or external compulsion.

According to Aristotle, how is a person's character primarily built and assessed?

Character is developed and judged through the exercise of deliberate choice.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which modern legal distinction mirrors the Aristotelian question of whether a defendant knew what he was doing?

The distinction between intentional acts like murder and negligent acts like manslaughter.

Under what two conditions is an action defined as voluntary by Aristotle?

The action must originate in the agent and the agent must know the particular circumstances of the act.

An action is considered involuntary when it is performed under _____ or in ignorance of relevant circumstances.

compulsion

What characterizes an action done under compulsion?

The moving principle is external to the agent and the agent contributes nothing to the action.

Why does Aristotle restrict praise and blame to voluntary actions only?

Only voluntary actions are considered true expressions of a person's deliberate character.

Aristotle's doctrine of the _____ defines virtue as a stable disposition to choose the intermediate point between extremes.

mean
