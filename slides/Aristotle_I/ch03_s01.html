The Structure of Virtue: The Mean

Distributive Justice

Focus

The division of shared communal goods, such as honors, public offices, and civic benefits.

Principle

**Proportionality.** Goods are distributed based on a person's contribution, worth, or merit.

Distinction

This is not modern "redistributive justice" (taking from some to give to others); rather, it is the initial allocation of benefits to those who have earned a stake in the community.

Corrective Justice

Focus

Restoring fairness after a wrong has been committed or a contract has been breached.

Principle

**Arithmetic.** The goal is to restore the balance by stripping the wrongdoer of their gain and compensating the victim for their loss.

Key Feature

In corrective justice, the social standing or merit of the individuals involved is irrelevant; the law treats the parties as equals to rectify the specific harm.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Corrective Justice

A form of justice concerned with rectifying transactions or harms arithmetically, ensuring that gains and losses are balanced regardless of the parties' merit.

Distributive Justice

The proportionate allocation of communal goods (offices, honors, benefits) based on the merit or contribution of the recipients.

Nomos

Law, custom, or convention; the established rules of a specific community.

Phronesis

Practical wisdom; the intellectual virtue required to identify the mean in specific situations.

Physis

Nature; the inherent character of a thing that holds true independently of cultural or legal arrangements.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Proairesis

Deliberate choice; the combination of rational deliberation and the will that characterizes a responsible moral agent.

The Mean

The virtuous intermediate state between the vices of excess and deficiency, relative to the situation and the person.

Voluntary Action

An act that originates within the agent who has knowledge of the particular circumstances involved.
