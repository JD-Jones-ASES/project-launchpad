Politics as the Master Science

Scope

It determines which sciences are studied in a community and coordinates other esteemed faculties like military strategy, domestic management, and rhetoric.

Objective

The end of politics is "The Good of Man." While the good of an individual is important, the good of the community or nation is "greater and more perfect to discover and preserve."

Key Principles of the Mean

Relative, Not Absolute

The mean is not a mathematical midpoint (like 6 being the mean of 2 and 10). It is "relative to us"—the right amount for the specific person in the specific situation.

Avoidance of Extremes

Virtue is the middle state between two vices: Excess (too much) and Defect (too little).

Emotional Calibration

Virtue involves feeling emotions (fear, anger, pity, etc.) "when we ought, on what occasions, towards whom, why, and as we should."

Non-Universal Application

Some actions (e.g., murder, adultery, theft) and feelings (e.g., envy, malevolence) have no mean; they are inherently bad regardless of the degree.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Arete

Virtue or excellence; the quality that makes a thing or person perform its function well.

Doctrine of the Mean

The principle that every virtue is a middle state between the vices of excess and deficiency.

Ergon

Function, work, or characteristic activity of a thing (e.g., the ergon of a knife is to cut).

Eudaimonia

Often translated as "happiness," it refers to a state of objective human flourishing, "living well," and "doing well."

Habituation

The process of forming character through the repetition of specific actions until they become second nature.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Master Science

A science (like Politics) that orders and coordinates other disciplines toward a singular, higher end.

Phronesis

Practical wisdom; the intellectual virtue that enables a person to discern the relative mean in specific, real-world circumstances.

Politike

Politics; the comprehensive science of the human community and its collective good.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle, what is the ultimate aim of every art, science, action, and moral choice?

Some good.

How does Aristotle define the 'Chief Good'?

That which all things aim at and which is desired for its own sake.

In Aristotle's hierarchy of ends, what is the relationship between 'master-arts' and the arts ranging under them?

The ends of master-arts are more choice-worthy because the lower arts are pursued for their sake.

Why does Aristotle identify politics ($\pi o\lambda\iota\tau\iota\kappa\acute{\eta}$) as the 'master science' of the human good?

It orders and coordinates all other sciences and crafts toward the good of the whole community.

Why is the good of the community considered 'greater and more perfect' than the good of an individual?

Achieving the good for a whole nation is more noble and 'godlike' than doing so for a single person.

Term: Eudaimonia ($\epsilon\upsilon\delta\alpha\iota\mu o u\acute{\iota}\alpha$)

Definition: A condition of genuine human flourishing or 'living well,' representing an objective state rather than a subjective feeling.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the 'pretty general agreement' regarding the name of the highest of all goods?

Happiness (or eudaimonia).

What do 'the multitude' typically mistake for happiness, according to Aristotle?

Palpable and apparent things like pleasure, wealth, or honor.

According to Aristotle, why is a young man not a fit student of Moral Philosophy?

He lacks experience in the actions of life and tends to follow his passions instead of reason.

In the study of ethics, what must a student already possess to profitably 'receive' the principles of nobleness and justice?

A background of being well-trained in good habits.

What is the primary method of Aristotle's 'function argument'?

Identifying the characteristic work or purpose (ergon) of a human being to determine their excellence.

Term: Ergon ($\acute{\epsilon}_x000D_ho\gamma o u$)

Definition: The function, work, or characteristic activity that defines what a thing is and how it is judged as 'good.'
