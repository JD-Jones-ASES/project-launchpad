Q: How is the natural law "promulgated" if it is not written in human statutes?

According to Aquinas, natural law is promulgated by God instilling it into the human mind so that it is known naturally through the light of reason.

Q: What is the relationship between the eternal law and the natural law?

Natural law is not a separate entity from the eternal law; rather, it is the rational creature’s participation in the eternal law. It is an "imprint" of the Divine light on the human mind, allowing humans to discern good from evil.

Q: According to the text, what occurs when a human law is "unjust"?

Following the tradition of St. Augustine, Aquinas argues that a law which is not just is "no law at all." It is considered a perversion of law and lacks genuine binding force in conscience.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beatific Vision

The ultimate end of human existence, which exceeds natural human capacities and requires Divine Law for guidance.

Common Good

The welfare and interest of the entire community, which serves as the primary objective of legitimate law.

Divine Law (*lex divina*)

The portion of God's law revealed specifically through Scripture to guide humanity toward their supernatural end.

Eternal Law (*lex aeterna*)

The supreme reason and unchangeable plan of God’s governance over the universe.

Human Law (*lex humana*)

Particular determinations of laws devised by human reason and enacted by legitimate authority.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Natural Law (*lex naturalis*)

The rational creature's participation in the eternal law, enabling the discernment of good and evil through natural reason.

Ordinance of Reason

The requirement that law be a rational directive aimed at a specific end (the good), rather than an arbitrary choice.

Practical Reason

The use of reason directed toward action and the pursuit of the good, as opposed to speculative reason directed toward the apprehension of truth.

Promulgation

The act of publicly declaring or notifying the community of a law, which is necessary for the law to have binding force.

Social Contract

The modern political theory that the state is an artificial construction created by individuals to protect their own interests or rights.
