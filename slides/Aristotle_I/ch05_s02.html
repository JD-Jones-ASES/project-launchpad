Q: How is the natural law "promulgated" if it is not written in human statutes?

According to Aquinas, natural law is promulgated by God instilling it into the human mind so that it is known naturally through the light of reason.

Q: What is the relationship between the eternal law and the natural law?

Natural law is not a separate entity from the eternal law; rather, it is the rational creature’s participation in the eternal law. It is an "imprint" of the Divine light on the human mind, allowing humans to discern good from evil.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beatific Vision

The ultimate end of human existence, which exceeds natural human capacities and requires Divine Law for guidance.

Common Good

The welfare and interest of the entire community, which serves as the primary objective of legitimate law.

Divine Law (lex divina)

The portion of God's law revealed specifically through Scripture to guide humanity toward their supernatural end.

Eternal Law (lex aeterna)

The supreme reason and unchangeable plan of God’s governance over the universe.

Human Law (lex humana)

Particular determinations of laws devised by human reason and enacted by legitimate authority.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Natural Law (lex naturalis)

The rational creature's participation in the eternal law, enabling the discernment of good and evil through natural reason.

Ordinance of Reason

The requirement that law be a rational directive aimed at a specific end (the good), rather than an arbitrary choice.

Practical Reason

The use of reason directed toward action and the pursuit of the good, as opposed to speculative reason directed toward the apprehension of truth.

Promulgation

The act of publicly declaring or notifying the community of a law, which is necessary for the law to have binding force.

Social Contract

The modern political theory that the state is an artificial construction created by individuals to protect their own interests or rights.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Which philosopher provided the foundation for Aquinas's belief that political authority should direct citizens toward virtue?

Aristotle

In which specific work and questions is Thomas Aquinas's "Treatise on Law" found?

The Summa Theologica, Prima Secundae, Questions 90 through 97.

What is Aquinas's formal four-part definition of law?

An ordinance of reason for the common good, made by him who has care of the community, and promulgated.

According to Aquinas, what does it mean for a law to be an "ordinance of reason"?

It means the law is rational rather than arbitrary.

What is the intended aim of a law if it satisfies the requirement of the "common good"?

The welfare of the entire community rather than the private interest of the ruler.

Who possesses the authority to make law according to Aquinas's definition?

He who has care of the community (legitimate authority).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is "promulgation" considered essential for a law to obtain binding force?

A law must be notified to rational creatures so they can know and follow it.

How does Aquinas answer the objection that Natural Law is not a law because it requires no public declaration?

He argues that God promulgated it by instilling it into the human mind to be known naturally.

Under what condition are those not present during a law's declaration still bound to observe it?

They are bound if the law is notified or can be notified to them by others after promulgation.

What is the highest and most fundamental type of law in Aquinas's hierarchy?

Eternal Law

How does Aquinas define "Eternal Law"?

God's rational governance of the entire created order or universe.

What is the relationship between Natural Law and Eternal Law?

Natural Law is the rational creature's participation in the Eternal Law.
