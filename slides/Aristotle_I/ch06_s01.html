Aristotle on Constitutions and Revolution: A Study Guide

Study Slides

The Anatomy of Revolution (Stasis)

Psychological Factors

Envy, ambition, and resentment drive instability.

Inequality

Both excessive inequality and a total lack of recognition for merit (too little inequality) are destabilizing.

Constitutional Decay

Even a well-designed constitution will fail if its principles are pushed to extremes (e.g., a democracy becoming mob rule).

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many actual constitutions did Aristotle study to inform his political theories in the Politics?

158.

Unlike Plato's pursuit of an abstract "Good," Aristotle insists the best constitution is the best _____.

Achievable.

What is the primary difference between Plato's Republic and Aristotle's Politics regarding the "ideal city"?

Plato focuses on an abstract philosophical vision, while Aristotle considers practical constraints like history and geography.

Aristotle's combination of normative seriousness with practical realism is often described as his _____ temperament.

Empirical.

What standard does Aristotle use to evaluate whether a constitution is a "correct" or "deviant" form?

The common good.

Term: Polity ($politeia$).

The best practically achievable constitution for most cities, combining elements of democracy and oligarchy.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In Aristotle's political theory, a polity is an application of the _____ to political life.

Doctrine of the mean.

What are the two extreme constitutional forms that a polity seeks to balance?

Extreme democracy and extreme oligarchy.

Why does Aristotle consider extreme democracy and extreme oligarchy inherently unstable?

Both represent the triumph of faction over the common good.

According to Aristotle, what social group serves as the foundation of a stable political mean?

The middle class.

Why does a large middle class promote political stability?

It moderates the extremes of wealth and poverty and weakens factionalism.

Aristotle defines extreme democracy as rule by the _____, for the _____.

Poor.
