Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle, what is the prerequisite for an action to be subject to praise or blame?

The action must be voluntary.

What are the two primary causes of involuntary actions in Aristotle's ethics?

Compulsion or ignorance.

How does Aristotle classify an action where a person is forced to choose between two evils, such as a man being extorted?

He classifies it as more like a voluntary act than an involuntary one.

Term: $proairesis$

Definition: Deliberate choice involving rational deliberation and a commitment to acting on that deliberation.

In Aristotelian ethics, what distinguishes a moral action from a mere impulse or a habit?

The presence of $proairesis$, or deliberate choice.

Virtue is not defined by isolated acts, but by a whole pattern of _____.

deliberate choice

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which virtue does Aristotle use as his paradigm case for examining the mean between excess and deficiency?

Courage.

Between which two feelings or states of mind is Courage the mean?

Fear and boldness.

What is the common definition of fear mentioned by Aristotle?

The expectation of evil.

According to Aristotle, why is a man who fears disgrace considered 'good' and 'honourable'?

Because it is right and noble to fear disgrace.

Does Aristotle consider a person 'Brave' in the proper sense if they are merely fearless of poverty or disease?

No, because these fears do not proceed from one's own viciousness or fault.

What does Aristotle identify as the 'greatest dread' because it is the end of all things?

Death.
