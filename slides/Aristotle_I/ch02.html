Relative, Not Mathematical

Emotional Precision

The mean does not imply moderate or weak emotions. It means feeling the "right emotions, at the right intensity, toward the right objects, at the right time, for the right reasons."

Exceptions

Some actions (murder, adultery) and feelings (envy, malevolence) are inherently bad and have no "mean" state.

Aquinas and the Synthesis of Grace and Nature

Imperfect Happiness

Aquinas identifies Aristotle’s *eudaimonia* (virtuous activity in this life) as "imperfect happiness." It is the highest good achievable through natural human powers.
