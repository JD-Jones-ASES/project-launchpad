Man as a "Political Animal" (Politikon Zoon)

Teleological Development

Just as an acorn's telos (end) is the oak tree, the human telos is found in the city (polis).

The Hierarchy of Community

Human society develops naturally from the household (for basic existence) to the village, and finally to the city.

Citizenship as Activity

Status vs. Activity

Citizenship is not merely a legal status or a document; it is an active participation in the common life of the community.

Constitutional Variation

The requirements for citizenship change based on the government type (e.g., wealth in an oligarchy, virtue in an aristocracy).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Aristocracy

A correct form of government ruled by a few who prioritize virtue and the common good.

Imago Dei

A theological concept meaning "Image of God"; used to argue for the irreducible dignity of every human being.

Logos

Reason, speech, or rational account; the capacity that enables humans to engage in moral and political discourse.

Oligarchy

A deviant form of government where the few rule for their own private interest, often based on wealth.

Politikon Zoa

"Political animals"; Aristotle’s term for human beings, emphasizing that our nature is fulfilled within a political community.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Polity

A correct form of government ruled by the many; considered the most practically achievable form of government for most cities.

Telos

The end, purpose, or goal toward which a natural thing develops.

Tyranny

A deviant form of government where a single ruler governs for their own private benefit; identified as the worst form of government.
