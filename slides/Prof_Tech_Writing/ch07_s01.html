The "Why" Test

A practical tip to determine document type: if the reader asks "How do I do this?", write instructions; if they ask "How does this work?" or "Why does this happen?", write a process description.

Important Terms

Instructions

Step-by-step directions that tell a reader how to perform a task. They use imperative mood and second person.

Process Description

A written explanation of how something happens or how a system works, intended for a reader who wants to understand the process rather than perform it.

Imperative Mood

A verb form used to give commands or make requests, speaking directly to the reader (e.g., "Press the button").

Indicative Mood

A verb form used to state facts or describe what happens in reality (e.g., "The pump circulates water").

Mechanism Description

A specific type of process description that explains how an object, device, or system is constructed and how its component parts work together.

The Fundamental Difference: Purpose and Audience Goal

Instructions

are action-oriented. Their goal is to enable the reader to successfully complete a task. They guide the reader through a series of steps they must take.

Process Descriptions

are understanding-oriented. Their goal is to explain how something functions, how a sequence of events unfolds, or how a system operates.

Recognizing Which Document Type You Need

• If the reader needs to act, write instructions.

• If the reader needs to comprehend, write a process description.

Grammatical Moods: Indicative vs. Imperative

Indicative Mood

This mood states facts or describes reality. It's the standard for factual statements.

Imperative Mood

This mood is used to give commands, orders, or directions.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are instructions in technical writing?

Step-by-step directions that tell a reader how to do something, assuming the reader will perform the task.

What grammatical mood and person do instructions typically use?

Instructions typically use the imperative mood (e.g., “Press the button”) and speak directly to the reader in the second person.

What is the fundamental purpose of a process description?

To explain how something works or how something happens so the reader can understand it, rather than perform it.

What grammatical mood and person do process descriptions typically use?

Process descriptions typically use the indicative mood (e.g., “The pump circulates water”) and are written in the third person.
