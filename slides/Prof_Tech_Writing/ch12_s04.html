Important Terms

Professional Identity

The image and reputation you present to the working world through your resume, online profiles, communications, and professional conduct.

LinkedIn

The world's largest professional social networking platform, used for job searching, recruiting, networking, and sharing industry-related content.

Portfolio

A curated collection of work samples that demonstrates your skills, accomplishments, and professional growth to potential employers or admissions officers.

Digital Footprint

The trail of data you leave online, encompassing all your activities, posts, and profiles. Employers actively screen candidates' digital footprints.

Your Digital First Impression

• Treat your LinkedIn profile as a professional document, similar to a resume.

• Use a professional photo.

• Write in complete sentences.

• Present your experiences with strong action verbs and quantified results, as you would on a resume.

What Employers Look For (Positives)

• A professional online presence (LinkedIn, portfolio).

• Evidence that your online activities and interests match your resume.

• Good communication skills in your posts.

• Community involvement or creative projects.

What Concerns Employers (Negatives)

• Inappropriate photos or language.

• Complaints about previous employers or coworkers.

• Discriminatory or offensive content.

• Evidence of dishonesty (inconsistencies between resume and social media).

• No online presence at all, which can appear unusual in the digital age.

The Employer Search Test

• Search your own name in a web browser.

• Is the first page of results something you would comfortably show a future employer, college admissions officer, or scholarship committee?

• If not, take steps to improve your digital footprint by building positive professional content (like a LinkedIn profile or portfolio) and adjusting privacy settings on personal social media accounts.

• You can have both a personal online life and a professional one; just keep them separate using privacy settings on personal accounts while maintaining public professional profiles.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the purpose of the Summary paragraph in a LinkedIn profile?

It's a 3–5 sentence 'elevator pitch' that introduces you, highlights key skills and interests, and indicates what opportunities you are seeking.

When creating the 'Experience' section on LinkedIn, what advice should students follow?

Mirror resume entries, using the same action verbs and quantified results to describe responsibilities and achievements.

What specific elements should be included in the 'Education' section of a student's LinkedIn profile?

Your high school, expected graduation date, relevant coursework, and honors.

How do 'Skills' on LinkedIn gain credibility?

LinkedIn allows other people (connections, colleagues) to 'endorse' your skills, which adds external validation.
