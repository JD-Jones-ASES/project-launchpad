Introduction to Inquiry and Response Letters

Definition

Inquiry letters formally request information, products, or services, while response letters provide the answers.

Purpose

These two letter types facilitate a fundamental exchange in business: asking for and providing information. They are among the most common forms of business correspondence.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is an inquiry letter?

A formal letter requesting information, products, or services from a person or organization.

What is a response letter?

A letter that answers an inquiry completely and professionally.

What basic exchange in business correspondence do inquiry and response letters represent?

The fundamental exchange where someone needs something, and someone provides it.

What three things should a reader immediately understand from an effective inquiry letter?

Who the writer is, what information they need, and why they need it.

What should the opening paragraph of an inquiry letter include?

Identification of the writer, a clear statement of purpose, and what prompted the letter.

What should the middle paragraph(s) of an inquiry letter contain?

Specific questions or requests, ideally using numbered or bulleted lists for multiple items, stated precisely.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What should the closing paragraph of an inquiry letter include?

A thank you to the reader, the writer's contact information, and any relevant deadline.

What is a common outcome of vague requests in an inquiry letter?

Vague requests typically receive vague answers.

What were the three main shortcomings of the 'Before' (vague) inquiry letter example?

It did not identify the writer, did not specify the needed information, and provided no way for the reader to respond.

Why is it beneficial to use numbered lists for questions in an inquiry letter, especially with more than two items?

It makes the letter easier to read and the reader's response easier to write, increasing the likelihood of complete answers.

In the Alice Chen inquiry letter, what additional type of information did she briefly include, and what was its purpose?

She mentioned her qualifications (e.g., AP Biology, volunteering) to help the reader understand if she was a realistic candidate for the program.

Why should an inquiry letter include a clear deadline with a reason for it?

It provides urgency and context for the request, making it more likely to be addressed within the writer's necessary timeframe.
