Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the two essential questions you must answer before writing anything?

1. Who will read this? (Your audience.) 2. What should they do after reading it? (Your purpose.)

What happens if you don't know your audience or purpose before writing?

Your writing will wander, and your reader will too, making the communication ineffective.

Define "Audience" in the context of writing.

The person or people who will read your writing. Your audience determines everything about how you write.

What key elements of your writing are determined by your audience?

The words you choose, the level of detail you include, the tone you use, and how you organize your information.

Define "Tone" in writing.

The attitude or feeling conveyed by a piece of writing, similar to tone of voice in speech. It depends on your audience and purpose.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What questions should you ask yourself during 'The Audience Test' before writing?

What do they already know? What do they need to know? What is their relationship to you? How formal do they expect the communication to be?

Define "Purpose" in the context of workplace writing.

The reason you are writing—what you want your reader to know, believe, feel, or do after reading your message.

List some common practical purposes of workplace writing.

To inform, to request, to persuade, to instruct, to document, or to respond.

Why is effective workplace writing described as 'reader-centered'?

Because you are writing to accomplish something through your reader, not just to express yourself.
