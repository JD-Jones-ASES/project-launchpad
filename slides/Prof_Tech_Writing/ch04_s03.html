Chapter 4, Section 3

Internal vs. External Communication in Professional Writing

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Internal Communication

Messages exchanged among people *within* the same organization (e.g., employees, managers). It assumes shared context, jargon, and organizational structure.

External Communication

Messages sent to people *outside* the organization (e.g., customers, media, general public, regulatory bodies). It requires more explanation and a polished, positive tone.

Three Key Differences

Internal and external messages differ primarily in:

Stakeholder Analysis

Identifying all individuals or groups affected by or interested in an organization's actions helps tailor messages effectively, as different stakeholders require different information.

Message Adaptation

The process of adjusting the same core facts to suit different audiences by changing tone, detail, and framing.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Dissemination

The act of distributing information widely and effectively, ensuring all relevant parties *receive* it, not just that it was sent.

Compliance

The act of adhering to rules, policies, regulations, or instructions. Memos related to compliance often require explicit language to ensure recipients understand mandatory actions.

Policy vs. Procedure

A policy is a general rule or guideline, while a procedure outlines the specific steps to follow to comply with a policy.

Important Terms (1/3)

Memorandum (memo)

A formal written message used for internal communication within an organization.

Header block

The formatted section at the top of a memo containing the To, From, Date, and Subject (RE) fields.

Action item

A specific task assigned to a specific person with a specific deadline.

Internal communication

A message intended for people within the same organization.

External communication

A message intended for people outside the organization.

Important Terms (2/3)

Stakeholder

Any person or group that has an interest in or is affected by an organization's decisions or actions.

Policy

An official rule or guideline established by an organization to govern behavior, decisions, or operations.

Procedure

A specific series of steps to be followed in a defined order to accomplish a task or comply with a policy.

Inverted pyramid

A writing structure that puts the most important information first, followed by supporting details, with background last.

Lead (lede)

The opening sentence or paragraph of an announcement, containing the most critical information.

Important Terms (3/3)

Bulletin

A brief official announcement intended to inform a group about a specific event, change, or piece of news.

Directive

An official instruction or order issued by someone in authority, requiring specific action from the recipients.

Disseminate

To distribute or spread information widely, especially to ensure all relevant parties receive it.

Compliance

The act of following rules, policies, regulations, or instructions as required by an authority.

What Changes Between Internal and External Messages (1/2)

Focus on Benefits

Shift from operational needs to audience advantages.

Simplify Language

Remove jargon, explain complex terms.

Broaden Context

Provide necessary background that internal readers already possess.

Polish Tone

Adopt a more formal, positive, and public-facing voice.

Reduce Detail

Omit internal logistics, budget figures, and sensitive data.

What Changes Between Internal and External Messages (2/2)

Call to Action

Adapt to external audience needs (e.g., "bring ID" vs. "complete training").

Dissemination and Compliance

Dissemination

More than just sending information; it means ensuring that all relevant parties *receive* and ideally *understand* the message. An unread email or unshared document is not properly disseminated.

Compliance

Refers to following rules, policies, or instructions. Memos related to compliance (e.g., new safety regulations, expense reporting changes) are often mandatory.
