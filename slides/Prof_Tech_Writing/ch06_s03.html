Keeping Records

Important Terms (1/2)

Complaint Letter

A formal written communication sent by a customer or client to a company to express dissatisfaction with a product, service, or billing error and to seek a resolution.

Adjustment Letter

A formal written response from a company to a customer's complaint, acknowledging the problem, explaining what happened, and stating the proposed resolution.

Desired Resolution

The specific action or outcome a customer requests in their complaint letter to rectify a problem (e.g., a refund, a replacement, a repair, a credit).

Good-faith effort

Demonstrating that one has genuinely attempted to solve a problem through initial steps (e.g., contacting customer service, trying troubleshooting) before escalating to a formal complaint.

Manufacturing Defect

A flaw or imperfection in a product that originated during its production process, often affecting a batch of items.

Important Terms (2/2)

Customer Resolution Specialist

A company employee whose role involves directly handling and resolving customer complaints and issues, often through formal correspondence.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of a complaint letter?

To get a specific problem resolved, not to vent frustration.

What is an "adjustment letter"?

A formal response from a company to a customer's complaint that acknowledges the problem, explains what happened, and states the resolution.

What is the critical distinction between an effective complaint letter and an ineffective one?

An effective complaint letter is specific, factual, and solution-oriented, while an ineffective one is angry, vague, or threatening.

What is the first part of the Four-Part Complaint Structure?

State the problem clearly and specifically, including dates, order numbers, product names, and any other identifying details.

In a complaint letter, after stating the problem, what should you explain next?

The impact of the problem on you, ensuring it is factual and not emotional.

Why is it important to document what you have already tried in a complaint letter?

It shows you have made a good-faith effort to resolve the issue yourself (e.g., calling customer service, returning an item).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the final crucial element to include in a complaint letter?

A clear statement of your desired resolution, such as a refund, replacement, or apology, being specific about what you want.

Describe a common mistake people make when writing complaint letters.

Using emotional language (e.g., "furious," "unacceptable") instead of sticking to facts and specific details. Emotional language can make the writer seem unr...

Why is it beneficial to keep copies of all complaint letters and responses?

To create a paper trail of professional, factual correspondence that serves as strong evidence if the issue needs to be escalated.

What three things should a good adjustment letter accomplish?

It should acknowledge the problem, explain what happened (without making excuses), and clearly state the resolution.

What is the first part of the Three-Part Adjustment Structure?

Acknowledge and apologize: Thank the customer for writing, acknowledge the problem, and offer a sincere, brief apology.

When explaining what happened in an adjustment letter, what two key principles should you follow?

Be honest without being defensive, and take responsibility without blaming the customer, even if they contributed to the problem.
