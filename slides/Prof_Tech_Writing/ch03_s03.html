Warnings, Cautions, and Notes in Instructions

Signal Words Communicate Risk Levels

Professional technical writing uses standardized signal words (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION, NOTICE) to indicate the severity and type of risk involved.

DANGER

Indicates a hazard that will cause death or serious injury. Used for the most extreme risks.

WARNING

Indicates a hazard that could cause death or serious injury. More common than DANGER.

CAUTION

Indicates a hazard that could cause minor injury or equipment damage. Most commonly used in general instructions.

Key Concepts (2/2)

NOTICE

Indicates information important for avoiding equipment damage or data loss, with no risk of personal injury. Also commonly used.

Avoid Overusing Warnings

Excessive use of safety notices desensitizes readers, making them ignore genuine risks. Use them only when a true hazard exists.

Notes Provide Helpful, Non-Critical Information

Notes offer useful context, tips, or explanations that improve the reader's experience but are not essential for completing the task successfully or safely.

Visually Distinguish Notices and Notes

Safety notices and notes should be formatted distinctly from the main instructional steps to make them stand out and be easily recognizable.

Maintain Consistent Visual Signals

Use the same visual style (e.g., bold red for DANGER, blue for NOTICE) for each type of notice throughout a document so readers can quickly learn and recognize their meaning.

Important Terms (1/2)

Signal Words

Standardized terms (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION, NOTICE) used in technical writing to communicate the level and type of risk associated with an instruction.

DANGER

A signal word indicating a hazard that will cause death or serious injury if the instruction is not followed.

WARNING

A signal word indicating a hazard that could cause death or serious injury if the instruction is not followed.

CAUTION

A signal word indicating a hazard that could cause minor injury or equipment damage if the instruction is not followed.

NOTICE

A signal word indicating important information for avoiding equipment damage or data loss, where there is no risk of personal injury.

Important Terms (2/2)

Note

Additional information that is helpful or provides useful context, tips, or explanations, but is not required to complete the step or to prevent damage/injury.

The Golden Rule: Place Safety Notices Before the Step

Why "Before"?

Readers need to know about a potential danger or precaution before they take an action. If a warning appears after the action, it's too late – the damage may already be done.

Safety notices alert readers to risks such as equipment damage, data loss, or personal injury if a step is performed incorrectly.

What is the "Golden Rule" for placing safety notices within a procedure?

Which two signal words are most commonly used in school assignments and early career technical writing?

CAUTION and NOTICE are most often used in these contexts, as DANGER and WARNING apply to more severe, industrial-level risks.

In what settings are DANGER and WARNING notices primarily used?

DANGER and WARNING notices are primarily used in industrial, medical, and construction settings where physical safety is gravely at stake.

What is a 'common mistake' to avoid when using warnings in technical documentation?

Overusing warnings. If every step has a warning, readers stop paying attention to them, effectively warning about nothing.
