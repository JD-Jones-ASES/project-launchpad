Warnings, Cautions, and Notes in Instructions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Placement of Safety Notices

All warnings, cautions, and notes must appear *before* the step or action they apply to. Providing information after the reader has taken a risky action renders the notice useless.

Avoiding Overuse of Warnings

Using too many warnings can desensitize readers, causing them to disregard genuinely important safety information. Warnings should be reserved for actual, significant risks.

Important Terms (1/2)

Safety Notice

An interruption in the normal flow of instructions designed to alert the reader to a potential risk, such as damage to equipment, loss of data, or personal injury.

Signal Word

A standardized word (DANGER, WARNING, CAUTION, NOTICE) used to indicate the specific type and severity of a hazard, typically placed at the beginning of a safety notice.

DANGER

A signal word indicating an imminently hazardous situation which, if not avoided, *will* result in death or serious injury. Reserved for the most extreme risks.

WARNING

A signal word indicating a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, *could* result in death or serious injury. More common than DANGER.

Important Terms (2/2)

CAUTION

A signal word indicating a potentially hazardous situation which, if not avoided, *could* result in minor or moderate injury, or equipment damage.
