Techniques for Balanced Writing

Specific strategies, such as letting evidence precede conclusions, using comparisons and benchmarks, acknowledging limitations, and writing for the decision-maker, help achieve the right balance.

Avoiding Hiding Behind Complexity

Translating dense technical language into accessible, outcome-oriented terms for the target audience is crucial for effective communication and decision-making.

Important Terms (1/2)

White Paper

A detailed report or guide that informs readers concisely about a complex issue and presents the issuing body's philosophy on the matter. Often used to promote a product or viewpoint.

Technical Proposal

A document outlining a proposed project, product, or solution, typically submitted in response to a request. It demonstrates capability and aims to persuade the reader to adopt the proposed approach.

The Authority Balance

The sweet spot in technical and persuasive writing where credibility is built through objective evidence and analysis, rather than subjective self-promotion.

Superlatives

Words used to describe the highest degree of a quality (e.g., "best," "most innovative," "unmatched"). When unsupported by evidence, they can undermine credibility.

Data Without Interpretation

Presenting raw facts and figures without explaining their meaning, significance, or implications for the reader.

Important Terms (2/2)

Data with Analysis

Presenting raw facts and figures along with explanations of patterns, comparisons, and conclusions drawn from them, making the data meaningful and persuasive.

Evidence-Based Argument

An argument built upon specific, verifiable facts, data, research, and expert testimony, allowing the reader to draw logical conclusions.

Capacity Factor

(In the context of the example) A measure of the actual energy output of a power plant over a period, divided by its maximum possible output over the same period.

LCOE (Levelized Cost of Energy)

(In the context of the example) The net present value of the unit cost of electricity over the lifetime of a generating asset.

The Core Challenge: Balancing Technical and Persuasive Writing

Too much technical detail

results in a "dry reference manual" that loses the reader's interest and fails to convey the significance of the information.

Too much persuasion

makes the document sound like a "sales brochure" that lacks credibility and trustworthiness because it prioritizes selling over factual accuracy.

The Goal

To produce "authoritative writing" that guides the reader to a desired conclusion through sound evidence and analysis, without appearing manipulative.
