Important Terms (1/2)

Abstract

A short summary, typically 150–250 words, that provides readers with the key points of a document (problem, findings, recommendation) before they read the full text.

Statement of Work (SOW)

A detailed description of the tasks, deliverables, and timeline for a project, often included in proposals and contracts (though not a primary white paper section).

Methodology

The systematic approach or set of methods used to conduct research, gather data, or solve a problem.

Primary Source

Original, firsthand evidence such as interviews, surveys, experiments, or direct observations.

Secondary Source

A source that analyzes, interprets, or summarizes information from primary sources, such as textbooks, review articles, or news reports.

Important Terms (2/2)

Credibility

The quality of being trusted and believed, based on expertise, evidence, and reliability.

Authority

Recognized expertise or official standing that gives weight to a person's or organization's claims on a subject.

Technical Feasibility

The practical possibility of building, implementing, or carrying out a proposed solution with available technology, resources, and expertise.

Introduction to White Papers

Definition

An authoritative, in-depth report (5-15 pages) presenting a well-researched argument or solution to a complex problem.

Purpose

To present evidence and analysis to build a persuasive case on a complex issue. Unlike a proposal that states intent, a white paper explains "what the evidence shows, and what it means."

Applications

Used across industries (technology, government, nonprofits) for explaining innovations, justifying policies, or advocating for funding.
