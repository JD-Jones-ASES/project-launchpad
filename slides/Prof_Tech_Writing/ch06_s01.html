Key Concepts (2/2)

Importance of Documentation

Keeping copies of all correspondence, receipts, and case numbers creates a crucial paper trail, essential for tracking progress, follow-up, or escalation if the issue remains unresolved.

Important Terms

Complaint Letter

A formal written communication from a customer detailing a problem with a product, service, or billing, with the goal of achieving a specific resolution.

Adjustment Letter

A formal written response from a business to a customer's complaint, acknowledging the issue, explaining its cause, and outlining the steps taken to resolve it.

Resolution

The specific outcome or action desired by the complainant, or provided by the business, to rectify a problem (e.g., a refund, replacement, repair, or credit).

Good-Faith Effort

Actions taken by a customer to try and resolve a problem through standard channels (e.g., contacting customer service) before resorting to a formal complaint letter.
