Important Terms (1/3)

Presentation

A spoken communication, usually supported by visual aids, delivered to an audience for a specific purpose.

Slide deck

The collection of slides used as visual support during a presentation.

Key message

The single most important idea you want your audience to remember after your presentation.

Audience engagement

Techniques and strategies that capture and hold the audience's attention.

Call to action

A clear statement telling the audience what you want them to do next.

Important Terms (2/3)

Talking points

Brief phrases or notes that remind a speaker of the main ideas to cover.

Template

A pre-designed slide layout that provides consistent formatting across a presentation.

Transition (presentation)

A verbal or visual bridge that connects one section of a presentation to the next.

Visual hierarchy

The arrangement of elements on a slide so that the most important information stands out first.

Handout

A printed or digital document given to the audience summarizing the presentation's key points.

Important Terms (3/3)

Speaker notes

Written prompts visible only to the presenter, used to guide delivery without reading slides.

Bullet point

A short phrase or sentence in a list, marked by a dot or symbol, used to organize information on slides.

Q&A

The question-and-answer period following a presentation, where the audience asks questions and the speaker responds.

Bridge (rhetorical)

A technique for redirecting a conversation or question back to your key message.
