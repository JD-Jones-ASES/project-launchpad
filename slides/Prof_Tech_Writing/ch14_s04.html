Crafting Effective Speaker Notes for Presentations

Key Concepts (1/2)

Speaker Notes vs. Scripts

Speaker notes are brief prompts (key phrases, data points, reminders) visible only to the presenter, guiding natural delivery. A script is a word-for-word manuscript to be read aloud.

Important Terms

Speaker Notes

Written prompts or bullet points, visible only to the presenter, used to guide their delivery of a presentation without reading directly from the slides. They help maintain a natural, conversationa...

Script

A complete, word-for-word manuscript of a presentation or speech that is intended to be read aloud.

Prompts

Short, concise cues or reminders used in speaker notes to trigger a memory or idea without providing the full text.

Transition Sentence

A sentence or phrase used in a presentation to smoothly connect the content of one slide or topic to the next, helping the audience follow the logical progression of ideas.

Timing Note

A brief reminder within speaker notes indicating the planned duration for discussing a specific slide or point, helping the presenter manage their overall presentation time.

Crafting Effective Transitions

Cause-effect

"Because X happened, the next question is Y."

Contrast

"While A costs this much, B offers similar results for less."

Sequence

"Now that we've covered step 1, let's move to step 2."

Question

"So, we know X and Y. The next question is: How do we start?"
