Mastering Internal vs. External Communication

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Internal Communication

Messages exchanged among people within the same organization (e.g., employees, managers). These messages often assume shared knowledge and can be direct and operational.

Three Key Differences

Stakeholder

Any individual or group affected by or having an interest in an organization's decisions, actions, or communications. Stakeholders can be internal (employees) or external (customers, investors).

Message Adaptation

The process of tailoring information (facts, tone, detail, framing) to suit the specific needs and background of a particular audience.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Dissemination

The act of widely distributing or spreading information to ensure all relevant parties receive it. It implies not just sending, but ensuring receipt and awareness.

Compliance

The act of adhering to rules, policies, regulations, or instructions set by an authority. Memos requiring compliance are not optional and create an expectation of adherence.

Important Terms (1/2)

Internal communication

A message intended for people within the same organization.

External communication

A message intended for people outside the organization.

Stakeholder

Any person or group that has an interest in or is affected by an organization's decisions, actions, or communications.

Policy

An official rule or guideline established by an organization to govern behavior, decisions, or operations.

Procedure

A specific series of steps to be followed in a defined order to accomplish a task or comply with a policy.

Important Terms (2/2)

Disseminate

To distribute or spread information widely, especially to ensure all relevant parties receive it.

Compliance

The act of following rules, policies, regulations, or instructions as required by an authority.

Memorandum (memo)

A formal written message used for internal communication within an organization.

Header block

The formatted section at the top of a memo containing the To, From, Date, and Subject (RE) fields.

Define 'stakeholder' in the context of organizational communication.

A stakeholder is any person or group that has an interest in or is affected by an organization's decisions, actions, or communications.

Provide examples of both internal and external stakeholders for a company.

Internal stakeholders include employees. External stakeholders include customers, investors, regulators, and the general public.
