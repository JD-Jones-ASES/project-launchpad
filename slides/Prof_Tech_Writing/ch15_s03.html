Important Terms (1/3)

Technical Proposal

A detailed, formal document that presents a proposed solution to a technical problem, including methodology, qualifications, timeline, and budget, often submitted in response to a Request for Propo...

Request for Proposal (RFP)

A document issued by an organization to solicit proposals from various vendors or contractors for a specific project or service.

Executive Summary

A concise, one- to two-page overview of a technical proposal that summarizes the problem, proposed solution, qualifications, and expected outcomes, designed to be read independently.

Problem Statement / Needs Analysis

The section of a proposal that demonstrates a deep understanding of the client's problem, often by restating it in the proposer's own words and adding expert insight.

Proposed Solution / Technical Approach

The core section of a proposal that explains *what* will be done and *how* it will be done, detailing methods, tools, and processes.

Important Terms (2/3)

Statement of Work (SOW)

A detailed breakdown of tasks, deliverables, milestones, timelines, and acceptance criteria for a project, serving as a contractual description of the work.

Methodology

The specific approach or system of methods used to perform the work, including an explanation of why these methods are appropriate and how quality will be ensured.

Qualifications and Experience

The skills, training, certifications, and past project experience that demonstrate a person's or organization's ability to perform a task or fulfill a role.

Management Plan

A section of a proposal that explains how the project will be organized, supervised, and controlled, including roles, communication methods, and risk management.

Deliverables

The tangible or intangible goods or services that will be provided to the client as part of a project, as specified in the Statement of Work.

Important Terms (3/3)

Acceptance Criteria

The specific standards or conditions that must be met for a deliverable or project phase to be considered complete and satisfactory by the client.
