Connecting Problem to Solution

Crucial Link

Every element of your solution must be directly traceable back to a specific aspect of the problem you identified.

Common Mistake: The Disconnected Solution

Describing a problem in detail but then proposing a solution that doesn't fully address all the aspects of that problem. This leaves the reader with lingering questions.

Fix

After drafting, review your solution against your problem statement. Use explicit connecting phrases (e.g., "To address the 31% of students who cited long wait times...") to make the link undeniable.
