Key Concepts

Definition of Technical Description

A written explanation of an object, mechanism, or system that conveys its physical characteristics, components, and function with enough detail for the reader to understand or visualize it.

Standard Structure for Technical Descriptions

Describing Mechanisms and Systems

While still general-to-specific, these descriptions must emphasize how parts interact, transferring motion, force, or energy, rather than just listing them.

Important Terms (1/3)

Specification

A detailed description of the requirements, dimensions, materials, or performance standards that a product or system must meet.

Requirement

A specific condition or capability that a product, system, or document must satisfy.

Tolerance

The acceptable range of variation in a measurement or dimension (e.g., ± 0.5 mm).

Nominal

The intended or named value of a measurement, as opposed to the actual measured value.

Dimension

A measurable physical quantity such as length, width, height, or diameter.

Important Terms (2/3)

Schematic

A simplified diagram that shows the arrangement and connections of components in a system.

Cross-section

A view of an object as if it has been cut through to reveal its internal structure.

Bill of Materials (BOM)

A complete list of parts, components, and raw materials needed to build a product.

Shall/Should/May

Formal terms used in specifications to indicate:

Compliance

Meeting all the requirements stated in a specification or standard.

Important Terms (3/3)

Metric

A standard unit of measurement, or a measurable indicator used to evaluate performance.

Parameter

A measurable characteristic or variable that defines the boundaries of a system or process.

Threshold

The minimum or maximum acceptable value for a measurement or performance indicator.

Criteria

The standards or benchmarks used to evaluate whether something meets requirements (singular: criterion).

Mechanism

A system of parts working together to perform a function, such as a lock or a gear train.

Organizing a Technical Description: General-to-Specific

Spatial

Describing parts based on their physical arrangement (e.g., left to right, top to bottom).

Functional

Describing parts based on their importance or role in the object's operation.

Sequential

Describing parts in the order they are assembled or encountered during use.

A Complete Technical Description: The Stapler Example

Overview

Introduces the stapler, its model number, purpose, capacity, staple type, and overall dimensions/weight. This is the broadest view.

Body

Describes the two main assemblies (upper arm, base) and their construction material and connection. This zooms in slightly from the whole.

Upper Arm

Focuses specifically on this major component, detailing its internal parts (staple channel, pusher, strike plate) and their individual functions.

Base

Describes the other major component, detailing the anvil and its settings, as well as the non-slip pad.
