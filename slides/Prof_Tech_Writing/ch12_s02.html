Important Terms (1/2)

Action Verb

A specific, concrete verb used to describe an applicant's direct involvement and what they did (e.g., organized, designed, led).

Quantify

To express a result or accomplishment using numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, or other measurable terms to provide concrete evidence of impact.

Resume Deadwood

Vague or passive phrases that take up space on a resume without communicating specific value or accomplishments (e.g., responsible for, duties included, helped with).

Passive Description

A statement that describes a general role or responsibility without detailing the specific actions taken or results achieved.

Active Accomplishment

A statement that clearly describes a specific action performed by the applicant and its measurable outcome or impact, usually starting with an action verb.

Important Terms (2/2)

Power Verb

A strong, vivid action verb chosen to make resume descriptions more impactful, specific, and compelling.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the fundamental difference between a weak resume and a strong one?

A weak resume tells the reader what the job was, while a strong resume tells what the applicant did and what happened because of it.

What is an 'action verb' in the context of a resume?

An action verb describes a specific, concrete action taken by the applicant, such as 'organized,' 'designed,' or 'led.'

What does it mean to 'quantify' a result on a resume?

To express a result or accomplishment using numbers, percentages, dollar amounts, or other measurable terms.

Transform the weak resume line 'Responsible for cash register' into a strong, active accomplishment.

Processed an average of 85 transactions per shift with 99.8% accuracy.

What do common passive phrases like 'Responsible for,' 'Duties included,' or 'Helped with' communicate on a resume?

They describe a role or where you stood, not what specific actions you took or what you accomplished.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Define 'resume deadwood' and provide an example.

Resume deadwood refers to phrases (e.g., 'responsible for,' 'duties included,' 'helped with') that take up space without communicating value or accomplishments.

Convert the passive resume line 'Responsible for taking orders and handling the cash register at a busy restaurant' into an active accomplishment.

What is the primary benefit of using strong action verbs on your resume?

Strong action verbs make your resume vivid and specific, clearly illustrating your contributions and impact.

List three strong action verbs from the 'Problem-Solving' category.
