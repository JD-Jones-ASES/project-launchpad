The Professional Email: Mastering Tone by Recipient

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Matching Tone to Recipient

Professional tone is not a one-size-fits-all setting; it's a spectrum adjusted based on specific factors to ensure your message is received positively.

Factors Influencing Tone

Benefits of Proper Tone

Getting the tone right shows respect, builds trust, and increases the likelihood that recipients will help you or respond favorably.

General Principles for All Recipients

Across all categories, effective professional emails state their purpose clearly, provide sufficient context for the reader to respond, and match the level of formality to the existing relationship.

Important Terms

Professional Tone

The overall attitude, style, and formality of your writing, which changes depending on the communication situation and recipient.

Power Dynamic

The relationship between individuals based on authority, influence, or hierarchical position (e.g., supervisor-employee, teacher-student).

Internal Jargon

Specialized words or phrases used by a particular group or profession that are often not understood by outsiders.

Text-speak

Informal abbreviations, acronyms, and phonetic spellings commonly used in text messaging (e.g., "u" for "you," "lol").

Cold Email

An unsolicited email sent to someone you don't know and who isn't expecting to hear from you, typically with the goal of making a new connection or request.
