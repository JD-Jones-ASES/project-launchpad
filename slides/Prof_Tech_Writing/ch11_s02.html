Important Terms (1/2)

Table of Contents (TOC)

A list, usually at the beginning of a document, that outlines its sections and their corresponding page numbers or links, providing an overview and navigation aid.

Cross-references

Internal links or explicit mentions that direct readers to related content elsewhere within the same document, often using section numbers or specific headings for precision.

Vague Headings

General or uninformative section titles (e.g., "Introduction," "Other Stuff") that do not clearly tell the reader what content will be found within that section.

Specific Headings

Clear, descriptive, and often task-oriented section titles (e.g., "Entering and Editing Grades," "Troubleshooting Common Problems") that accurately preview the content for the reader.

Self-contained Piece

A unit of information (a "chunk") that provides all necessary context and details for its specific topic or task without requiring the reader to consult other sections immediately.

Important Terms (2/2)

One Question Test

A method to evaluate if a "chunk" of information is appropriately scoped by asking if its heading can effectively answer a single user question (e.g., "How Do I Reset My Password?").

Exemplary Model Document Practices (Gradebook Pro Guide)

Setting Expectations

Clearly states the estimated time commitment for completing the task ("About 15 minutes").

Listing Prerequisites

Provides a "What You Will Need" section to ensure users gather necessary items before beginning, preventing frustration.

Action-Oriented Formatting

Uses numbered steps for actions ("Step 1: Log In for the First Time") and bullet points for lists of information or prerequisites.

Strategic Cross-references

Includes a "What's Next?" section that guides users to related tasks or further information using specific section references.

Providing Support

Clearly lists a help contact for users encountering unresolvable issues.
