Important Terms (1/3)

Proposal

A written plan that identifies a problem, presents a solution, and requests permission or funding to carry it out.

Solicited

Requested by someone; a solicited proposal responds to a formal invitation or specific need.

Unsolicited

Not requested; an unsolicited proposal is sent on your own initiative to address a problem you have identified.

RFP (Request for Proposal)

A formal document from an organization inviting proposals for a specific project.

Scope of work

The specific tasks, deliverables, and boundaries of a proposed project.

Important Terms (2/3)

Budget

A detailed financial plan showing expected costs for a project, typically broken down by category.

Timeline

A schedule showing when each phase of a project will be completed, including key dates.

Milestone

A significant checkpoint, achievement, or turning point in a project's timeline.

Deliverable

A tangible product, service, or result that must be provided at the end of a project or project phase.

Stakeholder

Anyone who is affected by or has an interest in a project's outcome.

Important Terms (3/3)

ROI (Return on Investment)

The benefit gained from an investment compared to the cost spent, often expressed as a ratio or percentage.

Cost-benefit

An analysis comparing the costs of a project to its expected benefits to determine its overall value.

Feasibility

Whether a plan is realistic and achievable given available resources, time, and constraints.

Implementation

The process of putting a plan or proposal into action.

Internal vs. External Proposals

Internal Proposals

These are written for individuals or departments within your own organization (e.g., a manager, school principal, or executive board).

External Proposals

These are addressed to individuals or organizations outside your own (e.g., a potential client, a grant-making foundation, or a city council).

Solicited vs. Unsolicited Proposals

Solicited Proposals

These are direct responses to a formal invitation or request.

Unsolicited Proposals

These are initiated by you, without any prior request from the recipient.
