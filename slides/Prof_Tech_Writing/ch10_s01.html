Mastering Proposals: Budgets and Timelines

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Budgets as Practical Plans

A budget moves a proposal beyond a "wish list" by detailing expected costs, fostering transparency, and showing practical planning.

Budget Principles

Key guidelines for creating a credible budget, including specificity, honesty, completeness, transparent calculations, and incorporating a contingency.

Timelines for Project Execution

A timeline maps out the project's phases from start to finish, using milestones and deliverables to show progress and accountability.

Cost-Benefit Analysis and ROI

Beyond just listing costs, proposals should explain *why* the project is a worthwhile investment, translating benefits into tangible value, often in financial terms (Return on Investment).

Stakeholder Consideration

A strong proposal identifies and addresses the concerns and interests of all individuals or groups affected by or invested in the project's outcome.

Key Concepts (2/2)

The Complete Proposal Flow

A structured process for developing a proposal, from initial research to final review and presentation.

Important Terms (1/2)

Milestone

A significant checkpoint or achievement in a project's timeline; a moment where something important is completed or a decision must be made.

Deliverable

A tangible product or result that must be provided at the end of a project or project phase (e.g., a report, a trained group, a prototype).

Cost-Benefit Analysis

An analysis comparing the costs of a project to its expected benefits to determine its overall value.

ROI (Return on Investment)

A measure of the benefit gained compared to the cost spent; often expressed as a percentage or ratio.

Stakeholder

Anyone who is affected by or has an interest in a project's outcome.

Important Terms (2/2)

Implementation

The process of putting a plan into action.
