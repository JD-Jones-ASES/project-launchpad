Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is it important to include a 'range of document types' in your portfolio?

To show variety in your skills and prove you can write more than just one type of document (e.g., emails), demonstrating broader capabilities to the reader.

When selecting pieces based on 'quality of writing,' which principles from the course should you look for?

Clear audience awareness, concise language, precise details, logical structure, and a persuasive yet professional tone.

How can a portfolio effectively demonstrate a writer's 'growth'?

By including both an earlier piece and a later, more polished piece, showing measurable improvement in skill over time.

Describe the 'Would I Send This?' test for evaluating potential portfolio pieces.

For each piece you consider, ask yourself: 'Would I actually send this to a real boss, client, or teacher?'

What action should you take if a piece fails the 'Would I Send This?' test?

The piece either needs more substantial revision, or it should be replaced with a stronger selection.

List the four minimum components required for organizing a professional writing portfolio.

1. A cover page. 2. A table of contents. 3. Brief introductions for each piece. 4. The documents themselves.
