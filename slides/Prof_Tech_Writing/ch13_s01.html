Analytical Reports

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Analytical vs. Informational Reports

Informational reports state facts ("what are the facts?"), while analytical reports interpret facts, identify patterns, and draw conclusions ("what do the facts mean?").

Key Concepts (2/2)

Comparison Tables

A highly effective tool for presenting numerical or categorical data, allowing readers to quickly compare different options across various criteria.

Avoiding Bias

It is crucial for analytical reports to present all relevant data, even if it contradicts a personal preference, to maintain credibility and provide an honest assessment.

Important Terms (1/4)

Analytical Report

A report that examines data or a situation, identifies patterns, and draws conclusions about what the information means.

Recommendation Report

An analytical report that adds specific actions the reader should take based on the conclusions.

Criteria

The standards or measures used to evaluate options or judge quality; singular: **criterion**.

Findings

The specific facts, data points, and observations discovered during research or investigation.

Conclusions

The interpretations or judgments drawn from the findings—what the data means.

Important Terms (2/4)

Recommendations

Specific actions suggested based on the conclusions; what should be done.

Methodology

The systematic approach or methods used to gather and analyze information.

Data Visualization

The use of charts, graphs, tables, and diagrams to present data in a visual format.

Bar Chart

A chart using rectangular bars to compare quantities across categories.

Line Chart

A chart using connected data points to show trends over time.

Important Terms (3/4)

Pie Chart

A circular chart divided into slices to show proportions of a whole.

Trend

A general direction or pattern in data over time.

Correlation

A relationship between two variables where changes in one are associated with changes in the other.

Bias

A systematic error or distortion that skews results or interpretations in a particular direction, often due to a preconceived notion.

Benchmark

A standard or reference point used for comparison and evaluation.

Important Terms (4/4)

Metric

A specific, measurable value used to track performance or progress.
