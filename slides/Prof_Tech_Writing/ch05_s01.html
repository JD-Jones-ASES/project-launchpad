Key Concepts (1/2)

The Importance of Precision

In technical and professional writing, precise language is crucial to prevent misunderstandings, errors, and financial costs. It's not just about numbers, but about every word choice.

Eliminating Weasel Words

Weasel words are vague, hedging terms that sound meaningful but lack specific information. They force readers to guess and should be removed or replaced with concrete details.

The "Compared to What?" Test

When using comparative terms (e.g., "faster," "heavier"), always ensure a clear reference point is provided to give the comparison context and meaning.

Choosing the Right Level of Detail

The amount of detail required in a document depends entirely on its purpose and its intended audience. Providing too much or too little detail signals a lack of understanding of the document's role.

The Foundation of Technical Communication: Precision

Beyond Numbers

While technical documents are known for precise measurements and exact materials, precision extends to the words chosen.

Consequences of Vagueness

Vague language in professional and technical contexts leads to wasted time, costly mistakes, and overall inefficiency.

Eliminating Weasel Words

Definition

Weasel words are ambiguous, non-committal terms that lack specific meaning (e.g., "fairly," "somewhat," "pretty much," "approximately" without an unknown value).

The Problem

They allow writers to avoid making specific claims, leaving readers to interpret or guess the intended meaning.
