Key Concepts

Consistency Builds Credibility

Uniform formatting, language, and presentation across documents signal attention to detail, organization, and professionalism, which enhances a reader's trust in your work.

Purpose of Conventions

Agreed-upon rules (conventions) for formatting, punctuation, and usage ensure uniformity, allowing readers to focus on the content without being distracted by shifting styles.

Systematic Application

Applying a style sheet is best done as a dedicated pass, separate from content revision or proofreading, often utilizing search functions to catch inconsistencies efficiently.

Start Early

Developing a style sheet at the beginning of a project, rather than waiting until the end, helps prevent "style drift" and simplifies final consistency checks.

Important Terms (1/2)

Style Guide

A document that establishes rules for formatting, punctuation, terminology, and other conventions to ensure consistency across a set of documents or an organization's publications.

Consistency

Uniformity in formatting, language, and presentation across all documents in a set or within an organization; it is the primary goal of using a style guide.

Conventions

Agreed-upon rules for formatting, punctuation, and usage that a group or individual follows for the sake of uniformity and clarity.

Style Sheet

A simplified, typically one-page, reference document listing the specific formatting conventions used in a particular document or set of documents, such as a professional portfolio.

Oxford Comma (Serial Comma)

A comma placed before the coordinating conjunction (usually "and" or "or") in a list of three or more items (e.g., "red, white, and blue").

Important Terms (2/2)

Title Case

A capitalization style for titles and headings where all major words (nouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, pronouns, and some conjunctions) are capitalized.

Sentence case

A capitalization style for titles and headings where only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized, similar to a regular sentence.

What is a Style Guide?

• Punctuation (e.g., comma usage, spaces after periods)

• Terminology (e.g., how to refer to specific company names or technical terms)

• Grammar and usage (e.g., active vs. passive voice preferences)

Inconsistent

Consistent

"The meeting is on March 15 at 3:00 p.m. We need four copies of the document by Monday. Please contact Dr. Williams, Ms. Chen, or Mr. Nakamura-Torres. Bring the Q3 Report and the Budget Summary."

How does a portfolio style sheet differ from a full organizational style guide?

What is one essential formatting decision a portfolio style sheet should address regarding dates?

Choosing a consistent date format, such as 'March 15, 2026' (spelled out) or '03/15/2026' (numeric), with professional writing typically preferring to spell ...

What is an important decision for number conventions to include in a style sheet?

Deciding whether to spell out numbers one through nine and use numerals for 10 and above, or to use numerals throughout.

What aspect of time presentation should be standardized in a style sheet?

Choosing a consistent time format, such as '3:00 PM,' '3:00 p.m.,' or '15:00'.

What two elements related to titles and headings should a style sheet specify?

Capitalization rules (e.g., Title Case vs. Sentence case) and formatting (e.g., bold, italic, or underlined).

What punctuation and grammar choices should be made for lists in a style sheet?

Whether to end bullet points with periods, and whether to use full sentences or fragments in bullet lists.
