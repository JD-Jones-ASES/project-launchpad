Style Guide Basics for Your Professional Portfolio

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Importance of Consistency

Uniformity in formatting, language, and presentation across documents is crucial. Inconsistency can suggest carelessness and distract readers from content.

Consistency Builds Credibility

A well-structured, consistently formatted portfolio signals attention to detail, organization, and professionalism, thereby enhancing the writer's credibility.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Preventing "Style Drift"

Writing down your stylistic decisions in a style sheet helps prevent accidental changes in conventions midway through a project, ensuring sustained uniformity.

Important Terms (1/2)

Style Guide

A document that establishes rules for formatting, punctuation, terminology, and other conventions to ensure consistency across a set of documents or an organization's publications.

Consistency

Uniformity in formatting, language, and presentation across all documents in a set or organization.

Conventions

Agreed-upon rules for formatting, punctuation, and usage that a group follows for uniformity.

Style Sheet

A one-page reference listing the specific formatting conventions used in a particular document or set of documents, typically for personal or smaller-scale projects.

Oxford Comma (Serial Comma)

A comma used before the conjunction (and/or) in a list of three or more items (e.g., "red, white, and blue"). A common style preference.

Important Terms (2/2)

Title Case

A capitalization style for titles and headings where all major words are capitalized (e.g., "The Quick Brown Fox").

Sentence Case

A capitalization style for titles and headings where only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized (e.g., "The quick brown fox").

Dedicated Pass

A specific review stage where the writer focuses exclusively on checking for style and formatting consistency, separate from reviewing content or grammar.

Example: Inconsistent vs. Consistent Style

Inconsistent

"The meeting is on march 15th at 3PM. We need 4 copies of the doc by Mon. Please contact Dr. Williams, Sarah Chen, or Mr. Nakamura-Torres. Bring the Q3 Report and the budget summary."

Consistent

"The meeting is on March 15 at 3:00 p.m. We need four copies of the document by Monday. Please contact Dr. Williams, Ms. Chen, or Mr. Nakamura-Torres. Bring the Q3 Report and the Budget Summary."
