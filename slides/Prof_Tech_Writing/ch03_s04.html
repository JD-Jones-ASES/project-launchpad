What Are Instructions?

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Instructions vs. Descriptions

Instructions tell someone how to do something (action-oriented, for the doer), while process descriptions tell someone how something works (for understanding, not necessarily performing).

Instructions are for the Doer

The primary purpose of instructions is to guide an individual through a task, meaning every part of the instruction set should facilitate action.

Importance of Good Instructions

Clear, accurate instructions are vital across all fields (healthcare, engineering, technology, etc.) to ensure tasks are completed correctly, safely, and efficiently. Bad instructions lead to waste...

The End User

Effective instruction writing always starts with a clear understanding of the specific person who will follow the instructions, including their prior knowledge and available tools.

Building Blocks of Instructions

A complete set of instructions typically includes a clear title, an introduction, prerequisites, numbered steps, warnings/cautions/notes, and a completion statement.

Important Terms (1/3)

Imperative mood

A verb form that gives a direct command or instruction (e.g., “Click the button,” “Remove the cap”).

Procedure

A set of steps performed in a specific order to complete a task or achieve a result.

Step

A single action within a procedure.

Substep

A smaller action nested within a main step.

Prerequisite

Something that must be true or done before the instructions can begin (e.g., materials, tools, permissions, knowledge).

Important Terms (2/3)

Caution

A notice about a risk of minor injury or equipment damage.

Warning

A notice about a risk of serious injury or major damage.

Note

Additional information that is helpful but not required to complete the step.

Sequential

Arranged in a specific order where each step depends on the previous one.

Parallel

Steps or tasks that can be performed at the same time or in any order.

Important Terms (3/3)

End user

The person who will actually use a product or follow the instructions.

Usability

How easy and effective something is to use.

Troubleshooting

The process of identifying and solving problems.

Revision

The process of reviewing and improving a piece of writing.

From assembling furniture and cooking meals to setting up new devices and conducting scientific experiments, instructions are a ubiquitous part of daily life.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Why is it crucial to test instructions with someone else, even if they appear perfect on paper?

You have no idea whether instructions actually work until someone else tries to follow them. Writers unconsciously skip things that feel obvious to them.

What is the primary characteristic to look for when selecting a tester for a Fresh-Eyes Test?

Choose someone who has never done the task before. The more unfamiliar the tester is, the better the test will be at revealing gaps.

During a Fresh-Eyes Test, what should you strictly avoid doing when presenting the task to the tester?

Do not explain the task verbally or demonstrate it first. Hand them only the written instructions and the necessary materials, then step back.

Key Concepts (2/2)

List three specific behaviors or actions you should take notes on when observing a tester during a Fresh-Eyes Test.

Any three of these: Pauses or re-reads a step, does something out of order, does something unintended, asks a question, makes an error, or skips a step.

What is the purpose of the 'debrief' stage after a Fresh-Eyes Test?

To gather the tester's invaluable perspective by asking them what was confusing, what was helpful, and what they would change about the instructions.

What is the final, crucial step after debriefing a tester in a Fresh-Eyes Test, and how often should it ideally be done?

Revise the instructions based on observations. If possible, test again. Professional technical writers often test and revise three or more times.

What is the most common problem revealed by testing instructions?

Missing steps. Writers know the task so well that they unconsciously skip steps, and testing reveals these gaps.
