Instructions are for the Doer

The primary purpose of instructions is to guide an individual through a task, step-by-step, ensuring they can successfully perform it. Every sentence should directly facilitate action.

Importance of Good Instructions

The End User

This is the specific person who will follow the instructions. Understanding their existing knowledge, skills, and available tools is paramount for writing effective, accessible instructions.

Building Blocks of Instructions

Effective instructions share common components: a clear title, an introduction, prerequisites, numbered action-oriented steps, appropriately placed warnings/cautions/notes, and a completion statement.

Important Terms (1/3)

Imperative mood

A verb form that gives a direct command or instruction (e.g., “Click the button,” “Remove the cap”).

Procedure

A set of steps performed in a specific order to complete a task or achieve a result.

Step

A single action within a procedure.

Substep

A smaller action nested within a main step.

Prerequisite

Something that must be true or done before the instructions can begin (e.g., materials, tools, permissions, prior knowledge).

Important Terms (2/3)

Caution

A notice about a risk of minor injury or equipment damage.

Warning

A notice about a risk of serious injury or major damage.

Note

Additional information that is helpful but not required to complete the step.

Sequential

Arranged in a specific order where each step depends on the previous one.

Parallel

Steps or tasks that can be performed at the same time or in any order.

Important Terms (3/3)

End user

The person who will actually use a product or follow the instructions.

Usability

How easy and effective something is to use.

Troubleshooting

The process of identifying and solving problems.

Revision

The process of reviewing and improving a piece of writing.

The Real-World Importance of Good Instructions

Healthcare

Nurses write post-discharge care instructions for patients; clarity prevents complications like infections.

Engineering

Technicians write procedures for calibrating equipment; accuracy ensures correct readings and functionality.

Technology

Software companies write setup guides for apps; ease of use drives adoption and positive reviews.

Food Service

Restaurants create food preparation procedures; adherence to safety steps protects customers.

Manufacturing

Engineers write assembly instructions for factory workers; clarity prevents defective products and workplace injuries.
