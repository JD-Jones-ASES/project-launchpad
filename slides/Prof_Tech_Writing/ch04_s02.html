Important Terms

Bulletina

A brief, official announcement intended to inform a group about a specific event, change, or piece of news.

Inverted Pyramid

A writing structure that places the most important information first, followed by supporting details, with background and minor details last.

Lead (or Lede)

The opening sentence or paragraph of an announcement or news story, containing the most critical information.

The Nature of Announcements and Updates

Purpose

Unlike memos that request action, announcements and updates primarily serve to inform. Examples include new hires, office closures, or event changes.

Primary Challenge

Getting the target audience to actually read and absorb the information, especially in busy professional environments.

The Inverted Pyramid Structure

Origin

Journalists developed this structure over a century ago to ensure critical news was conveyed immediately.

Core Principle

Flip the traditional essay structure (which builds to a main point) upside down for professional communication.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of an announcement or update memo, distinguishing it from other memos?

Announcements and updates simply inform a group about a specific event, change, or piece of news, rather than requesting action.

What is the biggest challenge when sending announcement-style communications?

The biggest challenge is getting people to actually read them.

How does the Inverted Pyramid differ from a traditional essay structure?

In a traditional essay, you build up to your main point. In the Inverted Pyramid, you flip that structure upside down, putting the main point first.

List the three main components of the Inverted Pyramid in order of importance.

1. Most important information (who, what, when, where, why). 2. Supporting details (context, background). 3. Minor details and background (nice to know, but ...

Why is the Inverted Pyramid especially effective for professional communication like announcements?

It respects the reader's time by ensuring they get the essential information upfront, even if they only skim the communication.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is 'the lead' (or 'lede') in an announcement?

The lead is the opening sentence or paragraph of an announcement or news story that contains the most critical information.

What essential question should a strong lead answer for the reader?

A strong lead should answer the reader's immediate question: 'What do I need to know?'

What is a 'buried lead' in an announcement?

A buried lead occurs when the actual news or most important information is hidden after several sentences of background or unnecessary setup.

What is the problem with a 'buried lead'?

It forces the reader to work to find the essential information, increasing the likelihood they will stop reading before they get the main point.

What is the first step in writing an effective announcement?

Lead with the news: state what is happening, when, and where in your first sentence or two.
