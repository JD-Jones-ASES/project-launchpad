Study Slides

Key Concepts

Bulletins

Brief, official announcements intended to inform a group about a specific event, change, or piece of news. Their primary challenge is ensuring they are actually read.

The Lead (or Lede)

The opening sentence or paragraph of an announcement or news story that contains the most critical information (who, what, when, where, why). A strong lead answers the reader's immediate question: ...

The Buried Lead

A common writing mistake where essential information is hidden or delayed by introductory background or less important details. This hinders a busy reader's ability to quickly grasp the core message.

The "Busy Reader" Test

A practical tip for writers to evaluate their announcements. If a busy colleague reads only the first sentence, will they have the essential information? If not, the opening needs to be rewritten.

Important Terms
