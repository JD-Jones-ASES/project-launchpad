Important Terms (1/2)

Memorandum (Memo)

A formal written message used for internal communication within an organization, signaling that its content is organized, formatted, and important enough to be kept on file.

Header Block

The formatted section at the top of a memo containing the `TO`, `FROM`, `DATE`, and `RE` fields, which provide essential information about the memo's context.

RE Line (Regarding/Subject)

The specific subject line of a memo, similar to an email's subject, used to clearly state the memo's topic and assist with filing and retrieval.

Purpose Statement

The first paragraph of a memo's body, which immediately states the reason for writing and the main point of the message.

Details (Memo Body)

The middle paragraphs of a memo's body, providing the necessary information, context, explanations, and impact of the message.

Important Terms (2/2)

Action or Closing (Memo Body)

The final paragraph of a memo's body, which states any required actions, deadlines, next steps, or provides contact information for questions.

What Is a Memo?

Announcing policies or changes

For example, a new company-wide policy or an update to an existing one.

Requesting action

Directing employees or departments to complete specific tasks or submit information by a deadline.

Summarizing decisions

Documenting the outcome of a meeting, project discussion, or committee review.

Creating a record

Establishing an official written account of events, timelines, or agreements for future reference.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a memorandum (memo)?

A formal written message used for internal communication within an organization.

How does a memo primarily differ from an email in terms of structure and formality?

Memos are more structured and formal, signaling that content is important enough to be organized, formatted, and kept on file, unlike often informal emails.

Name three key purposes that memos serve within an organization.

Announcing policies or changes, requesting action, summarizing decisions, or creating a record.

Under what conditions should you typically use a memo instead of an email?

When the message is (1) directed to a group, (2) important enough to be filed or referenced later, (3) announcing a policy, decision, or formal request, or (...

What is a memo's 'header block'?

The formatted section at the top of a memo containing the To, From, Date, and Subject (RE) fields.

What four pieces of information does a memo's header block provide to the reader?

Who the memo is for (TO), who wrote it (FROM), when it was written (DATE), and what it is about (RE/SUBJECT).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What does 'RE' stand for in the memo header block?

Regarding (sometimes written as SUBJECT).

What is a crucial characteristic of an effective RE line in a memo?

It should be specific and informative, clearly indicating the memo's content, similar to an email's subject line.

Why is it important for the RE line to be specific?

It helps readers immediately understand the memo's topic and allows for easy retrieval if filed later.

What should be clearly specified in the TO field of a memo?

The exact and complete audience, such as 'All Engineering Staff' rather than vague terms like 'Team'.

Why should the sender's title be included in the FROM field of a memo?

The title tells the reader why the sender has the authority to send the message.

How should the DATE field be formatted in a memo to ensure clarity?

Use a complete, unambiguous format, writing out the full month (e.g., 'January 8, 2026,' not '1/8/26').
