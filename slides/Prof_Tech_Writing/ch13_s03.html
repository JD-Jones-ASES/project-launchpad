Understanding Findings, Conclusions, and Recommendations

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Three-Step Logical Chain

The fundamental structure of analytical and recommendation reports, moving from raw data to interpretation to action.

Findings: Factual Reporting

These are objective, verifiable data points, observations, or measurements without any interpretation or opinion. They answer "What did the data show?"

Conclusions: Interpreting the Data

Recommendations: Proposing Action

These are specific, actionable steps derived from the conclusions. They answer "What should we do?" and aim to address the issues or opportunities identified.

The Sorting Test

A practical method to differentiate between findings, conclusions, and recommendations by asking specific questions about each statement's nature (verifiable, interpretative, actionable).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Benchmarks

Standards or reference points used to provide context and meaning to data. They allow for comparison (e.g., industry average, historical data).

Metrics

Specific, measurable values used to track performance or progress over time. Good metrics are relevant and quantifiable.

Context for Numbers

The critical need to provide background for any numerical data presented, including its scale, how it compares to benchmarks, and its trend over time.

Important Terms (1/2)

Finding

A specific, verifiable fact, data point, observation, or measurement collected during research or analysis. It contains no interpretation or opinion.

Conclusion

An interpretation or inference derived from one or more findings, explaining what the data means, identifying patterns, or suggesting causes. It must be supported by evidence.

Recommendation

A specific, actionable step or proposed course of action that logically follows from the conclusions and aims to address a problem or capitalize on an opportunity.

Three-Step Chain (Findings, Conclusions, Recommendations)

The sequential logical progression that underpins effective analytical reports, where each step builds upon the previous one.

The Sorting Test

A diagnostic technique to determine if a statement is a finding, conclusion, or recommendation by evaluating if it can be verified, if it interprets data, or if it proposes an action.

Important Terms (2/2)

Benchmark

A standard or point of reference against which something can be measured or compared to provide context.

Metric

A quantifiable measure used to track and assess the performance, progress, or state of a particular aspect.
