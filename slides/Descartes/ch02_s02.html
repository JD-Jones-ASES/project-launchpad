Descartes’ Method of Doubt – Roots and Legacy

Study Slides

The Pyrrhonist Tradition

Core Argument

For every question regarding science, ethics, or religion, there are equally valid arguments on both sides.

The Goal of Epoche

Because reason cannot resolve these contradictions, the wise person must practice *epoche* (suspension of judgment).

The Result of Ataraxia

By withholding judgment, one achieves *ataraxia*—a state of mental tranquility—by eliminating the anxiety caused by holding uncertain beliefs.

Comparison: Skepticism vs. Cartesian Doubt

Feature. Pyrrhonism

Cartesian Doubt

Objective. To remain in doubt as a final destination.

To use doubt as a tool to find certainty.

Result. *Ataraxia* (tranquility of soul).

An unshakeable foundation for knowledge.

Philosophical Stance. Radical skepticism.

Skepticism in the service of anti-skepticism.

The Augustinian Precedent: *Si Fallor, Sum*

The Argument

In *City of God*, Augustine posited: *"Si enim fallor, sum"* ("If I am mistaken, I exist").

The Logic

A being that does not exist cannot be deceived. Therefore, the very act of being deceived proves the existence of the one being deceived.

David Hume (1711–1776)

Habits of Mind

Hume argued that our beliefs in causation, the external world, and personal identity are not based on rational proof but are merely "habits of mind" provided by nature for utility.

Impact

His work "awakened" Immanuel Kant from his "dogmatic slumber" and scandalized the philosophical establishment.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Academic Skeptics

Members of Plato’s Academy who argued for probabilism and the withholding of assent from claims of certain knowledge.

Ataraxia

A state of tranquility or peace of mind achieved by suspending judgment on unanswerable questions.

Brain in a Vat

A 20th-century thought experiment by Hilary Putnam simulating the "evil genius" scenario through technology rather than deception by a spirit.

Epoche

The Greek term for "suspension" or "withholding"; the practice of refusing to judge contested questions.

Probabilism

The philosophical stance of acting on what is most plausible while acknowledging that certain knowledge is unavailable.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Pyrrho of Elis

The founder of Pyrrhonism who lived out his skepticism by refusing to trust his sensory perceptions.

Si fallor, sum

Latin for "If I am mistaken, I exist/am"—Augustine's argument against skepticism.

Sextus Empiricus

The writer whose rediscovered texts in the 16th century preserved the detailed arguments of the Pyrrhonists.
