Important Terms (1/2)

Pure Intellection (or Conception)

The faculty of understanding or conceiving ideas clearly and distinctly using the mind alone, without relying on sensory input or mental imagery.

Imagination

The faculty of forming mental images; Descartes describes it as the application of the faculty of knowledge to a body immediately present to it, requiring a "particular effort of mind."

Chiliagon

A figure with a thousand sides. Descartes uses it as an example to differentiate imagination from pure intellection, as one can conceive of a chiliagon but cannot distinctly imagine all its sides.

Corporeal Things

Pertaining to the body or physical matter; material things.

Phantom Limb Pain

The sensation of pain in a limb that has been amputated. Descartes uses this phenomenon to illustrate how even internal sensations can be deceptive and disconnect from the actual physical body.
