Study Slides

The Jesuit Education at La Flèche

Classical Languages

Latin and Greek.

Humanities

Logic and rhetoric.

Science and Theology

Mathematics, Aristotelian natural philosophy, and scholastic theology.

Major Works and Later Career

Discourse on the Method (1637)

Published with three scientific essays on optics, meteorology, and geometry.

Meditations on First Philosophy (1641)

His seminal work on epistemology and the mind-body problem. It was notably published with "Objections and Replies," a dialogue between Descartes and other leading thinkers.

Glossary of Key Terms and Figures

Key Concepts (1/2)

In which French town was René Descartes born in 1596?

La Haye en Touraine.

The town of La Haye en Touraine was eventually renamed _____ in honor of its most famous resident.

Descartes.

What was the profession of Descartes' father?

A lawyer and minor nobleman.

Descartes' father served as a councillor in the parliament of which French province?

Brittany.

Who raised Descartes after his mother died when he was just over a year old?

His maternal grandmother.

Which physical condition led Descartes to spend his mornings thinking in bed throughout his life?

A sickly constitution prone to illness.

Key Concepts (2/2)

At what age was Descartes sent to the Jesuit college of La Flèche?

Ten or eleven years old.

Which French monarch founded the prestigious Jesuit college of La Flèche in 1604?

King Henry IV.

How many years did Descartes spend studying at La Flèche?

Eight years.

Besides Latin and Greek, which traditional subjects comprised the curriculum at La Flèche?

Logic, rhetoric, mathematics, Aristotelian natural philosophy, and scholastic theology.

Why was Descartes granted the privilege of sleeping late in the mornings at La Flèche?

His delicate health.

Which specific subject at La Flèche did Descartes recall as the only one offering "genuine certainty"?

Mathematics.
