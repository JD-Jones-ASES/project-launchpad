Important Terms (1/2)

Deceiver

One who intentionally misleads or causes others to err. Descartes argues God cannot be a deceiver due to His perfect nature.

Error

For Descartes, a privation or lack of knowledge, specifically a wrong judgment, rather than an inherent faculty. It implies a deviation from truth.

Perfection

The state of being complete, without defect or flaw. God is defined as supremely perfect.

Non-being (Nought)

The absolute absence of existence or perfection. Humans are seen as intermediate between God (supreme being) and non-being.

Understanding (Intellect)

The mental faculty responsible for apprehending ideas, perceiving concepts, and forming clear and distinct perceptions. It does not affirm or deny on its own.

Important Terms (2/2)

Will (Free Will/Liberty of Choice)

The mental faculty responsible for making judgments, affirming or denying, choosing to act or not to act. Descartes considers it infinite in its scope.

Final Causes

The ultimate purpose or end for which something exists or happens. Descartes suggests that attempting to understand God's final causes is beyond human capacity and "temerity."
