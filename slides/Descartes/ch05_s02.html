The Problem of Evil and the Problem of Error

• Error is not a positive faculty or capacity that God created.

• Instead, error is a privation—an absence of the knowledge, clarity, or proper restraint that should guide our judgment.

• Therefore, God is not responsible for our intellectual mistakes, just as he is not responsible for our moral failings.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Thomas Aquinas, what is the primary nature of truth?

Truth is not primarily a property of ideas in the mind, but a relation: the relation of conformity between the intellect and reality.

What is Aquinas's Latin formula for the correspondence theory of truth?

'Adaequatio intellectus et rei,' meaning the adequation (matching) of the intellect to the thing.

For Aquinas, where does truth primarily reside?

Truth resides primarily in the intellect.

How does Aquinas's criterion for truth differ from Descartes's?

Aquinas held that truth is correspondence between intellect and world, while Descartes held that truth is clarity and distinctness in the mind itself.

What significant shift in the standard of truth marks the transition from Aquinas to early modern philosophy?

The standard of truth moved from the world (Aquinas's correspondence with external reality) to the mind (Descartes's internal clarity and distinctness).
