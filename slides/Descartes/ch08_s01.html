Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Arc of the Meditations

Descartes' deliberate six-part journey from universal doubt to the reconstruction of certain knowledge, demonstrating a systematic approach to philosophy.

Method of Doubt

A systematic philosophical tool where all beliefs are called into question to discover what, if anything, remains undeniably certain.

The Cogito and First-Person Perspective

The foundational discovery that "I think, therefore I am" establishes the existence of the knowing subject as the primary starting point for modern epistemology.

Mind-Body Problem

Descartes' precise articulation of the fundamental challenge of understanding how an immaterial mind can interact with a material body, which remains a central debate.

Criterion of Clarity and Distinctness

The idea that truth is determined by what the mind perceives clearly and distinctly, shifting the arbiter of truth to internal mental perception.

Important Terms (1/3)

Meditations on First Philosophy

Descartes' seminal work, structured as a six-day intellectual journey of doubt and reconstruction.

Evil Genius (or Demon)

A hypothetical, supremely powerful and cunning deceiver introduced in Meditation I to push the method of doubt to its absolute extreme, questioning even mathematical truths.

Cogito

Latin for "I think," central to Descartes' famous dictum "Cogito, ergo sum" ("I think, therefore I am"), establishing the indubitable existence of the thinking self.

Thinking Thing (res cogitans)

Descartes' initial definition of himself as a being whose entire essence is to think, doubt, will, imagine, and perceive.

Wax Argument

An argument in Meditation II used to demonstrate that the mind, not the senses, truly grasps the essential nature (extension, flexibility, changeability) of things.

Ontological Argument

Descartes' second proof of God's existence in Meditation V, arguing that existence is an essential perfection of God, just as having three angles is essential to a triangle.

Pure Extension (res extensa)

Descartes' definition of matter as solely possessing the property of extension (occupying space), knowable by reason alone, devoid of inherent forces.

Mind-Body Distinction

The philosophical idea that the mind (thinking substance) and the body (extended substance) are two fundamentally different kinds of substances.

The Most Enduring Insights

The Method of Doubt

Established as a powerful philosophical tool for critical inquiry, pushing positions to their logical extremes to find bedrock certainty.

The Cogito and First-Person Perspective

Shifted the starting point of epistemology from the external world to the knowing subject, making the mind's internal experience central.

The Mind-Body Problem

Articulated the problem of interaction between mind and body with such precision that it has become a central and enduring challenge for philosophy, neuroscience, and psychology.

The Criterion of Clarity and Distinctness

Introduced an "internalist" picture of knowledge, where the mind itself is the ultimate judge of truth, profoundly influencing the Enlightenment's emphasis on reason.

What radical skeptical hypothesis does Descartes introduce in Meditation I to doubt even mathematical truths?

What is the first foundational certainty Descartes recovers in Meditation II, and what is its famous Latin formulation?

His own existence as a thinking being, encapsulated by 'Cogito, ergo sum' ('I think, therefore I am').
