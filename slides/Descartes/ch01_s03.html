Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Epistemology

The branch of philosophy that studies the nature, sources, scope, and limits of human knowledge.

Foundationalism

The view that all knowledge rests on a small set of "basic" beliefs that are known without inference from other beliefs.

Heterodox

Ideas or beliefs that are at odds with established or accepted doctrines, particularly in a religious context.

Index of Forbidden Books

A list of publications deemed heretical or contrary to morality by the Catholic Church, which Catholics were forbidden from reading.

Scholastic Philosophy

The traditional philosophical system that Descartes sought to replace, which he viewed as having an unreliable foundation.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

The Sorbonne

The faculty of theology in Paris to whom Descartes addressed his dedicatory letter.

Synopsis

A brief summary or general survey; in this context, the section where Descartes outlines the "great arc" of his six-day argument.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary ambition of Descartes' Meditations on First Philosophy?

To rebuild all human knowledge from scratch on a foundation of absolute certainty.

Define foundationalism in the context of epistemology.

The view that all knowledge rests on a small set of basic beliefs known without inference from others.

Who is considered the most famous foundationalist in the Western tradition?

René Descartes.

Under what condition is a belief considered 'basic' in foundationalist theory?

It must be known without being derived or inferred from other beliefs.

Descartes compares the project of rebuilding human knowledge to what structural act?

Demolishing a building with a shaky foundation to rebuild it from the ground up.

Why did Descartes reject the 'old building' of scholastic philosophy?

He considered it too unreliable to repair piece by piece.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How is the literary structure of the Meditations organized?

As a first-person monologue conducted over the course of six days.

What is the pedagogical purpose of casting the Meditations as an interior monologue?

To force the reader to participate in and conduct the philosophical experiment themselves.

Descartes' strategy of stripping away every doubtable belief to reach a bedrock of certainty is known as _____.

Foundationalism.

To which institution's theologians was the dedicatory letter of the Meditations addressed?

The Sorbonne.

Why did Descartes argue that rational proofs of God are more persuasive to unbelievers than scripture?

Unbelievers do not accept scripture as authoritative but cannot dismiss a valid rational argument.

What practical purpose did the dedicatory letter to the Sorbonne serve for Descartes?

It sought to place the work under the Church's protection to avoid the consequences of publishing heterodox ideas.
