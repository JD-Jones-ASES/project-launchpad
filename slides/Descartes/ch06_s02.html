Important Terms (1/3)

Ontological Argument

A philosophical argument for the existence of God based on the concept of God's being or existence.

Anselm of Canterbury (1033–1109)

Italian-born Benedictine monk, Archbishop of Canterbury, theologian, and philosopher. Formulated the ontological argument in his *Proslogium*. Coined *fides quaerens intellectum*.

*Proslogium* (1077–78)

Anselm's work containing his original formulation of the ontological argument.

*Fides quaerens intellectum*

Latin for "faith seeking understanding," Anselm's conviction that reason can illuminate what faith already holds.

"That than which nothing greater can be conceived"

Anselm's definition of God, central to his ontological argument.

Important Terms (2/3)

Gaunilo of Marmoutiers (fl. 1080)

Benedictine monk who authored *On Behalf of the Fool*, offering the famous "perfect island" objection to Anselm's argument.

Thomas Aquinas (1225–1274)

Influential Dominican friar, philosopher, and theologian. A major critic of the ontological argument, known for his *Summa Theologica* and the "Five Ways."

*Summa Theologica*

Aquinas's monumental work of systematic philosophy and theology.

Nominal Definition

A definition that describes a thing by its name or common understanding, without providing a full or intrinsic grasp of its essence.

Essence

What a thing intrinsically is; its fundamental nature or "whatness."

Important Terms (3/3)

Existence

The fact or state of being real or actual; "thatness."

A Priori Argument

An argument whose premises are knowable independently of experience.

A Posteriori Argument

An argument whose premises are based on empirical observation or experience.
