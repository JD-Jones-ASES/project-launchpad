Important Terms (1/2)

Archimedean Point

A hypothetical leverage point from which one could move the Earth; used by Descartes to signify a singular, absolutely certain truth upon which all other knowledge could be built.

Cogito, Ergo Sum

Latin for "I think, therefore I am." Descartes' most famous philosophical statement, representing his first indubitable certainty.

Radical Doubt

The method of systematically questioning and rejecting any belief that admits even the slightest possibility of error, employed by Descartes to uncover foundational truths.

Res Cogitans

Latin for "thinking thing." This is Descartes' conclusion about the essential nature of the self, distinct from a body.

Imagination

Important Terms (2/2)

Intellection (Pure Understanding)

The mind's faculty for grasping concepts and truths directly through thought, without relying on sensory input or imaginative images.

Malicious Demon (or Deceiver)

A hypothetical, all-powerful, and cunning being introduced in Meditation I to push doubt to its extreme, suggesting that all sensory experiences and even mathematical truths could be illusions.
