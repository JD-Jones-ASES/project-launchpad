Key Concepts (2/2)

Hume's Skepticism about Causation

David Hume's argument that we never directly observe necessary connections between events, only constant conjunctions. Our belief in causation arises from habit and custom, not rational insight.

Hume's Skepticism about the Self

Hume's conclusion that introspection reveals only a "bundle of perceptions" in flux, not a permanent, enduring self (the _res cogitans_ Descartes described).

Important Terms (1/2)

Rationalism

The philosophical position that reason is the primary or exclusive source of knowledge, and that at least some genuine knowledge is possible independently of sensory experience.

Empiricism

The philosophical position that all knowledge originates in sensory experience, and that there are no innate ideas.

Innate ideas

Ideas that are present in the mind from birth, or from the mind's very nature, not derived from sensory experience.

_Esse est percipi_

Latin for "To be is to be perceived," George Berkeley's slogan for his idealist philosophy, asserting that physical objects are collections of ideas existing only when perceived.

_Critique of Pure Reason_

Immanuel Kant's monumental work (1781, revised 1787) in which he argued that the mind actively organizes experience through its own forms (space, time, and categories of understanding like causation).

Important Terms (2/2)

Synthetic a priori

A type of judgment identified by Kant that is both independent of experience (a priori) and genuinely informative, adding new knowledge to our understanding.

The two great opposing traditions are Rationalism and Empiricism.

What is the rationalist view concerning innate ideas?

Rationalists hold that the mind comes equipped with innate ideas or innate rational faculties from birth, not derived from sensory experience.

What is the empiricist view concerning innate ideas?

Empiricists reject the existence of innate ideas, positing that the mind begins as a blank slate (tabula rasa) filled only by sensory experience.

Which model for knowledge did Rationalists typically embrace, and why?

Rationalists embraced mathematics as the model for all knowledge because it is seen as certain, universal, and arrived at by pure thought.
