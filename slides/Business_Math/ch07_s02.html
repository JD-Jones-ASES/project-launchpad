Business Applications: Depreciating Business Assets Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Depreciation

The accounting process of allocating the cost of a tangible asset over its useful life. It reflects the asset's loss of value due to wear and tear, obsolescence, or age.

Tax Deduction

Depreciation reduces a business's taxable income, thereby lowering the amount of income tax owed.

Salvage Value

The estimated resale or trade-in value of an asset at the end of its useful life.

Useful Life

The estimated period over which an asset is expected to be productive for a business.

Depreciable Amount (or Depreciable Base)

The portion of an asset's cost that can be depreciated. Calculated as: `Cost - Salvage Value`.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Book Value

The original cost of an asset minus its accumulated depreciation to date.

Straight-Line Depreciation

A method that spreads the depreciable amount evenly over the asset's useful life, resulting in the same depreciation expense each year. Simple and common for financial reporting.

Important Terms (1/2)

Asset

An item of economic value owned by a business, expected to provide future benefit (e.g., vehicles, equipment, computers, furniture).

Depreciation

The systematic reduction in the recorded cost of a fixed asset over its useful life.

Salvage Value

The estimated residual value of an asset at the end of its useful life, after all depreciation has been expensed.

Useful Life

The estimated period (in years, units of production, or other measures) an asset is expected to be used by a business.

Depreciable Amount (Depreciable Base)

The total amount of an asset's cost that will be expensed over its useful life (Cost - Salvage Value).

Important Terms (2/2)

Book Value

The value of an asset as recorded in a company's accounting records, calculated as its original cost minus accumulated depreciation.

Straight-Line Rate

The percentage of an asset's depreciable amount that is expensed each year under the straight-line method (1 / Useful Life).

DDB Rate (Double Declining-Balance Rate)

Twice the straight-line rate, used in the double declining-balance depreciation method.

Accelerated Depreciation

Any depreciation method that records more depreciation expense in the early years of an asset's life than in its later years.
