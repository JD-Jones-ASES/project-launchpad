Key Concepts

The positional value of a digit in a number, determining its magnitude (e.g., ones, tens, hundreds, tenths).

Rounding

The process of approximating a number to a specified place value, making it simpler to work with while remaining close to the original value.

Decimal Operations

Specific rules for adding, subtracting, multiplying, and dividing numbers that include a decimal point, ensuring accuracy in calculations involving parts of whole numbers.

Important Terms (1/3)

Place Value

The value of a digit determined by its position within a number (e.g., in 47, the 4 is in the tens place, meaning 40).

Rounding

The act of replacing a number with a nearby, simpler number, usually to a specified place value.

Rounding Up

Increasing the rounding digit by one when the digit to its right is 5 or greater.

Rounding Down

Keeping the rounding digit the same when the digit to its right is less than 5.

Estimation

A rough calculation or approximation of a value.

Important Terms (2/3)

Front-End Estimation

A method of estimation where numbers are rounded to their leftmost (leading) digit before calculation.

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

An acronym representing the sequence for evaluating mathematical expressions: Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication and Division (from left to right), Addition and Subtraction (from left to right).

Decimal Point

The symbol used to separate the whole number part from the fractional part of a number.

Divisor

The number by which another number (the dividend) is divided.

Dividend

The number being divided in a division problem.

Important Terms (3/3)

Quotient

The result of a division problem.

Factors

The numbers being multiplied together to get a product.

Product

The result of a multiplication problem.

Sum

The result of an addition problem.

Difference

The result of a subtraction problem.

Place Value

Definition

The value a digit holds based on its position in a number. Each place to the left is ten times greater than the place to its right.

Important Note for Money

When working with currency, always round to exactly two decimal places (the nearest cent) unless otherwise instructed. For example, write $$25.50$, not $$25.5$.

Estimation

Purpose

To quickly approximate an answer or check the reasonableness of an exact calculation.

Front-End Estimation

A common method where each number involved in a calculation is rounded to its leading (leftmost) digit before performing the operation.

Example

Estimate the sum $487 + 2,135 + 926$.

Business Application

Useful for tasks like quickly verifying if a supplier's invoice total seems reasonable before a detailed audit.
