Important Terms (1/2)

Data Presentation

The process of displaying information, often numerical, in a visual format like graphs or charts to make it understandable and informative.

Double Bar Chart

A type of bar graph used to compare two sets of data for several categories, with bars for each category placed side-by-side.

Pie Chart

A circular graph divided into slices, illustrating numerical proportion. Each slice represents a category's percentage contribution to the whole.

Market Share

The percentage of total sales within a specific market that a company or product holds.

Central Angle (in a pie chart)

The angle formed at the center of a circle by the two radii that define a slice of the pie chart. It's calculated as (percentage / 100) 360 degrees.

Important Terms (2/2)

Misleading Graph

A graph that intentionally or unintentionally distorts data to create a false impression, often by manipulating axes, scales, or visual elements.

Axis Truncation

A technique where the vertical (or horizontal) axis of a graph does not start at zero, exaggerating the appearance of small differences between data points.

3D Effect Distortion

The use of three-dimensional effects in charts (e.g., 3D pie charts) that can visually distort the size or proportion of data segments, making some appear larger or smaller than they truly are.

Why is it important for businesses to turn raw data into graphs and charts?

Raw numbers alone don't tell a story. Graphs communicate results, aid decision-making, help report outcomes, and identify misleading claims to managers, inve...

What are two key business skills related to data presentation?

Choosing the right type of graph and creating it honestly.

When is a double bar chart an effective tool for business data presentation?

When comparing two or more sets of data (e.g., stores, products, departments) across different categories or time periods at a glance.

What elements are essential for readability in a double bar chart?

Color-coding for different data sets and a clear legend.

What should the vertical axis of a double bar chart always start at, and why?

It should start at $0 to ensure an honest representation of the data and prevent exaggeration of differences.

Hartwell Sporting Goods' Northside store had $265,000 in quarterly sales, and Southside had $224,000. What was the percent difference in sales, using Southside as the base?

($265,000 - $224,000) / $224,000 100% = 41,000 / 224,000 100% ≈ 18.3%.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What type of business data is best represented by a pie chart?

Data that shows parts of a whole, such as market share, budget allocation, or proportions of a total.

How do you calculate the central angle for a slice in a pie chart given its percentage?

Multiply the percentage (as a decimal or whole number) by 3.6 degrees (since 360 degrees / 100% = 3.6 degrees/%).

PeakView Analytics states Java Junction has 32% market share in a $2,500,000 annual market. What is Java Junction's annual revenue?

$2,500,000 0.32 = $800,000.

Morning Brew captures an additional 3% market share, going from 24% to 27%. If the total market is $2,500,000, what is its new annual revenue?

$2,500,000 0.27 = $675,000.

What is a common technique used in graphs to mislead viewers by exaggerating small differences?

Starting the vertical axis at a value other than zero (a truncated axis).
