Business Applications of Interest: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Simple Interest in Business Loans

Used primarily for short-term loans, where interest is calculated only on the original principal amount.

Banker's Year (360 Days)

A common practice in commercial loans where the year is assumed to have 360 days for simpler interest calculations. This affects the time (t) component of the simple interest formula.

Compound Interest in Savings and Loans

Interest is calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods. This leads to faster growth in savings or higher costs in loans over time.

Compounding Frequency

The number of times per year that interest is calculated and added to the principal. Higher frequency generally leads to more interest earned or paid.

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

A standardized measure that reflects the total amount of interest paid on a deposit account, taking into account compounding. It allows for a direct comparison of different savings products.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Certificates of Deposit (CDs)

A type of savings product where money is deposited for a fixed period at a specific interest rate, typically higher than regular savings accounts, with a penalty for early withdrawal.

Comparing Financial Options

The ability to evaluate different loan or investment options by calculating their future value or total interest to identify the most financially advantageous choice.

Important Terms (1/3)

Interest (I)

The cost of borrowing money or the income earned from lending money.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money borrowed or deposited.

Rate (r)

The annual percentage rate of interest, expressed as a decimal.

Time (t)

The duration of the loan or investment, expressed in years.

Maturity Value (A)

The total amount of money due at the end of a loan term or the total value of an investment at the end of its period, including principal and accumulated interest. Also known as Future Value.

Important Terms (2/3)

Banker's Year

A convention where a year is considered to have 360 days for interest calculations, simplifying division.

Compounding Frequency (n)

The number of times per year that interest is compounded (e.g., monthly n=12, quarterly n=4, semiannually n=2, annually n=1).

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

The effective annual rate of return of an investment, taking into account the effect of compounding interest.

Certificate of Deposit (CD)

A type of savings account with a fixed interest rate and term, typically offering higher returns than standard savings but with penalties for early withdrawal.

Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the original principal amount.

Important Terms (3/3)

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.
