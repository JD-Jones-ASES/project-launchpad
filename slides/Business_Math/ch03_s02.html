Scale Factor

A ratio used to multiply ingredient quantities when scaling a recipe up or down. It's calculated by dividing the new desired number of servings by the original number of servings.

Exchange Rate

The value of one country's currency in terms of another currency. It's used to convert prices or amounts between different currencies for international transactions.

Quality Control Sampling

A method used in manufacturing where a small, random sample of products is inspected to estimate the defect rate for an entire production run, allowing businesses to predict overall quality.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Production Ratios

Relationships that define output based on input, such as labor hours per unit or items produced per machine. These ratios help businesses plan production capacity and efficiency.

Blueprint Scale

A ratio that relates measurements on a drawing (like a blueprint) to the actual measurements of the object or space it represents, used to translate designs into real-world dimensions.

Important Terms (1/2)

Proportional Reasoning

A mathematical skill involving the ability to compare quantities using multiplication or division and understand how changes in one quantity affect another proportionally.

Unit Price

The cost of a single unit of a product, calculated by dividing the total price by the number of units.

Scale Factor

The number by which a quantity is multiplied to scale it up or down. In recipes, it's (New Servings / Original Servings).

Exchange Rate

The rate at which one currency can be exchanged for another.

Defect Rate

The percentage or proportion of defective items found within a sample or an entire production run.

Important Terms (2/2)

Proportion

An equation stating that two ratios are equal. Often written as a/b = c/d.

Cross-multiplication

A method for solving proportions where you multiply the numerator of one ratio by the denominator of the other, and vice versa (if a/b = c/d, then ad = bc).
