Chapter 8, Section 1: The Math: Reading and Creating Graphs

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Graphs and Charts as Visual Tools

They convert complex numerical data into easily digestible pictures, facilitating quicker interpretation and storytelling compared to raw tables.

Bar Graphs

Use rectangular bars to visually compare quantities across different categories. They can be vertical or horizontal, and their length/height represents the value.

Line Graphs

Display data points connected by lines, primarily used to illustrate how a quantity changes over a continuous period, revealing trends and rates of change.

Circle Graphs (Pie Charts)

Divide a circle into sectors (slices) where each sector represents a category's proportional share of a whole, with the size of the slice reflecting its percentage.

Misleading Graphs

Graphs intentionally or unintentionally designed to distort data, giving a false impression through visual trickery such as truncated axes or inconsistent scales.

Important Terms

Bar Graph

A graph that uses rectangular bars to compare quantities across different categories. The length or height of each bar is proportional to the value it represents.

Double Bar Graph

A type of bar graph that shows two or more sets of data side-by-side for each category, allowing for direct comparison between groups. Also known as a grouped bar graph.

Line Graph

A graph that displays data points connected by straight line segments, commonly used to show how a quantity changes over time (a time series).

Circle Graph (Pie Chart)

A circular graph divided into sectors (slices), where each sector represents a category's percentage share of a total. The size of each sector is proportional to its percentage.

Misleading Graph

A graph that presents data in a way that gives a false or distorted impression, often through visual manipulation techniques like truncated axes or inappropriate scaling.

Bar Graphs

Title

Explains the overall subject of the graph.

Axis Labels

Describe what each axis represents (e.g., categories on one axis, values on the other).

Scale

The increments of numbers along the value axis, indicating how values are measured.

Legend

Necessary for graphs comparing multiple data sets, indicating what each color or pattern represents.

Line Graphs

Increasing Trend

The line generally moves upward from left to right, indicating growth.

Decreasing Trend

The line generally moves downward from left to right, indicating decline.

Stable (Flat) Trend

The line remains relatively level, showing little change.

Fluctuating Trend

The line moves up and down without a clear long-term direction.

What is the primary purpose of graphs and charts in a business context?

Graphs and charts convert numbers into visual pictures, allowing for instant interpretation and evaluation of data, such as monthly sales figures.

Bar graphs, line graphs, circle graphs (pie charts), and misleading graphs.

Key Concepts (2/2)

If a bar graph shows quarterly sales of $85,000 (Q1) and $130,000 (Q4), what is the difference between the highest and lowest quarter's sales?

The difference is $130,000 - $85,000 = $45,000.

Define a 'Line Graph'.

A line graph displays data points connected by straight line segments and is most commonly used to show how a quantity changes over time (a time series).

In a line graph, what does the horizontal axis typically represent, and what does the vertical axis represent?

The horizontal axis typically represents time, and the vertical axis represents the measured quantity.

How do you interpret an 'increasing trend' versus a 'decreasing trend' on a line graph?

An increasing trend shows the line generally going up from left to right, while a decreasing trend shows the line generally going down from left to right.

What does the steepness (slope) of a line on a line graph indicate?

The steepness of the line indicates the rate of change. A steep upward line signifies rapid growth, while a gentle upward line means slow growth.

What is the formula for calculating the rate of change between two points on a line graph?

Rate of change = (Change in vertical value) / (Change in horizontal value) = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁).
