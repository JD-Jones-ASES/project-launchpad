Important Terms (1/2)

Percent

A ratio indicating parts per one hundred.

Part ($P$)

The portion of the whole being considered in a percent problem.

Rate ($R$)

The percent in decimal form; represents the proportional relationship between the part and the base.

Base ($B$)

The whole or total amount; the quantity to which the part is compared.

Percent Change

The relative change in a quantity, expressed as a percentage of its original value.

Important Terms (2/2)

Percent Increase

A positive percent change, indicating growth.

Percent Decrease

A negative percent change, indicating reduction.

Successive Percent Changes

Two or more percentage changes applied sequentially to an amount, where each subsequent change is based on the new, adjusted amount.

Percents and Decimals

Percent to Decimal

To convert a percent to its decimal form, you divide by 100. This is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the left.

Decimal to Percent

To convert a decimal to a percent, you multiply by 100. This is equivalent to moving the decimal point two places to the right and adding the percent symbol.

Percents and Fractions

Percent to Fraction

To convert a percent to a fraction, write the percent value as the numerator over a denominator of 100, then simplify the fraction to its lowest terms.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the etymology and basic meaning of the word “percent”?

The word "percent" comes from the Latin 'per centum', meaning "per hundred." A percent expresses a number as a fraction of 100.

How do you convert a percent to a decimal?

Divide the percent by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the left).

How do you convert a decimal to a percent?

Multiply the decimal by 100 (move the decimal point two places to the right).

Convert 68% to a decimal.

0.68

Convert 0.075 to a percent.

7.5%

How do you convert a percent to a fraction?

Write the percent as a ratio over 100 and then simplify the fraction.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you convert a fraction to a percent?

Divide the numerator by the denominator, then multiply the resulting decimal by 100 (or move the decimal point two places to the right).

Convert 5/8 to a percent.

62.5% (5 ÷ 8 = 0.625; 0.625 × 100 = 62.5%)

Explain the difference between 0.5% and 50%.

0.5% is a very small number, equivalent to 0.005 as a decimal. 50% is half, equivalent to 0.50 as a decimal.

In the percent equation P = R × B, what do P, R, and B represent?

P = Part, R = Rate (as a decimal), B = Base (the whole amount).

What is the formula to find the 'part' (P) in a percent problem?

P = R × B

What is the formula to find the 'rate' (R) in a percent problem?

R = P / B (then convert the decimal to a percent)
