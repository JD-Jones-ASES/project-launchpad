Key Concepts (1/2)

Mean

The arithmetic average of a dataset, calculated by summing all values and dividing by the number of values. It's often used when data is symmetrically distributed.

Range

A simple measure of data dispersion, calculated as the difference between the maximum and minimum values in a dataset. It indicates the spread of the data.

Out-of-Specification (OOS)

Data points or products that fall outside predefined acceptable limits or tolerances. Identifying OOS items is crucial for quality control.

Weighted Average

An average where different data points contribute differently to the final sum, based on assigned "weights" that reflect their relative importance.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Outlier

A data point that is significantly distant from other observations in a dataset. Outliers can heavily influence the mean, making it a less representative measure.

Frequency Distribution

A table that summarizes the occurrence of data values by grouping them into classes or intervals and showing how many times values fall into each class (frequency).

Class Width

The size or range of each interval in a frequency distribution. It is calculated by dividing the total range of data by the desired number of classes and then rounding up.

Cumulative Frequency

The running total of frequencies in a frequency distribution, showing the total number of observations up to and including a particular class.
