The Math: Interest Formulas - Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Interest

The cost of borrowing money or the reward for lending it, typically expressed as a percentage of the principal over a period.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money deposited, invested, or borrowed.

Interest Rate (r)

The annual percentage charged or earned on the principal, always converted to a decimal (e.g., 6% becomes 0.06) for use in formulas.

Time (t)

The duration of the loan or investment, always expressed in years. Months are divided by 12, and days typically by 360 (Banker's Year) unless specified otherwise.

Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the original principal, meaning the dollar amount of interest earned or owed remains constant each period.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Maturity Value (A)

The total amount at the end of a simple interest loan or investment, calculated as principal plus all earned interest (`A = P + I` or `A = P(1 + rt)`).

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on the original principal and on all accumulated interest from previous periods, leading to faster growth due to "interest on interest."

Compounding Periods (n)

The number of times per year interest is calculated and added to the principal. Common frequencies include annually (n=1), semiannually (n=2), quarterly (n=4), and monthly (n=12).

Future Value (A) with Compound Interest

The total amount after `t` years, including compound interest, calculated using the formula `A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)`.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simple Interest

A type of interest where earnings are calculated only on the initial principal amount.

Maturity Value (A)

The total amount repaid or received at the end of a simple interest loan or investment, including the principal and all simple interest.

Compound Interest

A type of interest where earnings are calculated on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods.

Compounding Periods (n)

The number of times per year that interest is calculated and added to the principal.

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

The effective annual rate of return, taking into account the effect of compounding interest, used to compare different interest rates on a level playing field.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is interest in financial terms?

Interest is the cost of using someone else's money or the reward for letting someone use yours.

Define 'Principal' and state its variable.

The principal ($P$) is the original amount of money that is deposited, invested, or borrowed before any interest is added.

What does the 'Interest Rate' ($r$) represent, and how must it always be used in formulas?

The interest rate is the percent charged (or earned) per year on the principal. It must always be written as a decimal (e.g., 6% = 0.06).

How is 'Time' ($t$) measured in interest formulas, and what are the standard conversions for months and days?

Time ($t$) is measured in years. For months, $t = \text{months}/12$. For days, use Banker's year ($t = \text{days}/360$) or Exact interest ($t = \text{days}/...

State the Simple Interest Formula and define each variable.

$I = Prt$, where $I$ = interest earned (or owed), $P$ = principal, $r$ = annual interest rate as a decimal, $t$ = time in years.

What is the key characteristic of simple interest calculation?

With simple interest, the interest is calculated only on the original principal. You earn (or owe) the same dollar amount of interest every year.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Calculate the simple interest earned on $2,000 at 5% per year for 3 years.

$I = Prt = 2,000 \times 0.05 \times 3 = $300.00.

Find the simple interest on $4,500 at 8% for 9 months.

Convert months to years: $t = 9/12 = 0.75$ years. Then, $I = 4,500 \times 0.08 \times 0.75 = $270.00.

Define 'Maturity Value' (or Future Value for simple interest).

The maturity value is the total amount at the end of a loan or investment — the principal plus all interest earned.

State the Maturity Value Formula for simple interest.

$A = P + I$ or $A = P(1 + rt)$, where $A$ is the maturity value.

Marcus borrows $6,000 at 7% simple interest for 2 years. What is the maturity value he must repay?

$A = P(1 + rt) = 6,000(1 + 0.07 \times 2) = 6,000(1.14) = $6,840.00.

Provide the rearranged formula to find the Principal ($P$) given interest, rate, and time.

$P = I / (rt)$
