Fixed Costs

Business expenses that remain constant regardless of the production or sales volume (e.g., rent, insurance, salaries). These costs must be paid even if no units are sold.

Variable Costs

Expenses that change in direct proportion to the number of units produced or sold (e.g., raw materials, packaging per unit, sales commissions).

Revenue

The total income generated from selling products, calculated by multiplying the selling price per unit by the quantity sold.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Break-Even Point

The critical sales volume (in units) at which a business's total revenue exactly equals its total costs, resulting in neither a profit nor a loss. It signifies the minimum level of sales required t...

Contribution Margin per Unit

The difference between the selling price per unit and the variable cost per unit. This amount from each sale contributes to covering fixed costs and, once fixed costs are covered, generating profit.

Important Terms (1/2)

Markup

The amount added to the cost of an item to determine its selling price.

Markdown (Discount)

A reduction from the original selling price.

Successive Markdowns

Occur when an item is discounted more than once, with each discount applied to the most recent price, not the original price.

Fixed Costs

Expenses that stay the same regardless of how many units are produced or sold (e.g., rent, insurance, salaries).

Variable Costs

Expenses that change with the number of units (e.g., materials, packaging, shipping per unit).

Important Terms (2/2)

Revenue

The total income from selling a product.

Break-Even Point

The number of units at which total revenue equals total cost — the business is neither making a profit nor taking a loss.
