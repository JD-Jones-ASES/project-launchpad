Important Terms (1/3)

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money borrowed.

Monthly Interest Rate (r)

The annual interest rate divided by 12 (for monthly payments).

Number of Payments (n)

The total number of monthly payments over the loan's term.

Monthly Payment (M)

The fixed amount paid each month to repay a loan.

Total Paid

The sum of all monthly payments made over the loan's term ($M \times n$).

Important Terms (2/3)

Total Interest

The difference between the Total Paid and the original Principal ($Total Paid - P$).

Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

The annual cost of borrowing, expressed as a percentage, which includes the interest rate and other charges.

Down Payment

An initial upfront payment made when purchasing an item, reducing the amount that needs to be financed.

Amortization

The process of gradually paying off a debt over a period of time through a series of regular payments.

Amortization Table

A schedule that shows how each payment is applied to both the principal and interest, and the remaining loan balance.

Important Terms (3/3)

Average Daily Balance

The average of the balances in a credit card account for each day of a billing cycle.

Finance Charge

The total cost of credit, including interest and other fees, applied to a credit card balance.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the 'average daily balance' method used for in credit card finance charge calculations?

The average daily balance method is used to calculate the balance on which interest is charged by taking into account the balance on each day of the billing ...

What is the first step in calculating the average daily balance for a credit card?

Step 1 is to track the balance for each period within the billing cycle, noting any purchases or payments made and how many days each balance was held.

After tracking balances for each period in a credit card billing cycle, what is the next step in finding the average daily balance?

The next step is to sum the daily balances by multiplying each balance by the number of days it was held and adding these products together.

Once the sum of daily balances for a credit card billing cycle is calculated, how do you find the average daily balance?

Divide the sum of daily balances by the total number of days in the billing cycle to get the average daily balance.

How is the finance charge for a credit card calculated using the average daily balance?

Finance Charge = Average Daily Balance × (Annual Interest Rate / 365) × Number of Days in Billing Cycle.
