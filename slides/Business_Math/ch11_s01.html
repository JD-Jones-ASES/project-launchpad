The Math: Loan Calculations and Amortization

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Installment Loans

Loans repaid through a fixed number of equal payments made at regular intervals, such as auto loans and mortgages.

Monthly Payment Formula

A specific formula used to calculate the consistent monthly payment required to repay an installment loan, considering the principal, monthly interest rate, and total number of payments.

Total Interest Paid

The total amount of money paid in interest over the life of an installment loan, calculated by subtracting the original principal from the total amount paid.

Credit Card Finance Charges (Average Daily Balance Method)

A method used to calculate the interest charged on credit card balances, based on the average balance across all days in a billing cycle.

Important Terms (1/2)

Installment Loan

A loan that is repaid over time with a set number of scheduled, equal payments.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money borrowed in a loan.

Monthly Interest Rate (r)

The annual interest rate converted to a decimal and then divided by 12, used in monthly payment calculations.

Total Number of Payments (n)

The total count of individual payments that will be made over the life of a loan.

Finance Charge

The interest charged on a credit card balance.

Important Terms (2/2)

Average Daily Balance Method

A common method for calculating credit card finance charges, which involves summing daily balances, dividing by the number of days in the billing cycle, and then applying the daily interest rate.

Amortization

The systematic process of reducing the principal of a debt over a set period through regular payments that include both principal and interest.

Total Paid and Total Interest

Total amount paid

= $M \times n$ (monthly payment times total number of payments)

Total interest paid

= (Total amount paid) - $P$ (total paid minus the original principal)
