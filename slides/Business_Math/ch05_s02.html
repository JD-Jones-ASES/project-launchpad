Key Concepts (1/2)

Payroll Processing Fundamentals

Every business with employees must process payroll, which involves calculating gross pay, applying correct deductions, and issuing net pay.

Sales employees may earn commissions based on a tiered structure, where different percentages are applied to sales within specific ranges.

Gross Pay Calculation - Salary

Salaried employees receive a fixed amount per pay period, which is determined by dividing their annual salary by the number of pay periods in a year.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Net Pay Determination

Net pay (take-home pay) is calculated by subtracting total deductions from gross pay.

Comparing Compensation Packages

Evaluating different job offers requires a thorough comparison of annual gross pay, itemized deductions, and ultimately, annual net pay, while also considering non-financial factors.

FICA Tax Components

The FICA tax comprises Social Security (6.2% up to an annual cap) and Medicare (1.45% on all income), with employers matching these contributions.

Important Terms (1/3)

Gross Pay

The total amount of an employee's earnings before any deductions are taken out.

Net Pay

The amount of an employee's earnings after all deductions have been subtracted; also known as take-home pay.

Deductions

Amounts subtracted from an employee's gross pay, such as taxes, insurance premiums, and retirement contributions.

Overtime

Hours worked beyond the standard workweek (e.g., 40 hours) or workday, typically paid at a higher rate (e.g., 1.5 times the regular rate).

Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA)

A federal law that establishes minimum wage, overtime pay eligibility, recordkeeping, and youth employment standards affecting employees in the private sector and in Federal, State, and local gover...

Important Terms (2/3)

Graduated Commission

A commission structure where the percentage earned on sales increases as sales volume reaches certain tiers or levels.

Commission

A payment made to an employee based on the amount of sales they achieve.

Salary

A fixed regular payment, typically paid monthly or biweekly, made by an employer to an employee, especially a professional or white-collar worker.

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)

A U.S. federal payroll tax deducted from paychecks to fund Social Security and Medicare.

Federal Income Tax

A tax levied by the U.S. federal government on individual income.

Important Terms (3/3)

State Income Tax

A tax levied by individual states on income earned within that state.

Health Insurance Premium

The recurring payment required to keep a health insurance policy active.

401(k)

A retirement savings plan sponsored by an employer that allows employees to invest a portion of their pay before taxes are taken out.

Biweekly

A payment schedule where employees are paid every two weeks, resulting in 26 pay periods per year.

Semimonthly

A payment schedule where employees are paid twice a month (e.g., on the 15th and 30th), resulting in 24 pay periods per year.
