Chapter 5, Section 1: The Math: Working with Time and Rates Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Decimal Hours

A standardized way to express time for calculation, converting minutes into a decimal fraction of an hour (minutes / 60).

Gross Pay

The total amount of money an employee earns before any deductions are subtracted.

Overtime Pay

Federal law mandates that most hourly workers receive 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for any hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek.

Salary

A fixed amount of pay for a set period (typically annually), divided across regular pay periods regardless of specific hours worked.

Commission

Pay based directly on the volume of sales an individual generates.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Graduated Commission

A commission structure where different rates apply to different levels (tiers) of sales, often increasing the rate as sales volume grows.

Piecework

Pay based on the number of units produced or tasks completed by an employee.

Payroll Deductions

Mandatory or voluntary amounts subtracted from gross pay, such as taxes (FICA, federal, state), health insurance, or retirement contributions.

Net Pay

The final amount of money an employee receives after all deductions have been subtracted from their gross pay, also known as "take-home pay."

Working with Time

Example

If someone works from 8:00 AM to 4:30 PM, that is 8 hours and 30 minutes, or 8.50 decimal hours.

Payroll Deductions and Net Pay

FICA (Social Security + Medicare)

Typically 7.65% of gross pay.

Federal Income Tax

Varies significantly based on income, filing status, and allowances. A given percentage (e.g., 12%) will be provided for problems.

State Income Tax

Varies by state; a given percentage (e.g., 3-5%) will be provided.

Other Deductions

Health insurance premiums, retirement contributions, union dues, etc. These can be fixed dollar amounts or percentages.

Example

Kevin's gross pay is $2,400.00. Deductions: FICA 7.65%, Federal tax 12%, State tax 4%, Health insurance $85.00.
