Chapter 5, Section 1: The Math: Working with Time and Rates Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Decimal Hours

A standardized way to express time for calculation, converting minutes into a decimal fraction of an hour (minutes / 60).

Gross Pay

The total amount of money an employee earns before any deductions are subtracted.

Overtime Pay

Federal law mandates that most hourly workers receive 1.5 times their regular hourly rate for any hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek.

Salary

A fixed amount of pay for a set period (typically annually), divided across regular pay periods regardless of specific hours worked.

Commission

Pay based directly on the volume of sales an individual generates.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Graduated Commission

A commission structure where different rates apply to different levels (tiers) of sales, often increasing the rate as sales volume grows.

Piecework

Pay based on the number of units produced or tasks completed by an employee.

Payroll Deductions

Mandatory or voluntary amounts subtracted from gross pay, such as taxes (FICA, federal, state), health insurance, or retirement contributions.

Net Pay

The final amount of money an employee receives after all deductions have been subtracted from their gross pay, also known as "take-home pay."

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are decimal hours?

Decimal hours express time as a decimal number of hours rather than hours and minutes.

How do you convert minutes to a decimal for use in decimal hours?

To convert minutes to a decimal, divide the number of minutes by 60. The formula is: Decimal hours = hours + (minutes / 60).

Convert 7 hours 30 minutes to decimal hours.

7 + (30 / 60) = 7 + 0.50 = 7.50 hours.

A worker clocks in at 8:00 AM and out at 4:30 PM. How many decimal hours did they work?

The worker worked 8 hours 30 minutes. Converted to decimal hours: 8 + (30 / 60) = 8.50 hours.

What is gross pay?

Gross pay is the total amount earned before any deductions are subtracted.

How is gross pay calculated for hourly workers?

Gross pay = Hours worked × Hourly rate.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the standard overtime rate under federal law for most hourly workers?

Most hourly workers receive overtime pay at 1.5 times their regular rate for all hours worked beyond 40 in a workweek.

What is the formula for calculating total gross pay for an hourly worker who worked overtime?

Gross pay = (40 × Regular rate) + (Overtime hours × 1.5 × Regular rate).

What is a salary?

A salary is a fixed amount paid for a set time period (usually a year), regardless of hours worked.

How is pay per period calculated for a salaried employee?

Pay per period = Annual salary / Number of pay periods per year.

How many pay periods are there in a year for someone paid biweekly?

There are 26 pay periods in a year for someone paid biweekly.
