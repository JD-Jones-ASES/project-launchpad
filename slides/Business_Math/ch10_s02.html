Business Applications: Optimization in Business

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Optimization in Business

The process of finding the best possible outcome (e.g., maximum profit, minimum cost) given a set of limited resources and specific constraints.

Linear Programming (LP)

A mathematical method used to determine the optimal solution for problems that can be expressed as a linear objective function subject to a system of linear inequalities (constraints).

Objective Function

A linear equation that represents the quantity to be maximized (e.g., profit, revenue) or minimized (e.g., cost). It is typically denoted by `Z`.

Constraints

Linear inequalities that represent the limitations or restrictions on the resources available or requirements that must be met (e.g., labor hours, raw materials, production capacity, nutrient requi...

Non-negativity Constraints

A common type of constraint ($x \ge 0, y \ge 0$) ensuring that the quantity of items produced or used cannot be negative.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Feasible Region

The graphical area defined by the intersection of all constraints. Any point within this region represents a possible (feasible) solution that satisfies all limitations.

Important Terms (1/2)

Linear Programming

A mathematical technique for solving optimization problems where the objective function and constraints are all linear.

Optimization

The process of finding the most effective or favorable solution to a problem.

Objective Function

The mathematical expression (a linear equation) that describes the quantity to be maximized or minimized.

Constraints

The limiting conditions or restrictions expressed as linear inequalities that must be satisfied by the variables in a linear programming problem.

Feasible Region

The set of all points that satisfy all the constraints of a linear programming problem.

Important Terms (2/2)

Corner Points (Vertices)

The points where the boundary lines of the feasible region intersect. These are crucial because the optimal solution always lies at one of these points.

Resource Allocation

The process of assigning available resources to various uses to achieve specific objectives.

$Z = 3x + 4y$

Constraints

Feasible Region

Unbounded region in the first quadrant, above both simplified constraint lines. When the feasible region is unbounded, a minimum (but not necessarily a maximum) can still exist at a corner point.

Corner Points & Evaluation

Example 2: Minimize Cost — GreenGrow Fertilizer (2/2)

Solution

Use approximately $1.33$ pounds of Mix A and $3.33$ pounds of Mix B (exactly $4/3$ lb A and $10/3$ lb B) to achieve a minimum cost of $17.33 per bag.

Quick Practice Verification

Optimal blend provides $4(4/3) + 2(10/3) = 16/3 + 20/3 = 36/3 = 12$ units of nitrogen and $4/3 + 2(10/3) = 4/3 + 20/3 = 24/3 = 8$ units of phosphorus, exactly meeting the requirements.
