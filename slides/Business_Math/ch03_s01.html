Rates

A special type of ratio that compares two quantities with *different* units. Examples include miles per hour or dollars per pound.

Unit Rates

A rate where the denominator is 1. To find a unit rate, divide the numerator by the denominator. Unit rates are particularly useful for comparing efficiency or value (e.g., price per ounce).

Proportions

An equation that states two ratios are equal (`a/b = c/d`). Proportions are used to solve for unknown quantities when a relationship between two ratios is known.

Cross-Multiplication

A key property for solving proportions. If `a/b = c/d`, then `ad = bc`. This allows you to eliminate denominators and solve for an unknown variable.

Important Terms (1/2)

Ratio

A comparison of two quantities using division.

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities with different units.

Unit Rate

A rate with a denominator of 1.

Proportion

An equation stating that two ratios are equal.

Direct Variation

A relationship where two quantities `x` and `y` are related by an equation of the form `y = kx`, where `k` is a constant.

Important Terms (2/2)

Constant of Proportionality (k)

The constant value `k` in a direct variation relationship `y = kx`, representing the constant ratio `y/x`.

Ratios

Notation

Ratios can be written as a fraction (`a/b`), with a colon (`a:b`), or using words ("a to b").

Units

When simplifying a ratio, both quantities *must* be in the same units. If they are not, one must be converted to match the other before simplification.

Simplifying Ratios

To simplify a ratio, divide both parts by their Greatest Common Factor (GCF), similar to simplifying a fraction.

Unit Conversion Example

A board is 3 feet long and 8 inches wide. Ratio of length to width.

Order Matters

The order in which quantities are presented in a ratio is crucial. `3:2` (girls to boys) is different from `2:3` (boys to girls).

Rates and Unit Rates

Rates

A comparison of two quantities with *different* units.

Unit Rates

A special type of rate where the denominator is 1. This makes it easy to understand "how much per one unit" of the second quantity.

Comparing Unit Rates

Unit rates are excellent for comparing different options to find the best value or efficiency.

Proportions

Notation

`a/b = c/d` (read as "a is to b as c is to d").

Cross-Multiplication Property

This is the fundamental tool for working with proportions. If `a/b = c/d`, then you can multiply diagonally to get `ad = bc`.

Solving Proportions

Use cross-multiplication to solve for an unknown variable.

Checking for Proportions

To determine if two ratios form a proportion, cross-multiply them. If the cross-products are equal, they form a proportion.

Setting Up Proportions from Word Problems

Ensure that the units on both sides of the proportion match in their respective positions. If the first ratio is "miles over hours," the second ratio must also be "miles over hours."
