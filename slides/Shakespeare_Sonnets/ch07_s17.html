Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 17

Sonnet LXXV

Study Slides

Key Themes

Love as necessary sustenance and as obsession

The beloved is both food (life-giving necessity) and a miser's hoard (anxiety-producing possession).

Emotional oscillation

The sonnet's structure mimics the swing between feasting and starvation, pride and doubt.

Fear of loss

The "filching age" introduces time as a potential thief of the beloved.

Key Literary Devices

Simile

"As food to life," "as sweet-season'd showers" — the beloved as essential nourishment.

Economic metaphor

The miser and his wealth; "treasure," "filching" — love figured as anxious possession.

Alimentary imagery

Feasting, starving, gluttoning — a sustained food metaphor for emotional experience.

Vocabulary

Strife

Conflict, painful internal struggle

Filching

Petty stealing, pilfering

Surfeit

To have more than enough; to overindulge to the point of sickness

Pine

To waste away with longing or grief

Gluttoning

Consuming excessively, gorging
