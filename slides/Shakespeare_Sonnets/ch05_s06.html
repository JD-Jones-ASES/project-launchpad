Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 6

Sonnet XXXVIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Allusion

The nine classical Muses are invoked to be surpassed; elevating the beloved above divine figures is a hyperbolic act of devotion.

Rhetorical question

The opening question ("How can my Muse want subject?") implies the obvious answer — it cannot — and establishes the poem's celebratory premise.

Metaphor

"Invention light" — the beloved is a lamp that illuminates the poet's creative faculty.

Volta

The couplet shifts from celebrating the beloved's inspiration to assigning roles: speaker as worker, beloved as recipient of glory.
