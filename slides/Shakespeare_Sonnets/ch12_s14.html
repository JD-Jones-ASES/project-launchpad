Sonnet CXL — Managed Cruelty

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Simile

dying patient / physician as model for strategic comforting lies

Pragmatic conditional

if you push me to despair, I will grow mad and slander you

Antithesis

"bear thine eyes straight, though thy proud heart go wide"

Irony

asking for a lie as an act of wise management

What is the threat implicit in the opening two lines?

If she continues to be cruel, his tongue-tied patience will break, and his suffering will find expression in words -- potentially damaging slander of her.

What is the 'better' advice the speaker offers in lines 5-6?

Though he cannot teach her to love him, she should at least pretend to love him -- like a doctor telling a dying patient only good news, the lie is merciful.

What is the tone and logic of Sonnet CXL?

Calculating and self-interested on both sides -- the speaker asks not for genuine love but for the performance of it, as mutually protective pretence.
