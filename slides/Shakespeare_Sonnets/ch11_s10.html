Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 11, Section 10

Sonnet CXXVI

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Unique structure

Six couplets with blank lines in place of the conventional closing

Personification

Nature as a sovereign mistress who hoards the youth but must pay her

Echo of Hamlet

"Quietus" (a final reckoning or release through death) resonates

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is structurally unusual about Sonnet CXXVI?

It has only 12 lines (six couplets) instead of the standard 14, and the final two lines in the original quarto are replaced by blank parentheses -- marking a...

What does 'thou, my lovely boy, who in thy power / Dost hold Time's fickle glass, his fickle hour' mean?

The boy (the Fair Youth) holds Time's mirror and its hours in his power -- he seems to control or defy Time, growing more beautiful as he ages.

What does 'who hast by waning grown' mean?

A paradox -- the boy has actually grown more beautiful through aging (waning), as if diminishment becomes enhancement.

What role does Nature play in Sonnet CXXVI?

Nature as 'sovereign mistress over wrack' keeps plucking the boy back from decay, using him to disgrace Time by defeating its natural effects.

What is Nature's ultimate obligation regarding the boy?

Her audit (final account) must eventually be answered -- she must render up the boy to Time and death; she can delay but not permanently keep her treasure.

What does 'her quietus is to render thee' mean?

Nature's final settlement (quietus = a legal discharge of debt) is to hand the boy over to Time -- death is the final payment Nature must make.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is Sonnet CXXVI often considered a farewell to the Fair Youth sequence?

Its irregular form (12 lines, blank final parentheses) and its address to the 'lovely boy' about inevitable death mark it as a coda -- ending the young man s...

What does 'thy lovers withering, as thy sweet self grow'st' suggest?

The boy's beauty grows as those who love him age and wither -- he is contrasted with his admirers as a kind of anti-Time figure, at least temporarily.
