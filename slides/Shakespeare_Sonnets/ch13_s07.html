Chapter 13, Section 7

Sonnet CXLVIII

Study Slides

Key Poetic Devices

Philosophical dilemma

either-or structure examines whether perception or judgment is the source of distortion

Personified Love

Love as cunning agent who deliberately maintains the speaker's blindness

Sun simile

even the sun cannot see through clouds — excusing, if not excusing, the speaker's clouded vision

"Watching and tears"

physical explanations (sleeplessness, weeping) for cognitive distortion — grounded in Renaissance optics and humoral theory

What two possibilities does the speaker consider?

Either his eyes are genuinely defective (seeing falsely), or his judgment has fled and he censures falsely what his eyes see rightly.

Why does the speaker say Love's eye cannot be true (lines 9-10)?

Because love's eye is vexed with watching (sleepless vigil) and with tears -- a clouded, weary eye cannot judge accurately.

What comparison does the speaker make in lines 11-12?

Even the sun cannot see until the sky clears -- the speaker's love-clouded vision is like the sun obscured by clouds, unable to see truly.
