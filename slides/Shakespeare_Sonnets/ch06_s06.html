Chapter 6, Section 6

Sonnet XLVIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended conceit

Locks, keys, chests, bars, thieves, wards — all sustain the metaphor of the beloved as a jewel that cannot be secured.

Antithesis

"most worthy comfort, now my greatest grief" — presence is comfort, absence is grief.

Paradox

The heart as an open rather than locked container — the very place of love is not secure.

Irony

The speaker who guarded trifles carefully left his greatest treasure unguarded.
