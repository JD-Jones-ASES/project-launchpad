Chapter 12, Section 2

Sonnet CXXVIII — Music, Touch, and Erotic Envy

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Paradox

Pun

"jacks" (instrument parts / impudent fellows)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What situation does Sonnet CXXVIII describe?

The mistress is playing a keyboard instrument (virginal or harpsichord) while the speaker watches, consumed with desire and envy of the instrument.

What are the 'jacks' the speaker envies?

The jacks are the key mechanisms of the keyboard instrument -- they leap up to touch the mistress's fingers, arousing the speaker's jealousy.

What does the speaker wish for in lines 9-12?

He wishes he could exchange places with the 'dancing chips' (the keys) so that his lips could receive what the keys receive from the mistress's tender hands.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What does the couplet ask?

Give the keys (jacks) your fingers to play upon, but give me your lips to kiss -- the speaker offers a trade, claiming lips are more precious than fingers.

What is the tone of Sonnet CXXVIII compared to the more anguished Dark Lady sonnets?

It is playful and light -- the jealousy of the keyboard is a charming conceit rather than the dark obsession found in later sonnets.
