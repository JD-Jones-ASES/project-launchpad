Sonnet XII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Anaphora

"When I do count ... When I behold ... When I see" — repeated "when" clauses build relentless cumulative weight.

Personification

Time as a reaper with a scythe; summer as a dying old man with a white beard borne on a bier.

Funereal imagery

The bier carrying harvested grain — abundance and death fused in one image.

Time's scythe

The iconic image of Death/Time as the Grim Reaper, mowing all living things.

What images does Sonnet 12 use to show the effects of time?

The clock, day sinking into night, violet past prime, sable curls silvered white, lofty trees barren of leaves, summer's green gathered into sheaves on a bier.

What is 'sable curls, all silvered o'er with white' referring to?

Black hair gone grey with age — a direct image of human aging inserted among images of natural decay to show that humans follow the same seasonal cycle.
