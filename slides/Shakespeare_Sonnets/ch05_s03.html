Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 3

Sonnet XXXV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Nature imagery

Roses, fountains, eclipses, buds — a catalog of beautiful things corrupted by their inherent flaws, normalizing the beloved's transgression.

Legal vocabulary

"Authorizing trespass," "adverse party," "advocate," "lawful plea," "accessary" — the speaker frames his own moral confusion as a courtroom drama.

Oxymoron

"Sweet thief" — the beloved's charm and their wrongdoing coexist inseparably.

Civil war metaphor

The internal conflict between love and moral judgment is given the weight of a nation at war with itself.
