Important Terms (1/5)

Beguil'd (Sonnet 59)

Deceived; led astray. Refers to the brain's false belief in its own originality.

Burthen (Sonnet 59)

Burden; also, the refrain of a song. Conveys both the weight of creation and the repetition of old themes.

Record (Sonnet 59)

(stressed on second syllable) A written account; a chronicle. Implies a sense of official permanence.

Character (Sonnet 59)

Writing; inscription. Refers to thought being set down in written form.

Revolution (Sonnet 59)

A turning around; the orbital period of a planet. Signifies the cyclical nature of time.

Important Terms (2/5)

Wh'r (Sonnet 59)

A contraction of "whether," used for rapid enumeration of possibilities.

Sequent toil (Sonnet 60)

Successive effort; the relentless, orderly pushing forward, like waves.

Nativity (Sonnet 60)

Birth; the moment of being born. In Elizabethan astrology, it also referred to one's horoscope.

Confound (Sonnet 60)

To destroy utterly; to bring to nothing. Describes Time's annihilation of its own gifts.

Transfix (Sonnet 60)

To pierce through; to impale. Time's violent action against youth's flourishing.

Important Terms (3/5)

Parallels (Sonnet 60)

Lines of latitude; also, wrinkles on a face. Time "delves" these lines into beauty's brow.

Scythe (Sonnet 60)

The curved blade of Father Time or the Grim Reaper, used for mowing. Symbolizes time's ultimate destructive power.

Heavy eyelids (Sonnet 61)

Eyelids weighted by exhaustion, struggling to stay open.

Tenure (Sonnet 61)

A holding; the terms on which something is held; duration of office. "Scope and tenure of thy jealousy" refers to the full reach and terms of jealous surveillance.

Watchman (Sonnet 61)

One who keeps guard through the night. The poet transforms his insomnia into an active duty for love.

Important Terms (4/5)

Sin of self-love (Sonnet 62)

Excessive love of oneself; narcissism.

Remedy (Sonnet 62)

A cure; to set right.

Grounded (Sonnet 62)

Deeply rooted; firmly established. Describes how self-love is ingrained in the heart.

Surmount (Sonnet 62)

To overcome; to surpass.

Glass (Sonnet 62)

A mirror.

Important Terms (5/5)

Beated (Sonnet 62)

Worn; beaten (the face made old).
