Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 2

Sonnet LX

Study Slides

Key Themes

Time as relentless destroyer

Every moment advances toward death as inevitably as waves toward shore.

The life cycle

Birth, maturity, and decline are compressed into a single inexorable sequence.

Verse as immortality

The couplet's promise — that verse will stand — is the primary consolation against time's power.

Key Literary Devices

Simile

Waves/minutes comparison (lines 1–2) establishes the sonnet's central argument visually.

Personification

Time is given a scythe, a hand, and the capacity to "transfix," "delve," and "feed."

Anaphora

Repeated "And" in lines 11–14 builds the catalog of Time's destructions to a climax before the couplet's turn.

Vocabulary

Sequent

Sequential, following in order

Nativity

Birth

Transfix

To pierce through and destroy

Parallels

The parallel lines of wrinkles on a face

Delves

Digs, carves
