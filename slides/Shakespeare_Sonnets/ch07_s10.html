Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 10

Sonnet LXVIII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Authentic versus artificial beauty

The beloved represents natural, unadorned beauty against a cosmetically falsified age.

The beloved as living museum

Nature preserves the beloved as a display of what genuine beauty once looked like.

Moral condemnation of cosmetic artifice

Borrowing beauty from the dead is framed as a moral desecration.

Key Literary Devices

Map metaphor

The beloved's face is a map or faithful record of a past era.

Temporal structure

"Before...before...ere..." creates a sequence distinguishing genuine past from corrupt present.

Contrast

Natural beauty (the beloved) versus "bastard signs," "false painting," and borrowed "golden tresses."

Vocabulary

Bastard signs

Illegitimate, false marks of beauty

Sepulchres

Tombs, burial vaults

Shorn

Cut away

Fleece

Hair, here used of the dead's hair made into wigs

Yore

Former times, the past
