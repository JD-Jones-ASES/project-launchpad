Chapter 4, Section 14

Sonnet XXXI — "Thy bosom is endeared with all hearts"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Oxymoron

"the grave where buried love doth live"

Metaphor

the beloved's bosom as a treasury or tomb hung with trophies

Religious diction

obsequious, holy, religious love, interest of the dead

Paradox

friends removed (dead) but hidden living in the beloved

Accumulation

the gradual transfer of all loves to the single beloved
