Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 3

Sonnet XLV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended conceit

The four-element framework from classical philosophy organizes both XLIV and XLV as a philosophical diptych.

Oxymoron

"present-absent" captures the paradox of mediated contact — the elements are simultaneously here and there.

Personification

Air and fire become gentle diplomatic envoys carrying love across distance.

Cyclical structure

The couplet loops the emotional state back to sadness, denying any stable resolution.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the 'other two' elements mentioned at the start of Sonnet XLV?

Slight air and purging fire — the two swift elements, contrasted with earth and water from Sonnet XLIV. Air represents thought; fire represents desire.
