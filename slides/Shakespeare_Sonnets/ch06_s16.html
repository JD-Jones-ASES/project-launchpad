Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 16

Sonnet LVIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended master/slave metaphor

"slave," "vassal," "servant," "beck" — feudal and chattel slavery combined.

Paradox

"imprison'd absence of your liberty" — freedom causes bondage.

Legal metaphor

"charter," "privilege," "pardon," "crime" — the beloved's rights as an absolute legal franchise.

Brutal couplet

"waiting so be hell" — the poem's most honest acknowledgment of the cost of submission.
