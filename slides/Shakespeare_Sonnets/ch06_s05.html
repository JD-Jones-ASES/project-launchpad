Important Terms (1/2)

Vassal

A person bound in feudal service to a lord; stronger than "servant," suggesting a formal legal bond of subjugation.

Beck

A nod or gesture of command; to be "at someone's beck" means to be ready to respond to the slightest signal.

Imprison'd absence of your liberty

A striking paradox where the speaker is confined or restricted (imprisoned) precisely because the beloved is free (absence of the beloved due to their liberty creates the speaker's confinement).

Patience, tame to sufferance

Patience that has been subdued or broken, becoming a passive, unresisting endurance of suffering. "Tame" suggests its natural resistance has been crushed.

Bide each check

To endure or withstand every reproof, setback, or rebuff without complaint or resistance.

Important Terms (2/2)

Charter

A royal or legal grant of rights and privileges; here, it refers to the beloved's absolute, almost kingly, right to do as they please.

Self-doing crime

A wrong committed against oneself by oneself; the beloved is given the paradoxical right to pardon themselves for their own misdeeds, highlighting their extreme power and lack of accountability.

Diptych

A work of art or literature presented in two panels or parts; used here to describe Sonnets 57 and 58 as a pair.

Self-abnegation

The denial of one's own interests in favor of the interests of others; self-sacrifice.
