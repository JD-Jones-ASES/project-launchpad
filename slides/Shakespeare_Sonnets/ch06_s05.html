Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 5

Sonnet XLVII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Food/feast metaphor

"famish'd for a look," "painted banquet" — visual longing is treated as hunger, the portrait as nourishment.

Legal/diplomatic language

"league" continues from the legal conceit of XLVI, but now describes alliance rather than conflict.

Paradox

"thyself away, art present still with me" — absence is denied through imaginative presence.

Closure

The couplet extends the comfort to sleep itself, suggesting the consolation is truly complete.
