Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 3

Sonnet III

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Mirror metaphor

The glass is both literal prompt and symbol — the face seen in it must be reproduced or lost.

Agricultural metaphor

"Unear'd womb" and "tillage of thy husbandry" — reproduction as plowing and cultivation.

Window metaphor

"Through windows of thine age" — eyes as windows through which old age can glimpse lost youth in a child.

Rhetorical questions

Lines 5–8 challenge the youth to name any woman who would refuse him, making childlessness inexcusable.
