Important Terms (1/4)

Vial

(Sonnet VI) A small container; metaphorically used here for the womb, holding the "distill'd" essence of beauty for new life.

Usury

(Sonnet VI) Lending money at excessive interest, generally condemned in Elizabethan England. Shakespeare redefines it here as a beneficial "lending" of beauty to produce more beauty, arguing it is ...

Self-will'd

(Sonnet VI) Stubbornly determined, refusing advice; implies wilfulness and pride, cardinal sins of prioritizing self over duty.

Orient

(Sonnet VII) The East, where the sun rises; evokes splendor and newness, reflecting the Youth's prime.

Pilgrimage

(Sonnet VII) A sacred journey. The sun's daily arc is likened to a sacred journey, elevating the Youth's beauty to a quasi-religious significance.

Important Terms (2/4)

Reeleth

(Sonnet VII) Staggers or sways, like someone overcome by age or drink; vividly captures the sun's apparent loss of control as it declines, mirroring human aging.

Concord

(Sonnet VIII) Harmony or agreement, especially in music; represents the ideal social and familial harmony, contrasting with the Youth's "singleness."

Singleness

(Sonnet VIII) The state of being unmarried; also, a single musical note. The pun suggests the Youth is "ruining" his potential by remaining alone.

Parts

(Sonnet VIII) Individual musical lines that combine in harmony; also, personal roles or qualities. The double meaning highlights the Youth's failure to fulfill both musical and social roles.

Issueless

(Sonnet IX) Without children or offspring; a legal term that makes childlessness sound like a failure to execute a proper will for one's legacy.

Important Terms (3/4)

Makeless

(Sonnet IX) Without a mate; also implies "peerless" or "matchless." The world mourning the childless Youth is like a widow who has lost someone irreplaceable.

Unthrift

(Sonnet IX) A spendthrift; one who wastes money. Used ironically to show that wasted beauty is far more destructive than wasted money, as beauty cannot simply circulate.

Unprovident

(Sonnet X) Lacking forethought; failing to provide for the future. Carries financial and legal connotations, suggesting a failure of duty.

Beauteous Roof

(Sonnet X) A metaphor for the Youth's body, his lineage, or the house that shelters his beauty.

Ruinate

(Sonnet X) To ruin or destroy.

Important Terms (4/4)

Repair

(Sonnet X) To restore or renovate; presented as the Youth's duty to his "beauteous roof."
