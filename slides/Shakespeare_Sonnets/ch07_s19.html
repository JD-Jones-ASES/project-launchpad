Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 19

Sonnet LXXVII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Practical response to time

Instead of lamenting time's destruction, the speaker offers instruments for awareness and engagement.

Writing as self-knowledge

The blank book is a tool for preserving memory and thought against time's theft.

The chapter's conclusion

The chapter moves from fearful meditation on time to purposeful, active engagement with it.

Key Literary Devices

Triple conceit

Three objects (glass, dial, book) are each extended into metaphor.

Metaphor of childbirth

Written thoughts are "children nurs'd, deliver'd from thy brain" — the mind gives birth to written ideas.

Personification

Time is "thievish," its progress a stealthy theft toward eternity.

Vocabulary

Dial

A sundial

Waste blanks

Empty, blank pages

Shady stealth

The imperceptible, creeping movement of the sundial's shadow

Offices

Duties, habitual practices, useful tasks

Enrich

To make richer; to increase in value or depth
