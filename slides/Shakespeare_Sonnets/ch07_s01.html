Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 1

Sonnet LIX

Study Slides

Key Themes

Cyclical time and originality

Nothing is truly new; all creation recycles what has gone before.

The beloved's surpassing worth

Even the highest praise of ancient times would be insufficient for such beauty.

History and memory

The speaker wishes recorded history could provide a visual precedent for the beloved's beauty.

Key Literary Devices

Periphrasis

"Five hundred courses of the sun" for five hundred years — an elevated circumlocution.

Rhetorical structure

Three possibilities posed (lines 13–14) before the couplet resolves them.

Irony

The couplet turns philosophical uncertainty into a compliment: ancient poets praised worse; therefore, they would have praised the beloved most of all.

Vocabulary

Beguil'd

Deceived, misled

Burthen

Burden; also a musical refrain or repeated theme

Antique

Ancient, belonging to a former era

Character

Written characters; writing itself

Revolution

Cycle, return to an earlier state
