Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 15

Sonnet LVII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended metaphor

Master/slave throughout, reinforced by "sovereign," "servant," "sad slave," "vassal" (in companion Sonnet LVIII).

Hyperbole

"world-without-end hour" — a liturgical phrase applied to a single waiting hour.

Irony

The permitted thought — imagining the beloved making others happy — is itself a source of pain.

Personification

Love as a "true fool" in the couplet, excusing everything.
