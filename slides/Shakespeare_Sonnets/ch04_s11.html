Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 4, Section 11

Sonnet XXVIII — "How can I then return in happy plight"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Personification

day and night as conspiring persecutors

Flattery as irony

the speaker praises the beloved to win sympathy from oppressive forces

Polyptoton

"day doth daily," "night doth nightly"

Archaism

"twire" (to peep or gleam faintly)

Color metaphor

"swart-complexion'd night" — night as a dark-faced figure
