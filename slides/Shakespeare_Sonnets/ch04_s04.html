Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 4, Section 4

Sonnet XXI — "So is it not with me as with that Muse"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Irony

the speaker criticizes rivals' comparisons while using comparisons himself

Catalogue

the rival's cosmic comparisons (sun, moon, sea, April flowers)

Rhetorical question

implied in "So is it not with me"

Metaphor

praise as commercial selling

Allusion

the tradition of the Petrarchan blazon, which the speaker subverts
