Important Terms (1/5)

bail

Security given for the release of a prisoner, allowing them to go free pending a hearing.

beshrew

A mild curse; to invoke evil upon.

covetous

Excessively desirous; greedy.

disdain

Contempt; scorn; to regard with contempt.

engross'd

Taken over entirely; absorbed; also a legal term meaning to write out a document in formal script (making it binding).

Important Terms (2/5)

large

Generous; also: broad, coarse (in meaning).

mortgag'd to thy will

Pledged as security for a debt; also, entirely subjected to her desire. "Will" carries its triple meaning: desire, legal will, and the name Will.

mourn

To feel or express sorrow.

mourning / morning

A pun Shakespeare exploits; the eyes are in mourning (grief) and they become her face as the morning sun becomes the eastern sky.

pent

Confined; shut in; imprisoned.

Important Terms (3/5)

perforce

Necessarily; of necessity; by force.

rigour

Severity; harshness; strict enforcement of a rule or penalty.

ruth

Compassion; pity; sorrow for the misfortune of another.

statute of thy beauty

The legal authority conferred by her beauty—the right she claims, as a creditor, to enforce the full terms of the bond.

suit thy pity like in every part

Let your heart dress itself in pity the same way your eyes have dressed themselves in mourning—match the expression of your heart to the expression of your eyes.

Important Terms (4/5)

surety-like

In the manner of a surety—someone who assumes legal responsibility for another's debt.

the full star that ushers in the even

The evening star—the planet Venus, which appears in the west at dusk.

to boot

In addition; as a bonus.

torment

Severe pain; suffering; to cause suffering.

usurer

A moneylender who charges excessive interest, condemned by both law and morality in Shakespeare's England.

Important Terms (5/5)

vouchsafe

To grant condescendingly; to give or permit.

ward

A prison cell; also, guardianship over a ward (a person under legal protection).

Will

Simultaneously: the name William (the poet's own name); sexual desire or appetite; a legal will or testament.
