Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 11

Sonnet LXIX

Study Slides

Key Themes

Appearance versus inner character

Outward beauty is universal and undeniable; inward worth, judged by deeds, is another matter.

The gap between reputation and reality

The beloved's "odour" (inner character) does not match his "show" (external beauty).

Moral accountability

For Shakespeare, beauty carries a moral dimension — the beloved's actions tarnish his appearance.

Key Literary Devices

Nature metaphor

"Fair flower" contrasted with "rank smell of weeds" — beauty vs. moral corruption.

Structural movement

The sonnet moves from unreserved praise to qualified praise to criticism.

Synecdoche

"All tongues, the voice of souls" — tongues stand for the entire community of observers.

Vocabulary

Want

Lack, be deficient in

Churls

Rude, surly people; the thoughts turn churlish (uncharitable)

Rank

Foul-smelling, overgrown and offensive

Soil

The cause, the reason (as in "that is the soil of the problem")

Common

Ordinary; morally base, promiscuous
