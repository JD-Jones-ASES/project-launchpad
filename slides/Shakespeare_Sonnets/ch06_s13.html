Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 13

Sonnet LV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Classical allusion (exegi monumentum topos)

Horace's claim that verse outlasts bronze is the model; Shakespeare applies it to the beloved.

Personification

"Sluttish time" as a slovenly housekeeper; "Mars his sword" as a named destroyer.

Anaphora

Multiple "nor... nor" and "and" constructions accumulate the forces verse overcomes.

Confident future tense

"shall outlive," "shall burn," "shall you pace forth" — all assertions, no hedging.
