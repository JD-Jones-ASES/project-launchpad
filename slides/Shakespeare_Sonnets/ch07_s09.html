Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 9

Sonnet LXVII

Study Slides

Key Themes

The beloved as authentic beauty in a corrupt age

The beloved is genuine; the world around him is artificial and infected.

Nature's bankruptcy

The natural world has spent all its beauty resources on the beloved alone.

Temporal decline

The present age is explicitly inferior to a past age of genuine beauty.

Key Literary Devices

Rhetorical questions

The repeated "Why should he...?" creates a tone of indignant wonder.

Economic metaphor

"Bankrupt," "exchequer," "gains," "wealth" — Nature is figured as a depleted treasury.

Irony

The beloved's presence, meant to uplift, instead only legitimizes the corrupt age's pretensions.

Vocabulary

Infection

Moral corruption, contamination

Lace

To adorn, to embellish

Exchequer

A treasury or financial reserve

Bankrupt

Emptied of resources, insolvent

Gains

Profits, earnings
