Chapter 6, Section 9

Sonnet LI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Hyperbole

"mounted on the wind," "winged speed no motion shall I know" — impossible exaggerations for desire's urgency.

Personification

Desire is given a body that races, neighs, and moves in a "fiery race."

Ironic pardon

The horse is comically forgiven for its slowness in the couplet — now that desire supersedes it.

Antithesis

(structural): The poem's first half excuses slowness; the second half celebrates impossibly fast return.
