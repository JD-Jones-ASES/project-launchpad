Important Terms (1/4)

Profan'd

Desecrated; treated as common what should be sacred. Used in the context of lips that have made false vows.

Scarlet ornaments

Refers to the Dark Lady's red lips; "scarlet" also carries connotations of sin and prostitution in Elizabethan usage.

Seal'd false bonds

Executed fraudulent contracts; a metaphor for making promises of love that were not intended to be kept.

Revenues of their rents

Income properly belonging to others; metaphorically, the conjugal rights or love owed to other partners.

Tu Quoque

Latin for "you also"; a rhetorical counter-charge that deflects an accusation by pointing out the accuser's equal guilt.

Important Terms (2/4)

Careful housewife

A woman who manages her household diligently; used ironically in Sonnet 143 to describe the Dark Lady neglecting her "child" (the speaker).

Feather'd creatures broke away

A hen or other poultry that has escaped; a mundane image used to deflate the romantic register.

Will

A significant pun, potentially meaning desire/lust, the name William (Shakespeare), or a specific rival.

Still (verb)

To quiet, calm, or make silent.

Suggest (verb)

To prompt, tempt, or incite to evil, carrying a diabolical connotation.

Important Terms (3/4)

Colour'd ill

Dark-complexioned; also morally tainted or evil in character.

Foul pride

Corrupt arrogance; here, the Dark Lady's sexual license and self-aggrandizement.

Fiend

A demon or devil, marking a complete inversion from an "angel."

Hell

At once the theological place of damnation and an Elizabethan slang term for the female body.

Doom

A judgment or sentence; also, in Elizabethan English, death or ultimate fate.

Important Terms (4/4)

Hate away

To send hate away, or dismiss it. This phrase contains a pun on "Hathaway," Shakespeare's wife's surname.

Languish'd

Wasted away from longing; suffered from unrequited love.
