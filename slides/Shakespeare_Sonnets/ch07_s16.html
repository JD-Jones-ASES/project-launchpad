Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 16

Sonnet LXXIV

Study Slides

Key Themes

Body versus spirit

The body is "dregs," worthless; the spirit (preserved in verse) is what the beloved truly possesses.

Consolation through verse

Unlike LXXI and LXXII's requests for forgetting, LXXIV offers positive consolation.

Legal metaphor

Death is an arrest without bail; verse is the spirit's legal representative that remains.

Key Literary Devices

Legal metaphor

"Fell arrest," "bail," "interest," "consecrate" — death framed in legal/financial terms.

Body/spirit dualism

The contemptible body vs. the precious spirit is the sonnet's central contrast.

Self-referential couplet

"This" points to the poem itself — a moment of meta-poetic self-awareness.

Vocabulary

Fell

Cruel, deadly

Bail

Legal release through payment; here, impossible payment

Interest

A legal stake or claim; a portion belonging to someone

Consecrate

Dedicated, set apart as holy or specially belonging

Dregs

The worthless sediment left after the valuable liquid is drawn off
