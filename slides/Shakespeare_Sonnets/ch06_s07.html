Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 7

Sonnet XLIX

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Anaphora

"Against that time" repeated three times to build anticipatory dread.

Extended legal conceit

"audit," "sum," "reasons," "strength of laws," "allege," "cause" — all drawn from legal discourse.

Metaphor

"that sun, thine eye" — the beloved's warm gaze is a sun whose withdrawal signals emotional cold.

Hyperbole of self-deprecation

Raising his hand in testimony against himself is a dramatically humble gesture.
