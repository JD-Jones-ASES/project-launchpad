Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 14

Sonnet LXXII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Self-deprecation as love

The speaker erases himself out of concern for the beloved's reputation and to spare him the embarrassment of loving the unworthy.

Truth versus virtuous falsehood

The tension between honest assessment of worth and love's desire to praise.

Memory and burial

The speaker asks that his name, like his body, be simply buried and forgotten.

Key Literary Devices

Ironic self-deprecation

The sonnet is itself evidence of the speaker's talent — making the self-condemnation paradoxically moving.

Legal language

"Task you to recite" — the world as a court demanding justification.

Litotes

"Things nothing worth" — understatement that in its baldness achieves devastating force.

Vocabulary

Task

To require, to demand of someone

Merit

Worth, deserving quality

Virtuous lie

A well-intentioned falsehood designed to do more good than truth can

Niggard

Miserly, stingy

Desert

Merit, what one deserves
