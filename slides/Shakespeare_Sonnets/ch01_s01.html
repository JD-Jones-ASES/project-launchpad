Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 1, Section 1

Life and Times

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• Shakespeare was born in April 1564 in Stratford-upon-Avon to a civic-minded family

• His education at King's New School was intensely classical, grounding him in Ovid, Virgil, and Cicero

• He married Anne Hathaway in 1582; they had three children — Susanna, Hamnet, and Judith

• The "lost years" (1585–1592) are a biographical gap filled largely by speculation

• By 1592 he was established enough in London to draw the jealousy of rival playwright Robert Greene

• He joined the Lord Chamberlain's Men around 1594, uniquely serving as both shareholder and actor

Key Concepts (2/2)

• The Globe theatre (built 1599) held approximately 3,000 spectators drawn from every social rank

• He dedicated his two narrative poems to Henry Wriothesley, Earl of Southampton

• He retired gradually from London around 1610–1611 and died in Stratford on 23 April 1616

Key Terms (1/2)

high bailiff

The chief civic officer of a town, equivalent to mayor — the role John Shakespeare attained in Stratford

Lord Chamberlain's Men

The playing company Shakespeare joined around 1594; renamed the King's Men under royal patronage in 1603

shareholder

A part-owner of the theatrical company, giving Shakespeare a financial stake in the enterprise beyond a playwright's fee

lost years

The biographical gap between 1585 (baptism of the twins) and 1592 (first firm evidence of Shakespeare in London)

Globe

The open-air amphitheatre built in 1599 from the timbers of an earlier playhouse, capable of holding approximately 3,000 spectators

Key Terms (2/2)

patronage system

The practice of poets and playwrights dedicating works to wealthy nobles in hopes of financial reward or social protection

Blackfriars

The smaller indoor roofed theatre used by the King's Men from 1609 onwards

Key Concepts (1/2)

When and where was William Shakespeare born?

April 1564 (traditionally April 23, St George's Day) in Stratford-upon-Avon, Warwickshire; his baptism is recorded on April 26.

What was John Shakespeare's occupation and civic role?

He was a glover who rose to become high bailiff (the town's top civic officer) of Stratford-upon-Avon.

What kind of education did Shakespeare receive at Stratford's King's New School?

An intensely classical education: Latin grammar, rhetoric, Ovid, Virgil, Cicero, moral philosophy through the Stoics, and Roman poetry — six days a week.

Why is Ovid's Metamorphoses important to understanding Shakespeare?

Whom did Shakespeare marry, and what unusual circumstances surrounded the marriage?

He married Anne Hathaway in November 1582 at age 18. She was 26, already three months pregnant, requiring a special licence to skip the usual banns.

What are the 'Lost Years' in Shakespeare's biography?

The period between 1585 (baptism of his twins) and 1592 (first evidence of his presence in London), about which almost nothing is documented.
