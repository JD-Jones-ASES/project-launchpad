Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 2

Sonnet II

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Military metaphor

"Forty winters shall besiege thy brow / And dig deep trenches" — age as an invading army attacking the face.

Financial metaphor

"Treasure," "sum my count," "beauty's use," "succession" — beauty treated as capital that must yield a return.

Clothing metaphor

"Youth's proud livery" becomes a "tatter'd weed" — beauty as a garment that wears out.

Personification

Beauty's "treasure" as something with an owner and an accountant who will demand a reckoning.
