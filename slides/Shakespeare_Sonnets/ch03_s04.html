Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 4

Sonnet IV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended financial metaphor

Legacy, lend, spend, niggard, usurer, traffic, audit, executor — beauty is a loan from Nature that demands repayment through reproduction.

Oxymoron

"Beauteous niggard" and "profitless usurer" — admiring and condemning the youth simultaneously.

Personification

Nature as a generous but contract-bound lender; the grave as the repository of wasted beauty.

Paradox

The profitless usurer — a money-lender who makes no profit because he only lends to himself.
