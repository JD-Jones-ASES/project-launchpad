Key Concepts (1/2)

Where and approximately when was the sonnet invented?

In the court of Holy Roman Emperor Frederick II in Sicily around the 1230s. Giacomo da Lentini is most often credited with its invention.

What earlier traditions did Giacomo da Lentini draw on for the sonnet?

The Sicilian strambotto (an eight-line folk lyric) for structure, and Provencal troubadour poetry for content — with its idealized beloved and suffering lover.

Richard Tottel's anthology Songes and Sonettes introduced the sonnet to a wide English readership and sparked the Elizabethan sonnet vogue.
