Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 8

Sonnet VIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Paradox

The youth is "music to hear" yet hears music sadly — his nature is harmonious but his life is discordant.

Extended music metaphor

Harmony as a model for family life; strings as spouses; the chord of sire, child, and mother as the ideal.

Oxymoron

"Speechless song" — the family's harmony communicates without words.

Wordplay

"Parts" means both musical parts (voices in a composition) and the roles the youth should play (husband, father).
