Sonnet CLIV — The Anacreontic Coda (Part 2)

Study Slides

Key Poetic Devices

"General of hot desire"

Cupid as military commander — bathos in being disarmed by a sleeping and a virgin hand

"Legions of true hearts"

the brand has warmed countless lovers — universalizing the speaker's condition across time

Epigrammatic couplet

"Love's fire heats water, water cools not love" — compressed paradox as final summation, one of Shakespeare's most memorable closing lines

Chiasmus / antithesis

fire/water, heats/cools — the couplet's structure mirrors its logic of irreversibility

What is Cupid called in line 7 of Sonnet CLIV?

'The general of hot desire' -- Cupid is the commander-in-chief of all burning desire, here disarmed by a virgin hand.

What does 'sleeping, by a virgin hand disarm'd' suggest?

Chastity (the virgin) temporarily defeats Love -- but only while Love sleeps; the disarming is transient, not permanent.

What happens at the cool well?

The brand is quenched there, the well takes Love's heat permanently, and grows into a bath and healthful remedy for diseased men.

What is the speaker's specific situation in lines 12-14?

He is the mistress's thrall -- he came to the bath for a cure but found none, proving by this experience that 'Love's fire heats water, water cools not love.'
