Time's Universal Destruction (Sonnet 64)

Absence of Poetic Consolation (Sonnet 64)

Unlike some other "time sonnets," Sonnet 64 offers no redemptive turn in its couplet, instead expressing pure grief and fear over anticipated loss, highlighting a moment of profound despair.

The Fragility of Beauty (Sonnet 65)

Beauty is depicted as incredibly vulnerable, likened to a flower against the "wrackful siege" of time, which even conquers "brass, nor stone, nor earth, nor boundless sea."

Important Terms (1/4)

fell

(Sonnet 64): Fierce; savage; destructive. Refers to the cruel, powerful action of time.

defac'd

(Sonnet 64): Erased; disfigured; ruined. Time not only destroys but removes identity and legibility.

rich-proud cost

(Sonnet 64): The expensive, ostentatious display of past civilizations' grand monuments. Conveys both vanity and magnificence.

down-raz'd

(Sonnet 64): Completely demolished; razed to the ground. Emphasizes total destruction.

interchange

(Sonnet 64): Exchange; alternation of state. Refers to the ceaseless, purposeless change between sea and land.

Important Terms (2/4)

ruminate

(Sonnet 64): To think deeply; to chew over. Describes the poet's slow, contemplative process of confronting a terrible discovery.

o'ersways

(Sonnet 65): Overpowers; overcomes; sways by superior force. Mortality is the stronger force in all contests.

plea

(Sonnet 65): A legal argument; a case presented in court. Beauty cannot win a lawsuit against time.

wrackful

(Sonnet 65): Causing wreck and ruin; destructive. Describes the devastating "siege" of time.

stout

(Sonnet 65): Strong; resistant; courageous in resistance. Even impregnable rocks are not stout enough against time.

Important Terms (3/4)

spoil

(Sonnet 65): Plunder; what is taken in a raid. Time is cast as a raider stripping beauty.

miracle

(Sonnet 65): A wonder beyond natural explanation. The survival of beauty in ink is presented as something defying natural rules.

needy nothing

(Sonnet 66): Worthless nothingness dressed up as something important or joyful.

forsworn

(Sonnet 66): Renounced or given up on oath; betrayed. Refers to purest faith being unfortunately placed or broken.

rudely strumpeted

(Sonnet 66): Prostituted; dishonored in a crude and violent manner. Refers to maiden virtue being corrupted.

Important Terms (4/4)

limping sway disabled

(Sonnet 66): Weakened or put at a disadvantage by imperfect, hobbling authority. Strength hobbled by incompetent power.

miscall'd simplicity

(Sonnet 66): Simple truth wrongly called foolishness or lack of sophistication.

captive good attending captain ill

(Sonnet 66): Goodness enslaved and serving under the command of evil.
