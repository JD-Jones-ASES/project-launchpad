Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 3

Sonnet LXI

Study Slides

Key Themes

Absence and longing

Love without the beloved's presence becomes a form of sleepless torment.

Self-knowledge and correction

The volta is a self-correcting moment — the speaker dismisses his own imaginative hypothesis and finds the true cause within himself.

Jealousy and devotion

The speaker projects jealousy onto the beloved, then redirects it into a more painful self-awareness.

Key Literary Devices

Volta

The sharp "O, no!" at line 11 pivots from hypothetical jealousy to the truth of the speaker's own love.

Personification

Shadows given the power to "mock" the speaker's sight.

Dramatic irony

The watchman's vigil is rendered absurd and poignant by the couplet's revelation.

Vocabulary

Slumbers

Sleep

Pry

To spy or intrude

Range and purpose

Watchman

Key Concepts (2/2)

What literary device is used in 'my own true love that doth my rest defeat'?

Personification and military metaphor: his love is the enemy general that defeats ('routs') his rest, keeping him as a sleepless watchman against his will.
