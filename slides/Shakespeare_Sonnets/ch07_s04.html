Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 4

Sonnet LXII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Self-love and its correction

Vanity is redeemed by being revealed as love for the beloved.

The mirror as truth

The glass is the instrument of honest self-knowledge against delusion.

Age and beauty

The speaker's aged face, revealed honestly, forces a reorientation of love.

Key Literary Devices

Anaphora

"And all my soul, and all my every part" builds the totality of self-love's possession.

Volta

"But when" at line 11 introduces the deflating reality of the mirror.

Metaphor

"Painting my age with beauty of thy days" — the beloved's youth is a paint applied over the speaker's aged self.

Vocabulary

Possesseth

Takes complete hold of, dominates

Gracious

Beautiful, pleasing in appearance

Beated and chopp'd

Beaten down and roughened, weather-worn

Antiquity

Old age; a former, ancient era

Iniquity

Wickedness, moral wrongdoing
