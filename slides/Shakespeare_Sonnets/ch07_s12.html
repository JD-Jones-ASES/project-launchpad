Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 12

Sonnet LXX

Study Slides

Key Themes

Slander as paradoxical proof of worth

Being attacked confirms the beloved's exceptional value.

Beauty attracts vice

The canker worm/sweetest bud image makes this structural and inevitable.

The cost of perfection

The beloved cannot fully escape suspicion because envy is inseparable from outstanding beauty.

Key Literary Devices

Simile

"A crow that flies in heaven's sweetest air" — slander accompanies beauty as inevitably as a crow flies in clean air.

Nature metaphor

The canker/bud image from gardening — vice attacks the sweetest and purest.

Concession

The couplet concedes what even the defense cannot fully overcome.

Vocabulary

Suspect

Suspicion (noun); under suspicion

Canker

A destructive grub or worm; also a type of disease affecting plants

Prime

The prime of life; youthful vitality

Assail'd

Attacked, subjected to assault

Enlarg'd

Set free, released without restraint
