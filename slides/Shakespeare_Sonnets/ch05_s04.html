Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 4

Sonnet XXXVI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Paradox

One love, two persons; spiritual unity requiring physical separation.

Self-abnegation

The speaker erases himself publicly to protect the beloved — a radical act of sacrificial love.

Repeated couplet

The word-for-word echo in Sonnet XCVI suggests either profound intentionality or a textual puzzle of the manuscript tradition.
