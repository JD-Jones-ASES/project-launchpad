Important Terms (1/2)

Generative Tension

A core conflict or unresolved problem that serves as the creative engine or driving force for a work of art, in this case, the emotional and physical distance between lovers.

Triptych

A work of art (in this case, a set of three poems: sonnets 43-45) that is divided into three sections or panels, usually with a central theme connecting them.

Four Elements Doctrine

An ancient Greek philosophical concept proposing that all matter consists of four primary substances: earth (solidity), water (liquidity), air (gas), and fire (energy/heat), often used symbolically...

Conceit

An elaborate or extended metaphor that compares two very dissimilar things, often drawing out the comparison through several lines or an entire poem. Often imaginative and clever.

Immortalizing Power of Verse

The literary concept that through poetry, the subject of the poem (a person, love, beauty) can achieve eternal life or remembrance, transcending physical decay and the passage of time.
