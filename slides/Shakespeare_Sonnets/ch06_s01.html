Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 1

Sonnet XLIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Paradox / Oxymoron

"When most I wink, then do mine eyes best see"; "darkly bright"; "sightless eyes" — the poem is built on deliberate contradictions that capture the disorienting power of longing.

Antanaclasis

"thy shadow's form form happy show" repeats "form" as both noun and verb.

Light imagery

"bright," "clearer light," "shade shines" — the beloved is consistently associated with radiance that no earthly light source can match.
