Chapter 6, Section 12

Sonnet LIV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended analogy

Rose vs. canker bloom as sustained illustration of inner versus outer beauty.

Oxymoron

"sweet deaths" — death becomes the source of the rose's most lasting gift.

Metaphor

Poetry as distillation, concentrating truth as alchemists concentrate essence.

Archaic diction

"vade" (fade), "foison" (abundance in Sonnet LIII) — Shakespearean vocabulary.
