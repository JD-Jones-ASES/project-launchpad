Key Concepts (1/2)

The Rival Poet

The emergence of other writers ("alien pens") who also look to the young man as a Muse, challenging the speaker's previously exclusive poetic relationship.

The Patron-Poet Relationship

Reflects the Elizabethan social reality where poets sought patronage from wealthy individuals. The youth's approval is both practical and emotionally significant, leading to competition.

Important Terms (1/5)

Invoked

Called upon; summoned. In poetry, to call upon the Muse for divine inspiration.

Alien pen

A foreign or rival writer's instrument, symbolizing an intruder or competitor in the speaker's poetic relationship with the youth.

Disperse

To scatter or distribute widely; in this context, rival poets spreading their work.

Compile

To compose; to gather and put together in writing, suggesting a more humble, crafted act than divine inspiration.

Mend the style

To improve the manner or surface of writing, implying a superficial enhancement rather than fundamental creation.

Important Terms (2/5)

Rude ignorance

The speaker's self-deprecating description of his own lack of formal learning or poetic skill, often used ironically.

Gracious numbers

Pleasing verses; "numbers" is a Renaissance term for metrical poetry.

Sick Muse

The speaker's weakened or ailing poetic inspiration, metaphorically describing his creative vitality being drained by the rival's presence.

Lovely argument

A beautiful subject or theme for a poem; "argument" in Elizabethan usage refers to the subject matter.

Travail

Labor; toil; great effort, acknowledging the rival's diligent work.

Important Terms (3/5)

Invent

To discover or find; to devise. In Renaissance rhetoric, *inventio* was the art of finding ideas and arguments.

He robs thee of, and pays it thee again

A paradox describing how the rival's praise is merely a reflection of the youth's inherent qualities, taken from him and returned.

Faint

To grow weak; to lose courage; to feel dizzy with awe, describing the speaker's intimidation and creative paralysis.

Better spirit

A greater poetic genius; "spirit" could refer to a person of exceptional creative power.

Saucy bark

An impertinent or bold small boat; a self-deprecating but defiant image the speaker uses for himself.

Important Terms (4/5)

Broad main

The open ocean; the wide expanse of deep water, representing the youth's vast worth.

Soundless deep

Water too deep to be measured, symbolizing the profound depths of the youth's worth explored by the rival.

Tall building

A ship of imposing construction and height, representing the rival's superior poetic skill.

Cast away

Shipwrecked; destroyed; discarded, with a deliberate pun on poetic ruin or emotional rejection.

Epitaph

An inscription on a tombstone; a memorial tribute.

Important Terms (5/5)

Rehearse

To speak of; to recite; to repeat.
