Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 15

Sonnet LXXIII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Three metaphors of mortality

Autumn, twilight, and dying fire represent the speaker's aging in escalating urgency.

Intensification through compression

Each metaphor narrows the timeframe, creating a mounting sense of imminence.

Love strengthened by loss

The couplet's paradox — perceiving decline strengthens love — is the sonnet's emotional climax.

Key Literary Devices

Triple conceit

Three extended metaphors in three quatrains — the sonnet's structural masterstroke.

Paradox

The fire is consumed by what nourished it; approaching loss intensifies love.

Allusion

"Bare ruin'd choirs" evokes the dissolution of the monasteries under Henry VIII.

Vocabulary

Choirs

The choir loft sections of a church; arched wooden structures

Fadeth

Fades, diminishes

Seals up

Closes completely, as in sealing a tomb

Expire

To breathe one's last; to die

Consum'd

Consumed, destroyed by its own fuel
