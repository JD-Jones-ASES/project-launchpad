Chapter 5, Section 9

Sonnet XLI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Escalation

"Pretty wrongs" at the start versus "break a twofold truth" at the end — the sonnet enacts the dismantling of its own initial minimization.

Rhetorical question

"What woman's son / Will sourly leave her?" — a cultural appeal that simultaneously excuses and, in context, exposes the inadequacy of the excuse.

Chiasmus

The couplet's parallel structure ("Hers by thy beauty... / Thine by thy beauty...") assigns blame symmetrically across both betrayals.
