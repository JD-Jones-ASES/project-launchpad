Key Concepts (1/2)

What are 'Siren tears / Distill'd from limbecks foul as hell within' (lines 1-2)?

Siren tears are the deceptive, enchanting weeping of a temptress; limbecks are alembics (distillation vessels) -- the speaker was deceived by tears that seem...

What does 'applying fears to hopes, and hopes to fears' describe?

A state of psychological confusion -- the speaker was perpetually undoing himself, converting every positive into a negative and vice versa.

What was the speaker doing 'when I saw myself to win'?

Losing -- the paradox is that each moment of apparent victory was actually defeat; success in the bad love was spiritual loss.

What is the 'madding fever' of line 8?

The frenzied, fever-like state of infatuation or delusion that drove the speaker's eyes out of their proper sockets (out of their spheres).

What does 'O benefit of ill!' mean in line 9?

A paradoxical celebration -- the speaker finds that the advantage of suffering through evil is discovering a deeper truth: better is made better by contrast ...

What does the couplet claim about the speaker's return?

He returns 'rebuk'd to my content' -- having been punished by his straying, he gains three times more than he lost, finding the relationship strengthened.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does 'ruin'd love, when it is built anew, / Grows fairer than at first' function as a theme?

It argues that love tested and restored is stronger and more beautiful than untested love -- damage and repair produce superior architecture.

What literary device is 'Still losing when I saw myself to win'?

Paradox and chiasmus -- the statement inverts expected logic, enacting the confusion it describes.
