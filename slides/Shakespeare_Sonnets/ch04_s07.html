Chapter 4, Section 7

Sonnet XXIV — "Mine eye hath play'd the painter and hath stell'd"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Extended conceit

eye as painter, heart as canvas, body as frame, beloved's eyes as windows

Metaphor

"table of my heart" (table = flat painting surface)

Wordplay

"perspective" as both artistic technique and visual truth

Volta

the couplet's qualification of all the preceding visual celebration

Paradox

the eyes' cunning "want" (lack) what would perfect their art
