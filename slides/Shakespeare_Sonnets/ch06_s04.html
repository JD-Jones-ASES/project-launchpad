Chapter 6, Section 4

Sonnet XLVI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended legal conceit

Nearly every term — "mortal war," "plead," "defendant," "impannell'd," "quest," "verdict," "moiety," "due," "right" — belongs to legal discourse.

Allegory

The eye and heart are personified as litigants in a lawsuit.

Jury metaphor

The thoughts, as tenants of the heart, are both jury and interested parties — a subtle irony about objectivity.
