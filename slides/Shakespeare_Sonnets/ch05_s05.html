Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 5

Sonnet XXXVII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Simile

The decrepit father and active child open the poem with a domestically vivid image of vicarious participation.

Paradox

A shadow providing substance — insubstantial reflected glory that nevertheless nourishes and satisfies.

Horticultural metaphor

"Engrafted" — grafting joins a weak cutting to a strong host; the speaker's love is such a graft.

Hyperbole

"Ten times happy me" — the arithmetic of love multiplies ordinary happiness.
