Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 14

Sonnet LVI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Apostrophe

"Sweet love, renew thy force" — love is addressed directly as if it were a person.

Imperative mood

Urgency created by a series of commands to love.

Double analogy

Ocean/shores and winter/summer as two parallel reframings of absence.

Alliteration

"sweet," "sad," "summer's," "sharp" — the 's' sounds give the poem a soft, yearning quality.
