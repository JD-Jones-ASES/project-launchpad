Sonnet XXIX — "When in disgrace with fortune and men's eyes"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Catalogue

the list of envied qualities creates the emotional weight of the octave

Volta

the sharp turn at "Yet... Haply I think on thee" (lines 11–12)

Simile

the lark rising from sullen earth

Paradox

poverty made rich by love's memory

Archaism

"haply" (by chance or perhaps)
