Key Concepts (1/2)

What occasion prompts Sonnet CXXII?

The speaker explains why he has given away the beloved's gift of tables (writing tablets) -- he defends himself against an implied accusation of ingratitude.

What are 'tables' in the Elizabethan sense?

Writing tablets or a notebook -- a personal gift from the beloved, expected to be kept and used to record memories.

Why does the speaker say he gave the tables away?

Because he trusts his memory more -- the beloved is 'full character'd with lasting memory' in his brain, recorded beyond the reach of oblivion or physical loss.

What does 'beyond all date; even to eternity' mean?

The memory of the beloved in the speaker's brain will outlast any fixed term -- it is eternal, not subject to the limits of a physical notebook.

What does 'poor retention' refer to (line 9)?

The physical writing tablets -- they can hold only a limited amount and are inferior to the human mind's capacity for lasting memory.
