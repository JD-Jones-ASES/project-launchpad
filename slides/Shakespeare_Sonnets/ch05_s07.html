Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 7

Sonnet XXXIX

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Paradox

Spiritual unity requires physical separation to enable genuine, un-self-referential praise.

Oxymoron

"Sour leisure" — absence is unpleasant but creates time; bitter circumstance producing sweet possibility.

Volta

The turn from the problem of self-praise (Q1-Q2) to the discovered consolation of absence (Q3) to the paradoxical resolution (couplet).

Wordplay

"Make one twain" — the beloved's distance teaches the speaker to split what is one into two, enabling the very praise the beginning declared impossible.
