Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 16

Sonnet XVI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Self-deprecation

"My barren rhyme" — the poet strategically diminishes his own verse to argue for reproduction's superiority.

Garden metaphor

"Maiden gardens, yet unset" — eligible women as unplanted gardens, ready for cultivation.

Pun

"Lines of life" — both the lines of genealogy (children) and the lines of verse; both can "repair" life, but living lines are more powerful.

Paradox in couplet

"To give away yourself keeps yourself still" — reproduction is paradoxically both a giving and a keeping.
