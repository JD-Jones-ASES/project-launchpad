Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 2

Sonnet XXXIV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended metaphor

Continued from XXXIII; the beloved is the sun who promised a bright day, and the storm is the betrayal.

Legal diction

"Physic," "offender," "relief," "offence," and "cross" create a quasi-courtroom atmosphere, elevating the personal hurt into a matter of justice.

Metaphor of salve and wound

Partial healing that cannot cure disgrace — a distinction between pain (temporary) and shame (permanent).

Volta

The couplet pivots sharply from grievance to near-forgiveness through the image of "pearl" tears.
