Chapter 3, Section 10

Sonnet X

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Exclamatory rebuke

"For shame!" — the most direct and confrontational opening in the sequence so far.

House metaphor

"That beauteous roof to ruinate" — family lineage as a beautiful house that must be maintained by an heir.

Paradox

Loved by many, loving none — the contradiction at the heart of the youth's character.

Volta

The shift from accusation (quatrains) to personal appeal ("O! change thy thought, that I may change my mind") marks the emotional turning point.
