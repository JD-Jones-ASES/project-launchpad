Chapter 5, Section 1

Sonnet XXXIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended metaphor (conceit)

The sun-and-cloud imagery sustained across all 14 lines is the poem's structural backbone. The beloved IS the sun; the speaker's world is the landscape illuminated and then darkened.

Personification

The sun has a "sovereign eye," a "celestial face," and moves with disgrace — giving it human pride and shame, preparing the reader for the beloved's emotional parallel.

Volta

Line 11 ("Even so my sun") marks the pivot from nature description to personal application.

Alliteration

"Gilding... green," "forlorn... face" and "sovereign... sun... stain... staineth" in the couplet reinforce the musical texture.
