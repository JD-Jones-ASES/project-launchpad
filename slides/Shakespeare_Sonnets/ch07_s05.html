Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 5

Sonnet LXIII

Study Slides

Key Themes

Verse as a fortress against time

The speaker uses military language ("fortify," "confound") to frame poetry as defense.

The distinction between life and beauty

Verse can preserve beauty in memory even after physical life ends.

Youth and spring

The beloved's beauty is figured as a "spring" treasure that time steals.

Key Literary Devices

Personification

Time is given an "injurious hand" and a "cruel knife."

Metaphor

"Youthful morn" / "age's steepy night" — the life journey as a day's arc.

Military metaphor

"Fortify" and "confound" cast the speaker's poetic project as preparation for battle.

Vocabulary

O'erworn

Worn out, used up by time

Steepy

Steep; a steep descent into darkness

Travell'd on

Journeyed, progressed

Fortify

To build defenses in preparation for attack

Confounding
