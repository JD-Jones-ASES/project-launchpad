Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 6

Sonnet LXIV

Study Slides

Key Themes

Ruin as meditation

Observing historical and natural decay is the path to the speaker's personal terror.

The paradox of possession

Having what one loves makes the prospect of its loss into a kind of pre-emptive death.

Absence of consolation

Unlike Sonnets LXIII and LXV, Sonnet LXIV offers no verse-immortality promise — only grief.

Key Literary Devices

Anaphora

"When I have seen" / "When sometime" structures the first 12 lines as a catalog of ruined witnesses.

The exchange image

"Increasing store with loss, and loss with store" — the chiasmic balance of sea and shore.

Simile (couplet)

The thought of loss is compared to "a death" that can only weep.

Vocabulary

Fell

Cruel, savage

Defac'd

Defaced, obliterated

Down-raz'd

Razed to the ground

Confounded

Destroyed, thrown into ruin

Ruminate

To meditate deeply, to chew over in the mind
