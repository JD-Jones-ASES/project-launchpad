Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 13

Sonnet LXXI

Study Slides

Key Themes

Self-abnegating love

The speaker's love is so selfless that he wishes for his own erasure rather than the beloved's pain.

Death and decomposition

The "vile world with vilest worms" creates a grim landscape of mortality.

Social shame

The couplet introduces a practical reason for forgetting — the world's mockery of grief.

Key Literary Devices

Apostrophe

The entire sonnet is addressed directly to the beloved — a dramatic monologue of farewell.

Paradox

Writing a poem to request being forgotten — the very verse that might immortalize also records the wish for oblivion.

Alliteration

"Surly sullen bell" — the consonance of s mimics the heavy tolling.

Vocabulary

Surly

Gruff, forbidding in tone

Sullen

Heavy, dull, and mournful

Compounded

Mixed or blended; dissolved into

Rehearse

To repeat, to recite aloud

Moan

An expression of grief; public mourning

Key Concepts (1/2)
