Important Terms (1/6)

attend time's leisure

To wait patiently for time's slow unfolding; implies a feeling of being trapped by the indifferent passage of time.

badges of either's woe

A poignant image in Sonnet 44; refers to tears as symbolic representations of the sorrow shared by both the heavy elements of earth and water, which constitute the speaker's body.

bar

(In Sonnet 46) Both a barrier and a legal term for a court or the legal profession; signifies the legal dispute between the eye and the heart.

closet never pierced with crystal eyes

(In Sonnet 46) Refers to the heart's claim of being a private, unbreachable sanctuary where the beloved resides, hidden from the external gaze of the eye.

darkly bright

A Shakespearean paradox (Sonnet 43) describing the luminous clarity of vision experienced in the darkness of dreams; seeing brilliantly in a sightless state.

Important Terms (2/6)

doth commerce

(In Sonnet 47) Engages in trade or exchange; here, refers to the reciprocal sharing of the beloved's image between the eye and the heart.

due to thee

(In Sonnet 46) What is owed or rightfully belongs to the beloved, specifically their image.

dull substance

(In Sonnet 44) Refers to the physical body, emphasizing its heavy, inert, and slow nature, in contrast to agile thought.

earth and water

(In Sonnet 44) Two of the four classical elements, associated with coldness, wetness, heaviness, and slowness; represents the physical body's limitations.

imperfect shade

(In Sonnet 43) The dream-image of the beloved; it is only a copy or reflection, yet powerful enough to transform the night.

Important Terms (3/6)

injurious distance

(In Sonnet 44) Distance personified as a harmful agent actively causing suffering by separating the lovers.

life's composition

(In Sonnet 45) The ideal balance of the four classical elements that constitutes a healthy, living human being; disruption of this balance leads to illness or melancholy.

love and beauty

(In Sonnet 46) Refers to the more abstract, inner qualities of the beloved that the heart claims to possess.

mortal war

(In Sonnet 46) An intense, life-or-death conflict, used metaphorically to describe the dispute between the eye and the heart.

Neoplatonic idea

A philosophical concept (mentioned in context for 43) that the lover carries the beloved's ideal image imprinted on the soul, which can be perceived in certain states like sleep or intense thought.

Important Terms (4/6)

nimble thought

(In Sonnet 44) Thought conceived as swift and agile, capable of instantly traversing great distances, unlike the slow body.

painted banquet

(In Sonnet 47) A metaphor for a mental or imaginative feast; the beloved's image serves as a visual and emotional sustenance for the eye and heart, even in absence.

present-absent

(In Sonnet 45) A Shakespearean paradox; refers to the elements (and thus thought/desire) being simultaneously with the beloved and absent from the speaker.

purging fire

(In Sonnet 45) Cleansing, refining fire; associated with the burning energy of desire and the vital spirit.

recur'd

(In Sonnet 45) Restored to its proper, healthy state; cured or brought back to wholeness.

Important Terms (5/6)

shadow

(In Sonnet 43) Can mean an image, a reflection, or literal darkness/shade; Shakespeare plays on all meanings simultaneously, as the beloved's image shines even in darkness.

slight air

(In Sonnet 45) Light, insubstantial air; emphasizes the element's lack of weight and swiftness, associated with intellect and thought.

store

(In Sonnet 47) A supply or reserve; the imagination provides a continuous supply of the beloved's image.

tender embassy

(In Sonnet 45) A gentle or affectionate diplomatic mission; the light elements (thought and desire) act as messengers of love to the beloved.

unrespected

(In Sonnet 43) Unnoticed, disregarded; refers to ordinary things seen during the day that lack true significance compared to the beloved's image.

Important Terms (6/6)

wink

(In Sonnet 43) In Elizabethan usage, commonly meant to close one's eyes in sleep, not necessarily to blink one eye.
