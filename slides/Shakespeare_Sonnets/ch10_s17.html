Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 10, Section 17

Sonnet CXVI — Study Guide

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Negative definition

Love is defined by what it resists — alteration, removal, Time.

Nautical imagery

The "ever-fixed mark" and "wandering bark" draw on contemporary navigation.

Astronomical metaphor

Love as a star whose position can be taken but whose worth is immeasurable.

Logical wager

The couplet is a proof by contradiction — an ultimate rhetorical challenge.

Liturgical echo

The opening mirrors the marriage service's "Speak now or forever hold your peace."

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the opening legal metaphor of Sonnet CXVI?

'Let me not to the marriage of true minds admit impediments' -- borrowing from the Anglican marriage service to declare that true love has no legitimate obst...

How does the speaker define love that is not 'true love'?

Love that alters when it finds alteration, or bends when the beloved attempts to leave -- conditional love is no real love.

What is the 'ever-fixed mark' (line 5)?

A navigational beacon or lighthouse -- love is unmovable, looking on tempests without being shaken, just as a fixed mark guides ships through storms.

What is 'the star to every wandering bark' (lines 7-8)?

A navigational star (like the North Star) -- love guides every wandering ship, its height measurable though its true worth is unknowable.

What does 'Time's fool' mean in line 9?

A victim or plaything of Time -- the speaker insists love is not subject to Time's power, even though rosy lips and cheeks fall within Time's sickle.

What is 'the edge of doom' in line 12?

The very end of the world (Doomsday) -- love endures not just a lifetime but until the last possible moment of existence.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the dramatic wager of the couplet?

If this definition of love is proved wrong, then the speaker never wrote a word and no man ever loved -- staking his entire literary existence on the claim.

Why is Sonnet CXVI considered one of the greatest love poems in English?

It defines love through absolute negatives (what love is NOT), with escalating metaphors (beacon, star, defeater of Time), and stakes everything on its truth...
