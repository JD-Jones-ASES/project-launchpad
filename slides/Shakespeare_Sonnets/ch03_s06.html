Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 6

Sonnet VI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Continuation of distillation metaphor

"Make sweet some vial" — the youth must fill a perfume vial (child) with his essence before winter destroys the flower.

Personification

"Winter's ragged hand" — winter given a physical, destructive hand that defaces beauty.

Paradox on usury

Reproduction is a "use" (interest) that is not forbidden usury because it benefits all parties willingly.

Carrot and stick

The ten-children fantasy (reward) and the worm-heir threat (punishment) bracket the argument.
