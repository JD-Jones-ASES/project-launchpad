Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 1, Section 2

The Works

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• Shakespeare wrote or co-wrote 37 plays, two long narrative poems, and 154 sonnets

• His plays are traditionally grouped into four categories: comedies, histories, tragedies, and late romances

• The comedies share a grammar of confused identities and festive resolution, but contain shadows of melancholy

• The histories form a loose cycle examining English kingship, power, and rebellion, with Falstaff as a towering comic creation

• The four great tragedies — Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, Macbeth — explore jealousy, revenge, madness, and corrupted ambition

• The late romances are stranger and more archaic, steeped in wisdom about loss and forgiveness

Key Concepts (2/2)

• Venus and Adonis (1593) and The Rape of Lucrece (1594) established Shakespeare's literary reputation; both were dedicated to Southampton

• The sonnets were circulating among private friends by 1598 and published in full by Thorpe in 1609

• The bulk of the sonnets are dated to the 1590s, though some may have been composed or revised as late as 1609

Key Terms (1/2)

comedies

Plays characterised by confused identities, crossed lovers, witty banter, and festive resolution — though Shakespeare's comedies retain notes of melancholy and exclusion

histories

Plays refashioning English historical chronicles into an examination of power, legitimacy, and rebellion, covering the reigns from Richard II to Henry VIII

tragedies

Plays of extreme psychological and moral intensity; the four great middle-period tragedies are Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, and Macbeth

late romances

The final phase of Shakespeare's drama — Pericles, Cymbeline, The Winter's Tale, The Tempest — featuring archaic plots of separation, reunion, and supernatural intervention

Venus and Adonis

Shakespeare's first published work (1593), dedicated to Southampton; a bestseller that established his literary reputation

Key Terms (2/2)

The Rape of Lucrece

The second narrative poem (1594), also dedicated to Southampton; a grave poem about honour, shame, and political power

Palladis Tamia

Francis Meres' 1598 literary survey, which referred to Shakespeare's "sugared sonnets among his private friends" — the earliest evidence of the sonnets' circulation

The Passionate Pilgrim

A pirated anthology (1599) that included two of the sonnets, placing them in existence by that date

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many plays did Shakespeare write or co-write, and over roughly how many years?

37 plays over approximately 25 years of career, plus two long narrative poems and 154 sonnets.

What are the four broad categories of Shakespeare's plays?

Comedies, histories, tragedies, and late romances — though Shakespeare himself observed no such boundaries.

Name three features the Shakespearean comedies have in common.

Confused identities, crossed lovers, and a final resolution in marriage and reconciliation — yet always with a shadow of melancholy at the edge.

What is Falstaff from the Henry IV plays considered, and why?

Perhaps Shakespeare's greatest comic creation — a figure so alive he seems to overflow the plays that contain him.

What are the four great tragedies of Shakespeare's middle period?

Hamlet, Othello, King Lear, and Macbeth — all written within about six years around the turn of the seventeenth century.

What Roman tragedies did Shakespeare write, and how do they differ in tone from the great four?

Julius Caesar, Antony and Cleopatra, and Coriolanus — they bring a cooler, more political intelligence to many of the same themes.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Shakespeare dedicated Venus and Adonis to the Earl of Southampton and called it 'the first heir of my invention.' What does this reveal?

He did not yet regard his plays as serious literary achievements, valuing his printed poetry over his commercial theatrical work.

What themes link both narrative poems to the sonnets?

Beauty that invites destruction, the tyranny of desire, and the consolation of art — all central preoccupations of the sonnet sequence.

How do the sonnets and plays share an emotional world?

Both circulate the same obsessions: the cruelty of time, the inadequacy of language to capture beauty, and love shading into obsession and shame.

What does the broad scholarly consensus say about when the sonnets were composed?

Mostly in the 1590s, with some possibly revised or newly composed as late as 1603–1609; two sonnets appeared in a pirated anthology in 1599.
