Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 6, Section 8

Sonnet L

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Personification

The horse is given instinct, sympathy, and an expressive groan — it becomes a mirror for the speaker's inner state.

Metaphor

"Heavy" runs through the poem as both literal and emotional weight; "my joy behind" gives sorrow a spatial direction.

Extended conceit

The entire journey becomes a figure for love and separation.

Pathetic fallacy

The external world (the horse, the road) reflects the speaker's emotion.
