Important Terms (1/4)

motley

The multi-colored costume worn by a court jester; by extension, a fool. To make oneself "a motley to the view" means to play the fool publicly.

gor'd

Wounded; stabbed; pierced with a sharp point. Metaphorically, the speaker has "gor'd mine own thoughts" by straying.

blenches

Flichings; turnings aside; moments of wavering or infidelity.

essays

Attempts; trials; tests. "Worse essays" refers to inferior loves or diversions that proved the beloved's superiority by contrast.

grind

To wear down; to sharpen by friction. "Mine appetite I never more will grind / On newer proof" means no longer sharpening one's desire by testing it on new experiences.

Important Terms (2/4)

brand

A mark burned into flesh as a sign of disgrace; a stigma. The speaker's "name receives a brand," indicating public infamy.

the dyer's hand

A vivid image referring to a dyer whose hands are permanently stained by dyes, symbolizing how one's profession can indelibly mark one's identity or character.

eisel

Vinegar; a bitter liquid used as a remedy or penance.

double penance

Penance beyond what is required. The speaker is willing to endure severe punishment for his faults.

vulgar

Common; of or belonging to the common people. "Vulgar scandal" refers to common gossip and public censure.

Important Terms (3/4)

o'er-green

To cover over with green growth; to restore freshly. Love "o'er-greens" the speaker's bad reputation as grass covers a wound.

abysm

Abyss; a bottomless depth. Into this "profound abysm" the speaker casts all concern for others' opinions.

adder's sense

The hearing of a deaf adder. The adder was believed to stop its ears against charmers, symbolizing the speaker's deliberate shutting out of external voices (critics and flatterers).

latch

To catch; to perceive; also to close. The eye "latches" onto objects but doesn't hold them, as they are reshaped by the mind.

rud'st

Roughest; most crude or unrefined.

Important Terms (4/4)

favour

Face; expression; appearance. A common Renaissance usage.

feature

Form; shape; appearance; the lineaments of a face. Retains its broader sense of the whole face and figure.

flattery

Excessive or insincere praise; here, potentially self-deception in how love perceives reality.
