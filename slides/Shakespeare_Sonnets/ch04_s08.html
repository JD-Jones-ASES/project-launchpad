Chapter 4, Section 8

Sonnet XXV — "Let those who are in favour with their stars"

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Simile

princes' favorites as marigolds that die at a royal frown

Allusion

"book of honour" as a record of fame, susceptible to erasure

Contrast

public honor vs. private love, structured across three quatrains

Hyperbole

"a thousand victories" to emphasize the warrior's long record

Couplet resolution

the personal happiness statement after the impersonal examples
