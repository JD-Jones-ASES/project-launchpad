Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 7

Sonnet LXV

Study Slides

Key Themes

Beauty's absolute vulnerability

Ranked against brass, stone, and the sea, beauty is weakest of all.

Time's invincibility

The rhetorical questions perform the hopelessness of finding any natural defense against time.

Verse as miracle

The couplet frames poetic immortality not as a certainty but as an almost impossible miracle.

Key Literary Devices

Rhetorical questions

Lines 11–14 fire three unanswerable questions to demonstrate helplessness before Time.

Simile

Beauty's action is "no stronger than a flower."

Military metaphor

"Wrackful siege of battering days" casts Time as a besieging army.

Vocabulary

O'ersways

Overpowers, overwhelms

Wrackful

Ruinous, causing wreck and destruction

Alack

An expression of distress or lament (alas)

Spoil

Plunder; the act of stripping and ruining

Forbid

To prevent, to stop
