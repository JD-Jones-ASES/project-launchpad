Chapter 3, Section 13

Sonnet XIII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Lease metaphor

The youth "holds" himself only for the duration of his life, as a tenant holds a lease — temporary, not permanent.

House metaphor

The "fair house" of lineage that must be upheld against the "barren rage of death's eternal cold."

Pronoun shift

"You/your" instead of "thou/thy" — a more respectful, tender form of address in Elizabethan English.

Aphoristic couplet

"You had a father: let your son say so" — the most compressed, emotionally direct statement in the sequence.
