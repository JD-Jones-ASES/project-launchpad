Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 7, Section 18

Sonnet LXXVI

Study Slides

Key Themes

Constancy in love and verse

The speaker's thematic monotony is a virtue — the expression of devoted, singular love.

Novelty versus constancy

The age demands variation; the speaker offers the paradox of the sun — same yet perpetually fresh.

Verse as self-portrait

So distinctive is the speaker's style that his every word identifies him.

Key Literary Devices

Rhetorical self-questioning

Four "Why" questions (lines 1–10) set up the sestet's resolution.

Clothing metaphor

"Noted weed" — the speaker's style as a recognizable garment.

Solar simile (couplet)

The sun, daily new and old, is the model for how constant love remains perpetually alive.

Vocabulary

Barren

Lacking, empty, unproductive

Variation

Change, novelty in style

Compounds strange

Unusual combinations of words or unusual compound words

Noted weed

A well-known garment; a recognizable style

Argument

Theme, subject matter
