Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 9

Sonnet IX

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Personification

The world as a widow mourning the youth's childless death.

Contrast

Private widow (consoled by children's faces) vs. world-widow (unconsoled) — amplifying the scale of loss.

Economic analogy

Financial waste vs. beauty's waste — money redistributes; beauty destroyed by non-reproduction simply ends.

Escalating moral language

From "niggard" and "glutton" (earlier sonnets) to "murd'rous shame" — the accusation intensifies.
