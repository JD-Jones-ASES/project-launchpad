Chapter 10, Section 14

Sonnet CXIII — Study Guide

Study Slides

Literary Devices

Conceit

The eye living in the mind, processing the outer world through an inner image of the beloved.

Merismus

The cataloguing of opposite extremes (mountain/sea, day/night, crow/dove) covers the whole range of visible reality.

Paradox

True faithfulness of mind corrupts the eye's truthful function.

Personification

The eye acts independently, delivering or failing to deliver forms to the heart.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What strange effect does absence have on the speaker's eye in Sonnet CXIII?

The eye becomes 'in the mind' -- it ceases to deliver accurate images to the heart, becoming effectively blind to the outer world.

What does 'doth part his function and is partly blind' mean?

The eye divides its function: it seems to see outwardly but is essentially blinded inwardly by being filled with the beloved's image.

What happens to all objects the speaker's eye encounters?

Whether bird, flower, mountain, sea, crow, or dove -- all shapes are transformed and reshaped into the beloved's features.

What does 'replete with you' mean in the penultimate line?

The speaker's mind is completely filled with the beloved -- there is no room for any other image; the beloved saturates all perception.

What is the paradox of the couplet?

'My most true mind thus maketh mine [eye] untrue' -- the mind's absolute faithfulness to the beloved makes the eye faithless to external reality.

How is this sonnet an extension of the love-blindness theme?

Total devotion distorts perception -- the beloved's image overlays everything, making the lover unable to see the world as it actually is.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What literary device is 'shapes them to your feature'?

A metaphor of creative distortion -- love as sculptor reshaping all external objects into the single beloved form.

What does Sonnet CXIII suggest about obsessive love?

It colonizes perception entirely; the external world becomes irrelevant because the lover's mind projects the beloved onto every experience.
