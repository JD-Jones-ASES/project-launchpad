Chapter 3, Section 14

Sonnet XIV

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Extended conceit

The eight-line disclaimer followed by the revelation that the youth's eyes replace the stars as the source of prophecy.

Volta

The "But" at line 9 — after all that is denied, the true source of knowledge is revealed.

Elevated stakes

The youth's choice determines not just his personal fate but the survival of truth and beauty themselves.

Conditional prophecy

"Truth and beauty shall together thrive, / If from thyself, to store thou wouldst convert" — a positive outcome is possible, but conditional.
