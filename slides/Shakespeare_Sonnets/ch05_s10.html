Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 5, Section 10

Sonnet XLII

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Irony / self-aware consolation

"Sweet flattery!" names the logical trick as the speaker performs it — a moment of painful lucidity within consolation.

Paradox

The oneness-of-persons argument (echoing XXXVI, XXXIX) used as psychological comfort — love's metaphysics pressed into service as a survival mechanism.

Cross metaphor

The speaker "bears this cross" — absorbing the suffering caused by both betrayals, sacrificially.

Arithmetic of loss

"I lose both twain" — the mathematical finality of total loss stated plainly.
