Shakespeare's Sonnets: A Reading Companion

Chapter 3, Section 11

Sonnet XI

Study Slides

Key Literary Devices

Exact compensation argument

Waning and growing as a mathematical exchange — the child's growth offsets the parent's aging.

Reductio ad absurdum

If all chose not to reproduce, humanity would end in sixty years — exposing the absurdity of the youth's logic.

Seal-and-print metaphor

Nature as master craftsman who carved the youth as a signet seal for multiplication, not single display.

Tricolon contrast

"Wisdom, beauty, and increase" vs. "folly, age, and cold decay" — the two paths set out in parallel three-part lists.

'Herein lives wisdom, beauty, and increase' — having children preserves all three. Without it, only 'folly, age, and cold decay' remain.
