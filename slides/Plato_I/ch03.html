Important Terms (1/2)

Dikasterion

An Athenian law court, staffed by citizen jurors rather than professional judges or lawyers. Cases were decided entirely by a majority vote with no deliberation.

Klepsydra

Literally "water-thief"; a water clock used in Athenian courts to measure each speaker's allotted time.

Asebeia

The Greek word for impiety — disrespect toward the gods recognized by the city. One of the two formal charges against Socrates.

Daimonion

Socrates' inner divine voice or sign, which warned him when he was about to do something wrong. Its existence was cited in the indictment as evidence that Socrates was introducing new divinities.

Parrhesia

"Frank speech" — the valued quality of speaking openly and without artifice. Socrates turned this into a radical refusal to use any tricks of persuasion whatsoever.
