Q: What was Socrates' counter-proposal for his penalty after being found guilty?

He initially proposed that he should receive free meals at the Prytaneum, an honor typically reserved for Olympic victors and war heroes, because his service to the city was more valuable than a sports victory.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Why did Socrates refuse to bring his family into court to plead for mercy?

He believed it was a violation of justice to use emotional manipulation to sway the jury. He argued that a defendant should convince the jury through truth and argument, not by arousing pity.

Q: What are the two possibilities Socrates envisioned for death?

Socrates posited that death is either annihilation (a dreamless, restful sleep) or a migration of the soul to another place where one can question the great heroes and thinkers of the past.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

In what year did the trial of Socrates take place?

The trial took place in $399$ BC.

What was the name of the Athenian law court where Socrates was tried?

The $dikasterion$.

How many Athenian citizens composed the jury for Socrates' trial?

The jury consisted of $501$ citizens.

What distinguished the $dikasterion$ from modern courts regarding legal professionals?

It had no judges, no professional lawyers, and no public prosecutors.

What device was used to measure the time allotted for speeches in the Athenian court?

The $klepsydra$ (a water clock).

How did Athenian jurors indicate a vote for acquittal?

They used a solid bronze token.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which type of bronze token was used by jurors to indicate a vote for conviction?

A token with a hole in the center.

In the Athenian legal system, how was the final penalty determined after a conviction?

The jury chose between a penalty proposed by the accuser and one proposed by the defendant.

Why did Socrates consider his 'old accusers' more dangerous than the specific individuals in court?

Their accusations were based on years of rumor and gossip absorbed by the jurors since childhood.

Which playwright's comedy The Clouds helped create the public caricature of Socrates as a charlatan?

Aristophanes.

The 'old accusers' falsely associated Socrates with the study of heavenly bodies and natural phenomena, a field known as _____.

Natural philosophy.

What is 'sophistry' as defined in the context of Socrates' trial?

The art of winning arguments through rhetoric regardless of the truth.
