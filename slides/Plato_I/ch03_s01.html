Q: What was Socrates' counter-proposal for his penalty after being found guilty?

He initially proposed that he should receive free meals at the Prytaneum, an honor typically reserved for Olympic victors and war heroes, because his service to the city was more valuable than a sports victory.

II. Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Why did Socrates refuse to bring his family into court to plead for mercy?

He believed it was a violation of justice to use emotional manipulation to sway the jury. He argued that a defendant should convince the jury through truth and argument, not by arousing pity.

Q: What are the two possibilities Socrates envisioned for death?

Socrates posited that death is either annihilation (a dreamless, restful sleep) or a migration of the soul to another place where one can question the great heroes and thinkers of the past.
