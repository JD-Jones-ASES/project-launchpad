Quiz Question 1

Justin Martyr proposed the concept of $\textit{logos spermatikos}$ to argue that Greek philosophers had what relationship to Christian truth?

A. They possessed partial glimpses of the divine Word through the cultivation of 'seeds' of truth.

B. They were essentially atheists who accidentally stumbled upon biological truths.

C. They were divinely inspired prophets equal in status to the Hebrew patriarchs.

D. They had plagiarized their best ideas from the secret writings of Moses.

Hint: Consider the metaphor of 'seeds' and how a seed relates to a fully grown plant.

Quiz Question 2

How did Augustine of Hippo reconcile Plato's Theory of Forms with the Christian understanding of a sovereign Creator?

A. He argued that the Forms were the $\textit{rationes aeternae}$ residing within the mind of God.

B. He rejected the Forms as physical idols that distracted from spiritual worship.

C. He claimed the Forms were created beings similar to angels or celestial spirits.

D. He suggested that the Forms existed in a separate, parallel realm that God also obeys.

Hint: Think about where an immaterial, eternal blueprint would logically exist in a monotheistic system.

Quiz Question 3

In the context of Augustine's thought, how does the doctrine of divine simplicity resolve the Euthyphro dilemma?

A. It posits that God's will and God's nature are identical, making the source of goodness internal to God.

B. It suggests that God chooses to follow an external standard of goodness to set an example.

C. It argues that God's power is so absolute that He can make evil actions good by decree.

D. It claims that human reason is too simple to understand the complex parts of God's character.

Hint: Consider the implication of God being 'Goodness itself' rather than merely possessing goodness as a trait.

Quiz Question 4

Boethius's use of the figure 'Lady Philosophy' in the $\textit{Consolation of Philosophy}$ is a prime example of which literary technique also used by Plato?

A. $\textit{Prosopopoeia}$, or giving a voice to an abstract idea.

B. $\textit{Elenchus}$, or the method of logical refutation through questioning.

C. $\textit{Anamnesis}$, or the theory that all learning is actually recollection.

D. $\textit{Allegory}$, used specifically to hide truths from the uninitiated.

Hint: Focus on the term used to describe the personification of the Laws in the Crito.

Quiz Question 5

Which intellectual shift allowed for the 'Baptism of Plato' to eventually be joined by an 'Aristotelian synthesis' in the High Middle Ages?

A. The recovery of Aristotle's works through Arabic translations and Islamic commentaries.

B. The complete rejection of Augustine's Platonic framework by the Vatican.

C. The discovery of the original Greek manuscripts of the $\textit{Republic}$ in Rome.

D. The decree by the Council of Florence that Aristotle was a 'Christian before Christ.'

Hint: Identify the cultural intermediaries who kept Greek logic alive while the West lost its knowledge of Greek.

Quiz Question 6

D. It is the set of laws found in the Bible that apply only to nature and physics.
