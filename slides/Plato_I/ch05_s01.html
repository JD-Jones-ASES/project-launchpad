The Legacy of Plato and the Foundations of Western Philosophy

Study Slides

The Academy

Curriculum

Beyond philosophy, students studied geometry, astronomy, and harmonics (the mathematical study of musical intervals).

Purpose

Plato believed mathematics trained the mind to think abstractly and grasp universal truths rather than physical particulars.

The Entrance Inscription

"Let no one ignorant of geometry enter here."

Aristotle: The Independent Critic

Plato’s View

Looked upward toward eternal Forms as the source of reality.

Aristotle’s View

Looked outward at the natural world, arguing that universals exist within particular things rather than apart from them.

The School of Athens

Raphael’s fresco captures this tension, depicting Plato pointing to the heavens and Aristotle gesturing toward the earth.

Neoplatonism

The One

A supreme, simple source of being beyond description.

The Emanation

Reality flows from "The One" to Nous (Divine Mind/Forms), then to the Soul, and finally to the material world.

The Goal

Human life and philosophy aim for a mystical union and ascent back to "The One."

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Alfred North Whitehead, the European philosophical tradition consists of a series of _____ to Plato.

footnotes

What was the title of the 1929 book by Alfred North Whitehead that famously characterized the legacy of Plato?

Process and Reality

What does Alfred North Whitehead mean when he refers to Western philosophy as "footnotes to Plato"?

The fundamental questions Plato asked are still the primary subjects of philosophical debate today.

Concept: Metaphysics

Definition: The branch of philosophy concerned with the ultimate nature of reality, including what exists and how the world is structured.

What central question regarding piety and goodness is posed in Plato's dialogue, the Euthyphro?

Is goodness defined by God's command, or does God command it because it is already good?

In the Apology, what did Socrates famously claim about the value of an unexamined life?

The unexamined life is not worth living.

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to the Apology, what pursuit should matter more than wealth, reputation, or survival?

The pursuit of wisdom.

What principle regarding legal obedience does Socrates maintain in the dialogue Crito?

We must obey just laws even when it involves a personal cost of everything.

What ethical stance does Socrates take in the Crito regarding responding to injustice?

We must never return injustice for injustice.

In what year did Plato found the Academy?

$387$ BC

Where was Plato’s Academy located in relation to Athens?

In a grove sacred to the hero Akademos on the outskirts of the city.

How did the Academy differ from a modern school in its structure?

It was a community of thinkers who discussed philosophy, mathematics, and science rather than a system of classrooms and exams.
