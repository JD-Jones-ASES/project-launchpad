The Legacy of Plato and the Foundations of Western Philosophy

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Historical Context (1/2)

Curriculum

Beyond philosophy, students studied geometry, astronomy, and harmonics (the mathematical study of musical intervals).

Purpose

Plato believed mathematics trained the mind to think abstractly and grasp universal truths rather than physical particulars.

The Entrance Inscription

"Let no one ignorant of geometry enter here."

Plato’s View

Looked upward toward eternal Forms as the source of reality.

Aristotle’s View

Looked outward at the natural world, arguing that universals exist within particular things rather than apart from them.

Key Concepts and Historical Context (2/2)

The School of Athens

Raphael’s fresco captures this tension, depicting Plato pointing to the heavens and Aristotle gesturing toward the earth.

The One

A supreme, simple source of being beyond description.

The Emanation

Reality flows from "The One" to *Nous* (Divine Mind/Forms), then to the *Soul*, and finally to the material world.

The Goal

Human life and philosophy aim for a mystical union and ascent back to "The One."
