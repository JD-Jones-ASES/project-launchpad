The Transition from Mythos to Logos

Mythos

Traditional stories about the gods used to explain natural phenomena (e.g., lightning as Zeus’s thunderbolt, seasons as Persephone’s descent to the underworld).

Logos

Reasoned argument based on observation and logic. This approach sought natural, rather than mythological, answers.

The Milesians

Philosopher. Proposed Arche

Key Contributions/Beliefs

Thales. Water

Regarded as the first Western philosopher. Proposed water as the fundamental substance. Predicted a solar eclipse and measured pyramids using shadows.

Anaximander. Apeiron

Argued water was too specific. Proposed the "boundless" or "indefinite"—an infinite, formless substance from which all things emerge.

Anaximenes. Air

Explained qualitative differences through quantitative changes (rarefaction/condensation). Air becomes fire (thinning) or stone (thickening).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Apeiron

The "boundless" or "indefinite"; Anaxander’s proposed infinite, formless origin of all things.

Arche

The fundamental substance, first principle, or origin of the universe.

Atomos

Literally "un-cuttable"; the tiny, indivisible particles proposed by Democritus.

Kenon

The "void" or empty space in which atoms move.

Logos

A Greek word meaning "reason," "account," or "rational principle"; the governing order of the universe.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Mythos

Explanations of the world through traditional stories and the actions of gods.

Nous

"Mind" or "intellect"; the cosmic ordering force introduced by Anaxagoras.

Physis

"Nature" or "the way things grow"; the root of the word "physics."

Physikoi

A term for the pre-Socratic "investigators of nature."

Transmigration of the Soul

The belief that the soul is immortal and is reborn in new bodies (human or animal) after death.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What single revolutionary conviction was shared by all Pre-Socratic thinkers?

The world could be understood through reason rather than myth.

Term: $\alpha\rho\chi\eta$ (Arche)

The fundamental substance or first principle from which all things derive and return.

Which city is traditionally identified as the birthplace of Western philosophy?

Miletus.

According to Thales of Miletus, what is the $\alpha\rho\chi\eta$ of all things?

Water.

Which philosopher was credited with predicting a solar eclipse and measuring the height of pyramids using shadows?

Thales of Miletus.

What was the major intellectual breakthrough of Thales, regardless of his specific answer to the nature of reality?

Seeking a natural rather than mythological explanation for the world's composition.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why did Anaximander argue that the $\alpha\rho\chi\eta$ could not be a specific element like water or fire?

Because specific elements are defined by their opposition to one another.

Term: $\alpha\pi\epsilon\iota\rho o u$ (Apeiron)

The infinite, formless, or "boundless" substance proposed by Anaximander as the origin of all things.

According to Anaximenes, what is the fundamental substance of the universe?

Air.

In Anaximenes' theory, by what process does air become fire?

Rarefaction (becoming thinner).

In Anaximenes' theory, by what process does air become wind, cloud, water, earth, and stone?

Condensation (becoming thicker).

What significant scientific concept did Anaximenes anticipate by explaining qualitative differences through quantitative changes?

The foundations of modern chemistry and physics.
