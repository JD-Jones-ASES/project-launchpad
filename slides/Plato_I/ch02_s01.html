Important Terms (1/2)

King Archon (archon basileus)

The Athenian magistrate responsible for cases involving religion and homicide.

Meletus

The young poet who served as the primary prosecutor in the case against Socrates, accusing him of impiety and corrupting the youth.

Impiety (asebeia)

A serious charge in democratic Athens, meaning a failure to honor the gods of Athens, disrespect for divine law, or sacrilege. It could carry the death penalty.

Naxos

A large island in the Aegean Sea where Euthyphro's family owned an estate, the setting for the incident involving his father.

Diviner (mantis)

A person who claims special knowledge of the gods' will, often interpreting omens, dreams, or inspired speech. Euthyphro identifies as a mantis.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the setting for Plato's dialogue Euthyphro?

The dialogue opens outside the Porch of the King Archon in Athens.

Why has Socrates been summoned to court at the beginning of the Euthyphro?

He has been indicted by Meletus on charges of impiety (asebeia) and corrupting the minds of young people.

Who is Meletus in the context of the Euthyphro?

Meletus is a young poet who served as the primary prosecutor against Socrates, accusing him of impiety and corrupting the youth.

What were the potential consequences of the charges against Socrates in democratic Athens?

Charges of impiety and corrupting the youth were serious offenses that could carry the death penalty.

What is Euthyphro's reason for being at the court?

Euthyphro is there to prosecute his own father for murder, believing his father committed an impious act.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the central irony of Socrates' meeting with Euthyphro?

Socrates, who is accused of impiety, encounters Euthyphro, who claims to be an expert on piety and is prosecuting his own father in its name.

What is the dramatic sequence of the dialogues Euthyphro, Apology, and Crito?

Euthyphro takes place before Socrates' trial, Apology describes the trial, and Crito takes place after the trial, in prison, forming a continuous narrative.

How is Euthyphro characterized in the dialogue?

Euthyphro is described as a professional religious expert, a diviner (mantis), confident, self-important, and unreflective in his assumptions about piety.
