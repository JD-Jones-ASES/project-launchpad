Important Terms (1/3)

Philosophy

Philosophos

A "lover of wisdom," coined by Pythagoras, signifying a seeker of truth rather than one who already possesses it.

Ethics (Moral Philosophy)

The branch of philosophy that asks how we ought to live, focusing on questions of good, justice, and moral duty. From Greek *ethos* ("character" or "custom").

Metaphysics

The branch of philosophy that asks what is ultimately real, exploring questions about existence, the nature of reality beyond the physical world, and ultimate principles.

Important Terms (2/3)

Logic

The branch of philosophy that studies the rules of correct reasoning, focusing on what makes an argument valid and when conclusions truly follow from premises.

Political Philosophy

The branch of philosophy that asks how societies should be organized, examining questions about government authority, justice, and citizens' obligations to the state.

Dialectic (Dialektike)

The method of seeking truth through structured conversation—asking questions, testing answers, and following the argument wherever it leads.

Polis (plural poleis)

The independent city-state, the basic political unit of ancient Greece (e.g., Athens, Sparta).

Mythos

Explanations based on traditional stories, myths, or divine interventions.

Important Terms (3/3)

Logos

Explanations based on reason, logic, and natural processes.
