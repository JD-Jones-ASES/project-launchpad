Important Terms (1/3)

Dualism

A philosophical position that divides reality into two fundamentally different kinds of things, often mind and matter, or soul and body. Plato's dualism sharply separates the immaterial, immortal s...

Logistikon

Plato's term for the rational part of the soul, responsible for thought, reason, and seeking truth; it should rule the other parts.

Thumoeides

Plato's term for the spirited part of the soul, associated with emotions like anger, honor, and courage; it should support reason's authority.

Epithumetikon

Plato's term for the appetitive part of the soul, driven by physical desires like hunger, thirst, and sexual urges; it should obey reason.

Hylomorphism

Aristotle's metaphysical theory that every substance is a composite of matter and form. In living beings, the body is matter and the soul is its form.

Important Terms (2/3)

Nutritive Soul

The lowest level of soul for Aristotle, responsible for basic life functions like growth, nourishment, and reproduction, found in plants.

Sensitive Soul

The second level of soul for Aristotle, adding capacities for sensation, desire, and movement, found in animals.

Rational Soul

The highest level of soul for Aristotle, unique to humans, encompassing the capacities for thought, reasoning, and deliberation. It includes the lower levels.

Hexis
