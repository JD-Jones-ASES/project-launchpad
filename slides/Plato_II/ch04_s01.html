Key Concepts (2/2)

The Final Argument for Immortality

Socrates' comprehensive argument, which asserts the soul's immortality by demonstrating that the soul, as the bringer of life, cannot admit its opposite (death) and therefore must be indestructible.

Important Terms (1/3)

Natural Philosophy

The ancient equivalent of science, involving investigations into the physical causes of natural phenomena, material composition, and the structure of the cosmos.

Anaxagoras (c. 500–428 BC)

A pre-Socratic philosopher who proposed that *nous* (Mind) was responsible for ordering the cosmos but disappointed Socrates by using purely physical explanations for specific phenomena.

Nous (Mind)

An intelligent principle proposed by Anaxagoras as the cause and ordering principle of all things.

Second Sailing (deuteros plous)

A metaphorical turn from studying reality directly through the senses to studying it indirectly through arguments, definitions, and rational inquiry (logoi).

Logoi

Greek word for "words," "arguments," "reasons," or "accounts." In Socrates' "second sailing," it refers to the method of examining reality through rational discourse.

Important Terms (2/3)

Hypothesis

A proposed explanation or principle used as a starting point for reasoning and investigation, which is then tested for consistency.

Aitia

Greek for "cause" or "explanation." In Plato's usage, it refers to the formal reason why a thing is what it is, often linked to its participation in a Form.

Forms

Perfect, eternal, unchanging, non-physical blueprints or essences that exist independently of the physical world (e.g., the Form of Beauty, the Form of Life). Physical things "participate" in Forms.

Athanatos

Greek for "deathless" or "immortal," referring to something not subject to death.

Anolethros

Greek for "indestructible" or "imperishable," meaning something that cannot be destroyed or cease to exist.

Important Terms (3/3)

Psyche

The Greek word for "soul" or "life principle." For Plato, it is the animating force that makes a living being alive.
