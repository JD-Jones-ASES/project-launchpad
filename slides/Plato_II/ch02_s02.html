Important Terms (1/2)

Meno

A dialogue by Plato (c. 385 BC) named after Meno of Thessaly, known for the slave boy demonstration and the theory of recollection.

Phaedo

Another Platonic dialogue, which provides the metaphysical explanation for the theory of recollection, linking it to the soul's prior knowledge of the Forms.

René Descartes (1596–1650)

French philosopher, often called the father of modern philosophy, who argued for the existence of innate ideas (e.g., God, infinity, mathematical truths) implanted by God.

Immanuel Kant (1724–1804)

German philosopher who, in *Critique of Pure Reason*, argued for *a priori* knowledge, asserting that fundamental categories like space and time are conditions for experience, not derived from it.

Tabula Rasa

Latin for "blank slate," a concept promoted by British empiricists like John Locke, which argues that the mind begins devoid of innate knowledge and all understanding comes from sensory experience.
