Important Terms (1/2)

Affinity (suggeneia)

Kinship or natural likeness. In this context, the soul's resemblance to the divine and unchanging realm.

Forms

Plato's term for the eternal, perfect, invisible realities (e.g., Beauty Itself, Justice Itself) that particular physical things only approximate. They are grasped by the mind, not the senses.

Sepulchres

Burial vaults or tombs. Mentioned in the ghost passage as places where impure souls/ghosts might prowl.

Lyre

A stringed musical instrument, central to ancient Greek music. Used by Simmias in his analogy to represent the body, with its harmony representing the soul.

Misology (misologia)

From Greek *misos* (hatred) + *logos* (word, argument, reason). The hatred of argument or reason, which Socrates warns against.

Important Terms (2/2)

Misanthropy (misanthropia)

Hatred of people. Socrates uses this as an analogy to explain the origins of misology.
