Important Terms (1/3)

Tectonic plates

Rigid pieces of Earth's lithosphere that move slowly over the asthenosphere.

Lithosphere

The rigid outer layer of Earth, consisting of the crust (oceanic or continental) and the uppermost portion of the mantle. It is broken into tectonic plates.

Asthenosphere

The partially molten, plastically deforming layer of the upper mantle beneath the lithosphere, allowing tectonic plates to move.

Divergent boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates are moving apart from each other, leading to the creation of new crust.

Mid-ocean ridge

An underwater mountain range formed at a divergent boundary in an ocean basin where new oceanic crust is generated.

Important Terms (2/3)

Rift valley

A long, narrow depression formed at a divergent boundary beneath a continent where the continental crust is stretching and thinning.

Convergent boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates are moving toward each other, resulting in subduction or mountain building.

Subduction

The process by which a denser tectonic plate (usually oceanic) is forced beneath a less dense plate at a convergent boundary, descending into the mantle.

Island arc

A chain of volcanic islands formed above a subducting oceanic plate at an oceanic-oceanic convergent boundary.

Transform boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates slide horizontally past each other, neither creating nor destroying crust.

Important Terms (3/3)

Fault

A fracture or zone of fractures in the Earth's crust where blocks of rock move relative to each other, especially common at transform boundaries.

Mantle convection

The slow circulation of hot mantle rock that rises, spreads, cools, and sinks, exerting a drag on the base of tectonic plates.

Ridge push

A gravitational force that causes elevated material at mid-ocean ridges to slide downward and outward, pushing the plate away from the ridge.

Slab pull

The force exerted by a cold, dense subducting plate sinking into the mantle under its own weight, pulling the rest of the plate behind it.
