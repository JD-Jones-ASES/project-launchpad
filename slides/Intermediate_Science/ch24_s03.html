Series and Parallel Circuits

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Circuit Diagrams

Standardized graphical representations using schematic symbols to depict circuit components and their connections, allowing for clear and universal understanding among physicists and engineers.

Series Circuits

An arrangement where components are connected in a single, unbroken loop, creating only one path for current.

Parallel Circuits

An arrangement where components are connected across the same two points, creating multiple separate paths (branches) for current to flow.

Combination Circuits

Circuits that contain both series and parallel arrangements of components. These are analyzed by systematically simplifying parallel and series sections until a single equivalent resistance is found.

Important Terms (1/2)

Series Circuit

A circuit arrangement where components are connected end-to-end, forming a single path for current.

Parallel Circuit

A circuit arrangement where components are connected across the same two points, creating multiple alternative paths for current.

Circuit Diagram (Schematic Diagram)

A simplified, standardized drawing that uses symbols to represent electrical components and their connections in a circuit.

Schematic Symbol

A graphical icon used in circuit diagrams to represent a specific electrical component (e.g., resistor, battery, wire).

Equivalent Resistance ($R_T$)

The single resistance that could replace all resistors in a circuit (or part of a circuit) without changing the total current or voltage in the rest of the circuit.

Important Terms (2/2)

Ohm's Law

The fundamental relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in a circuit: $V = IR$. This law is used extensively in analyzing both series and parallel circuits.

Define a 'series circuit.'

A circuit in which all components are connected in a single loop, one after another, so that there is only one path for current to flow.

What is the consequence if any component breaks or is removed in a series circuit?

The entire circuit is interrupted, and current stops flowing.

State the rule for current in a series circuit.

Current is the same everywhere in a series circuit ($I_{total} = I_1 = I_2 = ...$). Every coulomb of charge passes through every component.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How is the total resistance calculated in a series circuit?

Total resistance equals the sum of individual resistances ($R_T = R_1 + R_2 + ...$).

What is the effect of adding more resistors in series on the total resistance and current?

Adding more resistors in series always increases the total resistance and therefore decreases the current (for a fixed battery voltage).

How does voltage divide among resistors in a series circuit?

In a series circuit, voltage divides in proportion to resistance; the larger resistor will have a larger voltage drop.

Define a 'parallel circuit.'

A circuit in which components are connected across the same two points, creating multiple paths for current to flow.

What happens to a parallel circuit if one branch breaks?

Current continues to flow through the other branches, as there are multiple paths.

State the rule for voltage in a parallel circuit.

Voltage is the same across each branch ($V_{total} = V_1 = V_2 = ...$), because every branch is connected directly across the battery terminals.
