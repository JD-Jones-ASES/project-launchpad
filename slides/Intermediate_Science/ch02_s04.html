Important Terms (1/3)

Magnetic field

A region of space around a magnet or a moving electric charge in which magnetic forces can be detected. Earth's magnetic field extends from the interior out into space.

Dynamo theory

The scientific explanation for Earth's magnetic field, proposing that it is generated by the convective motion of electrically conducting liquid iron in the outer core acting as a self-sustaining e...

Geodynamo

The specific term for Earth's natural dynamo, referring to the process by which the planet generates its magnetic field.

Magnetic poles

The locations on Earth's surface where the magnetic field lines are vertical (pointing straight down into or up out of the ground).

Magnetic declination

The angle between the direction a compass needle points (magnetic north) and true geographic north, which varies with location.

Important Terms (2/3)

Paleomagnetism

The study of the record of Earth's past magnetic field preserved in rocks, used to reconstruct ancient field directions and intensities.

Magnetic reversal

A phenomenon where Earth's magnetic north and south poles switch places, resulting in a change in the field's polarity.

Magnetosphere

The region of space surrounding Earth that is dominated by Earth's magnetic field, acting as a shield against the solar wind.

Solar wind

A continuous stream of high-energy charged particles (mostly protons and electrons) emitted by the Sun.

Aurora (borealis/australis)

Spectacular displays of light in the upper atmosphere near Earth's magnetic poles, caused by solar wind particles colliding with atmospheric gases.

Important Terms (3/3)

Geomagnetic storm

A temporary disturbance of Earth's magnetosphere caused by intense solar activity, which can affect technology and extend auroras to lower latitudes.
