Biochemistry: The Molecules of Life

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Biochemistry

The study of the chemical composition and processes of living organisms, bridging chemistry and biology.

Monomers and Polymers

Many large biological molecules (polymers) are built from repeating smaller units (monomers).

Dehydration Synthesis (Condensation Reaction)

A chemical reaction that joins two monomers by removing a molecule of water, forming a new covalent bond. This process builds polymers.

Hydrolysis

A chemical reaction that breaks a covalent bond between two monomers by adding a molecule of water. This process disassembles polymers.

Carbohydrates

Organic compounds (C, H, O in a 1:2:1 ratio) that serve as primary energy sources and structural components.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Lipids

A diverse group of nonpolar, water-insoluble (hydrophobic) organic compounds. They function in long-term energy storage, membrane structure, and signaling.

Proteins

Highly diverse polymers of amino acids, performing a vast array of functions including enzymes, structural support, transport, and immunity.

Nucleic Acids

Polymers of nucleotide monomers responsible for storing and transmitting genetic information.

Investigation: Testing Foods for Biomolecules

Starch Test (Iodine)

Iodine solution turns dark blue-black in the presence of starch.

Simple Sugar Test (Benedict's Solution)

Benedict's solution, when heated in a water bath, changes color from blue to green, yellow, orange, or red in the presence of simple sugars (the warmer the color, the more sugar).

Protein Test (Biuret Reagent)

Biuret reagent changes from blue to purple or violet in the presence of protein.

Lipid Test (Brown Paper)

Rubbing a food sample on brown paper and letting it dry will leave a translucent (greasy) spot if lipids are present.

What is biochemistry?

The branch of chemistry that studies the structure, function, and interactions of the molecules found in living organisms, bridging chemistry and biology.

What are the four great classes of carbon-based molecules central to biochemistry?

Carbohydrates, lipids, proteins, and nucleic acids.

Name three important polysaccharides found in living organisms and state their primary functions.

Starch (energy storage in plants), Glycogen (energy storage in animals), and Cellulose (structural component of plant cell walls).

Why do starch, glycogen, and cellulose have different properties despite being made solely of glucose monomers?

The glucose units are linked in different geometric arrangements, demonstrating that a molecule's structure determines its function.

What is the defining characteristic that groups lipids together, and are they considered true polymers?

They are largely nonpolar, hydrophobic, and insoluble in water. No, they are not true polymers as they are not built from identical monomer subunits.

Describe the basic structure of a fat or oil molecule.

It consists of one glycerol molecule bonded to three fatty acid chains.
