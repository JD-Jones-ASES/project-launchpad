Key Concepts

Forces Cause Motion Changes

A force is a push or pull that can make an object start moving, stop, change direction, speed up, slow down, or even deform its shape. Without a force, an object's motion will not change.

Force is a Vector Quantity

Forces have both magnitude (strength) and direction. This means they must be added and subtracted considering their direction, and their SI unit is the newton (N).

Important Terms (1/3)

Force

A push or a pull exerted on an object that can cause a change in its motion (start, stop, change direction, speed up, slow down) or shape. It is a vector quantity measured in newtons (N).

Newton (N)

The SI unit of force, named after Isaac Newton.

Contact Forces

Forces that require physical contact between objects to be exerted, such as applied force, friction, normal force, tension, spring force, and air resistance.

Non-Contact Forces (Field Forces)

Forces that act over a distance without the objects physically touching, such as gravitational force, magnetic force, and electric force.

Applied Force

A direct push or pull exerted by a person or machine on an object.

Important Terms (2/3)

Friction

A force that opposes the sliding or rolling motion of one surface over another. It acts parallel to the contact surface and opposite to the direction of motion.

Normal Force

The support force exerted by a surface on an object resting on it. It always acts perpendicular ("normal") to the surface.

Tension

The pulling force transmitted through a string, rope, cable, or chain when it is pulled taut.

Spring Force

The push or pull exerted by a compressed or stretched spring, proportional to its deformation.

Air Resistance (Drag)

A friction-like force exerted by air on a moving object, opposing its motion.

Important Terms (3/3)

Gravitational Force (Gravity)

The attractive force between any two objects that have mass. Near Earth's surface, it pulls objects downward.

Net Force (Resultant Force)

The vector sum of all individual forces acting on an object. It determines if an object will accelerate and in which direction.

Equilibrium

A state where the net force on an object is zero. An object in equilibrium either remains at rest or moves at a constant velocity (zero acceleration).

Free-Body Diagram (FBD)

A simplified drawing used to analyze forces, depicting an object as a dot or box with labeled arrows representing all the forces acting on it, showing their magnitude and direction.
