Connecting Concepts: Structure Determines Function

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Structure Determines Function

This is the core unifying principle stating that the physical arrangement, composition, or organization of an object or system dictates its capabilities, behavior, or role.

Hierarchical Organization in Living Systems

The human body is organized from cells to tissues, organs, organ systems, and an organism. At each level, the structure of a component is precisely adapted to its specific job.

Cell Specialization

The process where cells develop distinctive structures (e.g., biconcave red blood cells, long nerve axons) that enable them to perform particular functions more efficiently.

Atomic Structure and Chemical Reactivity

While atomic number (protons) defines an element, the arrangement of its electrons, particularly valence electrons, dictates its chemical behavior, bonding preferences, and reactivity.

Unifying Principle

Important Terms (1/3)

Structure Determines Function

The scientific principle that the form, composition, or organization of something dictates its role or behavior.

Cell Specialization

The process by which cells develop specific structures to perform specific functions.

Homeostasis

The ability of an organism to maintain internal stability, often involving feedback loops where structural components (receptors, control centers, effectors) are matched to regulatory functions.

Divergent Boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates pull apart, leading to features like rift valleys and mid-ocean ridges.

Convergent Boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates move towards each other, often resulting in subduction zones, trenches, volcanic arcs, and mountain ranges.

Important Terms (2/3)

Transform Boundary

A plate boundary where two tectonic plates slide horizontally past each other, primarily causing faults and earthquakes.

Subduction Zone

An area where one tectonic plate is forced beneath another, typically an oceanic plate beneath a continental or another oceanic plate.

Atomic Number

The number of protons in an atom's nucleus, which determines the element's identity.

Valence Electrons

The electrons in the outermost shell of an atom, which are primarily involved in chemical bonding and determine an element's chemical reactivity.

Electron Configuration

The distribution of electrons of an atom or molecule in atomic or molecular orbitals.

Important Terms (3/3)

Periodic Trends

Predictable patterns in the properties of elements across the periodic table, such as atomic radius, ionization energy, and electronegativity, which are consequences of atomic structure.

Free-Body Diagram

A diagram used in physics to visualize the forces acting on an object, illustrating the "structure" of the force environment.

Newton's Second Law

The law stating that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass ($F_{net} = ma$).

Crumple Zones

Structurally engineered areas in vehicles designed to deform during a collision, absorbing energy and protecting passengers.
