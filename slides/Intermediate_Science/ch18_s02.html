Key Concepts (1/2)

Universal Language of Chemistry

Chemical equations offer a standardized, concise, and internationally understood way to describe chemical reactions, eliminating the need for lengthy word descriptions.

Structure of a Chemical Equation

Every chemical equation consists of reactants on the left, an arrow indicating the reaction direction, and products on the right, separated by plus signs if there are multiple substances.

Reactant

A substance that enters into and is consumed in a chemical reaction. Reactants are written on the left side of the equation arrow.

Product

A substance that is formed as a result of a chemical reaction. Products are written on the right side of the equation arrow.

Coefficient

A number placed before a chemical formula in an equation to indicate how many units (molecules or formula units) of that substance participate in the reaction.
