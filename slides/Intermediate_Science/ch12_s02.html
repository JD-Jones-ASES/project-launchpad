Important Terms (1/3)

Electromagnetic radiation

Energy emitted from the Sun in the form of waves, including visible light, ultraviolet (UV) radiation, and infrared (IR) radiation.

Albedo

The fraction of incoming solar radiation that is reflected by a surface. Light surfaces have high albedo, dark surfaces have low albedo.

Positive feedback loop

A cycle in which the effect reinforces its own cause, leading to further change in the same direction.

Infrared (IR) radiation

Invisible thermal energy re-emitted by Earth's surface after absorbing solar energy, felt as heat.

Greenhouse effect

The process by which certain gases in the atmosphere absorb infrared radiation emitted by Earth's surface and re-emit it, warming the lower atmosphere.

Important Terms (2/3)

Greenhouse gases

Atmospheric gases that absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, including water vapor (H2O), carbon dioxide (CO2), methane (CH4), and nitrous oxide (N2O).

Radiation

The transfer of energy through electromagnetic waves; the only way energy travels through the vacuum of space.

Conduction

The transfer of thermal energy through direct contact between molecules.

Convection

The transfer of thermal energy by the movement of a fluid (liquid or gas); the dominant heat transfer mechanism in the atmosphere.

Convection current

The circulatory motion of fluid (air or water) driven by differences in density due to heating and cooling.

Important Terms (3/3)

Radiative balance

The state where Earth radiates back to space the same amount of energy it receives from the Sun, maintaining a stable average temperature.

Enhanced greenhouse effect

The additional warming of Earth's atmosphere due to human-caused increases in greenhouse gas concentrations, trapping more outgoing infrared radiation.

The Sun: Earth's Energy Engine

• The Sun is the primary source of energy that powers all Earth's weather and climate systems.

• A minuscule fraction of the Sun's vast energy output reaches Earth.

• How this energy is processed upon arrival fundamentally shapes daily temperatures, wind patterns, and global climate.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary engine that drives Earth's weather?

The Sun, by radiating enormous amounts of energy into space.

What forms of electromagnetic radiation does solar energy reach Earth as?

Visible light, ultraviolet (UV) radiation, and infrared (IR) radiation.

What percentage of incoming solar energy is immediately reflected back into space?

About 30%.

Define albedo.

Albedo is the fraction of incoming solar radiation that is reflected by a surface.

Compare the albedo of fresh snow to the open ocean.

Fresh snow has a high albedo (0.80–0.90), reflecting 80–90% of sunlight. The open ocean has a low albedo (0.06), absorbing 94% of sunlight.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What happens to the 70% of solar energy that is not reflected by Earth?

About 20% is absorbed by the atmosphere, and about 50% is absorbed by Earth's surface.

What form of energy does the Earth's surface re-emit after absorbing solar energy?

It re-emits invisible thermal energy called infrared radiation (IR).
