Important Terms (1/2)

Law of Conservation of Mass

The scientific principle stating that mass in an isolated system is neither created nor destroyed by chemical reactions or physical transformations.

Balanced Chemical Equation

A chemical equation in which the number of atoms for each element is the same on both the reactant and product sides.

Coefficient

A number placed in front of a chemical formula in an equation to indicate how many molecules or formula units of that substance are involved in the reaction.

Subscript

A small number written after a chemical symbol in a formula, indicating the number of atoms of that element in one molecule or formula unit.

Balancing by Inspection

A method for balancing chemical equations by systematically adjusting coefficients through trial and error until the number of atoms of each element is equal on both sides.

Important Terms (2/2)

Stoichiometric Ratio

The ratio of the coefficients of reactants and products in a balanced chemical equation, representing the fixed proportions in which substances react and are produced.

Understanding Coefficients and Subscripts

Subscripts

These are the small numbers written *within* a chemical formula (e.g., the '2' in H₂O or O₂).

Coefficients

These are the full-sized numbers placed *in front of* a chemical formula (e.g., the '2' in 2H₂O).
