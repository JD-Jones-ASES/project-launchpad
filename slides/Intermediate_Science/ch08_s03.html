Atomic Radius

This is a measure of the size of an atom, typically defined as half the distance between the nuclei of two identical bonded atoms.

Ionization Energy

The energy required to remove the outermost electron from a neutral atom in the gas phase. It indicates how tightly an atom holds its electrons.

Electronegativity

A measure of how strongly an atom attracts electrons in a chemical bond. It quantifies an atom's "electron-pulling" power in a shared bond.

Important Terms (1/2)

Periodic

Repeating at regular intervals; in chemistry, refers to the predictable patterns of element properties in the periodic table.

Atomic radius

Half the distance between the nuclei of two identical atoms bonded together; a measure of atomic size.

Ionization energy

The minimum energy required to remove the most loosely held electron from a neutral gaseous atom.

Electronegativity

A measure of an atom's ability to attract shared electrons in a chemical bond.

Nuclear charge

The positive charge of the atomic nucleus, determined by the number of protons.

Important Terms (2/2)

Electron shielding

The reduction of the effective nuclear charge on an electron due to the presence of other electrons, especially inner-shell electrons, which block some of the nuclear attraction.

Energy level (or Shell)

A region around the nucleus where electrons are likely to be found; higher energy levels are farther from the nucleus.

Large atoms
