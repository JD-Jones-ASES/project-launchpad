Important Terms (1/6)

Cell division

The process by which one cell splits into two, essential for growth, repair, and reproduction.

Cell cycle

The ordered sequence of events that a cell goes through from its formation until it divides into two new daughter cells.

Interphase

The longest phase of the cell cycle, during which the cell grows, carries out its normal functions, and copies its DNA in preparation for division.

G$_1$ phase (first gap)

Sub-phase of interphase where the cell grows, produces new proteins and organelles, and performs normal functions.

S phase (synthesis)

Sub-phase of interphase where the cell copies (replicates) all of its DNA.

Important Terms (2/6)

G$_2$ phase (second gap)

Sub-phase of interphase where the cell continues to grow and produces specific proteins needed for cell division.

Sister chromatids

Two identical copies of a chromosome, joined together by a centromere, formed during S phase.

Centromere

The constricted region where sister chromatids are joined.

Chromosome

A structure made of DNA tightly coiled around proteins called histones, carrying genetic information.

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid)

A long, double helix molecule that carries the genetic instructions for building proteins and controlling cell functions.

Important Terms (3/6)

Histones

Proteins around which DNA coils tightly to form chromosomes.

Mitosis

The process by which a cell's nucleus divides into two genetically identical nuclei.

Mitotic spindle

A structure made of microtubules that forms during prophase and helps separate chromosomes during mitosis.

Centrioles

Small organelles in animal cells from which the mitotic spindle grows.

Nuclear envelope

The double membrane surrounding the nucleus, which breaks down during prophase and reforms during telophase.

Important Terms (4/6)

Nucleolus

A dense structure within the nucleus, involved in ribosome synthesis, which disappears in prophase and reappears in telophase.

Metaphase plate

An imaginary line along the middle of the cell where chromosomes align during metaphase.

Cytokinesis

The division of the cytoplasm following mitosis, resulting in two separate daughter cells.

Cleavage furrow

An inward pinch in animal cells formed by contracting microfilaments during cytokinesis.

Cell plate

A structure formed from Golgi vesicles in plant cells that grows outward to become a new cell wall during cytokinesis.

Important Terms (5/6)

Daughter cells

The two genetically identical cells produced from one parent cell after cell division.

Cancer

A group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and division of abnormal cells due to failures in cell cycle regulation.

Tumor

A mass of abnormal cells.

Benign tumor

A non-cancerous tumor that stays in one place and does not invade surrounding tissues.

Malignant tumor

A cancerous tumor that can invade nearby tissues and spread to other parts of the body.

Important Terms (6/6)

Metastasis

The process by which cancer cells spread from their original site to other parts of the body.

Apoptosis

Programmed cell death, a self-destruct mechanism triggered by the cell when problems like DNA damage cannot be fixed.

Carcinogens

Environmental factors (substances or agents) that damage DNA and can cause cancer.

When Cell Division Goes Wrong: Cancer (1/2)

Cell Cycle Regulation

Controlled by molecular signals and checkpoints (checking for DNA damage, replication completion, spindle attachment).

Apoptosis

Programmed cell death if problems are irreparable.

Cancer Cause

Mutations in genes that regulate the cell cycle cause cells to ignore stop signals and divide uncontrollably.

Tumors

Masses of abnormal cells.

Nature of Cancer

Not one disease, but over 200, each arising from different mutations.

When Cell Division Goes Wrong: Cancer (2/2)

Carcinogens

Environmental factors (e.g., tobacco smoke, UV radiation, certain chemicals, viruses) that damage DNA and contribute to mutations.

Cancer Treatments

Many target the cell cycle (e.g., chemotherapy stopping spindle formation, radiation damaging DNA of rapidly dividing cells).
