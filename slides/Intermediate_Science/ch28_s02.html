Polymers and Monomers

Important Terms (1/2)

Functional group

A specific arrangement of atoms within an organic molecule that determines the molecule's chemical properties and reactivity.

Hydroxyl group

The functional group -OH, consisting of an oxygen atom covalently bonded to a hydrogen atom. When attached to a carbon backbone, it defines the compound as an alcohol.

Alcohol

An organic compound containing a hydroxyl (-OH) functional group.

Carboxyl group

The functional group -COOH, in which a carbon atom is double-bonded to an oxygen and single-bonded to a hydroxyl group (-OH). It characterizes organic acids and can release H+ ions in solution.

Organic acid (Carboxylic acid)

An organic compound containing a carboxyl (-COOH) functional group, capable of donating a hydrogen ion and acting as an acid.

Important Terms (2/2)

Amino group

The functional group -NH2, consisting of a nitrogen atom bonded to two hydrogen atoms. The lone pair of electrons on nitrogen can accept an H+ ion, making amines act as weak bases.

Amine

An organic compound containing an amino (-NH2) functional group.

Polymer

A large molecule composed of many repeating smaller units (monomers) bonded together in a chain.

Monomer

A small molecule that can bond with other identical or similar molecules to form a polymer.

The Hydroxyl Group (-OH) — Alcohols

Structure

An oxygen atom single-bonded to a hydrogen atom, and also single-bonded to a carbon atom in the main chain.

Compound Class

Molecules containing a hydroxyl group are called **alcohols**.

Polymers — Chains of Repeating Units

Definition

**Polymers** are very large molecules (macromolecules) that are constructed from many small, identical or similar, repeating units.

Monomers

Each of these small, individual repeating units is called a **monomer** (from Greek "mono" meaning one, and "meros" meaning part).

Concept

Think of polymers as long chains made by snapping together hundreds or thousands of identical building blocks (monomers).
