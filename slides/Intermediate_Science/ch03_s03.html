Important Terms (1/2)

Energy level (Shell)

A specific region around the nucleus where electrons are found, also called a shell. Each energy level can hold a limited number of electrons, with those closer to the nucleus having lower energy.

Electron configuration

A description of how electrons are distributed among the energy levels (shells) of an atom. For example, the configuration 2-8-1 means 2 electrons in the first level, 8 in the second, and 1 in the ...

Bohr diagram

A visual representation of an atom's electron configuration. It shows the nucleus at the center, surrounded by concentric circles representing energy levels, with dots or crosses depicting electrons.

Valence electron

An electron in the outermost energy level (shell) of an atom. Valence electrons are the electrons involved in chemical bonding and determine an element's chemical reactivity.

Lewis dot structure (Electron dot diagram)

A diagram that shows the symbol of an element surrounded by dots representing its valence electrons. These diagrams are used to predict how atoms will bond.

Important Terms (2/2)

Octet rule

The principle that atoms tend to gain, lose, or share electrons until they achieve a full outer shell, typically containing eight valence electrons, for maximum stability.

Noble gases

Elements (like helium, neon, and argon) that have completely filled outermost energy levels, which makes them very stable and chemically unreactive.
