Key Concepts (1/2)

Natural Greenhouse Effect

A natural process where specific atmospheric gases absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, trapping heat and warming Earth's surface by about 33°C, making liquid water and life possible.

Albedo

The reflectivity of a surface; high albedo surfaces (like ice) reflect more solar radiation, while low albedo surfaces (like oceans) absorb more.

Important Terms (1/2)

Greenhouse effect

The process by which certain gases in Earth's atmosphere absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, trapping thermal energy and warming the planet.

Albedo

The fraction of incoming solar radiation that is reflected by a surface.

Greenhouse gases

Gases in the atmosphere that absorb and re-emit infrared radiation, contributing to the greenhouse effect (e.g., H2O, CO2, CH4, N2O, CFCs).

Enhanced greenhouse effect

The additional warming caused by the increase in greenhouse gas concentrations due to human activities, primarily burning fossil fuels and deforestation.

Carbon source

Any process or reservoir that releases more carbon into the atmosphere than it absorbs.

Important Terms (2/2)

Carbon sink

Any process or reservoir that absorbs more carbon from the atmosphere than it releases.

Keeling Curve

The continuous record of atmospheric CO2 concentration measured at the Mauna Loa Observatory in Hawaii since 1958, showing a steady upward trend and seasonal oscillations.
