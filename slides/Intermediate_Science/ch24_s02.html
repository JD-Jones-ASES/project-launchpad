Important Terms (1/2)

Voltage

The potential difference between two points in a circuit, measuring how much energy is available to move each coulomb of charge from one point to another. Measured in volts (V).

Volt (V)

The unit of voltage, equal to one joule per coulomb ($1V = 1J/C$).

Resistance

A measure of how strongly a material opposes the flow of electric current. Measured in ohms ($\Omega$).

Ohm ($\Omega$)

The unit of resistance, equal to one volt per ampere ($1\Omega = 1V/A$).

Ohm's Law

The relationship stating that the voltage ($V$) across a conductor equals the current ($I$) through it multiplied by its resistance ($R$), expressed as $V = IR$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Resistivity

An intrinsic property of a material that quantifies its opposition to current flow. Materials with low resistivity are good conductors.

Voltage: The Electrical Push

Definition

Voltage is the potential difference between two points in a circuit. It quantifies the amount of energy available to move each coulomb of charge from one point to another.

Units

Voltage is represented by the letter $V$ and measured in **volts (V)**. One volt is defined as one joule of energy per coulomb of charge ($1V = 1J/C$).

Analogy

Think of voltage as the water pressure in a plumbing system; higher pressure pushes water with more force.

Sources

Batteries and power supplies create voltage.

Resistance: Opposition to Current

Definition

Resistance measures how strongly a material opposes the flow of electric current.

Units

Resistance is represented by the letter $R$ and measured in **ohms ($\Omega$)**. One ohm is defined as one volt per ampere ($1\Omega = 1V/A$).

Ohm's Law

The Equation

The voltage across a conductor is equal to the current through it multiplied by its resistance.

Key Implications

Rearranging Ohm's Law

Memory Aid (VIR Triangle)

A visual tool where V is at the top, and I and R are at the bottom. Covering the desired variable reveals the calculation needed (e.g., cover V, get $I \times R$; cover I, get $V/R$).

Worked Example: Ohm's Law Calculation

Knowns

$V = 9.0V$, $I = 0.30A$

Formula

Rearrange Ohm's Law to solve for $R$: $R = V/I$

Calculation

$R = \frac{9.0V}{0.30A} = 30\Omega$

Answer

The resistor has a resistance of $30\Omega$.
