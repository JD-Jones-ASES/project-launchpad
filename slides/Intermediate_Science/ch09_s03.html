Newton's Second Law: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Newton's Second Law

States that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

Mathematical Representation

Expressed by the equation $F_{net} = ma$, where $F_{net}$ is the net force, $m$ is the mass, and $a$ is the acceleration.

Direction of Acceleration

The acceleration of an object always occurs in the same direction as the net force acting on it.

Direct Proportionality (Force-Acceleration)

If the net force on an object is increased (while mass stays constant), its acceleration will increase proportionally.

Inverse Proportionality (Mass-Acceleration)

If an object's mass is increased (while net force stays constant), its acceleration will decrease proportionally.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Net Force Calculation (One Dimension)

For forces acting along a single line, the net force is found by assigning positive and negative signs to forces based on an chosen direction and adding them algebraically.

Important Terms (1/2)

Newton's Second Law

The principle that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass ($F_{net} = ma$).

Net Force ($F_{net}$)

The vector sum of all individual forces acting on an object. It is the overall force responsible for an object's acceleration.

Mass ($m$)

A measure of the amount of matter in an object and its inertia (resistance to changes in motion). Measured in kilograms (kg).

Acceleration ($a$)

The rate at which an object's velocity changes, either in speed or direction. Measured in meters per second squared (m/s²).

Newton (N)

The SI unit of force. One Newton is defined as the force required to accelerate a one-kilogram mass at one meter per second squared ($1 N = 1 kg \cdot m/s^2$).
