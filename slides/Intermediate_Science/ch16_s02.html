From DNA to Protein: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/3)

Central Dogma of Molecular Biology

The fundamental principle describing the flow of genetic information: DNA → RNA → Protein.

Transcription

The process of creating a messenger RNA (mRNA) copy from a DNA gene sequence. It occurs in the nucleus and is catalyzed by RNA polymerase.

RNA Polymerase

The enzyme responsible for unwinding the DNA and synthesizing a complementary mRNA strand during transcription.

Messenger RNA (mRNA)

A single-stranded RNA molecule that carries the genetic code from the DNA in the nucleus to the ribosomes in the cytoplasm for protein synthesis.

Translation

The process of synthesizing a protein by reading the mRNA sequence at a ribosome. It involves codons and transfer RNA (tRNA) molecules.

Key Concepts (2/3)

Codon

A sequence of three consecutive mRNA bases that specifies a particular amino acid or signals a stop in protein synthesis.

Transfer RNA (tRNA)

Small RNA molecules that carry specific amino acids to the ribosome, matching them to the corresponding codons on the mRNA during translation.

Ribosomes

Cellular structures, located in the cytoplasm, where mRNA is translated into protein.

Mutations

Permanent changes in the DNA sequence of a gene. They can be caused by errors during DNA replication, environmental factors, or spontaneously.

Substitution Mutation

A type of mutation where one DNA base is replaced by another. Its effect can range from no change in the protein to a change in a single amino acid.

Important Terms (1/2)

Transcription

The process of copying a gene's DNA sequence into a messenger RNA (mRNA) molecule.

Translation

The process of reading the mRNA sequence and assembling a chain of amino acids to build a protein.

Codon

A sequence of three mRNA bases that codes for a specific amino acid or a stop signal during protein synthesis.

Mutation

A permanent change in the DNA sequence of a gene.

RNA Polymerase

The enzyme that builds an mRNA molecule during transcription.

Important Terms (2/2)

tRNA (transfer RNA)

A type of RNA molecule that carries specific amino acids to the ribosome during translation.

From DNA to Protein: The Cellular Blueprint in Action

Enzymes

Speed up chemical reactions.

Structural proteins

Build tissues.

Hormones

Carry signals throughout the body.

Antibodies

Fight infections.

Transcription: Copying the Message

Location

Occurs in the **nucleus** of eukaryotic cells.

Purpose

To make a portable, disposable copy of a gene's instructions, much like making a photocopy of a master blueprint.

Translation: Building the Protein

Location

Occurs at **ribosomes** in the cytoplasm.

Mechanism

The mRNA message is read in groups of three bases called **codons**.

Transfer RNA (tRNA)

molecules play a crucial role. Each tRNA molecule has an "anticodon" that is complementary to an mRNA codon, and it carries the specific amino acid corresponding to that codon.

Mutations: Changes in the Code

Harmful

Many mutations produce defective or nonfunctional proteins, leading to genetic disorders such as sickle cell disease or cystic fibrosis.
