Important Terms (1/3)

Gene

(Life Science) A unit of heredity that is transferred from a parent to offspring and is held to determine some characteristic of the offspring.

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

(Life Science) The carrier of genetic information in nearly all organisms, consisting of two long polynucleotide chains twisted into a double helix and joined by hydrogen bonds between the compleme...

Heredity

(Life Science) The passing on of physical or mental characteristics genetically from one generation to another.

Ocean Current

(Earth Science) A continuous, directed movement of ocean water generated by forces acting upon the water, such as the breaking of waves, wind, the Coriolis effect, and temperature and salinity diff...

Water Cycle (Hydrologic Cycle)

(Earth Science) The continuous process by which water circulates throughout the Earth's atmosphere, land, and oceans, involving evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and runoff.

Important Terms (2/3)

Salinity

(Earth Science) The saltiness or dissolved salt content of a body of water or soil, typically expressed in parts per thousand (ppt).

Reactant

(Chemistry) A substance that takes part in and undergoes change during a reaction.

Product

(Chemistry) A substance that is formed as a result of a chemical reaction.

Balanced Equation

(Chemistry) A chemical equation in which the number of atoms of each element and the total charge are the same on both the reactant side and the product side.

Wavelength

(Physics) The spatial period of a periodic wave – the distance over which the wave's shape repeats. It is the distance between consecutive corresponding points of the same phase on the wave.

Important Terms (3/3)

Frequency

(Physics) The number of occurrences of a repeating event per unit of time. For waves, it is the number of wave cycles that pass a point per unit of time.

Electromagnetic Spectrum

(Physics) The range of all types of electromagnetic radiation, from extremely long radio waves to extremely short gamma rays, including visible light.

Cross-Branch Connection

A conceptual link or shared principle identified between terms or ideas originating from different scientific disciplines.

Information Flow

The process by which data, instructions, or meaning are transmitted, received, and processed within a system or across different systems, often in a structured or patterned manner.
