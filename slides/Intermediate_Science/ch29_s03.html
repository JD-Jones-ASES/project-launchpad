Important Terms (1/3)

Electromagnetic Induction

The process of producing a voltage and current in a conductor by exposing it to a changing magnetic field.

Faraday's Law of Induction

The scientific law stating that the induced voltage in a circuit is proportional to the rate of change of magnetic flux through the circuit and the number of turns.

Lenz's Law

A principle of electromagnetism stating that the direction of an induced current is such that it opposes the cause producing it (the change in magnetic field).

Generator

A machine that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy, typically by rotating a conductor in a magnetic field to induce a current.

Alternating Current (AC)

An electric current that periodically reverses direction, produced by generators.

Important Terms (2/3)

Transformer

An electrical device that changes the voltage of an alternating current by electromagnetic induction, using primary and secondary coils wound around an iron core.

Primary Coil ($N_p$)

The coil in a transformer that is connected to the input AC voltage.

Secondary Coil ($N_s$)

The coil in a transformer where the induced output voltage is produced.

Step-up Transformer

A transformer designed to increase voltage, having more turns in the secondary coil than in the primary coil ($N_s > N_p$).

Step-down Transformer

A transformer designed to decrease voltage, having fewer turns in the secondary coil than in the primary coil ($N_s < N_p$).

Important Terms (3/3)

Power Loss ($P_{lost} = I^2R$)

The energy dissipated as heat in a conductor due to its resistance when current flows through it.

Faraday's Law of Induction

Strength of the magnetic field

A stronger magnetic field undergoing change will induce a greater voltage.

Number of turns in the coil

A coil with more turns will intercept more of the changing magnetic field, resulting in a higher induced voltage.

Speed of change

The faster the magnetic field changes (e.g., moving a magnet more quickly), the greater the induced voltage.

Transformers

Step-up transformer

If $N_s > N_p$, the secondary coil has more turns than the primary, so $V_s > V_p$. The voltage is increased.

Step-down transformer

If $N_s < N_p$, the secondary coil has fewer turns than the primary, so $V_s < V_p$. The voltage is decreased.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is electromagnetic induction?

The production of a voltage (and, if the circuit is closed, a current) in a conductor exposed to a changing magnetic field.

Who independently discovered electromagnetic induction and when?

English physicist Michael Faraday and American physicist Joseph Henry, in 1831.

What is the crucial condition for electromagnetic induction to occur?

The magnetic field through the conductor must be changing. A steady magnetic field produces no voltage.

According to Faraday's observations, what specific actions can induce a current in a coil of wire?

Moving a magnet into or out of a coil; moving a coil toward or away from a stationary magnet; or changing the current in a nearby coil.

What three factors determine the magnitude of the induced voltage according to Faraday's law?

Key Concepts (2/2)

State Lenz's Law.

The direction of an induced current is always such that it opposes the change in magnetic field that produced it.

A generator is a device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy by rotating a coil of wire inside a magnetic field.
