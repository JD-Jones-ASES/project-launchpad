Important Terms (1/2)

Covalent bond

A bond formed when two atoms share one or more pairs of electrons, typically between two nonmetals with similar electronegativities.

Lewis structure

A diagram that shows all valence electrons in a molecule, using dots for lone pairs and lines for bonding pairs. Also called an electron dot structure.

Polar covalent bond

A covalent bond in which electrons are shared unequally because the atoms have different electronegativities, resulting in partial positive and negative charges on the bonded atoms.

Nonpolar covalent bond

A covalent bond in which electrons are shared equally or nearly equally because the atoms have similar electronegativities.

Electronegativity

A measure of how strongly an atom in a bond attracts the shared electrons toward itself.

Important Terms (2/2)

VSEPR model (Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion)

A model that predicts molecular shapes based on the principle that electron pairs around a central atom repel each other and arrange themselves as far apart as possible.

Single, Double, and Triple Bonds

Single Bond

Involves one shared pair of electrons (2 electrons total). Represented by a single line (e.g., H-H in H$_2$).

Double Bond

Involves two shared pairs of electrons (4 electrons total). Represented by a double line (e.g., O=O in O$_2$). Each oxygen atom has 6 valence electrons; sharing two pairs gives each atom an octet.

Triple Bond

Involves three shared pairs of electrons (6 electrons total). Represented by a triple line (e.g., N#N in N$_2$). Each nitrogen atom has 5 valence electrons; sharing three pairs completes both octets.

Molecular Shape: Introduction to VSEPR

Linear

2 electron groups around the central atom (e.g., CO$_2$).

Trigonal Planar

3 electron groups around the central atom (e.g., BF$_3$).

Tetrahedral

4 electron groups around the central atom (e.g., CH$_4$).

Bent

4 electron groups around the central atom, but some are lone pairs (e.g., H$_2$O, which has 2 bonding pairs and 2 lone pairs, forcing a bent shape).

Trigonal Pyramidal

4 electron groups around the central atom, but one is a lone pair (e.g., NH$_3$, which has 3 bonding pairs and 1 lone pair).
