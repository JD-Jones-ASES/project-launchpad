Important Terms (1/3)

Cell theory

The fundamental principle in life science stating that all living things are made of cells, cells are the basic unit of life, and all cells come from pre-existing cells.

Convection current

The circular flow of material (e.g., magma in the mantle, air in the atmosphere) driven by heating from below and cooling from above, a mechanism for heat transfer.

Atomic number

The number of protons in an atom's nucleus, which uniquely defines an element and its chemical identity.

Velocity

A vector quantity describing the rate of change of an object's displacement, including both speed and direction.

Homeostasis

The ability of an organism or system to maintain stable internal conditions despite external changes, a key concept in biology and system regulation.

Important Terms (2/3)

Plate boundary

The zones where Earth's tectonic plates meet, characterized by distinct geological activities like earthquakes and volcanoes.

Electronegativity

A chemical property measuring an atom's tendency to attract shared electrons in a chemical bond.

Conservation of energy

A fundamental principle in physics stating that the total energy in a closed system remains constant; energy can be transformed from one form to another but cannot be created or destroyed.

ATP (adenosine triphosphate)

The primary molecule that cells use to store and transfer energy for metabolic processes.

DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid)

The nucleic acid that carries the genetic instructions used in the growth, development, functioning, and reproduction of all known living organisms.

Important Terms (3/3)

Wavelength ($\lambda$)

The spatial period of a periodic wave, the distance over which the wave's shape repeats.

Conservation of mass

A fundamental principle in chemistry stating that matter is neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction; the total mass of reactants equals the total mass of products.

Natural selection

The primary mechanism of evolution, where individuals with traits better suited to their environment are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those advantageous traits to their offspring.

Adaptation

A heritable trait or characteristic that improves an organism's ability to survive and reproduce in its environment.
