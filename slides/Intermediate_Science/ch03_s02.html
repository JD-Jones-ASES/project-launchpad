Key Concepts (1/2)

Three Subatomic Particles

Atoms are composed of protons, neutrons, and electrons, each with unique characteristics that determine an atom's identity and behavior.

Charge Balance

Protons carry a +1 charge, electrons a -1 charge, and neutrons have no charge. In a neutral atom, the number of protons equals the number of electrons, resulting in a net zero charge.

Atomic Number (Z): The Element's Identity

The number of protons in an atom's nucleus (atomic number, $Z$) uniquely identifies an element. Changing the number of protons changes the element.

Mass Number (A): Nucleon Count

The mass number ($A$) represents the total count of protons and neutrons (nucleons) in an atom's nucleus. It is calculated as $A = Z + N$.

Important Terms (1/3)

Proton

A subatomic particle found in the nucleus with a positive electric charge (+1) and a relative mass of approximately 1 atomic mass unit (amu). Its number defines the element.

Neutron

A subatomic particle found in the nucleus with no electric charge (neutral) and a relative mass of approximately 1 amu.

Electron

A subatomic particle with a negative electric charge (-1) and an extremely small mass (about $1/1,836$ amu). Electrons are located in energy levels outside the nucleus and determine chemical behavior.

Nucleons

A collective term for protons and neutrons, which are both found in the nucleus.

Atomic Number ($Z$)

The number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, uniquely identifying an element.

Important Terms (2/3)

Mass Number ($A$)

The total number of protons and neutrons in the nucleus of an atom. Calculated as $A = Z + N$.

Isotope

Atoms of the same element (same number of protons) but with different numbers of neutrons, leading to different mass numbers.

Ion

An atom that has gained or lost one or more electrons, resulting in a net electric charge.

Cation

A positively charged ion formed when an atom loses one or more electrons.

Anion

A negatively charged ion formed when an atom gains one or more electrons.

Important Terms (3/3)

Scientific Notation

A method for expressing very large or very small numbers as a coefficient (between 1 and 10) multiplied by a power of 10.

Ions: Gaining or Losing Electrons

Ions

are atoms that have gained or lost one or more electrons, resulting in a net electric charge.

Cations

If an atom loses one or more electrons, it will have more protons than electrons, resulting in a net positive charge. Positively charged ions are called cations (e.g., $Na^+$).

Anions

If an atom gains one or more electrons, it will have more electrons than protons, resulting in a net negative charge. Negatively charged ions are called anions (e.g., $Cl^-$).

Element Identity

When an atom forms an ion, only the number of electrons changes; the number of protons (and thus the element's identity) remains constant.
