Signs of a Chemical Reaction

Four primary indicators suggest a chemical change:

Exothermic Reactions

Chemical reactions that release energy, usually as heat, light, or sound, into their surroundings. The products have less chemical energy than the reactants, and the surroundings become warmer.

Endothermic Reactions

Chemical reactions that absorb energy, usually as heat, from their surroundings. The products have more chemical energy than the reactants, and the surroundings become cooler.

Activation Energy

The minimum amount of energy required to initiate a chemical reaction. Even exothermic reactions need an initial "push" to overcome this energy barrier before they can proceed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Catalysts and Enzymes

Substances that speed up a chemical reaction by lowering its activation energy without being consumed in the process. Enzymes are biological catalysts essential for life processes.

Important Terms (1/3)

Chemical reaction

A process that involves the rearrangement of the molecular or ionic structure of a substance, as opposed to a change in physical form or a nuclear reaction.

Reactants

The starting substances in a chemical reaction.

Products

The new substances formed as a result of a chemical reaction.

Physical change

A change in the form or appearance of a substance, but not in its chemical composition (e.g., melting ice, dissolving sugar).

Color change

An observable alteration in the hue of substances, often indicating a chemical reaction has taken place.

Important Terms (2/3)

Gas production

The release of gaseous substances, evidenced by bubbles or fizzing, which is a common sign of a chemical reaction.

Temperature change

An increase or decrease in the temperature of the surroundings, indicating energy is being released or absorbed by a chemical reaction.

Precipitate formation

The creation of an insoluble solid from a solution during a chemical reaction.

Precipitate

The solid that forms and separates from a liquid solution during a chemical reaction.

Exothermic

Describes a chemical reaction that releases energy to its surroundings, typically as heat, causing the surroundings to warm up.

Important Terms (3/3)

Endothermic

Describes a chemical reaction that absorbs energy from its surroundings, typically as heat, causing the surroundings to cool down.

Activation energy

The smallest amount of energy that molecules must possess for a chemical reaction to occur.

Catalyst

A substance that increases the rate of a chemical reaction without itself undergoing any permanent chemical change.

Enzyme

A type of protein that acts as a biological catalyst, speeding up specific biochemical reactions.
