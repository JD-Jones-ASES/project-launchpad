Seafloor Spreading: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Continental Drift's Missing Mechanism

Before seafloor spreading, geologists lacked an explanation for *how* continents moved, despite evidence suggesting they did.

Discovery of Mid-Ocean Ridges

Advanced sonar technology revealed complex underwater topography, including vast, globe-encircling mountain ranges called mid-ocean ridges, which became central to understanding crustal movement.

Hess's Seafloor Spreading Hypothesis

Proposed that the ocean floor is continually created at mid-ocean ridges as magma rises and solidifies, then moves away from the ridge crest like a conveyor belt, carrying continents with it.

Mantle Convection as the Driver

Seafloor spreading connected the observed movement of the surface to the slow circulation of rock deep within the Earth, known as mantle convection.

Mid-Ocean Ridges

The most significant discovery was a global network of underwater mountain ranges called **mid-ocean ridges**.

Harry Hess's Seafloor Spreading Hypothesis (1962)

The Idea

American geologist Harry Hess proposed that the ocean floor is not permanent but is constantly being created and destroyed through a process he named **seafloor spreading**.

How Seafloor Spreading Works

Solving Wegener's Problem

Hess's hypothesis provided the crucial mechanism that Wegener's continental drift lacked. It showed that continents don't "plow" through oceanic crust but rather ride on top of moving plates.

Connection to Mantle Convection

Seafloor spreading linked the movement of the Earth's surface to the internal heat engine of **mantle convection**, which drives the slow circulation of rock deep within the Earth.

From Hypothesis to Theory: Plate Tectonics

Overwhelming Evidence

The evidence for seafloor spreading was undeniable, providing the long-sought mechanism for continental drift.

Unified Theory

By the mid-1960s, scientists combined continental drift, seafloor spreading, and new data on earthquake locations and plate boundaries into a comprehensive theory: **plate tectonics**.
