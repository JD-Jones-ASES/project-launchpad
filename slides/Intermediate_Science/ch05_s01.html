Important Terms (1/3)

Atom

The basic unit of matter, consisting of a nucleus (protons and neutrons) surrounded by electrons. It is the fundamental building block for all substances.

Element

A pure substance consisting only of atoms that all have the same numbers of protons in their atomic nuclei (e.g., carbon, oxygen, iron).

Cell

The basic structural, functional, and biological unit of all known organisms. Cells are composed of various molecules, which in turn are made of atoms.

Organelle

A specialized subunit within a cell that has a specific function (e.g., nucleus, mitochondria, ribosomes).

Cell Membrane

The semipermeable barrier that surrounds the cytoplasm of a cell, made of phospholipid molecules.

Important Terms (2/3)

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic Acid)

The molecule that carries genetic instructions for the development, functioning, growth, and reproduction of all known organisms and many viruses.

Plate Tectonics

Earth's Magnetic Field

A magnetic field generated by convection currents in Earth's liquid outer core, which shields the planet from harmful solar radiation.

Convection Currents

The transfer of heat by the circulation or movement of the heated parts of a liquid or gas; in Earth's mantle, these currents drive plate tectonics.

Kinematic Equations

A set of mathematical formulas used in physics to describe the motion of objects without considering the forces that cause the motion (e.g., $v = d/t$, $a = \Delta v / \Delta t$).

Important Terms (3/3)

Diffusion

The net movement of particles from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration, often observed in molecular motion within cells.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What causes the density differences among Earth's layers, according to the text?

Heavier atoms like iron sank toward the center when Earth was young and molten, leading to the observed density stratification.

How do physics concepts, specifically kinematic equations, apply to individual atoms?

Kinematic equations like v = d/t and a = v/t apply to individual atoms. For example, atoms in a gas zip around at hundreds of meters per second.

What is temperature a measure of at the atomic level?

Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of moving atoms.

How do atoms contribute to phenomena like plate tectonics (Earth Science) through physics principles?

The convection currents that drive plate tectonics are ultimately atoms in the mantle moving in response to heat, a process governed by physics.

Explain how Earth's interior processes create suitable conditions for life on the surface.

The rock cycle produces soil for plants. Convection currents drive plate tectonics, creating habitats like mountains, ocean basins, and volcanic islands.

What protects life on Earth from harmful solar radiation, and how is it generated?

Earth's magnetic field protects life. It is generated by the dynamo effect in the liquid outer core.
