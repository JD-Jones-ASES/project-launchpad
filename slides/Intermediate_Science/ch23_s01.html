Important Terms (1/2)

Solution

A homogeneous mixture in which one or more substances (solutes) are uniformly distributed within another substance (solvent).

Solute

The substance that is dissolved in a solution, typically present in a smaller amount.

Solvent

The substance that does the dissolving in a solution, typically present in a larger amount. Water is often called the "universal solvent."

Hydration

The process where water molecules surround and pull apart ions or polar molecules from a solid, dispersing them throughout the solution.

Solubility

The maximum amount of a solute that can dissolve in a given amount of solvent at a specific temperature.

Important Terms (2/2)

Unsaturated solution

A solution that can dissolve more solute at a given temperature.

Saturated solution

A solution that has dissolved the maximum amount of solute possible at a given temperature; any additional solute will not dissolve and will settle.

Supersaturated solution

An unstable solution containing more solute than the normal maximum solubility at a given temperature, often formed by carefully cooling a saturated solution.

Concentration

A measure of how much solute is dissolved in a given amount of solvent or solution.

Molarity (M)

A unit of concentration equal to the number of moles of solute per liter of solution (mol/L).
