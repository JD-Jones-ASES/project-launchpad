Important Terms (1/2)

Speed

The rate at which an object covers distance. It is a scalar quantity (magnitude only, no direction) and is calculated as distance divided by time.

Average speed

The total distance traveled divided by the total time taken for the journey.

Instantaneous speed

The speed of an object at a specific moment in time.

Velocity

The rate at which an object changes its position. It is a vector quantity, possessing both magnitude (speed) and direction. Calculated as displacement divided by time.

Displacement ($\Delta x$)

The change in an object's position, measured from its starting point to its ending point, including direction.

Important Terms (2/2)

Scalar quantity

A physical quantity that has magnitude but no direction (e.g., speed, distance, time, temperature).

Vector quantity

A physical quantity that has both magnitude and direction (e.g., velocity, displacement, force).

Position-time graph

A graph that plots an object's position on the vertical axis against time on the horizontal axis. Its slope represents velocity.

Velocity-time graph

A graph that plots an object's velocity on the vertical axis against time on the horizontal axis. The area under its curve represents displacement.
