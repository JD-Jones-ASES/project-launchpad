Important Terms (1/2)

Evolution

The change in the inherited characteristics of a population over successive generations.

Fossil

The preserved remains or traces of an organism that lived in the past.

Fossil Record

The total collection of fossils that have been discovered, providing a chronological archive of life's history on Earth.

Homologous Structure

A body part that is similar in structure across different species because it was inherited from a common ancestor, even though its function may differ.

Analogous Structure

A body part that performs a similar function in different species but has a different evolutionary origin and internal structure, resulting from convergent evolution.

Important Terms (2/2)

Convergent Evolution

The process by which unrelated species evolve similar features or traits because they have to adapt to similar environmental challenges or ecological niches.

Vestigial Structure

A body structure that is reduced in size or function compared to the same structure in an ancestral species, providing evidence of evolutionary change.

Radiometric Dating

A method used by geologists to determine the absolute age of fossils and the rocks that contain them, based on the decay of radioactive isotopes.

Biogeography

The study of where organisms live and why, examining the geographic distribution of species as evidence for evolutionary processes.

Introduction to Evidence for Evolution

Definition of Evolution

The change in the inherited characteristics of a population over successive generations. It explains both the vast diversity of life and the fundamental similarities shared by all organisms.

The Fossil Record

Fossils

Any preserved evidence of a past organism. This includes mineralized bones, shells, impressions (molds and casts), preserved footprints, and organisms trapped in amber or ice.

Fossil Record

The collective body of fossils forms a chronological archive of life on Earth, providing direct evidence of how life has changed.

What does the fossil record reveal about the prevalence of extinction?

The fossil record indicates that over 99% of all species that have ever lived are now extinct, documenting species that thrived and then disappeared.

Geologists use radiometric dating methods, such as Carbon-14 dating for younger samples and potassium-argon dating for much older rocks, to determine their age.
