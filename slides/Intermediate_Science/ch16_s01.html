Important Terms (1/3)

DNA (Deoxyribonucleic acid)

The molecule that carries the genetic instructions for the development, functioning, and reproduction of all known living organisms.

Double helix

The spiral-staircase like three-dimensional structure of DNA, consisting of two strands twisted around each other.

Nucleotide

The monomer (building block) of DNA and RNA. Each nucleotide consists of a sugar molecule (deoxyribose in DNA), a phosphate group, and a nitrogen-containing base.

Adenine (A)

One of the four nitrogenous bases found in DNA; always pairs with Thymine.

Thymine (T)

One of the four nitrogenous bases found in DNA; always pairs with Adenine.

Important Terms (2/3)

Guanine (G)

One of the four nitrogenous bases found in DNA; always pairs with Cytosine.

Cytosine (C)

One of the four nitrogenous bases found in DNA; always pairs with Guanine.

Base pair

A pair of complementary bases (A-T or G-C) that form a rung of the DNA ladder, held together by hydrogen bonds.

Complementary base pairing

The specific rule that Adenine always pairs with Thymine, and Guanine always pairs with Cytosine in DNA.

Gene

A segment of DNA that contains the instructions for building a specific protein (or set of proteins); the basic unit of heredity.

Important Terms (3/3)

Alleles

Different versions of the same gene.

Replication

The process by which a cell copies its DNA before cell division, producing two identical DNA molecules.

Helicases

Enzymes that break the hydrogen bonds between base pairs, "unzipping" the DNA double helix during replication.

DNA polymerase

An enzyme that links new nucleotides together to form a new complementary DNA strand during replication.

Semiconservative replication

The method of DNA replication where each new DNA molecule consists of one original (parental) strand and one newly synthesized (daughter) strand.
