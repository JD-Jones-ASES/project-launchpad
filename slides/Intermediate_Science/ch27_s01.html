Important Terms (1/2)

Weather

The day-to-day state of the atmosphere at a specific place and time, including temperature, precipitation, wind speed, humidity, and air pressure.

Climate

The long-term average of weather conditions in a region, typically calculated over at least 30 years, describing the expected patterns of temperature, precipitation, and other atmospheric variables.

Paleoclimatology

The scientific study of past climates using natural records preserved in various proxy data sources.

Proxy Data

Indirect evidence used to reconstruct past climate conditions when direct measurements (like thermometers) were unavailable. Examples include ice cores, tree rings, and ocean sediment layers.

Ice Cores

Climate Zones (1/2)

Tropical (A)

Characterized by year-round warmth (average temperature above 18°C every month) and high rainfall. This group includes tropical rainforests and savannas.

Dry (B)

Defined by evaporation exceeding precipitation, leading to arid or semi-arid conditions. This includes hot deserts (e.g., Sahara) and cold deserts (e.g., Gobi).

Temperate (C)

Features moderate temperatures with distinct seasons, experiencing warm or hot summers and mild winters. Examples include Mediterranean, humid subtropical, and oceanic climates.

Continental (D)

Found in the interiors of large continents at mid-latitudes, characterized by a large temperature range between summer and winter. Winters are cold, and summers can be warm to hot.

Polar (E)

Extremely cold climates year-round, with average temperatures below 10°C in the warmest month. This group includes tundra and ice cap climates.

Natural Climate Variability

These are long-term, cyclical changes in Earth's orbit, including:

Volcanic eruptions

Major eruptions inject sulfur dioxide and ash into the stratosphere, which can reflect sunlight and cause a temporary global cooling effect lasting one to three years.

Solar output variations

Small, cyclical changes in the sun's energy output (e.g., the 11-year solar cycle) can cause minor temperature fluctuations on Earth.

Ocean circulation changes

Shifts in major ocean current patterns (such as El Niño and La Niña events) redistribute heat around the globe, influencing regional climates for periods of months to years.
