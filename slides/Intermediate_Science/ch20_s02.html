Important Terms (1/3)

X-ray Imaging

A medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency electromagnetic waves to create shadow images of internal body structures, particularly bones.

Electromagnetic Waves (EM Waves)

Waves that consist of oscillating electric and magnetic fields, travelling at the speed of light. X-rays and radio waves are examples of EM waves.

Ionizing Radiation

Radiation (like X-rays) that has enough energy to remove electrons from atoms, potentially causing damage to living tissue.

Ultrasound Imaging

A medical imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to generate real-time images of soft tissues and organs by detecting reflected echoes.

Sound Waves

Mechanical waves that travel through a medium by vibrating particles; used in ultrasound imaging.

Important Terms (2/3)

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

A sophisticated medical imaging technique that uses strong magnetic fields and radio waves to produce detailed cross-sectional images of soft tissues, distinguishing between different types of tiss...

Hydrogen Nuclei

The nucleus of a hydrogen atom, consisting of a single proton; these nuclei behave like tiny magnets and are central to MRI technology.

Resonance

The phenomenon where an oscillating system responds with maximum amplitude to a driving force at a specific frequency; a principle used in MRI.

Genetic Testing

The analysis of a person's DNA to identify changes (mutations) in genes or chromosomes that may indicate a disease or increased risk for a disease.

DNA Base Sequence

The specific order of nucleotide bases (Adenine, Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine) in a DNA molecule, which carries genetic information.

Important Terms (3/3)

PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction)

A laboratory technique used to make millions of copies of a specific DNA segment, crucial for genetic testing.
