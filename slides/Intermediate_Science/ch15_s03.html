Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of photosynthesis?

To convert light energy into chemical energy (glucose) for the plant, essentially creating its own food.

Briefly describe the overall chemical reaction for photosynthesis.

6CO₂ + 6H₂O + Light Energy → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ (Carbon dioxide + Water + Light energy yields Glucose + Oxygen)

What is the main product of cellular respiration used by cells for energy?

ATP (Adenosine Triphosphate), which is the direct energy currency of the cell.

Compare the reactants and products of photosynthesis and cellular respiration.

The reactants of photosynthesis (CO₂ and H₂O) are the products of cellular respiration, and vice-versa (O₂ and C₆H₁₂O₆). They are complementary processes.

Where in the cell does cellular respiration primarily occur?

Mainly in the mitochondria, though glycolysis (the first stage) occurs in the cytoplasm.

What is the difference between aerobic and anaerobic respiration?

Aerobic respiration requires oxygen and produces a large amount of ATP. Anaerobic respiration occurs without oxygen and produces much less ATP.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Define "atmosphere."

The layer of gases surrounding a planet or other celestial body, held in place by gravity.

What is the "greenhouse effect" and why is it important for Earth?

The natural process by which certain gases in Earth's atmosphere trap heat, warming the planet. It's crucial for maintaining temperatures suitable for life.

Name the two lowest layers of Earth's atmosphere and one key characteristic of each.

Troposphere (where most weather occurs, closest to Earth's surface) and Stratosphere (contains the ozone layer, temperature increases with altitude).
