Applications of Electromagnets

Their ability to be controlled makes electromagnets crucial in many technologies, including doorbells, MRI machines, Maglev trains, scrapyard cranes, and electric relays.

Important Terms

Electromagnetism

The branch of physics dedicated to studying the interrelationship between electricity and magnetism, recognizing them as different facets of a single fundamental force.

Solenoid

A device consisting of a cylindrical coil of wire through which an electric current flows, generating a magnetic field that is strong and uniform inside the coil, similar to a bar magnet.

Oersted's Experiment

The compass needle pointed north, as it typically would.

Closed Circuit (Current Flowing)

The compass needle swung to point perpendicular to the wire.

Reversing Current

The compass needle's deflection also reversed direction.

Applications of Electromagnets

Doorbells and Buzzers

An electromagnet rapidly pulls and releases a metal striker to create sound.

Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)

Superconducting electromagnets create powerful, uniform magnetic fields for detailed internal body imaging in medical diagnostics.

Maglev Trains

Electromagnets levitate trains above tracks to eliminate friction and propel them forward at high speeds.

Scrapyard Cranes

Large electromagnets are used to lift and release heavy loads of scrap metal by simply switching the current on or off.

Electric Relays and Switches

Electromagnets enable a small current to control a much larger current, crucial for automation and communication systems.
