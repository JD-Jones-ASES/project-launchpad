Key Concepts (1/2)

Reflection

The bouncing of a wave off a surface. The angle at which a wave strikes a surface (angle of incidence) is equal to the angle at which it bounces away (angle of reflection), both measured from the n...

Refraction

The bending of a wave as it passes from one medium into another at an angle. This occurs because the wave's speed changes as it enters a new medium, causing it to change direction.

Diffraction

The spreading of a wave as it passes through an opening or around an obstacle. This effect is most noticeable when the size of the opening or obstacle is similar to the wave's wavelength.

Interference

The interaction and combination of two or more waves that overlap in the same region of space.

Wave Nature of Light

Thomas Young's double-slit experiment demonstrated that light exhibits interference patterns, providing strong evidence that light behaves as a wave.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Optical Technologies

Reflection and refraction are the foundational principles behind mirrors, lenses, fiber optics, and the human eye, enabling vision correction and various imaging devices.

Important Terms (1/3)

Reflection

The bouncing of a wave off a surface.

Angle of Incidence

The angle at which an incoming wave strikes a surface, measured from the normal.

Angle of Reflection

The angle at which a wave bounces away from a surface, measured from the normal.

Normal

An imaginary line perpendicular to a surface at the point where a wave strikes or passes through.

Concave Mirror

An inward-curving mirror that focuses reflected light to a point.

Important Terms (2/3)

Convex Mirror

An outward-curving mirror that spreads reflected light outward, providing a wider field of view.

Refraction

The bending of a wave as it passes from one medium into another at an angle due to a change in speed.

Concave Lens

A lens that is thinner in the middle and diverges (spreads out) light rays, making objects appear smaller.

Convex Lens

A lens that is thicker in the middle and converges (focuses) light rays to a focal point, producing magnified images.

Total Internal Reflection

A special case of refraction where all light reflects back inside a dense medium when it hits a boundary at a sufficiently steep angle.

Important Terms (3/3)

Diffraction

The spreading of a wave as it passes through an opening or around an obstacle.

Wavelength

The distance between successive crests or troughs of a wave.

Interference

The interaction of two or more waves that overlap in the same region of space.

Constructive Interference

Interference that occurs when waves align crest-to-crest or trough-to-trough, increasing the amplitude.

Destructive Interference

Interference that occurs when wave crests align with troughs, decreasing or canceling the amplitude.
