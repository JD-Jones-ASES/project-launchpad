Important Terms (1/4)

Mineral

A naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical composition and an orderly internal arrangement of atoms (a crystal structure).

Crystal

A solid in which atoms are arranged in a regular, repeating three-dimensional pattern called a crystal lattice, often reflected in its external shape.

Mohs hardness scale

A scale from 1 (softest, talc) to 10 (hardest, diamond) used to rank the scratch resistance of minerals.

Luster

The way a mineral's surface reflects light, described as either metallic (shiny like metal) or nonmetallic (glassy, waxy, pearly, silky, earthy, or dull).

Streak

The color of the powder left behind when a mineral is rubbed across an unglazed porcelain plate. It is often a more reliable identification property than surface color.

Important Terms (2/4)

Cleavage

The tendency of a mineral to break along specific flat planes, reflecting weaknesses in its internal crystal structure.

Fracture

The way a mineral breaks when it does not exhibit cleavage, resulting in rough or irregular surfaces.

Specific gravity

A measure of the density of a mineral compared to the density of water.

Igneous rock

Rock formed from the cooling and solidification of magma (underground) or lava (at the surface).

Magma

Molten rock found beneath Earth's surface.

Important Terms (3/4)

Lava

Molten rock that has erupted onto Earth's surface.

Coarse-grained (phaneritic)

Igneous rock texture with large, visible mineral crystals, indicating slow cooling underground.

Fine-grained (aphanitic)

Igneous rock texture with tiny, microscopic mineral crystals, indicating rapid cooling at or near the surface.

Sedimentary rock

Rock formed from the accumulation and cementation of sediments (fragments of other rocks, mineral grains, or organic material) or from the precipitation of minerals from solution.

Clastic sedimentary rocks

Sedimentary rocks made from fragments (clasts) of pre-existing rocks.

Important Terms (4/4)

Chemical and biochemical sedimentary rocks

Sedimentary rocks formed from minerals precipitating out of water or from the accumulation of organic matter/shells.

Fossils

Preserved remains or traces of ancient organisms, commonly found in sedimentary rocks.

Metamorphic rock

Rock that has been transformed from a pre-existing rock by heat, pressure, or chemical processes without fully melting.

Rock cycle

The continuous process by which rocks are created, transformed, destroyed, and re-created over geologic time.

Introduction to Minerals and Rocks

• Rocks appear simple but hold a history of geological processes.

• They form deep underground, on ancient seabeds, or through intense heat and pressure.

• Every rock records its formation processes.

A naturally occurring, inorganic solid with a definite chemical composition and an orderly internal arrangement of atoms (a crystal structure).

What four criteria must a substance meet to be classified as a mineral?

It must be (1) naturally occurring, (2) inorganic, (3) a solid with a definite chemical composition, and (4) a characteristic crystal structure.
