Mitigation and Adaptation to Climate Change

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Mitigation

Actions aimed at reducing or preventing greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions to limit the extent of future climate change. It addresses the root causes of global warming.

Adaptation

Strategies developed to prepare for and adjust to the current and projected impacts of climate change, thereby reducing harm and taking advantage of potential new opportunities.

Renewable Energy

Energy derived from naturally replenished sources (solar, wind, hydroelectric, geothermal, biomass) that produce little to no GHG emissions during operation, making them critical for decarbonizing ...

Energy Efficiency

Using less energy to achieve the same task or outcome. It's often the cheapest and quickest way to reduce energy consumption and emissions.

Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)

Technologies that prevent CO2 emissions from entering the atmosphere by capturing them from industrial sources or directly from the air, then storing them permanently underground.

Key Concepts (2/2)

International Agreements

Global frameworks and treaties (like the UNFCCC and Paris Agreement) designed to foster international cooperation and coordinated action to address climate change, recognizing its global nature.

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)

A UN body that reviews and synthesizes global scientific literature on climate change to provide comprehensive assessments informing policy.

Important Terms (1/4)

Mitigation

Actions to reduce or prevent greenhouse gas emissions to limit future climate change.

Adaptation

Actions taken to prepare for and adjust to the current and future effects of climate change.

Renewable Energy

Energy from sources naturally replenished on human timescales (solar, wind, hydro, geothermal, biomass).

Solar energy

Electricity generated from sunlight using photovoltaic cells or concentrated solar thermal systems.

Wind energy

Electricity generated from the kinetic energy of moving air using turbines.

Important Terms (2/4)

Hydroelectric energy

Electricity generated by channeling flowing water through turbines, often using dams.

Geothermal energy

Electricity or heat derived from the Earth's internal heat.

Nuclear energy

Electricity generated from nuclear fission, producing very low operational greenhouse gas emissions, though not renewable.

Energy efficiency

Using less energy to accomplish the same task (e.g., LED lighting, insulation, efficient vehicles).

Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)

Technologies to capture CO2 from industrial sources or the atmosphere and store it underground.

Important Terms (3/4)

Point-source capture

Capturing CO2 from the exhaust of specific industrial facilities.

Direct air capture (DAC)

Extracting CO2 directly from ambient air using chemical processes.

United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (UNFCCC)

The primary international treaty on climate change, established in 1992.

Kyoto Protocol

An international treaty (1997) that set binding emission reduction targets for developed countries.

Paris Agreement

A landmark international agreement (2015) where nearly all nations pledged to limit global warming to well below 2°C, pursuing 1.5°C.

Important Terms (4/4)

Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC)

The leading international body for assessing climate change science.

Other Mitigation Strategies

Reducing methane emissions

Implementing gas capture at landfills, using feed supplements for livestock, and repairing leaks in natural gas infrastructure.

Sustainable agriculture

Reducing nitrogen fertilizer use (to lower N2O emissions), improving rice paddy management (to lower CH4), and practicing no-till farming (to increase soil carbon).

Reducing deforestation

Protecting vital carbon sinks and biodiversity hotspots, especially tropical forests.

Electrification

Shifting heating, cooking, and transportation from fossil fuels to electricity, provided that electricity is generated from clean, renewable sources.
