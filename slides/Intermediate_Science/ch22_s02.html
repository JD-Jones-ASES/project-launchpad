Key Concepts

Lunar Phases

The Moon doesn't produce its own light; its varying appearance (phases) is due to different amounts of its sunlit side facing Earth as it orbits. The cycle takes about 29.5 days.

Important Terms (1/2)

Lunar phase

The changing appearance of the Moon as seen from Earth, caused by the varying angle of sunlight illuminating the Moon's surface during its orbit.

Eclipse

An event in which one celestial body passes into the shadow of another, temporarily blocking sunlight.

Solar eclipse

An eclipse that occurs when the Moon passes directly between the Sun and Earth, casting a shadow on Earth's surface.

Lunar eclipse

An eclipse that occurs when Earth passes between the Sun and Moon, and Earth's shadow falls on the Moon.

Umbra

The darkest, central part of a shadow, where light is completely blocked. (Specifically mentioned for the Moon's shadow during a total solar eclipse).

Important Terms (2/2)

Tidal force

The gravitational force exerted by one body on another that varies across the second body's diameter, causing it to stretch or deform.

Spring tides

Exceptionally high and low tides that occur when the Sun, Moon, and Earth are aligned (during New Moon and Full Moon), combining their tidal forces.

Neap tides

Smaller tides that occur when the Sun and Moon are at right angles relative to Earth (during First and Third Quarter), causing their tidal forces to work against each other.

Giant impact hypothesis

The leading scientific theory for the Moon's origin, proposing that the Moon formed from debris ejected after a Mars-sized body collided with early Earth.

Lunar Phases

Nature of Lunar Light

The Moon does not generate its own light; it reflects sunlight.

Cause of Phases

As the Moon orbits Earth, the angle at which we view the sunlit portion of its surface changes. This cycle of changing appearances is called lunar phases.

Orbital Period

The Moon completes one full orbit around Earth, and thus one cycle of phases, approximately every 29.5 days.

Important Clarification

Lunar phases are *not* caused by Earth's shadow. Earth's shadow only causes a lunar eclipse. Phases are a result of the changing geometry of the Sun, Moon, and Earth.
