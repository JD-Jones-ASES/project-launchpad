Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Acceleration

Acceleration is the rate at which an object's velocity changes over time. It's a fundamental concept in kinematics, describing how motion itself changes.

Acceleration as a Vector Quantity

Calculating Acceleration

Acceleration ($a$) is calculated as the change in velocity ($\Delta v$) divided by the time interval ($\Delta t$) over which the change occurs: $a = \Delta v / \Delta t = (v_f - v_i) / (t_f - t_i)$.

Units of Acceleration

The SI unit for acceleration is meters per second squared ($m/s^2$). This means that the velocity changes by a certain number of meters per second *during each second*.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Relationship between Velocity and Acceleration Signs

An object speeds up when its velocity and acceleration have the *same sign*. An object slows down when its velocity and acceleration have *opposite signs*.

Acceleration on a Velocity-Time Graph

The slope of a velocity-time ($v$-$t$) graph represents the acceleration of an object. A straight line on a $v$-$t$ graph indicates uniform (constant) acceleration.

Uniform Acceleration

This refers to acceleration that remains constant over time. It simplifies motion problems significantly and is a common assumption in introductory physics.

Important Terms

Acceleration

The rate at which an object's velocity changes over time. It is a vector quantity, measured in meters per second squared ($m/s^2$).

Deceleration

A common term for acceleration that opposes the direction of motion, causing an object to slow down. In physics, it's generally understood as acceleration with a sign opposite to the velocity.

Uniform acceleration

Acceleration that remains constant in both magnitude and direction over a given time interval. On a velocity-time graph, this is represented by a straight line.

Acceleration on a Velocity-Time Graph

Positive slope

Indicates positive acceleration (velocity is increasing in the positive direction, or decreasing in the negative direction towards zero).

Negative slope

Indicates negative acceleration (velocity is decreasing in the positive direction, or increasing in the negative direction).

Zero slope (horizontal line)

Indicates zero acceleration, meaning the velocity is constant.

Steeper slope

Represents a greater magnitude of acceleration, meaning the velocity is changing more rapidly.
