Endure

To suffer something difficult, unpleasant, or painful patiently, or to remain in existence; last. The chapter questions whether the nation "could endure" the immense challenges posed by the war.

Union

Refers to the United States of America, specifically the federal government and the loyal states (the North) during the Civil War. It represents the concept of the nation as a single, unified entity.

Confederacy (Confederate States of America)

The independent government formed in 1861 by eleven Southern states that seceded from the United States, primarily over the issue of slavery and states' rights.

Important Terms (2/2)

Remembrance

The act or process of recalling past events or people, or the collective memory and commemoration of historical events, often through memorials, writings, or cultural practices.
