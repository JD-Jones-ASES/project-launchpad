Key Concepts (1/2)

Founding Principles of Government

The U.S. Constitution, particularly its Preamble, established the fundamental purposes and democratic nature of the American government, emphasizing that power originates from "We the People."

Early Presidential Leadership

George Washington's first presidency set vital precedents for leadership, emphasizing humility, dependence on citizens, divine guidance, and a peaceful transfer of power.

Advocacy for Women's Rights

Abigail Adams's powerful letter to her husband, John Adams, highlights early calls for women's inclusion and rights in the new legal framework, foreshadowing future struggles for equality.

National Identity and Symbols

"The Star-Spangled Banner," born from the War of 1812, became a potent symbol of American resilience, patriotism, and the enduring spirit of the nation.
