Important Terms (1/2)

New Nation

Refers to the United States in its nascent stage after gaining independence from Great Britain, facing the tasks of self-governance and state-building.

Laws

The codified rules and regulations necessary to govern the populace, maintain order, protect rights, and define the functions of government in the newly formed republic.

Leaders

The individuals tasked with guiding the new nation, including those who drafted foundational documents, served in early government roles, and shaped public opinion.

Shared Identity

The sense of collective belonging, common purpose, and unified culture that the diverse states and citizens of the United States needed to develop to function as a cohesive nation.
