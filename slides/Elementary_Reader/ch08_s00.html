Important Terms (1/3)

Emigrant

A person who leaves one country or region to live in another.

Expedition

A journey undertaken for a specific purpose, especially exploration.

Frontier

The border of settled land; the edge of the known world.

Prairie

A large, open area of grassland.

Wilderness

A wild, uncultivated region with no human inhabitants.

Important Terms (2/3)

Lewis and Clark Expedition

A historic journey (1804-1806) led by Meriwether Lewis and William Clark, commissioned by President Thomas Jefferson, to explore the vast territory west of the Mississippi River.

Rocky Mountains

A major mountain range in western North America, representing a significant natural barrier and landmark encountered by early explorers and pioneers.

Great Falls of the Missouri River

A series of five major waterfalls on the Missouri River in present-day Montana, a significant natural wonder documented by Lewis and Clark.

Oregon Trail

A 2,000-mile historic route used by hundreds of thousands of American pioneers in the 1840s to travel from Missouri to the Pacific Northwest.

St. Louis

A major city in Missouri, serving as a significant starting point and hub for westward bound emigrants and traders in the 19th century.

Important Terms (3/3)

Independence, Missouri

A town located on the extreme frontier of Missouri, serving as a common rendezvous point for emigrant parties preparing to embark on the Oregon Trail.

Sacagawea

A young Shoshone woman who served as an invaluable interpreter and guide for the Lewis and Clark Expedition, her presence signaling peaceful intentions to Native American tribes.

Folk Songs

Traditional songs that are passed down orally from person to person, often changing over time, reflecting the experiences and culture of a community.

Wagon Trains

Groups of covered wagons traveling together across the plains and mountains, providing safety and companionship for emigrants heading West.

The Oregon Trail (1840s)

The Great Migration

In the 1840s, thousands of Americans embarked on the Oregon Trail, a challenging two-thousand-mile journey from Missouri to the Pacific Northwest, seeking new opportunities and land.

Francis Parkman's Account

In 1846, a young Harvard student named Francis Parkman traveled part of the trail, documenting his experiences in his influential book, *The Oregon Trail*.
