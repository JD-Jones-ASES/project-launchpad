Important Terms (1/2)

Allegory

A story, poem, or picture that can be interpreted to reveal a hidden meaning, typically a moral or political one. In this unit, *The Pilgrim's Progress* is a prime example, where characters and eve...

Blessed

Favored by God; happy in a deep, spiritual way. This term is central to the Beatitudes, describing the profound well-being granted to those who live virtuously.

Burden

A heavy load, either physical or spiritual; something difficult to bear. In the context of *Little Women* and *The Pilgrim's Progress*, it represents life's challenges, sins, or spiritual struggles.

Meek

Gentle, humble, and patient; not easily angered or provoked. One of the virtues praised in the Beatitudes, associated with inheriting the earth.

Pilgrim

A person who journeys to a sacred place for religious reasons; metaphorically, a person on a spiritual journey. The concept frames the moral development of the March sisters and Christian's quest.

Important Terms (2/2)

Righteousness

The quality of being morally right and just. The Beatitudes emphasize hungering and thirsting for righteousness as a path to spiritual fulfillment.

B. The Beatitudes (Matthew 5:3–12, King James Version)

Context

These are the opening statements of Jesus' Sermon on the Mount, outlining the qualities of truly blessed people. They are counter-cultural, valuing humility and spiritual focus over worldly success.

Key Beatitudes and Their Meanings

Overall Message

The Beatitudes describe a radical ethic focused on internal character, spiritual values, and selfless actions, promising profound spiritual rewards and a unique form of happiness.
