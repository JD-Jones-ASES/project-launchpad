Important Terms (1/2)

Naturalist

A person who studies and is an expert on natural history, especially one who observes nature firsthand. (e.g., John Muir, Henry David Thoreau)

Deliberately (Living)

To live with conscious intention and purpose; to make choices with careful thought rather than by chance or habit, as advocated by Thoreau.

Romantic Poetry

A literary movement (late 18th to mid-19th century) emphasizing emotion, individualism, the glorification of nature, and the past. William Wordsworth is a key figure.

Fascicles

Small handmade booklets of poems, often sewn together, like those Emily Dickinson created.

Auroral

Resembling the dawn; characterized by a golden, fresh, and luminous quality. (Used by Thoreau to describe his cabin.)

Important Terms (2/2)

Tarn

A small mountain lake, typically formed in a cirque excavated by a glacier. (Used by Thoreau to describe Walden Pond.)

Jocund

Cheerful; merry; full of high spirits and gladness. (Used by Wordsworth to describe the company of daffodils.)

Abash

To make someone feel ashamed, embarrassed, or discomfited. (Used by Dickinson in "Hope Is the Thing with Feathers" to describe what a storm cannot do to hope.)

Extremity

The most desperate need or severe difficulty. (Used by Dickinson to note that hope never asks for a crumb even in such circumstances.)

Undergrowth

A dense growth of shrubs and other plants, especially under trees in woodland. (Used by Frost to describe where the road bent.)
