Important Terms (1/2)

Reconstruction

The process by which the U.S. government readmitted the Confederate states and addressed the rights of formerly enslaved people after the Civil War (1865-1877).

Freedmen

The term used for formerly enslaved African Americans after the Civil War.

Freedmen's Bureau (Bureau of Refugees, Freedmen, and Abandoned Lands)

A U.S. government agency established in 1865 to aid freed slaves in the South during the Reconstruction era. It provided food, housing, medical aid, and established schools.

Important Terms (2/2)

Literacy

The ability to read and write. For freedmen, achieving literacy was a powerful symbol of freedom and a crucial tool for economic, social, and political empowerment.

Fifteenth Amendment

Ratified in 1870, this constitutional amendment granted African American men the right to vote.

Segregation

The enforced separation of different racial groups in a country, community, or establishment. While legally mandated segregation (Jim Crow) came later, its roots were visible during Reconstruction.
