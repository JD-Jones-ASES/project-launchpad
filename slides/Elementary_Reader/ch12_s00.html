Important Terms (1/2)

Plantation

A large farm, especially in the Southern United States, where crops like cotton or tobacco were grown, primarily by the labor of enslaved people.

Emancipation Proclamation

An executive order issued by President Abraham Lincoln on January 1, 1863, declaring all enslaved people in Confederate states to be free.

Tuskegee Institute

A historically black college founded by Booker T. Washington in Alabama, focused on vocational training, practical skills, and agricultural education.

Metaphor

A figure of speech in which a word or phrase is applied to an object or action to which it is not literally applicable, for example, "a mask that grins and lies" or "a caged bird."

Pneumonia

A serious infection of the lungs, common and often deadly in the era described in "The Last Leaf."

Important Terms (2/2)

"We Wear the Mask"

"Caged Bird"

A central metaphor in Dunbar's poem "Sympathy," symbolizing the oppression and longing for freedom experienced by African Americans.

Greenwich Village

A neighborhood in New York City historically known as a hub for artists and bohemians, serving as the setting for O. Henry's "The Last Leaf."

O. Henry

The pen name of William Sydney Porter, an American short story writer famous for his witty, often ironic, and surprise endings.

Surprise Ending

A narrative technique in which the conclusion of a story features an unexpected twist or revelation that changes the reader's understanding of events.
