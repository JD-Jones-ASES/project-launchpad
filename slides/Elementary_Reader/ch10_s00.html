Important Terms (1/2)

Stratagems

(from Douglass) Clever tricks or plans, especially those used to deceive an enemy or achieve a goal. Douglass used these to teach himself.

Urchins

(from Douglass) Young children, often mischievous or poorly dressed. Douglass exchanged bread with these children for reading lessons.

Incompatible

(from Douglass) So different in nature as to be incapable of coexisting; unable to live or work together. Education and slavery were seen as incompatible by slaveholders.

Out of kilter

(from Truth) Out of order; not right or in proper balance. Sojourner Truth used this phrase to describe the societal state concerning rights.

Intellect

(from Truth) The ability to think and reason. Men at the convention argued women lacked sufficient intellect for equal rights.

Important Terms (2/2)

Refuge

(from Whittier) A safe place; shelter. The slave mother hoped God would prove a refuge for her children.

Abolitionist

A person who supported or worked for the immediate termination of slavery. Douglass, Truth, Whittier, and Stowe were all significant figures in the abolitionist movement.

Refrain

(from Whittier) A literary device where a line or set of lines is repeated several times over the course of a poem or song. Whittier uses this to emphasize the mother's grief.

Sympathy

(from Stowe) Shared feeling or understanding with what is grand and noble. Eva possessed this quality, enhancing her reading of the Bible.

Arbor

(from Stowe) A shady garden shelter, typically made of a trellis or open framework covered with climbing plants. Tom and Eva sat in an arbor.
