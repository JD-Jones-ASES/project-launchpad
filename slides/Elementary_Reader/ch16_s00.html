Important Terms (1/2)

Immigration

The act of coming to live permanently in a foreign country. At the turn of the 20th century, this involved millions arriving in the U.S. seeking new opportunities.

Industrialization

The large-scale development of industries in a country or region, leading to significant economic and social transformation, characterized by factory growth, mass production, and technological inno...

Urbanization

The process by which towns and cities are formed and become larger as more people move from rural areas to urban centers, often in search of work and a better life.

Tenements

Overcrowded, poorly maintained apartment buildings, typical in late 19th and early 20th-century urban centers, often housing multiple families in small, unsanitary spaces.

Ellis Island

The primary immigration inspection station in New York Harbor from 1892 to 1954, serving as the gateway for millions of European immigrants entering the United States.

Important Terms (2/2)

Angel Island

An immigration station in San Francisco Bay, which operated from 1910 to 1940, primarily processing Asian immigrants, often with harsher and longer detention experiences than Ellis Island.

Nativism

The policy of protecting the interests of native-born or established inhabitants against those of immigrants. This often led to discrimination, xenophobia, and restrictive immigration policies.

Muckrakers

Investigative journalists and writers who exposed corruption, social problems, and corporate abuses in politics and business during the Progressive Era, using their work to advocate for reform.

Settlement Houses

Community centers, such as Jane Addams's Hull House, established in urban immigrant neighborhoods to provide social services, education, and advocacy for the poor and new arrivals.
