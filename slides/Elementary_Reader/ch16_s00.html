Important Terms (1/2)

The New Colossus

A sonnet written by Emma Lazarus in 1883 for the pedestal of the Statue of Liberty, famously welcoming immigrants to America.

Mother of Exiles

The symbolic name given to the Statue of Liberty in Lazarus's poem, representing America's role as a refuge for those fleeing oppression or seeking a new start.

Tenements

Overcrowded, often dilapidated apartment buildings in cities, especially common in the late 19th and early 20th centuries, where many poor immigrants lived in harsh conditions.

Sanitarian

A public health worker; someone concerned with improving public health and hygiene, particularly in urban environments.

Klondike Gold Rush

A migration by an estimated 100,000 prospectors to the Klondike region of Yukon, in northwestern Canada, between 1896 and 1899, after gold was discovered there.

Important Terms (2/2)

Demesne

An estate or domain; the land attached to a manor, providing revenue for the owner. Used in "The Call of the Wild" to describe Judge Miller's property.

Crimean War (1853–1856)

A military conflict fought between Russia and an alliance of Britain, France, and the Ottoman Empire, notable for early battlefield reporting.

Light Brigade

A cavalry unit of the British Army involved in a disastrous charge during the Battle of Balaclava in the Crimean War, famously immortalized by Tennyson.

Poet Laureate

A poet officially appointed by a monarch or government, typically expected to compose poems for state occasions. Alfred, Lord Tennyson held this position for Britain.

Harlem Renaissance

An intellectual and cultural revival of African American music, dance, art, fashion, literature, theater, and politics centered in Harlem, New York City, during the 1920s and 1930s.

Public Reaction

Author

Historical Context
