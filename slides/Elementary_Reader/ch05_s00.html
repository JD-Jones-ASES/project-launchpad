Important Terms (1/2)

Almanac

A yearly publication containing useful information, calendars, weather predictions, and wise sayings. Benjamin Franklin's *Poor Richard's Almanack* was a famous example.

Diligence

Careful and persistent work or effort. A key virtue promoted by Franklin's proverbs.

Extended Metaphor

A comparison between two unlike things that continues throughout a series of sentences in a paragraph or lines in a poem. Anne Bradstreet uses this in "The Author to Her Book."

Hearth

The floor of a fireplace; by extension, a symbol for the home and its comforts.

Hornbook

A single page containing the alphabet, numbers, and often the Lord's Prayer, protected by a thin sheet of transparent horn, used as a primer for colonial children.

Important Terms (2/2)

Primer

A basic reading textbook for young children. *The New England Primer* was the most popular such book in colonial America.

Proverb/Aphorism

A short, well-known saying stating a general truth or piece of advice. Benjamin Franklin's *Almanack* was full of proverbs.

Thrift

The wise and careful use of money and resources, avoiding waste. A core principle advocated by Benjamin Franklin.

Vanity

Excessive pride in one's appearance, achievements, or possessions; also, the quality of being worthless or futile.

Virtue

Behavior showing high moral standards; a good quality or habit. Colonial education and literature often aimed to instill virtue.
