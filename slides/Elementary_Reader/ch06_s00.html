Important Terms (1/2)

Rhetoric

Declaration

A formal or explicit statement or announcement. The most famous example from this era is the Declaration of Independence, a formal statement of separation.

Grievances

Real or imagined wrongs or complaints forming grounds for protest. The colonists compiled long lists of grievances against British policies and the King.

Tyranny

Cruel and oppressive government or rule. The colonists often accused King George III and the British Parliament of tyrannical actions.

Liberty

The state of being free within society from oppressive restrictions imposed by authority on one's way of life, behavior, or political views. A core value for the American revolutionaries.

Important Terms (2/2)

Self-governance

The ability or right of a people or nation to govern themselves; autonomy. A primary goal for the colonists seeking independence.

Patriot

A person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to defend it against enemies or detractors. In the context of the Revolution, colonists who supported independence.

Loyalist

A colonist who remained loyal to the British crown during the American Revolution.
