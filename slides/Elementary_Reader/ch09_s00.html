Important Terms (1/2)

Abolitionist

An individual who supports the abolition (ending) of slavery.

Chariot

A two-wheeled vehicle pulled by horses; in the Bible, sometimes associated with divine transport or journeys to heaven.

Conversion

A profound spiritual change, often involving a commitment to a religious faith, as experienced by John Newton.

Deliverance

The act of being rescued or set free, especially by divine intervention or God's power.

Grace

God's undeserved favor, love, and mercy towards humanity, often seen as the source of salvation and transformation.

Important Terms (2/2)

Hymn

A song of praise to God, typically sung in worship services, often poetic and set to music.

Psalms

A collection of 150 sacred songs and poems found in the Old Testament of the Bible.

Refrain

A repeated line or group of lines in a song or poem; a chorus.

Shepherd

A person who tends, feeds, and guards sheep; metaphorically, a term used in the Bible to describe God's care, protection, and guidance for His people.

Spiritual

A religious folk song created by African Americans during the period of slavery, expressing deep faith, sorrow, and hope.
