The Sonnets in Context: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The 1609 Quarto Publication

The first and only authorized collection of Shakespeare's sonnets, published by Thomas Thorpe, yet its origins and Shakespeare's involvement remain debated.

Controversy over Authorization

Scholars debate whether Shakespeare personally arranged and authorized the 1609 publication or if Thorpe acquired the manuscript without his blessing.

The "Mr. W.H." Dedication

A brief, cryptic dedication by the publisher T.T. (Thomas Thorpe) to "Mr. W.H.," sparking centuries of speculation about whether W.H. was the sonnets' inspirer (the Fair Youth) or their procurer.

Enduring Appeal

The sonnets' lasting significance stems from their direct address, in the first person, to fundamental human questions about love, beauty, time, betrayal, and the power of language.

Important Terms (1/3)

Stationers' Company

The guild in London responsible for regulating the book trade, where new books were registered for publication.

Quarto

A book size resulting from printing on sheets folded twice, creating four leaves (eight pages). The 1609 edition of Shakespeare's Sonnets was a quarto.

London stationer

A printer, publisher, or bookseller in London during Shakespeare's time. Thomas Thorpe was a stationer.

Thomas Thorpe (T.T.)

The London stationer who registered and published *Shake-speares Sonnets* in 1609.

Mr. W.H.

The mysterious individual to whom Thomas Thorpe dedicated the 1609 Quarto, whose identity and role remain highly debated.

Important Terms (2/3)

Begetter

A key word in the dedication to "Mr. W.H."; its interpretation (either "inspirer" or "procurer") is central to identifying W.H.

Fair Youth

The beautiful young man, likely an aristocrat, who is the primary subject of Sonnets 1-126, celebrated by the poet.

Dark Lady

The mysterious woman with dark features who is the subject of Sonnets 127-152, depicted as an object of tormented desire.

Rival Poet

A competing poet mentioned in Sonnets 78-86, seen as vying for the Fair Youth's patronage and affection.

The 1609 Quarto and Its Publication

Registration and Content

In 1609, Thomas Thorpe, a London stationer, registered *Shake-speares Sonnets. Never before Imprinted.* The volume contained 154 sonnets followed by *A Lover's Complaint*.
