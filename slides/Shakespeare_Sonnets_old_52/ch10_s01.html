Chapter 10, Section 1: Constancy and the Muse

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Return of the Muse

After a period of silence and neglect, the speaker's poetic inspiration is rekindled, leading to a prolific burst of verse dedicated to renewing old vows and affirming love.

Constancy and Endurance

The central theme of the chapter is the unwavering nature of true love, asserting its ability to persist despite the passage of time, the changes of fortune, or the speaker's own human failings.

Defining Love

Beyond merely describing affection, these sonnets actively seek to define the essence of love, portraying it as an "ever-fixed mark" impervious to change.

Time and Perception

The chapter explores how beauty, like time, moves subtly but inexorably, and how enduring love defies the destructive power of time.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Human Frailty vs. Ideal Love

The speaker acknowledges personal imperfections and public perception, contrasting these human limitations with the ideal, steadfast nature of the love he defines in Sonnet 116.

Philosophical Heart of the Sequence

These sonnets are presented as the emotional and intellectual climax of Shakespeare's work, providing profound insights into the nature of love and fidelity.

Important Terms (1/2)

Muse

In classical mythology, one of the nine goddesses who preside over the arts and sciences; here, personified as the speaker's source of poetic inspiration.

Sonnet Sequence

A group of sonnets linked by theme or by an address to a particular person, forming a longer poetic work.

Apostrophe

A rhetorical device in which a speaker directly addresses an absent person, an inanimate object, or an abstract idea. Sonnet C, for example, is an apostrophe to the Muse.

Constancy

The quality of being faithful and dependable; steadfastness. A core theme of Chapter 10.

Ever-fixed mark

A phrase from Sonnet 116, describing love as an unchanging point of reference, like a lighthouse or a navigational star, that is not moved by storms or shifting circumstances.

Important Terms (2/2)

Topical Sonnet

A sonnet that contains references to contemporary events or political figures, often in an encoded or symbolic manner. Sonnet 107 is identified as one such example.
