Important Terms (1/4)

term of life

For the duration of one's life; a legal phrase denoting a period bounded by death.

revolt

Rebellion; desertion; the turning away of affection.

title

A legal right of ownership or possession; a claim. The speaker uses it to describe his "happiness" as a secured claim.

humour

Here: mood, inclination, or whim. Indicates the youth's potential capriciousness.

alter'd new

Changed into something new; transformed, specifically by infidelity.

Important Terms (2/4)

wrinkles strange

Unusual expressions or lines on the face that might signal a troubled or treacherous heart.

Eve's apple

The fruit from the Garden of Eden, beautiful and appealing but containing the origin of humanity's fall. Used as an allusion to charge the youth's beauty with spiritual danger.

show

Outward appearance; display; the visible surface as distinguished from inner reality.

moving others

Stirring emotion, affection, or action in other people.

husband (verb)

To manage frugally and wisely; to conserve. Used to describe how "lords and owners" manage their gifts.

Important Terms (3/4)

stewards

Those who manage property or resources on behalf of an owner; those who hold power in trust for another. Contrasts with "lords and owners."

base infection

A low or ignoble corruption; a degrading disease.

outbraves

Surpasses in beauty, display, or dignity.

fester

To suppurate; to rot inwardly; to become infected and putrid. A violent, physical word.

canker

A destructive growth or ulcer, especially in a plant; often referring to an internal flaw or corruption.

Important Terms (4/4)

budding name

A reputation just beginning to grow or develop.

sweets

Pleasures, delights; also, in this context, something that sweetens or disguises.

tells the story

Reveals or recounts the events or nature of something.
