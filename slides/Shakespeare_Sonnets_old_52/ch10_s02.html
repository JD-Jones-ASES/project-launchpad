Important Terms (1/4)

Fury

Poetic frenzy; divine inspiration of the Muses in classical tradition.

Lays

Songs; lyric poems; short musical compositions.

Argument

The subject or theme of a poem; its rhetorical purpose.

Resty

Sluggish; lazy; reluctant to move or act.

Prevent'st

Outrun; forestall; arrive before, thereby stopping or hindering.

Important Terms (2/4)

Truant

One who neglects duty; an absentee.

Truth in beauty dy'd

Truth dyed (colored) in beauty; truth embodied in a beautiful form.

Gilded tomb

An ornate, gold-covered monument, implying elaborate but ultimately perishable memorials.

Haply

Perhaps; by chance.

Merchandiz'd

Turned into merchandise; made commercial, implying cheapening or trivializing.

Important Terms (3/4)

Philomel

The nightingale, from the mythological princess Philomela, often associated with mournful or beautiful song.

Burthens

Burdens; also, a musical term for the bass accompaniment or refrain of a song.

Poverty

Insufficiency; lack; poor quality (of verse or artistic output).

Scope

Range; extent; opportunity.

Over-goes

Surpasses; exceeds; goes beyond.

Important Terms (4/4)

Blunt

Dull; not sharp; lacking refinement.

Pass

Purpose; aim; end.

Process

A course of events; a legal writ.

Steal

To take secretly; to pass imperceptibly.

Hue

Color; appearance; complexion.
