Important Terms (1/5)

scanted

Stinted; given too little of. Implies a measured shortfall rather than outright neglect.

bonds

Obligations and legal ties. Carries both emotional weight and a precise legal sense.

dear-purchas'd right

A right dearly bought, at great cost; something earned and owed.

level of your frown

Within range, as of a weapon aimed level. To be taken aim at.

surmise, accumulate

To add conjecture upon conjecture, building a flimsy case from unproven assumptions.

Important Terms (2/5)

appeal

A legal term for a formal plea or counter-argument; a formal defense.

prolepsis

A classical rhetorical strategy of anticipating and answering objections or accusations before they are raised by an opponent.

eager compounds

Sharp, bitter medicinal preparations, often herbal mixtures. "Eager" means keen-edged or tart.

purge

To take a purgative; to cleanse the body by forced evacuation, a standard Galenic remedy.

meetness

Fitness; appropriateness; suitability.

Important Terms (3/5)

policy

Worldly cunning; calculated strategy. Often carried a slightly sinister connotation in Shakespeare's time, suggesting manipulation.

rank of goodness

Surfeited or sickened by goodness; "rank" meaning swollen, overfull, or corrupt from excess.

fell sick of you

Became ill; but also, fell ill *because* of you, highlighting the paradox of the youth's overwhelming goodness.

Siren tears

The deceptive, enchanting weeping of an enchantress. Refers to the Sirens of classical myth who lured sailors to their destruction.

limbecks

Alembics; alchemical distillation vessels used to purify or concentrate substances. Here, "foul" implies something vile being refined into its deceptive essence.

Important Terms (4/5)

spheres (eyes out of their spheres)

In Ptolemaic cosmology, planets moved in crystalline spheres. Eyes "out of their spheres" implies being jolted from their proper orbits due to astonishment or madness.

madding fever

A feverish frenzy; a maddening heat or derangement.

benefit of ill

Profit or advantage gained through suffering; a paradox where adversity leads to greater good.

befriends me now

Proves useful to me now; serves as a benefit in the present.

nerves

Sinews; tendons.

Important Terms (5/5)

ransom

A sum paid for release; to free from captivity or obligation by payment.
