Important Terms (1/3)

Quatrain

A stanza of four lines, typically with an alternating rhyme scheme (e.g., ABAB). Shakespearean sonnets feature three quatrains.

Couplet

Two consecutive lines of verse that rhyme (e.g., GG) and are typically of the same meter. In a Shakespearean sonnet, the closing couplet often provides a conclusion or twist.

Anaphora

The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or lines for emphasis.

Personification

Attributing human qualities or characteristics to inanimate objects, abstract concepts, or animals.

Apostrophe

A direct address to an absent person, an abstract quality, or an inanimate object.

Important Terms (2/3)

Antithesis

The juxtaposition of contrasting ideas, often in parallel grammatical structures, to highlight differences.

Oxymoron

A figure of speech that combines contradictory terms, creating a paradoxical effect.

Synecdoche

A figure of speech in which a part is made to represent the whole, or vice versa.

Enjambment

The continuation of a sentence or phrase from one line of poetry to the next without a pause or break, creating a sense of flow or urgency.

End-stopping

A line of poetry that concludes with a natural grammatical pause, often marked by punctuation, creating a sense of deliberation or finality.

Important Terms (3/3)

Imagery Clusters

Groups of related metaphors or images that recur throughout a body of work, building interconnected meaning through their repetition and variation.
