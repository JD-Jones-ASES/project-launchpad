Important Terms (1/4)

painting need

Cosmetic enhancement; the need for artificial improvement. The speaker implies the youth's natural beauty requires no poetic "makeup."

barren tender of a poet's debt

A meager, unfruitful fulfillment of a poet's obligation. Refers to insufficient or worthless poetic tribute.

slept in your report

Remained silent about your qualities; failed to record your praises.

extant

Still existing; currently alive. Emphasizes the youth's living presence.

modern quill

A contemporary writer's pen. Often implies "ordinary" or "fashionable" rather than exceptional in Shakespeare's usage.

Important Terms (2/4)

immured

Walled in; enclosed; confined within walls. Suggests the youth's unique qualities are intrinsically contained within him.

lean penury

Extreme poverty; want. Describes a poet lacking the intellectual or artistic resources to praise the youth adequately.

counterpart

An exact copy; a perfect likeness. Implies a poem that faithfully reproduces the youth's truth.

fond

Foolish; also: affectionate. Used to gently rebuke the youth's appetite for excessive flattery.

comments richly compiled

Elaborate, collected annotations or praises. Refers to the rival's detailed, ornate writing.

Important Terms (3/4)

golden quill

A precious, excellent writer's pen. A metaphor for the rival's superior (or showy) poetic craft.

precious phrase by all the Muses filed

Language refined and polished by the nine classical goddesses of artistic inspiration. "Filed" means smoothed and perfected.

unlettered clerk

A church official or scribe who cannot read and must rely on rote responses. A self-diminishing image used by the speaker.

able spirit

A writer of genuine talent and power, acknowledging the rival's gifts.

holds his rank before

Maintains a position ahead of the spoken words; has priority. The speaker claims his unspoken love is more important.

Important Terms (4/4)

proud full sail

The magnificent, fully rigged vessel of the rival's verse. A powerful nautical metaphor for the rival's grand poetry.

inhearse

To enclose as in a hearse or coffin; to bury alive. A vivid and unusual word describing the rival's verse silencing the speaker's thoughts.

compeers

Companions; equals; fellow poets. Referring to the rival poet's associates.

astonished

Struck with wonder and awe (stronger than modern usage).

affable familiar ghost

An easy-to-speak-with, courteous, or pleasant spirit who is intimately known (familiar), implying a supernatural muse or collaborator.
