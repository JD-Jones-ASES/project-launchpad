Important Terms (1/4)

fell arrest

A fierce, absolute seizure; describes death as a violent legal arrest without possibility of bail.

consecrated

Dedicated to a sacred purpose; made holy through dedication.

dregs

The sediment at the bottom of a vessel; the least valuable residue. Used dismissively to refer to the body.

coward conquest of a wretch's knife

A contemptuous phrase for the body's vulnerability to death, implying the body lacks the spirit's courage and is easily defeated.

sweet-season'd showers

Timely, nourishing rains that arrive in their proper season, symbolizing the beloved's essential nature.

Important Terms (2/4)

filching

Stealing; pilfering by petty theft. Used to describe "filching age" (time) stealing one's treasures.

pine and surfeit

To waste away from want (pine) and to overindulge to the point of sickness (surfeit). Describes the lover's extreme emotional states.

barren of new pride

Empty of new ornament or novelty; lacking fashionable display or stylistic innovation.

compounds strange

Unusual combinations; exotic new mixtures of style, possibly referring to experimental poetry.

noted weed

Well-known garment; a recognizable or familiar style of writing. "Weed" means garment (from Old English *waed*).

Important Terms (3/4)

argument

Subject matter; the main theme or topic of a literary work.

glass

A mirror or looking-glass. Often a *memento mori* in the sonnet tradition, reflecting transience.

dial

A sundial or clock face, used to observe the passage of time.

shady stealth

The imperceptible, silent movement of a sundial's shadow, symbolizing time's quiet but relentless progress.

waste blanks

Empty spaces; blank pages in a book, waiting to be filled. "Waste" implies unused or vacant.

Important Terms (4/4)

offices

Duties; functions; tasks assigned to an agent. Here, the mirror, dial, and book perform "offices" of reflection.

mouthed graves

Graves imagined as having mouths, gaping open to receive the dead. Links wrinkles to the inevitable end.

Pride and Fear

Privacy and Display

He desires to be "with you alone," to hoard his beloved for himself, but then wants the world to "see my pleasure," to display his joy publicly.

Feast and Famine

He swings between being "all full with feasting on your sight" (surfeit) and being "clean starved for a look" (pine). He is never truly at peace, always either overwhelmed or deprived.
