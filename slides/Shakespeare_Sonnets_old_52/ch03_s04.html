Important Terms (1/3)

Store

A stock or supply; also, the means of reproduction. To be "made for store" implies an intention by nature for breeding and increasing the world's beauty. (Sonnet XI, XIV)

Seal

A carved device used to impress a wax seal on documents, authenticating them. Metaphorically, the Youth is a seal meant to print copies of himself. (Sonnet XI)

Endow'd

Given gifts by nature; equipped with qualities. Implies an endowment, a perpetual fund whose interest should be spent, not hoarded. (Sonnet XI)

Brave

Splendid; magnificent (older meaning). Used in Sonnet XII to describe a "brave day sunk in hideous night," setting a tone of grandeur destroyed.

Sable

Black; dark (from heraldry). Refers to dark hair that turns white, adding a formal weight to the image of aging. (Sonnet XII)

Important Terms (2/3)

Bier

A frame or carriage for conveying a coffin. Used in Sonnet XII to describe harvested grain, making the harvest explicitly funerary.

Scythe

The traditional implement of the Grim Reaper, symbolizing Time's weapon. A powerful and recurrent motif. (Sonnet XII)

Breed

Offspring; also, the act of reproduction. A plain, biological term used to underscore the essential simplicity of the solution to time's decay. (Sonnet XII)

Semblance

Appearance; likeness; outward form. Refers to the Youth's visible beauty that should be passed on. (Sonnet XIII)

Lease

A legal term for the right to occupy and use property for a fixed period. Implies that beauty is not owned outright but held temporarily. (Sonnet XIII)

Important Terms (3/3)

Determination

The legal expiry of a term, or the end of a lease. A child would prevent beauty's "determination" or termination. (Sonnet XIII)

Husbandry

Careful management; stewardship of an estate. Also implicitly refers to the duties of a husband, elegantly doubling the poem's demands. (Sonnet XIII)

Astronomy

Here used loosely for astrology—the practice of reading stars to predict future events. (Sonnet XIV)

Prognosticate

To foretell; to predict. Used in the context of astrology, but here applied to the dire consequences of the Youth's choice. (Sonnet XIV)

Doom and date

A formulaic phrase meaning the final, irreversible conclusion or appointed ending. (Sonnet XIV)
