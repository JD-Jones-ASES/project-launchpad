Important Terms (1/4)

dear (S. 87)

Precious, costly, but also beloved. The pun suggests the youth is too expensive both in price and affection for the poet to maintain.

estimate (S. 87)

The assessed value or worth of something. Refers to the youth's self-knowledge of their own value.

charter (S. 87)

A formal legal document granting rights or privileges. The youth's inherent worth grants them freedom from the speaker's claims.

bonds (S. 87)

Legal obligations or ties. The speaker's claims on the relationship are "determinate" (expired).

patent (S. 87)

An exclusive right granted by authority. The speaker's "patent" to the youth's love is revoked due to lack of merit.

Important Terms (2/4)

misprision (S. 87)

A legal term for error, misunderstanding, or failure to report a crime. Here, the gift of love was founded on a mistake in judgment.

set me light (S. 88)

To treat with contempt; to regard as worthless or insignificant.

forsworn (S. 88)

Having broken a sworn oath; perjured.

attainted (S. 88)

Convicted; stained with dishonor; found guilty. A legal term implying formal condemnation.

vantage (S. 88)

Advantage; benefit; gain.

Important Terms (3/4)

lameness (S. 89)

Literally, a physical disability; here, a figurative weakness or defect of character.

halt (S. 89)

To limp; to walk with difficulty.

profane (S. 89)

To treat something sacred with disrespect; to violate. The speaker fears his mention of the youth's name would dishonor it.

haply (S. 89)

By chance; perhaps.

bent to cross (S. 90)

Inclined to obstruct; determined to work against.

Important Terms (4/4)

rearward (S. 90)

The rear guard of an army; that which follows behind. To "come in the rearward of a conquer'd woe" is to attack after the main battle is already lost.

purpos'd overthrow (S. 90)

A deliberate defeat; a planned destruction.

onset (S. 90)

The first attack; the beginning of a battle or assault.
