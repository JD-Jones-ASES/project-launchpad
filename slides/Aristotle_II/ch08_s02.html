Study Slides

The Four Causes

Cause. Greek Term

Definition

Material. *Hyle*

The "stuff" or underlying substrate out of which something is made.

Formal. *Eidos*

The structure, pattern, or defining shape that makes a thing what it is.

Efficient. —

The agent or process that brings the thing into being.

Final. *Telos*

The purpose, goal, or end for which a thing exists.

Potentiality and Actuality

Potentiality (*dynamis*)

The power or capacity of a thing to become something else or to act in a specific way (e.g., a seed is potentially an oak tree).

Actuality (*energeia*)

The fulfillment of that potential; the state of a thing when it is actually doing what it has the capacity to do.

Relationship

Matter represents potentiality, while form represents actuality. The development of natural things is the movement from potentiality toward actuality.

The Unmoved Mover

Pure Actuality

It contains no potentiality or matter.

Pure Thought

It is "thought thinking itself."

The Object of Desire

It moves the cosmos not by physical force, but by being the object of the cosmos’s desire and love.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Dynamis

Power, capacity, or potentiality; the ability of a thing to act or become.

Eidos

Form, structure, or species; the defining pattern of a thing. Unlike Plato, Aristotle believed forms do not exist separately from matter.

Energeia

Actuality, activity, or "being-at-work"; the fulfillment of a capacity.

Hyle

Matter or "stuff"; the material substrate of a thing. Originally meant "wood" or "timber."

Ousia

Being, substance, or essence; the primary category of things that exist in their own right.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Proton kinoun akineton

"The first mover unmoved"; the first principle of motion that is pure actuality and thought.

Telos

End, goal, or purpose; that for the sake of which a thing exists.
