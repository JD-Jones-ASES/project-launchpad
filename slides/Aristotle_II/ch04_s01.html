Study Slides

The Mastery of Metaphor

Genus to Species

Using a general term for a specific one (e.g., "lying" for the specific act of anchoring).

Species to Genus

Using a specific term for a general one (e.g., "ten thousand" for the general concept of "many").

Species to Species

Substituting one specific action for another within the same class (e.g., "cleft the water" for "taking away").

Proportional (Analogy)

A four-part relationship where item A is to B as C is to D (e.g., a cup is the "shield of Dionysus").

The Twin Virtues of Style: Clarity and Elevation

Virtue. Definition

Risk of Excess

Clarity (sapheneia). Language that is easily understood by the audience.

Meanness: Use of only ordinary words leads to a flat, prose-like style.

Elevation (to onkos). Language that possesses dignity, beauty, and distinction.

Riddle/Jargon: Use of only rare or exotic words leads to obscurity.

Epic Poetry vs. Tragedy

Scope and Magnitude

Epic is long and can present multiple episodes and threads of action. Tragedy is concentrated, achieving a higher intensity of focus.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Eudaiphyia

A good natural endowment; being naturally well-gifted. Used by Aristotle to describe the innate talent for metaphor.

Hexameter

The six-beat meter of Greek epic poetry; described as the most "stately and most massive" meter.

Mesotes (The Mean)

The structural insight that virtue or excellence lies between two extremes (excess and deficiency).

Metaphora (Metaphor)

The application of a name that belongs to one thing to something else.

Sapheneia (Clarity)

The virtue of style that ensures the audience can understand the language; its deficiency leads to "meanness."

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

To Onkos (Elevation)

The virtue of style that provides dignity and weight; its excess leads to "riddles" or "jargon."

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle, what is the definition of metaphor ($metaphora$)?

The application of a name that belongs to one thing to something else.

Aristotle describes metaphor as a sign of genius because it implies a natural eye for _____.

resemblances.

Which element of poetic diction is unique because it cannot be taught or learnt from others?

Metaphor.

Name the type of metaphor that applies a genus name to a specific instance or species.

Genus to species (e.g., using "lying" for anchoring).

Using "ten thousand" to signify the general concept of "many" is an example of which type of metaphor?

Species to genus.

What type of metaphor is illustrated by substituting "drew away the life" for "taking away"?

Species to species.

Key Concepts (2/2)

A proportional metaphor is structured around what logical concept?

Analogy (e.g., A is to B as C is to D).

Term: $eudaiphyia$

Definition: A good natural endowment or being naturally well-gifted, necessary for making metaphors.

How does Aristotle's view of metaphor differ from his view of other poetic elements like meter and grammar?

Metaphor is an innate talent, whereas others are part of $techne$ (a learnable craft).

According to Aristotle, what is the likely result of a poet mastering rules of diction but lacking the gift for metaphor?

Technically correct but ultimately forgettable work.

What is the primary cognitive function of metaphor in Aristotle's theory?

It creates understanding by connecting two domains through an unexpected similarity.

In the metaphor describing the sun as "sowing the god-created light," what domain is used to explain the sun's action?

Farming (sowing seed).
