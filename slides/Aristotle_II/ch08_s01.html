Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: What was Horace’s primary contribution to the understanding of poetry’s purpose?

Horace formulated the dual purpose of poetry as aut prodesse aut delectare: to both benefit (instruct) and delight the audience.

Q: Which three unities became the standard for judging drama during the Italian Renaissance?

The unities of time, place, and action (often attributed to Castelvetro).

Q: How did William Shakespeare’s tragedies align with Aristotelian principles despite ignoring the unities of time and place?

His tragedies featured heroes of genuine greatness undone by errors of judgment, with plots moving through reversal and recognition toward a catastrophic, yet inevitable, end.

Q: What is the "trivium" in the medieval university curriculum, and what are its Aristotelian roots?

The trivium consists of grammar, logic, and rhetoric. Logic stems from Aristotle’s Organon, grammar from the Poetics and Rhetoric, and rhetoric from the Rhetoric itself.

Q: According to the text, why is Aristotle’s lost book on comedy significant?

It would have provided a penetrating analysis of comedy as the imitation of the "ridiculous" (the laughably absurd) rather than the wicked, potentially offering a theory of "comic catharsis."

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: How does Syd Field’s Screenplay relate to Aristotle?

It is a practical adaptation of Aristotelian story structure for cinema, emphasizing the three-act structure and the placement of reversal and recognition at moments of maximum tension.

Q: What was St. Augustine’s stance on the use of classical rhetoric in the church?

He believed that preachers should use the resources of classical rhetoric to move audiences toward the Gospel, arguing that the power of speech should not be reserved for pagan orators.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Which Roman poet's work is considered the great Aristotelian document of Latin literary theory?

Horace

What is the Latin title of Horace's influential verse letter on poetry written around 19 BC?

Ars Poetica

According to Horace's 'Ars Poetica', what are the two primary purposes of poetry?

To delight and to instruct.

What does the Latin phrase 'aut prodesse aut delectare' mean in the context of Horace's literary theory?

Either to benefit or to delight.

Which famous phrase from Horace's 'Ars Poetica' translates to 'as is painting, so is poetry'?

Ut pictura poesis

In Horace's literary discipline, what does 'the file' (lima) represent?

The discipline of revision.

Key Concepts (2/2)

During which century was the Greek text of Aristotle's 'Poetics' first printed?

The sixteenth century (1508).

Which Renaissance scholar is associated with the 'three unities' that became a standard for judging drama?

Castelvetro

Identify the three dramatic unities derived by Renaissance critics from Aristotle's work.

Time, place, and action.

Which group of seventeenth-century neoclassical dramatists wrote under the strict discipline of the three unities?

French dramatists (e.g., Corneille, Racine, Molière).

In his prefaces, how did the playwright Corneille interact with Aristotelian criticism?

He defended his departures from the unities with ingenuity.

Which French tragedian achieved a 'concentrated intensity' by strictly observing the unities of action and time?

Racine
