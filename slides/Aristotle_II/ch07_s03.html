Study Slides

Metaphor and Liveliness (Energeia)

Proportional Metaphors

These are the most effective, as they create a "math-like" relationship between two things (e.g., "the spring has been taken out of the year" to describe fallen youth).

Actuality (Energeia)

The highest achievement of style is making the audience "see" things in progress. This involves giving life to inanimate objects and representing things in a state of activity.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the Greek term for 'style' in Aristotle's rhetoric?

$\lambda \epsilon \xi \iota \varsigma$ (lexis)

What is the Greek term for the 'arrangement' of a speech's parts?

$\tau \alpha \xi \iota \varsigma$ (taxis)

According to Aristotle, style must always be in service of _____.

Argument (or substance).

Why does Aristotle consider style a 'practical necessity' despite preferring logic?

Due to the defects and character of the audience.

Which three factors constitute the management of the voice in delivery?

Volume, pitch, and rhythm.

According to Aristotle, the way something is said primarily affects its _____.

Intelligibility.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which Athenian orator is cited as the first to give oratorical prose a 'poetical colour'?

Gorgias.

What are the two fundamental requirements for 'good' style?

Clarity and appropriateness.

How is clarity achieved in oratorical prose?

By using words that are current and ordinary.

What is the effect of using 'strange' or 'out of the way' language in prose?

It makes the language appear more stately and strikes the hearer.

According to the rule of 'naturalness', a writer must disguise their _____.

Art (or artificiality).

Why is artificiality in speech detrimental to persuasion?

Hearers become prejudiced and think the speaker has a design against them.
