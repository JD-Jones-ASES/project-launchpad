Sir Philip Sidney (1554–1586)

The "Golden World"

Sidney argued that while historians record what happened and philosophers provide abstract precepts, poets create a "golden world" that embodies universal truths in engaging stories.

The Superiority of Poetry

He claimed poetry is the best medium for teaching and moving humans toward "the good" because it engages the mind, heart, and imagination simultaneously.

Augustine of Hippo (354–430)

Integration

In De Doctrina Christiana, he argued that Christian preachers should utilize classical rhetorical tools (ethos, pathos, and logos) to proclaim the Gospel.

Legacy

This tradition links Aristotle and Cicero to medieval sermon literature and modern Christian oratory.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Ekstasis (Transport)

The sense of being lifted out of ordinary experience, which Longinus identifies as the primary effect of truly great literature.

Hypsos (The Sublime)

Literally "height" or "elevation"; the quality of language that goes beyond mere competence to achieve overwhelming greatness.

Middle Commentary on the Poetics

The c. 1175 work by Averroes that reinterpreted Greek dramatic concepts for the Arabic-speaking world.

The Three Unities

A prescriptive dramatic theory requiring unity of action, time, and place; these rules dominated European drama from the 16th through the 18th century.

Poetica d'Aristotele Vulgarizzata et Sposta

The influential and controversial 1570 commentary by Lodovico Castelvetro.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

De Doctrina Christiana (On Christian Teaching)

Augustine’s work arguing for the use of classical rhetoric in Christian preaching.

The Defence of Poesy

Sir Philip Sidney’s philosophical defense of imaginative literature against moralistic and Puritan critiques.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is credited with integrating Aristotle's ethics and politics into a Christian theological framework?

Thomas Aquinas

What is the traditional name given to the author of the 1st-century treatise "On the Sublime"?

Longinus

What central question does Longinus explore in "On the Sublime"?

What makes literature truly great or immortal rather than merely competent.

Term: $hypsos$

Definition: Height or elevation in language that lifts the reader out of ordinary experience.

According to Longinus, the sublime produces a shock of recognition and a sense of transport known as _____.

$ekstasis$

How does the sublime differ from persuasive or pleasant work in Longinian theory?

The sublime overwhelms the reader through transport rather than simply convincing or pleasing them.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the two sources of the sublime that Longinus considers to be "gifts of nature"?

Greatness of thought and strong, inspired emotion.

Which three sources of the sublime can be cultivated according to Longinus?

Certain figures of speech, noble diction (metaphor), and dignified composition.

While Aristotle focuses on the emotional effect of tragedy, Longinus focuses on the immortality of a _____.

single line or passage

Who was the greatest medieval Arabic commentator on Aristotle, known as "The Commentator"?

Averroes (Ibn Rushd)

What cultural constraint did Arabic commentators face when translating Aristotle's "Poetics"?

Arabic culture had no tradition of theatrical performance.

How did Averroes reinterpret the Aristotelian concept of tragedy?

As a form of praise poetry.
