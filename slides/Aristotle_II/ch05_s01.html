Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Antistrophos

Literally "turning in response"; used to describe rhetoric as the counterpart or mirror image of dialectic.

Deliberative Rhetoric

Political oratory (*symbouleutikon*) used to advise an assembly for or against a future course of action based on expediency.

Enthymeme

A rhetorical syllogism drawn from probable premises; the core of rational proof in rhetoric.

Epideictic Rhetoric

Ceremonial oratory (*epideiktikon*) used to praise or blame a person, focusing on the noble or the base in the present.

Ethos

The speaker’s character, expertise, and perceived trustworthiness as established through their speech.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Forensic Rhetoric

Legal oratory (*dikanikon*) used to accuse or defend regarding past actions, focusing on justice or injustice.

Logos

The rational argument or proof provided by the words of the speech itself.

Noble

That which is desirable for its own sake and worthy of praise; the foundational concern of epideictic rhetoric.

Pathos

The emotional state produced in the audience by the orator to influence their judgment.

Sophists

Professional teachers in ancient Greece who taught persuasion for a fee, often criticized for prioritizing appearance over truth.
