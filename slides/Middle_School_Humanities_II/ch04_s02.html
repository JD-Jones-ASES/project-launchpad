Important Terms

Flying buttress

An arched stone support transferring the outward thrust of a heavy stone vault to an outer pier, allowing Gothic walls to be thinner, filled with large windows, and built to greater heights.

Gothic architecture

The dominant architectural style of High Middle Ages cathedrals, defined by the pointed arch, ribbed vault, and flying buttress, enabling extraordinary height and extensive stained-glass windows.

A World Building Toward Heaven: The High Middle Ages (1150–1350)

Monumental Architecture

The building of soaring cathedrals like Notre-Dame de Paris, showcasing unprecedented engineering feats like the **flying buttress**.

Intellectual Advancement

The establishment of universities where reason pursued divine truth, and the development of complex philosophical systems.

Economic Expansion

The growth of extensive trade networks.

Sudden End

This period of flourishing was abruptly curtailed in 1347 with the arrival of the Black Death.
