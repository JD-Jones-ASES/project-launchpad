Important Terms (1/2)

Simony

The buying or selling of church offices, appointments, or sacraments, a common form of corruption in the medieval Church.

Indulgence

A certificate issued by the Catholic Church that claimed to reduce the punishment in purgatory for sins already forgiven, often sold for money by the early sixteenth century.

Purgatory

In Catholic teaching, a state or place of purification after death for souls who are in God's grace but still need cleansing from sin before entering heaven.

Sola Fide

Latin for "by faith alone." Martin Luther's central doctrine that a person is justified (made right with God) solely through faith in Jesus Christ, not by good works or penances.

Diet of Worms

A formal assembly of the Holy Roman Empire held in 1521, where Martin Luther famously refused to recant his writings, declaring his conscience bound by Scripture.

Important Terms (2/2)

Sola Scriptura

Latin for "by Scripture alone." The Reformation doctrine that the Bible is the sole, supreme, and final authority for Christian faith and practice, above popes or church councils.

Sola Gratia

Latin for "by grace alone." The Reformation doctrine that salvation is entirely God's free and undeserved gift, not the result of human merit or effort.

Predestination

A theological doctrine, especially emphasized by John Calvin, that God from eternity has chosen who will be saved, based solely on His will, not on any foreseen human merit.
