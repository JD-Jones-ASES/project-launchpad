Key Concepts (1/2)

What social and economic system did William the Conqueror import to England after the Battle of Hastings?

He imported feudalism, an organization of society around the exchange of land for military service, from the continent.

Describe the basic structure of the feudal pyramid.

At the top was the king, followed by great lords (barons/earls), then knights, and at the bottom, the vast majority were villeins (serfs/peasants).

What was the theoretical basis of the king's power regarding land in the feudal system?

In theory, the king owned all the land in the kingdom, making him the ultimate landlord.

Define "vassal" in the context of the feudal system.

A vassal was a person who held land from a lord in exchange for loyalty and military service. A person could be both a lord to those below them and a vassal ...

What was the ceremony called that sealed the relationship between a lord and a vassal, and what did it involve?

The ceremony was called homage. The vassal knelt before his lord, placed his hands between the lord's hands, and swore an oath of fealty (faithfulness).
