Important Terms (2/3)

Constitutions of Oxford (1408)

English laws that effectively banned any English Bible translation without episcopal (bishop's) approval, a reaction to earlier reform movements like John Wycliffe's.

German New Testament (1522) / Complete German Bible (1534)

Martin Luther's groundbreaking translations of the Bible into German, based on original Greek and Hebrew texts. They were highly influential in standardizing the modern German language.

English New Testament (1525)

William Tyndale's English translation of the New Testament, smuggled into England, which profoundly influenced the English language and later versions, including the King James Bible.

King James Bible (1611)

An authorized English translation of the Christian Bible for the Church of England, which drew heavily upon William Tyndale's earlier work, incorporating much of his memorable phrasing.

Diet of Worms

An imperial assembly in 1521 where Martin Luther was summoned to recant his views. Upon his refusal, he was declared an outlaw by Emperor Charles V.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Why are primary sources particularly important for understanding the Protestant Reformation?

Because the Reformation was primarily an 'argument in words'—sermons, letters, pamphlets, and books—expressing reformers' beliefs about salvation and frustra...

What was the main purpose of Martin Luther's Ninety-Five Theses?

To challenge the theological basis and moral implications of the sale of indulgences within the scholarly tradition of academic debate.

In what year did Martin Luther publish the Ninety-Five Theses?

1517.

What language were Luther's Ninety-Five Theses originally written in, and how did they reach a wider audience?

They were written in Latin, but the printing press quickly ensured they reached a much wider audience beyond scholars.

According to Luther's Thesis 1, what did Jesus mean by 'Repent'?

He willed that the entire life of believers should be one of continuous repentance.

What was Luther's view on the Pope's power to remit penalties, as stated in Thesis 5?

The Pope neither desires nor is able to remit any penalties except those imposed by his own authority or church law.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which of Luther's Ninety-Five Theses directly attacked the common sales pitch for indulgences, specifically regarding souls leaving purgatory?

Thesis 27: 'They preach mere human folly who say that as soon as the money clinks in the chest, the soul flies out of purgatory.'

What did Luther claim in Thesis 36 was sufficient for a Christian to receive full remission of penalty and guilt?

True repentance, even without letters of indulgence.

What did Luther identify as 'the true treasure of the church' in Thesis 62?

The most holy Gospel of the grace and glory of God.

What political criticism did Luther make in Thesis 86 regarding the Pope's wealth?

He questioned why the Pope, whose wealth was immense, didn't build St. Peter's Basilica with his own money rather than that of poor believers.

Why did Luther write the 'Address to the Christian Nobility of the German Nation'?

He appealed to German princes to reform the Church, believing they had the right and responsibility since the church hierarchy refused.

What did Luther call the defensive strategies used by the Roman party to prevent reform?

The 'three walls.'
