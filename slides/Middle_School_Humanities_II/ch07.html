Important Terms (1/3)

simony

Buying or selling church offices; named for Simon Magus (Acts 8). Widely practiced despite condemnation — wealthy families bought positions for relatives.

indulgence

A certificate claiming to reduce punishment in purgatory for already-forgiven sins. Originally for penance; sold for money by Luther's time.

purgatory

Catholic doctrine of a state of post-mortem purification before heaven. Protestant Reformers denied its existence.

sola fide

'By faith alone.' Salvation received through faith in Christ, not earned by human effort.

sola scriptura

'By Scripture alone.' The Bible is the sole supreme authority — above popes, councils, and tradition.

Important Terms (2/3)

sola gratia

'By grace alone.' Salvation is entirely God's free gift, not earned by merit.

Diet of Worms (1521)

The Holy Roman Empire assembly before which Luther refused to recant; the Emperor declared him an outlaw.

predestination

Calvin's doctrine that God from eternity elected who would be saved, based solely on God's own will.

Act of Supremacy (1534)

Henry VIII declared Supreme Head of the Church of England; ended papal authority in England.

Council of Trent (1545–1563)

The Catholic response to the Reformation; reformed abuses and clarified doctrine.

Important Terms (3/3)

Jesuits

The Society of Jesus, founded by Ignatius of Loyola (1540); the elite intellectual and missionary force of the Counter-Reformation.

cuius regio, eius religio

'Whose realm, his religion.' The principle of the Peace of Augsburg (1555): each prince determined his territory's religion.

Peace of Augsburg (1555)

Treaty acknowledging permanent religious division in the Holy Roman Empire.
