Important Terms (1/3)

Norman

From 'Northman.' Descendants of Viking settlers in Normandy, France. By 1066, Christianized and French-speaking but still militarily formidable.

housecarl

Professional warrior in the household of an Anglo-Saxon king or lord. The backbone of Harold's army at Hastings; the institution disappeared after the Conquest.

Domesday Book

The 1086 survey of virtually every estate in England, commissioned by William. Named 'Doomsday' because its decisions were final and without appeal.

feudalism

The system of land (fief) granted in exchange for military service (fealty). Creates a hierarchy of lords and vassals from king to knight.

vassal

A person who holds land from a lord and owes military service and loyalty in return.

Important Terms (2/3)

fief

The land granted by a lord to a vassal in exchange for loyalty and military service.

Investiture Controversy

The dispute (c. 1076–1122) between popes and Holy Roman Emperors over the right to appoint bishops and abbots.

scholasticism

The medieval method of using philosophical reasoning (especially Aristotelian logic) to explore theology. Founded by Anselm; perfected by Aquinas.

Plantagenet

The royal dynasty founded by Henry II in 1154, ruling England until 1485.

common law

The body of English law built from judicial decisions, created by Henry II's system of royal judges on circuit.

Important Terms (3/3)

Magna Carta

'Great Charter,' agreed to by King John at Runnymede, June 15, 1215. Established the rule of law: even the king is subject to law.

chivalry

The code of medieval knighthood: loyalty, courage, protection of the weak, and Christian devotion.

Constitutions of Clarendon (1164)

Henry II's assertion that clergy accused of crimes should be tried in royal, not church, courts.
