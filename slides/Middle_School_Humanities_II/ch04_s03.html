Guild

Apprentice

The lowest rank in a guild, typically a boy placed with a master craftsman to learn a trade. Apprentices received board, lodging, and training, usually without wages, for several years.

Journeyman

Master

The highest rank in a guild, a craftsman who had produced an exceptional "masterpiece" and been admitted to full guild membership. Only masters could open their own shops and take on apprentices.
