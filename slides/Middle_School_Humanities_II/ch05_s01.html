Important Terms (1/3)

Chronicles

A historical record of events in chronological order, often detailed and narrative, as exemplified by Froissart's work.

Frame-story

A literary technique where an overarching narrative (the frame) sets the stage for a series of individual stories, as seen in Boccaccio's *Decameron*.

Hundred Years' War

A series of conflicts waged between the Kingdom of England and the Kingdom of France from 1337 to 1453.

Black Death

One of the most devastating pandemics in human history, peaking in Europe from 1347 to 1351, causing widespread death and social disruption.

Peasants' Revolt (1381)

A major uprising across large parts of England, primarily driven by economic and social grievances exacerbated by the Black Death.

Important Terms (2/3)

Chivalry

The medieval knightly system with its religious, moral, and social code, which was increasingly challenged during this period.

Middle English

The form of the English language spoken in England from the Norman Conquest (1066) until the late 15th century, the language in which Chaucer wrote.

Pilgrimage

A journey, often arduous, made to a sacred place as an act of religious devotion, a central theme in *The Canterbury Tales*.

Satire

The use of humor, irony, exaggeration, or ridicule to expose and criticize people's stupidity or vices, particularly in the context of contemporary politics and other topical issues, a technique us...

Longbowmen

English archers equipped with longbows, whose skill proved devastatingly effective against French cavalry and crossbowmen in battles like Crécy.

Important Terms (3/3)

Genoese crossbowmen

Mercenary soldiers from Genoa, hired by the French army, who were outmatched by English longbowmen at Crécy.
