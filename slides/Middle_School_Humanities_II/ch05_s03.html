Key Concepts (1/2)

What was Europe's estimated population before the Black Death struck in 1347?

Approximately 75 million people, which was the highest it had ever been.

How much did Europe's population decline by 1400 due to the Black Death?

It fell to somewhere between 45 and 50 million, representing a loss of about 30-40%.

How long did it take for Europe's population to recover to pre-plague levels?

Approximately 150 years, not until the sixteenth century, and even later in some regions.

Was the Black Death a single event or a recurring catastrophe?

It was a recurring catastrophe. After the first great wave (1347–1353), plague returned repeatedly throughout the late fourteenth and fifteenth centuries.

What fundamental economic principle explains the Black Death's impact on labor?

When something becomes scarce, it becomes more valuable. After the plague, the most scarce thing was people (labor).
