Important Terms (1/3)

Pilgrimage

A journey to a sacred place as an act of religious devotion. Before the Crusades, it was a personal spiritual journey; the Crusades transformed it into a more organized, often military, undertaking.

Arabic Numerals

The numerical system (0, 1, 2, 3...) that originated in India and was transmitted to Europe via Arab scholars. They revolutionized mathematics.

Algebra (al-jabr)

A branch of mathematics that uses symbols to represent unknown quantities, developed and advanced significantly in the Islamic world.

Ibn Sina (Avicenna, c. 980–1037)

A Persian polymath and physician whose medical encyclopedia, *The Canon of Medicine*, was a standard text in European universities for centuries.

Ibn Rushd (Averroes, 1126–1198)

An Arab polymath who wrote extensive commentaries on Aristotle, crucial for reintroducing Aristotle's philosophy to Western Europe.

Important Terms (3/3)

Chevalier

French word for "horseman" or "knight," from which the term "chivalry" is derived.

Peace of God / Truce of God

Eleventh-century movements by the Church to limit violence by knights, forbidding attacks on non-combatants (women, clergy, peasants) and restricting warfare on certain holy days.

Chivalry: The Code of the Christian Warrior

Origin of the Term

The word "chivalry" comes from the French *chevalier* (horseman, knight).

Defining Chivalry

It was a code of conduct for medieval knights, combining military skill with Christian piety, loyalty to one's lord, and courteous behavior, especially towards women and defeated foes.

Shaped by the Crusades

The shared experience of fighting for a holy cause far from home fostered a culture of valor, loyalty, and honor among knights.

Idealized Behavior

The relationship between Richard I and Saladin, who exchanged gifts during the Third Crusade, exemplifies the chivalric ideal of respecting even one's enemies.
