Important Terms (1/4)

Florins

British silver coins, each worth two shillings, used in the early 20th century. The children want "modern" ones to ensure they are accepted in shops.

Litter and stabling

**Litter** refers to the straw or bedding used in a horse's stall. **Stabling** refers to the act of housing horses in a stable or barn.

Topaz

A semi-precious gemstone, typically yellow or blue, owned by the children's mother.

Sapphire

A precious gemstone, usually blue, found in a ring given to Mother by Father.

Garnet

A semi-precious gemstone, usually deep red, mentioned as part of Mother's limited jewelry collection.

Important Terms (2/4)

Brooch

A decorative pin or clasp, often set with jewels, worn on clothing. Mother has a small one containing great-grandpapa's hair.

Tiara

A jewelled ornamental crown or headband worn by women on very formal occasions. Lady Chittenden had one, which Jane refers to as a "tarrer."

Receiver of stolen goods

A legal term for a person who knowingly accepts or possesses property that has been stolen. Cyril correctly identifies this as a serious crime, fearing their mother might unwittingly become one.

Bedlam

The popular nickname for Bethlem Royal Hospital, a famous historical psychiatric asylum in London, known for its chaotic and inhumane conditions. Cyril uses it to scare the others about being decla...

Maniacs

A derogatory term for people who are wildly mentally ill, often used to describe patients in asylums. Cyril exaggerates this to frighten his siblings.

Important Terms (3/4)

Asters

Daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer and autumn, often in shades of purple, pink, or white.

Zinnias

Brightly coloured garden flowers that bloom from summer into fall.

Bower

A pleasant, shady retreat or shelter, often made of leafy branches or climbing plants. The children transform their house into a "flowery-boweryness" with flowers.

Bonnet

A woman's hat, typically tied under the chin with ribbons. By 1902, the term could be used more loosely for any lady's hat.

Ring-stand

A small stand, often found on a dressing table, designed to hold rings.

Important Terms (4/4)

Pincushion

A small, stuffed cushion used for holding pins and needles, a common item on a lady's dressing table.
