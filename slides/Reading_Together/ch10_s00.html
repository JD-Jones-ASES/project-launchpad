Important Terms (1/3)

The Last of the Mohicans

A famous adventure novel by James Fenimore Cooper (1826) set during the French and Indian War. Cyril's reading of this book directly inspires his wish for Red Indians.

Psammead (Sand-fairy)

The ancient magical creature that grants wishes, often with unexpected and challenging results. In this section, it is irritable and confirms a wish has been granted, hinting at its dangerous nature.

Two-shilling pieces (florins)

British coins worth two shillings each. The children initially wanted 100 pounds in these coins.

Queen Victoria

The Queen who ruled Britain from 1837 to 1901. Her image appeared on British coins of the era.

Boot-tree

A foot-shaped wooden form inserted into boots or shoes to help them keep their shape. Anthea uses one as a tool to break the water-jug.

Important Terms (2/3)

Missionary-box

A small, locked collection box kept in homes for donating money to missionaries or charity. Breaking it open was considered a serious offense.

Seven-and-fourpence, Four shillings, Half-a-crown, Eight shillings, Coppers

Liberty box

A decorative box, likely from Liberty's, a famous and fashionable London department store, offered by Anthea as a bribe to Martha.

Trencher

A flat wooden board traditionally used as a plate for serving food.

Damask

A rich, patterned fabric. In the text, "deepening of the damask of Martha's complexion" is a figurative way to describe her face getting redder (from agitation or anger).
