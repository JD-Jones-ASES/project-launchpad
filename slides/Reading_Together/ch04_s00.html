Important Terms (1/2)

Indian ink

A deep black ink originally from China, used for drawing and writing.

Wagonette

A four-wheeled, horse-drawn carriage with bench seats facing each other, typically open at the back.

Nineveh

The ancient capital of the Assyrian Empire, located in modern-day Iraq, renowned as one of the greatest cities of the ancient world.

Medway

A river in Kent, England, flowing through towns like Maidstone and Rochester. Boating on it was a popular leisure activity.

Latin proses

Exercises where students were required to write passages or compositions in Latin, a standard part of education for English children during this historical period.

Important Terms (2/2)

Sennacherib

A historical king of ancient Assyria (reigned approximately 705-681 BC), known for his military campaigns and building projects, including the expansion of Nineveh.
