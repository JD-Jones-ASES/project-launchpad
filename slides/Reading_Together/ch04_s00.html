Chapter 4 - Wings

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Lift Generation

Wings are shaped as airfoils to efficiently generate lift, primarily through pressure differences (Bernoulli's Principle) and the deflection of air downwards (Newton's Third Law).

Airfoil Characteristics

The specific curvature (camber), leading edge, trailing edge, and chord line of an airfoil dictate its aerodynamic performance.

Angle of Attack (AoA)

The angle between the wing's chord line and the relative wind, which is a critical factor in determining the amount of lift and drag generated.

Aerodynamic Forces

The four fundamental forces acting on an aircraft in flight are Lift (upward), Weight (downward), Thrust (forward), and Drag (rearward).

Important Terms (1/4)

Airfoil

Any surface, such as a wing, propeller blade, or rudder, designed to obtain a useful reaction (e.g., lift) from air flowing over it.

Chord Line

An imaginary straight line connecting the leading edge and trailing edge of an airfoil.

Camber

The curvature of the airfoil's upper and lower surfaces.

Leading Edge

The front edge of an airfoil that first meets the airflow.

Trailing Edge

The rear edge of an airfoil where the airflow over the upper and lower surfaces rejoins.

Important Terms (2/4)

Angle of Attack (AoA)

The acute angle between the chord line of an airfoil and the direction of the relative wind.

Relative Wind

The direction of the airflow with respect to the aircraft. It is parallel to and opposite the flight path.

Lift

The aerodynamic force generated by an airfoil that opposes weight and acts perpendicular to the relative wind.

Drag

The aerodynamic force that opposes the forward motion of an aircraft and acts parallel to the relative wind.

Induced Drag

Drag created as a byproduct of lift, mainly due to the formation of wingtip vortices.

Important Terms (3/4)

Parasitic Drag

Drag caused by the resistance of air flowing over the aircraft's non-lifting parts, comprising form drag, skin friction, and interference drag.

Stall

The condition in aerodynamics where the angle of attack increases beyond a certain point such that the airflow separates from the airfoil's upper surface, resulting in a rapid decrease in lift.

Aspect Ratio

The ratio of the wingspan to the average chord of a wing. A high aspect ratio indicates a long, slender wing.

Wing Loading

The total weight of the aircraft divided by its wing area.

Ailerons

Movable control surfaces located on the outer trailing edge of the wings, used to control roll (banking).

Important Terms (4/4)

Flaps

Hinged surfaces on the trailing edge of the wing, designed to increase lift and drag, especially during takeoff and landing.

Spoilers

Devices on the upper surface of the wing that, when deployed, disrupt airflow, reduce lift, and increase drag. Used for descent and roll control.

Slats

Movable surfaces on the leading edge of the wing that, when extended, increase wing camber and delay airflow separation, improving lift at high angles of attack.

Winglets

Upward-curved extensions at the wingtips designed to reduce induced drag by mitigating wingtip vortices.
