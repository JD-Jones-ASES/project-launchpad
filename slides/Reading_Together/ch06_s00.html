Important Terms (1/2)

Siege

A military blockade and assault on a fortified place, typically a castle or city, with the aim of compelling a surrender. Often involves cutting off supplies.

Provisions

Supplies of food, drink, or equipment, especially for a journey or military expedition.

Rationing

The controlled distribution of scarce resources, goods, or services, typically implemented during times of war or scarcity.

Famine

Extreme scarcity of food, often leading to widespread starvation and death, typically caused by crop failure, war, or natural disaster.

Keep

The strongest and most heavily fortified part of a castle, often serving as the primary living quarters for the lord and his family, and a final refuge during an attack.

Important Terms (2/2)

Moat

A deep, wide ditch, typically filled with water, surrounding a castle, serving as a primary defensive barrier.

Garrison

The troops stationed in a fortress or town to defend it.

Scarcity

The state of being scarce or in short supply; a lack of resources.

Morale

The confidence, enthusiasm, and discipline of a person or group at a particular time. Crucial for survival during a siege or famine.

Social Hierarchy and Food Distribution

Lord and Lady

Typically, the highest-ranking individuals would receive the best (or least bad) portions for the longest time, reflecting their status. Their survival was often seen as critical for maintaining or...

Knights and Soldiers

The fighting men would also be prioritized to maintain the castle's defense, though their rations would still be severe.

Servants, Peasants, and Commoners

These individuals would usually receive the smallest and poorest quality rations, suffering the most directly from the scarcity. Children and the elderly among them would be particularly vulnerable.

Potential for Mutiny

Extreme inequality in food distribution could lead to unrest, rebellion, or attempts to seize food, further destabilizing the already fragile situation.

Survival Strategies and Ethical Dilemmas

Foraging Expeditions

Small, risky sorties outside the castle walls to search for food, often meeting enemy patrols.

Negotiation/Surrender

The lord might attempt to negotiate terms with the besieging army, possibly surrendering the castle to save the lives of its inhabitants.

Last Stand/Breakout Attempt

A desperate attempt by the **garrison** to fight their way through enemy lines, sacrificing the castle but perhaps saving some lives.

Ethical Questions

The crisis raises profound ethical questions: Who deserves to eat? Is it right to sacrifice some for the survival of others? How far should one go to survive?
