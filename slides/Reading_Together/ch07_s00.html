Important Terms (1/3)

Hank

A coil or loop of something, like yarn, string, or rope. (e.g., "hank of tarred twine")

Tarred twine

String coated with tar to make it waterproof, often used for outdoor and marine purposes.

Cobbler's wax

A sticky wax used by shoemakers to coat their thread, making stitches hold tightly in leather.

Suet roly-poly

A traditional English dessert made by spreading jam on pastry made with suet (hard beef fat), rolling it up, and steaming it.

Reconnoitre

To explore or survey an area, especially to gather information about an enemy's position. It's a military term.

Important Terms (2/3)

Battering-ram

A heavy beam of wood, often with a metal head, used to smash down castle doors and gates during a siege.

Intrepid

Very brave and fearless, especially in the face of danger.

Bodiam Castle

A real medieval castle in East Sussex, England, built in 1385, famous for its wide moat. Anthea references it.

Crossbows

Weapons that shoot short arrows (bolts) using a horizontal bow mounted on a wooden stock.

Longbows

Very large bows, about six feet tall, used by English archers. They required great strength to draw.

Important Terms (3/3)

Javelins

Light spears designed to be thrown at an enemy rather than used for thrusting.

Apace

Quickly, at a fast pace. (e.g., "a heap of stones grew apace")

Pennoned

Decorated with pennons—small triangular flags attached to the tips of knights' lances.

No quarter

Mercy shown to a defeated enemy, especially by sparing their lives. To give "no quarter" means no one will be spared.

Beleaguered

Surrounded by enemy forces; under siege. (e.g., "the beleaguered fortress")

The Invisible Dinner and Cyril's Discovery

• The children are in the gloomy banqueting-hall, hungry, with the dinner rendered invisible and unfeelable by the Psammead's magic.

• Cyril finds forgotten biscuits in his pocket, which are real, though crumbled and tasting odd from their contact with tarred twine, fir-cones, and cobbler's wax.

• He divides them fairly, prompting Robert to question why these biscuits are real but the main dinner isn't.

The "Ownership" Rule of Magic

• Cyril theorizes that the biscuits are real because "WE had them," suggesting a personal possession makes them tangible.

• Jane adds that food in one's pockets is real, while Robert suggests food in one's mouth would also be real.

• Cyril experiments by "biting" the air above the table, and a real piece of bread appears in his mouth. He discovers that even if the rest vanishes, he knows he has it.

• All the children follow suit, magically eating slices of mutton and, later, plain suet roly-poly directly from the air above the table.

• Martha is aghast at the mess left on the plates, having no idea of the magical feast.

Preparing for the Siege

• After dinner, renewed with courage, Robert insists on reconnoitring from a castle tower.

• From the tower, they see the besieging camp around the moat: tents, men sharpening weapons, re-stringing bows, and polishing shields.

• They spot a battering-ram being dragged towards the castle, causing Cyril to feel pale with fear.

• The children discuss the castle's defenses (moat, drawbridge) and the absence of soldiers, leading Cyril to create a dramatic scenario of "intrepid survivors" (themselves).

• Anthea recalls historical defense tactics, like pouring boiling lead, mentioning specific features of Bodiam Castle.

• Jane expresses her fear, questioning if it's "only a game."

Arming and Provisioning the Garrison

• They find large, impractical medieval weapons (swords, lances, crossbows, longbows) that are too heavy for them.

• They settle on daggers (to be thrown or dropped) and stones as viable weapons.

• Heaps of stones and daggers are gathered in the gate-tower room.

• Anthea has a clever idea: asking Martha for biscuits for tea and specifically requesting they be put in their pockets. This bypasses the invisibility charm, as the "air" turns into real biscuits ...

• They also prepare pots of cold water to pour on attackers, substituting for the unavailable hot lead.

• The afternoon passes quickly, with Robert being the only one who consistently feels the danger is real.

The Attack Begins (1/2)

• As tea-time approaches, they eat biscuits (with water from the well) and save some reserves.

• A sudden, loud trumpet blast signals the attack, confirming Robert's fears.

• They rush to the windows, seeing the besiegers moving. Robert bravely (and cheekily) puts his tongue out at an enemy figure.

• Jane is still very scared, while Cyril's pallor turns to redness as he tries to be brave.

The Attack Begins (2/2)

• Robert defiantly shouts "No!" to the surrender demand.

• He urges the others to cheer and rattle daggers to show courage, despite their high, weak cheers.

• Another shout from the camp confirms the attack has begun.

• The room grows dark, and Jane notes the "dreadfully thin" moat, with sunset approaching.
