Chapter 3: Being Wanted - Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Need for Belonging

A fundamental human psychological need to feel connected, accepted, and integrated into social groups or relationships, crucial for mental and emotional health.

Social Acceptance

The act of being recognized and included by others, leading to feelings of security, validation, and shared identity within a community or relationship.

Validation

The act of affirming someone's feelings, thoughts, or existence as legitimate and understandable, which contributes significantly to an individual's self-worth and sense of being valued.

Self-Worth

An internal sense of one's own value and capabilities, independent of external opinions or achievements, forming the bedrock of healthy self-esteem.

Reciprocity in Relationships

The mutual exchange of feelings, support, and actions in a relationship, where both parties feel valued and contribute to each other's sense of being wanted.

Key Concepts (2/2)

External vs. Internal Validation

The distinction between seeking approval and affirmation from others (external) versus deriving a sense of value and acceptance from within oneself (internal).

Important Terms (1/2)

Affiliation

The tendency to associate with others and form social bonds.

Attachment Theory

A psychological model explaining the long-term interpersonal relationships between humans, emphasizing the need for secure connections.

Social Inclusion

The process of ensuring that individuals and groups participate fully in society and have the same rights and opportunities.

Loneliness

A distressing feeling of being alone or separate from others, often stemming from a lack of social connection or a mismatch between desired and actual social contact.

Rejection Sensitivity

The tendency to anxiously expect, readily perceive, and intensely react to rejection, even in ambiguous social situations.

Important Terms (2/2)

Prosocial Behavior

Actions intended to benefit others, often motivated by a desire for social approval, belonging, or empathy.

Self-Esteem

An individual's overall subjective evaluation of their own worth.
