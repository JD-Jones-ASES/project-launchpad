Important Terms (1/2)

Hash

Leftover meat chopped up small and cooked again with potatoes or vegetables. Historically considered a plain or boring meal.

Petticoat

An undergarment worn under a dress or skirt. Wearing only a petticoat in public would be considered highly improper or akin to going out in one's underwear.

Sundial

A garden instrument that tells time by the shadow cast by the sun on a marked plate. A common feature in traditional gardens.

Darned

Mended a hole in fabric by weaving thread back and forth with a needle. A traditional method of repairing clothes, necessary before inexpensive clothing was widely available.

Shilling

A former British coin worth twelve old pennies. Twenty shillings made one pound.

Important Terms (2/2)

Vulgar fractions

An old-fashioned term for ordinary fractions like 3/4 or 1/2. "Vulgar" in this context means "common" or "ordinary," not rude.

Army and Navy Stores

A famous department store in London that historically sold a wide variety of goods, from toys to furniture, and was known for its thick mail-order catalogues.

Psammead (Sand-fairy)

The magical creature discovered by the children, which grants them one wish each day that lasts until sunset. (Introduced in previous chapters, but central to the plot here).
