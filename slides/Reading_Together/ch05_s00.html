Important Terms (1/2)

Blind-cord

The string or cord used to raise or lower a window blind. (Relevant when the maid pauses with her hand on it.)

Belfry

The part of a church tower where the bells are hung. (Where the owls and starlings are startled, and where the children are found.)

Gamekeeper

A person employed to look after wildlife on a country estate and to watch for poachers. (The Cook's Cousin, who leads the rescue party and is armed.)

Desperadoes

Dangerous criminals or outlaws. (The term the gamekeeper uses to describe what he expects to find at the top of the tower.)

Parapet

A low wall along the edge of a roof or tower, meant to stop people from falling off. (Where the children are standing when the door is opened.)

Important Terms (2/2)

Entreaty

An earnest, pleading request. (The children's desperate pleas to be taken down.)

Allay

To calm or reduce someone's worry or fear. (Robert tries to allay the Vicar's wife's anxiety.)

Flags

Large, flat stones used for paving floors, especially in old churches. (The children step out onto these in the church-porch.)

Vicarage

The house where the Vicar (a clergyman in charge of a parish) lives. (Where the maid and other servants are, and where the children are taken after rescue.)

Bell-ringers' loft

The room in a church tower where people stand to pull the ropes that ring the bells above. (Part of the tower the rescue party climbs through.)

Trapped in the Tower

Realization and Panic

After their magical flight, the children find themselves stranded at the very top of an English church tower. The magic has worn off, leaving them in a dangerous and unfamiliar situation.

A Desperate Plan for Rescue

Cyril's Strategy

Cyril devises a plan to yell for help, instructing Robert to make a "railway engine" yell and himself to do a "coo-ee" like his father's. The girls are told to yell as they please.

First Yells

Their combined "fourfold yell" breaks the silence of the evening, immediately attracting attention from the Vicarage.

Adult Misinterpretations and Fear

The Maid's Reaction

A maid at the Vicarage, startled by the first yells, flees, faints, and then reports seeing a ghost. This shows the immediate, superstitious fear evoked by the unfamiliar sounds.

Vicar's Wife's Panic

The Vicar's wife is terrified for her husband and reinforces the idea of a "dangerous lunatic," urging Andrew to "catch it."

Andrew's Reluctance

Andrew is hesitant and cautious, suspecting a "trap" and expressing reluctance to go alone. His actions are driven by self-preservation, feigning bravery later.

The Rescue Mission

Confirmation of Location

Andrew shouts, and the children confirm their location: "CHURCH! TOWER! TOP!" and that they "CAN'T! DOOR LOCKED!"

Assembling the Rescue Party

The Vicar, Andrew, and the Cook's Cousin (a gamekeeper armed with a gun) form the rescue party. The Vicar's wife pleads for caution.

The Confrontation at the Door

Keeper's Caution

The gamekeeper kicks the door and shouts. Cyril, hoarse from yelling, replies. The keeper interrogates them, asking how many, if they are armed, and threatening to use his gun if they try "tricks."

Children's Compliance

The children, desperate, promise to come down quietly.

Vicar's Observation

The Vicar notes a "female voice," starting to question the "dangerous lunatic" theory.

Opening the Door

The keeper, still cautious, makes them move to the other side of the tower before unbolting the door, which is stiff from disuse. He then throws it open, expecting criminals.

The Reveal and Descent (1/2)

A "Pack of Kiddies"

The gamekeeper is astonished to find only children, lowering his gun in surprise.

The Vicar's Interrogation

The Vicar severely questions them, demanding an explanation.

Children's Plea

The children, particularly Jane, are more concerned with getting down than explaining, making urgent "entreaties."

Cyril's Ongoing Syphon Struggle

Cyril continues to struggle with the syphon, trying to keep it hidden even during the descent, nearly falling.

The Descent

The descent is difficult in the dark. The gamekeeper helps, but Cyril must remain "independent" because of the syphon.

The Reveal and Descent (2/2)

Arrival at the Bottom

They finally reach the church-porch. The gamekeeper grabs Cyril and Robert, still wary, believing they might try to run.

In the Vicarage Study

Vicar's Wife's Relief and Scolding

The Vicar's wife rushes in, relieved to find her husband safe but immediately scolds the children for "frightening everybody out of their wits."

Children's Fabricated Story

Robert, trying to "allay" anxiety and get home, invents a story: they went up, fell asleep, and woke to find the door locked. Jane meekly agrees they are "ashamed."

Unanswered Questions

The Vicar is still puzzled about who locked the door.

Desire to Go Home

Robert and Anthea express their lateness and concern for Martha, hoping for a carriage.

Cyril's Secret

Cyril remains hunched over, protecting the syphon, while the adults question them. The true magical explanation for their presence remains unspoken.
