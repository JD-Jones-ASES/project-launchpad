Important Terms (1/2)

Flightless

(Adj.) Lacking the ability to fly; typically refers to birds or insects that have evolved to live without using wings for flight.

Vestigial Structures

(Noun) An anatomical feature or organ that was once useful in an organism's ancestor but has since lost its original or all function over evolutionary time. Wings might become vestigial in flightle...

Aerodynamics

(Noun) The branch of mechanics that deals with the motion of air and other gaseous fluids, and with the forces acting on bodies in motion relative to such fluids.

Lift

(Noun) The component of the aerodynamic force that is perpendicular to the direction of motion relative to the fluid. Essential for flight.

Drag

(Noun) The resistance force exerted on an object moving through a fluid.

Important Terms (2/2)

Adaptation

(Noun) A change or adjustment in a species over time that makes it better suited to its environment.
