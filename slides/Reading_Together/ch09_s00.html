Important Terms (1/3)

Tin baths

Large metal tubs used for bathing before modern indoor plumbing became common. Water would be heated and carried to the tub.

Megatherium

An extinct genus of giant ground sloths from thousands of years ago, which the Psammead fondly remembers.

Wash-hand basin

A bowl or sink, often kept in a bedroom, used for washing hands and face before modern bathrooms were widespread.

Waterbury watch

A type of cheap, mass-produced pocket watch made by the Waterbury Clock Company, common and affordable during the era.

Flannels

A suit or trousers made of flannel, a soft woven fabric, often worn by young gentlemen for casual outings during the Edwardian era.

Important Terms (2/3)

Straw hat

A hat woven from straw, commonly worn by men in summer during the Edwardian era.

Languid

Describing a slow, relaxed, and often lazy manner, used here to characterize the grown-up Lamb's voice and demeanor.

Club (Gentlemen's Club)

A private social establishment in London where members (typically wealthy men) could eat, drink, read, and socialize.

Baptismal names

The formal names given to a person at their christening or baptism, often multiple names.

Stand Sam

An old British expression meaning to pay for everyone's food or drinks.

Important Terms (3/3)

Blow-out

British slang for a big, satisfying, and often indulgent meal.

Pall

A dark, heavy cloth spread over a coffin or tomb; used metaphorically here to describe a feeling of gloom or despair falling over the children.
