Quadratic Equations and Functions

Study Slides

Solving by Factoring

Procedure

Set the equation to zero, factor the quadratic expression, and solve each linear factor for $x$.

Applications

Best used when the quadratic is easily factorable into integers or simple fractions.

Example Formats

Solving Using Square Roots

Procedure

Isolate the squared term and take the square root of both sides.

Important Note

When taking the square root of a constant, consider both the positive and negative roots ($\pm\sqrt{k}$).

Real Solutions

If $k < 0$, there is "no real solution."

The Quadratic Formula

Procedure

Identify constants $a$, $b$, and $c$, substitute them into the formula, and simplify the resulting radical and fraction.

Efficiency

Used when factoring is difficult or when exact irrational solutions (containing radicals) or decimal approximations are required.

The Discriminant

Key Features of a Parabola

Direction of Opening

If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upward (has a minimum). If $a < 0$, it opens downward (has a maximum).

Axis of Symmetry

The vertical line that divides the parabola into two congruent halves. Calculated as $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$.

Vertex

The highest or lowest point on the graph. In vertex form, it is $(h, k)$. In standard form, the $x$-coordinate is $-\frac{b}{2a}$, and the $y$-coordinate is $f(-\frac{b}{2a})$.

Intercepts

Domain and Range

Applied Problems and Optimization

Projectile Motion

Modeled by $h = -16t^2 + v_0t + s_0$, where $h$ is height, $t$ is time, $v_0$ is initial velocity, and $s_0$ is initial height.

Area Optimization

Finding the dimensions of a rectangle that maximize area given a fixed amount of fencing.

Business Models

Profit ($P$) and Revenue ($R$) functions are often quadratic. Break-even points occur when $P = 0$. Maximum profit or revenue is found at the vertex.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Axis of Symmetry

A vertical line, $x = -b/2a$, that passes through the vertex of a parabola.

Discriminant

The value $b^2 - 4ac$ used to determine the number and type of solutions to a quadratic equation.

Hypotenuse

The longest side of a right triangle, opposite the right angle; often the result of a square root calculation in geometric quadratic problems.

Irrational Solution

A solution that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction; usually involves a radical that cannot be simplified to an integer.

Maximum/Minimum Value

The $y$-coordinate of the vertex of a quadratic function, representing the highest or lowest point of the parabola.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Parabola

The U-shaped graph of a quadratic function.

Quadratic Formula

The formula used to find the solutions of any quadratic equation in standard form.

Standard Form (Equation)

An equation written as $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$.

Vertex

The point $(h, k)$ where a parabola changes direction.

Vertex Form

A quadratic function written as $f(x) = a(x - h)^2 + k$, where $(h, k)$ is the vertex.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Zero Product Property

A property stating that if $ab = 0$, then $a = 0$ or $b = 0$; the basis for solving by factoring.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the standard form of a quadratic equation?

$ax^2 + bx + c = 0$

According to the Zero Product Property, if the product of two factors is zero, what must be true of at least one factor?

At least one factor must equal zero.

To solve $x^2 + 6x + 8 = 0$ by factoring, which two numbers must multiply to 8 and add to 6?

2 and 4

What is the first step in solving $x^2 + 5x = -6$ by factoring?

Add 6 to both sides to set the equation to zero.

Solve the equation $x^2 - 10x + 25 = 0$ by factoring.

$x = 5$

How is the equation $3x^2 - 12x = 0$ solved using factoring?

Factor out the greatest common factor, $3x$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the context of rectangle area problems, if the length is 5 more than the width ($w$), what is the algebraic expression for area?

$w(w + 5)$

What algebraic expression represents the product of two consecutive positive integers $n$ and $n + 1$?

$n(n + 1)$

In the projectile motion equation $h = -16t^2 + 80t$, what does $h = 0$ represent?

The object is at ground level.

A right triangle has legs $x$ and $x + 7$ with a hypotenuse of 13. What equation represents this relationship?

$x^2 + (x + 7)^2 = 13^2$

What algebraic expression represents the product of two consecutive positive even integers $n$ and $n + 2$?

$n(n + 2)$

If $x^2 = k$ and $k > 0$, what are the two real solutions for $x$?

$x = \pm \sqrt{k}$
