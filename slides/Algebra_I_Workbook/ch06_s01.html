Laws of Exponents

Rule Name. Algebraic Definition

Description

Product of Powers. $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$

When multiplying powers with the same base, add the exponents.

Power of a Power. $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$

When raising a power to a power, multiply the exponents.

Power of a Product. $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$

When raising a product to a power, apply the exponent to each factor.

Zero Exponent. $a^0 = 1$ ($a eq 0$)

Any non-zero base raised to the power of zero is 1.

Negative Exponent. $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$

A negative exponent indicates the reciprocal of the base raised to the positive power.

Quotient of Powers. $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$

When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponent of the denominator from the numerator.

Polynomial Basics

Standard Form

Writing a polynomial so that the terms are in descending order by degree (highest exponent to lowest).

Classification by Degree

The degree is the highest exponent (or sum of exponents) in any single term (e.g., $3x^2$ is degree 2/quadratic; $4n^3$ is degree 3/cubic).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Binomial

A polynomial containing exactly two terms (e.g., $2x + 7$).

Degree of a Polynomial

The highest degree of any term in the polynomial, which determines its classification (e.g., quadratic, cubic).

Difference of Squares

A special product pattern where $(a+b)(a-b)$ results in $a^2 - b^2$.

Monomial

A polynomial consisting of only one term (e.g., $4n^3$).

Negative Exponent

A rule stating that $a^{-n}$ is the same as $1/a^n$. This is often used to represent very small decimal values.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Perfect Square Trinomial

The result of squaring a binomial, following the pattern $a^2 + 2ab + b^2$ or $a^2 - 2ab + b^2$.

Polynomial

An algebraic expression consisting of one or more terms added or subtracted.

Scientific Notation

A method of writing numbers as the product of a decimal between 1 and 10 and a power of 10.

Standard Form (Polynomial)

A format where terms are organized from the highest degree to the lowest degree.

Trinomial

A polynomial containing exactly three terms (e.g., $3x^2 - 5x + 7$).

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Zero Exponent Rule

The principle that any non-zero number raised to the zero power equals 1.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to the Product of Powers property, how is the expression $x^a \cdot x^b$ simplified?

$x^{a+b}$

Simplify the expression $2^3 \cdot 2^4$ using exponent rules.

$2^7$

What is the result of the Power of a Power property applied to $(x^a)^b$?

$x^{ab}$

Simplify the expression $(a^2)^5$.

$a^{10}$

The Power of a Product property states that $(xy)^n$ is equivalent to _____.

$x^n y^n$

Simplify the expression $(-2n)^4$.

$16n^4$

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the formula for the volume of a cube with side length $s$?

$V = s^3$

Simplify the volume expression for a cube with side length $2x^2$.

$8x^6$

What is the area of a square tile with side length $3a^2$?

$9a^4$

Simplify the expression for the volume of a rectangular box with dimensions $2x$, $3x$, and $x^2$.

$6x^4$

According to the Zero Exponent property, any non-zero number $a$ raised to the power of $0$ ($a^0$) equals _____.

$1$

What is the value of the expression $-6^0$?

$-1$

Quiz Question 1

Simplify the following expression and write the answer using positive exponents: $(3x^3 y^{-1})^2 \cdot (2x^{-2} y^2)^3$.

A. $72y^4$

B. $72x^{12}y^8$

C. $17y^4$

D. $\frac{72}{y^4}$

Hint: Apply the power rule to both sets of parentheses before using the product rule to combine the like bases.

Quiz Question 2

A cube has a side length of $5y$. A second cube has a side length of $2y$. What is the ratio of the volume of the first cube to the volume of the second cube?

A. $125:8$

B. $5:2$

C. $25:4$

D. $125y:8y$

Hint: Recall that the volume of a cube is determined by the formula $V = s^3$.

Quiz Question 3

A garden border has outer dimensions of $(3x + 10)$ meters by $(2x + 6)$ meters. The inner garden has dimensions of $3x$ meters by $2x$ meters. Find the polynomial expression that represents the ar...

A. $38x + 60$

B. $6x^2 + 38x + 60$

C. $10x + 32$

D. $38x - 60$

Hint: Subtract the area of the inner rectangle from the area of the outer rectangle.

Quiz Question 4

Multiply and simplify: $(2n - 3)(n^2 + 2n - 1)$. Which of the following is the resulting polynomial in standard form?

A. $2n^3 + n^2 - 8n + 3$

B. $2n^3 + 7n^2 - 8n + 3$

C. $2n^3 + n^2 + 8n + 3$

D. $2n^3 - 3n^2 + 3$

Hint: Ensure that every term in the first factor is multiplied by every term in the second factor.

Quiz Question 5

Simplify the following expression using positive exponents: $\frac{8 \times 10^{-5}}{4 \times 10^{-9}}$.

A. $2 \times 10^4$

B. $2 \times 10^{-14}$

C. $2 \times 10^{-4}$

D. $0.5 \times 10^4$

Hint: When dividing powers with the same base, subtract the exponent of the denominator from the exponent of the numerator.

Quiz Question 6

A computer's hard drive stores $2^{12}$ files. If each file occupies $2^{-3}$ megabytes, what is the total storage used in megabytes?

A. $512$

B. $32,768$

C. $16$

D. $5.12 \times 10^{-1}$

Hint: Multiply the number of files by the size per file using the product rule for exponents.
