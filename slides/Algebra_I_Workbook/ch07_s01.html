Chapter 7, Section 1

Algebraic Factoring and Applications

Study Slides

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

Monomials

Find the largest numerical factor and the highest power of each variable common to all terms.

Polynomials

Divide each term by the GCF and write the result as a product (e.g., $10a^3 - 25a = 5a(2a^2 - 5)$).

Negative Leading Coefficients

When the leading coefficient is negative, it is often helpful to factor out a negative GCF (e.g., $-6x^2 + 9x - 3 = -3(2x^2 - 3x + 1)$).

Factoring by Grouping

• Group the first two terms and the last two terms.

• Factor out the GCF from each group.

• If a common binomial factor remains, factor it out (e.g., $3x + 3y + ax + ay = 3(x+y) + a(x+y) = (3+a)(x+y)$).

Factoring Trinomials ($ax^2 + bx + c$)

Case 1 ($a = 1$)

Find two integers that multiply to $c$ and add to $b$.

Case 2 ($a > 1$)

Use trial and error or grouping to find factors that produce the correct middle term.

GCF First

Always check for a GCF before attempting trinomial factoring (e.g., $6x^2 + 18x + 12 = 6(x^2 + 3x + 2) = 6(x+1)(x+2)$).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Binomial

A polynomial consisting of exactly two terms (e.g., $x^2 - 49$).

Difference of Squares

A squared term subtracted from another squared term, factorable into the sum and difference of the square roots.

Factor

To write an expression as a product of other expressions.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The highest degree monomial that divides every term of a polynomial without a remainder.

Grouping

A method of factoring four-term polynomials by extracting GCFs from pairs of terms.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Monomial

A polynomial consisting of a single term (e.g., $12x^3$).

Perfect Square Trinomial

A trinomial that is the result of squaring a binomial (e.g., $(x+5)^2$).

Prime Polynomial

An expression that cannot be factored into lower-degree polynomials with integer coefficients.

Trinomial

A polynomial consisting of exactly three terms (e.g., $ax^2 + bx + c$).

Zero Product Property

A rule stating that if the product of two or more factors is zero, at least one of the factors must be zero.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of the monomials $12x^3$ and $8x^2$?

$4x^2$

What is the GCF of the monomials $15a^2b^3$ and $20ab^4$?

$5ab^3$

Find the GCF of the monomials $18m^4$ and $24m^2$.

$6m^2$

Find the GCF of the monomials $14p^3q^2$ and $21p^2q^3$.

$7p^2q^2$

Factor the expression $6x + 15$ by extracting the GCF.

$3(2x + 5)$

Factor the expression $8n^2 - 12n$ by extracting the GCF.

$4n(2n - 3)$

Key Concepts (2/2)

Factor the expression $16k^4 - 24k^2$ by extracting the GCF.

$8k^2(2k^2 - 3)$

Factor the trinomial $15x^2 + 10x - 5$ by extracting the GCF.

$5(3x^2 + 2x - 1)$

Factor the trinomial $12a^3 - 8a^2 + 4a$ by extracting the GCF.

$4a(3a^2 - 2a + 1)$

Factor the trinomial $-6x^2 + 9x - 3$ by extracting a negative GCF.

$-3(2x^2 - 3x + 1)$

Factor the polynomial $3x + 3y + ax + ay$ using the grouping method.

$(3 + a)(x + y)$

Factor $x^2 + 3x + 2x + 6$ by grouping.

$(x + 3)(x + 2)$

Quiz Question 1

What is the greatest common factor (GCF) of the monomials $24a^4b^3$ and $36a^2b^5$?

A. $12a^2b^3$

B. $6a^2b^3$

C. $12a^4b^5$

D. $72a^2b^3$

Hint: Focus on finding the largest integer that divides both coefficients and the lowest exponent for each variable present in both terms.

Quiz Question 2

Factor the polynomial completely: $-15x^3y + 10x^2y^2 - 5xy$.

A. $-5xy(3x^2 - 2xy + 1)$

B. $-5xy(3x^2 - 2xy)$

C. $5xy(-3x^2 + 2xy - 1)$

D. $-5x(3x^2y - 2xy^2 + y)$

Hint: When the leading term is negative, it is conventional to factor out a negative GCF; don't forget the placeholder for the final term.

Quiz Question 3

Factor the expression by grouping: $x^3 - 2x^2 - 9x + 18$.

A. $(x - 2)(x - 3)(x + 3)$

B. $(x^2 - 9)(x - 2)$

C. $(x + 2)(x - 3)(x + 3)$

D. $(x - 2)(x^2 + 9)$

Hint: Group the terms into two pairs and look for a difference of squares after the initial grouping step.

Quiz Question 4

Factor the trinomial completely: $x^2 - 5x - 84$.

A. $(x - 12)(x + 7)$

B. $(x + 12)(x - 7)$

C. $(x - 21)(x + 4)$

D. $(x - 14)(x + 6)$

Hint: Search for two numbers that multiply to $-84$ and have a sum of $-5$.

Quiz Question 5

Factor the trinomial where $a > 1$: $6x^2 - 7x - 5$.

A. $(3x - 5)(2x + 1)$

B. $(3x + 5)(2x - 1)$

C. $(6x - 5)(x + 1)$

D. $(2x - 5)(3x + 1)$

Hint: Consider the factors of the product of the first and last coefficients ($-30$) that sum to the middle coefficient.

Quiz Question 6

Which of the following represents the completely factored form of $16x^4 - 81$?

A. $(4x^2 + 9)(2x + 3)(2x - 3)$

B. $(4x^2 + 9)(4x^2 - 9)$

C. $(2x + 3)^2(2x - 3)^2$

D. $(4x^2 + 9)^2$

Hint: Apply the difference of squares pattern twice.
