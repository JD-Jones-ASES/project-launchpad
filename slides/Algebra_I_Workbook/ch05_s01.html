Methods for Solving Systems

Graphing

Writing equations in slope-intercept form ($y = mx + b$) to identify the point of intersection $(x, y)$.

Substitution

Solving one equation for a single variable and substituting that expression into the second equation. This is often efficient when one variable has a coefficient of 1 or -1.

Elimination (Addition/Subtraction)

Adding or subtracting equations to eliminate one variable. This often requires multiplying one or both equations by a constant to create opposite coefficients for a variable.

Systems of Linear Inequalities

Solution Region

The set of all points $(x, y)$ that satisfy all inequalities in the system.

Boundary Lines

Lines that define the edges of the solution region. They are solid for "greater than or equal to" ($\ge$) or "less than or equal to" ($\le$) and dashed for "greater than" ($>$) or "less tha...

Bounded vs. Unbounded

A region is bounded if it is enclosed on all sides (often forming a polygon); it is unbounded if it extends infinitely in at least one direction.

Vertices

The corner points of a bounded solution region, found where boundary lines intersect.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Bounded Region

A solution region of a system of inequalities that is entirely enclosed by boundary lines, forming a finite shape.

Break-Even Point

The point at which total revenue ($R$) equals total cost ($C$), resulting in zero profit or loss.

Consistent System

A system of equations that has at least one solution.

Elimination Method

A technique for solving systems by adding or subtracting equations to remove one variable, often involving multiplying equations by constants.

Inconsistent System

A system of equations that has no solution because the lines are parallel and never intersect.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Slope-Intercept Form

The form of a linear equation $y = mx + b$, where $m$ is the slope and $b$ is the y-intercept.

Substitution Method

A technique for solving systems by expressing one variable in terms of another and replacing it in the remaining equation.

System of Equations

A set of two or more equations with the same variables.

Unbounded Region

A solution region of a system of inequalities that extends infinitely in at least one direction.

Vertex

A corner point of a solution region where two boundary lines intersect.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a system of equations?

A set of two or more equations that are dealt with simultaneously.

How is the solution to a system of linear equations identified on a graph?

It is the point where the lines representing the equations intersect.

A system of equations is classified as _____ if it has at least one solution.

consistent

What classification is given to a system of equations that has no solution?

Inconsistent

A consistent system with exactly one solution occurs when the lines have different _____.

slopes

Under what condition regarding slopes and intercepts does a system have no solution?

The lines have the same slope but different $y$-intercepts.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the relationship between two lines in a consistent system with infinitely many solutions?

They have the same slope and the same $y$-intercept, representing the same line.

In the substitution method, what is the first procedural step?

Solve one of the equations for one of the variables in terms of the other.

When using the substitution method, what does it indicate if the variables cancel out and result in a true statement like $5 = 5$?

The system has infinitely many solutions.

When using the substitution method, what does it indicate if the variables cancel out and result in a false statement like $0 = 12$?

The system has no solution.

What is the primary goal of the elimination (addition/subtraction) method?

To add or subtract the equations so that one of the variables is cancelled out.

Procedurally, what must be done in the elimination method if neither variable has the same or opposite coefficients in both equations?

Multiply one or both equations by a constant to create opposite coefficients for one variable.

Quiz Question 1

A system of equations consists of $y = 5x - 2$ and $10x - 2y = 4$. Which of the following best classifies this system?

A. Consistent with infinitely many solutions

B. Inconsistent with no solution

C. Consistent with exactly one solution

D. Inconsistent with infinitely many solutions

Hint: Try rewriting both equations into slope-intercept form to compare their slopes and intercepts.

Quiz Question 2

Solve the following system using the substitution method: $y = 2x + 1$ and $3x + 2y = 16$. What is the resulting ordered pair $(x, y)$?

A. $(2, 5)$

B. $(3, 7)$

C. $(5, 2)$

D. $(2, 4)$

Hint: Plug the expression for $y$ from the first equation into the $y$ variable of the second equation.

Quiz Question 3

Use the elimination method to solve the system $4x + 3y = 14$ and $3x - 2y = 2$. What is the value of $x$ and $y$?

A. $x = 2, y = 2$

B. $x = 4, y = -1$

C. $x = 2, y = 3$

D. $x = 1, y = 3.33$

Hint: Multiply each equation by a constant so that the coefficients of $y$ become $6$ and $-6$.

Quiz Question 4

A chemist needs 150 mL of a 22% acid solution. They have a 10% acid solution and a 40% acid solution available. Which system of equations correctly models this scenario where $x$ is the volume of t...

A. $x + y = 150$ and $0.10x + 0.40y = 33$

B. $x + y = 150$ and $0.10x + 0.40y = 0.22$

C. $0.10x + 0.40y = 150$ and $x + y = 33$

D. $x + y = 33$ and $10x + 40y = 150$

Hint: One equation should represent the sum of the volumes, while the other represents the sum of the pure acid amounts.

Quiz Question 5

A plane flies 1,200 miles with a tailwind in 4 hours. The return trip against the same wind takes 5 hours. What is the speed of the plane in still air ($p$) and the speed of the wind ($w$)?

A. $p = 270$ mph, $w = 30$ mph

B. $p = 300$ mph, $w = 60$ mph

C. $p = 250$ mph, $w = 50$ mph

D. $p = 285$ mph, $w = 15$ mph

Hint: Use the formula $Rate \times Time = Distance$ to find the ground speeds for both the trip out and the trip back.

Quiz Question 6

An investor places $12,000 in two bonds. One pays 3% annual interest and the other pays 5% annual interest. If the total interest earned in one year is $480, how much was invested at each rate?

A. $6,000 at 3% and $6,000 at 5%

B. $4,000 at 3% and $8,000 at 5%

C. $8,000 at 3% and $4,000 at 5%

D. $5,000 at 3% and $7,000 at 5%

Hint: Let $x + y = 12,000$ and $0.03x + 0.05y = 480$ represent the principal and interest respectively.
