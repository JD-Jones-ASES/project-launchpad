Key Concepts

Rational Function

A function of the form $f(x) = p(x)/q(x)$, where $p(x)$ and $q(x)$ are polynomials and $q(x)$ is not the zero polynomial. These functions can have discontinuous graphs, unlike polynomials.

Vertical Asymptote (VA)

A vertical line $x = a$ that the graph approaches indefinitely as $x$ gets closer to $a$. VAs occur at values of $x$ where the *simplified* denominator of the rational function is zero.

Important Terms (1/2)

Rational Function

A function defined by a rational fraction, i.e., an algebraic fraction such that both the numerator and the denominator are polynomials.

Vertical Asymptote (VA)

A vertical line $x=a$ that the graph of a function approaches as the input $x$ approaches $a$.

Horizontal Asymptote (HA)

A horizontal line $y=L$ that the graph of a function approaches as the input $x$ approaches positive or negative infinity.

Degree of Numerator ($n$)

The highest exponent of the variable in the polynomial in the numerator.

Degree of Denominator ($d$)

The highest exponent of the variable in the polynomial in the denominator.

Important Terms (2/2)

Leading Coefficient

The coefficient of the term with the highest degree in a polynomial.

Simplified Form

A rational function where any common factors between the numerator and denominator have been cancelled. Crucial for correctly identifying vertical asymptotes and holes.

x-intercept

A point $(x, 0)$ where a graph intersects the x-axis.

y-intercept

A point $(0, y)$ where a graph intersects the y-axis.

Sign Chart

A visual tool used to determine the sign (positive or negative) of a function over different intervals on the number line, helping to determine if the graph is above or below the x-axis.
