Important Terms

Rational Expression

An expression that can be written as the ratio of two polynomials, where the denominator is not zero. (e.g., $\frac{x^2+1}{x-2}$)

Reciprocal

For a fraction $\frac{A}{B}$, its reciprocal is $\frac{B}{A}$. When dividing by a fraction, you multiply by its reciprocal.

Factor

To express a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials (e.g., $x^2 - 4 = (x-2)(x+2)$).

Common Factors

Factors that appear in both the numerator and the denominator of a rational expression, which can be canceled out.

Domain Restrictions

Values for the variable(s) that would make any denominator in the expression equal to zero, and thus must be excluded from the domain of the expression.
