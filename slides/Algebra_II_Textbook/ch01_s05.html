Zero and Negative Exponents

Special definitions for `a^0 = 1` (for `a ≠ 0`) and `a^-n = 1/a^n` (for `a ≠ 0` and positive integer `n`), where a negative exponent indicates a reciprocal.

Scientific Notation

A standardized way to write very large or very small numbers as a product `a × 10^n`, where `1 ≤ a < 10` and `n` is an integer, simplifying calculations and expressing magnitude.

Product and Quotient Rules for Radicals

Rules allowing multiplication and division of radicals by combining or separating their radicands, provided they have the same index.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Simplifying Radicals

The process of factoring out the largest perfect `n`th power from the radicand and extracting it from under the radical sign to write the radical in its simplest form.

Rationalizing the Denominator

A technique to rewrite a fraction so that its denominator does not contain any radicals, often by multiplying by an equivalent form of 1 (a radical or a conjugate).

Connection to Geometry

Powers and roots naturally arise when calculating side lengths from areas (square roots) or volumes (cube roots) of geometric shapes.

Important Terms (1/2)

Base

In the expression `a^n`, the number `a` being multiplied.

Exponent

In the expression `a^n`, the number `n` that indicates how many times the base is multiplied by itself.

Power

The entire expression `a^n`, representing the result of repeated multiplication.

Scientific Notation

A numerical format `a × 10^n` where `1 ≤ a < 10` and `n` is an integer.

Radical Sign

The symbol `√` used to denote a root.

Important Terms (2/2)

Radicand

The number or expression under the radical sign, denoted by `a` in `[n]a`.

Index

The small number `n` placed above the radical sign, indicating which root is being taken (e.g., square root `n=2`, cube root `n=3`). If omitted, the index is assumed to be 2.

Principal Square Root

For a non-negative number `a`, the unique non-negative number whose square is `a`.

Rationalize the Denominator

The process of converting a fraction with a radical in the denominator into an equivalent fraction without a radical in the denominator.

Zero Exponent

For any non-zero real number `a`, $a^0 = 1$.

Negative Exponent

For any non-zero real number `a` and positive integer `n`, $a^{-n} = 1/a^n$.

Connecting Roots to Geometry

Side Length from Area

If a square has an area `A`, its side length `s` is `s = √A`.

Side Length from Volume

If a cube has a volume `V`, its side length `s` is `s = [3]V`.
