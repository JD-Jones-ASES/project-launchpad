Circles: An Introduction to Their Equations

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of a Circle

A circle is the collection of all points in a plane that are equidistant (the radius) from a fixed central point.

Standard Form of a Circle

The algebraic equation $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$ represents a circle with center $(h, k)$ and radius $r$. This form is directly derived from the distance formula.

Center and Radius

In the standard form, $(h, k)$ is the coordinates of the center, and $r$ is the length of the radius (note that $r^2$ is on the right side of the equation).

Special Cases

General Form of a Circle

An equation of the form $x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$ is the general form of a circle's equation. To find the center and radius from this form, you must convert it to standard form.

Important Terms (1/2)

Circle

The set of all points $(x,y)$ in a plane that are a fixed distance (the radius) from a fixed point (the center).

Radius ($r$)

The fixed distance from the center to any point on the circle. Must be greater than 0 ($r > 0$).

Center ($(h,k)$)

The fixed point from which all points on the circle are equidistant.

Standard Form of a Circle

The equation $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$, where $(h,k)$ is the center and $r$ is the radius.

Unit Circle

A circle centered at the origin $(0,0)$ with a radius of $1$. Its equation is $x^2 + y^2 = 1$.

Important Terms (2/2)

General Form of a Circle

An equation of a circle expressed as $x^2 + y^2 + Dx + Ey + F = 0$.

Completing the Square

An algebraic method used to rewrite a quadratic expression of the form $x^2 + bx$ as a perfect square trinomial $(x + b/2)^2$ by adding $(b/2)^2$.
