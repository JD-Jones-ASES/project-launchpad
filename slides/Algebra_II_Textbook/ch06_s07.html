Rational Inequalities

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Rational Inequality Definition

An inequality that contains at least one rational expression, comparing it to a value (often zero) or another expression.

Strategy for Solving Rational Inequalities

A systematic five-step process:

Critical Values from Denominator

Zeros of the denominator are *always* excluded from the solution set because the rational expression is undefined at these values. They represent vertical asymptotes.

Critical Values from Numerator

Zeros of the numerator can be included in the solution set if the inequality involves "or equal to" ($\leq$ or $\geq$).

Sign Chart Logic

The sign of a rational expression will be consistent within any interval defined by consecutive critical values.
