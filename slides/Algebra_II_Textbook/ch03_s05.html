Important Terms

Completing the Square

An algebraic technique used to rewrite a quadratic expression `x^2 + bx` as a perfect square trinomial `(x + b/2)^2` by adding the term `(b/2)^2`.

Perfect Square Trinomial

A trinomial (polynomial with three terms) that is the square of a binomial, such as `x^2 + 2kx + k^2 = (x+k)^2`.

Square-Root Property

A method for solving equations of the form `u^2 = d`, which states that `u = ±√d`.

Quadratic Formula

A universal formula `x = (-b ± √(b^2 - 4ac)) / (2a)` that provides the solutions for any quadratic equation in the standard form `ax^2 + bx + c = 0`.

Discriminant (\( \Delta \))

The expression `b^2 - 4ac` within the Quadratic Formula, used to determine the number and type of solutions to a quadratic equation without fully solving it.

How do you complete the square for a monic expression of the form $x^2+bx$?

To make $x^2+bx$ a perfect square trinomial, add $(b/2)^2$. The expression becomes $x^2+bx+(b/2)^2$, which factors as $(x+b/2)^2$.

What value must be added to $x^2-6x$ to make it a perfect square trinomial?

Here $b=-6$, so $b/2 = -3$. The value to add is $(b/2)^2 = (-3)^2 = 9$.

What is the significance of applying the completing-the-square procedure to the general quadratic equation $ax^2+bx+c=0$?

Applying the completing-the-square procedure to the general quadratic equation $ax^2+bx+c=0$ is how the Quadratic Formula is derived.

State the Quadratic Formula.

If $ax^2+bx+c=0$ with $a eq 0$, then $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$.

Define the discriminant of a quadratic equation and its standard notation.

The discriminant of $ax^2+bx+c=0$ is denoted by $\Delta$ (Delta) and is defined as $\Delta = b^2-4ac$.
