Key Concepts (1/2)

Arithmetic Combinations of Functions

Composition of Functions `(f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))`

A powerful operation where the output of the "inner" function $g(x)$ becomes the input for the "outer" function $f$. It's read as "$f$ composed with $g$" or "$f$ of $g$".

Evaluation of Composition (Inside Out)

To evaluate $(f \circ g)(x)$, first calculate $g(x)$, then use that result as the input for $f$.

Non-Commutativity of Composition

In general, $(f \circ g)(x) \neq (g \circ f)(x)$. The order of composition matters.

Decomposition of Functions

The reverse process of composition, where a given complex function is broken down into two simpler functions $f$ and $g$ such that their composition $(f \circ g)(x)$ equals the original function.

Important Terms (1/2)

Sum of Functions

The function $(f+g)(x)$ formed by adding the outputs of $f(x)$ and $g(x)$.

Difference of Functions

The function $(f-g)(x)$ formed by subtracting the output of $g(x)$ from $f(x)$.

Product of Functions

The function $(fg)(x)$ formed by multiplying the outputs of $f(x)$ and $g(x)$.

Quotient of Functions

The function $(f/g)(x)$ formed by dividing the output of $f(x)$ by $g(x)$, provided $g(x) \neq 0$.

Composition of Functions

An operation that combines two functions, say $f$ and $g$, to produce a new function $(f \circ g)(x) = f(g(x))$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Inner Function

In a composition $(f \circ g)(x)$, $g(x)$ is the inner function, whose output is used as the input for the outer function.

Outer Function

In a composition $(f \circ g)(x)$, $f(x)$ is the outer function, which acts on the output of the inner function.

Domain of Composition

For $(f \circ g)(x)$, it's the set of all $x$ such that $x$ is in the domain of $g$ AND $g(x)$ is in the domain of $f$.
