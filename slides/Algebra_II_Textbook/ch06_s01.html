Simplifying Rational Expressions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Rational Expression

An algebraic fraction where both the numerator and denominator are polynomials. It's the polynomial equivalent of an ordinary fraction.

Domain Restriction (Excluded Value)

Any value of the variable that makes the denominator of a rational expression equal to zero, as division by zero is undefined. These values must be excluded from the domain.

Finding Domain Restrictions

To identify excluded values, factor the denominator completely, set each factor to zero, and solve for the variable.

Fundamental Property of Rational Expressions

Common *factors* (not terms) can be canceled from the numerator and denominator to simplify an expression. If $P, Q, R$ are polynomials and $Q \neq 0, R \neq 0$, then $PR/QR = P/Q$.

Simplifying Process

Involves factoring both the numerator and denominator completely, then canceling any identical factors.

Opposite Factors

When a factor in the numerator is the opposite (negative) of a factor in the denominator (e.g., $3-x$ and $x-3$), factor out $-1$ from one of them to reveal a common factor that can be canceled.

Important Terms

Rational Expression

An expression of the form $\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$, where $P(x)$ and $Q(x)$ are polynomials and $Q(x) \neq 0$. Its domain is the set of all real numbers for which $Q(x) \neq 0$.

Domain Restriction (Excluded Value)

Any value of the variable that causes the denominator of a rational expression to be zero. These values are not part of the expression's domain.
