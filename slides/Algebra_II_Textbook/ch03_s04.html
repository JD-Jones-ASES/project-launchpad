Important Terms (1/2)

Zero Product Property

The mathematical rule that states if a product of factors is equal to zero, then at least one of those factors must be zero.

Standard Form (of a Quadratic Equation)

The form \(ax^2+bx+c=0\), where \(a, b, c\) are real numbers and \(a \neq 0\). This is the required form for solving by factoring.

Factoring

The process of expressing a polynomial as a product of two or more simpler polynomials (its factors).

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest factor that two or more terms share. Factoring out the GCF is often the first step in factoring a polynomial.

AC Method

A technique used to factor quadratic trinomials of the form \(ax^2+bx+c\) when \(a \neq 1\).

Important Terms (2/2)

Difference of Squares

A special factoring pattern where \(a^2 - b^2\) factors into \((a-b)(a+b)\).

Solution (or Root)

A value of the variable that makes an equation true.

Consecutive Integers

Integers that follow each other in order (e.g., \(n\) and \(n+1\)).

Extraneous Solution

A solution obtained through valid algebraic steps but that does not satisfy the original equation or the conditions of the problem context.
