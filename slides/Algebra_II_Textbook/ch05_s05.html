Important Terms (1/2)

Difference of Squares

A binomial in the form $a^2 - b^2$, which factors into $(a+b)(a-b)$.

Sum of Cubes

A binomial in the form $a^3 + b^3$, which factors into $(a+b)(a^2 - ab + b^2)$.

Difference of Cubes

A binomial in the form $a^3 - b^3$, which factors into $(a-b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)$.

Quadratic Form

A polynomial that can be expressed as $au^2 + bu + c$, where $u$ is an expression involving $x$ (often $x^n$ for some integer $n > 1$).

Factor Theorem

States that for a polynomial $P(x)$, $(x-c)$ is a factor if and only if $c$ is a root (or zero) of $P(x)$, meaning $P(c)=0$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Zero of a Polynomial

A value $c$ for which the polynomial $P(c)$ evaluates to zero. If $c$ is a zero, then $(x-c)$ is a factor.

Synthetic Division

A shorthand method for dividing polynomials, especially useful when dividing by a linear factor of the form $(x-c)$. It is a key tool for applying the Factor Theorem efficiently.
