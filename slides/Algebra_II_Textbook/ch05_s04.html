Important Terms (1/2)

Dividend ($P(x)$)

The polynomial being divided.

Divisor ($D(x)$)

The polynomial by which another polynomial is divided.

Quotient ($Q(x)$)

The result of polynomial division, excluding the remainder.

Remainder ($R(x)$)

The polynomial left over after division, which has a degree less than that of the divisor (or is zero).

Linear Factor

A polynomial of the form $(x - c)$, where $c$ is a constant. Synthetic division is applicable only when the divisor is a linear factor.

Important Terms (2/2)

Standard Form

Writing a polynomial with terms arranged in descending order of their exponents. It's essential to put polynomials in standard form before dividing.

Root/Zero

If the remainder of a polynomial division $P(x) \div (x-c)$ is $0$, then $(x-c)$ is a factor of $P(x)$, and $c$ is a root (or zero) of the polynomial $P(x)$.
