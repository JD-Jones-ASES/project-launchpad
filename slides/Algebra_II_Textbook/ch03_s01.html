Key Concepts

Important Terms

The Imaginary Unit `i`

Definition

`i^2 = -1`. Equivalently, `i = √(-1)`.

Property for Negative Radicands

For any real number `b > 0`, we can write `√(-b) = i√b`.

Defining Complex Numbers

Standard Form

A **complex number** is any number of the form `a + bi`, where `a` and `b` are real numbers (`a, b ∈ R`).

Equality of Complex Numbers

Two complex numbers `a + bi` and `c + di` are equal if and only if their real parts are equal (`a = c`) and their imaginary parts are equal (`b = d`).

Complex Conjugates and Division

Complex Conjugate

The complex conjugate of `z = a + bi` is `z̄ = a - bi`. It is formed by changing the sign of the imaginary part.

Division

To divide complex numbers, we utilize the complex conjugate to "rationalize" the denominator (make it a real number).
