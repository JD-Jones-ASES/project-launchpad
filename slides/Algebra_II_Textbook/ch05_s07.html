Polynomial Inequalities

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Polynomial Inequality

An inequality involving a polynomial expression, such as $P(x) > 0$, $P(x) < 0$, $P(x) \ge 0$, or $P(x) \le 0$. The goal is to find all *x*-values that satisfy the inequality.

Zeros (Critical Numbers)

The *x*-values where a polynomial $P(x) = 0$. These numbers are crucial because a polynomial can only change sign at its zeros. They partition the number line into distinct intervals.

Intervals

The sections of the number line created by the critical numbers. Within each interval, the polynomial maintains a consistent sign (either entirely positive or entirely negative).

Important Terms (1/2)

Polynomial Inequality

An algebraic statement comparing a polynomial expression to zero or another polynomial using inequality symbols ($<, >, \le, \ge$).

Zeros of a Polynomial

The values of *x* for which the polynomial $P(x)$ equals zero. These are also known as roots.

Critical Numbers

In the context of polynomial inequalities, these are the zeros of the polynomial. They are "critical" because they mark the only points where the polynomial's sign can potentially change.

Sign Chart

A diagram or table used to determine the sign (positive or negative) of a polynomial expression over different intervals on the number line, based on its zeros.

Important Terms (2/2)

Multiplicity

The number of times a particular factor appears in the factored form of a polynomial, which corresponds to the number of times its associated zero occurs.
