Important Terms (1/2)

Exponential Growth

A pattern of increase in which the rate of growth is proportional to the current size of the quantity, leading to increasingly rapid growth over time.

Exponential Decay

A pattern of decrease in which the rate of decay is proportional to the current size of the quantity, leading to a gradual reduction toward zero.

Continuous Growth/Decay Model

The mathematical model $A(t) = A_0 e^{kt}$, representing growth or decay that occurs constantly, rather than at discrete intervals.

Growth Constant ($k$)

The constant that determines the rate of continuous exponential growth or decay. A positive $k$ indicates growth, a negative $k$ indicates decay.

Initial Amount ($A_0$)

The starting value of a quantity at time $t=0$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Half-Life

The amount of time it takes for a quantity undergoing exponential decay to decrease to half of its original value.

Doubling Time

The amount of time it takes for a quantity undergoing exponential growth to double its original value.

pH Scale

A logarithmic scale used to specify the acidity or basicity of an aqueous solution. It is defined as $pH = -\log_{10}[H^+]$, where $[H^+]$ is the hydrogen ion concentration.

Richter Scale

A logarithmic scale used to quantify the magnitude of an earthquake. It is defined as $M = \log_{10}(I/I_0)$, where $I$ is the intensity of the earthquake and $I_0$ is a reference intensity.
