Solving Equations by Taking Roots

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Important Terms (1/2)

Quadratic Equation

An equation that can be written in the form \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\). This section focuses on the simpler form \(x^2=c\).

Square-Root Property

The rule that states if \(u^2 = c\), then \(u = \pm \sqrt{c}\).

Principal Square Root

The non-negative square root of a number, denoted by the radical symbol (\(\sqrt{}\)). For example, \(\sqrt{9} = 3\), not \(\pm 3\).

Complex Solutions

Solutions to equations that involve the imaginary unit \(i\) (\(i = \sqrt{-1}\)), typically arising when taking the square root of a negative number.

Complex Conjugates

A pair of complex numbers of the form \(a+bi\) and \(a-bi\). When an equation with real coefficients has complex solutions, they always appear as conjugate pairs.

Important Terms (2/2)

Higher-Root Equation

An equation of the form \(x^n = c\), where \(n\) is an integer greater than 2.

Cube Root

The number that, when multiplied by itself three times, equals a given number. Denoted by \(\sqrt[3]{}\).

Geometry Applications

• If you find the area \(A\) of a square, its side length \(s\) is found by \(s = \sqrt{A}\). You only take the positive root because length cannot be negative.

• Always consider the context of the problem. While the mathematical solution to an equation like \(x^2=25\) is \(x=\pm 5\), if \(x\) represents a side length, only \(x=5\) is a meaningful answer.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the simplest form of a quadratic equation that can be solved by taking roots?

\(x^2 = c\), where \(c\) is a constant.

State the Square-Root Property for solving \(x^2=c\) when \(c > 0\).

If \(x^2 = c\) and \(c > 0\), then there are two real solutions: \(x = \sqrt{c}\) and \(x = -\sqrt{c}\), written as \(x = \pm\sqrt{c}\).

State the Square-Root Property for solving \(x^2=c\) when \(c = 0\).

If \(x^2 = c\) and \(c = 0\), then there is one real solution: \(x = 0\).

State the Square-Root Property for solving \(x^2=c\) when \(c < 0\).

If \(x^2 = c\) and \(c < 0\), then there are two complex (non-real) solutions: \(x = \pm i\sqrt{-c}\).

What does the "\(\pm\)" (plus or minus) symbol signify when solving equations by taking roots?

It indicates that there are two solutions: a positive root and a negative root.

When solving an equation of the form \((ax+b)^2=c\) where \(c<0\), what characteristic will the resulting complex solutions always have?

The two solutions will always be complex conjugates of each other.

How many real solutions does the equation \(x^n = c\) have if \(n\) is an odd integer?

One unique real solution: \(x = \sqrt[n]{c}\). This holds even if \(c\) is negative.

How many real solutions does the equation \(x^n = c\) have if \(n\) is an even integer and \(c > 0\)?

Two real solutions: \(x = \pm\sqrt[n]{c}\).
