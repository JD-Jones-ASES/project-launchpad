Factoring I: Foundations of Polynomial Factoring

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Factoring as Reverse Multiplication

The process of rewriting a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials, much like decomposing a number into its prime factors.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest monomial that divides every term in a polynomial evenly. Factoring out the GCF is always the first step in any factoring problem.

Sum and Difference of Cubes

Special factoring patterns for binomials of the form $A^3 + B^3$ and $A^3 - B^3$, which expand into a binomial and a trinomial factor.

Polynomials in Quadratic Form

Polynomials that can be expressed as $au^2 + bu + c$ by making a suitable substitution for a variable expression, $u$.

Multiplicity of a Zero

If $(x-r)^k$ is the highest power of a factor that divides a polynomial $p(x)$, then $r$ is a zero of $p$ with multiplicity $k$. This indicates how many times a factor appears.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Factoring Monic Trinomials ($x^2 + bx + c$)

To factor, find two numbers whose product is $c$ and whose sum is $b$.

Factoring Non-Monic Trinomials ($ax^2 + bx + c$ with $a eq 1$)

The AC Method involves finding two numbers whose product is $ac$ and whose sum is $b$, then rewriting the middle term and factoring by grouping.

Important Terms (1/2)

Factoring

The process of expressing a polynomial as a product of two or more polynomials.

Greatest Common Factor (GCF)

The largest monomial (including constants and variables with their lowest powers) that divides each term of a polynomial.

Quadratic Form

A polynomial that can be rewritten as $au^2 + bu + c$, where $u$ is some expression involving a variable.

Zero of a Polynomial

A value $r$ such that when $x=r$ is substituted into the polynomial $p(x)$, the result is $p(r)=0$. Also known as a root of the polynomial.

Multiplicity of a Zero

The number of times a particular zero appears as a root of a polynomial, determined by the exponent of its corresponding factor in the polynomial's factored form.

Important Terms (2/2)

Monic Trinomial

A trinomial where the leading coefficient (the coefficient of the term with the highest degree) is 1. For example, $x^2 + 5x + 6$.

AC Method

A technique used to factor trinomials of the form $ax^2 + bx + c$ (where $a eq 1$) by splitting the middle term.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is factoring in algebra?

Factoring is the reverse of multiplication: given a polynomial, we write it as a product of simpler polynomials.

Why is factoring essential in mathematics?

Factoring is essential for solving equations, simplifying rational expressions, and analyzing graphs.

What is the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of a polynomial?

The GCF of a polynomial is the largest monomial that divides every term evenly.

What is always the first step in any factoring problem?

Factoring out the GCF is always the first step.

List the steps to factor out the GCF from a polynomial.

1. Find the GCF of all terms (highest common numerical factor times the lowest power of each shared variable). 2. Write each term as GCF times something. 3. ...

Factor the polynomial completely: $12x^3 - 8x^2 + 4x$

The GCF is $4x$. So, $12x^3 - 8x^2 + 4x = 4x(3x^2 - 2x + 1)$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How can you verify that you have factored a polynomial correctly?

Always verify by multiplying the factored expression back out. The product must equal the original polynomial.

State the formula for factoring the sum of two cubes, $A^3 + B^3$.

$A^3 + B^3 = (A+B)(A^2 - AB + B^2)$

State the formula for factoring the difference of two cubes, $A^3 - B^3$.

$A^3 - B^3 = (A-B)(A^2 + AB + B^2)$

What is the SOAP memory aid used for in the context of sum and difference of cubes?

SOAP helps remember the signs in the trinomial factor: Same sign as the binomial, Opposite sign in the middle, Always Positive for the last term.

Factor the polynomial: $8x^3 + 27$

Recognize $8x^3 = (2x)^3$ and $27 = 3^3$. Using the sum of cubes formula: $(2x+3)(4x^2 - 6x + 9)$.

Factor the polynomial: $x^3 - 64$

Recognize $x^3 = x^3$ and $64 = 4^3$. Using the difference of cubes formula: $(x-4)(x^2 + 4x + 16)$.
