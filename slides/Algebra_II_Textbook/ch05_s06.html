Important Terms

Zero of a Polynomial (or Root)

A value $c$ such that $P(c) = 0$. These are the x-coordinates of the x-intercepts of the graph $y = P(x)$.

Multiplicity

If $(x - c)^k$ is a factor of $P(x)$ but $(x-c)^{k+1}$ is not, then $c$ is a zero of multiplicity $k$. This value $k$ determines how the graph behaves at the x-intercept $c$.

End Behavior

The direction of the graph of a polynomial function as $x$ approaches positive infinity ($x \to \infty$) and as $x$ approaches negative infinity ($x \to -\infty$).

Multiplicity and Graph Behavior at a Zero

Definition

If $(x - c)^k$ is a factor of $P(x)$, but $(x-c)^{k+1}$ is not, then $c$ is a zero of multiplicity $k$.

Rules for Graph Behavior

Zero $x=0$

The factor is $x^2 = (x-0)^2$. The multiplicity is $k=2$ (even). Therefore, the graph touches (bounces) the x-axis at $x=0$.

Zero $x=3$

The factor is $(x-3)^3$. The multiplicity is $k=3$ (odd). Therefore, the graph crosses the x-axis at $x=3$, with a flattened appearance.

Zero $x=-1$

The factor is $(x+1) = (x-(-1))^1$. The multiplicity is $k=1$ (odd). Therefore, the graph crosses the x-axis at $x=-1$.
