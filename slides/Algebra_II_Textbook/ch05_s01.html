Important Terms (1/3)

Polynomial

An algebraic expression consisting of one or more terms, where each term is a product of a number (coefficient) and one or more variables raised to non-negative integer powers.

Term

A single part of a polynomial, typically a product of a coefficient and a variable raised to an exponent (e.g., $5x^3$, $-2x$, $7$).

Coefficient

The numerical factor of a term in a polynomial (e.g., in $5x^3$, $5$ is the coefficient).

Degree of a Polynomial

The highest exponent of the variable present in any of its terms after the polynomial has been simplified.

Leading Term

The term in a polynomial (when written in standard form) that has the highest degree.

Important Terms (2/3)

Leading Coefficient

The coefficient of the leading term.

Constant Term

The term in a polynomial that does not contain a variable (a term with a degree of zero).

Standard Form

The arrangement of a polynomial's terms in descending order of their degrees.

Monomial

A polynomial with exactly one term (e.g., $7x^2$).

Binomial

A polynomial with exactly two terms (e.g., $3x - 5$).

Important Terms (3/3)

Trinomial

A polynomial with exactly three terms (e.g., $x^2 + 2x - 1$).

Like Terms

Terms that have the same variables raised to the same powers (e.g., $3x^2$ and $-7x^2$).

Evaluating a Polynomial

The process of substituting a specific numerical or algebraic value for the variable(s) in a polynomial and simplifying the resulting expression to find its value.

Anatomy of a Polynomial

Degree

This is the most important characteristic. It's simply the largest exponent on the variable.

Leading Term

This is the entire term that contains the highest exponent.

Leading Coefficient

This is the numerical part (coefficient) of the leading term.

Constant Term

This is the term that doesn't have a variable attached. It's essentially $a_0 x^0 = a_0 \cdot 1 = a_0$.

B. Evaluating a Polynomial

Process

To evaluate $P(x)$ at $x=a$, substitute the value $a$ for every $x$ in the polynomial and then simplify using the order of operations.

Notation

The result is written as $P(a)$.

Example (Numerical)

Let $P(x) = 2x^3 - 5x^2 + x - 4$.

Caution with Negative Values

Always enclose negative numbers in parentheses when substituting them, especially before applying exponents. For example, $(-2)^2 = 4$, but $-2^2 = -4$.
