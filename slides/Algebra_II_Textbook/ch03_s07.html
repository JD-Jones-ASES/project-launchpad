Important Terms (1/2)

Vertex

The unique turning point of a parabola, which represents either the highest (maximum) or lowest (minimum) point on the graph of a quadratic function.

Optimization

The mathematical process of finding the most efficient or effective way to use a situation or resource; specifically, finding the maximum or minimum value of a function.

Projectile Motion

The path an object takes when thrown or launched into the air and subjected only to the force of gravity (ignoring air resistance). Its height over time is modeled by a quadratic function.

Initial Velocity (`v_0`)

The speed and direction (upward or downward) of an object at the moment it begins its motion. A positive `v_0` indicates an upward launch.

Initial Height (`h_0`)

The vertical position of an object at the starting moment (`t=0`) of its motion.

Important Terms (2/2)

Revenue Function (`R(x)`)

A function that calculates the total income generated from selling `x` units of a product, typically given by the formula `R(x) = x * p(x)`, where `p(x)` is the price per unit.

Constraint Equation

An equation that describes a limitation or condition within a problem, often used to relate two or more variables and simplify an optimization problem into a single-variable quadratic function.

Feasible Domain

The set of all possible input values for a variable that are realistic, logical, and relevant within the context of a real-world problem (e.g., positive lengths, non-negative time).
