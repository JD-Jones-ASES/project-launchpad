Key Concepts (1/2)

Distributive Property as the Foundation

All polynomial multiplication relies on the distributive property, where each term of one polynomial is multiplied by every term of the other.

Monomial by Polynomial Multiplication

To multiply a single-term expression (monomial) by a multi-term expression (polynomial), distribute the monomial to each term within the polynomial.

Important Terms (1/2)

Monomial

A polynomial with only one term (e.g., $3x^2$, $5$).

Binomial

A polynomial with two terms (e.g., $x+2$, $3y-4$).

Polynomial

An algebraic expression consisting of one or more terms, where each term is a product of a constant and one or more variables raised to non-negative integer powers.

Distributive Property

An algebraic property that states $a(b+c) = ab + ac$. It is the core principle behind all polynomial multiplication.

Perfect Square Trinomial

A trinomial (polynomial with three terms) that results from squaring a binomial, such as $a^2 + 2ab + b^2$ or $a^2 - 2ab + b^2$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Difference of Squares

A binomial that results from multiplying the sum and difference of the same two terms, having the form $a^2 - b^2$.

Partial Products

The individual products generated before combining like terms when multiplying polynomials.
