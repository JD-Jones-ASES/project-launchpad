The New Way: The Emergence of the Modern Short Story

Shift in Focus

Characteristics of the Modern Short Story (e.g., Chekhov)

Lack of Traditional Resolution

Modern stories often conclude without clear-cut resolutions. Characters might realize something profound, but their external circumstances remain complicated or unresolved.

No Explicit Lessons or Rewards

Characters are not necessarily punished or rewarded, nor do they always learn a clear moral lesson by the story's end. The story reflects life's ambiguities rather than imposing a definitive judgment.
