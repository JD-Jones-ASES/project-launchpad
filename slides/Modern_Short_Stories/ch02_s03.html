Dmitri Dmitritch Gurov

The protagonist of the story; a middle-aged, married man from Moscow who is a banker but trained in arts and opera. He is cynical about women and marriage but frequently engages in extramarital aff...

Anna Sergeyevna von Dideritz

The "lady with the dog"; a young, married woman from Petersburg, living in S—, who is on holiday alone in Yalta. She is initially presented as shy and innocent.

Yalta

A popular resort town on the Black Sea coast of Crimea, known for its mild climate and as a destination for the Russian upper class. It also has a reputation for illicit romantic encounters.

Pomeranian

A breed of small, fluffy dog. Anna's white Pomeranian is a distinctive feature that gives her the moniker "the lady with the dog" and serves as Gurov's initial pretext for approaching her.

Béret

A soft, round, flat-crowned hat, often made of wool. Anna's béret is part of her distinctive attire.

Important Terms (2/2)

Patronymic

A component of a personal name based on the first name of one's father, grandfather, or an earlier male ancestor. "Dmitritch" (son of Dmitri) and "Sergeyevna" (daughter of Sergei) are examples.

Lorgnette

A pair of spectacles or opera glasses with a handle, typically held up to the eyes. Anna uses one while watching the steamer, suggesting a certain elegance or a desire to observe.

Groyne

A low wall or sturdy timber barrier built out into the sea from a beach to prevent coastal erosion or to protect a harbor. Gurov and Anna walk on the groyne to watch the steamer.

"The lower race"

Gurov's dismissive and contemptuous term for women, reflecting his misogynistic views and attempts to rationalize his infidelity.

"Flunkey"

A derogatory term for a person who performs servile duties or acts as a sycophant; a lackey. Anna uses this term to describe her husband, expressing her profound disdain and dissatisfaction with him.
