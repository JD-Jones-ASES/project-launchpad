Truthfulness in Modern Fiction

The modern short story often prioritizes portraying life's messiness and ambiguity, believing that open endings are a more truthful reflection of reality than tidy conclusions.

Open Endings as Evasion

A counter-argument suggesting that refusing to provide a resolution might be an evasion, a way for writers to appear sophisticated rather than to genuinely grapple with difficult conclusions.

Courage of Closed Endings

Providing a "closed ending" (a clear resolution) also requires courage, as it means committing to a specific meaning, outcome, and interpretation of events.

Key Concepts (2/2)

The Central Question

The core of the section is a genuine inquiry for the reader: Is an open ending the more honest choice, or is something valuable lost when a writer doesn't follow a story to its conclusion?

Purpose of Stories

How one answers the central question reveals personal beliefs about what stories are fundamentally for—whether they are meant to reflect reality, provide comfort, provoke thought, or offer meaning.

Important Terms

Open Ending

A narrative conclusion that intentionally leaves questions unanswered, conflicts unresolved, or character fates uncertain, mirroring the ambiguities of real life.

Resolution

The part of a story where the main conflict is settled, and the story's outcome is made clear.

Tidy Conclusion (Closed Ending)

An ending that provides a clear, definitive resolution to all major plot points and character arcs, often bringing a sense of completeness or closure.

Evasion

The act of avoiding or escaping something, in this context, avoiding the responsibility or difficulty of providing a definitive conclusion to a story.

Verdict

A judgment or decision, often implying a definitive statement about right or wrong, or the meaning of events.

The Ambiguity of Endings

Gurov and Anna

Their story ends as they face the "most complicated and difficult part of their life together," with no insight into their future actions or decisions.

Gabriel Conroy

His realization about himself, experienced alone, remains internal and unspoken as snow falls across Ireland, leaving his future changed perspective ambiguous.

The Girl and the Man

Their tense conversation concludes with no decision made about their future, as the train has not arrived, leaving their relationship and choices in suspension.

The Modern Short Story's Philosophy

Reflecting Reality

Modern writers frequently believe that life itself rarely provides neat conclusions or clear-cut resolutions. Therefore, fiction, to be truthful, should not pretend otherwise.

Honesty over Comfort

An open ending is seen as a more honest portrayal of human experience, even if it denies the reader the comfort of a definitive resolution or "verdict."

The Counter-Argument: Open Endings as Evasion

Artificiality

It's argued that a refusal to resolve a story can be just as artificial as a overly neat conclusion. Both are artistic constructs.
