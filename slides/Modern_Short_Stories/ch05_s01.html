Important Terms (1/2)

Subtext

The underlying and often unspoken meaning, themes, or intentions conveyed in a conversation or piece of writing. In Hemingway, it's paramount for understanding characters.

Dialogue

The conversation between two or more characters in a story. Hemingway heavily relies on dialogue to reveal character and advance plot.

Narration

The act of telling a story; the perspective from which a story is told. Hemingway often uses minimal, objective narration.

Interior Monologue

A literary device that presents a character's thoughts and feelings directly, as if overheard in the mind. Hemingway typically avoids this to emphasize external action and dialogue.

Symbolism

The use of objects, people, or ideas to represent something else. For example, "white elephants" serve as a key symbol in the story.

Important Terms (2/2)

Literary Style

The particular way an author writes, encompassing their word choice, sentence structure, use of imagery, and overall tone. Hemingway's style is famously minimalist and direct.

Ambiguity

The quality of being open to more than one interpretation; inexactness. Many aspects of "Hills Like White Elephants," especially its ending, are intentionally ambiguous.
