The "fer" Word Family

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Word Family Definition

A group of words that share a common base or root and are related in meaning, often through the addition of prefixes and suffixes.

The Latin Root "fer"

Originating from Latin, "fer" means "to carry," "to bear," or "to bring." This core meaning helps us understand the fundamental sense of all words in its family.

Prefixes as Meaning Modifiers

Prefixes are added to the beginning of a root word and change or refine its meaning (e.g., *re-* means "back" or "again," *con-* means "together").

Suffixes as Word Type Changers

Suffixes are added to the end of a root word and often change its grammatical function (e.g., from a verb to a noun or adjective, like *-ence* creating a noun, or *-able* creating an adjective).

Etymology and Vocabulary Expansion

Studying word families and their roots enhances etymological understanding, which in turn significantly boosts vocabulary acquisition and reading comprehension.

Important Terms (1/4)

Word Family

A group of words sharing a common base or root, often related in meaning and form through prefixes and suffixes.

Root Word

The basic part of a word that carries the primary meaning and cannot be broken down further. (e.g., *fer*).

Prefix

An affix placed before the root word to modify its meaning (e.g., *re-*, *con-*).

Suffix

An affix placed after the root word, often changing its grammatical function or part of speech (e.g., *-ence*, *-able*).

Etymology

The study of the origin and historical development of words.

Important Terms (2/4)

Derivation

The process of forming a new word from an existing word, often by adding prefixes or suffixes.

Confer

To grant or bestow (a title, degree, benefit); to have discussions or exchange opinions. (from *con-* "together" + *ferre* "to bring")

Defer

To postpone or put off to a later time; to yield respectfully to another's opinion or judgment. (from *de-* "down, away" + *ferre* "to carry")

Infer

To deduce or conclude information from evidence and reasoning rather than from explicit statements. (from *in-* "into, upon" + *ferre* "to bring")

Refer

To direct attention to, to mention, or to send someone/something to a place for help or information. (from *re-* "back, again" + *ferre* "to carry")

Important Terms (3/4)

Transfer

To move from one place to another; to pass something from one person to another. (from *trans-* "across" + *ferre* "to carry")

Suffer

To experience something unpleasant or painful; to tolerate or allow. (from *sub-* "under" + *ferre* "to bear")

Fertile

Capable of producing abundant vegetation or crops; capable of reproducing. (from *ferre* "to bear, bring" + *-ile* "capable of")

Circumference

The enclosing boundary of a curved geometric figure, especially a circle; the distance around it. (from *circum-* "around" + *ferre* "to carry/bear")

Proffer

To offer or present (something) for acceptance. (from *pro-* "forth" + *ferre* "to bring")

Important Terms (4/4)

Offer

To present something for acceptance or rejection; to propose a price. (from Old French *offrir*, from Latin *ob-* "towards" + *ferre* "to bring")

Aquifer

An underground layer of permeable rock, rock fractures, or unconsolidated materials (gravel, sand, or silt) from which groundwater can be extracted. (from Latin *aqua* "water" + *ferre* "to bear")

Common Prefixes and Their Impact on "fer" Words (1/2)

con-

Means "together" or "with."

de-

Means "down," "away," or "off."

in-

Can mean "in," "into," "on," or sometimes "not."

re-

Means "back" or "again."

trans-

Means "across," "beyond," or "through."

Common Prefixes and Their Impact on "fer" Words (2/2)

pro-

Means "forward" or "forth."

ob-/of-

Means "against," "toward," or "in the way."

sub-/suf-

Means "under" or "below."

dis-/dif-

Means "apart," "away," or "not."

circum-

Means "around."

Common Suffixes and Their Impact on "fer" Words

-ence/-ance

Forms nouns, denoting an action, state, or quality.

-able/-ible

Forms adjectives, meaning "capable of," "worthy of," or "able to be."

-er/-or

Forms nouns, typically denoting a person who performs an action or a thing that does something.

-ile

Forms adjectives, meaning "capable of," "pertaining to," or "having the characteristics of."

-ation

Forms nouns, indicating an action or process.
