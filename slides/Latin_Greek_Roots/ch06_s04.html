Important Terms (1/2)

Root

The basic part of a word that carries its primary meaning.

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and how their meanings have changed throughout history.

Word Family

A group of words that share a common root and, therefore, a related meaning.

`sens/sent`

A Latin root meaning "to feel" or "to perceive"; also extends to "judgment" and "opinion."

`vis/vid`

A Latin root meaning "to see" (introduced in Chapter 5).

Important Terms (2/2)

Perceive

To become aware of something through the senses or to understand or interpret something.

Judgment

The ability to make considered decisions or come to sensible conclusions; an opinion or conclusion reached after consideration.

Sensation

A physical feeling or perception resulting from something that happens to or comes into contact with the body.

Sentiment

A feeling or emotion; an attitude toward something.
