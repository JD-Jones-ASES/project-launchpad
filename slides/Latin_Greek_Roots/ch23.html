Chapter 23 — PHON / CHRON (Sound, Voice; Time)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root phon

— from Greek phone (sound, voice); appears in telephone, symphony, microphone, euphony

Root chron

— from Greek chronos (time); appears in chronology, anachronism, synchronize, chronicle

Prefix patterns with phon

tele- (far) + phon = telephone; sym- (together) + phon = symphony; eu- (good) + phon = euphony; micro- (small) + phon = microphone

Prefix patterns with chron

syn- (together) + chron = synchronize; ana- (against) + chron = anachronism

Greek vs. Latin parallels

phon vs. voc/vox (voice); chron vs. tempor (time)

Important Terms (1/2)

telephone

tele- (far) + phon (sound) — a device for transmitting speech over distances

symphony

sym- (together) + phon (sound) — a long musical composition; literally "sounding together"

microphone

micro- (small) + phon (sound) — a device that picks up and amplifies sound

euphony

eu- (good) + phon (sound) — pleasing, harmonious sound in speech or music

chronology

chron (time) + -logy (account) — the arrangement of events in time order

Important Terms (2/2)

anachronism

ana- (against) + chron (time) — something placed in the wrong time period

synchronize

syn- (together) + chron (time) — to cause things to happen at the same time

chronicle

chron (time) + -icle (record) — a historical account organized by time
