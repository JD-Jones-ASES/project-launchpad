Chapter 16, Section 4 – Word Family: From *tempor*

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Word Family

A group of words that share a common root or base, often also sharing related meanings and originating from the same linguistic source.

Root Word (*tempor*)

The basic part of a word that carries the primary meaning. The Latin root *tempor* means "time."

Affixes (Prefixes and Suffixes)

Morphemes (meaningful units) added to a root word to modify its meaning or grammatical function.

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and how their meanings have evolved over time. Understanding etymology, especially of root words, is a powerful tool for vocabulary building and comprehension.

Meaning Derivation

How the core meaning of "time" from *tempor* is extended and refined by prefixes and suffixes to create diverse but related words.

Important Terms (1/3)

Root Word

The fundamental part of a word that contains its core meaning, from which other words can be formed.

_tempor_

A Latin root meaning "time" or "season."

Word Family

A collection of words derived from a common root, often through the addition of various prefixes and suffixes.

Etymology

The historical development of a word, including its origin, changes in form, and changes in meaning.

Prefix

A letter or group of letters added to the beginning of a word to change its meaning (e.g., *con-* meaning "with" or "together").

Important Terms (2/3)

Suffix

A letter or group of letters added to the end of a word to change its meaning or grammatical function (e.g., *-ary* meaning "relating to").

Temporary

Lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent.

Contemporary

Existing, occurring, or living at the same time; belonging to or characteristic of the present time.

Contemporaneous

Existing or occurring during the same period of time.

Temporal

Relating to worldly rather than spiritual affairs; relating to time.

Important Terms (3/3)

Temporize

To avoid making a decision or committing oneself in order to gain time.

Extemporaneous

Spoken or done without preparation; impromptu.

Tempo

The speed at which a piece of music is played; the pace of an activity or event.

How Affixes Modify the Root

Prefixes like *con-* and *ex-

* indicate relationships *with* time or *out of* time.

Suffixes like *-ary*, *-al*, *-ize*, *-aneous*, *-ality

* change the word's grammatical function (noun, adjective, verb) and add specific qualifiers.
