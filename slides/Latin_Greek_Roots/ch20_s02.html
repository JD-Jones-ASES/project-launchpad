Trust was not merely a desirable trait in Roman civilization; it was a foundational pillar. A person's standing, whether a senator or a merchant, hinged on their reputation for trustworthiness.

`rootconnection`: `cred` and `jus/jur` (Law)

Dependence on Trust

For a legal system to function, there must be a basis for believing testimony and evidence.

Oaths (`jur`)

Witnesses in court swear oaths (related to `jur`), promising to tell the truth. This act is intended to reinforce the reliability of their statements.

Belief (`cred`)

The court and jury must then `believe` (cred) the testimony provided by witnesses. A `credible` witness offers trustworthy testimony.
