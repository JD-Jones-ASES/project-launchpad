Chapter 16 — TEMPOR

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

tempor/tempus = time (Latin *tempus*, genitive *temporis*)

English stem

*tempor-* (from the genitive form *temporis*)

Prefix patterns

con- (with/together), ex- (out of), tem- (base form)

Semantic range

These words describe how long things last (temporary, temporal), when they happen (contemporary), how prepared speakers are (extemporaneous), and how fast music moves (tempo)

Important Terms (1/2)

temporary

lasting only for a limited time; not permanent (tempor + -ary)

contemporary

living or occurring at the same time; sharing the same era (con- + tempor + -ary)

temporal

relating to time; of the earthly, passing world (tempor + -al)

extemporaneous

done without preparation; spoken on the spot (ex- + tempor + -aneous)

tempo

the speed at which a piece of music is played (from Latin *tempus* via Italian)

Important Terms (2/2)

tempest

a violent storm; originally any period of time or weather season (temp- + -est)
