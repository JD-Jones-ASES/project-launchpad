Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of a Word Family

A collection of words that are formed from the same base word or root, often through the addition of various prefixes and suffixes. These words are semantically and often grammatically related.

Base Words/Root Words

The core part of a word that carries its primary meaning and cannot be broken down further without losing its essential sense. It's the foundation upon which other words in the family are built.

Prefixes

Morphemes (meaningful units) added to the *beginning* of a base word to modify its meaning. Examples: `un-` (undo), `re-` (rewrite), `pre-` (preview).

Suffixes

Morphemes added to the *end* of a base word to change its meaning, part of speech, or grammatical function. Examples: `-er` (teacher), `-ly` (quickly), `-tion` (action).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Vocabulary Expansion

Learning one root word and its common affixes can unlock the meaning of many related words, making vocabulary acquisition more efficient and logical.

Important Terms (1/2)

Word Family

A group of words that share a common root or base word and are related in meaning and often form.

Root Word

The fundamental part of a word that contains its primary meaning and to which affixes can be added (e.g., `ject` in `project`, `reject`, `inject`).

Base Word

Similar to a root word, but often able to stand alone as a word (e.g., `play` in `replay`, `player`, `playful`). Sometimes used interchangeably with root word, but base words are typically free mor...

Prefix

An affix placed before a root or base word (e.g., `un-`, `re-`, `dis-`).

Suffix

An affix placed after a root or base word (e.g., `-able`, `-tion`, `-ing`).

Important Terms (2/2)

Affix

A general term for a morpheme that is attached to a word stem to form a new word or word form. Includes both prefixes and suffixes.

Morpheme

The smallest meaningful unit in a language. Words can consist of one or more morphemes.

Derivational Morpheme

An affix that changes the meaning or part of speech of a word (e.g., `happy` (adj) -> `unhappy` (adj); `govern` (verb) -> `government` (noun)).
