Chapter 10 — DUC / DUCT

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

duc/duct = to lead (Latin *ducere*)

Two forms

*duc* (present stem) and *duct* (past participle stem, from *ductus*)

Important connection

The word *aqueduct* combines this root with *aqua* (water, Chapter 14): a structure that "leads water"

Prefix patterns

Prefixes change the direction or nature of the leading — e- (out), pro- (forward), intro- (inward), de- (down), ab- (away), con- (together)

Important Terms (1/2)

conduct

to lead or direct together; to manage or carry out (con- + duct)

educate

to lead out knowledge; to teach (e- + duc + -ate)

introduce

to lead inward; to bring in or present something new (intro- + duce)

produce

to lead forward; to make or create (pro- + duce)

deduce

to lead down through reasoning to a conclusion (de- + duce)

Important Terms (2/2)

abduct

to lead away by force; to kidnap (ab- + duct)

duke

a nobleman of high rank; originally a Roman military commander (from Latin *dux*, leader)

aqueduct

a structure that channels water across a distance (aqua + duct)

Two Forms: duc and duct

duc form

educate, produce, introduce, deduce, conduct

duct form

abduct, aqueduct, conductor
