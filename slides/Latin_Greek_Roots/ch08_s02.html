Understanding the Latin Root 'leg/lect': Gathering, Reading, and Choosing

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Versatile Root `legere`

The Latin verb `legere` is one of the most prolific roots in English, contributing to numerous words across different semantic fields.

Original Meaning: "To Gather"

The fundamental sense of `legere` is akin to physically collecting items, like olives from the ground.

Expanded Meanings: "To Read" and "To Choose/Select"

From gathering objects, the meaning evolved to gathering words (reading) and gathering people (choosing). This "triple meaning" is central to understanding its derivatives.

Influence on English Vocabulary

The three core meanings directly explain words like `legible` (readable), `lecture` (a reading aloud), `select` (to choose apart), and `elect` (to choose out).

Connection to Law (`lex`)

The related Latin noun `lex` (law), meaning "chosen rules" or "gathered statutes," gives us words like `legal`.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Prefixes Modifying Meaning

Prefixes like `se-` (apart), `e-` (out), `nec-` (not), and `col-/con-` (together) combine with `legere` to create specific meanings, such as `select`, `elect`, `neglect`, and `collect`.

Important Terms (1/3)

`legere`

The Latin verb that forms the root `leg/lect`. Its core meanings are "to gather," "to read," and "to choose/select."

`legible`

(adj.) Clear enough to be read; able to be "gathered" or read easily.

`lecture`

(n.) A talk or discourse given to an audience, especially for instruction; historically, a "reading aloud" where knowledge is "gathered" and presented.

`select`

(v.) To carefully choose from a group; from Latin `se-` (apart) + `legere` (to choose) meaning "to choose apart."

`elect`

(v.) To choose (someone) to hold public office or some other position by voting; from Latin `e-` (out) + `legere` (to choose) meaning "to choose out."

Important Terms (2/3)

`lex`

(n.) The Latin noun for "law," derived from the idea of "chosen rules" or "gathered statutes."

`legal`

(adj.) Relating to the law.

`neglect`

(v.) To fail to care for properly or attend to; from Latin `nec-` (not) + `legere` (to choose), meaning "not to choose" or "to fail to pick up."

`collect`

(v.) To gather together; from Latin `col-/con-` (together) + `legere` (to gather).

`legenda`

(n.) Latin for "things to be read," the origin of the English word "legend."

Important Terms (3/3)

`legend`

(n.) An traditional story sometimes popularly regarded as historical but unauthenticated; a story so grand it seems almost too wonderful to be true; an extremely famous or notorious person.
