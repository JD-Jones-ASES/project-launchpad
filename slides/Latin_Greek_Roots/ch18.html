Chapter 18 — MORT / MOR (Death)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root mort

— from Latin mors, mortis (death); appears in mortal, immortal, mortify, mortgage, mortuary

Root mor

— from Latin mori (to die); appears in moribund

Prefix patterns

im- (not) + mort = immortal; post- (after) + mort = post-mortem

Suffix patterns

-al (relating to), -ify (to make), -uary (place of), -bund (about to)

Thematic contrast

mort/mor (death) is the natural opposite of vit/viv (life) from Chapter 17

Important Terms (1/2)

mortal

mort (death) + -al — subject to death; causing death; describes any creature that will eventually die

immortal

im- (not) + mort + -al — living forever; not subject to death; describes gods and legends

mortify

mort (death) + -ify (to make) — originally "to put to death"; now means to cause great embarrassment or shame

mortgage

mort (death) + gage (pledge) — a loan secured by property; the pledge "dies" when the debt is paid

moribund

mori (to die) + -bund (about to) — at the point of death; in terminal decline

Important Terms (2/2)

post-mortem

post- (after) + mort (death) — an examination conducted after death

mortuary

mort (death) + -uary (place of) — a place where dead bodies are kept before burial or cremation

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the Latin root mort mean?

Death (from Latin mors, mortis, meaning 'death')

What does the Latin root mor mean?

To die (from Latin mori, meaning 'to die')

mortal

mort (death) + -al (relating to) — subject to death; causing death

immortal

im- (not) + mort (death) + -al (relating to) — living forever; not subject to death

mortify

mort (death) + -ify (to make) — to cause great embarrassment or shame

mortgage

mort (death) + gage (pledge) — a loan agreement secured by property; literally a 'death pledge'

Key Concepts (2/2)

moribund

mori (to die) + -bund (about to) — at the point of death; in terminal decline

post-mortem

post- (after) + mort (death) — an examination conducted after death

mortuary

mort (death) + -uary (place of) — a place where dead bodies are kept before burial

What does the prefix im- mean in the word immortal?

Not — so immortal means 'not subject to death,' living forever

What does the phrase 'Memento mori' mean?

Remember that you will die — a Latin reminder of human mortality whispered to Roman generals

Why is a mortgage called a 'death pledge'?

The pledge 'dies' when the debt is paid off, or the property is forfeited (dies) if the borrower fails to pay
