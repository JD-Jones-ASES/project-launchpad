Chapter 17 — VIT / VIV (Life)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root vit

— from Latin vita, meaning "life"; appears in vital, vitamin, viable

Root viv

— from Latin vivere, meaning "to live"; appears in vivid, revive, survive, vivacious, convivial

Prefix patterns

re- (again) + viv = revive; sur- (beyond) + viv = survive; con- (together) + viv = convivial

Suffix patterns

-al (relating to), -id (having the quality of), -acious (full of), -able (capable of)

Thematic contrast

vit/viv (life) vs. mort/mor (death) — two root families covering the oldest contrast in human language

Important Terms (1/2)

vital

vit (life) + -al — absolutely necessary; essential to life (a vital organ, a vital decision)

vivid

viv (to live) + -id — intensely bright, clear, or lifelike; so energetic it seems alive

revive

re- (again) + viv — to bring back to life or consciousness

survive

sur- (beyond, over) + viv — to continue living after a danger or hardship

viable

vi (life) + -able — capable of living or working successfully

Important Terms (2/2)

vitamin

vit (life) + amine — a nutrient essential for life and health

convivial

con- (together) + viv + -ial — friendly, lively, and enjoyable; literally "living together"

vivacious

viv + -acious (full of) — attractively lively and animated

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the Latin root vit mean?

Life (from Latin vita, meaning 'life')

What does the Latin root viv mean?

To live (from Latin vivere, meaning 'to live')

vital

vit (life) + -al (relating to) — absolutely necessary; essential to life

vivid

viv (to live) + -id (having the quality of) — intensely bright, clear, or lifelike

revive

re- (again) + viv (to live) — to bring back to life or consciousness

survive

sur- (beyond, over) + viv (to live) — to continue living after a danger or hardship

Key Concepts (2/2)

viable

vi (life) + -able (capable of) — capable of living or working successfully

vitamin

vit (life) + amine (chemical compound) — a nutrient essential for life and health

convivial

con- (together) + viv (to live) + -ial (relating to) — friendly, lively, and enjoyable

vivacious

viv (to live) + -acious (full of) — attractively lively and animated

What does the prefix re- mean in the word revive?

Again — so revive means to bring something back to life again

What does the prefix sur- mean in the word survive?

Beyond or over — so survive means to live beyond a danger
