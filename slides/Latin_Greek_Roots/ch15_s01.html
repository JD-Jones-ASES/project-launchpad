Important Terms (1/2)

Epigraph

A short quotation or saying at the beginning of a book or chapter, intended to suggest its theme.

Virgil

(Publius Vergilius Maro) An ancient Roman poet (70–19 BC) renowned for his epic poem the *Aeneid*.

Aeneid

A Latin epic poem written by Virgil between 29 and 19 BC. It tells the legendary story of Aeneas, a Trojan hero who traveled to Italy and became the ancestor of the Romans.

Primo avulso non deficit alter aureus

Latin phrase meaning "When the first is torn away, a second golden [branch] does not fail."

Important Terms (2/2)

Simili frondescit virga metallo

Latin phrase meaning "and its stem sprouts with the same ore/metal."

The Context in Virgil's *Aeneid*

A Key or Passage

It literally opens the way to a profound and dangerous journey.

A Divine Token

It is sacred and imbued with divine power, representing divine favor or a necessary connection to the spiritual realm.

A Test of Destiny

The ease of its plucking signals Aeneas's fated success.

A Symbol of Renewal

Its instant regrowth signifies that the sacred power or resource it represents is not exhausted but perpetually renewed.
