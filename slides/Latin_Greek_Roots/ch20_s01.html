Important Terms (1/2)

Epigraph

A short quotation or saying at the beginning of a book or chapter, intended to suggest its theme.

Fides

Latin for "trust," "faith," "fidelity," or "good faith." In Roman morality, it was a virtue representing reliability and honesty.

Justitiae

Latin for "justice," "equity," or "righteousness."

De Officiis

A philosophical treatise by Cicero, written in 44 BC, discussing moral duties and ethics. The title translates to "On Duties" or "On Moral Obligations."
