Chapter 19 — JUS / JUR / JUDIC (Law, Right, Judge)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root jus / juris

— from Latin jus, juris (law, right); appears in justice, jurisdiction, jurisprudence

Root jur

— from Latin jurare (to swear an oath); appears in jury, perjury

Root judic

— from Latin judex (a judge); built from jus + dicere (to say); appears in judicial, adjudicate, prejudice

Prefix patterns

pre- (before) + judic = prejudice; ad- (toward) + judic = adjudicate; per- (wrongly) + jur = perjury

Key compound

judex = jus (law) + dicere (to say) — a judge is "one who says what the law is"

Important Terms (1/2)

justice

just (right, law) + -ice — fairness; the quality of being just

jury

jur (to swear) — a group of citizens sworn to judge a case fairly

jurisdiction

juris (of law) + dict (to say) + -ion — the authority to declare and apply the law within a territory

judicial

judic (judge) + -ial — relating to courts, judges, or the law

adjudicate

ad- (toward) + judic (judge) + -ate — to make a formal judgment on a dispute

Important Terms (2/2)

perjury

per- (wrongly) + jur (to swear) — the crime of lying under oath

prejudice

pre- (before) + judic (judgment) — an opinion formed before knowing the facts

jurisprudence

juris (of law) + prudentia (wisdom) — the study and philosophy of law
