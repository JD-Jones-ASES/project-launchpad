Unpacking the Latin Root "Vis/Vid" — The Power of Seeing and Knowing

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Roman Understanding of "Seeing"

For Romans, *videre* (to see) was not merely a physical act but a cognitive one, deeply intertwined with understanding, grasping truth, and undeniable proof (*evidentia*).

The Roots "Vis" and "Vid"

These are the primary forms derived from the Latin verb *videre*. "Vis" comes from *visus* (the past participle), while "vid" comes from the verb stem itself.

Connection Between Seeing and Knowing

The core insight of this root is the powerful link between what can be "seen" and what is thereby "known" or "understood." This connection is central to concepts of clarity and truth.

Influence on Vocabulary

The "vis/vid" root has shaped a significant number of English words across various domains, including legal proof, planning, improvement, and supervision, reflecting its original Latin meanings.

Important Terms (1/2)

videre (Latin verb)

The original Latin verb meaning "to see."

visus (Latin past participle)

The past participle of *videre*, meaning "seen." It is the source of the "vis" root.

vis (root)

One form of the Latin root, derived from *visus*, meaning "to see" or "seen."

vid (root)

The other form of the Latin root, derived from the verb stem of *videre*, also meaning "to see."

evidentia (Latin term)

A Roman legal term meaning "proof so clear that it can be seen by everyone," highlighting the connection between sight and truth.

Important Terms (2/2)

Video (English word)

A modern English word directly borrowed from Latin, meaning "I see," referring to technology that allows us to see recorded images.
