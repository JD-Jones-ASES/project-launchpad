Important Terms (1/2)

Root

The basic part of a word that carries the primary meaning. Roots are often derived from ancient languages like Latin or Greek.

`vis`/`vid`

A Latin root meaning "to see."

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and the way their meanings have changed throughout history.

Word Family

A group of words that share a common root and, therefore, often a related core meaning, even if their spellings or specific uses differ.

Communication

The process of conveying information, ideas, or feelings through language or other means. This section highlights the four major modes: speaking, hearing, writing, and seeing.

Introducing the Root `vis`/`vid`: To See (1/3)

`vision`

The ability to see; foresight or imagination. (What you **see** or can **see** in your mind.)

`visible`

Able to be seen. (Something that *can be seen*.)

`invisible`

Not able to be seen. (Something that *cannot be seen*.)

`visual`

Relating to seeing or sight. (Pertaining to what you **see**.)

`vista`

A pleasing view, especially one seen through a long, narrow opening. (A **view** or **seeing** a landscape.)

Introducing the Root `vis`/`vid`: To See (2/3)

`supervise`

To oversee a person or activity. (`super-` means "over," so to literally "**see over**" someone or something.)

`revise`

To re-examine and make corrections or improvements. (`re-` means "again," so to "**see again**" or "look over again.")

`evident`

Clearly visible; obvious. (Something that *can be clearly seen* or is *visible*.)

`evidence`

The available body of facts or information indicating whether a belief or proposition is true or valid. (Information that *allows you to see* the truth.)

`video`

A recording of moving visual images. (Literally, "I **see**" in Latin, referring to the visual content.)

Introducing the Root `vis`/`vid`: To See (3/3)

`television`

An apparatus for receiving and displaying television broadcasts. (`tele-` means "far," so "to **see** from afar.")

`provident`

Making careful provision for the future; thrifty. (`pro-` means "forward," so literally "**seeing forward**" or planning ahead.)

`improvise`

To create and perform spontaneously or without preparation. (`im-` means "not," `pro-` means "forward," so "not **seeing forward**" or not planning ahead.)
