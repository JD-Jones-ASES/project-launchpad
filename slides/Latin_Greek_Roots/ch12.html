Chapter 12 — FER / LAT

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

fer/lat = to carry, to bear, to bring

Suppletive pair

*ferre* (present) and *latus* (past participle) come from two originally unrelated Latin words — linguists call this a suppletive pair

Two form families

fer words (transfer, prefer, fertile, different, suffer) and lat words (translate, relate, legislate)

Prefix patterns

trans- (across), pre- (before), dif-/dis- (apart), suf-/sub- (under), re- (back)

Important Terms (1/2)

transfer

to move something from one place or person to another (trans- + fer)

prefer

to choose one thing over another; to carry it before others (pre- + fer)

fertile

able to bear growth; capable of producing abundant crops or offspring (fer + -tile)

different

not the same; distinct (dif- + fer + -ent: carried apart)

suffer

to bear pain or hardship; to carry a burden (suf-/sub- + fer)

Important Terms (2/2)

translate

to carry meaning across from one language to another (trans- + lat)

relate

to tell or narrate; to show a connection between things (re- + lat)

legislate

to make or enact laws; to carry law (leg- + lat + -ate)

The Fer Family

Transfer

(trans- across): move something across

Prefer

(pre- before): carry one choice before others

Fertile

(able to bear): land or beings that can produce growth

Different

(dif- apart): things that have been carried apart from each other

Suffer

(sub- under): carry a burden beneath yourself

The Lat Family

Translate

(trans- across): carry meaning across languages

Relate

(re- back): carry back; connect or narrate

Legislate

(leg- law): carry law into effect
