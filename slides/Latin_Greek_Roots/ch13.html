Chapter 13 — TERR

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

terr/terra = earth, land, ground (Latin *terra*)

How the root appears

terr appears in territory, terrain, subterranean; terra appears in Mediterranean, terrace, terra firma, terracotta

Key prefix pattern

sub- (under) creates subterranean; medi- (middle) creates Mediterranean; in- (into) creates inter

Connected chapter

Chapter 14 (aqua) also uses the prefix *sub-* in the same pattern: subterranean (under earth) / subaqueous (under water)

Important Terms (1/2)

territory

an area of land under the control of a ruler or state (terr + -itory)

terrain

a stretch of land, especially regarding its physical features (terr + -ain)

subterranean

existing or occurring beneath the earth's surface (sub- + terr + -anean)

Mediterranean

the sea in the middle of the lands (medi- + terr + -anean)

terrestrial

relating to the earth or land, as opposed to water or air (terr + -estrial)

Important Terms (2/2)

terrace

a raised, level area of ground; an outdoor platform (terr + -ace)

inter

to bury a dead body in the earth (in- + terra)

Terracotta and Terra Firma

Terracotta

(Italian *terra cotta* = baked earth): the reddish-brown clay material used for pots and sculptures — literally earth that has been fired

Terra firma

(Latin = firm/solid ground): used to describe the stability of dry land, especially in contrast to the sea
