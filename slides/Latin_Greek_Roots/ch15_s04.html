Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Word Families

A group of words derived from a single base word or root word, often through the addition of prefixes and suffixes, maintaining a core semantic connection.

Morphology

The study of word structure, focusing on how words are formed and their relationships to other words in the same language. Word families are a fundamental aspect of morphology.

Base Words

The simplest form of a word that can stand alone and carry meaning. It serves as the foundation for a word family.

Root Words

A core word part that carries the primary meaning and cannot stand alone as a word. Many English words derive from Latin or Greek roots.

Affixes

Morphemes (meaningful word parts) that are attached to base or root words to create new words or change their grammatical function. These include prefixes and suffixes.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Prefixes

Affixes added to the *beginning* of a base or root word, altering its meaning (e.g., *un-* in *unhappy*).

Suffixes

Affixes added to the *end* of a base or root word, often changing its part of speech or grammatical function (e.g., *-er* in *teacher*).

Important Terms (1/2)

Word Family

A collection of words sharing a common base or root word, often formed by adding affixes, and related in meaning.

Base Word

A complete word that can stand alone and to which prefixes or suffixes can be added (e.g., *play* in *player*).

Root Word

The most basic part of a word that carries the fundamental meaning, often originating from Latin or Greek, and cannot usually stand alone as an English word (e.g., *rupt* in *rupture*, *disrupt*).

Affix

A morpheme (a meaningful unit) attached to a base or root word to modify its meaning or function; includes prefixes and suffixes.

Prefix

An affix placed before a base or root word (e.g., *re-* in *rewrite*).

Important Terms (2/2)

Suffix

An affix placed after a base or root word (e.g., *-able* in *readable*).

Morpheme

The smallest meaningful unit in a language. Words are made up of one or more morphemes.

Derivational Morpheme

An affix that creates a new word or changes the grammatical category (part of speech) of a word (e.g., adding *-ness* to *kind* makes *kindness*, changing an adjective to a noun).

Inflectional Morpheme

An affix that adds grammatical information to a word without changing its essential meaning or part of speech (e.g., adding *-s* to *cat* to make it plural, or *-ed* to *walk* for past tense).

Etymology

The study of the origin and historical development of words. Understanding etymology can reveal shared roots and connections within word families.
