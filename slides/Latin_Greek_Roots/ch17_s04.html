Key Concepts

Word Families

A group of words that share a common root word and often have related meanings. Understanding one word in a family can help decipher others.

Latin and Greek Roots

Many English words are derived from Latin and Greek roots. Learning these fundamental building blocks is crucial for expanding vocabulary.

The Root "vit-"

Derived from the Latin word *vita*, meaning "life." This root forms the core meaning for a family of English words.

Affixes (Prefixes and Suffixes)

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and how their meanings have evolved over time. Understanding etymology provides deeper insight into word usage.

Important Terms (1/2)

Root

The basic part of a word that carries its primary meaning. It cannot be broken down further without losing its core sense.

Prefix

An affix placed at the beginning of a root word to modify its meaning.

Suffix

An affix placed at the end of a root word to modify its meaning or change its grammatical part of speech.

Word Family

A collection of words sharing a common root, often stemming from the same historical origin and bearing related meanings.

Etymology

The historical derivation of a word as shown by the analysis of its elements and earlier forms, and by tracing its development and relationships to other words.
