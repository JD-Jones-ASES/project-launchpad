Chapter 22 — GEO / GRAPH (Earth; To Write, To Draw)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root geo

— from Greek ge (earth); appears in geography, geology, geometry; produces scientific vocabulary about the Earth

Root graph / gram

— from Greek graphein (to write, to draw); appears in photograph, diagram, telegram, autograph, graphic

Distinction

-graphy = the process of writing/recording (geography, photography); -gram = something written or drawn (diagram, telegram)

Prefix patterns

auto- (self) + graph = autograph; tele- (far) + gram = telegram; dia- (across) + gram = diagram; photos (light) + graph = photograph

Greek vs. Latin

geo (scientific earth vocabulary) vs. Latin terr (everyday land vocabulary)

Important Terms (1/2)

geography

geo (earth) + -graphy (writing) — the study of Earth's physical features and peoples

geology

geo (earth) + -logy (study of) — the study of Earth's rocks, minerals, and structure

geometry

geo (earth) + metron (measure) — the branch of mathematics studying shapes and space

photograph

photos (light) + graph (to write) — an image created by capturing light

diagram

dia- (across) + gram (drawn) — a drawing that explains how something works

Important Terms (2/2)

telegram

tele- (far) + gram (written) — a message sent over great distances

graphic

graph (to write, draw) + -ic — relating to visual art, drawing, or vivid description

autograph

auto- (self) + graph (to write) — a person's own signature
