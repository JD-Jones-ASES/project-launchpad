Chapter 16, Section 2

The Story of *tempor*: Understanding Time-Related Words

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Latin Root *tempus

*: The foundation of many English words, *tempus* originally meant "time," and its stem *temporis* gives rise to a family of words concerning when, how long, and how fast things occur.

Temporal Duration and Permanence

Words like "temporary" directly reflect a limited period of time, emphasizing impermanence, contrasting with concepts of lasting or eternal.

Simultaneity and Shared Eras

"Contemporary" illustrates how prefixes (like *con-*) can modify the root to signify "with time" or "at the same time," referring to people or events sharing the same historical period.

Time vs. Eternity/Spirituality

The term "temporal" highlights a philosophical distinction between the earthly, passing world (related to time) and the eternal or spiritual realm, shaping Western thought.

Action "Out of Time"

"Extemporaneous" signifies actions performed "out of the time" in the sense of being unprepared or spontaneous, done in the moment without prior planning.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Evolution of Meaning

The word "tempest" provides a compelling example of semantic shift, evolving from "a period of time" or "a season" to specifically mean a "violent storm" due to weather's defining role in seasons.

Measuring Time in Music

The musical term "tempo" is a direct descendant, borrowed from Italian, emphasizing how the root *tempus* is used to control and measure the flow of time, even in artistic contexts.

Important Terms (1/2)

Tempus

(Latin) The original word meaning "time."

Temporis

(Latin) The genitive stem of *tempus*, from which many English words are derived.

Temporary

Existing or lasting for only a limited period of time; not permanent.

Contemporary

Temporal

Important Terms (2/2)

Extemporaneous

Spoken or done without preparation; improvised. (Literally "out of the time").

Tempest

A violent windy storm. (Originally meant "a period of time" or "a season," evolving due to the character of weather within seasons).

Tempo

(Musical term) The speed at which a passage of music is or should be played. (From Italian, directly from *tempus*).
