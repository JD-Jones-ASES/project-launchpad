Important Terms (1/2)

Epigraph

A brief quotation, saying, or poem placed at the beginning of a literary work or one of its divisions to suggest its theme.

Leges bonae

Latin for "good laws." This refers to effective, just, and beneficial legislation that serves the public interest and promotes societal well-being.

Mali mores

Latin for "bad customs" or "evil habits." This encompasses societal practices, behaviors, or norms that are detrimental, unethical, or cause harm and disorder within a community.

Macrobius (Ambrosius Theodosius Macrobius)

A Roman provincial official and Neoplatonist philosopher who lived around the turn of the 5th century CE. He is known for his philosophical and encyclopedic works.

"Leges bonae" (Good laws)

This refers to laws that are effective, just, and contribute positively to society. They are laws that resolve conflicts, protect rights, promote order, and foster communal harmony.
