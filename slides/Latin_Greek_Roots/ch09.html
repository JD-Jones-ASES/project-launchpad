Chapter 9 — PORT

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

port = to carry (Latin *portare*)

How the root appears

port shows up consistently across its English derivatives (portable, transport, export, import, deport, report, portal, portfolio)

Prefix patterns

Each derivative is shaped by a prefix that modifies the direction or nature of the carrying: ex- (out), im- (in), trans- (across), re- (back), de- (away)

Important Terms (1/2)

portable

able to be carried or moved easily (port + -able)

export

to send goods out to another country for sale (ex- + port)

import

to bring goods into a country from abroad (im- + port)

transport

to carry or move from one place to another (trans- + port)

deport

to expel someone from a country; to carry away (de- + port)

Important Terms (2/2)

report

to give an account of something; to carry back information (re- + port)

portal

a doorway or gate; an entry point (port + -al)

portfolio

a case for carrying documents; a collection of work (port + folium, leaf/sheet)

How the Prefix Changes the Meaning

ex-port

carry *out* to other countries

im-port

carry *in* from other countries

trans-port

carry *across* distances

de-port

carry *away* (in disgrace or expulsion)

re-port

carry *back* information to an audience
