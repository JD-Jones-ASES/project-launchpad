Important Terms (1/2)

_ferre_

The Latin verb meaning "to carry" or "to bear." It is notable for its irregular conjugation.

_latus_

A Latin past participle root meaning "carried" or "borne." It serves as the past tense form for _ferre_, despite having a different etymological origin.

Suppletive Pair

A linguistic term describing two or more forms of a word (often a verb) that are grammatically related but derive from different, historically unrelated roots.

Root

The basic form of a word from which other words are derived through the addition of prefixes, suffixes, or other grammatical changes.

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and the way in which their meanings have changed throughout history.

Important Terms (2/2)

Prefix

An affix placed before the root of a word (e.g., _trans-_, _pre-_, _dis-_).

Words Derived from the _fer-_ Root (from _ferre_):

_transfer_

Formed from the prefix _trans-_ (meaning "across") and _ferre_ (to carry). Therefore, _transfer_ means "to carry across," such as moving something from one place to another.

_prefer_

Composed of _pre-_ (meaning "before") and _ferre_ (to carry). To _prefer_ something literally means "to carry [it] before" others, indicating a choice or favoritism.

_fertile_

Derived directly from _ferre_ in the sense of "to bear" or "to produce." A _fertile_ thing or being is "able to bear" fruit, offspring, or ideas.

_suffer_

A combination of _sub-_ (an older form of _sub-_ meaning "under") and _ferre_ (to carry). To _suffer_ means "to carry under" a burden, typically one of pain, hardship, or distress.

Words Derived from the _lat-_ Root (from _latus_):

_translate_

Formed from _trans-_ (meaning "across") and _latus_ (carried). To _translate_ means "to carry across" meaning or words from one language to another.

_relate_

Composed of _re-_ (meaning "back" or "again") and _latus_ (carried). To _relate_ means "to carry back" or connect one thing to another, showing a connection or resemblance.

_legislate_

Derived from _lex_ (Latin for "law") and _latus_ (carried/brought). To _legislate_ means "to carry law" or "to bring law into being," referring to the process of making laws.

Suppletive Pairs in English

_go_ and _went_

The past tense of "go" is "went," which originally came from the Old English verb _wend_ (to turn, to proceed). These are clearly two distinct roots that merged to form one verb.

_good_ and _better_

The comparative form of "good" is "better," stemming from a completely different Germanic root.
