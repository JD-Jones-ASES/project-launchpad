Understanding the Latin Root "leg" / "lect"

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root Forms

The primary forms of this root are `leg` and `lect`, which stem from Latin.

Multiple Meanings

The root carries three main semantic branches: "to read," "to choose/gather," and "law," each derived from different Latin sources.

Latin Origin

The root's meanings trace back to the Latin verb *legere* (to read, choose, gather) and its past participle *lectus*, as well as the Latin noun *lex, legis* (law).

Variant Forms

Roots can sometimes appear in slightly altered forms within English derivatives; for instance, `lig` is a variant of `leg`.

Derivative Words

Many English words are formed by combining this root with prefixes or suffixes, carrying the core meaning of the root into new contexts.

Important Terms (1/2)

Root

The basic part of a word that carries the primary meaning and cannot be broken down further.

Derivative

A word formed from another word or root, especially by the addition of a prefix or suffix.

Variant Form

A slightly altered spelling or pronunciation of a root that retains the same core meaning (e.g., `leg` becoming `lig`).

Latin *legere*

The Latin verb meaning "to read," "to choose," or "to gather." It is a primary source for the English root `leg`/`lect`.

Latin *lectus*

The past participle form of *legere*. This form often gives rise to the `lect` variant of the root in English derivatives.

Important Terms (2/2)

Latin *lex, legis*

The Latin noun meaning "law." This is the source of the "law" meaning associated with the `leg` root.

Etymology

The study of the origin of words and the way in which their meanings have changed throughout history. (Though not explicitly listed as a term, this section deals with etymological concepts).

Detailed Notes (1/2)

From Latin *legere* (to read, choose, gather)

From Latin *lex, legis* (law)

Legible

Directly from the "to read" meaning of *legere*. Something that is `legible` can be `read`.

Select

From the "to choose/gather" meaning of *legere* (specifically *selectus*, past participle of *seligere*, "to choose out"). To `select` is to `choose` out of a group.

Legal

Directly from *lex, legis* (law). Something `legal` is related to `law` or is permitted by `law`.

Detailed Notes (2/2)

Elect

Similar to `select`, from *eligere* (to choose out). To `elect` someone is to `choose` them by vote.
