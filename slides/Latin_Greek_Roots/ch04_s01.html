Important Terms (1/2)

Epigraph

A short quotation or saying at the beginning of a book, chapter, or section, intended to suggest its theme.

Proverb

A short, popular saying that expresses a common truth or belief.

Latin

An ancient Italic language spoken in the Roman Republic and Empire, which heavily influenced many modern European languages, including English.

"Verba volant, scripta manent"

A Latin proverb meaning "Spoken words fly away, written words remain."

Oral Tradition

The transmission of cultural material and traditions from one generation to another verbally, rather than in writing.

Important Terms (2/2)

Written Tradition

The preservation and transmission of knowledge, culture, and history through written texts and documents.

Permanence

The state or quality of lasting or remaining unchanged indefinitely; immutability.

Ephemeral

Lasting for a very short time; transient.

The Function of an Epigraph

Set the Tone

Establish the mood or atmosphere of the chapter (e.g., serious, reflective, philosophical).

Foreshadow Themes

Hint at the central ideas or conflicts that will be explored.

Provide Context

Offer a conceptual framework or a philosophical underpinning for the discussions to follow.

Spark Thought

Encourage readers to ponder a particular idea before delving into the detailed text.
