Important Terms (1/3)

Word Family

A collection of words derived from the same base or root word, often sharing semantic connections.

Root Word

The core morpheme of a word that imparts the primary meaning; it cannot be broken down into smaller meaningful units.

Etymology

The branch of linguistics concerned with the origin and historical development of words, providing insight into their current meanings.

Prefix

A morpheme added to the beginning of a word to alter its meaning (e.g., *ex-* in *extraterrestrial*).

Suffix

A morpheme added to the end of a word to change its grammatical function or meaning (e.g., *-ible* in *terrible*).

Important Terms (2/3)

'terr' (from *terra*)

A root meaning 'earth' or 'land,' found in words like *terrain* and *terrestrial*.

'terr' (from *terrere*)

A root meaning 'to frighten' or 'to cause terror,' found in words like *terror* and *terrify*.

Terrestrial

Relating to the Earth or to land; inhabiting or existing on land (as opposed to air or water).

Territory

An area of land under the jurisdiction of a ruler or state; also, an area defended by an animal or group.

Terror

Extreme fear; a state of intense apprehension and dread.
