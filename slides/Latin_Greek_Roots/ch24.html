Chapter 24 — PSYCH / PATH / HYDR / THERM (Mind; Feeling; Water; Heat)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Root psych

— from Greek psyche (soul, mind, breath of life); appears in psychology, psyche

Root path

— from Greek pathos (feeling, suffering); appears in empathy, sympathy, apathy, pathology

Root hydr

— from Greek hydor (water); appears in hydrogen, hydrant, dehydrate

Root therm

— from Greek therme (heat); appears in thermal, thermostat, thermometer

Prefix patterns with path

em- (in) + path = empathy; sym- (with) + path = sympathy; a- (without) + path = apathy

Key Concepts (2/2)

Greek vs. Latin parallels

path vs. sens/sent (feeling); hydr vs. aqua (water)

Important Terms (1/3)

psychology

psych (mind) + -logy (study of) — the scientific study of the mind and behavior

psyche

from Greek psyche — the human soul, mind, or spirit

empathy

em- (in) + path (feeling) — the ability to feel into another person's experience

sympathy

sym- (with, together) + path (feeling) — feelings of pity and concern for another's suffering

apathy

a- (without) + path (feeling) — a complete absence of feeling or interest; indifference

Important Terms (2/3)

pathology

path (suffering, disease) + -logy (study of) — the study of the causes and effects of diseases

hydrogen

hydr (water) + genes (producing) — an element that produces water when burned

hydrant

hydr (water) + -ant — an outdoor water pipe for firefighting

thermal

therm (heat) + -al — relating to heat or warmth

thermostat

therm (heat) + statos (standing) — a device that regulates temperature automatically

Important Terms (3/3)

thermometer

therm (heat) + metron (measure) — an instrument for measuring temperature
