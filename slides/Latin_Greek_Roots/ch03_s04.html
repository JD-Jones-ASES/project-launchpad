The "aud" Word Family

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Latin Root "aud"

The fundamental building block for this word family, meaning "to hear" or "sound."

Word Families

A group of words that share a common root and often a similar core meaning, despite variations in prefixes, suffixes, or usage.

Morphemes

The smallest meaningful units in a language. Roots like "aud" are morphemes, as are prefixes (e.g., *in-*), and suffixes (e.g., *-ible*, *-ence*).

Vocabulary Building

Learning common roots significantly expands vocabulary by enabling deduction of meaning for unfamiliar words.

Contextual Understanding

The root "aud" consistently points to an idea of hearing or sound, helping to infer the meaning of words even without prior knowledge.

Important Terms (1/2)

aud

(Latin root) Meaning "to hear" or "sound."

Audible

(adjective) Capable of being heard; loud enough to be heard.

Audience

(noun) A group of listeners or spectators at a public event.

Audio

(adjective/noun) Relating to sound or its reproduction; sound, especially when recorded or broadcast.

Audition

(noun/verb) A trial performance to assess a performer's abilities, often in singing, acting, or music; to perform in an audition.

Important Terms (2/2)

Auditorium

(noun) The part of a theater, concert hall, or other public building in which the audience sits.

Inaudible

(adjective) Incapable of being heard; so faint as to be impossible to hear.

Auditory

(adjective) Relating to the sense of hearing.

Audiology

(noun) The branch of science concerned with the sense of hearing, especially the identification and rehabilitation of hearing impairment.

Audiologist

(noun) A healthcare professional specializing in the identification, diagnosis, treatment, and monitoring of disorders of the auditory and vestibular systems.
