Understanding the Root 'Cogn' and the Concept of Knowing

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Latin Root `cogn`

This root, derived from Latin, is fundamental to English words describing mental processes, awareness, and thinking. It directly translates to "to know," "to think," or "to be aware."

The Greek Roots `gnosc` and `gnos`

These Greek roots carry a meaning parallel to `cogn` – "to know" or "knowledge." They are commonly found in more specialized, particularly scientific or medical, terminology in English.

Important Terms (1/2)

`cogn` (Latin root)

Meaning "to know," "to think," or "to be aware." Forms words like *cognition*, *recognize*.

`gnosc`/`gnos` (Greek roots)

Meaning "to know" or "knowledge." Forms words like *diagnose*, *prognosis*.

`vis`/`vid` (Latin roots)

Meaning "to see." Forms words like *visual*, *video*.

`sens`/`sent` (Latin roots)

Meaning "to feel" or "to perceive." Forms words like *sense*, *sentient*.

Cognition

The mental action or process of acquiring knowledge and understanding through thought, experience, and the senses.

Important Terms (2/2)

Recognize

To identify someone or something from having encountered them before; to know again.

Diagnose

To identify the nature of an illness or other problem by examination of the symptoms.

Prognosis

The likely course of a disease or ailment; a forecast of the likely outcome of a situation.

Perception

The ability to see, hear, or become aware of something through the senses; the way in which something is regarded, understood, or interpreted.
