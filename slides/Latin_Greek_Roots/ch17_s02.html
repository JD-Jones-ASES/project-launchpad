Chapter 17, Section 2 - Narrative: The Root Story of Life (`vit` and `viv`)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Latin Roots of Life

The foundational roots **vita** (life) and **vivere** (to live) from Latin are the source of a large family of English words.

Roman Appreciation for Life

The Romans deeply valued life, expressing this through poetry, philosophy, and language, especially in their debates about different ways of living.

Philosophical Dimensions of Life

Energy and Renewal from `vivere`

Many words derived from the root **viv** convey a sense of vibrancy, intensity, bringing things back to life, or continuing to live in the face of challenges.

Modern Scientific Application

The word **vitamin** illustrates how ancient roots can underpin modern scientific terminology, describing substances "vital" for life.

Key Concepts (2/2)

The Duality of Life and Death

The roots **vit** and **viv** are conceptually linked to and often contrasted with roots related to death (**mort** and **mor**), representing a fundamental human linguistic opposition.

Important Terms (1/3)

`vita` (Latin)

The Latin word meaning "life."

`vivere` (Latin)

The Latin word meaning "to live."

`vital`

Essential or necessary for life; extremely important.

`vita activa`

A Latin phrase referring to the "active life" of public service and civic engagement, as contrasted with a life of contemplation.

`vita contemplativa`

A Latin phrase referring to the "contemplative life" dedicated to study, reflection, and intellectual pursuits.

Important Terms (2/3)

`vivid`

Producing powerful feelings or strong, clear images in the mind; intensely bright or colorful, seeming alive.

`vivacious`

(Especially of a woman) Attractively lively and animated; full of spirit.

`revive`

To restore to life or consciousness; to give new strength or energy to.

`survive`

To continue to live or exist, especially in spite of danger or hardship; to remain alive after an event.

`vitamin`

Any of a group of organic compounds which are essential for normal growth and nutrition and are required in small quantities in the diet because they cannot be synthesized by the body.

Important Terms (3/3)

`amine`

A type of organic chemical compound (part of the word "vitamin").

`convivial`

(Of an atmosphere or event) Friendly, lively, and enjoyable; (of a person) fond of good company and feasting.

`convivium` (Latin)

A Latin word meaning "a feast" or "living together."

Words from `vivere`: Overflowing with Energy and Renewal

`Vivid`

When something is described as **vivid**, it's so bright, intense, and striking that it almost seems to possess a life of its own. Think of a **vivid** dream, **vivid** colors, or a **vivid** memory.

`Vivacious`

A **vivacious** person is characterized by an abundance of liveliness, spirit, and animation, practically overflowing with enthusiasm and energy.
