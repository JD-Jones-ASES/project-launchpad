Key Concepts (1/2)

Greek Roots and Scientific Vocabulary

The Greek language is a foundational source for a vast array of scientific, medical, and technical terms in English, exemplified by `psyche`, `pathos`, `hydr`, and `therm`.

Evolution of Root Meanings

Many Greek roots, like `psyche`, evolved in meaning over time, transitioning from concrete observations (like "breath") to abstract concepts ("mind" or "soul").

`Psyche` (Mind/Soul)

Originally meaning "breath," this root developed to signify the soul and eventually the mind, encompassing one's inner mental and emotional life.

`Pathos` (Feeling/Suffering)

This root relates to emotions and suffering, forming the basis for words describing human feelings, empathy, and the study of disease.

`Hydr` (Water)

Derived from the Greek word for water, this root is central to vocabulary concerning water systems, chemistry, and engineering.

Key Concepts (2/2)

`Therm` (Heat)

Originating from the Greek word for heat, this root is used in terms related to measuring, regulating, and retaining heat.

Important Terms

`psyche`

(Greek root) Originally "breath," then "soul," now "mind" or "inner mental and emotional life."

`pathos`

(Greek root) "Feeling" or "suffering."

`hydr`

(Greek root, from `hydor`) "Water."

`therm`

(Greek root, from `therme`) "Heat."

`Pathos`: Feeling and Suffering

Empathy

combines `en-` (in) with `pathos`, meaning to feel "into" another person's experience, deeply understanding and sharing their emotions.

Sympathy

combines `syn-` (together) with `pathos`, signifying feeling "with" someone, often expressing pity or sorrow for their situation.

Pathology

is the study of disease (`-logy`), which literally translates to the "study of suffering," reflecting the root's connection to physical and mental affliction.

`Hydr`: The Essence of Water

Hydrogen

, a fundamental chemical element, is so named because it "generates water" (`genes`, born) when it burns, forming H2O.

`Therm`: The Power of Heat

Thermal

clothing is specifically designed to hold in your body's heat, indicating its function related to temperature.
