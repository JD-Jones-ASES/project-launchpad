Chapter 20 — CRED (To Believe, To Trust)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root cred

— from Latin credere (to believe, to trust, to entrust); the foundation of English words about belief and trust

Prefix patterns

in- (not) + cred = incredible; dis- (away) + cred = discredit; ac- (toward) + cred = accredit

Suffix patterns

-ible (able to be), -ulous (tending to), -ential (relating to), -it (past participle)

Thematic connection

cred (to believe) connects to jus/jur (law) — legal systems depend on trustworthy witnesses

The spectrum

credible (worthy of trust) → incredible (beyond trust) → credulous (trusting too easily)

Important Terms (1/2)

credible

cred + -ible — able to be believed; convincing

credential

cred + -ential — a document proving qualifications or trustworthiness

credit

from credere — trust given for future payment; recognition for work

creed

from credo ("I believe") — a statement of beliefs or principles

discredit

dis- + cred + -it — to damage the reputation or trust in someone

Important Terms (2/2)

accredit

ac- + cred + -it — to officially recognize as meeting standards

incredible

in- + cred + -ible — originally not believable; now commonly used to mean extraordinary

credulous

cred + -ulous — too willing to believe; gullible
