Chapter 11, Section 2

Understanding the Latin Root `ced` and Its English Family

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Dual Meaning of `cedere`

The Latin verb *cedere* simultaneously means "to go" or "to move" and "to yield" or "to give way." This duality is central to understanding its English derivatives.

Reflection of Roman Politics

The dual meaning of *cedere* mirrored the constant shifts in Roman politics, encompassing both the advancement and retreat of power, influence, and arguments.

Prefixes as Meaning Modifiers

English words derived directly from *cedere* often use prefixes (e.g., *pro-*, *re-*, *con-*, *ex-*) to specify the direction or nature of the "going" or "yielding."

The Past Participle `cessus`

The past participle form, *cessus*, forms a separate but related branch of English words, often implying a "going toward" or "going forward."

Language in Motion

The study of the root *ced* serves as an example of how language itself is dynamic, constantly evolving and moving, much like life itself.

Important Terms (1/2)

cedere

(Latin verb) Meaning "to go," "to move," "to yield," or "to give way." The core root discussed in the section.

proceed

(English verb) To "go forward" (from *pro-* meaning "forward" + *cedere*).

recede

(English verb) To "go back" or pull away (from *re-* meaning "back" + *cedere*).

concede

(English verb) To "yield completely" or admit that an opponent is right (from *con-* meaning "together," here intensifying + *cedere*).

exceed

(English verb) To "go beyond" a limit or surpass expectations (from *ex-* meaning "out of" or "beyond" + *cedere*).

Important Terms (2/2)

cessus

(Latin past participle) The form of *cedere* that gives rise to another set of English words.

access

(English noun/verb) "A going toward"; a way to approach or enter (from *ad-* meaning "to" or "toward" + *cessus*).

process

(English noun/verb) "A going forward"; a series of steps moving toward a result (from *pro-* meaning "forward" + *cessus*).

predecessor

(English noun) Someone who "went before"; the person who held a position before the current occupant (from *prae-* meaning "before" + *de-* meaning "away" + *cessus*).
