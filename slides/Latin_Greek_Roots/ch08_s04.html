Important Terms

`leg/lect`

A Latin root meaning "to read," "to choose," or "to gather." It refers to the action of understanding or selecting information from written material.

`legere`

The Latin verb from which `leg/lect` is derived, specifically meaning "to gather." This original meaning helps to link "to read" (gathering information) and "to choose" (gathering specific items).

`scrib/script`

A Latin root (introduced in Chapter 4) meaning "to write." It refers to the act of forming letters, words, or texts.

Root

The fundamental part of a word that carries its primary meaning. Understanding roots helps in deciphering the meanings of many related words.

Word Family

A group of words that share a common root and often have related meanings, even if their spellings vary slightly.

Understanding the Root `leg/lect`

To Read

When you read, you are *gathering* information, meaning, and understanding from the written words on a page. The act of decoding symbols to construct comprehension is a form of gathering.

To Choose

Similarly, when you choose something, you are *gathering* specific items or options from a larger set. You are selecting or picking out what is desired.

The Connection to `scrib/script`

`scrib/script` (to write)

This root represents the *encoding* process. It is the act of a "scribe" or writer who physically forms letters, words, and sentences to convey a message. It is the creation of the written artifact.

`leg/lect` (to read, choose, gather)

This root represents the *decoding* process. It is the act of the "reader" who interprets, understands, and "gathers" the meaning that the writer encoded.
