Chapter 8 — LEG/LECT (To Read, Choose, and Gather)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning and origin

"leg" comes from Latin *legere* (to read, choose, gather); "lect" comes from the past participle *lectus*; "lig" appears as a variant in some derivatives

How the root appears

As "leg" in words like legible and legal; as "lect" in words like lecture, select, elect, neglect, collect; in the noun *lex* (law) underlying *legal*

Important morpheme patterns

Prefixes direct the gathering/choosing action — *se-* (apart), *e-* (out), *col-/con-* (together), *neg-* (not)

Important Terms (1/2)

legible

able to be read; clear enough to gather meaning from (*leg* + *-ible*)

lecture

a talk or presentation; originally a reading aloud (*lect* + *-ure*)

select

to choose carefully from a group (*se-* + *lect*)

legal

relating to the law (*leg* [law] + *-al*)

elect

to choose by voting (*e-* + *lect*)

Important Terms (2/2)

neglect

to fail to care for; to not pick up or attend to (*neg-* + *lect*)

collect

to bring together; to gather items into a group (*col-* + *lect*)

legend

a traditional story; originally Latin for "things to be read" (*leg* + *-end*)

Word Family Analysis (1/2)

select

(*se-* = apart): choose apart from others; single out carefully

elect

(*e-* = out): choose out by voting; pick someone to represent the group

neglect

(*neg-* = not): NOT choose; fail to pick up or attend to

collect

(*col-/con-* = together): gather together into one place

lecture

(*-ure* = act of): the act of gathering and presenting knowledge aloud

Word Family Analysis (2/2)

legible

able to be read (the handwriting can be gathered by the eye)

legend

things to be read — stories gathered and passed down

The Legal Connection: Lex and Leg

legal

relating to law (from *lex*)

illegal

not legal (not according to chosen rules)
