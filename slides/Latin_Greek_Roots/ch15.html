Chapter 15 — LUC / LUM / LUS

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning

luc/lum/lus = light (from Latin *lux*, *lumen*, and *lustrare*)

Three variant forms

luc (from *lux*), lumin (from *lumen*, a lamp), lus/lustr (from *lustrare*, to shine/purify)

Physical and metaphorical light

These roots describe both literal light (a glowing lamp) and intellectual clarity (a lucid explanation, an illuminating example)

Cross-chapter connection

Lucifer = luci (light) + fer (carry, Chapter 12) — the morning star that "carries light"

Important Terms (1/2)

illuminate

to light up; to make clear or easier to understand (il- + lumin + -ate)

translucent

allowing light through but not clear vision — like frosted glass (trans- + luc + -ent)

lucid

bright and clear; easy to understand (luc + -id)

luminous

giving off its own light; glowing (lumin + -ous)

illustrate

to explain using pictures or examples; originally to "light up" a text (il- + lus + -trate)

Important Terms (2/2)

elucidate

to explain fully; to bring out of confusion into clarity (e- + lucid + -ate)

luster

a gentle shine or glow on a polished surface (lus + -ter)

Lucifer

the Roman name for the morning star (Venus) — literally "light-bearer" (luci + fer)

Three Latin Words for Light

Lux

pure light, brightness — *lucidus* (clear, bright) → **lucid**, **translucent**, **elucidate**

Lumen

a lamp or source of light — → **luminous**, **illuminate**

Lustrare

to purify or make shine — → **luster**, **illustrate**

Translucent vs. Transparent

Translucent

(trans- through + luc light): light passes through but you cannot see clearly — frosted glass

Transparent

light and vision both pass through — clear glass
