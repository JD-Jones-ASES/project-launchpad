Word Family - Cred

Key Concepts

The Root `cred`

The fundamental meaning of the Latin root `cred` is "to believe" or "to trust." This core meaning is central to all words in its family.

Vocabulary Expansion

Recognizing the root `cred` allows you to infer the meaning of unfamiliar words that contain it, even if you haven't encountered them before.

Words About Being Believed or Believable

Credible (adj.)

This word directly relates to the ability to be believed. If a story is "credible," it means it is plausible and trustworthy. A "credible witness" is one whose testimony can be believed.

Credibility (n.)

This is the noun form of credible, referring to the quality of being believable or trustworthy. Building one's credibility is essential in many professions, such as law or journalism.

Credence (n.)

This term refers to the belief in or acceptance of something as true. To "give credence" to a rumor means to believe it.
