Self-Development and Growth

The core idea is that the human mind or self is not merely shaped by external forces but possesses an internal drive and mechanism for self-improvement, learning, and evolving understanding.

Important Terms (1/2)

Psyche

Logos

Auxon

From the Greek verb "auxanein," meaning "to grow," "to increase," or "to augment." It implies an active process of expansion and development.

Heraclitus (c. 535 – c. 475 BCE)

A pre-Socratic Greek philosopher from Ephesus known for his doctrine of change being central to the universe and for the concept of the "Logos" as an ordering principle.

Epigraph

A short quotation or saying at the beginning of a book or chapter, intended to suggest its theme.

Important Terms (2/2)

Self-Actualization

A concept in humanistic psychology (Maslow, Rogers) describing the fulfillment of one's full potential, an inherent drive towards growth and becoming the best version of oneself.

Cognitive Development

The field of study concerned with how our mental processes, such as perception, memory, thinking, and problem-solving, develop over the lifespan.
