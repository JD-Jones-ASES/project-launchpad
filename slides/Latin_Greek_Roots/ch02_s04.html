Root/Base Word

The fundamental part of a word that carries its primary meaning and cannot be broken down further without losing that meaning. It's the core around which a word family is built.

Affixes

Morphemes (meaningful units of language) that are attached to a root word to create new words or change a word's grammatical function.

Derivational Morphology

The process of forming new words by adding affixes to a root word, often changing the word's grammatical category (e.g., from a verb to a noun, like *teach* to *teacher*).

Important Terms (1/2)

Word Family

A collection of words that share a common root and are related through prefixes, suffixes, or inflectional endings.

Root Word (or Base Word)

The core part of a word that provides its fundamental meaning, to which affixes can be added.

Morpheme

The smallest meaningful unit in a language. Roots and affixes are types of morphemes.

Affix

A morpheme that is attached to a root word to form a new word or modify its meaning/function.

Prefix

An affix placed before a root word (e.g., *re-*, *un-*).

Important Terms (2/2)

Suffix

An affix placed after a root word (e.g., *-ing*, *-able*, *-tion*).

Derivation

The linguistic process of forming a new word from an existing word, often resulting in a change of word class (e.g., noun from verb).

Inflection

The modification of a word to express different grammatical categories such as tense, mood, voice, aspect, person, number, gender, or case, without changing its part of speech.

Etymology

The study of the origin and historical development of words.

`dis-`

Vocabulary Acquisition

Reading Comprehension

When encountering an unfamiliar word, breaking it down into its root, prefix, and suffix can help unlock its meaning, improving overall text comprehension.

Spelling

Word families can provide clues for spelling. Words within the same family often share common spelling patterns related to their root.

Etymology

Word families often reveal the historical development of words, tracing them back to their Latin, Greek, or Old English origins.
