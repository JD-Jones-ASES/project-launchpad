Chapter 5 — VIS/VID (To See)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Root meaning and origin

"vis" comes from the Latin past participle *visus*; "vid" comes from the verb stem *videre* (to see)

How the root appears

As "vis" in words like visible, vision, visual, supervise, revise; as "vid" in words like evident, video, provide

Important morpheme patterns

Prefixes create directional meanings — *super-* (over), *re-* (again), *pro-* (ahead), *e-* (out)

Important Terms (1/2)

visible

able to be seen (*vis* + *-ible*)

vision

the ability to see; a mental image of the future (*vis* + *-ion*)

visual

relating to sight or seeing (*vis* + *-ual*)

evident

clearly seen; obvious; standing out so plainly it cannot be denied (*e-* + *vid* + *-ent*)

video

a recording of moving images; Latin for "I see" (*vid* + *-eo*)

Important Terms (2/2)

supervise

to watch over and direct the work of others (*super-* + *vis*)

revise

to look at again in order to correct or improve (*re-* + *vis*)

provide

to see ahead and prepare; to supply what is needed (*pro-* + *vid*)

Two Root Forms

vis

from the past participle *visus* — appears in *visible*, *vision*, *visual*, *supervise*, *revise*

vid

from the verb stem *videre* — appears in *evident*, *video*, *provide*

Word Family Analysis

super-

(over/above): *supervise* = to see over; to watch from above and direct

re-

(again): *revise* = to see again; to look at one's work a second time to improve it

pro-

(ahead/before): *provide* = to see ahead; to foresee needs and prepare for them

e-

(out): *evident* = seen out clearly; so visible it stands out without question
