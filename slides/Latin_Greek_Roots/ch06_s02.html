Important Terms (1/3)

*sentire

*: (Latin verb) "to feel," "to perceive," and "to form a judgment/opinion."

*sensus

*: (Latin past participle of *sentire*) The source of the `sens` root, particularly relating to physical perception.

Sensory

Relating to the physical senses (sight, hearing, touch, taste, smell).

Sensitive

Capable of feeling or perceiving things deeply, whether physical touch or emotional experiences.

Sentiment

A feeling or opinion, often an attitude toward a situation or idea.

Important Terms (2/3)

Consent

To "feel together" (con- + *sentire*); to agree to something, sharing the same judgment or opinion.

Dissent

To "feel apart" (dis- + *sentire*); to differ in opinion or refuse to agree.

Assent

To "feel toward" (ad-/*as-* + *sentire*); to express agreement, often by nodding or indicating approval.

*sententia

*: (Latin noun) "a considered opinion"; the direct origin of the English word "sentence."

Sentence (grammatical)

A complete expression of a thought; a finished piece of meaning that is fully "felt out" or considered.

Important Terms (3/3)

Sentence (judicial)

The judgment or punishment delivered by a judge after weighing the facts of a case; the judge's considered opinion.
