Understanding the Latin Root `ced/cede/cess`

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Core Meaning

The primary meanings of the root `ced/cede/cess` are "to go" or "to yield." It also encompasses "to withdraw" from its original Latin form.

Latin Origin

The root stems from the Latin verb `cedere`, which means "to go, to yield, to withdraw."

Word Building

Combining this root with various prefixes (like `pro-`, `re-`, `con-`, `ad-`) creates a wide array of English words with specific meanings related to movement or concession.

Important Terms (1/2)

Root

The basic element of a word that carries the primary meaning, to which prefixes and suffixes can be added.

`ced/cede/cess`

The specific Latin root discussed in this section, meaning "to go" or "to yield."

`cedere`

The original Latin verb from which the root is derived, meaning "to go, to yield, to withdraw."

`cessus`

The past participle of `cedere`, which gives rise to the `cess` variant of the root in English.

Variant Forms

Different spellings or manifestations of the same root that arise due to historical linguistic changes or grammatical functions.

Important Terms (2/2)

Derivatives

Words formed by adding prefixes, suffixes, or other roots to a base root.

Compound Words

Words formed from two or more existing words or roots.

Example Derivatives and Their Meanings (1/2)

`proceed`

(pro- "forward" + `ced` "to go"): To move forward; to begin or continue a course of action.

`recede`

(re- "back" + `cede` "to go"): To go or move back or further away from a previous position; to gradually diminish.

`concede`

(con- "thoroughly" + `cede` "to yield"): To admit that something is true or valid after first denying or resisting it; to surrender or grant.

`access`

(ad- "to" + `cess` "gone"): The means or opportunity to approach or enter a place; the right or opportunity to use or benefit from something.

`excess`

(ex- "out" + `cess` "gone"): An amount of something that is more than necessary, permitted, or desirable; an "over-going."

Example Derivatives and Their Meanings (2/2)

`procession`

(pro- "forward" + `cess` "gone"): A number of people or vehicles moving forward in an orderly way, especially as part of a ceremony or demonstration.

`secede`

(se- "apart" + `cede` "to go/yield"): To formally withdraw from a federation or body, especially a political one.

`intercede`

(inter- "between" + `cede` "to go"): To intervene on behalf of another.
