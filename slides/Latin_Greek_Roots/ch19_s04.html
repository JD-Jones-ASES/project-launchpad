The Latin Root "jus"

This root is central to many English words and carries the core meanings of "law," "right," or "justice."

Semantic Shifts

Over time, the meaning of a word can evolve or shift. For instance, words originally related to "acting unjustly" might come to mean simply "to cause harm," as seen with the word "injure."

Understanding Legal Terminology

Many words in the "jus" family are fundamental to legal discourse, helping us understand concepts like authority, legal decisions, and judicial processes.

Important Terms (1/2)

just

justice

*(noun)* Fairness; the quality of being morally right or reasonable; the administration of law.

justify

*(verb)* To show or prove to be right or reasonable; to defend as legitimate or appropriate.

justification

*(noun)* The action of showing something to be right or reasonable; a good reason for something.

jurisdiction

*(noun)* The official power to make legal decisions and judgments; the territory or sphere of authority over which a legal body or person exercises power.

Important Terms (2/2)

jurist

*(noun)* An expert in law, typically a judge or legal scholar.

jury

*(noun)* A body of people (typically twelve) sworn to give a verdict in a legal case on the basis of evidence submitted to them in court.

perjury

*(noun)* The offense of willfully telling an untruth or making a misrepresentation under oath, typically in a court of law.

injure

*(verb)* To do physical harm or damage to (someone or something); to harm or wrong.

injury

*(noun)* An instance of being injured; harm or damage.
