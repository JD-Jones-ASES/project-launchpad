Important Terms (1/3)

Modal verb

A verb (like *querer*, *poder*, *preferir*) that expresses mood or modality (such as desire, ability, preference). In Spanish, these are often followed by an infinitive.

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., *hablar* - to speak, *comer* - to eat, *vivir* - to live). In English, it often translates as "to [verb]".

`querer`

To want; a stem-changing (-e to -ie) modal verb used to express desire.

`poder`

To be able to, can; a stem-changing (-o to -ue) modal verb used to express ability or possibility.

`preferir`

To prefer; a stem-changing (-e to -ie) modal verb used to express preference.

Important Terms (2/3)

`jugar al fútbol`

To play soccer. The verb *jugar* (to play) is often followed by `al` when referring to playing a sport.

`los fines de semana`

The weekends.

`hasta las nueve`

Until nine o'clock.

`después del desayuno`

After breakfast.

`un examen`

A test / an exam.

Important Terms (3/3)

`ayudarme`

To help me (combination of `ayudar` - to help, and the object pronoun `me` - me).

`este fin de semana`

This weekend.

`tengo que + infinitivo`

I have to (do something). This is another common structure expressing obligation.

`¿Te parece bien?`

Does that work for you? / Is that okay? A useful phrase for agreeing or asking for agreement.

`¡Claro que sí!`

Of course! / Absolutely! A strong affirmative response.

The Modal + Infinitive Structure

Incorrect

*Quiero como pizza.* (Literally: I want I eat pizza.)

Correct

*Quiero **comer** pizza.* (I want to eat pizza.)

Incorrect

*Ella puede va al parque.*

Correct

*Ella puede **ir** al parque.* (She can go to the park.)
