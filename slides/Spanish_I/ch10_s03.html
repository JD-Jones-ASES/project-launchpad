Important Terms (1/3)

Modal verb

A verb (like querer, poder, preferir) that expresses mood or modality (such as desire, ability, preference). In Spanish, these are often followed by an infinitive.

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., hablar - to speak, comer - to eat, vivir - to live). In English, it often translates as "to [verb]".

`querer`

To want; a stem-changing (-e to -ie) modal verb used to express desire.

`poder`

To be able to, can; a stem-changing (-o to -ue) modal verb used to express ability or possibility.

`preferir`

To prefer; a stem-changing (-e to -ie) modal verb used to express preference.

Important Terms (2/3)

`jugar al fútbol`

To play soccer. The verb jugar (to play) is often followed by `al` when referring to playing a sport.

`los fines de semana`

The weekends.

`hasta las nueve`

Until nine o'clock.

`después del desayuno`

After breakfast.

`un examen`

A test / an exam.

Important Terms (3/3)

`ayudarme`

To help me (combination of `ayudar` - to help, and the object pronoun `me` - me).

`este fin de semana`

This weekend.

`tengo que + infinitivo`

I have to (do something). This is another common structure expressing obligation.

`¿Te parece bien?`

Does that work for you? / Is that okay? A useful phrase for agreeing or asking for agreement.

`¡Claro que sí!`

Of course! / Absolutely! A strong affirmative response.

The Modal + Infinitive Structure

Incorrect

Quiero como pizza. (Literally: I want I eat pizza.)

Correct

Quiero comer pizza. (I want to eat pizza.)

Incorrect

Ella puede va al parque.

Correct

Ella puede ir al parque. (She can go to the park.)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a "modal + infinitive" structure in Spanish?

It's a combination of a conjugated modal verb (like querer, poder, preferir) with a second verb in its infinitive form.

What do modal + infinitive structures allow you to express in Spanish?

They allow you to say what someone wants, can, or prefers to do.

Which three common verbs are introduced as modals in this section?

Querer (to want), poder (to be able to/can), and preferir (to prefer).

When forming a modal + infinitive structure, what happens to the modal verb?

The modal verb is conjugated to match the subject, following its stem-changing (boot) pattern.

What happens to the second verb in a modal + infinitive structure?

The second verb remains in its infinitive form (-ar, -er, or -ir) and is NOT conjugated.

What is the general formula for a modal + infinitive sentence?

[conjugated modal] + [infinitive]

Key Concepts (2/2)

Translate: "Quiero estudiar."

I want to study.

Translate: "¿Puedes venir?"

Can you come? / Are you able to come?

Translate: "No pueden dormir."

They can't sleep.

How do you make a modal + infinitive sentence negative?

Place 'no' directly before the conjugated modal verb. For example: No quiero estudiar.

What is a very common mistake students make when using modal + infinitive structures?

Conjugating both verbs (e.g., "Quiero como pizza" instead of "Quiero comer pizza").

Why is "Quiero como pizza" incorrect?

The second verb (comer) must remain in the infinitive form, as only the first (modal) verb is conjugated to match the subject.
