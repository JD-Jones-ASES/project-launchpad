SER and IR Share Forms

The verbs ser (to be) and ir (to go) have identical preterite conjugations: fui, fuiste, fue, fuimos, fuisteis, fueron. Context is essential for distinguishing their meaning in a sentence.

HACER's Spelling Change

The verb hacer (to do/to make) has a unique spelling change in its third-person singular (él/ella/usted) preterite form, changing from 'c' to 'z' (hizo) to maintain the correct pronunciation.

Consistent Irregular Endings

Many irregular preterite verbs, including hacer, tener, and estar, share a common set of endings: -e, -iste, -o, -imos, -ieron.

Important Terms (1/2)

Pretérito Irregular

The irregular past tense in Spanish, used for completed actions or events in the past.

SER

To be; used for permanent characteristics, identity, origin, time, and essential qualities.

IR

To go; used for movement to a destination.

HACER

To do / to make; used for actions, creation, or giving an action.

TENER

To have; used for possession, age, and certain expressions (e.g., tener hambre).

Important Terms (2/2)

ESTAR

To be; used for temporary states, locations, conditions, and emotions.

U-stem verbs

A category of irregular preterite verbs where the stem vowel changes to 'u' (e.g., tener becomes tuv-).

I-stem verbs

A category of irregular preterite verbs where the stem vowel changes to 'i' (e.g., hacer becomes hic-).

Context

The surrounding words or situation that provide meaning and clarify ambiguity, particularly for distinguishing ser and ir.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the five most useful irregular preterite verbs introduced in this section?

Ser, ir, hacer, tener, and estar.

What is a key characteristic of truly irregular preterite verbs compared to regular ones?

Their forms look very different from their infinitives; they are not formed with predictable endings on the infinitive stem.

Which two irregular preterite verbs share the exact same forms in Spanish?

Ser (to be) and Ir (to go).

Conjugate 'ser' or 'ir' in the preterite for 'yo' and 'tú'.

Yo: fui; Tú: fuiste

Conjugate 'ser' or 'ir' in the preterite for 'él/ella/usted' and 'nosotros'.

Él/Ella/Usted: fue; Nosotros: fuimos

Conjugate 'ser' or 'ir' in the preterite for 'ellos/ellas/ustedes'.

Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes: fueron

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you distinguish between 'ser' and 'ir' when they share the same preterite forms like 'fui' or 'fue'?

Context. If the sentence includes a destination (a + place), it is almost certainly IR. If it describes what something was like or identifies who someone was...

Translate: "Yesterday I went to the library."

Ayer fui a la biblioteca.

Translate: "She was world champion."

Ella fue campeona del mundo.

Conjugate 'hacer' (to do / to make) in the preterite for 'yo' and 'tú'.

Yo: hice; Tú: hiciste

Conjugate 'hacer' (to do / to make) in the preterite for 'él/ella/usted'.

Él/Ella/Usted: hizo

What important spelling change occurs in the él/ella/usted form of 'hacer' in the preterite, and why?

The 'c' changes to 'z' (hizo, not hicó). This is to preserve the soft 's-sound' before the -o ending, as 'co' would produce a hard 'k-sound'.
