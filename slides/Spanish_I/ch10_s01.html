Chapter 10, Section 1: Verbos con cambio de raíz: E → IE

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Stem-Changing Verbs (e → ie)

Many Spanish verbs undergo a vowel change in their stem (the part of the verb before the -ar, -er, or -ir ending) when conjugated. For this group, the `e` in the stem transforms into `ie`.

`Nosotros` and `Vosotros` Exception

The stem change *does not* occur in the `nosotros` (we) and `vosotros` (you all, informal, Spain) forms. These forms remain regular, keeping the original `e` in their stem.

Regular Endings

Even with the stem change, the verb endings (`-o, -es, -e, -emos, -en`) remain the same as they would for any regular -ar, -er, or -ir verb. Only the stem vowel changes.

Spelling Awareness

It's important to pay close attention to spelling when conjugating these verbs, ensuring the correct stem is used (e.g., `preferir` becomes `prefiero`, not `priefero`).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Masculine Nouns Ending in -a

Some common abstract nouns in Spanish, such as `el problema`, `el programa`, `el sistema`, and `el tema`, are masculine despite ending in `-a`. This is usually due to their Greek origin.

Important Terms (1/3)

Verb Stem (Raíz)

The base part of a verb that remains when you remove the infinitive ending (-ar, -er, -ir). For `querer`, the stem is `quer-`.

Stem-Changing Verb (Verbo con cambio de raíz)

A verb that undergoes a vowel change in its stem when conjugated in certain forms.

Boot Pattern (Patrón de la bota)

A visual mnemonic to remember which forms of a stem-changing verb undergo a vowel change (all forms except `nosotros` and `vosotros`).

Conjugation

The process of changing a verb's form to match the subject (e.g., `yo quiero`, `tú quieres`).

`Querer`

To want; to love. A common `e → ie` stem-changing verb.

Important Terms (2/3)

`Preferir`

To prefer. Another common `e → ie` stem-changing verb.

`Entender`

To understand. An `e → ie` stem-changing verb (from example sentences).

`Pensar`

To think; to plan. An `e → ie` stem-changing verb (from example sentences).

`El problema`

The problem. An abstract noun that is masculine despite ending in `-a`.

`El programa`

The program. Another masculine abstract noun ending in `-a`.

Important Terms (3/3)

`El sistema`

The system. Another masculine abstract noun ending in `-a`.

`El tema`

The theme, the topic. Another masculine abstract noun ending in `-a`.
