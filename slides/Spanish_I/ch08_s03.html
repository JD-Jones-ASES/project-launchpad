Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Expanded Body and Health Vocabulary

You will learn additional Spanish terms for body parts and common health-related words and phrases, crucial for discussing well-being.

The Versatility of "¡Salud!"

The word "salud" holds three distinct meanings in Spanish (health, a toast, and a blessing after a sneeze), with context determining its use.

Possessive Adjectives

These words (e.g., mi, tu, su, nuestro) indicate who owns something. A key rule is that they agree in number (singular/plural) with the thing owned, not with the owner.

Gender Agreement for Nuestro/a

While most possessive adjectives only change for number, nuestro (our) is unique as it also changes for gender (nuestro for masculine nouns, nuestra for feminine nouns).

Distinguishing Tú vs. Tu

It's vital to differentiate between tú (the accented subject pronoun "you") and tu (the unaccented possessive adjective "your").

¡Ojo! tú (you) vs. tu (your)

tú

(with an accent mark): This is the subject pronoun "you" (informal singular). It performs the action of the verb.

tu

(without an accent mark): This is the possessive adjective "your" (informal singular). It shows ownership.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary function of possessive adjectives in Spanish?

They tell you whose something is, indicating ownership (e.g., my book, your family, our school).

How do possessive adjectives agree in Spanish?

They agree in number (singular or plural) with the thing owned, not with the owner.

Which possessive adjective is the only one that changes for gender (masculine/feminine)?

Nuestro/Nuestra (and their plural forms, nuestros/nuestras).

List the singular possessive adjectives in Spanish.

mi (my), tu (your, informal singular), su (his, her, its, your formal singular, their).

List the plural possessive adjectives in Spanish.

mis (my, plural), tus (your, informal plural), sus (his/her/its/your formal/their, plural), nuestros/nuestras (our, plural).

What are the three distinct meanings of the Spanish word '¡Salud!'?

1. Health (La salud es importante). 2. A toast (Cheers! / To your health!). 3. A blessing after a sneeze.

Who are 'curanderos' in the context of traditional medicine in Ecuador?

Traditional healers who use plant-based healing and rituals, often working alongside modern medical doctors.

What is 'medicina ancestral' in Ecuador?

Indigenous healing practices, particularly in regions like Otavalo, that utilize local herbs, flowers, and rituals to treat physical and emotional ailments.

Name two common plants used as home remedies in Ecuadorian traditional medicine.

La manzanilla (chamomile) and el jengibre (ginger).

What makes Ecuador's approach to healthcare unique for many families?

Many families blend both traditional healing (visiting a curandero) and modern medical practices (visiting a doctor), choosing based on the specific condition.
