Shopping for Prices: ¿Cuánto Cuesta? (How Much Does It Cost?)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Asking and Stating Prices

Learning the direct questions to inquire about an item's cost and the standard way to respond with the price.

Describing Value

Using adjectives like caro/a (expensive) and barato/a (cheap) to express an opinion on prices.

Seller-Buyer Interaction

Essential phrases for both sellers (offering help) and buyers (requesting to see items, accepting a purchase).

Negotiating Prices

Understanding how to ask for a discount and how a seller might respond by lowering a price.

Direct Object Pronouns (DOPs)

The core grammatical focus, explaining how lo, la, los, las replace direct objects (the items being bought or discussed) to avoid repetition and improve fluency.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Cultural Context of Markets

Glimpses into open-air markets in places like La Paz, Bolivia, and Otavalo, Ecuador, and the role of artisans.

Describing Value

caro/a

expensive (e.g., Es muy caro. - It's very expensive.)

barato/a

cheap, inexpensive (e.g., Es bastante barato. - It's quite cheap.)

Interacting with a Seller

Offering Assistance

A seller might greet you with "¿En qué le puedo ayudar?" (How can I help you?).

Requesting an Item

While not explicitly detailed, you might point and ask "¿Puedo ver ésa?" (Can I see that one?). The dialogue shows the seller responding with "aquí la tiene" (here it is) after the buyer points...

Expressing Intent to Buy

Once you've decided on an item, you can say "Me la llevo" (I'll take it - for a feminine item like la camisa). This phrase integrates a direct object pronoun (la), which we will explore furth...

Negotiating Prices

• To ask for a discount, you can use "¿Me hace un descuento?" (Will you give me a discount?).

• A seller who agrees to lower the price might use the verb bajar (to lower). Lo baja a sesenta dólares means "He lowers it to sixty dollars."

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the most common Spanish phrase to ask "How much does it cost?"

¿Cuánto cuesta?

How do you say "expensive" and "cheap" in Spanish?

Caro and barato.

What does "¿En qué le puedo ayudar?" mean in a shopping context?

"How can I help you?" (formal).

In the dialogue, what does "ésa" refer to, and what's its grammatical characteristic?

"That one" (feminine singular demonstrative pronoun), used to refer to a previously mentioned feminine item.

How would you say "Of course!" or "Certainly!" in Spanish, based on the provided dialogues?

Claro que sí or ¡Por supuesto!

What does "aquí la tiene" mean when a vendor hands an item to a customer?

"Here it is" (referring to a feminine item).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is "calidad" in English?

Quality.

If you want a "medium" size shirt (camisa), what word would you use?

Mediana.

What phrase does a customer use to say "I'll take it" (referring to a feminine item)?

Me la llevo.

What is the meaning of "su compra"?

Your purchase.

What are direct object pronouns (lo, la, los, las) and why are they useful in shopping conversations?

Direct object pronouns replace the direct object noun to avoid repetition, making conversations more natural and concise (e.g., "Compro la camisa. La compro.").

What does "artesano/a" mean?

Artisan, craftsperson.
