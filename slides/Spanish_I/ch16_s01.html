Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Negative Commands

Negative commands are instructions that tell someone not to perform an action. In English, they typically start with "Don't." In Spanish, they use "no" combined with a special verb form.

Important Terms (1/2)

Los mandatos negativos

Negative commands; instructions telling someone what not to do.

Present subjunctive (tú form)

A specific verb mood used for expressing wishes, emotions, doubts, and, in this context, negative commands. For now, it's presented as a specific set of endings to be applied.

Tú form

The informal singular "you" form in Spanish, used when addressing a friend, family member, or someone you know well.

What Are Negative Commands?

No hables con extraños en el aeropuerto.

(Don't talk to strangers in the airport.)

No comas en el avión si tienes alergia.

(Don't eat on the plane if you have an allergy.)

No escribas tu número de teléfono en el pasaporte.

(Don't write your phone number in the passport.)

No salgas sin tu maleta.

(Don't leave without your suitcase.)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a "mandato negativo" in Spanish?

A negative command tells someone what not to do.

How do negative commands generally start in English?

They start with "Don't".

How do negative commands generally start in Spanish?

They start with "no" followed by a special verb form.

What is the general formula for forming a negative "tú" command in Spanish?

no + present subjunctive (tú form)

How do you form a negative "tú" command for -ar verbs?

Drop the -ar ending and add -es.

Form the negative "tú" command for "hablar" (to speak).

No hables.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you form a negative "tú" command for -er / -ir verbs?

Drop the -er / -ir ending and add -as.

Form the negative "tú" command for "comer" (to eat).

No comas.

Form the negative "tú" command for "escribir" (to write).

No escribas.

Are negative "tú" commands always the same as affirmative "tú" commands for a given verb?

No, they are different for many verbs, as negative commands use the subjunctive form.

What are the affirmative and negative "tú" commands for "ir" (to go)?

Affirmative: Ve. Negative: No vayas.

What are the affirmative and negative "tú" commands for "ser" (to be)?

Affirmative: Sé. Negative: No seas.
