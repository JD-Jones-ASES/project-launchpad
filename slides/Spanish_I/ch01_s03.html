The accent mark on `sí` (yes) is not optional; it critically distinguishes it from `si` (if), highlighting the importance of correct spelling and pronunciation.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Global Reach of Spanish

Spanish is spoken across multiple continents, including North America, Central America, South America, Europe, Africa, and the Caribbean, with Mexico having the largest number of native speakers.

Formal vs. Informal "You" (`tú` vs. `usted`)

Spanish distinguishes between informal (`tú`) and formal (`usted`) ways to say "you," which is crucial for showing respect in different social contexts.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the difference between "sí" (with an accent) and "si" (without an accent) in Spanish?

"Sí" with an accent means "yes." "Si" without an accent means "if." The accent is crucial and changes the meaning.

Why is the accent mark on "sí" important?

The accent mark on "sí" is crucial because it differentiates it from "si" (meaning "if"), changing the word's meaning entirely. It is not optional.

How do you say "zero" in Spanish?

Cero (0).

How do you say "one" in Spanish, and what keyword can help you remember it?

Uno (1). Keyword: "UNO" (the card game), picturing one card.

How do you say "two" in Spanish, and what keyword can help you remember it?

Dos (2). Keyword: "DOSE," picturing two medicine capsules.

How do you say "three" in Spanish, and what keyword can help you remember it?

Tres (3). Keyword: "TRACE," picturing tracing three triangles.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you say "four" in Spanish, and what keyword can help you remember it?

Cuatro (4). Keyword: "QUATRO," picturing a quadrant divided into four sections.

How do you say "five" in Spanish, and what keyword can help you remember it?

Cinco (5). Keyword: "SINK-oh," picturing five dishes sinking in soapy water.

List the Spanish words for numbers 6 through 10.

Seis (6), siete (7), ocho (8), nueve (9), diez (10).

What is "La técnica de la palabra clave" (the keyword method) for learning vocabulary?

It's a memory technique where you associate a new Spanish word with a similar-sounding English word (the keyword) and create a mental image to link them.
