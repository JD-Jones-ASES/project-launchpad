Mastering Regular -ER Verbs in Spanish

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Regular -ER Verb Pattern

Regular -er verbs follow a predictable pattern for conjugation in the present tense, making them easy to learn and apply to many verbs.

Two-Step Conjugation

To conjugate a regular -er verb, first drop the -er ending to find the stem, then add the correct ending corresponding to the subject pronoun.

Distinct Endings

Regular -er verbs have specific endings for each subject (e.g., -o, -es, -e, -emos, -en), which differ from -ar verbs (except for the *yo* form).

Shared *Yo* Ending

The *yo* form (I) for both regular -ar and -er verbs always ends in -o.

Contextual Meaning of *Comer*

While *comer* generally means "to eat," its specific meaning can vary by region and context, often referring to the main meal of the day.

Important Terms (1/2)

Infinitivo (Infinitive)

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb, always ending in -ar, -er, or -ir in Spanish (e.g., *comer* - to eat).

Tallo (Stem)

The part of the verb that remains after dropping the -ar, -er, or -ir ending from the infinitive (e.g., *com-* from *comer*).

Conjugar (Conjugate)

To change the form of a verb to match the subject (who is performing the action) and the tense (when the action occurs).

Presente (Present Tense)

The verb tense used to describe actions happening now, habitual actions, or general truths.

The Conjugation Pattern for Regular -ER Verbs

Subject Pronoun. -ER Verb Ending

Example with *comer* (to eat)

yo (I). -o

*yo com**o***

tú (you, informal). -es

*tú com**es***

él/ella/usted (he/she/you, formal). -e

*él/ella/usted com**e***

nosotros/as (we). -emos

*nosotros/as com**emos***

ellos/ellas/ustedes (they/you all). -en

*ellos/ellas/ustedes com**en***
