Key Concepts

Narrative Progression with Preterite

The preterite tense is crucial for telling a story because it describes completed actions that move the narrative forward in time, establishing a sequence of events.

Sequencing Words (`Palabras de transición`)

Special adverbs and phrases like `Primero`, `Después`, `Luego`, `De repente`, and `Finalmente` are used to connect individual preterite actions, creating a coherent and flowing story.

Vocabulary for History and the Past

A dedicated set of vocabulary helps learners discuss historical events, time periods, and memories, enriching their storytelling capabilities.

Constructing a Narrative in the Preterite

Primero

Used to introduce the very first event in a series. (e.g., *Primero, llegué a la fiesta.* - First, I arrived at the party.)

Después

Used to indicate an event that happened subsequent to a previous one. (e.g., *Después, hablé con mis amigos.* - Then, I talked with my friends.)

Luego

Similar to `después`, meaning "next" or "then." (e.g., *Luego, bailamos y cantamos.* - Next, we danced and sang.)

De repente

Used to introduce a sudden or unexpected event. (e.g., *De repente, apagaron las luces.* - Suddenly, they turned off the lights.)

Finalmente

Used to signal the last event or the conclusion of the story. (e.g., *Finalmente, comimos el pastel.* - Finally, we ate the cake.)

Vocabulary for the Past and History (1/3)

la vez (f.)

Refers to a "time" or "occasion," as in `una vez` (once) or `dos veces` (twice).

la historia (f.)

Can mean both "history" (the study of the past) and "story" (a narrative).

el cuento (m.)

Specifically refers to a "story" or "tale," often shorter or fictional.

el pasado (m.)

The general concept of "the past."

el recuerdo (m.)

A "memory" or a "souvenir."

Vocabulary for the Past and History (2/3)

el principio (m.)

The "beginning" of something.

la guerra (f.)

"War."

la paz (f.)

"Peace."

el rey (m.)

"King."

el pueblo (m.)

Can mean "town" or "village," but also "people" as a collective group or nation.

Vocabulary for the Past and History (3/3)

la sorpresa (f.)

"Surprise."

la noticia (f.)

A "piece of news" or "the news" (when plural, `las noticias`).

el evento (m.) / el suceso (m.)

Both refer to an "event" or "happening." `Suceso` often implies something noteworthy or significant.

la época (f.)

An "era" or "period of time."

el siglo (m.)

A "century."
