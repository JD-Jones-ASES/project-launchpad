Mastering Subject Pronouns and the Verb 'Ser'

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Subject Pronouns (Los pronombres personales)

Words that indicate who is doing the action in a sentence, such as yo (I), tú (you), él (he), ella (she), usted (formal you), nosotros/nosotras (we), ellos/ellas/ustedes (they/you all).

Formal vs. Informal "You"

Spanish distinguishes between tú (informal, for friends, peers) and usted (formal, for adults, teachers, or showing respect).

"You All" in Latin America

Ustedes is the universal pronoun for "you all" in Latin American Spanish, regardless of the group's formality or gender.

Gendered "We"

Nosotros is used for an all-male or mixed-gender group, while nosotras is used specifically for an all-female group.

The Verb Ser ("to be")

One of the two Spanish verbs meaning "to be," ser is used for inherent, defining characteristics.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the purpose of a subject pronoun?

A subject pronoun tells us who is doing the action in a sentence.

List the singular subject pronouns in Spanish.

yo (I), tú (you - informal), él (he), ella (she), usted (you - formal)

List the plural subject pronouns in Spanish.

nosotros (we - masculine/mixed), nosotras (we - feminine), ellos (they - masculine/mixed), ellas (they - feminine), ustedes (you all)

When should you use 'tú'?

'Tú' is used with friends, classmates, and people your own age (informal 'you').

When should you use 'usted'?

'Usted' (Ud.) is used with adults you do not know well, teachers, or people you want to show respect to (formal 'you').

How do you say 'you all' in Latin American Spanish?

In Latin America, 'ustedes' is used for all groups of people, regardless of formality or gender.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the difference between 'nosotros' and 'nosotras'?

'Nosotros' is used for an all-male group or a mixed group. 'Nosotras' is used for an all-female group.

What does the Spanish verb 'ser' mean?

'Ser' means 'to be'.

How do you conjugate 'ser' for 'yo' (I)?

yo soy (I am)

How do you conjugate 'ser' for 'tú' (you - informal)?

tú eres (you are)

How do you conjugate 'ser' for 'él/ella/usted' (he/she/you - formal)?

él/ella/usted es (he/she is, you are)

How do you conjugate 'ser' for 'nosotros/nosotras' (we)?

nosotros/nosotras somos (we are)
