Chapter 3, Section 2 Study Guide: Los pronombres y el verbo ser

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Subject Pronouns

Words like "I," "you," "he," and "she" that indicate who is doing the action in a sentence. They change based on number (singular/plural) and gender, and also convey formality for "you."

`Tú` vs. `Usted`

Spanish has two forms for "you" (singular). `Tú` is informal, used with friends, family, and peers. `Usted` is formal, used with adults you don't know well, teachers, or anyone you want to show res...

`Ustedes` (Latin America)

In Latin American Spanish, `ustedes` is the universal plural "you all," used for both formal and informal groups.

`Nosotros` vs. `Nosotras`

The pronoun for "we" changes based on the group's gender: `nosotros` for all-male or mixed groups, `nosotras` for all-female groups.

`Ser` (to be)

One of two Spanish verbs meaning "to be." `Ser` is used to express permanent or defining characteristics and identities.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Conjugation of `Ser`

The verb `ser` changes form depending on the subject pronoun: `soy` (I am), `eres` (you are informal), `es` (he/she/it is, you are formal), `somos` (we are), `son` (they are, you all are).

Pronunciation Reminders:

soy

Rhymes with English "soy" (as in soy sauce).

eres

Sounds like "EH-res."

es

Sounds like the letter "S" when said out loud.

somos

Sounds like "SO-mos."

son

Sounds like the English word "son."
