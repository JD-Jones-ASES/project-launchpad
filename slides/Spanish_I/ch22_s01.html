El Subjuntivo: Una Introducción

Key Concepts (1/2)

Indicative Mood

The verb mood you've mastered, used for expressing facts, real events, and ongoing actions (e.g., Estudio mucho, Ayer comimos tacos).

Subjunctive Mood

A new verb mood used for expressing desires, hopes, emotions, doubts, or recommendations about actions that are uncertain, wished for, or hypothetical. It's often called the "wishful thinking" mood.

Important Terms (1/2)

Indicative Mood

Verb forms used to state facts, describe reality, or express certainty.

Subjunctive Mood

Verb forms used to express subjectivity, such as desires, emotions, doubts, recommendations, or hypothetical situations.

Trigger Phrase

A phrase or expression (e.g., Quiero que, Es importante que) that signals the need for the subjunctive mood in the following clause.

`que` (conjunction)

The word "that" which connects the main clause (containing the trigger phrase) to the dependent clause where the subjunctive verb is used.

Opposite Vowel Endings

The characteristic endings of the regular subjunctive mood, where `-ar` verbs take `-e` endings and `-er`/`-ir` verbs take `-a` endings.

Important Terms (2/2)

`Ojalá`

An expressive Spanish word meaning "I hope" or "God-willing," derived from the Arabic phrase `insha'Allah`. It always triggers the subjunctive.

The Core Pattern of the Subjunctive

Quiero que estudies más.

(I want you to study more.)

Es importante que hables con tu maestra.

(It's important that you speak with your teacher.)

Espero que tú aprendas mucho.

(I hope that you learn a lot.)

Ojalá que tengamos clase afuera.

(I hope / God-willing that we have class outside.)

Es necesario que practiquemos todos los días.

(It's necessary that we practice every day.)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary function of the indicative mood in Spanish?

The indicative mood describes real events, facts, and ongoing actions.

Give an example of a sentence that uses the indicative mood.

Examples include "Estudio mucho" (I study a lot) or "Ayer comimos tacos" (Yesterday we ate tacos).

What is the main purpose of the subjunctive mood (el subjuntivo) in Spanish?

The subjunctive mood is used to express desires, hopes, emotions, doubts, or recommendations about what someone else should do or might do.

What common phrase can help you remember the subjunctive mood's general purpose?

It's often referred to as the 'wishful thinking' mood of Spanish.

Describe the key structural pattern for sentences that use the subjunctive mood, as introduced in this section.

The key pattern is: Trigger phrase + que + new subject + subjunctive verb.

In the subjunctive pattern, whose action does the subjunctive verb express?

The subjunctive verb expresses the action of the 'new subject' or 'other person,' especially when that action is still uncertain or wished for by the speaker.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Identify the trigger phrase and the subjunctive verb in the sentence: "Quiero que estudies más."

Trigger phrase: "Quiero"; Subjunctive verb: "estudies."

Identify the trigger phrase and the subjunctive verb in the sentence: "Es importante que hables con tu maestra."

Trigger phrase: "Es importante"; Subjunctive verb: "hables."

What is the first step to forming the regular subjunctive of a verb?

Take the "yo" form of the present indicative.

What is the second step in forming the regular subjunctive?

Drop the "-o" ending from the "yo" form of the present indicative.

What are the subjunctive endings for regular -ar verbs?

-ar verbs use -e endings: -e, -es, -e, -emos, -en.

What are the subjunctive endings for regular -er and -ir verbs?

-er and -ir verbs use -a endings: -a, -as, -a, -amos, -an.
