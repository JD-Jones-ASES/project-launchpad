Mandatos Afirmativos con Tú: Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Affirmative Tú Commands

These are direct commands given to a single person you address with "tú" (informal "you"), telling them to do something (e.g., "Eat!", "Speak!").

Formation Rule for Regular Verbs

For all regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs, the affirmative tú command is formed by taking the él/ella/Ud. form of the verb in the present tense.

No Subject Pronoun 'tú'

It is crucial not to use the subject pronoun "tú" with the command. The command form itself implies you are speaking to "you." Adding "tú" sounds unnatural and can even be considered rude in Span...

Punctuation

In written Spanish, affirmative commands (like all exclamations) must begin with an inverted exclamation mark (¡) and end with a regular exclamation mark (!).

Applicability

This formation rule works consistently for all regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Maintaining Accent Marks

If the él/ella present-tense form of a verb already has an accent mark, it will remain in the command form. For regular verbs, this usually means no new accent marks are added.

Attaching Nouns/Pronouns/Location Words

Nouns, object pronouns, or place words can be attached after an affirmative tú command to provide more detail (e.g., "Wash the dishes").

Important Terms (1/2)

Mandato(s) afirmativo(s)

Affirmative command(s). Instructions telling someone to do something.

Tú command

An informal command directed at a single person, equivalent to "you" (singular informal).

Infinitive

The base form of a verb, unconjugated (e.g., hablar, comer, vivir).

Present tense (él/ella/Ud. form)

The verb conjugation used for "he," "she," or formal "you" in the present tense. This is the key to forming regular tú commands.

Los quehaceres del hogar

Household chores. A common context for giving and receiving commands.

Important Terms (2/2)

¡Ojo!

An expression meaning "Watch out!" or "Be careful!" Used to highlight important warnings.

Porque

A conjunction meaning "because." Used to explain the reason for a command.

Discovering the Pattern

• Consider the verb hablar (to speak). The él/ella/Ud. present-tense form is habla. Notice that the affirmative tú command is ¡Habla!

• For the verb comer (to eat), the él/ella/Ud. present-tense form is come. The command is ¡Come!

• Following this pattern, for estudiar (to study), the él/ella/Ud. present tense is estudia, so the command is ¡Estudia!

• For vivir (to live), the él/ella/Ud. present tense is vive, making the command ¡Vive!

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are "mandatos afirmativos" in Spanish?

They are affirmative commands, used to tell someone to do something (e.g., "Open the door.", "Eat your vegetables.").

What is the general purpose of commands in daily life?

Commands are used to give instructions, make requests, or tell people what to do.

How do you form regular affirmative tú commands in Spanish?

You take the él/ella/Ud. present-tense form of the verb.

What specific form of a regular verb's present tense does the affirmative tú command directly correspond to?

The él/ella/Ud. present-tense form.

Give the affirmative tú command for the regular -ar verb "hablar" (to speak).

¡Habla!

Give the affirmative tú command for the regular -er verb "comer" (to eat).

¡Come!

Key Concepts (2/2)

Give the affirmative tú command for the regular -ir verb "vivir" (to live).

¡Vive!

Should you include the subject pronoun "tú" when giving an affirmative tú command in Spanish?

No. You should never add "tú" to a command. The command form itself implies you are speaking directly to "you."

What effect does adding "tú" to a Spanish command, such as "¡Habla tú!", have on its tone?

It makes the command sound unnatural and potentially rude.

What specific punctuation marks are required when writing commands in Spanish?

An inverted exclamation point (¡) at the beginning and a regular exclamation point (!) at the end.

Which types of regular verbs (e.g., -ar, -er, -ir) follow the rule of using the él/ella/Ud. present-tense form for affirmative tú commands?

This rule works for all regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs.

Do accent marks on verbs change when they are used in the affirmative tú command form?

No, accent marks on verbs do NOT change in the command form.
