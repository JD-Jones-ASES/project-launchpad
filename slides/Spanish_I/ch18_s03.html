Key Concepts (1/2)

The Preterite Tense

Used to describe completed actions or events in the past, often with a clear beginning and end. It's essential for recounting specific occurrences.

Special `yo` forms

Key Concepts (2/2)

Sequence Words

Words like `ayer` (yesterday), `primero` (first), `después` (afterwards), and `al final` (in the end) are vital for organizing events in a chronological story.

Important Terms (1/3)

El pretérito

The preterite tense; used for actions completed in the past.

-ar verb

A verb ending in -ar in its infinitive form (e.g., hablar - to speak).

-er verb

A verb ending in -er in its infinitive form (e.g., comer - to eat).

-ir verb

A verb ending in -ir in its infinitive form (e.g., escribir - to write).

Estar + adjective

A construction used to express temporary states or emotions (e.g., estar contento/a - to be happy).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the main goal of learning the content in this section?

To combine all three types of preterite verbs (-ar, -er, -ir) with emotion adjectives to tell a complete story about what happened in the past.

What are the preterite endings for regular -ar verbs?

-é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -asteis, -aron

What are the preterite endings for regular -er and -ir verbs?

-í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -isteis, -ieron

What is the key difference in the 'yo' form between -ar preterite verbs and -er/-ir preterite verbs?

-ar verbs use -é (with an accent), while -er/-ir verbs use -í (with an accent).

How do you differentiate between the present and preterite tense for 'nosotros' -ar verbs like 'hablar'?

The 'nosotros' form for -ar verbs (e.g., 'hablamos') is the same in both present and preterite. Context clues must be used to determine the tense.

How do the 'nosotros' forms for -er verbs differ between the present and preterite tense?

They are different. For example, 'comemos' is present, while 'comimos' is preterite.

How are emotions typically expressed when telling a story in the past tense, according to the text?

By using 'estar' combined with an adjective (estar + adjective), such as 'estar avergonzado/a' or 'estar preocupado/a'.

'Me sentí muy avergonzado/a' (I felt very embarrassed) and 'estaba preocupado/a' (I was worried).
