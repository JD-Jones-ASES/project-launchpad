Important Terms (1/6)

Imperfect Tense (El imperfecto)

A past tense in Spanish used to describe ongoing, habitual, or repeated actions; descriptions of people, places, and things; and states of being in the past without a definite beginning or end.

(When I was a child/boy/girl) — The fundamental phrase for beginning a childhood memory.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary use of the imperfect tense when talking about childhood?

It is the perfect tool for sharing childhood memories—what you used to do, who you used to be, and what the world looked like through younger eyes.

What is the 'magic phrase' to begin a statement about childhood memories in Spanish, and what does it mean?

'Cuando era niño/a,' meaning 'When I was a child/boy/girl.'

Translate this sentence into English: 'Cuando era niño, jugaba con mi perro todos los días.'

'When I was a boy, I used to play with my dog every day.'

Translate this sentence into English: 'De niña, mi mamá me leía cuentos sobre animales.'

'As a girl, my mom used to read me stories about animals.'

How would you say 'We used to be very curious and we used to explore the forest' in Spanish?

'Éramos muy curiosos y explorábamos el bosque.'

Translate this question into English: '¿Tenías un animal favorito cuando eras pequeño?'

'Did you have a favorite animal when you were little?'

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to 'Recuerdos de mi niñez,' where did the narrator live as a child?

In a small town near the jungle in Costa Rica.

Describe the narrator's childhood home and garden from 'Recuerdos de mi niñez.'

The house was modest ('modesta'), but the garden was enormous ('enorme') and full of colorful flowers.

What special trip did the narrator's family take every summer?

They used to go to the Pacific beach.

What mysterious sight did the narrator witness at the beach at night?

Sea turtles ('tortugas marinas') coming to lay their eggs in the white sand. It was like a dream ('¡Era como un sueño!').

What kind of stories did the narrator's grandfather tell?

Stories about jungle animals ('animales de la selva'), such as jaguars walking near the town when it rained a lot.

How did the narrator feel listening to their grandfather's stories?

A little scared ('un poco asustado') and a little fascinated ('un poco fascinado').
