Important Terms (1/6)

Imperfect Tense (El imperfecto)

A past tense in Spanish used to describe ongoing, habitual, or repeated actions; descriptions of people, places, and things; and states of being in the past without a definite beginning or end.

Cuando era niño/a

(When I was a child/boy/girl) — The fundamental phrase for beginning a childhood memory.

De niño/a

(As a child/boy/girl) — An alternative phrase used similarly to "Cuando era niño/a."

Pueblo pequeño

(Small town) — A common setting for childhood memories.

(Jungle) — An example of a natural environment mentioned in the childhood narrative.

Introduction to the Imperfect Tense for Childhood Memories

Habitual or repeated actions

"I used to play," "We would go."

Ongoing actions or states

"She was reading," "They were living."

Descriptions

"The house was big," "He was tall," "It was a sunny day."

Emotions or mental states

"I was happy," "He felt curious."
