`Gustar` and Infinitives: Expressing What You Like to Do

The Rule

When the "subject" of your liking is an action (an infinitive), you always use the singular form of `gustar`, which is `gusta`.

Why `gusta`?

Even if you like many actions, each action is considered a singular concept. For example, `escuchar música` is one action, and that action is pleasing.

Flexibility

This construction is incredibly versatile. You can combine `gustar`-family verbs with any infinitive you know to express a wide range of activities and preferences.

`Preguntar` vs. `Pedir`: A Key Distinction

`Preguntar`

Use `preguntar` when you are asking a question to get information.

`Pedir`

Use `pedir` when you are asking for something to be received. This could be an object, a favor, or ordering food/drink.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How do you say what you like doing in Spanish using the 'gustar' construction?

Put an infinitive verb directly after 'gustar'.

When using 'gustar' with an infinitive, which form of 'gustar' do you always use?

You always use the singular form, 'gusta'.

Give an example of 'gustar' used with an infinitive.

Me gusta escuchar música. (I like listening to music.)

What is a key benefit of combining 'gustar'-family verbs with infinitives?

It allows you to express complex ideas using simple, existing building blocks.

What type of stem-change occurs in the verbs 'pedir' and 'servir'?

The 'e' in the stem changes to 'i' (e → i).

How does the 'e → i' stem change (as in 'pedir') differ from the 'e → ie' change (as in 'querer')?

Verbs like 'pedir' and 'servir' change 'e' to 'i', while verbs like 'querer' change 'e' to 'ie'.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which forms of the verb 'pedir' (to ask for) undergo the 'e → i' stem change?

All forms except 'nosotros' (ped- changes to pid-).

Conjugate 'pedir' (e → i stem-changing verb) in the present tense for 'yo', 'tú', 'él/ella/usted', 'nosotros', and 'ellos/ellas/ustedes'.

pido, pides, pide, pedimos, piden.

What is the primary difference in meaning and usage between 'preguntar' and 'pedir'?

'Preguntar' is used for asking questions you want answered. 'Pedir' is used for asking for things you want received (like an item or a favor).

Which verb would you use to ask 'How much does it cost?' ('¿Cuánto cuesta?')?

'Preguntar' (because you're asking a question for an answer).

Which verb would you use to ask for a coffee ('un café')?

'Pedir' (because you're asking for something to receive).

What does 'especialmente' mean in English?

Especially.
