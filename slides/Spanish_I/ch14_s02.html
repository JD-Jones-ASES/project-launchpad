Saber (Head Knowledge)

Important Terms

Saber (to know - facts/skills)

To have knowledge of facts, information, or to know how to perform a skill. Its `yo` form is `sé` (with an accent).

Conocer (to know - people/places/things)

To be acquainted with a person, familiar with a place (e.g., visited it), or familiar with a thing (e.g., a song, a book). Its `yo` form is `conozco`.

Dar (to give)

To present something to someone. Its `yo` form is `doy`.

Ver (to see)

To perceive with the eyes. Its `yo` form is `veo`.

La "a personal" (the personal 'a')

A required preposition `a` placed before a specific person (or sometimes a personified animal) when they function as a direct object in a sentence. It doesn't translate into English.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In English, "to know" is one verb. How many verbs are there for "to know" in Spanish?

There are two verbs in Spanish for "to know": "saber" and "conocer."

What is the primary meaning/use of the Spanish verb "saber"?

"Saber" is used for knowing facts, information, or how to do something (skills). It means 'know THAT' or 'know HOW'.

What types of "knowing" require the verb "conocer"?

"Conocer" is used for knowing or being familiar with people, places, or things.

Provide an example of using "saber" for a fact or piece of information.

Sé que hoy hay clase. (I know that there is class today.)

How do you express "knowing how to do something" (a skill) using "saber"?

Use "saber" followed by an infinitive, e.g., Sé nadar. (I know how to swim.)

Give an example of using "saber" for a specific detail like a name, address, or phone number.

¿Sabes el número de teléfono? (Do you know the phone number?)

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the irregular "yo" form of "saber"?

The "yo" form of "saber" is "sé." It has an accent mark to distinguish it from the reflexive pronoun "se."

Give an example of using "conocer" when referring to knowing a person.

Conozco a mi vecino. (I know my neighbor.)

How do you use "conocer" to talk about knowing a place?

Use "conocer" to indicate you have been there or are familiar with it, e.g., ¿Conoces Bogotá? (Do you know / have you been to Bogotá?)

How do you use "conocer" for being familiar with a specific thing (not a fact)?

Conocemos esa canción. (We know that song — we are familiar with it.)

What is the irregular "yo" form of "conocer"?

The "yo" form of "conocer" is "conozco." This -zco ending is a common pattern for verbs ending in -cer or -cir.
