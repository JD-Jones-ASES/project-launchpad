Planning a Trip (Planeando un viaje) (1/2)

Sofía

initiates by asking: ¿Adónde quieres ir en las vacaciones? (Where do you want to go on vacation?)

Diego

responds with his dream destination and a specific plan: ¡Quiero ir a Perú! Voy a visitar Machu Picchu. (I want to go to Peru! I'm going to visit Machu Picchu.)

Sofía

Sofía

offers helpful advice using negative commands: No olvides tu pasaporte. ¡Y no pierdas el equipaje! (Don't forget your passport. And don't lose your luggage!)

Planning a Trip (Planeando un viaje) (2/2)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the Spanish verb "viajar" mean?

To travel.

What does the Spanish verb "manejar" mean?

To drive.

What does "el boleto" mean in the context of travel?

The ticket (e.g., plane ticket).

What is "la reservación del hotel"?

The hotel reservation.

What essential document is "el pasaporte"?

The passport.

What does "el equipaje" refer to when traveling?

The luggage.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What does the reflexive verb "levantarse" mean?

To get up.

What is "el amanecer"?

The sunrise.

What does "los consejos" mean?

The advice.

How do you express future plans in Spanish using "ir a"?

You use "ir a" + infinitive (e.g., "Voy a visitar Machu Picchu" - I'm going to visit Machu Picchu).

Give two correct ways to say "I'm going to get up early" using the verb "levantarse."

1. "Me voy a levantar temprano." 2. "Voy a levantarme temprano."

How would you tell someone "Don't forget your passport" using a negative command?

"No olvides tu pasaporte."
