Expressing Future Plans (`ir a + infinitive`)

Reinforces the use of `ir a` followed by an infinitive verb to talk about what someone *is going to do* in the future (e.g., *Voy a visitar Machu Picchu* - I am going to visit Machu Picchu).

Negative Commands (Review)

Practiced through advice given in the conversation and reading, reminding you how to tell someone *not* to do something (e.g., *No olvides tu pasaporte* - Don't forget your passport).

Travel Vocabulary — Part 2 (Vocabulario del viaje — Parte 2)

viajar

(to travel): Used to express the act of going from one place to another.

manejar

(to drive): Essential if you plan to get around by car.

bailar

(to dance): A review verb from Chapter 12, relevant for experiencing local culture and nightlife.

Planning a Trip (Planeando un viaje) (1/2)

Sofía

initiates by asking: *¿Adónde quieres ir en las vacaciones?* (Where do you want to go on vacation?)

Diego

responds with his dream destination and a specific plan: *¡Quiero ir a Perú! Voy a visitar Machu Picchu.* (I want to go to Peru! I'm going to visit Machu Picchu.)

Sofía

expresses excitement and asks about preparations: *¡Qué aventura! ¿Ya tienes el boleto?* (What an adventure! Do you already have the ticket?)

Diego

confirms his bookings and an early start: *Sí, y tengo la reservación del hotel. Voy a levantarme muy temprano el primer día.* (Yes, and I have the hotel reservation. I'm going to get up very early...

Sofía

offers helpful advice using **negative commands**: *No olvides tu pasaporte. ¡Y no pierdas el equipaje!* (Don't forget your passport. And don't lose your luggage!)

Planning a Trip (Planeando un viaje) (2/2)

Diego

thanks her and emphasizes his careful preparation: *Gracias. Me voy a preparar muy bien para este viaje.* (Thanks. I'm going to prepare very well for this trip.)
