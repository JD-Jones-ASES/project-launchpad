Capítulo 16, Sección 2 - Los Verbos Reflexivos

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Reflexive Verbs (Verbos Reflexivos)

These verbs indicate that the subject of the sentence is both performing the action and receiving the action. The action "reflects" back on the subject.

Reflexive Pronouns

Essential components of reflexive verbs, these pronouns (me, te, se, nos, os, se) always precede the conjugated verb and must match the subject in person and number.

Conjugation of Reflexive Verbs

When conjugating, remove the "-se" from the infinitive, place the correct reflexive pronoun before the verb, and conjugate the verb normally according to its tense and subject.

Stem-Changing Reflexive Verbs

Some reflexive verbs follow stem-changing patterns (e.g., e>ie, o>ue, e>i) in all forms except the nosotros and vosotros forms, just like non-reflexive stem-changing verbs.

Body Parts with Definite Articles

When using reflexive verbs to talk about actions done to body parts (e.g., brushing hair/teeth), Spanish typically uses a definite article (el/la/los/las) instead of a possessive adjective (mi/tu/su).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Daily Routine Practice

Actively narrating your own daily routine using reflexive verbs is highlighted as an effective memorization strategy.

Important Terms (1/2)

Verbos reflexivos

Reflexive verbs; verbs where the subject performs the action upon itself (e.g., levantarse - to get oneself up).

Reflexive pronouns (Pronombres reflexivos)

Small words that accompany reflexive verbs, indicating that the action is reflexive. They are me (myself), te (yourself, informal), se (himself, herself, itself, yourself formal, themselves, ...

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., hablar, comer, vivir). Reflexive infinitives end in "-se" (e.g., levantarse).

Conjugated verb

A verb that has been changed to match its subject and tense (e.g., yo levanto, tú comes).

Stem-changing verbs (Verbos de cambio de raíz)

Verbs whose stem (the part before the -ar, -er, or -ir ending) changes its vowel sound in most conjugations (e.g., dormir > duermo).

Important Terms (2/2)

Rutina diaria

Daily routine; the sequence of activities one does every day.

Doña Blanca

A character from a traditional Latin American children's game and song featured in this section.

Jicotillo

A little wasp or beetle, a character in the "Doña Blanca" song.

Pilar

Pillar; an architectural support, mentioned in the "Doña Blanca" song as being made of gold and silver.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary characteristic of a reflexive verb in Spanish?

A reflexive verb indicates that the subject performs the action on themselves.

What are the small pronouns that accompany reflexive verbs called?

Reflexive pronouns.

Where are reflexive pronouns placed in relation to the conjugated verb?

Directly before the conjugated verb.

What must the reflexive pronoun always match in a sentence?

The subject of the sentence.

What is the reflexive pronoun for 'yo' (I)?

me

What is the reflexive pronoun for 'tú' (you - informal singular)?

te

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the reflexive pronoun for 'él, ella, usted' (he, she, you - formal singular)?

se

What is the reflexive pronoun for 'nosotros/nosotras' (we)?

nos

What is the reflexive pronoun for 'ellos, ellas, ustedes' (they, you - formal plural)?

se

How are reflexive verbs typically listed in a Spanish dictionary?

With '-se' attached to the infinitive (e.g., levantarse, bañarse, vestirse).

Describe the first step to conjugate a reflexive verb like 'levantarse'.

Remove the '-se' from the infinitive and replace it with the correct reflexive pronoun for the subject.

After placing the reflexive pronoun, what is the next step to conjugate the verb?

Conjugate the remaining verb (e.g., 'levantar') normally according to the subject.
