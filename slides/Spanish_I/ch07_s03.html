La Rutina Diaria (The Daily Routine)

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Describing Daily Routines

Learn to use -ar, -er, and -ir verbs with new vocabulary to articulate a full day's worth of activities in Spanish.

Consistent Vowel Pronunciation

Understand that Spanish vowels (a, e, i, o, u) each have a single, consistent pronunciation, which is crucial for clear and understandable speech.

Cultural Diversity in Daily Life

Recognize that daily rhythms, such as school schedules, meal times, and social customs (e.g., *el mate*, *la merienda* in Argentina), can vary significantly across different cultures.

Interactive Language Learning

Utilize songs and games as effective tools to practice pronunciation, reinforce vocabulary, and make language acquisition enjoyable.

La Merienda

Around 4 or 5 p.m., Argentines enjoy *la merienda*, an afternoon snack. Popular choices include *medialunas* (croissants) and hot chocolate or juice.

La Cena (Dinner)

Dinner in Argentina happens much later than in many other cultures, usually between 9 and 10 p.m.

Reading: La rutina de Pablo (Pablo's Routine) (1/3)

Home Life

Pablo lives in an apartment on the fourth floor.

Morning

He wakes up at 7 a.m., eats toast and drinks coffee with milk with his mom, finishes any unfinished homework, and takes the bus to school.

School

He reads a lot, learns new things, and his favorite class is science.

Afternoon

After school, he and his neighbor Matías run in the street or play soccer in the park.

Evening

His family eats dinner together around 9:30 p.m. They talk about their day, drink water or juice, and read a bit before going to sleep.
