Key Concepts (1/2)

Limited Imperfect Irregulars

The imperfect tense has only three irregular verbs: ser (to be), ir (to go), and ver (to see). This is significantly fewer than other past tenses like the preterite.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many irregular verbs are there in the imperfect tense in Spanish?

Only three.

Name the three irregular verbs in the Spanish imperfect tense.

Ser, ir, and ver.

Do stem-changing verbs and other 'tricky' verbs follow regular or irregular patterns in the imperfect tense?

They follow the regular imperfect endings perfectly.

How does the number of irregular verbs in the imperfect tense compare to the preterite tense?

There are significantly fewer irregular verbs in the imperfect (only three) compared to the preterite (dozens).

Conjugate the verb 'ser' (to be) in the imperfect tense.

era, eras, era, éramos, erais, eran.

When is 'ser' typically used in the imperfect tense?

To describe identity, characteristics, or states that existed over time in the past.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Conjugate the verb 'ir' (to go) in the imperfect tense.

iba, ibas, iba, íbamos, ibais, iban.

What are the main uses of 'ir' in the imperfect tense?

To describe habitual destinations or ongoing movement in the past.

Conjugate the verb 'ver' (to see/watch) in the imperfect tense.

veía, veías, veía, veíamos, veíais, veían.

When is 'ver' typically used in the imperfect tense?

To describe what someone used to see, watch, or observe regularly in the past.

Why is 'ver' considered an irregular verb in the imperfect, even though its endings match regular -er/-ir verbs?

Because its stem 've-' retains the 'e', unlike truly regular verbs (e.g., 'comer' -> 'com-') which drop to a bare stem before adding the imperfect endings.

List four common 'animales domésticos' (domestic animals) in Spanish.

Perro (dog), gato (cat), vaca (cow), caballo (horse).
