El Verbo IR: Going Places

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Verb "Ir" (To Go)

*Ir* is a highly irregular verb used to express movement towards a destination. Its forms must be memorized.

Common Places

A core vocabulary list of places is provided to use with the verb *ir*.

Mandatory Contractions "al" and "del"

In Spanish, the preposition *a* (to) followed by *el* (the masculine singular article) *must* contract to *al*. Similarly, *de* (from/of) followed by *el* *must* contract to *del*. These contractio...

No Contraction with Feminine/Plural Articles

Contractions *al* and *del* only occur with *el*. When *a* or *de* precede *la*, *los*, or *las*, no contraction takes place.

Multiple Meanings of "¡Vamos!"

The *nosotros/as* form, *vamos*, can mean "we go" or "we are going," but when used with an exclamation mark and lively tone, it often translates to "Let's go!"

The Verb "Ir" (To Go)

Subject Pronoun. Form of *Ir*

English Translation

yo. voy

I go, I am going

you (informal) go, you are going

él/ella/Ud.. va

he/she/you (formal) goes/is going

we go, we are going

you all (informal, Spain) go/are going

they/you all (formal) go/are going

Mandatory Contractions: "al" and "del" (1/2)

a la

*Voy a la biblioteca.* (I go to the library.)

a los

*Vamos a los estadios.* (We go to the stadiums.)

a las

*Ellas van a las iglesias.* (They go to the churches.)

de la

*El libro es de la escuela.* (The book is from the school.)

de los

*Los estudiantes son de los Estados Unidos.* (The students are from the United States.)

Mandatory Contractions: "al" and "del" (2/2)

de las

*La música es de las fiestas.* (The music is from the parties.)
