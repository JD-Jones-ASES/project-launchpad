Key Concepts

The Verb "Ir" (To Go)

Ir is a highly irregular verb used to express movement towards a destination. Its forms must be memorized.

Common Places

A core vocabulary list of places is provided to use with the verb ir.

Mandatory Contractions "al" and "del"

No Contraction with Feminine/Plural Articles

Contractions al and del only occur with el. When a or de precede la, los, or las, no contraction takes place.

Multiple Meanings of "¡Vamos!"

The nosotros/as form, vamos, can mean "we go" or "we are going," but when used with an exclamation mark and lively tone, it often translates to "Let's go!"

The Special Case of "¡Vamos!"

• When used in a statement, it means "we go" or "we are going."

• However, ¡Vamos! can also be an exclamation meaning "Let's go!" or "Come on!"

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the Spanish verb "ir" mean in English?

To go.

Is the Spanish verb "ir" regular or irregular?

It is completely irregular; its forms do not resemble the infinitive.

Conjugate "ir" for "yo" (I).

yo voy

Conjugate "ir" for "tú" (you - singular informal).

tú vas

Conjugate "ir" for "nosotros/nosotras" (we).

nosotros/nosotras vamos

Conjugate "ir" for "ellos/ellas/ustedes" (they/you all).

ellos/ellas/ustedes van

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the Spanish contraction "al" formed from?

a + el

What is the Spanish contraction "del" formed from?

de + el

Are the contractions "al" and "del" optional or mandatory in Spanish?

They are mandatory. You cannot write "a el" or "de el" as two separate words.

When do "a" or "de" NOT contract with a definite article?

When followed by feminine nouns (la) or plural nouns (los/las), e.g., "a la biblioteca" or "de los parques".

How would you say "I go to the park" in Spanish, using the correct contraction?

Yo voy al parque. (El parque is masculine singular, requiring 'al')

What does "la escuela" mean in English?

school
