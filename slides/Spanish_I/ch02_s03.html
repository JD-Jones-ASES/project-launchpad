CHAPTER 2, SECTION 3: MI ESCUELA (MY SCHOOL) Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Expanding School Vocabulary

Learn additional essential words for items found in a school environment and for the people you share it with.

Gender Agreement for Classmates

Understand how to use *el compañero* and *la compañera* to refer to male and female classmates, and *los compañeros* for mixed or male groups.

Cultural Context of Latin American Children's Rhymes

Experience "Pin Pon," a beloved traditional rhyme, to appreciate the rhythm and sounds of Spanish, even if you don't understand every word yet.

Insights into Mexican Schools

Gain knowledge about the structure, customs, and daily life of students in Mexico, including uniforms, school schedules, subjects, and national celebrations.

Reading Comprehension Practice

Apply your growing vocabulary and understanding of Spanish to read and comprehend a simple narrative about a student's school day.

New Vocabulary for Your School Day (1/2)

el papel

(paper): You'll use this word frequently in class.

la tarea

(homework): A very important word for any student! Remember it's feminine.

el uniforme

(uniform): This is a common sight in many schools, particularly outside the U.S.

el/la compañero/a

(classmate): Pay attention to the gender. Use *el compañero* for a male classmate and *la compañera* for a female classmate. If you're talking about a group with both males and females, or just a g...

el salón (de clase)

(classroom): This is where you spend much of your school day.

Reading Practice: "Un día en la escuela de María" (1/2)

Introduction

María introduces herself as a student at "Escuela Primaria Benito Juárez."

Morning Routine

She carries her *mochila* with *libros*, *cuadernos*, and *lápices*.

Classroom Description

Her *salón de clase* has a *pizarra*, many *sillas*, and *mesas*.

Teacher

Her teacher, Señora Gómez, is described as *muy amable* (very kind). This is a new adjective introduced in the reading.

Recess and Homework

During *el recreo*, she talks with her *compañeros*. At two in the afternoon (*A las dos de la tarde*), she goes home with a lot of *tarea*.

Reading Practice: "Un día en la escuela de María" (2/2)

Farewell

She ends with *¡Hasta mañana!* (See you tomorrow!).
