Key Concepts

Two Verbs for "To Be"

Spanish uses two verbs, *ser* and *estar*, where English uses only "to be." *Ser* is for inherent qualities, identity, and permanent characteristics, while *estar* is for temporary states, location...

Importance of Accent Marks

The accent marks on *estás*, *está*, and *están* are vital for correct spelling and pronunciation, distinguishing them from other words or verb forms.

Distinction from *Ser

*: Remember the core difference: *estar* tells *where* or *how* something is *right now*, while *ser* describes *what* something *is* inherently.

Important Terms (1/2)

Estar

A Spanish verb meaning "to be," primarily used for location, temporary states/feelings, and results of actions.

Ser

The other Spanish verb meaning "to be," used for permanent characteristics, identity, origin, time, and possession. (Introduced for contrast).

Conjugation

The process of changing a verb's form to match the subject (who is performing the action) and tense (when the action occurs).

Presente de indicativo

The simple present tense in the indicative mood, used for actions happening now or habitual actions.

Temporary states/feelings

Conditions or emotions that are not permanent and can change over time (e.g., tired, happy, sick, hot).

Important Terms (2/2)

Results of an action

The current condition or state of something that has come about because an action was performed on it (e.g., open, ready).

Location

The place where someone or something is situated.

Accent marks (acentos)

Diacritical marks (like á, é, í, ó, ú) that indicate which syllable in a word receives the stress, and sometimes differentiate words that are otherwise spelled the same.

Key Distinction: *Ser* vs. *Estar* (¡Ojo, cuidado!)

Use *ser* for identity, inherent qualities, and permanent characteristics.

(What it *is*.)

Use *estar* for location and temporary conditions/states.

(Where or how it *is right now*.)
