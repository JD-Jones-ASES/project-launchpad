Mastering Spanish Adjectives: Agreement and Placement

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Adjective Agreement (La Concordancia de los Adjetivos)

In Spanish, descriptive adjectives are not static; they change their form to match the noun they describe in both gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural). This ensures that the adj...

Gender Agreement

Adjectives modify their endings to match the gender of the noun.

Number Agreement

Adjectives modify their endings to match the number of the noun.

Adjective Placement

In Spanish, descriptive adjectives almost always come *after* the noun they describe, unlike in English where they usually come before. For example, "the red car" is *el coche rojo* (car red).

Adjectives Ending in -o/-a

These are the most common type and are highly flexible, having four forms: masculine singular (-o), feminine singular (-a), masculine plural (-os), and feminine plural (-as).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Adjectives Ending in -e or a Consonant

These adjectives are simpler in terms of gender agreement, as they maintain the same form for both masculine and feminine nouns. They only change for number (singular or plural).

Important Terms (1/3)

Adjetivos (Adjectives)

Words that describe or modify nouns (e.g., *alto*, *inteligente*, *azul*).

Concordancia (Agreement)

The grammatical rule requiring adjectives to match the gender and number of the noun they describe.

Género (Gender)

In Spanish, nouns are either masculine (often ending in -o or with *el/un*) or feminine (often ending in -a or with *la/una*). Adjectives must match this.

Número (Number)

Refers to whether a noun is singular (one) or plural (more than one). Adjectives must match this.

Masculino/Femenino (Masculine/Feminine)

The two grammatical genders in Spanish.

Important Terms (2/3)

Singular/Plural (Singular/Plural)

Refers to whether a noun (and its adjective) is referring to one item or multiple items.

¿Cómo es...? (What is... like?)

A common question used to ask for a description of a person or thing.

Joven (Young)

An adjective describing youth, which does not change for gender.

Serio/a (Serious)

An adjective describing seriousness, which changes for gender.

Un poco (A little bit)

A phrase used to qualify an adjective, meaning "somewhat" or "a bit."

Important Terms (3/3)

Muy (Very)

An intensifier, meaning "very," used before an adjective.

Bueno/a (Good)

An adjective meaning "good," which changes for gender.

Favorito/a (Favorite)

An adjective meaning "favorite," which changes for gender.

Describing Someone: Vocabulary from the Conversation (1/2)

¿Cómo es...?

- What is... like? (Used to ask for a description)

joven

- young (Does not change for gender; *joven* for masculine/feminine singular, *jóvenes* for plural)

serio/a

- serious (Changes for gender; *serio* for masculine, *seria* for feminine)

un poco

- a little bit (Used before an adjective to soften it, e.g., *un poco seria* = a bit serious)

muy

- very (Used before an adjective to intensify it, e.g., *muy inteligente* = very intelligent)
