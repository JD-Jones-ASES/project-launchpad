Chapter 6, Section 2

Los Verbos Regulares -ar (Regular -ar Verbs)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Regular -ar Verbs

These verbs constitute a large group in Spanish that follow a consistent conjugation pattern. Learning this pattern allows you to apply it to many verbs.

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb, always ending in -ar, -er, or -ir in Spanish. For this section, we focus on those ending in -ar (e.g., `hablar`, `estudiar`).

Verb Stem

The unchanging core part of a regular verb. It is found by removing the -ar ending from the infinitive (e.g., `hablar` becomes `habl-`).

Verb Ending

The part of the verb that changes to indicate who is performing the action (the subject) and the tense. For regular -ar verbs in the present tense, these endings are predictable.

Conjugation

The process of changing a verb's ending to match the subject (who is doing the action). This is how you make a verb "agree" with its subject.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Subject Pronouns

Words like `yo` (I), `tú` (you, informal), `él`/`ella`/`usted` (he/she/you, formal), `nosotros`/`nosotras` (we), `vosotros`/`vosotras` (you, plural informal in Spain), and `ellos`/`ellas`/`ustedes`...

Latin American Spanish Usage of "You" Plural

In Latin American Spanish, `ustedes` is used for all plural "you" situations, both formal and informal. The `vosotros`/`vosotras` form is reserved primarily for Spain.

Important Terms (1/2)

Infinitive

The base form of a verb, not conjugated for any subject or tense. In this section, these are verbs ending in -ar. (e.g., `hablar`, `estudiar`, `caminar`).

Verb Stem

The part of a regular verb that remains after removing the -ar ending. This part does not change during conjugation. (e.g., `habl-` from `hablar`, `estudi-` from `estudiar`).

Verb Ending

The part of a conjugated verb that is added to the stem to indicate the subject (who is doing the action). (e.g., `-o`, `-as`, `-a`, `-amos`, `-áis`, `-an`).

Conjugation

The process of modifying a verb's ending to show agreement with its subject and tense.

Regular Verb

A verb that follows a consistent and predictable pattern of conjugation, making it easier to learn and apply.

Important Terms (2/2)

Subject Pronoun

A word that takes the place of a noun and indicates the subject of a verb (e.g., `yo`, `tú`, `él`, `ella`, `usted`, `nosotros`, `nosotras`, `vosotros`, `vosotras`, `ellos`, `ellas`, `ustedes`).

Vosotros/Vosotras

The informal second-person plural subject pronoun ("you all") used predominantly in Spain.

Ustedes

The formal second-person plural subject pronoun ("you all"), also used as the informal plural "you all" in Latin American Spanish.

Step 2: Add the Ending That Matches the Subject

Subject Pronoun. Ending

Example with `hablar` (to speak)

`yo` (I). `-o`

`yo hablo` (I speak)

`tú` (you, informal). `-as`

`tú hablas` (you speak)

`él` (he). `-a`

`él habla` (he speaks)

`ella` (she). `-a`

`ella habla` (she speaks)

`usted` (you, formal). `-a`

`usted habla` (you speak)

`nosotros/nosotras` (we). `-amos`

`nosotros hablamos` (we speak)

`vosotros/vosotras` (you, plural informal, Spain). `-áis`

`vosotros habláis` (you all speak)

Model -ar Verb: `hablar` (to speak) (1/2)

Yo hablo

(I speak)

Tú hablas

(You speak - informal singular)

Él/Ella/Usted habla

(He/She speaks, You speak - formal singular)

Nosotros/Nosotras hablamos

(We speak)

Vosotros/Vosotras habláis

(You all speak - informal plural, Spain)

Model -ar Verb: `hablar` (to speak) (2/2)

Ellos/Ellas/Ustedes hablan

(They speak, You all speak - formal/informal plural Latin America)
