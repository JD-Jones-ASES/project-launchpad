The Preterite Tense of -er and -ir Verbs

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Preterite Tense for Completed Actions

The preterite tense is used to describe actions that were completed in the past, often at a specific point in time.

Shared Endings for -er and -ir Verbs

Unlike the present tense, regular -er and -ir verbs use the exact same set of endings in the preterite tense: -í, -iste, -ió, -imos, -ieron.

Conjugation Process

To conjugate a regular -er or -ir verb in the preterite, drop the -er or -ir ending from the infinitive and add the appropriate preterite ending.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Diminutives (-ito/-ita)

Suffixes like -ito and -ita are added to words to convey smallness, affection, or endearment. They are common in Latin American Spanish and are used to express warmth rather than just physical size.

Important Terms (1/2)

Pretérito

The simple past tense in Spanish, used for actions completed in the past.

Verbos -er/-ir

Spanish verbs whose infinitives end in -er (e.g., comer, aprender) or -ir (e.g., vivir, escribir).

Conjugación

The process of changing a verb's ending to match the subject and tense.

Infinitivo

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., comer, vivir).

Stem

The part of the verb that remains after removing the -ar, -er, or -ir ending (e.g., com- from comer).

Important Terms (2/2)

Diminutivo

A suffix (like -ito, -ita, -cito, -cita) added to a word to indicate smallness, tenderness, or affection (e.g., chiquitito, hijito).

Contexto

The surrounding words, sentences, and situation that help clarify the meaning of a word or phrase, especially useful for distinguishing verb tenses.

What is a key difference between the 'nosotros' preterite form of regular -er verbs and their present tense form?

The preterite form has an -i- (e.g., comimos), while the present tense form has an -e- (e.g., comemos). The -i- signals the past tense.
