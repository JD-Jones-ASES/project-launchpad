Important Terms (1/2)

Conjugate

To change the form of a verb to match its subject (who or what is performing the action) and tense (when the action occurs).

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb, typically ending in -ar, -er, or -ir in Spanish (e.g., *vivir* - to live).

Stem

The base part of a verb that remains after removing the infinitive ending.

Regular Verb

A verb that follows predictable patterns for conjugation, meaning its stem does not change, and it uses standard endings.

Present Tense

A verb tense used to describe actions happening now, habitual actions, or general truths.

Important Terms (2/2)

Endings

The suffixes added to a verb stem to indicate the subject and tense.

The Key Difference: -er vs. -ir Verbs

-er verbs

-ir verbs

use **-imos** (e.g., *vivimos* - we live)

Conversation: ¿Qué haces en un día normal? (1/2)

Sofía

Buenos días, Daniel. ¿Qué **comes** por la mañana? (What do you eat in the morning?)

Daniel

**Como** cereal y **bebo** jugo de naranja. ¿Y tú? (I eat cereal and drink orange juice. And you?)

Sofía

Yo **bebo** leche y **como** pan. Después **leo** un poco antes de ir a la escuela. (I drink milk and eat bread. Then I read a little before going to school.)

Daniel

¡Qué bueno! Yo **escribo** en mi diario todas las mañanas. ¿**Vives** cerca de la escuela? (How nice! I write in my journal every morning. Do you live near the school?)

Sofía

Sí, **vivo** en la calle Bolívar. **Corro** a la escuela en diez minutos. ¿Y tú? (Yes, I live on Bolívar Street. I run to school in ten minutes. And you?)

Conversation: ¿Qué haces en un día normal? (2/2)

Daniel

Yo **subo** al autobús a las siete y media. ¡**Vivimos** en partes **diferentes** de la ciudad! (I take the bus at seven thirty. We live in different parts of the city!)

Memory Tip: Routine Anchoring

How it works

Your brain already has your daily routine perfectly mapped out. By consciously attaching new Spanish words and verbs to the actions you perform every day, you create strong, natural associations.

Benefits

This makes words "stick" much more easily than rote memorization from a list. It transforms your daily life into a continuous, effortless study session.
