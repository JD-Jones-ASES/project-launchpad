Important Terms (1/2)

Conjugate

To change the form of a verb to match its subject (who or what is performing the action) and tense (when the action occurs).

Infinitive

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb, typically ending in -ar, -er, or -ir in Spanish (e.g., vivir - to live).

Stem

The base part of a verb that remains after removing the infinitive ending.

Regular Verb

A verb that follows predictable patterns for conjugation, meaning its stem does not change, and it uses standard endings.

Present Tense

A verb tense used to describe actions happening now, habitual actions, or general truths.

Important Terms (2/2)

Endings

The suffixes added to a verb stem to indicate the subject and tense.

Memory Tip: Routine Anchoring

How it works

Your brain already has your daily routine perfectly mapped out. By consciously attaching new Spanish words and verbs to the actions you perform every day, you create strong, natural associations.

Benefits

This makes words "stick" much more easily than rote memorization from a list. It transforms your daily life into a continuous, effortless study session.

Practical Application

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the first step to conjugate a regular -ir verb in the present tense?

Drop the -ir ending from the infinitive to find the verb stem. (e.g., vivir → viv-)

What is the second step to conjugate a regular -ir verb in the present tense?

Add the correct ending for the subject pronoun to the verb stem.

What is the only difference when conjugating regular -er and -ir verbs in the present tense?

The 'nosotros' (we) form is the only difference. All other endings for regular -er and -ir verbs are identical.

How do the 'nosotros' (we) forms differ between regular -er and -ir verbs?

Regular -er verbs use the ending '-emos' (e.g., comemos). Regular -ir verbs use the ending '-imos' (e.g., vivimos).

What is the typical ending for the 'yo' (I) form of regular -ir verbs?

-o (e.g., vivo, escribo, subo)

What is the typical ending for the 'tú' (you, informal) form of regular -ir verbs?

-es (e.g., vives, escribes, subes)

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the typical ending for the 'él/ella/usted' (he/she/you, formal) form of regular -ir verbs?

-e (e.g., vive, escribe, sube)

What is the typical ending for the 'nosotros/as' (we) form of regular -ir verbs?

-imos (e.g., vivimos, escribimos, subimos)

What is the typical ending for the 'ellos/ellas/ustedes' (they/you all) form of regular -ir verbs?

-en (e.g., viven, escriben, suben)
