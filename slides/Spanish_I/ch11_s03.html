Important Terms (1/3)

el mundo

(the world): Refers to the Earth or a global scope.

muchos

(many): An adjective indicating a large quantity.

el sur

(the south): A cardinal direction, often used to describe regions.

casi nunca

(almost never): An adverbial phrase indicating infrequency.

cerca del ecuador

(near the equator): Describes a geographical location close to the Earth's equator.

Important Terms (2/3)

todo el año

(all year long): An adverbial phrase indicating something happens continuously throughout the year.

¡Uy!

(Ugh! / Wow!): An exclamation expressing surprise, annoyance, or emphasis.

de verdad

(really): An adverbial phrase used for emphasis or to confirm truth.

ropa abrigada

(warm clothing): Clothing designed to provide warmth.

al aire libre

(outdoors, in the open air): Refers to activities or locations outside.

Important Terms (3/3)

la tuya

(yours): A possessive pronoun, referring to "your favorite [season]" in the context of the conversation.

Climate in Different Countries: A Global Perspective

Mexico (the South)

Experiences very hot summers with daily rain during the rainy season.

Argentina (Buenos Aires)

Has cold, humid, windy, and often cloudy winters.

Chile (the North/Atacama Desert)

Features an extremely dry desert climate where it `casi nunca llueve` (almost never rains).

Colombia (near the Equator)

Does not have four distinct seasons but rather a dry season and a rainy season, due to its proximity to the equator.

Venezuela

Showcases even more diversity, with mountains where it sometimes snows and tropical beaches (`playas tropicales`) that are sunny and hot `todo el año` (all year long).
