Key Concepts (1/2)

What two Spanish past tenses are primarily used for telling stories?

The imperfecto and the pretérito.

In the 'Holiday Memory Method,' what does the imperfecto represent metaphorically?

The camera panning the scene, setting the background and atmosphere.

In the 'Holiday Memory Method,' what does the pretérito represent metaphorically?

The camera zooming in on each specific moment or event.

What types of information does the imperfecto convey when narrating a past story?

Background descriptions, what things looked like, the weather, who was present, what people were doing in the background, and how you felt.

What types of information does the pretérito convey when narrating a past story?

Specific, completed actions or events that move the story forward (what happened first, next, the exciting moment, how it ended).

If you want to describe what the place looked like in a past memory, which tense would you use?

The imperfecto.

Key Concepts (2/2)

If you want to describe a specific action that happened first in a sequence of events, which tense would you use?

The pretérito.

Telling Stories in the Past.

Identify an imperfect verb from the 'El Día de los Muertos de la familia García' story that describes a state or background.

'Era' (from 'Era el dos de noviembre') or 'estaba' (from 'toda la familia García estaba reunida').

Identify a preterite verb from the 'El Día de los Muertos de la familia García' story that describes a completed action.

'Llegaron' (from 'los niños llegaron corriendo') or 'pusieron' (from 'pusieron las fotos').

What is the primary function of verbs like 'había' and 'era' in Luis's conversation about the party?

To set the scene and provide background descriptions (imperfect tense).

What is the primary function of verbs like 'Fue,' 'regaló,' and 'cortamos' in Luis's conversation about the party?

To describe specific, completed events that occurred during the party (preterite tense).
