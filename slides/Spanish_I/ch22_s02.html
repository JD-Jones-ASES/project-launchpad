Mis Metas (My Goals) - Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Expressing Personal Goals and Aspirations

Learn verbs and phrases essential for articulating what you dream of becoming, hope to achieve, and intend to do in the future.

Three Ways to Talk About the Future

Understand the nuances and common usage of ir + a + infinitivo (most common in daily speech), the simple future tense (formal/written), and quiero que + subjuntivo (expressing desires for others).

The Importance of the Subjunctive

Reinforce your understanding of the subjunctive mood for expressing wishes, hopes, and necessities, particularly when discussing desires or expectations for others.

Avoiding False Cognates

Recognize and correctly use the word éxito (success) and differentiate it from the English word "exit," which in Spanish is la salida.

Youth Contributions in Latin America

Gain cultural insight into how young people in various Latin American countries are actively shaping their future through environmentalism, entrepreneurship, journalism, and cultural preservation.

Key Vocabulary for Goals and Future

Soñar con ser [profesión]

"To dream of being [profession]." For example, Sueño con ser médica (I dream of being a doctor).

Esperar lograr [meta]

"To hope to achieve [goal]." For example, Espero lograr mis metas (I hope to achieve my goals).

Ir a intentar [acción]

"To be going to try to [action]." For example, Voy a intentar hablar más español (I'm going to try to speak more Spanish).

Debemos continuar [acción]

"We must continue [action]." For example, ¡Debemos continuar aprendiendo! (We must continue learning!).

Dialogue Analysis: Planes para el futuro

Mateo's plans

Voy a estudiar ciencias. Quiero ser científico. (Uses ir + a + infinitivo and Quiero ser...)

Valentina's dreams

Yo sueño con ser artista. Espero que mi familia apoye mi decisión. (Uses Sueño con ser... and Espero que + subjuntivo).

Mateo's advice

Es importante que tú sigas tu talento. (Uses Es importante que + subjuntivo).

Mateo's challenge

Para mí, es necesario que yo aprenda más matemáticas. ¡Pero voy a intentarlo! (Uses Es necesario que + subjuntivo and ir + a + infinitivo).

Reading Comprehension: Mis planes para el futuro

• She uses espero que mi familia tenga (I hope my family has) and es importante que todos los jóvenes logren (it's important that all young people achieve) — examples of the subjunctive for hopes...

• Quiero que mis amigos y yo hagamos un esfuerzo (I want my friends and I to make an effort) is another example of querer que + subjuntivo.

• Ojalá que el mundo del futuro sea más justo y más verde (Hopefully the world of the future will be more just and greener) uses Ojalá que which always triggers the subjunctive.

• Her own plans are expressed using ir + a + infinitivo: Voy a estudiar mucho, voy a practicar español e inglés, y voy a intentar ser una buena líder.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary focus of the "Mis metas" section?

To learn vocabulary for goals, achievements, and possibilities, and to review how to talk about the future in Spanish.

What is the Spanish word `éxito`?

Success.

What is a common false cognate for `éxito` among English speakers?

English speakers often mistakenly think `éxito` means 'exit'.

What is the correct Spanish word for 'exit'?

`La salida`.

Translate the sentence: 'El estudiante tuvo mucho éxito.'

The student had great success.

What does the Spanish verb `soñar con` mean?

To dream of.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Give an example sentence using `soñar con`.

`Sueño con ser médica.` (I dream of being a doctor.)

What does the Spanish verb `lograr` mean?

To achieve, to attain, to succeed in.

Give an example sentence using `lograr`.

`Espero lograr mis metas.` (I hope to achieve my goals.)

What does the Spanish verb `intentar` mean?

To try, to attempt.

Give an example sentence using `intentar`.

`Voy a intentar hablar más español.` (I'm going to try to speak more Spanish.)

What does the Spanish verb `continuar` mean?

To continue.
