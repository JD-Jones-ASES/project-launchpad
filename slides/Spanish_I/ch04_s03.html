Expanded Descriptive Vocabulary

Learning new adjectives to express a wider range of characteristics, including appearance (pretty, ugly, thin, fat), personality (fun, boring, friendly, unfriendly), and state (new, old).

Connecting Multiple Adjectives

When two or more adjectives are used to describe the same noun and are connected by "y" (and), all adjectives must agree with the noun in gender and number.

Using "Ser" for Descriptions

Adjectives of description are typically used with the verb "ser" (to be) to describe inherent or permanent qualities of people and things.

Adjective Agreement: The Golden Rule

• Mi padre es alto y delgado. (My father is tall and thin.)

• Mis tías son simpáticas y divertidas. (My aunts are friendly and fun.)

• Los libros son grandes y aburridos. (The books are big and boring.)

Cultural Note: Using "Gordo/a" and "Viejo/a"

• They appear frequently in everyday conversation, children's songs, and stories without any negative intent.

• For example, describing a cartoon character, a pet, or even a cherished elderly relative (like your great-grandmother) as "gordo" or "vieja" is perfectly normal and acceptable.

Applying Adjectives in Context: "La familia Rodríguez"

Lucía

is described as alta y delgada (tall and thin) and muy inteligente (very intelligent).

Roberto

is bajo (short) but muy divertido (very fun).

Miguel

is alto (tall) and muy serio (very serious). His books are grandes y aburridos (big and boring).

Isabella

is pequeña (small) and muy simpática (very friendly). She makes cosas bonitas (pretty things).

Carlos

(the baby) is gordo y muy divertido (fat/chubby and very fun).
