Mastering Irregular Verbs: The "-go" Pattern and Weather Expressions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The "-go" Irregularity

Four important verbs (`hacer`, `decir`, `poner`, `salir`) are irregular only in their `yo` form, which consistently ends in "-go." All other forms conjugate regularly for -er or -ir verbs.

Stem Change in `Decir`

While `decir` follows the "-go" pattern in the `yo` form (`digo`), it also exhibits an `e` to `i` stem change in all other forms except `nosotros`.

Multiple Uses of `Hacer`

This verb is highly versatile, used for "to do," "to make," asking questions, and describing weather.

`Hacer` for Weather Expressions

In Spanish, `hacer` (specifically the third person singular form, `hace`) is used for most weather expressions (e.g., `hace frío` - "it's cold"), unlike English which uses "to be."

Memorization Strategy: The Story Method

A personalized, vivid story incorporating the `yo` forms (`hago`, `digo`, `pongo`, `salgo`) can be an effective way to remember these irregular conjugations.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Additional Regular -AR Verbs

A set of common -ar verbs (`pasar`, `llevar`, `esperar`, `entrar`, `buscar`) are introduced, reinforcing regular -ar conjugation patterns and highlighting their diverse meanings.

Important Terms (1/3)

hacer

(to do, to make) - A versatile verb used for actions, creation, and weather.

decir

(to say, to tell) - Used for communicating speech or information.

poner

(to put, to place, to set) - Implies placing an object somewhere or setting something up.

salir

(to leave, to go out) - Describes departing from a place or engaging in social activities outside.

-go pattern

The unique characteristic of `hacer`, `decir`, `poner`, and `salir` where their `yo` (I) form ends with `-go`.

Important Terms (2/3)

Stem change (e → i)

A grammatical phenomenon where the vowel in the stem of a verb changes during conjugation; specifically seen in `decir` (e.g., `dice` from `decir`).

pasar

(to pass, to happen) - A regular -ar verb with two common meanings.

llevar

(to carry, to wear) - A regular -ar verb meaning to transport something or to have clothing on.

esperar

(to wait, to hope) - A regular -ar verb expressing anticipation or desire.

entrar

(to enter) - A regular -ar verb meaning to go inside.

Important Terms (3/3)

buscar

(to look for, to search) - A regular -ar verb meaning to seek out.

Irregular Verbs: The "-go" Pattern

The `yo` form is irregular and ends in `-go`

All other forms are regular

Once you know the `yo` form, the rest of the conjugations for these verbs follow the standard patterns for -er or -ir verbs.

Hacer (to do, to make)

Decir (to say, to tell)

Poner (to put, to place, to set)

Salir (to leave, to go out)

Special Use of `Hacer` for Weather

• `Hace frío.` (It's cold. – Literally: "It makes cold.")

• `Hace calor.` (It's hot. – Literally: "It makes hot.")

• `Hace sol.` (It's sunny. – Literally: "It makes sun.")

• `Hace viento.` (It's windy. – Literally: "It makes wind.")

What is the main irregularity pattern for the verbs hacer, decir, poner, and salir in the present tense?

How do the yo forms of hacer, decir, poner, and salir conjugate in the present tense?

yo hago, yo digo, yo pongo, yo salgo.

Are the non-yo forms of hacer, decir, poner, and salir entirely regular?

Yes, with the exception of decir, which also has an e → i stem change in all forms except yo and nosotros.

List three common uses for the verb hacer.

Doing homework (hacer la tarea), making food (hacer la cena), asking questions (hacer preguntas), and describing weather (hace frío/calor).

Conjugate hacer in the present tense (all forms).

yo hago, tú haces, él/ella/Ud. hace, nosotros/as hacemos, vosotros/as hacéis, ellos/ellas/Uds. hacen.

Besides the -go ending in the yo form, what other irregularity does decir have?

Decir has an e → i stem change in all forms except yo and nosotros.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does the yo form of decir (digo) differ from other stem-changing verbs?

The yo form (digo) has the -go ending but does not have the stem change (e → i).

What does the common Spanish phrase ¿Qué dices? mean?

"What do you say?"

Conjugate decir in the present tense (all forms).

yo digo, tú dices, él/ella/Ud. dice, nosotros/as decimos, vosotros/as decís, ellos/ellas/Uds. dicen.

What does poner la mesa mean?

To set the table.

How regular is poner outside of its yo form?

It is completely regular for an -er verb in all forms except yo.

Conjugate poner in the present tense (all forms).

yo pongo, tú pones, él/ella/Ud. pone, nosotros/as ponemos, vosotros/as ponéis, ellos/ellas/Uds. ponen.
