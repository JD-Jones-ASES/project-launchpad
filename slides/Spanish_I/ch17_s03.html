Important Terms (1/2)

El pretérito (The Preterite)

A past tense in Spanish used for actions completed at a definite point in the past.

Verbos en el pretérito

Verbs conjugated in the preterite tense, indicating completed past actions.

Palabras de secuencia (Sequencing words)

Words or phrases that help organize events in chronological order. Examples include:

Fue

The preterite form of ser (to be) or ir (to go), meaning "it was" or "he/she went." In this context, it is used as "it was."

"El cumpleaños de Laura" (Laura's Birthday) (1/3)

El sábado pasado, Laura _celebró_ su cumpleaños.

(Last Saturday, Laura celebrated her birthday.) — celebró (celebrated) is a completed action.

Primero, su familia _preparó_ una fiesta especial...

(First, her family prepared a special party...) — preparó (prepared) indicates the first action.

Su mamá _decoró_ la casa... y su papá _cocinó_ muchos platos...

(Her mom decorated the house... and her dad cooked many dishes...) — decoró (decorated) and cocinó (cooked) are completed actions.

Después, los amigos de Laura _llegaron_ a las seis...

(Afterwards, Laura's friends arrived at six...) — llegaron (arrived) marks the next event in sequence.

Todos _saludaron_ a Laura y le _entregaron_ regalos...

(Everyone greeted Laura and gave her gifts...) — saludaron (greeted) and entregaron (gave) are further completed actions.

"El cumpleaños de Laura" (Laura's Birthday) (2/3)

Luego, _bailaron_ y _cantaron_ por más de una hora.

(Then, they danced and sang for over an hour.) — bailaron (danced) and cantaron (sang) describe the activities that followed.

De repente, alguien _apagó_ las luces y todos _gritaron_: “¡Sorpresa!”

(Suddenly, someone turned off the lights and everyone shouted: “Surprise!”) — apagó (turned off) and gritaron (shouted) introduce a sudden event.

Laura _escuchó_ la música favorita y _caminó_ al centro del salón.

(Laura listened to her favorite music and walked to the center of the room.) — escuchó (listened) and caminó (walked) are Laura's reactions.

Finalmente, todos _comieron_ una torta de chocolate y _tomaron_ jugo...

(Finally, everyone ate a chocolate cake and drank juice...) — comieron (ate) and tomaron (drank) signal the concluding activities.

Laura _habló_ con cada amigo y _expresó_ su gratitud.

(Laura spoke with each friend and expressed her gratitude.) — habló (spoke) and expresó (expressed) complete Laura's interaction.

"El cumpleaños de Laura" (Laura's Birthday) (3/3)

¡_Fue_ un día perfecto!

(It was a perfect day!) — Fue describes the overall state of the day, a completed "being."

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary function of preterite verbs in a story, as demonstrated in "El cumpleaños de Laura"?

They move the story forward from one event to the next, describing completed actions in the past.

How do sequencing words like "primero" and "después" enhance a narrative told using the preterite tense?

They connect sentences and show the chronological order in which events happened, making the story clear and easy to follow.

In the story "El cumpleaños de Laura," what kind of actions are described using the preterite tense?

Completed, specific actions that occurred at a definite point in the past (e.g., Laura celebrated, her mom decorated, her friends arrived).

What does the word "fue" mean when used in the context of "¡Fue un día perfecto!"?

It means "It was." It is the preterite form of the verb "ser" (to be).

Describe the "Mental Timeline" technique for mastering the preterite tense.

It's a visualization technique where you imagine a horizontal line with "AHORA" (now) in the middle. The past is to the left, and the future is to the right.

Where on the "Mental Timeline" do you place events described by the preterite tense?

As a 'dot' on the left side of the line, representing a completed, finished action that happened and is over.

What is the first step in practicing the "Mental Timeline" for a preterite sentence?

Think of the completed event you want to describe (e.g., "I swam yesterday").
