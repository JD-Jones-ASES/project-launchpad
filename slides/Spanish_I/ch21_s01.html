Important Terms (1/2)

Pretérito

The simple past tense in Spanish, used for completed actions or events that occurred at a specific point in time. Often translated as "I did" or "I went."

Imperfecto

The descriptive past tense in Spanish, used for ongoing actions, habitual events, descriptions, and background information in the past. Often translated as "I was doing" or "I used to do."

Acciones completadas y específicas

Completed, specific actions; a key use for the pretérito.

El fondo en progreso

Ongoing or in-progress background; a key use for the imperfecto.

Acciones habituales en el pasado

Habitual past actions; describes what someone "used to do" or "would always do," using the imperfecto.

Important Terms (2/2)

Palabras clave

"Signal words"; time expressions or adverbs that strongly suggest using either the pretérito or imperfecto.

Angry, grumpy.

The Foundation: Two Windows to the Past

• The Imperfecto is like a video playing in the background. It shows what was happening continuously, habitually, or describes the setting, people, and feelings. It provides the context.

• The Pretérito is like a series of photographs. It captures specific, completed actions that occurred at particular moments, moving the story forward.

When to Use the Pretérito (The "Photographs")

Completed, specific actions

These are "one-time" events that have a clear beginning and end.

Actions that interrupt the background

When something ongoing is suddenly broken by a new event. The ongoing action is imperfecto, the interruption is pretérito.

A sequence of events

To narrate a series of actions that follow each other in order.

Specific number of times

If you explicitly state how many times an action occurred.

When to Use the Imperfecto (The "Video")

Ongoing/in-progress background

Actions that were happening over a period of time, without a clear end in mind, or that set the scene.

Habitual past actions

Describes what someone "used to do" or "would always do" repeatedly in the past.

Description of people, places, and weather

To tell what things were like.

Age, time, and feelings in the past

These are often states or conditions, not discrete events.

A Story Example (`Un cuento de dos tiempos`)

Fui

a la ventana. (pretérito - specific, completed action, sequence)

Abrí

la puerta y corrí afuera. (pretérito - sequence of specific actions)

Verbs that Change Meaning (`Ojo cuidado`)

Supe

la verdad ayer. (Pretérito: I found out the truth yesterday. - A specific event, gaining knowledge.)

Sabía

la verdad. (Imperfecto: I knew the truth (all along). - An ongoing state of knowledge.)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the two main past tenses in Spanish grammar that are often compared?

The pretérito and the imperfecto.

What is the core difference in function between the pretérito and the imperfecto?

The pretérito describes specific, completed events, while the imperfecto describes ongoing background, habitual actions, or descriptions in the past.

Which Spanish past tense is analogous to 'a photograph of a completed action' (una fotografía de una acción completada)?

El pretérito.

Which Spanish past tense is analogous to 'a video of what was happening in the background' (un video de lo que ocurría en el fondo)?

El imperfecto.

What is the key question to ask yourself when deciding whether to use the pretérito or the imperfecto?

Is this background/description (use imperfecto) or a specific, completed event (use pretérito)?

List one specific use for the pretérito tense related to actions.

To describe completed and specific actions (e.g., 'Comí tamales en la fiesta.').

Key Concepts (2/2)

When an action interrupts an ongoing background event, which tense is used for the interrupting action?

The pretérito (e.g., in 'Dormía cuando sonó el teléfono,' 'sonó' is pretérito).

Which tense is used to describe a sequence of events (e.g., 'first, then, finally')?

The pretérito (e.g., 'Llegué, saludé a todos y bailé toda la noche.').

If you mention an action that happened a specific number of times in the past, which tense should you use?

The pretérito (e.g., 'Lo llamé tres veces.').

List one specific use for the imperfecto tense related to ongoing events.

To describe ongoing or in-progress background (e.g., 'Llovía y la música sonaba.').

Which tense is used to describe habitual past actions, equivalent to 'used to' or 'would always'?

The imperfecto (e.g., 'Cada año, mi familia celebraba la Navidad con una posada.').

What tense do you use to describe people, places, or weather in the past?

The imperfecto (e.g., 'La abuela era muy alta y el jardín estaba lleno de flores.').
