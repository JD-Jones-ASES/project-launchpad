Important Terms (1/4)

el barrio

A distinct district or section of a city, often possessing its own unique identity, culture, and character.

la vecindad

Emphasizes the community of neighbors living closely together; in Mexico City, it also refers to a specific type of shared-courtyard housing complex in historic neighborhoods.

rascacielos

Skyscraper (literally "sky-scratcher").

el sol

The sun.

solecito

Little sun (an affectionate diminutive).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What percentage of Latin Americans reside in cities?

More than 80 percent.

Explain the difference in meaning between "el barrio" and "la vecindad."

How is the Spanish word "rascacielos" formed, and what does it mean?

It combines "rascar" (to scratch) and "cielos" (skies), literally meaning "sky-scraper." It refers to a skyscraper.

Provide the Spanish pattern for describing a country or city's location relative to another, using a compass point.

Use "al + compass direction + de + place name," e.g., "México está al norte de Guatemala."

Name two traditional Latin American children's songs discussed in the text.

"Sol solecito" and "Que llueva."

What is the central theme of the children's song "Sol solecito"?

It's a plea for the sun to come out and warm cold little hands, often sung after a rainy day.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How is the song "Que llueva" described, and what activity often accompanies it?

It's a circle-game song where children hold hands, dance, and sing about rain and a little bird.

What is a key benefit of traditional children's songs for Spanish language learning?

They are one of the oldest ways children learn Spanish vocabulary naturally across generations and regions, without realizing they are studying.

What is the historical significance of Mexico City's Zócalo?

It is one of the world's largest public squares, built on the ruins of the ancient Aztec capital, Tenochtitlán.

Which Latin American capital is known as the "Paris of South America," and why?

Buenos Aires, Argentina, due to its European-style boulevards, elegant architecture, and vibrant café culture.

What contributes to the distinct identity of Buenos Aires's neighborhoods?

Each neighborhood, like colorful La Boca, elegant Recoleta, or bohemian San Telmo, possesses its own unique character.

When was Lima, Peru, founded as one of the oldest Spanish-colonial capitals in South America?

In 1535.
