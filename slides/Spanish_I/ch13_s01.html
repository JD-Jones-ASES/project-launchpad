Chapter 13, Section 1: En la Tienda (At the Store) Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Essential Shopping Vocabulary

Learning words for various items of clothing (`la ropa`) and terms related to the shopping environment (`la tienda`, `el precio`, `el dinero`) is fundamental for real-world interactions.

Using `llevar` for Wearing

The versatile verb `llevar` is key for describing what someone is wearing, directly translating to "to wear" in this context.

In many Latin American markets, bargaining is not only accepted but expected and considered a social ritual, offering a unique cultural experience beyond a simple transaction.

Important Terms (1/6)

la ropa

clothing, clothes (always used in singular)

el zapato / los zapatos

shoe / shoes

la camisa

shirt (button-up or collared)

el vestido

dress

el pantalón / los pantalones

pants, trousers

Important Terms (2/6)

la falda

skirt

el calcetín / los calcetines

sock / socks

el sombrero

hat (wide-brimmed)

la gorra

baseball cap

el bolso

bag, purse, handbag

Important Terms (3/6)

la joya / las joyas

jewel / jewels, piece of jewelry

la talla

size (clothing size)

llevar

to wear, to carry

la tienda

store, shop

el mercado

market (open-air or indoor)

Important Terms (4/6)

el precio

price

el regalo

gift, present

el dinero

money

caro/cara

expensive

barato/barata

cheap, inexpensive

Important Terms (5/6)

la oferta

sale, special offer (`en oferta` = on sale)

el descuento

discount

el recibo

receipt

la tarjeta

card (e.g., `la tarjeta de crédito/débito` = credit/debit card)

el efectivo

cash (`pagar en efectivo` = to pay in cash)

Important Terms (6/6)

change (money returned); also: change (general)

regatear

to bargain, to haggle

onomatopeya

onomatopoeia (a word that imitates the sound it represents)
