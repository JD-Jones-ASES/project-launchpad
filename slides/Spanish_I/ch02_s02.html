Chapter 2, Section 2

Understanding Spanish Articles and Plural Nouns

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Gender of Nouns

Every noun in Spanish is either masculine (m.) or feminine (f.). This inherent quality dictates the form of the article used with the noun.

Article-Noun Agreement

Articles (words like "the" or "a/an") must always match the noun they precede in both gender (masculine/feminine) and number (singular/plural). This is a core rule of Spanish grammar.

Definite Articles

These articles correspond to "the" in English. Spanish has four forms: *el* (masculine singular), *la* (feminine singular), *los* (masculine plural), and *las* (feminine plural).

Indefinite Articles

These articles correspond to "a," "an," or "some" in English. Spanish has four forms: *un* (masculine singular), *una* (feminine singular), *unos* (masculine plural), and *unas* (feminine plural).

Pluralization Rules

Nouns become plural by adding *-s* if they end in a vowel, or *-es* if they end in a consonant. A special rule applies to nouns ending in *-z*, which changes to *-c* before adding *-es*.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Learning Nouns with Articles

Due to gender and exceptions, it's crucial to always learn a noun along with its definite article (e.g., *el libro*, not just *libro*) to correctly remember its gender and proper usage.

Important Terms (1/2)

Article

A word that precedes a noun to indicate whether the noun is specific or general. In Spanish, articles must match the gender and number of the noun.

Definite Article

Corresponds to "the" in English (*el, la, los, las*). Used to refer to a specific noun.

Indefinite Article

Corresponds to "a," "an," or "some" in English (*un, una, unos, unas*). Used to refer to a non-specific or general noun.

Masculine (m.)

One of the two grammatical genders for nouns in Spanish. Often, nouns ending in *-o* are masculine.

Feminine (f.)

One of the two grammatical genders for nouns in Spanish. Often, nouns ending in *-a* are feminine.

Important Terms (2/2)

Singular

Refers to a single item or person.

Plural

Refers to multiple items or people (two or more).

Noun

A word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.
