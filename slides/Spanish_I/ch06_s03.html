Mi día (My Day) Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Integrating Time and Verbs

Learn how to combine specific times (e.g., `a las ocho`) with -ar verbs (e.g., `llego`) to express when daily activities occur.

Essential Time Expressions

Master new vocabulary for parts of the day, such as `la mañana` (the morning), `al mediodía` (at noon), `la tarde` (the afternoon), and `la noche` (the night/evening), and how to use them with prep...

Distinguishing `mañana`

Understand the dual meaning of `mañana` as both an adverb ("tomorrow") and a noun ("the morning"), relying on context for clarification.

Cultural Perspectives on Punctuality

Explore the concept of "hora latina" and "hora cubana," where relaxed punctuality is common for social events, contrasting it with the expectation of punctuality in professional and academic settings.

Describing Daily Routines

Practice reading and understanding descriptions of daily schedules, like Ana's, and engaging in simple conversations about what someone does during their day.

Conversation: `¿Cómo es tu día?` (What's Your Day Like?)

Asking about time of arrival

`¿A qué hora llegas a la escuela?` (At what time do you arrive at school?)

Asking about activities in parts of the day

`¿Estudias español por las mañanas o por las tardes?` (Do you study Spanish in the mornings or in the afternoons?)

Asking about activities after school

`¿Escuchas música después de la escuela?` (Do you listen to music after school?)
