Important Terms (1/2)

El imperfecto (The Imperfect Tense)

A past tense in Spanish used to describe continuous, habitual, or descriptive actions and states in the past, rather than single completed events.

El pretérito (The Preterite Tense)

A past tense in Spanish used to describe completed actions that occurred at a specific point in the past. Often contrasted with the imperfect.

Ongoing action

An action that was in progress at a particular time in the past.

Habitual action

An action that was repeated regularly in the past, something one "used to do."

Background description

Details about the setting, weather, or circumstances surrounding past events.

Important Terms (2/2)

Signal words

Adverbs or phrases that often indicate the use of a particular verb tense, such as "todos los días" (every day) for the imperfect.

Conjugation

The process of changing a verb's ending to match the subject (who is performing the action) and the tense (when the action occurs).

Tildes (Accent Marks)

Diacritical marks used in Spanish to indicate stress or distinguish between words. Crucial for correct imperfect conjugation.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary function of the imperfect tense in Spanish?

The imperfect tense describes actions or states in the past that were ongoing, habitual, or set the scene, rather than single completed events.

How does the imperfect tense compare to the preterite tense using the 'snapshot vs. video' analogy?

The preterite is like a 'snapshot' of a single, completed event, while the imperfect is like a 'video playing in the background,' showing how things were or ...

List one main purpose for using the imperfect tense.

To describe ongoing actions or states in the past (e.g., 'The children were playing in the park').

List a second main purpose for using the imperfect tense.

To describe habitual or repeated actions in the past (e.g., 'We always used to eat rice and beans').

What kind of information does the imperfect tense provide as 'background description'?

It describes the setting, such as the weather, environment, or mood, while something else was happening (e.g., 'It was cold and it was snowing').

What specific aspects of the past (besides actions) does the imperfect tense commonly describe?

Age, time, or feelings in the past (e.g., 'I was ten years old,' 'It was three in the afternoon').

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the imperfect tense endings for regular -ar verbs?

-aba, -abas, -aba, -ábamos, -abais, -aban

What are the imperfect tense endings for regular -er and -ir verbs?

-ía, -ías, -ía, -íamos, -íais, -ían

How many irregular verbs are there in the entire imperfect tense, and what are they?

There are only three irregular verbs in the imperfect tense: ser, ir, and ver.

Conjugate 'hablar' (to speak) in the imperfect tense for 'yo' and 'él/ella/Ud.'.

Yo hablaba, Él/ella/Ud. hablaba.

What is notable about the 'yo' and 'él/ella/Ud.' forms for -ar verbs in the imperfect tense?

They share the exact same form (e.g., hablaba), and context tells you who is speaking.

Conjugate 'comer' (to eat) in the imperfect tense for 'nosotros' and 'ellos/ellas/Uds.'.

Nosotros comíamos, Ellos/ellas/Uds. comían.
