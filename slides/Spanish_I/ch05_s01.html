Grammar Spotlight: The "Stressed A-" Rule (¡Fíjate!)

• The noun remains feminine. This means that any adjectives modifying it must be feminine. For example, el agua fría (the cold water), not el agua frío.

• In the plural, the regular feminine article las is used: las aguas.

• Other common nouns that follow this rule include el hambre (hunger) and el águila (the eagle).

Key Concepts (1/2)

What role do mealtimes often play in Latin American culture?

Mealtimes are often shared with family and friends, serving as moments to talk, laugh, and celebrate.

Why do we say 'el agua' instead of 'la agua' in Spanish, even though 'agua' is a feminine noun?

When a feminine noun starts with a stressed 'a-' sound in the singular, Spanish uses 'el' to avoid two 'a' sounds crashing together.

Is 'agua' still considered a feminine noun despite using 'el' in the singular? How is this shown with adjectives and plural forms?

Yes, 'agua' is still feminine. This is shown by using feminine adjectives (e.g., 'el agua fría') and feminine plural articles ('las aguas').

Name two other Spanish nouns, besides 'agua,' that follow the rule of using 'el' in the singular despite being feminine.

'El hambre' (hunger) and 'el águila' (the eagle).

Which country is highlighted as one of the great food capitals of the world in the text?

Peru.

Describe 'El ceviche,' including its main ingredients and cultural significance.

El ceviche is a famous Peruvian dish made with fresh seafood marinated in lime juice with onions and hot peppers. It is a source of national pride.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the key ingredients of 'La papa a la huancaína'?

Boiled potatoes covered in a creamy, spicy cheese sauce made from ají amarillo (yellow chili pepper).

What historical significance does Peru hold regarding potatoes?

Peru is the homeland of the potato itself, with over 3,000 varieties growing in the Andes mountains.

What is a well-known traditional dish from Colombia mentioned in the text?

La arepa (corn cake).

For what famous food is Mexico known according to the text?

Los tacos.

What popular culinary tradition is Argentina famous for?

El asado (barbecue).

Which country celebrates 'la hallaca' at festivals? What type of dish is it?
