Important Terms (1/3)

Ir + a + infinitivo

A grammatical construction meaning "to be going to do [verb]". Example: Voy a estudiar (I am going to study).

El futuro próximo

The "near future" tense in Spanish, synonymous with the ir + a + infinitivo structure.

Infinitivo

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., estudiar - to study, comer - to eat).

El patio

A courtyard or patio, often an outdoor space connected to a house.

La plaza / La plaza mayor / El parque central

The central square or park in a Latin American town or city, serving as a focal point for community life.

Important Terms (2/3)

La iglesia

The church; often a prominent building surrounding the central plaza.

El ayuntamiento

The town hall or city council building; another key structure typically found around the plaza.

El paseo

A stroll or walk; also refers to the tradition of families and friends strolling around the plaza in the evenings.

La farmacia

The pharmacy.

El banco

The bank.

The school.

El supermercado

Key Concepts (1/2)

What Spanish grammatical structure is used to express the "near future"?

Ir conjugated + a + infinitive.

How do you form the "near future" in Spanish using "ir"?

Conjugate the verb "ir" (to go), add the preposition "a", and then use an infinitive verb (e.g., 'voy a estudiar').

What does the Spanish structure "ir + a + infinitivo" mean in English?

It means "to be going to do [something]".

Why is the "ir + a + infinitivo" structure considered very useful in Spanish?

It is widely used by Spanish speakers, especially in Latin America, for everyday future plans, allowing expression of almost any future idea.

How does the use of "ir + a + infinitivo" compare to the full future tense in everyday Spanish conversation?

While Spanish has a full future tense, "ir + a + infinitivo" is more commonly used for immediate or everyday future plans, particularly in Latin America.

Give a Spanish example sentence for "I am going to eat dinner."

Voy a comer la cena.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What type of song is "El patio de mi casa"?

It is one of the most beloved traditional children's game songs in the Spanish-speaking world.

What is the cultural significance of "El patio de mi casa"?

It's a living piece of shared cultural heritage, sung for generations across Latin America and Spain, often accompanied by movements.

What specific word related to a house is prominently featured in the song "El patio de mi casa"?

El patio (the courtyard or patio).

How do songs with movements, like "El patio de mi casa," help people remember words?

Connecting physical actions and rhythm to words creates stronger memory associations, making vocabulary recall more effective and permanent.

Describe the "mental map" strategy for learning Spanish vocabulary.

It's a strategy where you visualize your own neighborhood and mentally label familiar places in Spanish (e.g., 'la escuela,' 'el supermercado').

What is the benefit of using the "mental map" strategy for vocabulary acquisition?

By repeatedly connecting Spanish words to real, familiar locations, your brain forms stronger, more permanent associations, improving retention.
