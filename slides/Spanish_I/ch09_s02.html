Important Terms (1/3)

Ir + a + infinitivo

A grammatical construction meaning "to be going to do [verb]". Example: *Voy a estudiar* (I am going to study).

El futuro próximo

The "near future" tense in Spanish, synonymous with the *ir + a + infinitivo* structure.

Infinitivo

The basic, unconjugated form of a verb (e.g., *estudiar* - to study, *comer* - to eat).

El patio

A courtyard or patio, often an outdoor space connected to a house.

La plaza / La plaza mayor / El parque central

The central square or park in a Latin American town or city, serving as a focal point for community life.

Important Terms (2/3)

La iglesia

The church; often a prominent building surrounding the central plaza.

El ayuntamiento

The town hall or city council building; another key structure typically found around the plaza.

El paseo

A stroll or walk; also refers to the tradition of families and friends strolling around the plaza in the evenings.

La farmacia

The pharmacy.

El banco

The bank.

Important Terms (3/3)

La escuela

The school.

El supermercado

The grocery store or supermarket.

"El patio de mi casa" (My House's Courtyard)

Traditional Song

This is a beloved, traditional children's game song from the Spanish-speaking world. It’s part of a shared cultural heritage across Latin America and Spain.

Interactive Learning

Children typically stand in a circle and perform movements that accompany the lyrics. This active engagement (kinesthetic learning) is known to help in remembering words and phrases more effectively.

Vocabulary

The song describes features of a house and its courtyard (*el patio*), introducing vocabulary that will be relevant in later chapters.

Cultural Significance

These types of songs are not just for fun; they transmit culture, language, and shared experiences across generations.
