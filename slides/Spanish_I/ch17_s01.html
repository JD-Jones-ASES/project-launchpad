El pretérito de los verbos -ar

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Purpose of the Preterite Tense

The preterite tense is used to talk about actions that were completed at a specific point in the past. It's similar to adding "-ed" to verbs in English (e.g., "I walked," "they talked").

Regular -AR Verb Conjugation

To form the preterite of regular -ar verbs, remove the `-ar` ending from the infinitive and add the specific preterite endings: `-é`, `-aste`, `-ó`, `-amos`, `-asteis`, `-aron`.

Crucial Accent Marks

The `yo` form (`-é`) and the `él`/`ella`/`Ud.` form (`-ó`) must have accent marks. Forgetting them changes the verb to a different tense or mood, altering the meaning significantly.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Actions on a Timeline

Conceptually, the preterite places completed actions firmly on the "past" side of a timeline, signifying that they have a clear beginning and end.

Important Terms (1/2)

Pretérito (Preterite Tense)

A past tense in Spanish used to express actions completed in the past.

Verbos -ar (-AR Verbs)

Verbs in Spanish whose infinitive form ends in -ar (e.g., _hablar_, _caminar_, _estudiar_).

Stem

The base part of a verb that remains after removing the infinitive ending (-ar, -er, -ir). For example, the stem of _hablar_ is _habl-_.

Expresiones de tiempo (Time Expressions)

Adverbs or phrases that indicate when an action takes place, commonly used with the preterite to specify past timing.

Acento (Accent Mark)

A diacritical mark (´) placed over vowels in Spanish to indicate stress or distinguish between words, crucial for the `yo` and `él`/`ella`/`Ud.` preterite forms.

Important Terms (2/2)

Corrido

A traditional Mexican narrative ballad, often telling stories of history, revolution, or daily life.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary function of the Spanish pretérito tense?

To show completed actions that occurred in the past, similar to adding '-ed' in English.

What are the two steps to form the preterite of regular -ar verbs?

1. Remove the -ar ending to find the stem. 2. Add the preterite endings.

List the five preterite endings for regular -ar verbs.

-é, -aste, -ó, -amos, -aron

What is the preterite ending for the 'yo' form of regular -ar verbs?

-é (e.g., hablé)

What is the preterite ending for the 'tú' form of regular -ar verbs?

-aste (e.g., estudiaste)

What is the preterite ending for the 'él, ella, usted' forms of regular -ar verbs?

-ó (e.g., caminó)

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the preterite ending for the 'nosotros/nosotras' form of regular -ar verbs?

-amos (e.g., compramos)

What is the preterite ending for the 'ellos, ellas, ustedes' forms of regular -ar verbs?

-aron (e.g., trabajaron)

Why are accent marks mandatory on the 'yo' (-é) and 'él/ella/Ud.' (-ó) preterite forms of -ar verbs?

They change the meaning entirely; forgetting them results in writing a different tense (e.g., hablé vs. hable, habló vs. hablo).

What is unique about the 'nosotros' form of the preterite for regular -ar verbs?

It looks exactly the same as the present tense 'nosotros' form (e.g., 'caminamos').

How can you tell if an -ar verb's 'nosotros' form (e.g., 'caminamos') is present or preterite tense?

Context, specifically time words (e.g., 'hoy' vs. 'ayer'), makes the meaning clear.

Name three common time expressions used with the preterite tense.

Ayer (yesterday), anoche (last night), la semana pasada (last week), hace tres días (three days ago), de repente (suddenly).
