El Tiempo y La Naturaleza (Weather and Nature)

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Weather Expressions with `Hacer`

Many common weather conditions in Spanish are expressed using the verb `hacer` (to make/to do), such as `hace calor` (it is hot) or `hace sol` (it is sunny).

Specific Verbs for Rain and Snow

Unlike other weather conditions that use `hacer`, Spanish uses the specific verbs `llover` (to rain) and `nevar` (to snow) to describe raining and snowing, respectively.

`Estar` and `Hay` for Weather

Other verbs like `estar` (to be) are used for descriptive weather conditions like `está nublado` (it is cloudy), and `hay` (there is/are) is used for phenomena like `hay niebla` (there is fog).

Talking About the Weather: `¿Qué tiempo hace?`

Using `hacer`

Many common weather conditions use the verb `hacer` (to make/to do) in the third person singular (`hace`). This is an idiomatic use, meaning "it is [weather condition]".

Using specific verbs

For rain and snow, Spanish uses the verbs `llover` (to rain) and `nevar` (to snow) directly. These are conjugated in the third person singular.

Using `estar` and `hay`
