Key Concepts (1/2)

Expressing Emotions with *Estar

*: The verb *estar* is exclusively used with emotion adjectives because emotions are temporary states, reflecting how someone *is feeling right now* rather than a permanent characteristic.

Adjective Agreement

Nouns vs. Adjectives for Emotions

Spanish differentiates between nouns for feelings themselves (e.g., *la alegría* - joy) and adjectives that describe someone experiencing that feeling (e.g., *alegre* - joyful, used with *estar*).

Verbs of Feeling

There are specific verbs (e.g., *llorar*, *reír*, *sentir*) that describe emotional actions. Some, like *reír* and *sonreír*, have irregular stem changes in the preterite tense (e.g., e > í).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Emotional Memory Enhancement

Integrating emotional states with past events on a mental timeline can significantly enrich memory encoding and storytelling in Spanish by adding a layer of personal feeling to factual occurrences.

Cultural Insight: El muralismo mexicano (Mexican Muralism)

Diego Rivera (1886–1957)

Known for vast murals depicting Mexican history, the working class, and indigenous peoples. He believed art should be accessible to everyone in public spaces.
