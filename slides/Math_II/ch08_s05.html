Slope as Rate of Change

Key Concepts

Definition of Slope

Slope ($m$) quantifies the steepness and direction of a line. It is calculated as the ratio of the vertical change (rise) to the horizontal change (run) between any two points on the line.

Slope Formula

For two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, the slope is $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$, provided $x_1 eq x_2$.

The Slope Formula

• The term $(y_2 - y_1)$ represents the rise, which is the vertical change or difference in the y-coordinates.

• The term $(x_2 - x_1)$ represents the run, which is the horizontal change or difference in the x-coordinates.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the slope of a line measure in a general sense?

The slope measures how steep the line is, its direction (tilt), and most importantly, the rate of change—how fast one quantity changes compared to another.

What is the mathematical formula for the slope of a line passing through two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$?

The slope $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$, provided that $x_1 eq x_2$.

Explain what 'rise' and 'run' refer to in the context of calculating slope.

'Rise' refers to the vertical change or change in $y$ (numerator), and 'run' refers to the horizontal change or change in $x$ (denominator).

Describe the visual characteristic of a line with a positive slope.

A line with a positive slope ($m > 0$) rises from left to right.

Describe the visual characteristic of a line with a negative slope.

A line with a negative slope ($m < 0$) falls from left to right.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What type of line has a zero slope, and why?

A horizontal line has a zero slope ($m = 0$) because there is no vertical change (rise = 0), resulting in a slope of $\frac{0}{\text{run}} = 0$.

What type of line has an undefined slope, and why?

A vertical line has an undefined slope because there is no horizontal change (run = 0), which would result in division by zero in the slope formula.

What is the key distinction between a line with zero slope and a line with an undefined slope?

A horizontal line has zero slope (no rise), while a vertical line has undefined slope (no run, leading to division by zero).

Calculate the slope of the line passing through the points $(2, 3)$ and $(8, 9)$.

$m = \frac{9 - 3}{8 - 2} = \frac{6}{6} = 1$

Calculate the slope of the line passing through the points $(-1, 5)$ and $(3, -3)$.

$m = \frac{-3 - 5}{3 - (-1)} = \frac{-8}{4} = -2$

Calculate the slope of the line passing through the points $(1, -2)$ and $(4, 5)$.

$m = \frac{5 - (-2)}{4 - 1} = \frac{7}{3}$
