Slope Formula

For two points $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$, the slope $m$ is given by $m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1}$, provided $x_1 \neq x_2$.

Four Types of Slope

Lines can have positive, negative, zero, or undefined slope, each corresponding to a specific visual orientation and characteristic change in $y$ relative to $x$.

Important Terms (1/2)

Slope (m)

A measure of the steepness and direction of a line, or the rate at which one quantity changes with respect to another.

Rise

The vertical change between two points on a line, calculated as $y_2 - y_1$.

Run

The horizontal change between two points on a line, calculated as $x_2 - x_1$.

Rate of Change

The ratio describing how much one quantity changes, on average, for each unit of change in another quantity. It is the real-world interpretation of slope.

Positive Slope

A line that rises from left to right ($m > 0$).

Important Terms (2/2)

Negative Slope

A line that falls from left to right ($m < 0$).

Zero Slope

A horizontal line where there is no vertical change ($m = 0$).

Undefined Slope

A vertical line where there is no horizontal change, leading to division by zero in the slope formula.
