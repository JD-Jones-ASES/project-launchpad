Key Concepts (2/2)

The Special Case of $0.\overline{9}$

The repeating decimal $0.\overline{9}$ is mathematically equivalent to $1$. This is a direct consequence of the algebraic conversion method.

Important Terms

Terminating Decimal

A decimal that has a finite number of digits (it ends).

Repeating Decimal

A decimal that has a digit or a block of digits that repeats infinitely. A bar is often placed over the repeating block.

Rational Number

Any number that can be expressed as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b \neq 0$.

Place Value

The value of a digit in a number based on its position (e.g., in $0.75$, the $7$ is in the tenths place, and the $5$ is in the hundredths place).

Repeating Block

The sequence of digits that repeats indefinitely in a repeating decimal.

Terminating Decimals to Fractions

$0.75$

The last digit ($5$) is in the hundredths place.

$0.125$

The last digit ($5$) is in the thousandths place.

$2.6$

This is a mixed number. The whole number part ($2$) remains as is. The decimal part ($0.6$) is converted. The last digit ($6$) is in the tenths place.
