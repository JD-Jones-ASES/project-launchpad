Important Terms

Ratio

A comparison of two quantities by division, written as $a/b$, $a:b$, or "$a$ to $b$," where $b eq 0$.

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities measured in different units (e.g., miles per gallon, dollars per pound).

Unit Rate

A rate whose second quantity (denominator) is 1. It expresses how much of the first quantity corresponds to one unit of the second quantity (e.g., $70 \text{ miles/hour}$).

Proportion

An equation that states that two ratios are equal, typically written as $a/b = c/d$, where $b eq 0$ and $d eq 0$.

Cross Products (or Cross Multiplication)

A property of proportions that states if $a/b = c/d$, then $ad = bc$. This algebraic step is vital for solving proportions by converting them into linear equations.

Understanding Ratios

Notation

Ratios can be written as a fraction ($a/b$), using a colon ($a:b$), or with the word "to" ($a$ to $b$).

Denominator restriction

The second quantity, $b$, can never be zero because division by zero is undefined.

Units

Ratios can compare quantities with the same units (like wins to losses) or different units, which leads us to rates.

Rates and Unit Rates

Examples of Rates

$120$ miles in $2$ hours, $5$ dollars for $10$ apples, $15$ liters of water per $3$ minutes.

Unit Rate

To make rates easier to compare and understand, we often express them as unit rates. A unit rate has a denominator of $1$.

What is a Proportion?

Conditions

For a proportion to be valid, the denominators ($b$ and $d$) cannot be zero.

Purpose

Proportions are used to find an unknown value when you know three out of the four values in two equivalent ratios.

The Cross Products Property (Cross Multiplication)

Statement

If you have a proportion $a/b = c/d$, then you can "cross-multiply" to get the equivalent equation $ad = bc$.

How it works

You multiply the numerator of the first ratio by the denominator of the second ratio, and set it equal to the product of the denominator of the first ratio and the numerator of the second ratio.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a ratio?

A comparison of two quantities by division.

What are the three common ways to write a ratio of 'a' to 'b'?

As $a/b$, $a:b$, or 'a to b' (provided $b eq 0$).

What is the key characteristic that defines a 'rate'?

A rate is a ratio that compares two quantities measured in different units.

Define a 'unit rate'.

A unit rate is a rate where the denominator has a value of 1.

Give an example of how to calculate a unit rate.

Traveling $120$ miles in $2$ hours gives a unit rate of $120 \text{ mi} / 2 \text{ h} = 60 \text{ mi/h}$.

What is a proportion?

An equation that states two ratios are equal, typically written as $a/b = c/d$ (provided $b eq 0$ and $d eq 0$).

Key Concepts (2/2)

State the Cross Products property for a proportion $a/b = c/d$.

If $a/b = c/d$ (with $b eq 0$ and $d eq 0$), then $ad = bc$.

What is another common name for the Cross Products property?

Cross multiplication.

What is the general method for solving a proportion?

Cross-multiply, and then solve the resulting algebraic equation.

Solve the one-step proportion: $x/6 = 5/9$.

Cross-multiply: $9x = 6 \times 5 = 30$. Divide by 9: $x = 30/9 = 10/3$.

Solve the multi-step proportion: $(2x+1)/5 = 7/4$.

Cross-multiply: $4(2x+1) = 5 \times 7 = 35$. Distribute: $8x + 4 = 35$. Subtract 4: $8x = 31$. Divide by 8: $x = 31/8$.

How is a percent defined as a type of ratio?

A percent is a ratio whose second quantity (denominator) is $100$.
