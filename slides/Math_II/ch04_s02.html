Important Terms (1/2)

Distributive Property

The rule $a(b + c) = ab + ac$ and $a(b - c) = ab - ac$, which allows multiplication to be distributed over addition or subtraction.

Variable

A symbol, typically a letter (like $x$, $y$, $a$), used to represent an unknown number or a value that can change.

Term

A single number, a single variable, or a product of numbers and variables (e.g., $6x$, $15$, $-4x$, $3$). Terms are separated by addition or subtraction signs.

Like Terms

Terms that have the exact same variable parts (i.e., the same variables raised to the same powers). Only like terms can be combined (added or subtracted). For example, $6x$ and $2x$ are like terms,...

Factor

A number or expression that is multiplied by another number or expression. In $a(b+c)$, $a$ is a factor.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simplify

To reduce an algebraic expression to its simplest form, usually by performing all possible operations (like distribution) and combining like terms.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of applying the Distributive Property in this section?

To multiply a number by a sum or difference within expressions containing variables, and then simplify by combining like terms.

State the algebraic form of the Distributive Property for all real numbers $a$, $b$, and $c$.

$a(b + c) = ab + ac$ and $a(b - c) = ab - ac$.

How do you simplify the expression $3(2x + 5)$?

$3(2x + 5) = 3 \cdot 2x + 3 \cdot 5 = 6x + 15$.

When distributing a negative sign, such as in $-(4x - 3)$, what factor is effectively being distributed?

The factor effectively being distributed is $-1$.

Simplify the expression $-(4x - 3)$.

$ -1 \cdot 4x - (-1) \cdot 3 = -4x + 3$.

What is a common mistake when distributing a negative sign, and what is the correct outcome for $-(4x - 3)$?

A common mistake is to forget to distribute the negative to every term inside the parentheses (e.g., getting $-4x - 3$). The correct outcome is $-4x + 3$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the first step in the 'Distribute, Then Combine' strategy for simplifying expressions with parentheses and a factor in front?

Distribute each factor to every term inside the parentheses.

What is the second step in the 'Distribute, Then Combine' strategy after distributing the factors?

Combine like terms.

Simplify the expression $2(x + 4) + 3(x - 1)$.

$2x + 8 + 3x - 3 = (2x + 3x) + (8 - 3) = 5x + 5$.

A rectangle has a length of $(2x + 3)$ cm and a width of $(x - 1)$ cm. Write the initial expression for its perimeter.

$P = 2(2x + 3) + 2(x - 1)$.

If a rectangle has length $(2x + 3)$ cm and width $(x - 1)$ cm, what is the simplified expression for its perimeter?

$P = 2(2x + 3) + 2(x - 1) = 4x + 6 + 2x - 2 = 6x + 4$. The perimeter is $(6x + 4)$ cm.

Simplify the expression $4(3x - 2) - 2(x + 5)$.

$12x - 8 - 2x - 10 = 10x - 18$.
