Important Terms

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

For a set of fractions, this is the smallest positive integer that is a multiple of all the denominators. It is the key to efficiently clearing fractions from an equation.

Power of 10

Numbers like 10, 100, 1000, and so on. These are used in decimal clearing to shift decimal points to the right, converting decimal numbers into integers.

Coefficient

A numerical factor that multiplies a variable (e.g., in $5x$, 5 is the coefficient). Clearing fractions or decimals aims to make these coefficients integers.

Equivalent Equation

An equation that has the exact same solution set as another equation, even if they look different. Clearing fractions or decimals creates equivalent equations.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary strategy for solving equations that contain fractions or decimals?

Multiply every term by a number that clears the fractions or decimals, transforming it into an equation with integer coefficients.

Define "clearing fractions" in the context of solving equations.

The process of eliminating fractions from an equation by multiplying every term on both sides by the least common denominator (LCD) of all the fractions.

What specific number should you multiply by to clear fractions from an equation?

The least common denominator (LCD) of all the fractions in the equation.

Describe the first step to solve the equation x/3 + x/4 = 7 by clearing fractions.

Find the LCD of 3 and 4, which is 12. Then multiply every term in the equation by 12.

After multiplying every term in x/3 + x/4 = 7 by its LCD, what is the resulting simplified equation?

4x + 3x = 84.

What is the main advantage of clearing fractions or decimals when solving an equation?

It transforms the equation into one with integer coefficients, which is generally easier and less prone to arithmetic errors.

Key Concepts (2/2)

For the equation 2x - 1/3 = x + 4/6, what is the LCD you would use to clear the fractions?

The LCD of 3 and 6 is 6.

After multiplying every term in 2x - 1/3 = x + 4/6 by the LCD (6) and simplifying, what is the resulting equation?

2(2x - 1) = x + 4.

Define "clearing decimals" in the context of solving equations.

The process of eliminating decimals from an equation by multiplying every term on both sides by the smallest power of 10 that makes all coefficients integers.

What specific number should you multiply by to clear decimals from an equation?

The smallest power of 10 (e.g., 10, 100, 1000) that shifts all decimal points to the right enough to make every coefficient an integer.

To clear decimals from the equation 0.3x + 1.2 = 4.5, what number should you multiply every term by?

10, because the greatest number of decimal places is one.

After clearing decimals in 0.3x + 1.2 = 4.5, what is the new equation before solving?

3x + 12 = 45.
