Reflections over the Axes

Important Terms (1/2)

Coordinate Plane

A grid system formed by two perpendicular number lines used to locate points in a two-dimensional space. Also known as the Cartesian plane.

x-axis

The horizontal number line in the coordinate plane.

y-axis

The vertical number line in the coordinate plane.

Origin

The point where the x-axis and y-axis intersect, represented by the ordered pair $(0, 0)$.

Ordered Pair

A pair of numbers $(x, y)$ that gives the unique coordinates of a point on the coordinate plane.

Important Terms (2/2)

Coordinates

The specific values (x and y) in an ordered pair that indicate a point's position.

Quadrants

The four regions into which the coordinate plane is divided by the x-axis and y-axis, labeled I, II, III, and IV.

Reflection

A transformation that flips a point or shape across a line (the axis of reflection) or a point (the point of reflection), creating a mirror image.

The Four Quadrants

Quadrant I (QI)

Top-right region. Both x and y coordinates are positive $(+, +)$.

Quadrant II (QII)

Top-left region. x-coordinate is negative, y-coordinate is positive $(-, +)$.

Quadrant III (QIII)

Bottom-left region. Both x and y coordinates are negative $(-, -)$.

Quadrant IV (QIV)

Bottom-right region. x-coordinate is positive, y-coordinate is negative $(+, -)$.

Reflections over the Axes

Reflecting over the x-axis

The x-coordinate stays the same, and the y-coordinate changes its sign.

Reflecting over the y-axis

The x-coordinate changes its sign, and the y-coordinate stays the same.

Reflecting over the origin

Both the x-coordinate and the y-coordinate change their signs.
