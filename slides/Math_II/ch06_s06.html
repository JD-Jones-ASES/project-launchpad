Key Concepts

What are Composite Figures?

These are figures that are not simple geometric shapes (like a single rectangle or cylinder) but are instead composed of multiple basic shapes joined together or with parts removed.

These involve flat shapes, where you typically calculate total area or perimeter.

What is the first step in the strategy for finding the area, perimeter, surface area, or volume of a composite figure?

Identify the basic, simpler shapes that make up the composite figure.

What is the second step in calculating properties of composite figures?

Decide whether to add the areas/volumes of the parts (if the figure is made of combined pieces) or subtract them (if a piece is removed from a larger shape).

What are the final two steps in the strategy for composite figures?

Calculate each part separately, and then combine the results (add or subtract) as determined in the previous step.

Describe how to find the total area of a 2-D composite figure like a rectangle topped with a semicircle.

Calculate the area of the rectangle and the area of the semicircle separately, then add these two areas together to find the total area.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you calculate the total volume of a 3-D composite figure like a cylinder topped with a hemisphere (a silo)?

Calculate the volume of the cylinder and the volume of the hemisphere separately, then add these two volumes together to find the total volume.

When finding the perimeter of a 2-D composite figure that combines shapes (like a rectangle with a semicircular extension), what must you be careful not to count?

Do not count the internal boundaries where the shapes are joined. The perimeter should only include the outer edges of the combined figure.

A window is shaped like a rectangle 4 ft wide and 6 ft tall, topped with a semicircle. What is the radius of the semicircle?

The semicircle's diameter is equal to the width of the rectangle, which is 4 ft. Therefore, its radius is half of that, or 2 ft.

What is the formula for the area of a semicircle with radius 'r'?

A = (1/2)πr²

What is the formula for the volume of a hemisphere with radius 'r'?

V = (1/2)(4/3)πr³ = (2/3)πr³

When finding the surface area of a composite solid, what crucial detail must be remembered about faces where two pieces are joined?

The faces where two pieces are joined are hidden and should NOT be included in the total surface area calculation.
