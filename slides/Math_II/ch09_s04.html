Probability of Simple and Compound Events

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Probability of an Event ($P(E)$)

A measure of how likely an event is to occur. It is calculated as the ratio of the number of favorable outcomes to the total number of equally likely outcomes.

Probability Range

The probability of any event $E$ is always between 0 and 1, inclusive ($0 \le P(E) \le 1$).

Complementary Events

Two events are complementary if one event occurring means the other does not occur. The sum of their probabilities is 1.

Independent Events

Two events are independent if the outcome of one event does not influence or affect the outcome of the other event.

Multiplication Rule for Independent Events

If events $A$ and $B$ are independent, the probability that both $A$ and $B$ occur is the product of their individual probabilities.

Important Terms (1/2)

Probability of an Event

The numerical measure of the likelihood that a specific event will occur, expressed as a fraction, decimal, or percentage between 0 and 1.

Simple Event

An event consisting of a single outcome. For example, rolling a 3 on a die.

Compound Event

An event that combines two or more simple events. For example, rolling a 3 and flipping a coin to get tails.

Favorable Outcome

An outcome that satisfies the conditions of the specific event being considered.

Total Outcomes

The complete set of all possible results of a probability experiment.

Important Terms (2/2)

Impossible Event

An event with a probability of 0, meaning it can never happen.

Certain Event

An event with a probability of 1, meaning it is guaranteed to happen.

Complement of an Event (not E or E')

All outcomes in the sample space that are not part of event $E$.

Independent Events

Events where the occurrence of one does not change the probability of the other occurring.

What is the inclusive range of possible values for the probability of an event?

Probability is always between 0 and 1, inclusive: 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1.

What does a probability of 0 signify for an event?

A probability of 0 means the event is impossible.

What does a probability of 1 signify for an event?

A probability of 1 means the event is certain.

A standard six-sided die is rolled. What is the probability of rolling a number greater than 4?

Favorable outcomes: 5, 6 (2 outcomes). Total outcomes: 6. P(greater than 4) = 2/6 = 1/3.

From a standard 52-card deck, what is the probability of drawing a heart?

There are 13 hearts in a deck. P(heart) = 13/52 = 1/4.

Key Concepts (2/2)

A bag contains 3 red, 5 blue, and 2 green marbles. What is the probability of drawing a blue marble?

Total marbles = 3 + 5 + 2 = 10. Favorable (blue) = 5. P(blue) = 5/10 = 1/2.

What is the Complement Rule for probability?

The probability that event E does not occur is P(not E) = 1 - P(E).

If the probability of rain tomorrow is 0.35, what is the probability that it does not rain?

P(no rain) = 1 - P(rain) = 1 - 0.35 = 0.65.

State the Multiplication Rule for Independent Events.

If A and B are independent events, then the probability of both A and B occurring is P(A and B) = P(A) × P(B).

A fair coin is flipped twice. What is the probability of getting heads both times?

P(heads on 1st flip) = 1/2. P(heads on 2nd flip) = 1/2. Since flips are independent, P(heads and heads) = (1/2) × (1/2) = 1/4.
