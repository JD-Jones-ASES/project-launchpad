Slope-Intercept Form

A linear equation written as $y = mx + b$, where $m$ represents the slope and $b$ represents the $y$-coordinate of the $y$-intercept.

Identifying Slope ($m$) and $y$-intercept ($b$)

When an equation is in $y = mx + b$ form, the coefficient of $x$ is the slope ($m$), and the constant term is the $y$-intercept ($b$). The $y$-intercept is always an ordered pair $(0, b)$.

Important Terms (1/2)

Slope-Intercept Form

The format of a linear equation, $y = mx + b$, where the slope and $y$-intercept are directly visible.

Slope ($m$)

A measure of the steepness and direction of a line, calculated as the ratio of vertical change (rise) to horizontal change (run). It represents the rate of change.

$y$-intercept ($b$)

The point where a line crosses the $y$-axis. Its coordinates are always $(0, b)$. The value $b$ is the $y$-coordinate of this point.

Linear Equation

An equation whose graph is a straight line.

Rate of Change

How one quantity changes in relation to another; represented by the slope ($m$) in linear equations.

Important Terms (2/2)

Ordered Pair

A pair of numbers $(x, y)$ that describes the location of a point on the coordinate plane.

The Slope-Intercept Form Property

• $m$ represents the slope of the line.

• $b$ represents the $y$-intercept (specifically, the $y$-coordinate of the point $(0, b)$ where the line crosses the $y$-axis).

Linear Models: Cost Equation

Base fee

The $5 is a fixed cost, regardless of how many movies are rented. This is the initial value, so it represents the $C$-intercept (or $y$-intercept), $b = 5$.

Cost per movie

The $2 per movie is the rate of change. This is the slope, $m = 2$.

Equation

$C = 2m + 5$.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the standard form of the slope-intercept equation?

y = mx + b

In the slope-intercept form y = mx + b, what does the variable m represent?

The slope of the line.

In the slope-intercept form y = mx + b, what does the variable b represent?

The y-intercept of the line.

What are two key benefits of using the slope-intercept form?

It makes it easy to graph a line quickly and to write an equation from a graph.

Identify the slope (m) and y-intercept (b) for the equation y = 4x - 7.

m = 4, b = -7.

Identify the slope (m) and y-intercept (b) for the equation y = -23x + 5.

m = -23, b = 5.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Identify the slope (m) and y-intercept (b) for the equation y = x.

m = 1, b = 0.

How is the y-intercept value b typically expressed as a coordinate pair for plotting?

As (0, b).

What is the first step when graphing a linear equation given in slope-intercept form?

Plot the y-intercept (0, b).

After plotting the y-intercept, how do you use the slope m to find a second point for graphing?

Interpret m as "rise over run" (rise/run). From the y-intercept, count up/down the 'rise' units and left/right the 'run' units to locate another point.
