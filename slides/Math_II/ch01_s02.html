Product, Power, and Quotient Rules: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Product Rule for Exponents

When multiplying powers with the same base, keep the base and add the exponents. ($a^m a^n = a^{m+n}$)

Power Rule for Exponents

When raising a power to another power, keep the base and multiply the exponents. ($(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$)

Power of a Product

When raising a product of factors to a power, distribute the exponent to each individual factor within the product. ($(ab)^n = a^n b^n$)

Quotient Rule for Exponents

When dividing powers with the same base, keep the base and subtract the exponent of the denominator from the exponent of the numerator. ($a^m/a^n = a^{m-n}$, where $m > n$)

Important Terms (1/2)

Exponent

A number that indicates how many times the base is to be used as a factor. For example, in $a^n$, $n$ is the exponent.

Base

The number or variable that is being multiplied by itself, as indicated by the exponent. For example, in $a^n$, $a$ is the base.

Power

An expression written with a base and an exponent, such as $2^3$ or $x^5$.

Product Rule

The rule $a^m a^n = a^{m+n}$, used for multiplying powers with the same base.

Power Rule

The rule $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$, used for raising a power to another power.

Important Terms (2/2)

Power of a Product Rule

The rule $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$, used for raising a product to a power.

Quotient Rule

The rule $a^m/a^n = a^{m-n}$, used for dividing powers with the same base.

State the Product Rule for Exponents.

For any nonzero base $a$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$, $a^m a^n = a^{m+n}$. You keep the base and add the exponents.

What is a crucial condition for applying the Product Rule for Exponents?

The bases of the powers being multiplied must be the same.

Simplify the expression $x^5 x^3$.

$x^{5+3} = x^8$

Simplify the expression $4^2 4^6$.

$4^{2+6} = 4^8$

Simplify the expression $a^2 a a^4$.

$a^2 a^1 a^4 = a^{2+1+4} = a^7$

Key Concepts (2/2)

State the Power Rule for Exponents.

For any nonzero base $a$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$, $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$. You keep the base and multiply the exponents.

Simplify the expression $(x^4)^3$.

$x^{4 \times 3} = x^{12}$

Simplify the expression $(5^2)^5$.

$5^{2 \times 5} = 5^{10}$

State the Power of a Product rule.

For any nonzero bases $a$ and $b$ and positive integer $n$, $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$. You distribute the exponent to each factor inside the parentheses.

Simplify the expression $(3x)^4$.

$3^4 x^4 = 81x^4$

Simplify the expression $(2a^3)^2$.

$2^2 (a^3)^2 = 4a^{3 \times 2} = 4a^6$
