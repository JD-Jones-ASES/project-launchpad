Rational Number ($\mathbb{Q}$)

A number that can be written as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$ where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b eq 0$. Its decimal expansion either terminates or repeats.

Real Numbers ($\mathbb{R}$)

The set of all rational and irrational numbers. The entire number line is composed of real numbers.

Natural Numbers ($\mathbb{N}$)

The counting numbers: $\{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$.

Whole Numbers ($\mathbb{W}$)

The natural numbers including zero: $\{0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Integers ($\mathbb{Z}$)

All whole numbers and their negatives: $\{\ldots, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$.

Perfect Square

An integer that is the square of an integer (e.g., $1, 4, 9, 16, \ldots$). The square root of a perfect square is always a rational number.

Proof by Contradiction

A logical argument where one assumes the opposite of what is to be proven, and if this assumption leads to a logical inconsistency (a contradiction), then the original statement must be true.

What are Irrational Numbers?

$\sqrt{9}$

This simplifies to $3$, which can be written as $\frac{3}{1}$. Therefore, it is rational.

$\sqrt{5}$

Since $5$ is not a perfect square, $\sqrt{5}$ is an irrational number.

$0.121212\ldots$

This is a repeating decimal, which means it can be expressed as a fraction (in this case, $\frac{12}{99}$ or $\frac{4}{33}$). Therefore, it is rational.

$\frac{\pi}{2}$

Since $\pi$ is irrational, any non-zero rational multiple or quotient of $\pi$ (like $\frac{\pi}{2}$) will also be irrational.

$-\frac{7}{3}$

This is already in the form $\frac{a}{b}$ where $a=-7$ and $b=3$ are integers. Therefore, it is rational.

The Real Numbers (1/2)

Natural Numbers ($\mathbb{N}$)

$\{1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$ (counting numbers).

Whole Numbers ($\mathbb{W}$)

$\{0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$ (natural numbers plus zero).

Integers ($\mathbb{Z}$)

$\{\ldots, -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, \ldots\}$ (whole numbers and their opposites).

Rational Numbers ($\mathbb{Q}$)

All numbers that can be written as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$. This includes all integers, as any integer $n$ can be written as $\frac{n}{1}$.

Irrational Numbers

Numbers that cannot be written as a fraction. These are separate from rational numbers but are also real numbers.

The Real Numbers (2/2)

Real Numbers ($\mathbb{R}$)

The union of all rational and irrational numbers.

$-7$

This is an integer, and since integers are rational, it is also rational and real. (Integer, Rational, Real)

$\frac{5}{8}$

This is a fraction of integers, so it's rational. All rational numbers are real. (Rational, Real)

$\sqrt{16}$

This simplifies to $4$. $4$ is a natural number, a whole number, an integer, a rational number, and a real number. (Natural, Whole, Integer, Rational, Real)

$\sqrt{11}$

Since $11$ is not a perfect square, $\sqrt{11}$ is irrational. All irrational numbers are real. (Irrational, Real)

Approximating Irrational Numbers

• $4^2 = 16$ and $5^2 = 25$, so $4 < \sqrt{20} < 5$.
