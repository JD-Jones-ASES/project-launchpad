Key Concepts

Midpoint Definition

The midpoint of a line segment is the point located precisely halfway between its two endpoints. It divides the segment into two equal parts.

Averaging Coordinates

The formula works by independently averaging the x-coordinates and averaging the y-coordinates. This is a direct extension of finding the average of two numbers on a number line.

Finding a Missing Endpoint

If you know one endpoint and the midpoint, you can set up two separate equations (one for x, one for y) using the Midpoint Formula and solve for the coordinates of the unknown endpoint.

Applications

Finding midpoints is useful in various real-world scenarios, such as determining the center of a physical object, a geographical location, or a route.

Important Terms (1/2)

Midpoint

The central point of a line segment, equidistant from both endpoints.

Endpoint

One of the two points that define the boundaries of a line segment.

Coordinate Plane

A two-dimensional plane defined by a horizontal x-axis and a vertical y-axis, used to locate points using ordered pairs $(x, y)$.

x-coordinate

The first value in an ordered pair $(x, y)$, representing the horizontal position of a point.

y-coordinate

The second value in an ordered pair $(x, y)$, representing the vertical position of a point.

Important Terms (2/2)

Average

The sum of a set of numbers divided by the count of the numbers. In the Midpoint Formula, we average the x-coordinates and the y-coordinates separately.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the midpoint of a segment?

The midpoint of a segment is the point exactly halfway between its two endpoints.

Provide two examples of practical situations where finding a midpoint is useful.

Finding the center of a bridge span, or locating the halfway point of a hiking trail on a map.

State the Midpoint Formula for a segment with endpoints $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$.

$M = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\; \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$

How do you describe the Midpoint Formula in words?

To find the midpoint, average the x-coordinates and average the y-coordinates of the two endpoints.

Find the midpoint of the segment from $A(2, 8)$ to $B(10, 4)$.

$M = \left(\frac{2+10}{2},\; \frac{8+4}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{12}{2},\; \frac{12}{2}\right) = (6, 6)$

Calculate the midpoint of the segment connecting $(-5, 3)$ and $(7, -9)$.

$M = \left(\frac{-5+7}{2},\; \frac{3+(-9)}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{2}{2},\; \frac{-6}{2}\right) = (1, -3)$

The midpoint of segment $AB$ is $M(3, 5)$. If endpoint $A$ is $(1, 2)$, what are the coordinates of endpoint $B$?

Let $B=(x,y)$. For x: $\frac{1+x}{2} = 3 \Rightarrow 1+x=6 \Rightarrow x=5$. For y: $\frac{2+y}{2} = 5 \Rightarrow 2+y=10 \Rightarrow y=8$. So, $B = (5, 8)$.

A straight bridge runs from point $P(-2, 7)$ to point $Q(14, 3)$. What are the coordinates for the exact center of the bridge for placing a sign?

$M = \left(\frac{-2+14}{2},\; \frac{7+3}{2}\right) = \left(\frac{12}{2},\; \frac{10}{2}\right) = (6, 5)$. The sign should be placed at $(6, 5)$.

If $M(4, -1)$ is the midpoint of $CD$ and $C = (7, 3)$, what are the coordinates of point $D$?

Let $D=(x,y)$. For x: $\frac{7+x}{2} = 4 \Rightarrow 7+x=8 \Rightarrow x=1$. For y: $\frac{3+y}{2} = -1 \Rightarrow 3+y=-2 \Rightarrow y=-5$. So, $D = (1, -5)$.

How does the Midpoint Formula for the coordinate plane relate to finding the average of two numbers on a one-dimensional number line?

The Midpoint Formula is a two-dimensional version of finding the average. It simply applies the concept of averaging to each coordinate (x and y) independently.
