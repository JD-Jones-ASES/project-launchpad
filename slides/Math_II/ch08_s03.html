Key Concepts

Definition of a Midpoint

The midpoint of a line segment is the point that lies exactly halfway between its two endpoints. It divides the segment into two equal parts.

Averaging Coordinates

The core idea behind the midpoint formula is to find the average of the x-coordinates and the average of the y-coordinates separately.

Finding a Missing Endpoint

If you know one endpoint and the midpoint of a segment, you can set up two separate equations using the Midpoint Formula to solve for the coordinates of the unknown second endpoint.

Real-World Applications

The Midpoint Formula is useful for tasks such as locating the center of objects, determining halfway points, or finding the balance point in various situations.

Important Terms

Midpoint

The point that divides a line segment into two congruent (equal length) segments.

Endpoint

One of the two points that mark the start and end of a line segment.

Coordinates

A set of numbers that specify the location of a point in a coordinate system, typically $(x, y)$ in a two-dimensional plane.

Segment (Line Segment)

A part of a line that is bounded by two distinct endpoints.

Average

The sum of a set of numbers divided by the count of the numbers. In the Midpoint Formula, it refers to the arithmetic mean of the x-coordinates and y-coordinates.

Examples of Finding a Midpoint:

Q: With Positive Coordinates:

Q: With Negative Coordinates:

Find the midpoint of the segment from $(-5, 3)$ to $(7, -9)$.

Q: Checkpoint Example:

Find the midpoint of the segment from $(4, -2)$ to $(-6, 10)$.
