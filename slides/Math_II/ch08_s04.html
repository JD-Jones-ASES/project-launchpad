Graphing from a Table

Once a table of values is created, the coordinate pairs are plotted as points on the coordinate plane. These points will lie on a straight line, which is then drawn through them.

The $y$-Intercept

The specific point where a line crosses the $y$-axis. At this point, the $x$-coordinate is always 0. In the standard form $y = mx + b$, the $y$-intercept is the point $(0, b)$.

Horizontal Lines

Equations of the form $y = c$ (where $c$ is a constant) graph as a horizontal line. Every point on this line has the same $y$-coordinate, $c$.

Important Terms

Linear Equation in Two Variables

An algebraic equation that, when graphed, forms a straight line. It typically involves two variables (often $x$ and $y$) and can be expressed in the form $y = mx + b$.

$y$-Intercept

The point on the coordinate plane where a graph intersects the $y$-axis. For any point on the $y$-axis, its $x$-coordinate is 0. In the equation $y = mx + b$, the $y$-intercept is $(0, b)$.

The $y$-Intercept

• For $y = 3x - 4$: Here, $b = -4$, so the $y$-intercept is $(0, -4)$.

• For $y = -12x + 7$: Here, $b = 7$, so the $y$-intercept is $(0, 7)$.

• For $y = 5x$: This can be written as $y = 5x + 0$, so $b = 0$. The $y$-intercept is $(0, 0)$, which is the origin.

Horizontal Lines

• The line will pass through the point $(0, c)$ on the $y$-axis.

• Every point on this line has a $y$-coordinate of $c$. For example, for $y = 3$, points like $(-2, 3)$, $(0, 3)$, and $(4, 3)$ are all on the line.

Vertical Lines

Graph $y = 3$

This is a horizontal line where all $y$-coordinates are 3. It passes through $(0, 3)$.

Graph $x = -2$

This is a vertical line where all $x$-coordinates are -2. It passes through $(-2, 0)$.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a linear equation in two variables?

A linear equation in two variables is any equation that can be written in the form $y = mx + b$, where $m$ and $b$ are real numbers.

What does the graph of a linear equation in two variables always look like on the coordinate plane?

Its graph is always a straight line.

What are the steps to graph a linear equation using a table of values?

1. Choose several values for $x$. 2. Compute the corresponding $y$-values. 3. Plot the resulting points. 4. Connect the points with a straight line.

If you are graphing $y = 2x + 1$ and choose $x$-values of $-2, -1, 0, 1, 2$, what are the corresponding $y$-values?

For $x=-2, y=-3$; for $x=-1, y=-1$; for $x=0, y=1$; for $x=1, y=3$; for $x=2, y=5.

Define the $y$-intercept of a line.

The $y$-intercept of a line is the point where the line crosses the $y$-axis. At this point, $x = 0$.

In the equation $y = mx + b$, what is the $y$-intercept?

The $y$-intercept is the point $(0, b)$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Identify the $y$-intercept of the equation $y = 3x - 4$.

$(0, -4)$

Identify the $y$-intercept of the equation $y = -12x + 7$.

$(0, 7)$

Identify the $y$-intercept of the equation $y = 5x$.

$(0, 0)$

What is the $y$-intercept of $y = -2x + 3$?

$(0, 3)$

What is the general form of an equation that graphs as a horizontal line?

The equation $y = c$, where $c$ is a constant.

Through which point does a horizontal line given by $y = c$ pass?

It passes through the point $(0, c)$.
