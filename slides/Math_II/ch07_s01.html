Important Terms (1/2)

Scalene Triangle

A triangle with no congruent (equal length) sides.

Isosceles Triangle

A triangle with at least two congruent (equal length) sides. (Note: The angles opposite the congruent sides, called base angles, are also congruent).

Equilateral Triangle

A triangle with three congruent (equal length) sides. (Note: All three angles are also congruent, each measuring 60 degrees).

Acute Triangle

A triangle in which all three interior angles are acute (each less than 90 degrees).

Right Triangle

A triangle that has exactly one right (90-degree) angle. The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse.

Important Terms (2/2)

Obtuse Triangle

A triangle that has exactly one obtuse (greater than 90 degrees) angle.

Triangle Angle-Sum Property

The geometric rule stating that the sum of the measures of the interior angles of any triangle is 180 degrees.

Exterior Angle

An angle formed by one side of a triangle and the extension of an adjacent side.

Remote Interior Angles

The two interior angles of a triangle that are not adjacent to a given exterior angle.

Exterior Angle Theorem

The theorem stating that the measure of an exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the measures of its two remote interior angles.

The Triangle Angle-Sum Property

Finding a Missing Angle

If you know the measures of two angles in a triangle, you can always find the measure of the third angle by subtracting the sum of the two known angles from 180 degrees.

Angles in an Isosceles Triangle

Since the two base angles of an isosceles triangle are equal, if you know the vertex angle, you can find the base angles.

Real-World Applications

This property is widely used in architecture, engineering, and various other fields.

Exterior Angle Theorem

Finding an Exterior Angle

If you know the two remote interior angles, you can find the exterior angle.

Finding a Remote Interior Angle

If you know an exterior angle and one remote interior angle, you can find the other remote interior angle.
