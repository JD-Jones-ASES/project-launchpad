Key Concepts

Triangle Classification

Triangles can be categorized by the lengths of their sides (scalene, isosceles, equilateral) and by the measures of their interior angles (acute, right, obtuse).

Triangle Angle-Sum Property

The sum of the measures of the three interior angles of any triangle is always 180 degrees ($m A + m B + m C = 180^\circ$). This is a foundational concept used to find missing angles.

Exterior Angle

An angle formed outside a triangle by extending one of its sides. It forms a linear pair (adds up to 180 degrees) with the adjacent interior angle.

Remote Interior Angles

For any given exterior angle, the two interior angles of the triangle that are not adjacent to it are called the remote interior angles.

Important Terms (1/2)

Scalene Triangle

A triangle with no congruent (equal length) sides.

Isosceles Triangle

A triangle with at least two congruent sides. The angles opposite the congruent sides (base angles) are also congruent.

Equilateral Triangle

A triangle with three congruent sides. All three angles are also congruent, each measuring $60^\circ$.

Acute Triangle

A triangle in which all three interior angles are acute (each less than $90^\circ$).

Right Triangle

A triangle that has exactly one right angle (an angle measuring exactly $90^\circ$).

Important Terms (2/2)

Obtuse Triangle

A triangle that has exactly one obtuse angle (an angle measuring greater than $90^\circ$).

Triangle Angle-Sum Property

The rule stating that the sum of the measures of the interior angles of any triangle is $180^\circ$.

Exterior Angle

An angle formed by one side of a triangle and the extension of an adjacent side.

Remote Interior Angles

The two interior angles of a triangle that are not adjacent to a given exterior angle.

Exterior Angle Theorem

A theorem stating that the measure of an exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the measures of its two remote interior angles.

B. Classification by Angles

Acute Triangle

All three interior angles are acute, meaning each angle measures less than $90^\circ$.

Right Triangle

Exactly one interior angle is a right angle, measuring exactly $90^\circ$. The side opposite the right angle is called the hypotenuse, and the other two sides are called legs.

Obtuse Triangle

Exactly one interior angle is an obtuse angle, meaning it measures greater than $90^\circ$.

The Triangle Angle-Sum Property

Property Statement

The sum of the measures of the interior angles of any triangle is always $180^\circ$.

Intuitive Understanding

Imagine tearing off the three corners of any paper triangle and placing them side by side. You will find that they always form a straight line, which measures $180^\circ$.

Important Caution

An exterior angle is not one of the interior angles. It is supplementary (adds to $180^\circ$) to its adjacent interior angle. Always extend one side to clearly identify the exterior angle.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How is a scalene triangle classified by its sides?

A scalene triangle has no congruent sides.

What defines an isosceles triangle?

An isosceles triangle has at least two congruent sides.

Describe the side classification of an equilateral triangle.

An equilateral triangle has three congruent sides.

How is an acute triangle classified by its angles?

An acute triangle has three angles, each measuring less than $90^$.

What is a key characteristic of a right triangle?

A right triangle has exactly one $90^$ angle.

Define an obtuse triangle.

An obtuse triangle has exactly one angle greater than $90^$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

State the Triangle Angle-Sum Property.

The sum of the measures of the interior angles of any triangle is $180^$.

What is the formula for the Triangle Angle-Sum Property, given interior angles A, B, and C?

$m A + m B + m C = 180^$

A triangle has interior angles measuring $47^$ and $68^$. What is the measure of the third angle?

Using the Triangle Angle-Sum Property: $180^ - 47^ - 68^ = 65^$. The third angle measures $65^$.

An isosceles triangle has a vertex angle of $40^$. What are the measures of its two base angles?

Let each base angle be $x$. Since base angles are equal: $x + x + 40^ = 180^ \ 2x = 140^ \ x = 70^$. Each base angle measures $70^$.

A builder designs a roof truss in the shape of a triangle. The two base angles each measure $55^$. What is the measure of the peak angle?

Let the peak angle be $P$. $55^ + 55^ + P = 180^ \ 110^ + P = 180^ \ P = 70^$. The peak angle measures $70^$.

How is an exterior angle of a triangle formed?

An exterior angle of a triangle is formed by one side of the triangle and the extension of an adjacent side.
