Key Concepts (1/2)

Proportional Sides in Similar Figures

If two figures are similar, the ratios of their corresponding side lengths are equal. This fundamental property allows us to set up equations (proportions) to find unknown lengths.

Scale Factor

The ratio of any pair of corresponding side lengths between two similar figures. It indicates how much larger or smaller one figure is compared to the other.

Directionality of Scale Factor

The scale factor depends on the order in which the figures are compared. The scale factor from Figure A to Figure B is the reciprocal of the scale factor from Figure B to Figure A.

Important Terms (1/2)

Similar Figures

Figures that have the same shape but not necessarily the same size. Their corresponding angles are equal, and their corresponding side lengths are proportional.

Scale Factor

Proportion

An equation stating that two ratios are equal. For example, `a/b = c/d`.

Cross-Multiplication

A method for solving proportions where the product of the means equals the product of the extremes. In `a/b = c/d`, cross-multiplication gives `ad = bc`.

Corresponding Sides

Sides that are in the same relative position in two similar figures.
