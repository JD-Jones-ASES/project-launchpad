Important Terms (1/2)

Percent Change

The relative change in a quantity, expressed as a percentage of the original amount. It quantifies how much a value has increased or decreased.

Original Amount

The starting value of a quantity before any change occurs.

New Amount

The final value of a quantity after a percent change has occurred.

Rate (`r`)

The percentage of change, written as a decimal in formulas (e.g., 12% is `0.12`).

Multiplier

The factor `(1 + r)` or `(1 - r)` that, when multiplied by the original amount, directly yields the new amount after a percent change.

Important Terms (2/2)

Successive Percent Changes

Multiple percent increases or decreases that happen sequentially, with each subsequent change applied to the result of the previous change, not the original amount.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the formula for calculating the percent change from an original amount to a new amount?

Percent change = ((New Amount - Original Amount) / Original Amount) 100%.

How can you determine if a calculated percent change represents an increase or a decrease?

What is the 'multiplier' formula for calculating a new amount after a percent increase of r%?

New amount = Original amount (1 + r), where r is the rate written as a decimal.

What is the 'multiplier' formula for calculating a new amount after a percent decrease of r%?

New amount = Original amount (1 - r), where r is the rate written as a decimal.

In the context of percent change, what is the 'multiplier'?

The factor (1 + r) for an increase or (1 - r) for a decrease, which is multiplied by the original amount to find the new amount.

A shirt costs $25. Its price increases by 12%. Use the multiplier method to find the new price.

Multiplier = 1 + 0.12 = 1.12. New price = $25 1.12 = $28.00.

Key Concepts (2/2)

A laptop originally costs $640. It goes on sale for 15% off. Use the multiplier method to find the sale price.

Multiplier = 1 - 0.15 = 0.85. Sale price = $640 0.85 = $544.00.

A town's population fell from 8,500 to 7,650. Calculate the percent change.

Percent change = ((7650 - 8500) / 8500) 100% = (-850 / 8500) 100% = -10%. The population decreased by 10%.

How should successive percent changes be combined?

Successive percent changes should be multiplied, not added. Each change is applied using its respective multiplier.

A jacket with an original price of $80 is marked 20% off. What is the price after the first discount?

Price after first discount = $80 (1 - 0.20) = $80 0.80 = $64.00.

Following a 20% discount on an $80 jacket, a store coupon gives an additional 10% off the sale price. What is the final price?

Price after first discount = $64.00. Final price = $64.00 (1 - 0.10) = $64.00 0.90 = $57.60.
