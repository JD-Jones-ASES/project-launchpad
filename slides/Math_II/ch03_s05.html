Important Terms (1/2)

Customary System

A system of measurement used predominantly in the United States, including units such as feet, pounds, and gallons.

Metric System

A decimal-based system of measurement used worldwide and in scientific contexts, including units such as meters, grams, and liters. It is based on powers of 10.

Unit of Measurement

A standard quantity used to express a physical property (e.g., inch, kilogram, liter).

Conversion

The process of changing a measurement from one unit to another while maintaining the same value.

Length

A measure of distance. Customary units include inch (in), foot (ft), yard (yd), mile (mi). Metric units include millimeter (mm), centimeter (cm), meter (m), kilometer (km).

To move to a larger unit (e.g., from grams to kilograms)

You divide by the appropriate power of 10. The numerical value will get smaller, reflecting that you need fewer of the larger units. This involves moving the decimal point to the left.

To move to a smaller unit (e.g., from meters to centimeters)

You multiply by the appropriate power of 10. The numerical value will get larger, reflecting that you need more of the smaller units. This involves moving the decimal point to the right.
