Important Terms (1/2)

Pythagorean Theorem

A fundamental geometric theorem stating that the square of the hypotenuse ($c$) of a right triangle is equal to the sum of the squares of the two legs ($a$ and $b$), i.e., $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Hypotenuse

The longest side of a right triangle, always opposite the right (90-degree) angle.

Legs (of a right triangle)

The two shorter sides of a right triangle that form the right angle.

Diagonal

A line segment connecting two non-adjacent vertices of a polygon. In a rectangle, drawing a diagonal creates two right triangles.

Pythagorean Triple

A set of three positive integers $(a, b, c)$ such that $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Coordinate Grid

A system used to locate points in a plane using ordered pairs of numbers (coordinates), which can be used to visualize and calculate distances using the Pythagorean Theorem.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of the Pythagorean Theorem in real-world applications?

It is used to find a distance that forms part of a right triangle.

How can you apply the Pythagorean Theorem to find the diagonal of a rectangle?

A rectangle's diagonal forms a right triangle with its width and height as the legs. Use a² + b² = c² where a = width, b = height, and c = diagonal.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is a Pythagorean triple?

A set of three positive integers (a, b, c) such that a² + b² = c², which can represent the side lengths of a right triangle.

Name three common Pythagorean triples.

(3, 4, 5), (5, 12, 13), and (8, 15, 17).

A rectangular TV screen is 40 inches wide and 30 inches tall. What is the length of its diagonal?

The diagonal is 50 inches. (40² + 30² = 1600 + 900 = 2500, √2500 = 50)

A rectangular garden is 12 feet long and 5 feet wide. Find the length of its diagonal.

The diagonal is 13 feet. (12² + 5² = 144 + 25 = 169, √169 = 13)

A 20-foot ladder leans against a wall. The base of the ladder is 6 feet from the wall. How high up the wall does the ladder reach? Round to the nearest tenth.

The ladder reaches approximately 19.1 feet up the wall. (6² + h² = 20², h² = 400 - 36 = 364, h = √364 ≈ 19.07, rounds to 19.1)

Find the distance between the points (1, 2) and (4, 6) on a coordinate grid.

The distance is 5 units. (Horizontal leg = 4-1=3, Vertical leg = 6-2=4. Then 3² + 4² = 9 + 16 = 25, √25 = 5)
