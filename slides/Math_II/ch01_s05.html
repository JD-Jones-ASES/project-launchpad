Key Concepts

Unit Fraction Exponents ($a^{1/n}$)

An exponent of the form $1/n$ is equivalent to taking the $n$th root of the base. For a non-negative number $a$ and a positive integer $n$, $a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a}$.

• $27^{4/3} = (\sqrt[3]{27})^4 = 3^4 = 81$

• $16^{3/4} = (\sqrt[4]{16})^3 = 2^3 = 8$

Converting Between Radical and Exponent Form

From Radical to Rational Exponent

$\sqrt[n]{x^m} = x^{m/n}$

From Rational Exponent to Radical

$x^{m/n} = \sqrt[n]{x^m}$ (or $(\sqrt[n]{x})^m$)

Applying Exponent Rules to Rational Exponents

Product Rule

$x^a \cdot x^b = x^{a+b}$ (Add exponents when multiplying powers with the same base)

Quotient Rule

$x^a / x^b = x^{a-b}$ (Subtract exponents when dividing powers with the same base)

Power Rule

$(x^a)^b = x^{a \cdot b}$ (Multiply exponents when raising a power to another power)

Zero Exponent

$x^0 = 1$ (for $x eq 0$)

Negative Exponent

$x^{-a} = 1/x^a$ (for $x eq 0$)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What are rational exponents?

Exponents that are fractions, extending the idea of integer exponents and connecting them to radicals.

Define $a^{1/n}$ in terms of a radical.

For any nonnegative number $a$ and positive integer $n$, $a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a}$.

What does $a^{1/n}$ represent conceptually?

It is the number that, when raised to the power $n$, gives $a$. (Also known as the $n$th root of $a$.)

What are the conditions for the property $a^{1/n} = \sqrt[n]{a}$ to be true?

$a$ must be a nonnegative number, and $n$ must be a positive integer.

Evaluate $25^{1/2}$.

$25^{1/2} = \sqrt{25} = 5$.

Evaluate $8^{1/3}$.

$8^{1/3} = \sqrt[3]{8} = 2$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Evaluate $81^{1/4}$.

$81^{1/4} = \sqrt[4]{81} = 3$ because $3^4 = 81$.

Evaluate $32^{1/5}$.

$32^{1/5} = \sqrt[5]{32} = 2$ because $2^5 = 32$.

Evaluate $64^{1/3}$.

$64^{1/3} = \sqrt[3]{64} = 4$.

Define $a^{m/n}$ in terms of radicals, providing both equivalent forms.

For any nonnegative number $a$ and positive integers $m$ and $n$, $a^{m/n} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m = \sqrt[n]{a^m}$.

When evaluating $a^{m/n}$, which operation should generally be performed first (root or power) to keep numbers smaller?

Taking the $n$th root first, then raising to the $m$th power ($a^{m/n} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m$), usually keeps the numbers smaller.

Evaluate $8^{2/3}$.

$8^{2/3} = (\sqrt[3]{8})^2 = 2^2 = 4$.
