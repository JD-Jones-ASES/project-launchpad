Surface Area and Volume of Spheres Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of a Sphere

A three-dimensional shape where all points on its surface are an equal distance (the radius) from a central point (the center). Examples include basketballs, globes, and marbles.

Surface Area Formula

The formula used to calculate the total area of the outer surface of a sphere. It is given by $SA = 4 \cdot \text{pi} \cdot r^2$, where $r$ is the radius of the sphere.

Volume Formula

The formula used to calculate the amount of space a sphere occupies. It is given by $V = (4/3) \cdot \text{pi} \cdot r^3$, where $r$ is the radius of the sphere.

Radius vs. Diameter

The radius ($r$) is half the diameter ($d$). If a problem provides the diameter, always divide it by 2 to find the radius before using the surface area or volume formulas.

Exact vs. Approximate Answers

Answers can be given "in terms of pi" (exact answer, e.g., $196 \text{pi cm}^2$) or as a decimal approximation by using a value for pi (e.g., $3.14$ or a more precise value from a calculator).

Important Terms

Sphere

A perfectly round three-dimensional object in which every point on its surface is an equal distance from its center.

Radius ($r$)

The fixed distance from the center of a sphere to any point on its surface. It is a crucial measurement for calculating surface area and volume.

Center

The central point within a sphere from which all points on the surface are equidistant.

Diameter ($d$)

The distance across a sphere passing through its center. It is twice the length of the radius ($d = 2r$).

Key Reminders and Tips

Radius is King

Both formulas depend directly on the radius ($r$).

Diameter Conversion

If a problem provides the **diameter** ($d$) instead of the radius, always calculate the radius first by dividing the diameter by 2 ($r = d/2$). Failing to do this is a common mistake.

Units

Always include the correct units in your final answer (square units for surface area, cubic units for volume).
