Surface Area and Volume of Spheres

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Definition of a Sphere

A sphere is a set of all points in three-dimensional space that are equidistant from a central point. This fixed distance is known as the radius.

Surface Area of a Sphere

The total area of the outer surface of a sphere. The formula for the surface area ($SA$) of a sphere with radius $r$ is $SA = 4\pi r^2$.

Volume of a Sphere

The amount of space occupied by a sphere. The formula for the volume ($V$) of a sphere with radius $r$ is $V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$.

Important Terms (1/2)

Sphere

A perfectly round three-dimensional geometric object, like a ball, where every point on its surface is the same distance from its center.

Center

The central point within a sphere from which all points on the surface are equidistant.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a sphere to any point on its surface. It is a key measurement used in all sphere formulas.

Diameter ($d$)

The distance across a sphere passing through its center. It is twice the radius ($d = 2r$).

Surface Area ($SA$)

The total area of the "skin" or outer boundary of a three-dimensional object. For a sphere, it's the area of its curved surface.

Important Terms (2/2)

Volume ($V$)

The amount of three-dimensional space occupied by an object. For a sphere, it's the capacity or space enclosed within its surface.

Understanding Spheres

Definition

Formally, a sphere is defined as the collection of all points in space that are a fixed distance (the radius) from a central point (the center).

Solution

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a sphere?

A sphere is the set of all points in space that are a fixed distance (the radius) from a central point (the center).

List some key characteristics that distinguish a sphere from prisms and cylinders.

A sphere is perfectly round in every direction, has no flat faces, and no edges.

What is the formula for the surface area of a sphere with radius r?

SA = 4 pi r^2

What is the formula for the volume of a sphere with radius r?

V = (4/3) pi r^3

What important conversion step must be taken if a problem provides the diameter of a sphere instead of its radius?

You must divide the diameter by 2 to find the radius before substituting it into any sphere formulas.

Find the surface area of a sphere with a radius of 7 cm, expressing the answer in terms of pi.

SA = 4 pi (7)^2 = 4 pi 49 = 196 pi cm^2

Key Concepts (2/2)

Using pi approximately 3.14, calculate the surface area of a sphere with a radius of 7 cm to two decimal places.

SA = 4 (3.14) (7)^2 = 4 (3.14) 49 = 615.44 cm^2

A sphere has a diameter of 12 inches. Find its volume in terms of pi.

The radius is r = 12/2 = 6 in. V = (4/3) pi (6)^3 = (4/3) pi 216 = 288 pi in.^3

A sphere has a diameter of 12 inches. Using pi approximately 3.14, find its volume to two decimal places.

The radius is r = 6 in. V = (4/3) (3.14) (6)^3 = (4/3) (3.14) 216 = 904.32 in.^3

A regulation basketball has a diameter of 9.4 inches. Using pi approximately 3.14, calculate its surface area.

Radius r = 9.4/2 = 4.7 in. SA = 4 (3.14) (4.7)^2 = 4 (3.14) 22.09 approx 277.45 in.^2

Mars has a radius of approximately 3,390 km. Write the expression for its volume in terms of pi.

V = (4/3) pi (3,390)^3 km^3

Mars has a radius of 3,390 km. Calculate its volume in cubic kilometers, providing the numerical value as shown in the text (which implies pi is included in the number).

V = (4/3) (3,390)^3 approx 51,944,292,000 km^3, which is approximately 5.19 x 10^10 km^3.
