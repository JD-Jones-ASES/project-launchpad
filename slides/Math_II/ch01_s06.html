Key Concepts (1/2)

Scientific Notation Definition

A way to express numbers as a product of two parts: a coefficient (a number between 1 and 10, including 1) and a power of 10. The format is $a \times 10^n$, where $1 \le a < 10$ and $n$ is an integer.

Converting to Standard Form

The reverse process, where the decimal point is moved to the right for positive $n$ or to the left for negative $n$, adding zeros as necessary.

Multiplying in Scientific Notation

Multiply the coefficients ($a$ and $b$) and add the exponents ($m$ and $n$). The product $(ab) \times 10^{m+n}$ might need adjustment if $ab$ is not between 1 and 10.

Dividing in Scientific Notation

Divide the coefficients ($a$ by $b$) and subtract the exponents ($m$ minus $n$). The quotient $(a/b) \times 10^{m-n}$ might also need adjustment if $a/b$ is not between 1 and 10.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Orders of Magnitude

Represents the power of 10 closest to a given number. It's used to compare quantities; numbers that differ by roughly a factor of 10 differ by one order of magnitude.

Important Terms

Scientific Notation

A method of writing very large or very small numbers compactly as $a \times 10^n$, where $1 \le a < 10$ and $n$ is an integer.

Order of Magnitude

The power of 10 that is closest to a given number. It provides a quick way to estimate and compare the relative sizes of numbers.

Defining Scientific Notation

$a$ (the coefficient)

Must be a number such that $1 \le a < 10$. This means $a$ will have exactly one non-zero digit to the left of the decimal point.

$n$ (the exponent)

Must be an integer (positive, negative, or zero).

Converting from Standard Form to Scientific Notation

$93,000,000$

Move the decimal 7 places to the left to get $9.3$. Since $93,000,000$ is a large number, $n$ is positive. So, $9.3 \times 10^7$.

$0.000\,47$

Move the decimal 4 places to the right to get $4.7$. Since $0.000\,47$ is a small number, $n$ is negative. So, $4.7 \times 10^{-4}$.

$602,000,000,000,000,000,000,000$

Move the decimal 23 places to the left to get $6.02$. Since it's a large number, $n$ is positive. So, $6.02 \times 10^{23}$.

Converting from Scientific Notation to Standard Form

$3.6 \times 10^5$

The exponent is $5$ (positive). Move the decimal in $3.6$ five places to the right. $3.6 \rightarrow 360,000$.

$7.1 \times 10^{-3}$

The exponent is $-3$ (negative). Move the decimal in $7.1$ three places to the left. $7.1 \rightarrow 0.0071$.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is scientific notation?

A compact way to express very large or very small numbers using powers of 10, written in the form a × 10^n.

What is the primary purpose of using scientific notation?

To express very large or very small numbers concisely, making them easier to write and reducing the chance of errors from writing many zeros.

Describe the form of a number in scientific notation.

A number is in scientific notation when it is written as a × 10^n, where 1 ≤ a < 10 and n is an integer.

In the scientific notation a × 10^n, what is the rule for the value of 'a'?

The value of 'a' must be between 1 and 10 (inclusive of 1, exclusive of 10), meaning it has exactly one nonzero digit before the decimal point.

In the scientific notation a × 10^n, what type of number must 'n' be?

'n' must be an integer (positive, negative, or zero).

What is the first step when converting a number from standard form to scientific notation?

Move the decimal point so that there is exactly one nonzero digit to its left. This gives the value of 'a'.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you determine the exponent 'n' when converting a number ≥ 10 from standard form to scientific notation?

Count the number of places the decimal point was moved to the left. The exponent 'n' will be positive.

How do you determine the exponent 'n' when converting a number between 0 and 1 from standard form to scientific notation?

Count the number of places the decimal point was moved to the right. The exponent 'n' will be negative.

Convert 93,000,000 to scientific notation.

9.3 × 10^7 (decimal moved 7 places left).

Convert 0.00047 to scientific notation.

4.7 × 10^-4 (decimal moved 4 places right).

Convert 602,000,000,000,000,000,000,000 to scientific notation.

6.02 × 10^23 (decimal moved 23 places left).

Convert 0.000000031 to scientific notation.

3.1 × 10^-8 (decimal moved 8 places right).
