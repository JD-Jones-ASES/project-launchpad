Volume of Pyramids and Cones

Study Slides

Key Concepts

"Pointed" Solids

Pyramids and cones are characterized by tapering from a base to a single point called an apex or vertex.

The 1/3 Factor

A fundamental principle in geometry states that the volume of a pyramid or a cone is one-third the volume of a prism or cylinder, respectively, that has the same base area and the same height.

Volume of a Pyramid

The formula for the volume of any pyramid is $V = \frac{1}{3}Bh$, where $B$ represents the area of its polygonal base and $h$ is its height.

Base Area Calculation

For pyramids, the base area $B$ must be calculated first based on the shape of the base (e.g., square, rectangle, triangle).

Important Terms

Pyramid

A solid with one polygonal base and triangular lateral faces that meet at a common vertex (apex). Pyramids are named by the shape of their base (e.g., square pyramid, triangular pyramid).

Cone

A solid with one circular base and a curved lateral surface that tapers to a single point (the apex).

Apex (or Vertex)

The single point at the top of a pyramid or cone where all the lateral faces or the curved lateral surface meet.

Base Area ($B$)

The area of the bottom face of the pyramid or cone. For a pyramid, this will be the area of its polygonal base. For a cone, it's the area of its circular base ($\pi r^2$).

Height ($h$)

The perpendicular distance from the apex to the base of the pyramid or cone.

The "1/3 Factor" Relationship

• Volume of Pyramid = $\frac{1}{3}$ (Volume of corresponding Prism)

• Volume of Cone = $\frac{1}{3}$ (Volume of corresponding Cylinder)

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a pyramid?

A solid with one polygonal base and triangular lateral faces that meet at a common vertex.

What is a cone?

A solid with one circular base and a curved lateral surface that tapers to a point (the apex).

What is the 'apex' (or vertex) of a pyramid or cone?

The single point where the lateral faces (pyramid) or curved surface (cone) meet, to which the solid tapers.

How is a pyramid typically named?

By the shape of its base (e.g., a square pyramid, a triangular pyramid).

Describe the relationship between the volume of a pyramid and a prism with the same base area and height.

The volume of a pyramid is exactly one-third the volume of the corresponding prism.

How does the volume of a cone relate to the volume of a cylinder with the same base and height?

The volume of a cone is one-third the volume of the corresponding cylinder.

Key Concepts (2/2)

State the formula for the volume of a pyramid.

V = (1/3)Bh, where B is the area of the base and h is the height of the pyramid.

State the formula for the volume of a cone.

V = (1/3)πr²h, where r is the radius of the circular base and h is the height of the cone.

In the pyramid volume formula V = (1/3)Bh, what does 'B' specifically represent?

'B' represents the area of the pyramid's polygonal base.

In the cone volume formula V = (1/3)πr²h, what does 'r' specifically represent?

'r' represents the radius of the cone's circular base.

Calculate the volume of a square pyramid with a base side length of 9 cm and a height of 14 cm.

Base Area (B) = 9² = 81 cm². Volume (V) = (1/3)(81 cm²)(14 cm) = 378 cm³.

Find the volume of a rectangular pyramid whose base measures 6 in. by 4.5 in. and has a height of 10 in.

Base Area (B) = (6 in.)(4.5 in.) = 27 in². Volume (V) = (1/3)(27 in²)(10 in.) = 90 in³.
