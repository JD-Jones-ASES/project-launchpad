Volume of Pyramids and Cones

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Pyramids and Cones as "Pointed" Solids

Both shapes taper from a base to a single point at the top, known as the apex or vertex.

The $\frac{1}{3}$ Factor

A fundamental principle stating that the volume of a pyramid is one-third the volume of a prism with the same base area and height. The same relationship holds for cones and cylinders.

Volume of a Pyramid Formula

The volume ($V$) of any pyramid is calculated using the formula $V = \frac{1}{3}Bh$, where $B$ represents the area of its base and $h$ is its perpendicular height.

Volume of a Cone Formula

The volume ($V$) of a cone is calculated using $V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$, where $\pi r^2$ is the area of its circular base ($B$), $r$ is the radius of the base, and $h$ is its perpendicular height.

Base Area (B)

For pyramids, the base area $B$ will vary depending on the shape of the polygonal base (e.g., square, rectangle, triangle). For cones, the base is always circular, so $B = \pi r^2$.

Important Terms (1/2)

Pyramid

A solid three-dimensional shape with one polygonal base and triangular lateral faces that meet at a common point called the apex or vertex. Pyramids are named by the shape of their base (e.g., squa...

Cone

A solid three-dimensional shape with one circular base and a single curved lateral surface that tapers smoothly to a point called the apex.

Apex (or Vertex)

The single point at the top of a pyramid or a cone where all the lateral faces (for a pyramid) or the curved lateral surface (for a cone) meet.

Base

The bottom face of a pyramid (a polygon) or a cone (a circle). This is the face from which the solid tapers to the apex.

Height (h)

The perpendicular distance from the apex of the pyramid or cone to the plane containing its base.

Important Terms (2/2)

Base Area (B)

The calculated area of the pyramid's polygonal base or the cone's circular base.
