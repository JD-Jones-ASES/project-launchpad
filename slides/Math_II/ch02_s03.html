Real-World Application

Rational number operations are fundamental for solving practical problems, such as those found in recipes, measurements, and financial calculations.

Important Terms (1/2)

Rational Number

Any number that can be expressed as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b \neq 0$. This includes integers, fractions, mixed numbers, and terminating or repeating decimals.

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. It is essential for adding and subtracting fractions.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the absolute value of the numerator is greater than or equal to the absolute value of the denominator (e.g., $\frac{5}{2}$).

Mixed Number

A number consisting of an integer and a proper fraction (e.g., $2\frac{1}{2}$).

Reciprocal

The multiplicative inverse of a number. For a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, its reciprocal is $\frac{b}{a}$. Multiplying a number by its reciprocal always equals 1.

Converting Mixed to Improper Fractions

Multiply the whole number by the denominator, add the numerator, and place the result over the original denominator. For example, $5\frac{1}{3} = \frac{(5 \times 3) + 1}{3} = \frac{16}{3}$.
