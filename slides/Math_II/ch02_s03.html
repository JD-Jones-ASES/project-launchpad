Important Terms (1/2)

Rational Number

Any number that can be expressed as a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, where $a$ and $b$ are integers and $b eq 0$. This includes integers, fractions, mixed numbers, and terminating or repeating decimals.

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common multiple of the denominators of two or more fractions. It is essential for adding and subtracting fractions.

Improper Fraction

A fraction where the absolute value of the numerator is greater than or equal to the absolute value of the denominator (e.g., $\frac{5}{2}$).

Mixed Number

A number consisting of an integer and a proper fraction (e.g., $2\frac{1}{2}$).

Reciprocal

The multiplicative inverse of a number. For a fraction $\frac{a}{b}$, its reciprocal is $\frac{b}{a}$. Multiplying a number by its reciprocal always equals 1.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the general formula for adding or subtracting two fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$?

$a/b \pm c/d = (ad \pm bc) / bd$. (Using the Least Common Denominator (LCD) is often more efficient.)

What is the formula for multiplying two fractions $a/b$ and $c/d$?

$a/b \times c/d = (a \times c) / (b \times d)$.

What is the formula for dividing one fraction $a/b$ by another fraction $c/d$?

$a/b \div c/d = a/b \times d/c = (a \times d) / (b \times c)$, provided $c eq 0$.

How do you compute $5/6 - 3/8$?

Find the LCD of 6 and 8, which is 24. Convert to equivalent fractions: $20/24 - 9/24$. Subtract: $11/24$.

Compute $3/4 \times 10/9$.

Multiply numerators and denominators: $(3 \times 10) / (4 \times 9) = 30/36$. Simplify to $5/6$.

Compute $5/6 \div 15/8$.

Multiply by the reciprocal of the second fraction: $5/6 \times 8/15 = (5 \times 8) / (6 \times 15) = 40/90$. Simplify to $4/9$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Compute $2/3 + 5/12$.

Find the LCD of 3 and 12, which is 12. Convert $2/3$ to $8/12$. Add: $8/12 + 5/12 = 13/12$, or $1 \ 1/12$.

What are the two main methods for performing operations with mixed numbers?

1. Convert them to improper fractions first. 2. Work with the whole-number and fractional parts separately.

Compute $5 \ 1/3 - 2 \ 3/4$ by converting to improper fractions.

Convert to improper fractions: $16/3 - 11/4$. Find LCD (12): $64/12 - 33/12$. Subtract: $31/12$. Convert back to mixed number: $2 \ 7/12$.
