The total area of all the faces, or curved surfaces, of a three-dimensional solid. It represents the amount of material needed to cover the object entirely.

Rectangular Prism

A prism whose bases are rectangles. Its surface area is found by summing the areas of its six rectangular faces.

Triangular Prism

A prism whose bases are triangles. Its surface area is the sum of the areas of its two triangular bases and its three rectangular lateral faces.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Cylinder

A three-dimensional solid with two parallel and congruent circular bases connected by a curved lateral surface. When "unrolled," the curved lateral surface forms a rectangle.

Important Terms (1/2)

Surface Area (SA)

The sum of the areas of all the outer surfaces of a three-dimensional object.

Prism

A polyhedron with two identical, parallel faces (bases) and lateral faces that are rectangles.

Net

A flat pattern that can be folded along its edges to form a three-dimensional shape.

Bases

The two congruent, parallel polygonal faces of a prism or circular faces of a cylinder that define its shape.

Lateral Faces

The faces of a prism that are not its bases (usually rectangles). For a cylinder, it refers to the single curved surface between the two circular bases.

Important Terms (2/2)

Length ($l$)

One dimension of a rectangular prism, typically describing how long it is.

Width ($w$)

One dimension of a rectangular prism, typically describing how wide it is.

Height ($h$)

The perpendicular distance between the two bases of a prism or cylinder.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circular base to any point on its circumference.

Surface Area of a Rectangular Prism

• The top and bottom faces have dimensions length ($l$) and width ($w$), so their combined area is $2 \times (l \times w)$.

• The front and back faces have dimensions length ($l$) and height ($h$), so their combined area is $2 \times (l \times h)$.

• The two side faces have dimensions width ($w$) and height ($h$), so their combined area is $2 \times (w \times h)$.

Cylinders

• The height of this rectangle is the same as the height ($h$) of the cylinder.

• The length of this rectangle is the same as the circumference of the circular base. The circumference of a circle is given by $2\pi r$, where $r$ is the radius.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is surface area?

Surface area is the total area of all the faces (or curved surfaces) of a three-dimensional solid.

Define a prism.

A prism is a solid with two parallel, congruent polygonal bases and rectangular lateral faces.

How are prisms typically named?

Prisms are named by the shape of their bases (e.g., triangular prism, rectangular prism).

What shape are the lateral faces of any prism?

The lateral faces of a prism are always rectangular.

What is a net in the context of three-dimensional solids?

A net is a two-dimensional pattern that can be folded to form a three-dimensional solid.

Why are nets helpful for understanding surface area?

Nets are helpful for visualizing all the individual faces of a solid, which aids in calculating the total surface area.

Calculate the surface area of a rectangular prism with $l = 8$ cm, $w = 5$ cm, and $h = 3$ cm.

$SA = 2(8)(5) + 2(8)(3) + 2(5)(3) = 80 + 48 + 30 = 158 \text{ cm}^2$

Find the surface area of a rectangular prism with $l = 4.5$ cm, $w = 3$ cm, and $h = 2$ cm.

$SA = 2(4.5)(3) + 2(4.5)(2) + 2(3)(2) = 27 + 18 + 12 = 57 \text{ cm}^2$

Describe the faces of a triangular prism.

A triangular prism has two triangular bases and three rectangular lateral faces.

How do you calculate the surface area of a triangular prism?

The surface area of a triangular prism is the sum of the areas of its two triangular bases and its three rectangular lateral faces.
