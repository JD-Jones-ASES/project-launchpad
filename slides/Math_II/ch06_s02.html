For a rectangular prism with length ($l$), width ($w$), and height ($h$), the formula is $SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh$. This accounts for the area of three pairs of identical rectangular faces.

Important Terms (1/3)

Surface Area

The sum of the areas of all the surfaces of a three-dimensional object.

Prism

A polyhedron with two identical, parallel bases and rectangular lateral faces.

Net

A two-dimensional representation of a three-dimensional shape that can be folded to form the shape.

Base (of a prism/cylinder)

The two parallel, congruent faces that define the shape of a prism or cylinder.

Lateral Faces (of a prism)

The rectangular faces that connect the bases of a prism.

Important Terms (2/3)

Lateral Surface (of a cylinder)

The single curved surface that connects the two circular bases of a cylinder.

Rectangular Prism

A prism whose bases are rectangles. All six faces are rectangles.

Triangular Prism

A prism whose bases are triangles. It has two triangular faces and three rectangular faces.

Cylinder

A three-dimensional shape with two parallel circular bases and a curved lateral surface.

Length ($l$)

A dimension of a rectangular prism, typically referring to the longest side of the base.

Important Terms (3/3)

Width ($w$)

A dimension of a rectangular prism, typically referring to the shorter side of the base.

Height ($h$)

The perpendicular distance between the bases of a prism or cylinder.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circular base to any point on its circumference.
