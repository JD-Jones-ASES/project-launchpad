Important Terms (1/2)

Variable

A symbol, typically a letter (like *x*, *n*, *b*, *t*, *s*), used to represent an unknown numerical quantity in an equation.

Equation

A mathematical statement that asserts the equality of two expressions. It always contains an equals sign (=).

Perimeter

The total distance around the boundary or outside edge of a two-dimensional shape. For a triangle, it's the sum of the lengths of its three sides.

Rate

A measure of how quickly one quantity changes in relation to another. Often expressed as "per," such as miles per hour (mph), which is a unit of speed.

Distance-Rate-Time Formula

The fundamental formula `d = rt`, where `d` represents distance, `r` represents rate (speed), and `t` represents time.

Important Terms (2/2)

Fahrenheit (F)

A temperature scale where water freezes at 32 degrees and boils at 212 degrees.

Celsius (C)

A temperature scale where water freezes at 0 degrees and boils at 100 degrees.
