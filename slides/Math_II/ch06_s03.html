Chapter 6, Section 3

Volume of Prisms and Cylinders

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Volume Measurement

Volume quantifies the space inside a 3D figure, measured in cubic units (e.g., cm³, in.³, ft³).

General Volume of a Prism

The volume ($V$) of any prism is found by multiplying its base area ($B$) by its height ($h$): $V = Bh$.

Volume of a Rectangular Prism

A specific application of the prism formula, where the base area is length ($l$) times width ($w$), making the volume $V = lwh$.

Volume of a Triangular Prism

Requires first calculating the area of its triangular base ($B = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height of triangle}$), then multiplying by the prism's height ($h$).

Volume of a Cylinder

Similar to a prism, a cylinder's volume is its circular base area ($B = \pi r^2$) multiplied by its height ($h$): $V = \pi r^2 h$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Science Connection

There's a direct conversion between volume and liquid capacity: $1 \text{ cm}^3 = 1 \text{ mL}$. This is crucial for scientific and practical applications.

Important Terms (1/3)

Volume

The amount of three-dimensional space occupied by a solid. It is measured in cubic units.

Cubic Units

Units used to measure volume, representing the volume of a cube with sides of one unit length (e.g., cubic centimeters (cm³), cubic inches (in.³), cubic feet (ft³)).

Prism

A three-dimensional solid with two identical, parallel bases and rectangular sides. The shape of the base determines the name of the prism (e.g., rectangular prism, triangular prism).

Base Area ($B$)

The area of one of the parallel bases of a prism or cylinder. This is a crucial first step in calculating volume.

Height ($h$)

In the context of volume, the perpendicular distance between the two bases of a prism or cylinder. For a rectangular prism, this is often the third dimension distinct from length and width.

Important Terms (2/3)

Rectangular Prism

A prism with rectangular bases. Its volume is typically calculated as length × width × height.

Triangular Prism

A prism with triangular bases. Its volume requires calculating the area of the triangular base first.

Cylinder

A three-dimensional solid with two parallel, congruent circular bases connected by a curved surface.

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circle to any point on its circumference. In a cylinder, it's the radius of the circular base.

Diameter

The distance across a circle through its center. It is twice the radius ($d = 2r$).

Important Terms (3/3)

Capacity

The maximum amount that something can contain, often used in relation to liquid volume (e.g., liters, milliliters).
