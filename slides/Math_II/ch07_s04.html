Parts of a Right Triangle

A right triangle has one 90-degree angle. The side directly opposite the right angle is always the **hypotenuse**, which is also the longest side. The other two sides are called **legs**.

Important Terms (1/2)

Hypotenuse

In a right triangle, the side opposite the right angle. It is always the longest side.

Legs

The two sides of a right triangle that form the right angle.

Simplified Radical Form

The form of a square root where no perfect-square factor (other than 1) remains under the radical sign. For example, $\sqrt{75}$ simplifies to $5\sqrt{3}$.

Pythagorean Triple

A set of three positive integers ($a, b, c$) such that $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Important Terms (2/2)

Converse of the Pythagorean Theorem

A theorem stating that if the sides of a triangle ($a, b, c$) satisfy the relationship $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ (where $c$ is the longest side), then the triangle is a right triangle.
