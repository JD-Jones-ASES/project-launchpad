The Pythagorean Theorem ($a^2 + b^2 = c^2$)

This foundational formula states that in any right triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse (`c`) is exactly equal to the sum of the squares of the lengths of the two legs (`a` and `b`).

Legs

Simplified Radical Form

The form of a square root where no perfect-square factor (other than 1) remains under the radical symbol. For example, $\sqrt{50}$ simplifies to $5\sqrt{2}$.

Pythagorean Triple

A set of three positive integers ($a$, $b$, $c$) that precisely satisfy the Pythagorean Theorem, meaning $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$. Examples include (3, 4, 5) and (5, 12, 13).

Parts of a Right Triangle

Right Angle

This is the 90-degree angle within the triangle, typically marked with a square symbol.

Hypotenuse

This is the side opposite the right angle. Crucially, it is always the longest side of a right triangle. In the Pythagorean Theorem formula, the hypotenuse is represented by the variable $c$.

Legs

These are the two sides that form the right angle. They are the shorter sides of the right triangle. In the Pythagorean Theorem formula, the legs are represented by the variables $a$ and $b$.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary utility of the Pythagorean Theorem?

It connects the three sides of a right triangle with a simple equation, enabling the calculation of distances that cannot be measured directly.

In a right triangle, what term is used for the side opposite the right angle, and what is its distinguishing characteristic?

It is called the hypotenuse, and it is always the longest side of the triangle.

What are the names of the two sides of a right triangle that form the right angle?

They are called the legs.

State the Pythagorean Theorem using variables for the sides of a right triangle.

In a right triangle with legs $a$ and $b$ and hypotenuse $c$, the theorem states $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

A right triangle has a hypotenuse of 13 and one leg of 5. Find the length of the other leg.

$5^2 + b^2 = 13^2 \ 25 + b^2 = 169 \ b^2 = 144 \ b = 12$

A right triangle has legs of length 9 and 12. What is the length of its hypotenuse?

$9^2 + 12^2 = c^2 \ 81 + 144 = c^2 \ 225 = c^2 \ c = 15$

What does it mean for a square root to be in 'simplified radical form'?

A square root is in simplified radical form when no perfect-square factor (other than 1) remains under the radical. For example, $\sqrt{50} = 5\sqrt{2}$.

A right triangle has legs of length 4 and 7. Find the hypotenuse in simplified radical form.

$c^2 = 4^2 + 7^2 = 16 + 49 = 65 \ c = \sqrt{65}$. Since 65 has no perfect-square factors, it is already simplified.

A right triangle has legs of length 2 and 6. Find the hypotenuse in simplified radical form.

$c^2 = 2^2 + 6^2 = 4 + 36 = 40 \ c = \sqrt{40} = \sqrt{4 \times 10} = 2\sqrt{10}$.

Define a Pythagorean triple.

A Pythagorean triple is a set of three positive integers $a, b, c$ such that $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$.
