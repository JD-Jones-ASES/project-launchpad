Important Terms (1/2)

Inequality

A mathematical statement that compares two expressions using an inequality symbol (`<`, `>`, `\leq`, `\geq`).

Multi-step inequality

An inequality that requires two or more algebraic operations (like distribution, combining terms, or addition/subtraction) to isolate the variable.

Distribute (Distribution)

To multiply a term outside parentheses by each term inside the parentheses (e.g., `a(b + c) = ab + ac`).

Combine like terms

To simplify an algebraic expression by adding or subtracting terms that have the same variable raised to the same power (e.g., `5x + 2x = 7x`).

Least Common Denominator (LCD)

The smallest common multiple of the denominators of a set of fractions. Used to clear fractions from an equation or inequality.

Important Terms (2/2)

Reverse the inequality sign

The action of changing the direction of the inequality symbol (e.g., `<` to `>`, `\geq` to `\leq`), which is required when multiplying or dividing both sides of an inequality by a negative number.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the general strategy for solving multi-step inequalities?

The strategy is the same as for multi-step equations, but with the added rule of reversing the inequality when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.

When must you reverse the direction of an inequality sign?

You must reverse the inequality whenever you multiply or divide both sides of the inequality by a negative number.

What is the first step when solving an inequality that involves parentheses, such as $3(x - 2) \le 9$?

The first step is to distribute the number outside the parentheses to each term inside: $3x - 6 \le 9$.

Solve the inequality $-2(x + 4) > 6$.

Distribute: $-2x - 8 > 6$. Add 8: $-2x > 14$. Divide by -2 and reverse the sign: $x < -7$.

What is the initial step for solving an inequality like $4x - x + 5 > 14$?

The initial step is to combine like terms on the same side of the inequality: $3x + 5 > 14$.

Solve $4x - x + 5 > 14$.

Combine like terms: $3x + 5 > 14$. Subtract 5: $3x > 9$. Divide by 3: $x > 3$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you clear fractions from an inequality?

To clear fractions from an inequality, multiply every term on both sides by the least common denominator (LCD).

How can you handle decimals in an inequality like $0.5x + 1.2 \le 3.7$?

You can multiply every term on both sides by a power of 10 (e.g., 10) to convert decimals to whole numbers, or simply work with the decimals directly.

Solve $0.5x + 1.2 \le 3.7$.

Subtract 1.2 from both sides: $0.5x \le 2.5$. Divide by 0.5: $x \le 5$.

Write an inequality to represent this scenario: Plan A costs $30 per month. Plan B costs $18 per month plus $12 per gigabyte of data ($g$). For how many gigabytes is Plan B cheaper than Plan A?

The inequality is $18 + 12g < 30$.

Solve the inequality for the internet plan problem ($18 + 12g < 30$) to find when Plan B is cheaper than Plan A.

Solve $18 + 12g < 30$. Subtract 18: $12g < 12$. Divide by 12: $g < 1$. Plan B is cheaper when fewer than 1 gigabyte is used.
