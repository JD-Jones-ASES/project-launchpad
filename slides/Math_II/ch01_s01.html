Powers and Exponent Notation

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Exponents as Repeated Multiplication

An exponent indicates how many times a base number is multiplied by itself. This provides a compact way to write expressions like $3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3$ as $3^4$.

Base and Exponent

In the expression $a^n$, $a$ is the base (the number being multiplied), and $n$ is the exponent (or power), which tells you how many times to use the base as a factor.

Special Exponent Names

An exponent of 2 ($a^2$) is read as "a squared," and an exponent of 3 ($a^3$) is read as "a cubed."

Perfect Squares and Cubes

A perfect square is a whole number that results from squaring another whole number ($n^2$). A perfect cube is an integer that results from cubing another integer ($n^3$).

Negative Bases and Parentheses

The placement of parentheses is crucial when evaluating powers with negative bases.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Sign of Results with Negative Bases

Exponents in the Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

Exponents are evaluated after operations within parentheses/grouping symbols but before multiplication, division, addition, and subtraction.

Important Terms

Exponent

A number that tells how many times a base is used as a factor in repeated multiplication. In $a^n$, $n$ is the exponent. Also referred to as a "power."

Base

The number being multiplied by itself in an exponential expression. In $a^n$, $a$ is the base.

Power

Another term for an exponent, or the result of raising a base to an exponent (e.g., "$3^4$" is read as "three to the fourth power").

Perfect Square

Any whole number that can be expressed as $n^2$ for some whole number $n$ (e.g., $0, 1, 4, 9, \ldots$).

Perfect Cube

Any integer that can be expressed as $n^3$ for some integer $n$ (e.g., $1, 8, 27, \ldots$ for positive cubes).

Anatomy of an Exponential Expression

• $a$ is the base.

• $n$ is the exponent (or power).

• It means $a$ is multiplied by itself $n$ times: $a^n = \underbrace{a \times a \times a \times \ldots \times a}_{n \text{ factors}}$.

• $2^5 = 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32$. This is read as "two to the fifth power."

• $a^2$ is read as "$a$ squared."

• $a^3$ is read as "$a$ cubed."

Special Exponent Values: Perfect Squares and Cubes

Perfect Square

A whole number that can be obtained by squaring another whole number.

Perfect Cube

An integer that can be obtained by cubing another integer.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is an exponent?

An exponent tells how many times a base is used as a factor. In the expression $a^n$, $a$ is the base and $n$ is the exponent.

How is exponentiation similar to multiplication?

Just as multiplication is a shortcut for repeated addition, exponentiation is a shortcut for repeated multiplication.

In the expression $5^3$, what are the base and the exponent?

The base is 5 and the exponent is 3.

How is the expression $7^4$ read aloud?

"Seven to the fourth power."

What are the special names for $a^2$ and $a^3$?

$a^2$ is read as "a squared," and $a^3$ is read as "a cubed."

Define a perfect square.

A perfect square is any whole number that can be written as $n^2$ for some whole number $n$.
