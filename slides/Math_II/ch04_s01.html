Combining Like Terms

The core principle of simplifying expressions is that only like terms can be combined. To combine them, you add or subtract their coefficients while keeping the variable part exactly the same.

Simplification Process

Simplifying an expression involves identifying like terms, grouping them together, and then performing the indicated operations (addition or subtraction) on their coefficients.

Algebraic Expression

Term

A single number, a single variable, or a product of numbers and variables within an algebraic expression. Terms are separated by addition or subtraction signs.

Constant Term

A term in an algebraic expression that does not contain any variables. It is simply a number. For example, in $2x + 7$, the term $7$ is a constant term.

Like Terms

Terms that have exactly the same variable parts, including the same variables raised to the same powers. For instance, $4xy$ and $-2xy$ are like terms, but $4xy$ and $4x$ are not.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is an algebraic expression?

An algebraic expression is built from numbers, variables, and operations.

What is the primary goal when simplifying expressions?

To combine like terms.

Define 'term' in the context of algebraic expressions.

A term is a product of a number and one or more variables.

What is a coefficient?

The coefficient is the number part of a term.

What is a constant term?

A constant term is a term with no variable.

In the expression $5x^2 - 3x + 7$, identify all the terms.

The terms are $5x^2$, $-3x$, and $7$.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the expression $5x^2 - 3x + 7$, identify all the coefficients.

The coefficients are $5$, $-3$, and $7$.

What are like terms?

Like terms are terms that have exactly the same variable parts (same variables raised to the same powers).

Are $3x$ and $3x^2$ like terms? Explain why or why not.

No, they are not like terms because their variable parts have different exponents ($x$ vs. $x^2$).

Are $4xy$ and $4x$ like terms? Explain why or why not.

No, they are not like terms because one has the variable $y$ and the other does not, meaning their variable parts are different.

State the rule for combining like terms.

To combine like terms, add or subtract their coefficients and keep the variable part unchanged.

Provide the algebraic formula for combining like terms $ax^n$ and $bx^n$.

$ax^n + bx^n = (a + b)x^n$.
