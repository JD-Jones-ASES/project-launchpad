Important Terms (1/2)

Dimensional Analysis

A problem-solving strategy that uses conversion factors written as fractions to cancel unwanted units and leave desired units. Also known as the factor-label method.

Factor-label method

Another name for dimensional analysis, emphasizing the use of factors (conversion factors) and labels (units).

Conversion Factor

A ratio of equivalent measurements expressed as a fraction, used to convert a quantity from one unit to another. For example, $\frac{12 \text{ in}}{1 \text{ ft}}$.

Rate

A ratio that compares two quantities measured in different units, such as speed (km/h) or density (g/mL).

Density

A measure of mass per unit volume ($mass/volume$). It indicates how much "stuff" is packed into a given space.

Important Terms (2/2)

Customary System

A system of measurement primarily used in the United States, including units like inches, feet, miles, pounds, and quarts.

Metric System

An international system of measurement based on powers of ten, including units like centimeters, meters, kilometers, grams, kilograms, and liters.

What is another name for dimensional analysis?

The factor-label method.

Why are conversion factors written as fractions in dimensional analysis?

They are written as fractions so that unwanted units can cancel out, leaving only the desired units for the final answer.

What is the key conversion factor between inches and centimeters?

1 inch = 2.54 cm.

What is the key conversion factor between miles and kilometers?

1 mile = 1.609 km.

What is the key conversion factor between kilograms and pounds?

1 kg = 2.205 lb.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the key conversion factor between liters and quarts?

For what specific types of measurements is dimensional analysis 'especially useful'?

It is especially useful for converting rates such as speed, fuel efficiency, or density.

When converting a speed like 90 km/h to m/s, what units must be cancelled and what units must remain?

Kilometers must cancel to meters, and hours must cancel to seconds.

In science, how is density defined?

Density is defined as mass divided by volume: $density = \frac{mass}{volume}$.
