Key Concepts

Perimeter

The total distance around the boundary of a two-dimensional shape. It is a linear measurement.

Area

The amount of surface enclosed within the boundaries of a two-dimensional shape. It is measured in square units.

Important Terms (1/3)

Perimeter

The length of the boundary of a closed planar figure.

Area

The measure of the extent of a two-dimensional surface or region.

Base (of a triangle/trapezoid)

A side of a polygon, often used as a reference for calculating height or area. Triangles have one base; trapezoids have two parallel bases ($b_1$ and $b_2$).

Height (of a triangle/trapezoid)

The perpendicular distance from a base to the opposite vertex (for a triangle) or to the opposite parallel base (for a trapezoid).

Radius ($r$)

The distance from the center of a circle to any point on its circumference.

Important Terms (2/3)

Diameter ($d$)

The distance across a circle through its center; it is twice the radius ($d = 2r$).

Circumference ($C$)

The perimeter of a circle, calculated as $C = \pi d$ or $C = 2\pi r$.

Trapezoid

A quadrilateral with at least one pair of parallel sides.

Composite Figure

A geometric figure made up of two or more basic geometric shapes.

Fraction

A numerical quantity that is not a whole number (e.g., $1/2, 3/4$).

Important Terms (3/3)

Decimal

A number that contains a decimal point, representing a fractional part (e.g., $7.2, 5.5$).

$\pi$ (Pi)

A mathematical constant approximately equal to $3.14159$, representing the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. For many calculations, $3.14$ is used as an approximation.

General Concepts of Perimeter and Area

Perimeter

is a measure of length, so its units are linear (e.g., cm, ft, m).

Area

is a measure of surface, so its units are square units (e.g., cm$^2$, ft$^2$, m$^2$).

In geometry, what does 'perimeter' refer to?

Perimeter is the total distance around the outside edge of a two-dimensional shape.

In geometry, what does 'area' refer to?

Area is the measure of the space occupied by a two-dimensional surface or shape.

What is the formula for the area of a triangle?

A = (1/2)bh, where b is the base and h is the height.

What is the formula for the area of a trapezoid?

A = (1/2)(b1 + b2)h, where b1 and b2 are the lengths of the parallel bases and h is the height.

What is the formula for the area of a circle?

A = πr², where r is the radius of the circle.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the formula for the circumference of a circle?

C = 2πr or C = πd, where r is the radius and d is the diameter.

Calculate the area of a triangle with a base of 7.2 cm and a height of 5.5 cm.

A = (1/2)(7.2)(5.5) = (1/2)(39.6) = 19.8 cm².

Find the area of a trapezoid with bases 3/4 ft and 5/4 ft, and a height of 2/3 ft.

A = (1/2)(3/4 + 5/4)(2/3) = (1/2)(8/4)(2/3) = (1/2)(2)(2/3) = 2/3 ft².

Find the exact area of a circle with a radius of 6 inches.

A = π(6)² = 36π in².

Approximate the area of a circle with a radius of 6 inches, using π ≈ 3.14.

A = 36(3.14) = 113.04 in².
