Solving Two-Step Equations Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Two-Step Equations

These are equations that require exactly two inverse operations to isolate the variable. They typically follow the form `ax + b = c`.

Inverse Operations

Operations that "undo" each other. For example, addition is the inverse of subtraction, and multiplication is the inverse of division.

Order of Operations for Solving

Balancing Equations

To maintain the equality of an equation, any operation performed on one side of the equation must also be performed on the other side. This ensures the equation remains true.

Checking Solutions

After finding a potential solution for the variable, it's good practice to substitute that value back into the original equation to verify that it makes the equation true.

Important Terms (1/2)

Two-Step Equation

An algebraic equation that requires two inverse operations to isolate the variable and find its solution.

Inverse Operations

Mathematical operations that cancel each other out (e.g., adding 5 and subtracting 5; multiplying by 3 and dividing by 3).

Coefficient

The numerical factor that multiplies a variable in an algebraic term (e.g., in `2x`, `2` is the coefficient).

Constant Term

A term in an algebraic expression that has a fixed numerical value and does not contain a variable (e.g., in `2x + 3 = 11`, `3` and `11` are constant terms).

Variable

A symbol, typically a letter, used to represent an unknown numerical value in an equation.

Important Terms (2/2)

Solution

The specific value of the variable that makes an equation a true statement.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary characteristic that defines a "two-step equation"?

A two-step equation requires exactly two inverse operations to solve for the unknown variable.

What is the general form of a two-step equation as presented in this section?

The general form is $ax + b = c$.

What is the first key operation to undo when solving an equation of the form $ax + b = c$?

Undo addition or subtraction (the 'b' term) by adding or subtracting the constant from both sides.

What is the second key operation to undo after the first step when solving $ax + b = c$?

Undo multiplication or division (the 'a' coefficient) by dividing or multiplying both sides by the coefficient.

Why is it important to check your solution after solving a two-step equation?

Checking your solution verifies that the value found for the variable makes the original equation true.

What is the first mathematical step to solve the equation $2x + 3 = 11$?

Subtract 3 from both sides of the equation.

Key Concepts (2/2)

After subtracting 3 from both sides in $2x + 3 = 11$, what is the next step to solve for $x$?

Divide both sides of the resulting equation ($2x = 8$) by 2.

How do you undo the operation involving the constant term in an equation like $x/4 - 5 = 3$?

Add 5 to both sides of the equation.

If your equation simplifies to $x/4 = 8$, what operation would you use to solve for $x$?

Multiply both sides of the equation by 4.

What is the first step to solve the equation $-3x + 7 = -8$?

Subtract 7 from both sides of the equation.

After subtracting 7 from both sides in $-3x + 7 = -8$, what is the next step to solve for $x$?

Divide both sides of the resulting equation ($-3x = -15$) by -3.

What fundamental rule must you always follow when performing operations to solve an equation?

Always perform the same operation on both sides of the equation to maintain equality.
