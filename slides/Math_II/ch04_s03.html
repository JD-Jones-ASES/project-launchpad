Solving Two-Step Equations Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Two-Step Equations

These are equations that require exactly two inverse operations to isolate the variable. They typically follow the form `ax + b = c`.

Inverse Operations

Operations that "undo" each other. For example, addition is the inverse of subtraction, and multiplication is the inverse of division.

Order of Operations for Solving

Balancing Equations

To maintain the equality of an equation, any operation performed on one side of the equation *must* also be performed on the other side. This ensures the equation remains true.

Checking Solutions

After finding a potential solution for the variable, it's good practice to substitute that value back into the original equation to verify that it makes the equation true.

Important Terms (1/2)

Two-Step Equation

An algebraic equation that requires two inverse operations to isolate the variable and find its solution.

Inverse Operations

Mathematical operations that cancel each other out (e.g., adding 5 and subtracting 5; multiplying by 3 and dividing by 3).

Coefficient

The numerical factor that multiplies a variable in an algebraic term (e.g., in `2x`, `2` is the coefficient).

Constant Term

A term in an algebraic expression that has a fixed numerical value and does not contain a variable (e.g., in `2x + 3 = 11`, `3` and `11` are constant terms).

Variable

A symbol, typically a letter, used to represent an unknown numerical value in an equation.

Important Terms (2/2)

Solution

The specific value of the variable that makes an equation a true statement.
