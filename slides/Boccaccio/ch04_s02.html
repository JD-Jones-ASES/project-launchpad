The Decameron, Day the Third

Study Slides

The Theme of *Ingegno*

Central Value

The focus is on *ingegno*—intelligence, resourcefulness, and cleverness.

Narrative Function

These stories celebrate the human ability to shape circumstances through quick thinking and determination, imposing one's will on uncooperative situations.

The *Locus Amoenus* (Pleasant Place)

Order vs. Nature

The garden represents beautiful order imposed upon wild nature, mirroring how the clever protagonists of the day's stories impose order on their own lives.

Contrast to the Plague

The lush, fragrant, and life-filled garden stands in sharp contrast to the death and chaos of plague-ravaged Florence.

The Garden of the Third Day: Detailed Features

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Brigata

The company of ten young people (seven women and three men) who have fled Florence to tell stories in the countryside.

Cornice

The "frame" or transition narrative that connects the individual tales and describes the daily life of the storytellers.

Ingegno

A central theme of Day III; refers to wit, intelligence, resourcefulness, or cleverness applied to the pursuit of one's desires.

Locus Amoenus

Latin for "pleasant place"; a literary convention describing an idealized outdoor space, often characterized by shade, water, and flowers.

None

A canonical hour representing the mid-afternoon (traditionally around 3:00 PM).

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Seneschal

An official or steward in a noble household responsible for arrangements, provisions, and domestic management.

Tierce

A canonical hour representing the mid-morning (traditionally around 9:00 AM). "Half tierce" refers to the time shortly before this.
