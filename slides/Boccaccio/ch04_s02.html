Chapter 4, Section 2

The Decameron, Day the Third – Introduction

Study Slides

The Locus Amoenus (Pleasant Place)

Contrast

It stands in sharp opposition to the death and chaos of plague-ravaged Florence.

Symbolism

Just as the garden imposes order on wild nature through clipped hedges and manicured lawns, the protagonists of the stories impose their will on the world through their intellect.

Paradise on Earth

The setting echoes the Garden of Eden and the enclosed gardens of courtly romance, featuring diverse flora, abundant wildlife, and sophisticated waterworks.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does the garden setting mirror the thematic focus of the day’s stories?

> Answer: The garden represents the imposition of beautiful order on wild nature. This mirrors how the day’s protagonists use their cleverness to impose their will on difficult or uncooperative circumstances.

Q: What types of wildlife are observed by the company in the garden?

> Answer: The garden is peopled with roughly a hundred sorts of harmless creatures, including conies (rabbits), hares, goats, and fawns.

Q: What activities does the company engage in while waiting for the heat of the day to pass?

> Answer: While some sleep, others stay in the garden to read romances or play games such as chess and dice.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Who is the first storyteller charged by the queen to begin the tales?

> Answer: Filostrato is the first to tell a story on Day III.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What term is used to describe the frame narrative that connects the various tales of the Decameron?

The cornice.

Who is crowned queen at the beginning of the Third Day?

Neifile.

To what kind of setting does Neifile lead the brigata at the start of Day III?

A new palace surrounded by walled gardens of extraordinary beauty.

What literary convention, meaning "pleasant place," is reflected in Boccaccio's garden descriptions?

Locus amoenus.

In medieval culture, what two types of settings were commonly associated with enclosed gardens?

Eden and the gardens of courtly romance.

The garden of Day III is intended to stand in sharp contrast to which historical reality?

The plague-ravaged city of Florence.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the announced theme for the tales of Day III?

Those who, by their wit, obtained something they desired or recovered something they had lost.

What does the Italian term "ingegno" represent in the context of Day III?

Intelligence, resourcefulness, or cleverness applied to the pursuit of desire.

How does the garden setting symbolically mirror the theme of the protagonists' wit in Day III?

The garden imposes beautiful order on wild nature just as the characters impose their will on uncooperative circumstances.

On what day of the week does the Third Day begin?

Sunday.

Who was responsible for sending supplies ahead and preparing the new palace for the company?

The seneschal.

By what time of day had the company arrived at the new palace?

Well before half tierce.
