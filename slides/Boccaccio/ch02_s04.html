Study Slides

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: Why does Saladin feel the need to "devise a colourably reasonable pretext" for taking Melchisedech’s money?

Saladin has spent his treasure on "divers wars" and "lavish displays of magnificence." He needs a large sum of money quickly but does not want to use force to "constrain" the Jewish moneylender, as he prefers a seemingly legal or moral justificat...

Q: Describe the physical characteristics of the rings in Melchisedech's parable.

The original ring was of "extraordinary beauty and value." The two copies made by the "cunning artificer" were so similar to the original that "the maker himself could hardly tell which was the true ring."

Q: What was the traditional rule regarding the descent of the original ring?

The rule stated that whichever son was in possession of the ring by his father's bequest would be designated the heir and entitled to "receive from the rest the honour and homage due to a superior."

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: How does Saladin react when he realizes Melchisedech has evaded his trap?

Saladin admires the Jew's "excellent adroitness" and "circumspection." Instead of proceeding with his plan to harass Melchisedech, he decides to be honest about his financial needs.

Q: What is the final outcome of the interaction between Saladin and Melchisedech?

Melchisedech provides Saladin with the required funds. In return, Saladin repays the loan in full, gives Melchisedech "munificent gifts," and grants him an "honourable position" near his person, establishing a lifelong friendship.
