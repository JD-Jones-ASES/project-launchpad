Boccaccio’s Decameron and the Beffa Tradition

Study Slides

Marriage in 14th-Century Florence

Social Arrangement

Marriage was a negotiation between families regarding property, lineage, and social alliances. Dowries were essential, and the personal desires of the bride and groom were secondary.

The Gender Gap

While Church law required consent and viewed marriage as a sacred sacrament, social reality often forced women into arrangements that did not serve their needs.

Adultery as Social Fact

Unlike Dante, who treated adultery as a sin to be punished in Hell, Boccaccio treats it as a social fact. He focuses on the drama, danger, and contest of wits rather than issuing moral condemnations.

Literary Origins: The Fabliaux Tradition

French Fabliaux

12th- and 13th-century short, humorous verse tales often featuring clever wives outwitting cuckolded husbands.

The Roman de Renart

A cycle celebrating the triumph of cunning over brute strength.

Summary of Tales in Day VII

Tale. Protagonists

The Deception

VII.1. Gianni Lotteringhi & his wife

The wife uses a "ghost story" and signals to hide her lover from her simple-minded husband.

VII.3. Fra Rinaldo & a Florentine wife

A friar pretends to perform a religious "cure" on a child to provide cover for his affair with the mother.

VII.4. Tofano & Monna Ghita

After being locked out by her husband, Ghita fakes a suicide in a well to trick him into coming outside, then locks *him* out.

VII.5. The Jealous Husband

A husband disguises himself as a priest to hear his wife’s confession; she recognizes him and leads him to guard the wrong door.

VII.6. Madonna Isabella

Isabella hides one lover under a chicken coop and uses her husband to "rescue" him when another admirer arrives.

VII.7. Lodovico & Madonna Beatrice

Lodovico disguises himself as a servant to access his lover; a clever maid saves them when the husband discovers the truth.

VII.8. The String on the Toe

A husband ties a string to his wife's toe to track her movement; she replaces herself with a maid and shames him publicly.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

An elaborate practical joke or trick; the central organizing principle of the tales in Day VII.

Beffato

The dupe or the person who is deceived in a beffa.

Beffatore

The trickster or the person who orchestrates the deception.

Comare

Literally "co-mother" or godmother. Sexual relations with a *comare* were considered spiritual incest by the medieval Church.

Confraternita

A lay religious society dedicated to prayer and charity. Boccaccio often uses membership in these as a sign of a character’s gullibility.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Fabliaux

Short, humorous, and often bawdy medieval French verse tales that served as a literary antecedent to Boccaccio’s work.

Ingegno

Human intelligence, wit, and resourcefulness; the quality required to successfully execute or survive a beffa.

Motto

A witty remark or clever verbal retort; one of the two "engines" of Boccaccian comedy alongside the beffa.
