Chapter 8, Section 1

Day the Seventh of Boccaccio’s Decameron

Study Slides

The Beffa: Trickery as Art

A Trickster (beffatore)

One who exploits a target's weakness through superior wit or audacity.

A Dupe (beffato)

One who is deceived, usually due to a specific flaw such as vanity, jealousy, or gullibility.

Psychological Insight

The trickster must understand the dupe’s desire to believe in order to maintain domestic order or avoid humiliation.

Summary of Day VII Tales

Novel. Central Figures

The Trick/Deception

VII.1. Gianni Lotteringhi & Wife

The wife uses a "ghost story" and signals to hide her lover from her simple-minded husband.

VII.2. Peronella & Stratico

Peronella hides her lover in a tub and convinces her husband the lover is a buyer inspecting it.

VII.3. Fra Rinaldo & Godmother

A friar pretends to perform a religious "cure" on a child to provide cover for an affair.

VII.4. Tofano & Monna Ghita

After being locked out, the wife fakes a suicide in a well to trick her husband into coming outside so she can lock him out.

VII.5. The Jealous Husband

A husband disguises himself as a priest to hear his wife's confession; she recognizes him and feeds him a false story.

VII.6. Isabella & Leonetto

Caught with two admirers, Isabella hides one under a chicken coop and uses her husband to "rescue" the other.

VII.7. Lodovico & Beatrice

Lodovico disguises himself as a servant to access Beatrice; a maid’s quick thinking saves them when discovered.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

An elaborate trick, practical joke, or deception used as a primary engine of comedy in the Decameron.

Beffatore

The trickster or person who carries out the beffa.

Beffato

The dupe or victim of the trick.

Comare

Literally "co-mother" or godmother. Sexual relations with a comare were considered "spiritual incest" by the medieval Church.

Confraternita

A lay religious society dedicated to prayer and charity. In Day VII, Boccaccio often uses membership in these as a marker of a character's foolish credulity.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Fabliaux

Short, humorous, often bawdy medieval French verse tales that served as a precursor to Boccaccio’s style.

Ingegno

Intelligence, wit, and resourcefulness; the essential quality required to successfully execute a beffa.

Motto

A witty remark or clever verbal retort; alongside the beffa, it is one of the two great engines of Boccaccian comedy.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What Italian term refers to the elaborate tricks, deceptions, or practical jokes that serve as the organizing principle of the Day VII tales?

Beffa

In the context of a beffa, what quality does Boccaccio use to describe the intelligence and resourcefulness of a trickster?

Ingegno

According to Boccaccio, what are the two great engines of comedy in the Decameron?

The beffa and the motto.

What term is used to identify the dupe who is deceived in a beffa?

Beffato

What term is used to identify the trickster who exploits a dupe's weakness in a beffa?

Beffatore

Which gendered pattern is predominantly observed among the tricksters and dupes of Day VII?

The tricksters are almost always wives, and the dupes are almost always husbands.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which short, humorous verse tales from 12th and 13th-century France served as the most important literary antecedent for the beffa?

The French fabliaux.

What 12th-century French literary cycle celebrates the triumph of cunning over strength and influenced the beffa tradition?

The Roman de Renart (the Reynard cycle).

What psychological depth does Boccaccio add to the tradition of clever wives outwitting their husbands?

He provides his characters with clear motivations, such as desire, economic necessity, or pride.

Why do the husbands in Day VII typically fall for their wives' tricks despite the tricks being elaborate?

They want to trust their wives and preserve the appearance of domestic order.

What was the primary function of marriage in 14th-century Florence?

It was a social and economic arrangement negotiated between families.

What financial element was central to marriage negotiations among the merchant and noble classes in Boccaccio's time?

The dowry.
