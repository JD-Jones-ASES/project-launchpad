Study Slides

The Horseback Metaphor

The Metaphor

A well-told story is likened to a good horse; both should carry the listener effortlessly to their destination, making a long journey pass pleasantly.

The Failure

When the knight tells the story poorly, the "horse" (the story) becomes uncomfortable, leading Madonna Oretta to remark that the horse "trots too hard."

Characteristics of Poor Storytelling

Repetition

Repeating the same word multiple times (three, four, or six times).

Hesitation/Correction

Frequently "harking back" and saying "I said not well."

Confusion

Erring in the names of characters or mismatched events.

Poor Delivery

Using a mode of delivery that does not match the character of the persons or incidents in the story.
