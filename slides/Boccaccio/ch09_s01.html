Day VIII of Boccaccio’s Decameron

Study Slides

The Spectrum of the Beffa: Comedy vs. Cruelty

Harmless Comedy (Novel VIII.3)

The Calandrino tales exemplify "comic realism." These stories involve physical humor and psychological manipulation where the victim suffers no lasting financial or physical ruin.

Extreme Revenge (Novel VIII.7)

The tale of the Scholar and the Widow represents the opposite pole, where revenge becomes physically dangerous and psychologically devastating.

Summary of the Tales of Day VIII

Novel. Characters

Plot Summary

VIII.1. Gulfardo & the Wife of Gasparruolo

A soldier tricks a merchant’s wife by using borrowed money to pay for her favors, then "returning" the loan to the husband in her presence.

VIII.2. The Priest of Varlungo

A priest seduces a woman by leaving his cloak as security, then tricks her into returning it without paying the promised fee.

VIII.3. Calandrino, Bruno, & Buffalmacco

Friends convince the gullible Calandrino he has found an invisibility stone (heliotrope), leading to his public humiliation.

VIII.4. The Provost of Fiesole

A widow and her brothers trap a lecherous church official in a compromising position to be discovered by the bishop.

VIII.5. Three Judges from the Marches

Florentines humiliate three stingy provincial judges by stealing their breeches while they are presiding in court.

VIII.6. Calandrino and the Pig

Bruno and Buffalmacco steal Calandrino’s pig and rig a "divination test" to make it look like Calandrino stole it himself.

VIII.7. The Scholar and the Widow

A scholar, humiliated by a widow, retaliates by trapping her on a tower in the scorching sun, causing severe physical torture.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Beffa

A trick or prank, often involving the manipulation of a "dupe" by a more clever "trickster."

Commedia dell'arte

A later form of Italian improvisational theater featuring stock characters; Boccaccio’s Calandrino tales are seen as an early ancestor of this tradition.

Fiesole

A hilltop town overlooking Florence, historically an ancient Etruscan settlement and a medieval bishopric.

Guelphs and Ghibellines

The two primary warring factions in medieval Italian politics. Florence was a Guelph city but remained deeply divided by internal feuds.

Ingegno

Human wit, cleverness, or genius. In Day VIII, it is the primary engine of both comedy and revenge.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Oltrarno

Literally "beyond the Arno," the district of Florence on the south bank of the river, traditionally home to artisans and craftsmen.

The Marches (Marche)

A region in central Italy. In the Decameron, characters from this region are often depicted as provincial or naive compared to Florentines.

Vendetta

The recognized social institution of personal or family revenge in medieval Italy, which was sometimes acknowledged by law.

Vernaccia

A specific white wine from the Tuscan hill town of San Gimignano, used by Boccaccio to ground his tales in local culture.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What central question regarding the 'beffa' (trick) is raised by the transition from Day VII to Day VIII in the Decameron?

When does a clever joke stop being funny and become an act of cruelty?

What specific stone does Calandrino believe he has found in the Mugnone stream in Tale VIII.3?

The heliotrope (a stone of invisibility).

How does the outcome for the victim in the Calandrino tale (VIII.3) differ from the victim in the Scholar and the Widow (VIII.7)?

Calandrino suffers no lasting harm, while Elena is physically and psychologically devastated.

What shared quality of mind does Boccaccio use to link harmless pranks and lethal revenge in Day VIII?

Ingegno (wit or intelligence).

What literary style is defined by Boccaccio's use of real Florentine streets and everyday artisans as characters?

Comic realism.

In the context of Boccaccio's Florence, what does the term 'Oltrarno' literally mean?

Beyond the Arno.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which district of Florence was historically home to the artisans and craftsmen featured in the Calandrino tales?

The Oltrarno.

What 16th-century improvisational theater tradition was anticipated by the stock characters and situations in Boccaccio’s Calandrino tales?

Commedia dell'arte.

In Tale VIII.7, how does the scholar punish Elena for making him wait in the cold?

He traps her on a tower roof in the scorching July sun.

Who is the narrator of Tale VIII.7, the story of the Scholar and the Widow?

Pampinea.

Why should a reader not assume that a narrator's approval in the Decameron reflects Boccaccio's own opinion?

The multiple narrators create a polyphony of perspectives that may differ from the author's.

What medieval Italian social institution acknowledged the right to personal vengeance under specific circumstances?

Vendetta.
