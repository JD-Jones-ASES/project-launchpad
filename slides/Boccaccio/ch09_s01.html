The Spectrum of Comedy and Cruelty

The Lighthearted Pole (VIII.3)

The tale of Calandrino and the stone of invisibility represents broad, physical humor where the victim suffers no lasting social or financial ruin.

The Dark Pole (VIII.7)

The tale of the Scholar and the Widow illustrates a *vendetta* so extreme it borders on horror, featuring physical torture and psychological devastation.

Summary of the Tales (Day VIII)

Tale. Protagonists

Summary of the *Beffa*

VIII.1. Gulfardo & a Merchant's Wife

A soldier borrows money from a merchant to pay the merchant’s wife for sex, then "returns" the money to the husband in her presence.

VIII.2. The Priest of Varlungo

A priest seduces a woman using his cloak as collateral, then tricks her into returning the cloak without paying her.

VIII.3. Calandrino, Bruno, & Buffalmacco

Friends convince the gullible Calandrino he has found a stone of invisibility (the heliotrope); he is later beaten by his wife.

VIII.4. The Provost of Fiesole

A widow and her brothers trap a lecherous church official in bed and have him discovered by his bishop.

VIII.5. Three Judges from the Marches

Three stingy judges are publicly humiliated when their trousers are stolen while they are presiding over court.

VIII.6. Calandrino & the Pig

Bruno and Buffalmacco steal Calandrino's pig and rig a "divination" test to make Calandrino look like the thief.

VIII.7. The Scholar & the Widow

A scholar seeks extreme revenge on a widow who humiliated him by trapping her on a roof in the scorching sun.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

A trick, prank, or practical joke, often involving the public humiliation of a victim.

Commedia dell'arte

A later form of Italian improvisational theater (16th century) featuring stock characters and situations of deception that the Calandrino tales anticipated.

Guelphs and Ghibellines

The two primary political factions in medieval Italy; their conflict often served as a framework for *vendettas*.

Heliotrope

The mythical stone of invisibility that Calandrino believes he has found in the Mugnone stream.

Ingegno

Wit, cleverness, or genius; the mental agility required to create or escape a trick.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Oltrarno

Literally "beyond the Arno"; the Florentine neighborhood associated with the artisan class.

Polyphony

A literary technique where multiple narrators provide different perspectives, preventing the reader from settling on one "correct" moral interpretation.

Vendetta

The social and sometimes legal institution of personal or family revenge.

Vernaccia

A specific type of white wine from San Gimignano, used by Boccaccio to ground his tales in local Tuscan culture.
