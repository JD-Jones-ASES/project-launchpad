Chapter 10, Section 1

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day IX

Study Slides

Key Characteristics of Day IX

Tone

Notably lighter than previous days, focusing on comic relief, easier laughter, and lower stakes.

Brevity

Many stories are short anecdotes or brief comic scenes centered on a single reversal.

Structural Position

It acts as a moment of relaxation before the "grand ascent" of Day X, which focuses on themes of virtue and magnanimity.

Themes

Even without a mandatory theme, the tales revisit the work's central concerns: clerical hypocrisy, the comedy of fortune, female cleverness, and human gullibility.

Anticlerical Satire

The Target

Hypocrisy—specifically the gap between vows (chastity, poverty) and actual behavior (lust, cunning).

The Modes of Satire

The Fabliau and the Bed-Trick

Definition

A short, comic, often bawdy verse tale that originated in medieval France.

Boccaccio’s Transformation

He converted the verse fabliau into prose novella, adding psychological depth and narrative sophistication.

The Bed-Trick

A classic plot device where characters end up in the wrong beds due to darkness. Boccaccio emphasizes "spatial choreography," using specific props—like a baby's cradle—to drive the confusion.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Anticlerical Satire

A literary mode that mocks the clergy for failing to adhere to their religious vows and spiritual duties.

Bed-Trick

A narrative device in which characters unknowingly sleep with the wrong person due to darkness or deception.

Cluniac Reforms

Tenth-century movements aimed at correcting clerical corruption within the medieval Church.

Fabliau

A short, bawdy, comic tale in verse, originally popular in medieval France.

Mendicant Orders

Thirteenth-century religious orders (such as friars) that took vows of poverty; often the target of Boccaccio's satire regarding worldly cunning.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Mimesis

A term used by critic Erich Auerbach to describe literary realism—the representation of the everyday world as a serious subject for art.

Novella

A short prose tale; the form Boccaccio helped standardize by adapting earlier verse traditions.

Reprise

A musical or literary term for a return to earlier themes or styles; used to describe the "free theme" nature of Day IX.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In the structural arc of the Decameron, Day IX is best described as a _____ between the intense previous days and the grand finale.

reprise (or intermission)

How does the thematic requirement of Day IX differ from Days VII or VIII?

It has no prescribed subject or theme.

Compared to the elaborate and morally troubling tales of Days VII and VIII, the tone of Day IX is notably _____.

gentler

What narrative quality of the tales in Day IX demonstrates Boccaccio's artistry through brevity?

They are often short anecdotes or brief comic scenes.

The tale of the abbess's breeches (IX.2) revisits which recurring theme from the very first day of the Decameron?

Clerical hypocrisy.

Which earlier tale's comedy of fortune and mistaken identity is mirrored by the inn at Forlì in IX.6?

Andreuccio's adventures in Naples (II.5).

Key Concepts (2/2)

In Boccaccio's anticlerical satire, the target is specifically the _____ between religious ideals and the reality of clerical life.

gap (or hypocrisy)

Does the Decameron's anticlerical satire function as an attack on the Christian faith itself?

No, it attacks the failure of Christian institutions to live up to their own standards.

According to the text, why are Boccaccio's clergy characters considered comic rather than purely wicked?

They pretend to be more than human while acting conspicuously less than human.

Identify the mode of anticlerical satire used in Novel I.1 (Ser Ciappelletto).

Intellectual satire.

The tale of Masetto's pretended muteness (III.1) utilizes which mode of anticlerical satire?

Physical satire.

Which specific mode of satire is displayed in VI.10 through Fra Cipolla's improvisational genius?

Rhetorical satire.
