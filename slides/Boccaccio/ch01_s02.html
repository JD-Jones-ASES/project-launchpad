Study Slides

The Role of the Narrator

Significance

The narrator’s identity shapes the story. For example, Dioneo is an irreverent joker, while Filomena focuses on fortune and adventure.

Moral Complexity and Ambiguity

Complex Characters

Characters like the monstrous yet brilliant Ser Ciappelletto or the patient yet disturbing Griselda defy easy categorization as "good" or "bad."

Medieval Perspective

Medieval audiences were more comfortable with moral ambiguity and were expected to exercise their own judgment rather than being handed an easy answer.

Architectural Structure

Thematic Progression

Each "Day" has a theme.

The Arc

The overall collection moves from a focus on comedy and satire toward themes of nobility and virtue.

Internal Echoes

Tales within a specific Day often echo or contrast with one another to build a larger argument.

The Rigg Translation

Style

Faithful to the original Italian but written in Edwardian English.

Vocabulary

Includes archaic terms such as "whither" (where), "hath" (has), and "prithee" (I pray you).

Themes and Satire

Bawdiness

Many tales deal frankly with sexual desire and adultery. These are rooted in the *fabliau* tradition and are intended to illuminate human nature.

Anticlericalism

The Beffa

A central motif in the book is the *beffa*, or practical joke. These stories celebrate cleverness while often punishing gullibility with comic cruelty.
