Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: What is the significance of Fra Cipolla’s name in the context of Certaldo?

The name means "Brother Onion." The region of Certaldo was famous throughout Tuscany for its onions, making the name a fitting and humorous choice for a friar who found "good pasture" (plentiful donations) there.

Q: List four of the "nine qualities" Fra Cipolla attributes to his servant Guccio.

The text lists: lazy, uncleanly, a liar, negligent, disobedient, foul-mouthed, reckless, witless, and mannerless.

Q: Why were the townspeople of Certaldo so easily fooled by a parrot's feather?

The text explains that the luxuries of Egypt (like parrots) had not yet become common in Tuscany. The simple folk of the countryside had mostly never seen or heard of such a bird.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does Fra Cipolla describe the "payment" used in the land of Menzogna?

He describes people "paying in nought but unminted coin," which is a metaphorical reference to paying with false promises.

Q: What miracle does Fra Cipolla claim the charcoal can perform?

He tells the congregation that anyone marked with a cross from these coals will live secure for the year, protected from the touch of fire.

Q: Who takes over as King of the company at the end of Day VI?

Dioneo is crowned King after the Queen (Elisa) completes her reign.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: What is the "Ladies' Vale" (Valle delle Donne)?

It is a secluded, perfectly circular plain surrounded by six hills, featuring a crystal-clear lake and lush vegetation. The ladies of the company go there to bathe and relax.

Q: What theme does Dioneo set for the stories of the Seventh Day?

He ordains that the company shall discuss "the tricks that, either for love or for their deliverance from peril, ladies have heretofore played their husbands."

Key Concepts (1/2)

In the context of the Decameron, what does the title 'Fra' signify?

An abbreviation for frate, used for members of mendicant religious orders.

What is the English translation of the name 'Fra Cipolla'?

Brother Onion.

In which small Tuscan town does Fra Cipolla set his annual collection of donations?

Certaldo.

Why is the setting of Certaldo considered a form of self-mockery by Boccaccio?

It was Boccaccio's own birthplace or ancestral home.

What sacred relic does Fra Cipolla promise to show the townspeople of Certaldo?

A feather from the wing of the Angel Gabriel.

Which character in Chaucer’s 'Canterbury Tales' is often compared to Fra Cipolla due to their trade in fake relics?

The Pardoner.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Who are the two 'wily young wags' that plot to prank Fra Cipolla?

Giovanni del Bragoniera and Biagio Pizzini.

What physical object do the pranksters use to replace the supposed Angel Gabriel feather?

Lumps of charcoal.

How is Fra Cipolla’s rhetorical skill described in comparison to famous Roman orators?

He is so excellent a speaker he could be mistaken for Tully (Cicero) or Quintilian.

What is the name of Fra Cipolla's famously negligent and 'rascallion' servant?

Guccio.

What three derogatory nicknames are commonly applied to the servant Guccio?

Balena (Whale), Imbratta (Filth), and Porco (Hog).

How many specific negative qualities does Fra Cipolla claim his servant Guccio possesses?

Nine.
