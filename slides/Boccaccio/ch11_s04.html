Chapter 11, Section 4

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day X, Tale 5

Study Slides

Character Profiles

Character. Role

Key Motivation

Madonna Dianora. A noblewoman of Friuli

Desires to remain faithful to her husband while honoring her word.

Messer Ansaldo. A noble and wealthy suitor

Initially driven by "unbridled passion," later moved by compassion and honor.

Giliberto. Dianora’s husband

Values the "purity of the lady's soul" and the sanctity of a promise over his own pride.

The Necromancer. A magician/sorcerer

Performs the "impossible task" for money but ultimately chooses to match the nobility of the other men.

Magnificenza (Magnificence/Liberality)

Giliberto

gives up his exclusive right to his wife’s chastity to preserve her honor as a woman of her word.

Ansaldo

gives up the "prize" he has long pursued (Dianora) out of respect for the husband’s generosity.

The Necromancer

gives up a "vast sum" of money, refusing payment to ensure he is not outdone in liberality by the noblemen.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Ambassages

Messages or missions sent by a suitor (in this context, the messages sent by Ansaldo to Dianora).

Calends of January

The first day of January.

Friuli

A region in northeastern Italy near modern-day Udine; the setting of the tale.

Guerdon

A reward or recompense.

Liberality

The quality of being generous or giving freely; a key virtue explored on the Tenth Day.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Magnificenza

(Magnificence) A term used to describe the highest form of greatness and generosity in spirit and action.

Necromancer

A magician or sorcerer; in medieval literature, the term often referred to one who practiced learned magic.

The Brigata

The group of ten young people (seven women, three men) who tell stories to pass the time while escaping the plague in *The Decameron*.
