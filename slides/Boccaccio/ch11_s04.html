Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 11, Section 4

Magnanimity and Virtue in Boccaccio’s Decameron (Day X, Novel 5)

Study Slides

The Upward Spiral of Magnificenza

Giliberto (The Husband)

Prioritizes his wife’s honor and the sanctity of her word over his own pride and marital rights.

Messer Ansaldo (The Suitor)

Moved by the husband's nobility, he renounces his "unbridled passion" and releases the lady from her oath.

The Necromancer (The Magician)

Inspired by the nobility of the noblemen, he refuses his substantial monetary reward.

Character Analysis

Character. Role

Primary Motivation

Madonna Dianora. A noble lady of Udine

To rid herself of an importunate suitor while maintaining her silence.

Giliberto. Dianora's husband

Preservation of honor and protection from the necromancer's potential malice.

Messer Ansaldo. A noble suitor

Ardent love and desire for Dianora.

The Necromancer. A hired magician

Monetary gain (initially).

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Ambassages

Messages or advertisements sent by a suitor (in this context, via a messenger woman).

Brigata

The "gay company" of storytellers in The Decameron (though referred to here as the company extolling Messer Gentile).

Calends (of January)

The first day of the month.

Friuli

A region in northeastern Italy near modern-day Udine; chosen by Boccaccio to provide a pan-Italian scope to his work.

Guerdon

A reward or recompense.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Liberality

Generosity or the quality of being open-handed and noble in spirit.

Magnificenza (Magnificence)

A high medieval virtue involving greatness of spirit, particularly in the context of being lavishly generous or noble.

Necromancer

A term used loosely here for a magician, sorcerer, or "adept" who uses spells to manipulate the natural world.

Shrewd (Air)

In the context of Friuli’s climate, meaning piercingly cold or harsh.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the narrator of the tale involving Madonna Dianora and Messer Ansaldo?

Emilia

In which city and region of Italy is the story of Madonna Dianora set?

Udine, Friuli

What is the name of Madonna Dianora's wealthy husband?

Giliberto

What is Messer Ansaldo Gradense's reputation within the story?

He is known for his feats of arms and courtesy.

Why did Madonna Dianora set an "impossible task" for Messer Ansaldo?

To rid herself of his persistent and unwanted pursuit.

What specific task did Dianora require Ansaldo to perform in exchange for her love?

Produce a garden full of flowers and fruit in the dead of January.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How did Ansaldo manage to fulfill Dianora's impossible request?

He hired a necromancer for a substantial sum of money.

The motif where a character is set a feat deemed unachievable is known as the _____.

impossible task

For how long did the magical garden remain visible near the city?

Three days

What physical proof did Ansaldo first send to Dianora to show the task was complete?

Some of the finest fruits and flowers gathered from the magical garden.

How did Dianora react internally upon realizing Ansaldo had fulfilled his promise?

She was filled with distress and became the "saddest woman alive."

What was Giliberto's primary reason for allowing his wife to go to Ansaldo?

He valued the purity of her soul and feared the necromancer's potential mischief.
