Short-Answer Practice Questions

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Balladette

A short ballad or song; one of the musical forms performed by the *brigata* before their meal.

Brigata

The company of ten young people (seven women and three men) who have fled the plague to tell stories in the Italian countryside.

Champaign

A broad, open plain or meadow where the company sauntered at dawn.

Comfits

Sweetmeats or candy consumed by the company along with wine to repair "slight fatigue."

Courtly Love

A medieval literary convention involving idealized, ennobling romantic love, traditionally between a knight and a noble lady.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Fiammetta

The queen of Day V; the name Boccaccio traditionally gave to the woman he loved in his early romances.

Fortuna

The personification of luck or fortune, whose "unpredictable turning" is reflected in the alternating tragic and comic themes of the stories.

None

A traditional time of day (mid-afternoon) shortly after which the *brigata* reassembled for storytelling.

Seneschal

An official or steward in a medieval household responsible for arranging meals and domestic order.

Stampita

A type of musical composition or song performed by the company before eating.
