Boccaccio’s Tale of Lisabetta and the Pot of Basil (Decameron IV.5)

Study Slides

Imagery of Growth and Decay

The Head

Represents the secret, gruesome reality of the brothers' violence.

The Basil

Specifically "basil of Salerno," it grows with "exceeding great beauty and fragrance," nourished by Lisabetta’s tears and the "richness given to the soil by the decaying head."

Historical and Literary Context

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Basil of Salerno

A high-quality variety of basil used by Lisabetta to cover Lorenzo's head.

Fere

A companion or lover (used in the provided folk song translation).

Messina

A Sicilian port city where the events of the story take place.

Ottava Rima

The eight-line stanza form used by Boccaccio in his earlier poetry and later adopted by John Keats for his retelling of this story.

Pisan

A native of Pisa; Lorenzo’s city of origin.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

San Gimignano

The original home of the brothers' father.

Wight

An archaic term for a human being or person (used in the provided folk song translation).

Yare

An archaic term meaning quickly or readily.
