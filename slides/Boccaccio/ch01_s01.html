The Black Death in Florence (1348)

Symptoms

The disease was marked by *gavoccioli* (tumors in the groin or armpits) and black or livid spots on the skin. Death usually occurred within three days of the appearance of symptoms.

The Formation of the Brigata

The Seven Ladies

Pampinea (the eldest), Fiammetta, Filomena, Emilia, Lauretta, Neifile, and Elisa.

The Three Men

Pamfilo, Filostrato, and Dioneo.

The Governance

To ensure order, Pampinea proposes a daily rotating monarchy. Each "king" or "queen" has absolute authority for one day to plan the group's activities.
