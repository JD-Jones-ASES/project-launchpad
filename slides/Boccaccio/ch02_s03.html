Chapter 2, Section 3

The Tale of Ser Ciappelletto (Decameron, Day I, Novel 1)

Study Slides

Character Profiles

Character. Role

Key Traits

Ser Ciappelletto. Protagonist

A notary by profession; a liar, forger, blasphemer, and "perhaps the worst man that ever was born."

Musciatto Franzesi. Merchant/Knight

A wealthy man who employs Ciappelletto to collect debts from the violent Burgundians.

The Holy Friar. Confessor

An aged, learned, and venerable man who is completely deceived by Ciappelletto's deathbed confession.

The Two Usurers. Hosts

Florentine brothers in Burgundy who house Ciappelletto and fear the social/legal consequences of him dying unconfessed.

Panfilo. Narrator

His name means "all-loving" in Greek; he is described as the most serious and philosophical of the narrators.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: Why does Musciatto Franzesi choose Ser Ciappelletto to handle his business with the Burgundians?

Musciatto knows the Burgundians are violent and faithless. He decides he needs a man "so bad" that he can effectively oppose his own guile to theirs. Ciappelletto’s reputation for wickedness makes him the perfect agent for this task.

Q: What are the usurers afraid will happen if Ciappelletto dies without a confession?

They fear that because he is such a wicked man, he will refuse the sacraments and be denied a church burial. This would lead the local population, who already dislike the usurers' trade, to riot, loot their home, and potentially kill them.

Q: Name three "sins" Ciappelletto confesses to the friar.

He confesses to being a virgin, to fasting on bread and water but enjoying the water too much, and to once cursing his mother when he was a child.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: How does the friar react to Ciappelletto’s admission that he once spat in a church?

The friar smiles and tells him it is a "light matter," noting that even religious men spit in church all day long. Ciappelletto rebukes him, claiming the temple of God should be kept perfectly pure.

Q: What is the ultimate fate of Ciappelletto’s body after his death?

He is given a solemn vigil and a pompous funeral procession. He is interred in a marble tomb in a chapel where people light tapers, offer vows, and treat him as a saint (San Ciappelletto).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Avarice

Extreme greed for wealth; identified as one of the seven deadly sins in medieval theology.

Ciappelletto

A French diminutive meaning "Chapelet" (garland), applied to the protagonist as a nickname due to his small stature and a misunderstanding of his real name, Cepparello.

Cepparello

The protagonist's true name; a diminutive of *ceppo*, meaning "stump" or "log."

Extreme Unction

A Catholic sacrament involving the anointing of a person who is near death.

Notary

A person of professional standing (below a knight) authorized to perform legal formalities, such as drawing up contracts or deeds.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Penance

A punishment or act of devotion imposed by a priest after confession to atone for sins.

Ser

A title used for a notary or a man of professional standing.

Vernacular

The native language of a specific region (in this context, Tuscan Italian) as opposed to the formal Latin used by the Church.

Vespers

A service of evening prayer.

Vigil

A period of keeping watch over the body of the deceased, typically involving prayer, before burial.
