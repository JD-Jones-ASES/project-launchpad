Chapter 2, Section 3

The Tale of Ser Ciappelletto (Day I, Novel 1)

Study Slides

The Protagonist: Ser Ciappelletto

Professional Malice

A notary by trade, he took pride in forging legal documents and bearing false witness.

Social Chaos

He delighted in fomenting enmities and dissension among friends and family.

Moral Depravity

He was a blasphemer, a glutton, a practiced gamester who used false dice, and a man who "held all the sacraments vile things."

Violence

He was irascible and willing to commit murder or other foul crimes with alacrity.

The False Confession

Chastity

He claims to be a lifelong virgin.

Gluttony

He laments enjoying water too much after a fast or craving simple herbs.

Avarice

He claims to have shared all his earnings with the poor.

Anger

He describes his frequent anger as "righteous wrath" against those who break God’s commandments.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

In Boccaccio's Decameron, what does the name Panfilo mean in Greek?

All-loving.

Which narrator tells the first tale of the Decameron?

Panfilo.

What is the central theme introduced in the story of Ser Ciappelletto?

The gap between appearance and reality.

What is the actual Italian name of the character known as Ciappelletto?

Cepparello.

Why did the French call Cepparello "Ciappelletto"?

They mistook his name for "garland" (Chapelet) and used a diminutive for his small stature.

What was Ser Ciappelletto's professional occupation?

Notary.

Key Concepts (2/2)

For what criminal act did Ser Ciappelletto take the most pride?

Forging false documents.

How did Ser Ciappelletto feel about bearing false witness?

He did it with boundless delight, whether he was asked to or not.

What was Ciappelletto's attitude toward the sacraments of the Church?

He held them to be vile things and derided them with ribaldry.

Who was the wealthy merchant and knight who hired Ciappelletto to handle his affairs in Burgundy?

Musciatto Franzesi.

Why did Musciatto Franzesi choose Ciappelletto specifically to deal with the Burgundians?

He needed a man wicked enough to oppose his own guile to their faithless and violent nature.

Where did Ciappelletto go to recover money for Musciatto?

Burgundy.
