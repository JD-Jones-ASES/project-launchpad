Chapter 10, Section 3

Boccaccio’s The Decameron, Day IX, Novel 2

Study Slides

Character Analysis

Character. Role

Description

Elissa. Narrator

The storyteller who presents the tale as a sharp critique of those who take on the "governance and correction of others" despite their own folly.

Isabetta. The Protagonist

A noble and beautiful young nun who manages to turn a moment of public shame into a victory for personal freedom.

Madonna Usimbalda. The Abbess

A woman regarded as a pillar of holiness who is exposed as a hypocrite when her own secret lover is revealed.

The Sisters. The Antagonists

The other nuns who act as spies, driven by envy or zeal to catch Isabetta in a compromising position.

The Gallant. Isabetta’s Lover

A young man who visits Isabetta in secret and is prepared to defend her with force if necessary.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Anticlerical Satire

A genre of literature intended to mock or expose the perceived corruption, hypocrisy, or failings of the clergy and religious institutions.

Breeches

Short trousers fastened just below the knee; in this context, a garment belonging to the priest.

Chapter-house

A building or room used for business meetings and the discipline of members in a monastery or convent.

Coif

A close-fitting cap; used sarcastically by Isabetta to refer to the breeches on the abbess's head.

Grate

A partitioned screen through which nuns were permitted to speak with visitors from the outside world.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Lombardy

A region in Northern Italy where the story is set.

Points

The ties or laces used to fasten garments (specifically the breeches) in medieval clothing.

Psalter

In this context, the specific type of veil worn by the nuns in the convent.

Tale of Comic Economy

A narrative style characterized by brevity, speed, and a high-impact conclusion or "punchline."
