Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Which specific form of literature mocks the clergy and was common in the Middle Ages?

Anticlerical satire

What unique element does Boccaccio combine with satire in his contribution to anticlerical literature?

Narrative comedy

What 12th-century Latin poems are cited as early examples of anticlerical satire?

Goliardic poems

Who is the storyteller of the second tale on the ninth day of the Decameron?

Elissa

What is the central visual gag that resolves the conflict in Decameron Day IX, Novel 2?

The abbess wearing a priest's breeches on her head

The tale of the abbess and the nun is intended to expose the gap between public authority and _____.

Private behavior

Key Concepts (2/2)

In which Italian region is the 'very great repute' convent located?

Lombardy

What is the name of the young, beautiful nun from a noble family?

Isabetta

How did Isabetta first encounter her lover?

He was with one of her kinsmen at the convent grate

Who is the abbess of the convent in Lombardy?

Madonna Usimbalda

What was the general public's opinion of Madonna Usimbalda's character?

She was reputed to be a good and holy woman

Why did the sisters choose to watch Isabetta in secret rather than reporting her immediately?

To ensure there was no room for denial by catching her in the act
