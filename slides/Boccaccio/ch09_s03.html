Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: Who is Maso del Saggio and what is his role in the story?

Maso is a well-known Florentine wit whose name ironically means "Maso the Wise." He initiates the prank by convincing Calandrino of the existence of the heliotrope and the land of Bengodi.

Q: What are the purported powers of the "heliotrope" stone?

According to Maso, the heliotrope is a black stone that renders whoever carries it invisible to everyone else.

Q: Describe the mythical land of Bengodi as presented by Maso.

Bengodi is described as a land in the district of the Basques where vines are bound with sausages, a mountain is made of grated Parmesan cheese, and people spend their time making macaroni and ravioli boiled in capon's broth.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: Why did Bruno and Buffalmacco pelt Calandrino with stones at the Mugnone?

They pretended they could not see him and were "throwing" at the spot where he used to be. In reality, they were taking the opportunity to physically punish him for his greed and stupidity while maintaining the illusion that he was invisible.

Q: Why does Calandrino believe the stone's magic failed when he reached his house?

He believes the presence of a woman destroys the magical virtues of the stone. When his wife, Monna Tessa, sees and speaks to him, he assumes she is the cause of his sudden visibility.

Q: What real-world evidence suggests that Bruno and Buffalmacco were based on historical figures?

The text notes that Bruno di Giovanni and Buffalmacco (Buonamico Cristofani) were real painters in 14th-century Florence, and Giorgio Vasari recorded anecdotes about their pranks in his Lives of the Artists.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is considered the Decameron's only true recurring protagonist?

The painter Calandrino.

What does the name "Calandrino" mean in modern Italian usage?

A simpleton or easy mark.

Which character in the tale is a notorious joker whose name ironically means "the Wise"?

Maso del Saggio.

According to Maso del Saggio, what is the primary magical power of the heliotrope stone?

It makes its possessor invisible.

What is the common name for the real semi-precious stone known as the heliotrope?

Bloodstone.

Which two real-life Florentine painters are depicted as Calandrino's prankish friends?

Bruno and Buffalmacco.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which 16th-century author recorded real-life anecdotes about the painters Bruno and Buffalmacco?

Giorgio Vasari.

Where do Calandrino and his friends go to search for the heliotrope?

The Mugnone stream.

In the context of Boccaccio's tales, what is a "beffa"?

An elaborate trick or practical joke.

Where does Maso first encounter Calandrino to begin his trick?

The church of San Giovanni.

In Maso's tall tale, what is the name of the district in the land of the Basques where stones are found?

Bengodi.

What food item is used to bind vines in the fictional land of Bengodi?

Sausages.
