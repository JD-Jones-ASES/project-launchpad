Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day VIII, Novel 3

Study Slides

The Land of Bengodi and the Heliotrope

The Land of Bengodi

A mythical "district of the Basques" where vines are tied with sausages, a mountain is made of grated Parmesan cheese, and people do nothing but make macaroni and ravioli.

The Heliotrope

A stone Maso claims is found in the Mugnone stream. According to his "lapidary" discourse, the stone is nearly black and possesses the magical virtue of making its possessor invisible to others.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Who are the two painters that frequently accompany Calandrino and execute the prank?

> **Answer:** Bruno (di Giovanni) and Buffalmacco (Buonamico Cristofani).

Q: What are the specific physical characteristics of the heliotrope according to Maso del Saggio?

> **Answer:** It is described as a stone of varying sizes but nearly black in color.

Q: Why does Bruno suggest they wait until Sunday morning to search for the stone?

> **Answer:** He argues that the high sun on a workday would make white stones appear black and that there would be too many people around who might steal their idea.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does Calandrino transport the stones he finds in the Mugnone?

> **Answer:** He gathers them in the folds of his tunic, his gown (fastened by a leathern girdle), and his cloak.

Q: What is Calandrino’s plan for the wealth he expects to acquire using the stone?

> **Answer:** He intends to go to the moneychangers and help himself to groats and florins since no one will be able to see him.

Q: Why do the customs officers at the Porta a San Gallo let Calandrino pass without challenge?

> **Answer:** They had been "primed" (forewarned) by Bruno and Buffalmacco to let him pass as part of the joke.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Whom does Calandrino blame for the "miscarriage of his enterprise"?

> **Answer:** His wife, Monna Tessa, because he believes that "all things... lose their virtue in the presence of a woman."
