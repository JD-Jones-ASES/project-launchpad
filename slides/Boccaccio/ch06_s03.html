Chapter 6, Section 3

The Tale of Nastagio degli Onesti (Decameron, V, 8)

Study Slides

The Motif of the Infernal Hunt

The Hunter

Messer Guido degli Anastagi, a knight who committed suicide because of the cruelty of his beloved.

The Hunted

A naked damsel who rejected Guido’s love and exulted in his death.

The Punishment

Every Friday, Guido pursues the woman, kills her with the rapier he used to kill himself, rips out her "hard and cold heart," and feeds it to his mastiffs. She then rises to be hunted again.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the young nobleman from Ravenna who is the protagonist of the eighth tale of the fifth day?

Nastagio degli Onesti

From which two relatives did Nastagio degli Onesti inherit his immense wealth?

His father and one of his uncles

Who is the father of the damsel Nastagio falls in love with?

Messer Paolo Traversaro

What two factors are suggested as the causes for the Traversaro daughter’s haughty disdain for Nastagio?

Her uncommon beauty and her noble lineage

Why do Nastagio's kinsfolk and friends urge him to leave Ravenna for a time?

To cool his love for the damsel and reduce his excessive spending

To what specific location, three miles from Ravenna, does Nastagio move his camp?

Chiassi

Key Concepts (2/2)

In what month does the fine weather and season begin when Nastagio wanders into the pinewood?

May

At what specific time of day does Nastagio witness the supernatural vision in the forest?

The fifth hour

What kind of animals are seen biting and tracking the naked damsel in Nastagio's vision?

Two great and fierce mastiffs

Describe the horse ridden by the knight who pursues the naked damsel.

A black horse

What weapon does the ghostly knight carry in his hand during the hunt?

A rapier

What does Nastagio initially use to attempt to defend the damsel from the knight and dogs?

A branch of a tree
