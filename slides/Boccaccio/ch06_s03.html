Chapter 6, Section 3

The Tale of Nastagio degli Onesti (Decameron, Day V, Novel 8)

Key Themes and Motifs

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Who is the protagonist of the tale, and what is his primary conflict at the start of the story?

Nastagio degli Onesti is a wealthy nobleman from Ravenna. His primary conflict is his unrequited love for the daughter of Messer Paolo Traversaro, whose rejection of him is so harsh that he frequently contemplates suicide.

Q: Why did Nastagio’s friends and kinsfolk advise him to leave Ravenna?

They feared he would waste both his physical health and his vast inheritance ("substance") through his inordinate spending and emotional suffering while pursuing his lady.

Q: Describe the vision Nastagio witnesses in the pine forest of Chiassi.

He sees a naked woman running through the brambles, weeping and being bitten by two great mastiffs. Following her is a knight on a black horse, Messer Guido degli Anastagi, who threatens her with a rapier.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: What is the specific nature of the punishment inflicted upon the ghostly woman?

Every Friday, the knight catches her, kills her with the rapier he used to kill himself, rips open her back to remove her "hard and cold heart," and feeds it to his dogs. She then rises as if she had not died and the pursuit begins again.

Q: How long is the knight condemned to pursue the lady?

He is condemned to pursue her for as many years as the number of months she showed herself harsh to him.

Q: How does Nastagio use the "infernal hunt" to his own advantage?

He invites his beloved and her family to a banquet in the exact spot in the forest where the hunt occurs. He times the event for a Friday so they will witness the slaughter and understand the "divine punishment" for cruelty in love.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: What is the final outcome of the story for the women of Ravenna?

The lady marries Nastagio out of fear, and the tale concludes by stating that the women of Ravenna became "much more compliant with men's desires" after witnessing or hearing of the event.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Anon

A term meaning soon, shortly, or at once.

Chiassi

A location about three miles from Ravenna where Nastagio pitched his camp and witnessed the vision.

Divine Justice

The concept that God or a higher power delivers specific punishments for sins; in this tale, it is used to justify the violent "infernal hunt."

Infernal Hunt

A widespread medieval motif involving a ghostly huntsman pursuing a sinner (often a woman) through a forest for eternity.

Messer

A title of respect for a gentleman or nobleman (e.g., Messer Paolo Traversaro, Messer Guido degli Anastagi).

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pineta

The celebrated pine forests south of Ravenna, famous in classical and medieval literature.

Rapier

A thin, light, sharp-pointed sword used by the knight to kill the woman in the vision.

Ravenna

An ancient city on Italy’s Adriatic coast, known for its Byzantine mosaics and as the final resting place of Dante.

Romagna

The region in Italy where the city of Ravenna is located.
