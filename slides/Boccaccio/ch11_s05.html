Study Slides

Friendship and Reciprocity

The First Movement

Torello hosts Saladin (disguised as a merchant) in Pavia with unmatched grace, using a ruse to ensure the strangers accept his hospitality.

The Second Movement

Years later, Saladin recognizes a captured Torello in Alexandria. He repays the debt of honor by treating Torello not as a prisoner, but as an equal lord, eventually facilitating his magical return...

Quiz Question 1

In the first movement of the tale, what strategy does Messer Torello use to ensure the strangers (Saladin and his retinue) accept his hospitality?

A. He intercepts them with an armed escort and insists they stay for safety.

B. He instructs a servant to guide them to his estate via devious roads while they believe they are heading to Pavia.

C. He reveals his true identity as a high-ranking official and commands their presence at dinner.

D. He offers them a significant discount on their supposed merchandise if they lodge with him.

Hint: Consider the 'ruse' Saladin perceives when he realizes they have been brought to a private home instead of a public inn.

Quiz Question 2

What specific physical trait or action first causes Saladin to suspect that the 'Christian' falconer in Alexandria is actually Messer Torello?

A. A distinctive scar on Torello's hand that Saladin noticed in Pavia.

B. Torello's unique method of training hawks that Saladin had witnessed at the Ticino ford.

C. The way Torello's mouth shaped itself when he smiled during a conversation.

D. Torello's refusal to eat pork, which Saladin remembered as a quirk of his host.

Hint: Think about a subtle, involuntary facial expression that Saladin observed during their pleasant discourse in Italy.

Quiz Question 3

What is the primary reason the people of Pavia firmly believed Messer Torello d'Istria had died during the Crusade?

A. His wife received a signed death certificate from the Soldan's administration.

B. A different knight with a similar name was killed and buried in the sight of other Christians.

C. Saladin intentionally sent false reports of his death to prevent Torello from returning home.

D. The ship Torello was traveling on was confirmed lost at sea with no survivors.

Hint: Reflect on how a case of mistaken identity among the crusading host could lead to a false rumor.

Quiz Question 4

Messer Torello's wife, Adalieta, agrees to remarry only after a specific timeframe has passed. What is this duration?

A. Seven years and seven days.

B. One year, one month, and one day.

C. Three years.

D. Five hundred days.

Hint: Look for a specific temporal formula Torello gives to his wife to ensure her safety from persistent suitors while he is away.

Quiz Question 5

Which supernatural or 'romance' element does Boccaccio employ to facilitate the climax of the story?

A. A divine intervention by an angel who carries Torello across the sea.

B. A magical ring that allows Torello to become invisible and enter the wedding feast.

C. The transport of Torello in a single night from Alexandria to Pavia on a magical bed.

D. A prophetic dream sent to Adalieta warning her not to marry the second husband.

Hint: Consider the method of travel used by Torello to return home in time for the deadline.

Quiz Question 6

How does Messer Torello finally reveal his identity to his wife during the wedding banquet?

A. He removes his turban and reveals a birthmark on his forehead.

B. He sings a song that only he and his wife knew from their youth.

C. He drops the ring she gave him into a wine cup that she then drinks from.

D. He presents the crown given to him by Saladin as proof of his travels.

Hint: Focus on the ritual involving the exchange of a cup and a token of their original parting.
