Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 5, Section 3

The Tragedy of Ghismonda and Guiscardo (Decameron, Day IV, Novel 1)

Study Slides

Central Themes

True Nobility (Virtue vs. Birth)

A major intellectual pillar of the story is the argument that nobility is determined by a person's character and actions ("virtue") rather than their social class or lineage ("blood").

The Natural Force of Love

Ghismonda argues that love is an irresistible natural power. To deny it is to do violence to human nature.

Paternal Failure

Medieval audiences viewed Tancred’s refusal to remarry his daughter as a violation of his duty. His "humane" nature is eclipsed by a possessive devotion that leads to tragedy.

Renaissance Humanism

Ghismonda’s speech anticipates humanist ideals by prioritizing individual merit and the human experience over inherited status.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Why did Tancred refuse to let Ghismonda remarry after her first husband died?

He loved her with a "possessive devotion" and was reluctant to part with her, choosing to keep her near him for his own satisfaction rather than fulfilling his paternal duty.

Q: How did Ghismonda first communicate her instructions for a secret meeting to Guiscardo?

She wrote a letter and hid it inside a cane, which she gave to Guiscardo with the instructions to use it as a bellows for his fire.

Q: What physical location served as the meeting place for the lovers?

A disused grotto hewn into the rock, accessible via a secret stairway from Ghismonda’s room and an exterior orifice choked with brambles.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How did Tancred discover the affair?

He entered Ghismonda's room to speak with her while she was in the garden. He fell asleep on a divan behind bed curtains and awoke to see Ghismonda and Guiscardo together.

Q: What was Guiscardo’s sole response when Tancred accused him of dishonoring the prince?

He stated, "Love is more potent than either, you or I."

Q: What was Tancred’s primary grievance regarding Ghismonda's choice of lover, aside from the secrecy?

He was outraged that she chose a man of "lowest condition" and "humble birth" rather than a nobleman.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: How did Ghismonda prepare for the possibility of her lover's death?

She distilled a poisonous water from herbs and roots to have ready if Tancred took a harsh course of action.

Q: What was the "goodly present" Tancred sent to Ghismonda?

Guiscardo’s heart, placed inside a large, beautiful golden cup.

Q: What was Ghismonda’s final request to her father?

She requested that she be buried side by side with Guiscardo in a common tomb so they could be united in death as they were not allowed to be in life.
