Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 8, Section 3

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day VII, Novel 2

Study Slides

The Central Conflict

The Opportunity

The husband leaves early for work, allowing Peronella to host her lover, Giannello Sirignario.

The Crisis

The husband returns unexpectedly early, forcing Peronella to hide Giannello in a large "tun" (a large jar or vat).

The Resolution

Through verbal brilliance and audacity, Peronella convinces her husband that Giannello is a customer who has agreed to buy the jar for a higher price than the husband had previously arranged with a...

Class Consciousness and Economic Reality

The Jar as Commodity

The jar is not just a prop; it is a valuable household asset.

The Motivation for Adultery

The text suggests Peronella’s infidelity is partially motivated by her husband’s inability to provide adequate financial support.

The Linguistic Gap

There is a distinct contrast between Peronella’s "high verbal style" and the "low material reality" of her life as a spinner and wife of a mason.

Character Analysis

Character. Role

Key Traits

Peronella. The Wife

Quick-witted, audacious, verbally brilliant, and manipulative.

The Husband. The Mason

Poor, hardworking, pious (thanking God for his "honest" wife), and easily deceived.

Giannello Sirignario. The Lover

A "gallant" of the city; opportunistic and willing to follow Peronella’s lead.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Avorio

The name of the street in Naples where Peronella and her husband lived.

Beffa

A comic trick or prank, often involving the clever deception of a fool or a spouse.

Betimes

Early; in good time; promptly.

Filostrato

The narrator of this specific tale within the *Decameron*.

Gigliats

The currency mentioned in the story (e.g., the jar was sold for seven gigliats).

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Lees

The sediment or dregs found at the bottom of a container like a wine jar.

San Galeone

The saint whose feast day serves as the husband’s excuse for returning home early.

Tun

A large cask or jar used for storage; in this tale, it is the primary comic prop.
