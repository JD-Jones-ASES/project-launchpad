Chapter 8, Section 3

Boccaccio’s Decameron – Day VII, Novel 2 (The Tale of Peronella)

Study Slides

The Concept of the Beffa

Improvisation

Peronella must react with "breathtaking speed" when her husband knocks.

Verbal Brilliance

She does not just hide her lover; she uses language to put her husband on the defensive.

The Triumphant Performance

By the end of the story, the husband is not only deceived but is actually grateful to his wife for her "superior" business skills.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: What is the husband’s profession, and how does Peronella contribute to the household income?

The husband is a poor mason. Peronella earns money by spinning.

Q: Why does the husband return home earlier than expected on the day of the story?

He returns early because it is the feast of San Galeone, which is a holiday.

Q: What is the name of Peronella’s lover?

His name is Giannello Sirignario.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does Peronella immediately react when her husband knocks at the door?

She instructs Giannello to hide in a large tun (jar) and then goes to the door to berate her husband, accusing him of being lazy and not providing for the family.

Q: For what price did the husband claim to have sold the tun, and what price did Peronella claim she had secured?

The husband claimed to have sold it for five gigliats. Peronella claimed she had already sold it to a man for seven gigliats.

Q: What "defect" does Giannello pretend to find in the tun to justify his presence inside it?

He claims the tun is sound but "encrusted" with dry lees (sediment) and refuses to buy it unless it is scoured clean.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Describe the husband's actions while Giannello and Peronella are together at the end of the tale.

The husband strips to his vest, takes a light and a rasp, and climbs inside the jar to scrape the lees off the sides.

Q: How does Peronella participate in the "scouring" of the jar?

She thrusts her head and arm into the vent of the tun to "direct and admonish" her husband, telling him exactly where to scrape.

Who is the narrator of the second tale of Day VII in the Decameron?

Filostrato

In which city is the story of Peronella and the mason set?

Naples

What is the specific occupation of Peronella's husband?

A mason

How does Peronella supplement the household's meager income?

By spinning

What is the name of Peronella's lover?

Giannello Sirignario

What is the name of the street where Peronella and her husband live?

Avorio

Key Concepts (2/2)

At what time of day do Peronella and Giannello usually meet?

In the morning after the husband leaves for work

Why did Peronella's husband return home early on the morning of the story?

It was the feast of San Galeone, a holiday.

What was the husband's reaction to finding his door locked upon his early return?

He praised God for having such a good and honest wife.

Where does Peronella hide Giannello when her husband knocks at the door?

Inside a large tun (jar)

To what does Filostrato hope the stories of women's tricks will incite the ladies?

To publish them so that men may be aware of their ladies' wit.

What rhetorical strategy does Peronella use immediately after letting her husband into the house?

She goes on the attack, scolding him for being idle and poor.
