Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Brigata

The company of storytellers (seven women and three men) who have fled the plague.

Eloquenza

The art of speaking well; intelligence made visible through language.

Motto

A witty, pointed remark or apt retort delivered at the exactly right moment to avoid a negative outcome.

Seneschal

An official in a medieval or Renaissance household in charge of domestic arrangements and the supervision of servants.

Varlet

A servant or attendant; often used contemptuously to mean a rogue or knave.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Vonted Order

The usual or customary order in which the brigata took their places by the fountain.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the central theme decreed by Queen Elissa for the stories of Day VI?

Tales of individuals who used an apt retort to avoid loss, danger, or scorn.

What is the Italian term for a witty or pointed remark delivered at the right moment?

Motto.

Which member of the brigata serves as the queen of Day VI?

Elissa.

In Roman mythology and the Decameron, Elissa is another name for which legendary figure?

Dido, the queen of Carthage.

How do the tales of Day VI differ in length compared to other days in the Decameron?

They are the briefest tales in the collection.

What does Boccaccio celebrate through the focus on language in Day VI?

The power of a well-chosen word and perfectly timed remarks.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Define the Italian literary concept of 'eloquenza' as described in the text.

The art of speaking well, viewed as a form of visible intelligence.

Why is the first tale of Day VI (Madonna Oretta's story) significant to the work's overall theme?

It serves as Boccaccio's meditation on the craft and narrated art of storytelling itself.

What does Madonna Oretta's gentle criticism of a knight's narration represent in Day VI?

A statement on what makes a good story versus what ruins one.

What activities did the brigata engage in on the morning of Day VI before the heat of the sun?

They sauntered across the dewy mead, canvassed previous stories, and laughed at past incidents.

Which two characters sang about Troilus and Cressida while the others slept or played games?

Dioneo and Lauretta.

Where did the company gather for the storytelling session on Day VI?

By the fountain.
