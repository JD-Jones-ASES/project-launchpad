Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Bed-trick

A literary device involving a sexual encounter between two people where one or both are mistaken about the identity of their partner.

Comport

To behave or conduct oneself in a specific manner (e.g., Niccolosa studied how to comport herself debonairly).

Debonairly

In a cheerful, confident, and stylish manner.

Discreet

Careful and circumspect in one's speech or actions, especially in order to avoid causing offense or to gain an advantage.

Fabliaux

A metrical tale, typically a short ribald one, of a type found in early French poetry.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Farce

A comic dramatic work using buffoonery and horseplay and typically including crude characterization and ludicrously improbable situations.

Gallant

A dashing, fashionable young man; often used to describe Pinuccio.

Plain of Mugnone

The geographical setting of the inn, located near Florence.

Spatial Choreography

The planned movement of characters within a defined physical space to achieve a specific narrative or comedic effect.

Valises

Small traveling bags or suitcases.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In literature, what is the 'bed-trick' plot device?

A comic or dramatic scenario based on darkness, confusion, and mistaken identity in a bedroom.

According to the text, which book of the Bible contains an early example of the bed-trick involving Jacob and Leah?

Genesis

Where is the provincial inn in Panfilo's tale located?

The plain of Mugnone (near Forlì).

What is the name of the young Florentine gentleman who is in love with the host's daughter?

Pinuccio

Who is the 'trusty comrade' that accompanies Pinuccio to the inn?

Adriano

What realistic detail about medieval inns explains why the hosts and guests slept in the same room?

Privacy was a luxury reserved only for the wealthy.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How many beds were located in the host's single small bedroom?

Three

What is the name of the host's daughter?

Niccolosa

How old is Niccolosa in the story?

Fifteen or sixteen years old.

Why did the host only give lodging to a few people he knew?

He was in poor circumstances and had a very small house.

What did Pinuccio and Adriano put in their valises to look like legitimate travelers?

Straw (or similar stuffing).

From which direction did the two friends pretend to be arriving to deceive the host?

Romagna
