Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day VIII Introduction

Study Slides

The Broadening Scope of the Beffa

• Women practicing tricks on their husbands.

• Husbands practicing tricks on their wives.

• Tricks played between men.

• Men practicing tricks on women (a significant addition).

Ethical Shifts: Leggera vs. Crudele

Beffa leggera (Light trick)

Harmless pranks, often lighthearted, where the primary consequence is a bruised ego.

Beffa crudele (Cruel trick)

Pranks that escalate into something genuinely cruel, where cleverness is utilized as a weapon rather than a game.

Key Figures and Characters

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

An Italian term for a trick or a prank; the central thematic device of Day VIII.

Beffa Crudele

A "cruel trick" where cleverness becomes a harmful weapon.

Beffa Leggera

A "light trick" that is harmless or playful in nature.

Half Tierce

A time of day (approximately mid-morning) when the company attended Divine service.

Meridian

Midday; the narrators gathered to begin storytelling once the sun had passed this point.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

The Mead

A meadow where the company walked in the morning dew.

Vasari’s "Lives of the Artists"

A historical text (1550) that confirms the real-life existence of the characters Bruno and Buffalmacco.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the appointed queen for the eighth day of storytelling in The Decameron?

Lauretta.

What is the primary theme of the eighth day as established by Lauretta?

Tricks of every kind played between men and women.

How does the scope of trickery in Day VIII differ from that of Day VII?

Day VII focused only on wives tricking husbands, while Day VIII includes all gender combinations.

In Day VIII, trickery is no longer confined to the ______.

Marriage bed.

What narrative effect is created by the shift from marital tricks to universal tricks in Day VIII?

The symmetry of Day VII is deliberately broken.

How does the tone of the tales in Day VIII compare to the tone of Day VII?

Day VIII introduces darker themes and genuine cruelty, whereas Day VII was largely lighthearted.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was typically the worst consequence for a victim in the tales of Day VII?

A bruised ego.

When a trick escalates beyond a harmless prank in Day VIII, cleverness is described as a ______.

Weapon.

Term: Beffa leggera

Definition: A light, harmless trick in the Italian critical tradition.

Term: Beffa crudele

Definition: A cruel trick that goes beyond a harmless prank into something genuinely harmful.

How does Boccaccio use the juxtaposition of light and cruel tricks in Day VIII?

To force the reader to confront the ethical limits of trickery.

Who is the recurring gullible painter introduced as a central dupe in Day VIII?

Calandrino.
