Historical Realism and Archetypes

Historical Basis

Bruno and Buffalmacco were real Florentine painters mentioned in Vasari’s *Lives of the Artists* (1550). Calandrino (Nozzo di Perino) was also a historical figure.

Literary Transformation

Boccaccio transforms these real-world acquaintances into comic archetypes: the "witty pranksters" and the "eternal dupe."

Narrative Effect

Because these characters were recognizable Florentines to Boccaccio’s contemporary audience, their tales possess the texture of urban gossip elevated to the level of art.
