Summary of Day III Tales

Novel. Primary Characters

Key Plot Elements

III.1. Masetto

Masetto pretends to be deaf-mute to work at a convent; he provides the nuns with what they secretly desire.

III.2. A Clever Groom

A servant (groom) disguises himself as King Agilulf to visit the Queen; he uses quick thinking to escape detection.

III.3. A Lady and a Friar

A woman uses an unsuspecting friar as a go-between to facilitate an affair with her lover.

III.4. Dom Felice and Fra Puccio

A monk tricks a devout man into a motionless penance so the monk can visit the man's wife.

III.5. Zima

Zima convinces a man to let him speak to his silent wife, using a persuasive speech to win her over.

III.6. Ricciardo and Catella

Ricciardo tricks Catella into a darkened bathhouse by claiming her husband is unfaithful.

III.7. Tedaldo

Tedaldo returns to Florence in disguise to save an innocent man and reclaim his lover.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Bed-trick

A plot device involving a sexual encounter in the dark where one character is deceived regarding the identity of their partner.

Beffa

A trick or practical joke, often central to the tales involving clerical dupes or unfaithful spouses.

Double Entendre

A word or phrase open to two interpretations, one of which is usually risqué (prominent in III.10).

Fortuna

The personification of luck or chance that dictates the rise and fall of human affairs.

Ingegno

The Italian term for intelligence, wit, resourcefulness, or ingenuity; the central virtue celebrated in Day III.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Purgatory

In medieval Catholic theology, a state after death for the purification of souls; used as a comedic setting in Novel III.8.
