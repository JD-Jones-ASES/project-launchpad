Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the narrator of the seventh tale of Day II in the Decameron?

Panfilo

What is the overarching theme of Day II of the Decameron?

Fortune's Reversals

In medieval usage, as seen in Boccaccio's geography, to what region did "Babylon" usually refer?

Cairo or Egypt generally

Who is the beautiful daughter of the Sultan of Babylon in the seventh tale of Day II?

Alatiel

To which ruler was Alatiel initially sent by sea to be married?

The King of Algarve

Where is the region of Algarve located?

The southernmost part of modern Portugal

Key Concepts (2/2)

In the fourteenth century, what was the religious status of the Algarve territory?

It was a Muslim-ruled territory.

What is the total number of men Alatiel passes through before being restored to her father?

Nine

According to the commentary, the tale is a meditation on what concept at its most extreme?

Fortuna (Fortune)

What specific character trait does Alatiel exhibit throughout the story as a reflection of Boccaccio's exploration of fortune?

Passivity (things happen to her, not through her)

What is the "final irony" regarding Alatiel's reputation when she is returned to her husband?

She is presented as an untouched virgin.

How does Boccaccio handle sexual situations in this specific tale?

Through narrative irony rather than explicit description
