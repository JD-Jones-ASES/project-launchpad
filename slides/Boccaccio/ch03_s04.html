Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: What is the original purpose of Alatiel’s voyage?

She is being sent by her father, the Sultan of Babylon (Egypt), to marry the King of Algarve to solidify a dynastic alliance.

Q: How does Pericone da Visalgo eventually overcome Alatiel’s resistance to his advances?

He uses "Bacchus in the service of Venus" by plying her with blended wines during a lavish supper. Unaccustomed to wine, Alatiel loses her inhibitions and willingly goes to bed with him.

Q: Why does the Duke of Athens murder the Prince of the Morea?

The Duke is "smitten with a desire to see her" and, upon witnessing her beauty, decides to "despoil the Prince of his felicity" and take Alatiel for himself.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: How does Alatiel’s ability to communicate change throughout the story?

For much of the tale, she is a "deaf mute" to her captors because of the language barrier. This isolation only ends when she meets Antioco, who knows her language, and later Antigono, who was in her father's service.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Algarve

A region in southern Portugal, historically under Muslim rule during the 14th century.

Babylon

In medieval literary usage, a reference to Cairo or the Sultanate of Egypt.

Consort

A companion or spouse; usually refers to the partner of a reigning monarch.

Entreat

To ask earnestly; to beg or implore.

Fortuna

The personification of luck or fate, often depicted as a wheel that raises some and lowers others.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Klarenza

A location in the Morea (modern Peloponnese, Greece) where the Prince resided.

Morea

The medieval name for the Peloponnese peninsula in Greece.

Saracen

A term used in the Middle Ages to describe Muslims or people from the Middle East/North Africa.

Soldan

A medieval variant of the title "Sultan."

Wroth

Angry, furious, or full of wrath.
