Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: Who narrates the ninth tale of Day I?

> Answer: The tale is told by Elissa, who is the last to speak following the story told by Lauretta.

Q: Where is the lady in the story from, and what was the historical status of that region?

> Answer: She is from Gascony, a region in southwestern France that was part of the English-controlled Duchy of Aquitaine during the fourteenth century.

Q: What was the reputation of the King of Cyprus at the beginning of the tale?

> Answer: He was considered notoriously indolent, spiritless, and "faineant." He was so "spiritless" that he neglected to avenge affronts to others and tamely endured those offered to himself.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: Why did the lady decide to approach the king if she knew he would not grant her redress?

> Answer: She despaired of redress but sought an "alleviation of her grief" by making the king "sensible of his baseness."

Q: What was the long-term effect of the lady's words on the king's governance?

> Answer: The king moved from being apathetic to meeting out "rigorous justice" to all who offended the "majesty of his crown."

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the narrator of the ninth tale told on the first day of the Decameron?

Elissa

What is the primary geographical setting where the lady of Gascony seeks justice?

Cyprus

In the context of the Decameron, what is a 'motto'?

A pointed, witty remark used as a social weapon to shame, correct, or delight.

What medieval literary genre is characterized by providing instruction to rulers on proper conduct?

Speculum principis (or "mirror for princes")

What was the political status of the region of Gascony during the fourteenth century?

It was part of the English-controlled Duchy of Aquitaine.

According to the text, what is the effect of setting tales in regions like Gascony and Cyprus?

It broadens the geographical reach of the Decameron beyond Italy.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What specific personality trait defines the King of Cyprus at the beginning of the tale?

He was notoriously indolent (spiritless and faineant).

Why did the King of Cyprus fail to avenge affronts put upon others?

He was so spiritless that he even endured affronts offered to himself with reprehensible tameness.

How did the people of Cyprus use the king's passivity to their advantage?

They would vex or mortify him as a way to vent their own ill-humour.

What historical event serves as the temporal backdrop for the ninth tale of Day I?

The conquest of the Holy Land by Godfrey de Bouillon.

Where was the lady of Gascony returning from when she was outraged by ruffians in Cyprus?

A pilgrimage to the Holy Sepulchre in Jerusalem.

Instead of seeking redress, what did the lady tell the king she wished to learn from him?

How he patiently suffered the wrongs done to him so she could learn to endure her own.
