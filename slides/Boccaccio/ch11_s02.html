Study Slides

Magnificenza (Magnanimity)

Classical Roots

The concept is rooted in Aristotle’s Nicomachean Ethics, where megalopsychia (magnanimity) is described as the "crown virtue" of a great-souled individual.

Definition

In the context of Day X, it refers to a greatness of soul expressed through generous, active, and selfless deeds.

Application

The tales demonstrate this virtue through generosity in love, sacrifices made between enemies, and extreme patience.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Who is the reigning king of Day the Tenth?

> Answer: Panfilo.

Q: What is the specific theme assigned to the stories of Day X?

> Answer: Tales of those who have performed some generous or magnificent deed, whether in matters of love or otherwise.

Q: According to the text, what does the word "magnificenza" literally mean in its Latin origin?

> Answer: "Great-doing" (magnificentia).

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does the structural movement of the Decameron reflect a change in social conditions?

> Answer: It mirrors a movement from the disorder and plague of the early days toward courtesy and virtue by the final day.

Q: Which classical philosopher’s work influenced Boccaccio’s concept of the "great-souled person"?

> Answer: Aristotle (Nicomachean Ethics).

Q: What was the brigata discussing during their slow-paced walk at the start of Day X?

> Answer: They were discussing their future way of life.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Which storyteller provides the final tale of the Decameron, and what is the subject?

> Answer: Dioneo tells the final tale, which is the story of Patient Griselda.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Which day is considered the "moral summit" and grand finale of the Decameron?

Day the Tenth

What does the deliberate structural arc of the Decameron mirror?

The movement from plague and disorder to courtesy and virtue.

Who serves as the king of the Tenth Day in the Decameron?

Panfilo

What specific theme did Panfilo choose for the stories of Day X?

Generous or magnificent deeds.

Term: Magnificenza

Definition: Greatness of soul that expresses itself in generous action.

The term "magnificenza" is derived from the Latin magnificentia, which literally means _____.

great-doing

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to Aristotle's Nicomachean Ethics, what is the "crown virtue" of the great-souled person?

Magnanimity (megalopsychia).

How does the focus of Day X differ from the themes of earlier days like wit and fortune?

It celebrates virtue in its most active and selfless form.

Besides love, what other relationships are used to demonstrate generosity in Day X?

Generosity between enemies.

Which story is noted as the most discussed and debated tale in the entire Decameron?

The story of Patient Griselda.

Who is the narrator of the story of Patient Griselda?

Dioneo

How does Boccaccio prevent the tone of Day X from being purely solemn?

By including warm, humorous tales and maintaining characteristic irony.
