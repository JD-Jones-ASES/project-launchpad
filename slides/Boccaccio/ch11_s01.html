Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 11, Section 1

Day X of the Decameron – Magnanimity and Virtue

Study Slides

The "Noble Pagan"

Theological Implication

If moral excellence is possible outside of the Christian faith, it raises questions about the unique necessity of Christianity.

Historical Context

Boccaccio wrote this less than a century after the Crusades, making his admiring portrayal of Saladin a significant moral and political statement.

The Arc of the Decameron: From I.1 to X.10

The Starting Pole (I.1)

Ser Ciappelletto, a man defined by deception and blasphemy.

The Ending Pole (X.10)

Patient Griselda, a figure of superhuman endurance and virtue.

The Thematic Shift

The movement transitions from the survival tools of wit and *ingegno* (cleverness) to the higher ethical standards of selfless generosity.

Summary of Day X Tales

Tale. Primary Characters

Summary of Magnanimous Act

X.1. Ruggieri & King of Spain

The King rewards Ruggieri’s faithful service after a test involving coffers of earth and gold, illustrating the duty of rulers to recognize merit.

X.2. Ghino di Tacco & the Abbot

A bandit heals his enemy (the Abbot) through a strict diet and is subsequently rewarded with advocacy at the papal court.

X.3. Nathan & Mithridanes

Nathan offers his own life to his would-be assassin, Mithridanes, to avoid withholding generosity.

X.4. Messer Gentil

Gentil revives a woman buried alive and returns her and her child to her husband, choosing renunciation over possession.

X.5. Madonna Dianora

A "chain of generosity" where a husband, a suitor, and a magician all release one another from obligations and debts.

X.6. King Charles I

An aged king masters his desire for two young women, arranging noble marriages for them instead of abusing his power.

X.7. King Peter of Aragon

The King treats a poor girl’s unrequited love with courtesy and arranges a suitable marriage for her.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

A tale of trickery or a practical joke, common in the earlier days of the *Decameron*.

Ingegno

Human wit or cleverness; the primary tool characters use to navigate fortune in the work’s middle sections.

Magnificenza

Magnanimity; the "crown virtue" of Day X involving selfless generosity and sacrifice.

Megalopsychia

The Greek (Aristotelian) term for the "great-souled" person.

Noble Pagan

A literary figure who exhibits genuine moral excellence despite being outside the Christian faith.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Patient Griselda

The protagonist of X.10, known for her superhuman endurance and obedience under extreme trial.

The Brigata

The group of ten young people who flee the plague in Florence to tell stories.
