Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Beffa

A tale of a trick or practical joke, common in the earlier days of the Decameron.

Chichevache

A satirical medieval creature that consumes patient wives; used by Chaucer’s Clerk to mock the rarity of Griselda-like obedience.

De Amicitia

Cicero's "On Friendship," a classical text that influenced Boccaccio's portrayal of the bond between Titus and Gisippus in Tale X.8.

Exemplum

A moralizing story used to illustrate a specific virtue or lesson.

Ingegno

Human wit or cleverness; a major theme in the early days of the Decameron.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Magnificenza

Magnanimity; the core virtue of Day X, involving greatness of soul and selfless generosity.

Motto

A sharp, witty verbal retort; a key theme in Day VI.

Noble Pagan / Virtuous Infidel

A non-Christian character in medieval literature recognized for their genuine moral excellence.

The Clerk's Tale

The section of Geoffrey Chaucer’s Canterbury Tales that retells the story of Griselda, influenced by Petrarch’s Latin translation.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the governing theme or virtue of Day X in the Decameron?

Magnificenza (Magnanimity).

The Aristotelian term for the "crown virtue" of the great-souled person is _____.

Megalopsychia

Which character in Day I serves as the polar opposite to Griselda's virtue in Day X?

Ser Ciappelletto.

To what famous work is the movement of the Decameron from I.1 to X.10 often compared?

Dante's Divine Comedy.

How does Boccaccio's "paradise" in Day X differ from Dante's vision?

It is grounded in human virtue rather than divine grace.

What defines the virtue of 'Magnificenza' according to the text?

Selfless generosity and greatness of soul that requires sacrifice.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does the emotional register of Day X differ from earlier days focused on wit or trickery?

Laughter gives way to admiration and wonder.

In Novel X.5, what does the husband prioritize over his own honor?

His wife's word (or promise) to her suitor.

Which non-Christian figure in X.9 is portrayed as a peer to Christian moral standards?

Saladin.

In the medieval allegorical reading of Griselda, what does her husband Gualtieri represent?

Divine providence (God) testing the human soul.

Who translated the Griselda story into Latin, presenting it as a moral exemplum?

Petrarch.

What was the primary source Chaucer used for the Clerk's Tale?

Petrarch's Latin translation of the Griselda story.
