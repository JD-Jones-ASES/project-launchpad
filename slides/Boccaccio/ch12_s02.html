Chapter 12, Section 2

Giovanni Boccaccio and the Literary Landscape of the Decameron

Study Slides

Key Literary Works by Giovanni Boccaccio

Geoffrey Chaucer: *The Canterbury Tales*

The General Prologue

Features a "brigata" or gallery of pilgrims similar to Boccaccio’s, though Chaucer’s group is more socially diverse.

The Clerk’s Tale

A retelling of Boccaccio’s story of Patient Griselda, featuring an ironic "envoy."

The Franklin’s Tale

Echoes the themes of competing generosity found in *Decameron* X.5.

The Pardoner’s Prologue and Tale

Explores religious hypocrisy, comparable to the character of Ser Ciappelletto.

Historical Background

The Black Death (1347–1351)

The plague pandemic provides the framing for the *Decameron*. It had devastating social and psychological effects.

Mercantile Society

Boccaccio’s Florence was a hub of trade and urban life, moving away from medieval feudal certainties.

Political Context

The era was marked by the Hundred Years' War, the Great Schism, and the rise of Italian city-states.

Scholarly Approaches

The Ludic Perspective

Giuseppe Mazzotta views the work as a meditation on "play" and fiction's role in the social order.

Literary Self-Consciousness

Millicent Marcus explores how the *Decameron* reflects on its own nature as a work of fiction and the act of storytelling.

Sacred vs. Profane

Robert Hollander argues there is a moral seriousness and a tension between sacred and profane love beneath the comic surface.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Brigata

The group of storytellers in the *Decameron* (and by extension, the pilgrims in *The Canterbury Tales*).

Canzoniere

Francesco Petrarch’s collection of sonnets, representing the lyric tradition of idealized love.

De casibus virorum illustrium

Boccaccio’s work *The Fates of Illustrious Men*, focusing on the fall of great figures.

De mulieribus claris

Boccaccio’s *On Famous Women*, the first Western biographical collection devoted to women.

Genealogia deorum gentilium

Boccaccio’s encyclopedia of classical mythology.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Ludic

Relating to play; a term used by scholars like Mazzotta to describe the *Decameron* as a meditation on fiction and play.

Mercantile Society

The urban, trade-based social structure of 14th-century Florence portrayed in Boccaccio’s stories.

The Great Mortality

Another term for the Black Death pandemic of 1347–1351.

Trattatello in laude di Dante

Boccaccio's biography of Dante, known as *The Life of Dante*.
