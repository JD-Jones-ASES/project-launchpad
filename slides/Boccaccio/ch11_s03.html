Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 11, Section 3

Patient Griselda (The Decameron, Day X, Novel 10)

Study Slides

The Conditions of Marriage

Absolute Obedience

Griselda must study to comply with his wishes.

Stoicism

She must never be angry or complain, regardless of his words or actions.

Public Consent

She must agree to these terms in the presence of her father.

The Trials of Griselda

Trial Phase. Gualtieri's Actions

Griselda’s Response

The First Child. He pretends the vassals are dissatisfied with their daughter and sends a servant to imply the child must be killed.

She kisses and blesses the child, then hands her over without a word of protest.

The Second Child. He repeats the process with their son, claiming the vassals will not accept a grandson of Giannucolo as their lord.

She remains composed, stating that her pleasure is whatever pleases him.

Annulment. He fakes a papal dispensation to "put her away" and take a noble bride, sending her back to her father.

She returns her ring and clothing, asking only for a "shift" (a simple garment) to cover her body.

The "New Bride". He forces her to return to his house as a servant to prepare the wedding chamber for his "new wife."

She performs the menial labor with a "cheerful" countenance and a "lady-like" mien.

The Framing Narrative: The End of the Decameron

The Return to Florence

After fifteen days away from the city to escape the "season of pestilence" (the Black Death), the king of the day proposes they return to avoid scandal and weariness.

Final Reflections

The king notes that despite the telling of "gay stories" that might "stimulate concupiscence," the group maintained "seemliness" and the "sweet intimacy of brothers and sisters."

The Departure

The three young men and seven ladies return to Santa Maria Novella in Florence, where they depart to their respective homes and diversions.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Ay

Yes (used as an affirmative response).

Brigata

The company of ten young people (seven women, three men) who flee Florence to tell stories.

Certes

Certainly; assuredly.

Concupiscence

Strong sexual desire; lust.

Dissemble

To conceal one's true feelings or intentions; to pretend.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Dowry

Property or money brought by a bride to her husband on their marriage.

Guerdon

A reward or recompense.

Mien

A person's look or manner, especially one of a particular kind indicating their character or mood.

Palfrey

A docile horse used for riding, especially by women.

Seneschal

A steward or major-domo in a medieval noble household.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Shift

A simple, basic undergarment or dress.

Tierce

A canonical hour of prayer, roughly 9:00 a.m. (Half tierce refers to the time midway through this period).

Vassals

Persons granted the use of land, in return for rendering homage, fealty, and usually military service or its equivalent to a lord.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the narrator of the hundredth and final tale of the Decameron?

Dioneo

What is the general theme of the tenth day of the Decameron?

Magnanimity and Virtue

Why is Dioneo's choice of the story of 'Patient Griselda' considered deeply ironic?

He is usually the most irreverent narrator, yet he tells the most moral tale.

Which privilege did Dioneo hold throughout the Decameron regarding story themes?

The right to tell any tale he pleased regardless of the day's theme.

What is the title held by Gualtieri, the male protagonist of the tale?

Marquis of Saluzzo

Why did Gualtieri initially avoid taking a wife?

He believed it was too difficult to find a woman whose ways accorded with his own.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What did Gualtieri's vassals fear would happen if he died without an heir?

They would be left without a lord.

What condition did Gualtieri set for his vassals before agreeing to marry?

They must honor whomever he chose as a lady.

What is the name of Griselda's father?

Giannucolo

What was Griselda's social status before her marriage?

A poor peasant girl/shepherdess.

What specific promise did Gualtieri demand from Griselda before their marriage?

To obey him absolutely and never complain or be wroth.

What ritual did Gualtieri force Griselda to undergo before the wedding guests?

He had her stripped naked and then reclothed in fine garments.
