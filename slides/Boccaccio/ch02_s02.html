Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 2, Section 2

The Decameron, Day the First

Study Slides

The Transition from Chaos to Order

The Election of a Leader

Pampinea, the eldest woman and the one who originally proposed the retreat, is elected as the first queen.

The Establishment of Rules

The group (the *brigata*) agrees on a set of rules for their daily conduct and storytelling to ensure their time is spent in a healthy, virtuous, and pleasurable manner.

The Setting: Fiesole

Natural Beauty

Lush meadows, green grass, and olive trees.

Atmosphere

A "fair prospect" with cool air and light breezes, providing a refuge from the intense midday heat and the "sun high" in the sky.

The Purpose of Storytelling

Collective Solace

The invention of one person provides enjoyment for the entire company.

Emotional Stability

Games like dice often result in the loser being "vexed" and the winner or onlookers being no better off, whereas stories provide a shared benefit.

The Scope of Day I

Themes Introduced

Religious hypocrisy, interfaith wisdom, royal rebuke, and the wit of men and women.

The "Overture"

Collectively, the ten tales of the first day serve as an introduction to the broader themes Boccaccio develops throughout the entire work.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Brigata

The company of ten young people (seven women and three men) who flee the plague.

Cicalas

Insects (cicadas) found among the olive trees, noted for their sound during the heat of the day.

Fiesole

An ancient Etruscan town overlooking Florence, known for its villas and rural retreats.

None

A specific time of day (traditionally the ninth hour or mid-afternoon) when the queen roused the company.

Pampinea

The eldest woman of the group and the queen of Day I.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Pamfilo

The narrator chosen by Pampinea to lead the storytelling on the first day.

Vesper

The evening hour; the time by which the storytelling was expected to conclude.

Villa Palmieri

The estate traditionally identified as the location where the *brigata* first stayed.
