Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: Where is the country estate located, and what is the historical significance of that area?

The estate is located in the hills of Fiesole, an ancient Etruscan town overlooking Florence from the north. It is historically associated with villas and rural retreats.

Q: Who is elected as the first queen of the group, and why?

Pampinea is elected as the first queen. She is the eldest of the women and was the one who initially proposed the retreat from Florence.

Q: Why does Pampinea suggest storytelling instead of playing games like dice or chess?

She argues that in games of chance, the loser is inevitably vexed, and neither the winner nor the onlookers derive much benefit. In contrast, storytelling provides "solace" and amusement for the entire company.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What is the thematic rule for Day I, and how does it compare to other days in the work?

There is no prescribed theme for Day I. It and Day IX are the only two days where the narrators have complete freedom to choose their subjects.

Q: Which narrator is chosen to tell the very first story?

Pamfilo is commanded by the queen to lead off the storytelling.

Q: At what time of day does the storytelling take place, and why?

The storytelling begins shortly after none (mid-afternoon) and continues until the vesper hour. This time is chosen because the sun is high and the heat is too intense for other outdoor activities.

Key Concepts (1/2)

From which city did the ten young people flee to escape the plague?

Florence

To which ancient Etruscan town did the brigata travel for their retreat?

Fiesole

Who was the first person elected as queen of the company?

Pampinea

What status did Pampinea hold among the women of the group that led to her proposal of the retreat?

She was the eldest.

Which two days of the Decameron have no prescribed theme for storytelling?

Day I and Day IX

Why is Day I of the Decameron described as an "overture" to the entire work?

It introduces themes that are developed across all one hundred tales.

Key Concepts (2/2)

List three themes mentioned as being present in the tales of Day I.

Religious hypocrisy, interfaith wisdom, and royal rebuke.

What social purpose does the "civilized order" of the estate serve in the narrative frame?

It provides a deliberate contrast to the chaos of the plague.

At what canonical hour does the queen rouse the company to begin their activities?

None

According to the queen, why is it necessary to rise after None rather than sleeping?

Sleeping long in the daytime is injurious to health.

Where does the company gather to hear the queen's proposal for storytelling?

A meadow with green and luxuriant grass.

In what physical arrangement do the members of the company sit to listen to the tales?

They range themselves in a circle on the grass.
