The Historical and Clinical Context of the Plague

Origin and Spread

The pestilence began in the East and moved westward. In Florence, it persisted despite city-wide cleaning, the exclusion of the sick, and public religious supplications.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/4)

Albeit

Although; even though.

Becchini

A class of porters from the lower ranks who carried the dead to graves for hire.

Canonical Hours

The fixed times of daily prayer observed in monasteries (e.g., Matins, Vespers).

Canzonets

Short, light songs; some are sung by the ladies for their delectation.

Courtesy

In medieval usage, the refined, noble behavior expected of the upper class.

Discourse

Formal conversation or discussion on a subject.

Fortune

Fate or chance, often personified as the goddess Fortuna.

Indulgence

A remission of punishment for sin granted by the Church.

Novella

Key Concepts (1/2)

To what specific audience does Boccaccio dedicate 'The Decameron' in the Proem?

Women who are in love.

According to Boccaccio, why do women have a greater need for the solace of stories than men?

They have fewer distractions and are often confined to their chambers by social expectations.

What is the dual purpose of the stories in 'The Decameron' for its readers?

To provide pleasure from entertainment and 'good counsel' on what to pursue or shun.

What metaphor does Boccaccio use to describe the painful introduction of the plague relative to the stories that follow?

A steep and rugged mountain that leads to a fair and delectable plain.

In what year did the 'deadly pestilence' first appear in the city of Florence?

1348.

Where did the Black Death originate before propagating to the West?

The East.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the common name for the plague-related tumors that appeared in the groin or armpits?

Gavoccioli.

Apart from tumors, what secondary physical symptom served as an 'infallible token' of approaching death?

Black or livid spots appearing on the arms, thighs, or elsewhere.

How long was the typical duration between the appearance of plague symptoms and death?

About three days.

What event did Boccaccio witness involving two hogs that demonstrated the plague's virulence?

They died almost instantly after touching and tossing the rags of a deceased man.

What lifestyle did the 'temperates' adopt to avoid the plague?

They lived in secluded communities, eating delicate foods and avoiding all news of sickness.

How did the hedonistic group believe they could cure or avoid the plague?

By drinking freely, frequenting taverns, and satisfying every appetite while mocking the disaster.
