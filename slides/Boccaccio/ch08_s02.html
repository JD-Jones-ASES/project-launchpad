Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 8, Section 2

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day VII

Study Slides

The Central Theme: Domestic Deception

For Love

Acting out of genuine passion.

For Preservation

Acting out of self-interest or necessity.

The Setting: The Ladies’ Vale

Atmosphere

The day begins at dawn, with the star Lucifer still glowing in the sky as the group departs.

Nature

The valley is filled with singing nightingales and other birds that seem to "swell the chorus" of the human company.

Amenities

The seneschal prepares the area with tables under a "living canopy of trees" beside a lake filled with shoals of darting fish.

Leisure

Before the storytelling begins, the company enjoys wine, comfits, and singing. The seneschal provides beds equipped with "French coverlets" for those wishing to rest after breakfast.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: What unique privilege does Dioneo possess among the ten narrators?

Dioneo always tells the last tale of the day and has the freedom to choose any subject he likes, regardless of the day’s assigned theme.

Q: According to the day’s rubric, what are the two main reasons wives deceive their husbands?

Wives act either "for love" or for their "own preservation."

Q: How does Boccaccio’s portrayal of the "clever wife" differ from traditional medieval verse tales?

Boccaccio distinguishes his work through psychological realism and a clear sympathy for the wives, often depicting the husbands as foolish or jealous individuals who deserve to be outwitted.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What is a beffa?

A beffa is a practical joke, trick, or elaborate deception that serves as a central comic motif in the Decameron.

Q: Describe the scene at the Ladies’ Vale before the storytelling begins.

The company arrives at dawn, accompanied by birdsong. They eat breakfast under trees by a lake, watch fish, sing, and some take naps on beds furnished with French coverlets before the storytelling starts on carpets spread on the sward.

Q: Who is the first person to tell a story on Day VII?

Emilia is the first to begin the storytelling at the king’s request.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the king of the seventh day of storytelling in the Decameron?

Dioneo

Which narrator is described as the most playful and irreverent of the ten?

Dioneo

What is Dioneo's standing privilege throughout the Decameron regarding the order of stories?

He always tells the last tale of each Day.

What privilege does Dioneo have regarding the daily assigned theme?

He may choose any subject he likes.

What is the specific theme assigned to the seventh day of the Decameron?

The tricks wives play on their husbands.

According to the Day VII rubric, what are the two primary motivations for the wives' deceptions?

Love or self-preservation.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Term: Beffa

A practical joke, trick, or elaborate deception.

What is the range of severity for a 'beffa' in the Decameron?

It ranges from harmless fun to calculated cruelty.

What were the short, humorous, often bawdy medieval French verse tales that influenced Boccaccio?

Fabliaux

What is an 'exemplum' in the context of medieval literature?

A short illustrative story used in sermons to teach a moral lesson.

How does Boccaccio typically treat the traditional Latin 'exempla' in his writing?

He subverts them or turns them inside out.

What narrative quality distinguishes Boccaccio's treatment of clever wives from earlier traditions?

Psychological realism
