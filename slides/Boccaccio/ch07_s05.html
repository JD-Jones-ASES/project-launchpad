The Figure of Guido Cavalcanti

Intellectual Standing

He was an elite logician and natural philosopher, as well as a renowned lyric poet and the "first friend" of Dante Alighieri.

Character

He was known for being rich, gallant, and possessed of excellent discourse, yet he was often aloof and "addicted to speculation."

Reputation

Because of his inclination toward Epicurean philosophy, the common people (the "vulgar") rumored that his intellectual pursuits were intended to prove that God did not exist.

The Setting: San Giovanni and Santa Reparata

San Giovanni

The Baptistery of Florence, surrounded at the time by ancient Roman marble sarcophagi.

Santa Reparata

The site (now the location of the Florence Cathedral) where many of these marble tombs were later moved.

Symbolism

The setting among the "houses of the dead" provides the literal and metaphorical foundation for Cavalcanti’s witty retort.
