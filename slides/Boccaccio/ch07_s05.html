Historical Context: Florentine Social Companies

Companies of Gentlemen

Small, exclusive groups of residents who shared expenses and took turns entertaining one another.

Social Duties

These groups would honor visiting dignitaries and fellow citizens, ride through the city on notable days, and participate in tilts (jousting) during festivals or celebrations of victory.

Character Profiles

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Epicurean

A follower of the philosophy of Epicurus; in the context of this tale, it refers to the belief (often attributed to Cavalcanti by the public) that the soul is mortal and God does not exist.

Flout

To mock, insult, or treat with contemptuous disregard.

Logician

A person skilled in the art of logic and formal reasoning.

Messer

A title of respect for a gentleman or nobleman, similar to "Sir."

Natural Philosopher

An early term for a scientist or someone who studies the physical laws of the universe.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Piazza

A public square or open space in an Italian city, typically the center of civic life.

San Giovanni

The Baptistery of Florence, a significant religious and historical building.

Santa Reparata

An ancient cathedral in Florence (the site where the Duomo now stands); the piazza mentioned in the text is associated with this location.

Speculation

Deep intellectual contemplation or theoretical reasoning.

Vaulted

To leap or spring over an obstacle while supporting oneself with the hands.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the protagonist of the ninth tale of Day VI in Boccaccio's Decameron?

Guido Cavalcanti

Which Florentine poet and philosopher was Dante's closest friend and literary companion?

Guido Cavalcanti

In which work does Dante Alighieri refer to Guido Cavalcanti as his “first friend”?

The Vita Nuova

What are the primary characteristics of Guido Cavalcanti's poetry?

It is dense, philosophical, and concerned with the destructive power of love.

How did the historical Guido Cavalcanti die?

He died of malaria contracted during political exile.

What is the approximate life span of the poet Guido Cavalcanti?

c. 1255–1300

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which character in the Decameron tells the ninth tale of Day VI?

Elissa

In what specific Florentine location is Guido Cavalcanti cornered by a group of gentlemen?

Among the marble tombs near the church of San Giovanni.

What landmark building is known as the Baptistery of Florence?

San Giovanni

What objects surrounded the San Giovanni Baptistery during Boccaccio's time that served as public monuments?

Ancient Roman sarcophagi

Who was the historical Florentine nobleman and social leader who cornered Cavalcanti?

Betto Brunelleschi

Betto Brunelleschi from the Decameron lived a century before which famous Renaissance architect?

Filippo Brunelleschi
