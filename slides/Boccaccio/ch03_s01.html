Fortune’s Reversals in Boccaccio’s Decameron (Day II)

Study Slides

Ingegno vs. Fortuna

Ingegno

Refers to intelligence, resourcefulness, and native wit. It is the ability to think quickly and adapt to circumstances to turn a misfortune into an advantage.

The Narrative Tension

The "heroes" of these tales are not those who avoid the wheel of Fortune, but those who learn to "ride" it through the use of their ingegno.

Alatiel (II.7)

Social Satire

The tale concludes with Alatiel being returned to her father and married off "as a virgin." This is a pointed irony, suggesting that in her society, appearances alone determine social reality.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Angevin Court

The royal court in Naples where Boccaccio lived from approximately 1327 to 1340; the source of his intimate knowledge of the city.

Boethius (c. 480–524)

A Roman senator and philosopher whose work, The Consolation of Philosophy, introduced the metaphor of Fortune's wheel to medieval thought.

Fortuna

A quasi-divine, capricious force governing human affairs; represents both wealth and fate.

Ingegno

Intelligence, native wit, and resourcefulness; the ability to adapt and turn misfortune into advantage.

Picaresque

A genre of fiction featuring a clever, low-born protagonist (a picaro) who survives by their wits while navigating a corrupt or dangerous society.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Sicilian Vespers (1282)

A historical rebellion in Sicily against French Angevin rule, resulting in significant political upheaval used as a plot device in Day II.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In the context of the Decameron, what dual meanings did the medieval Italian word 'fortuna' encompass?

It simultaneously meant 'fortune' (wealth) and 'fate' (chance).

Which philosopher's image of 'Fortune's wheel' became the most influential metaphor for fate in medieval thought?

Boethius (in 'The Consolation of Philosophy').

Concept: Ingegno

Definition: The supreme human quality of intelligence, quick thinking, and the ability to turn misfortune to one's advantage.

What is the central organizing theme of Day II of the Decameron?

Fortune's Reversals.

How is the goddess Fortuna characterized in Boccaccio's worldview?

She is described as a capricious, irresistible, and morally indifferent force that raises and casts down mortals.

In the tales of Day II, what is identified as the essential element that makes a human story worth telling?

The human response to the inescapable forces of fortune.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Which character in Day II serves as a prime example of complete passivity in the face of fortune?

Alatiel (Novel II.7).

Which character begins their narrative as a 'fool' but evolves into a resourceful 'improviser' by the story's end?

Andreuccio da Perugia (Novel II.5).

Definition: Picaresque

A genre of fiction featuring a clever protagonist of low birth who survives a series of adventures in a corrupt social world.

Why is Andreuccio da Perugia (II.5) considered a forerunner of the picaresque tradition?

It follows a protagonist's episodic journey through the dangerous, socially stratified underside of a city.

In Andreuccio's first episode, how does the Sicilian woman manage to rob him?

She lures him into a trap by pretending to be his long-lost sister.

What does Andreuccio learn from his first misfortune involving the Sicilian prostitute?

He receives his first lesson in the duplicity and deceptive nature of the world.
