Chapter 6, Section 4

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day V, Novel 9 (Federigo degli Alberighi)

Study Slides

Symbolism of the Falcon

Federigo’s Identity

It is his last tie to his noble past and his only remaining "solace."

Supreme Sacrifice

By killing the bird to feed Monna Giovanna, Federigo destroys his last possession to uphold the laws of hospitality and courtesy.

The Catalyst for Change

The falcon is the reason Monna Giovanna visits him and the reason she eventually recognizes his "high desert" and chooses to marry him.

Short-Answer Practice Questions

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Alberighi

A historical Florentine noble family; Boccaccio uses the name to provide a sense of local history and realism.

Campi

The location of Federigo’s small estate outside of Florence where he retires in poverty.

Coppo di Borghese Domenichi

The character cited as the primary narrator of the story; described as a man of great reverence, memory, and "concinnity of speech."

Courtesy

The medieval social code of refined behavior, manners, and generosity, of which Federigo is considered a peerless example.

Guerdon

A reward or recompense; in this context, the "bestowal" of a lady’s favor or love.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Husbandman

A farmer or worker on an estate; Federigo avoids asking his husbandman for help due to his pride.

Largess

Generous spending or the giving of gifts; one of the ways Federigo originally sought to impress Monna Giovanna.

Monna

A contraction of *Madonna* ("my lady"); the standard respectful address for a married woman in medieval Italy.

Straitly

Living in a restricted, narrow, or impoverished manner.
