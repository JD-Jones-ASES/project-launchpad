Chapter 6, Section 4

Federigo’s Falcon (The Decameron, Day V, Novel 9)

Study Slides

Sacrifice and Symbolism

Federigo’s Identity

It is his last link to his noble past and his sole remaining "solace."

Absolute Devotion

By killing the bird to feed Monna Giovanna, Federigo demonstrates a level of devotion that transcends material value.

The Irony of Fate

Federigo destroys the very thing Monna Giovanna came to ask for in his attempt to honor her visit.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Concinnity

Elegance or harmony in style, specifically regarding Coppo di Borghese Domenichi’s speech.

Guerdons

Rewards or compensations; in this context, the favors or "charms" bestowed by a lady.

Hied

To go quickly or hasten (e.g., "hied him to her with all speed").

Husbandman

A person who tends the land; a farmer or worker on an estate.

Largess

Generous spending or the giving of gifts, often used by nobles to show status.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Mien

A person's look or manner, indicating their character or mood.

Monna

A contraction of Madonna (“my lady”); the standard respectful address for a married woman in medieval Italy.

Straitly

In a restricted or meager manner; living with very little money.

Trencher

A wooden plate or platter on which food is served.

Wont

Accustomed to or in the habit of doing something.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who is the narrator of the ninth tale on the Fifth Day of the Decameron?

Fiammetta

According to the text, what is the literary significance of the ninth tale of Day V?

It is considered the most perfectly constructed tale in the Decameron.

To which historical Florentine noble family does the protagonist Federigo belong?

The Alberighi family

What is the meaning of the medieval Italian title 'Monna'?

A contraction of Madonna, used as a respectful address for a married woman.

What primary reason is given for Federigo degli Alberighi losing his entire fortune?

He spent it courting Monna Giovanna through jousts, feasts, and largess.

What was Federigo's sole remaining possession after he was reduced to poverty?

A single magnificent falcon

Key Concepts (2/2)

Where was the small estate located where Federigo retired in his poverty?

Campi

Who does Fiammetta cite as the historical source for the story of Federigo?

Coppo di Borghese Domenichi

How does the text describe Monna Giovanna's initial response to Federigo's advances?

She cared 'not a jot' for his actions or for him.

Under what condition was Monna Giovanna named the heir to her husband's estate?

In the event that their son died without a lawful heir.

What specific item did Monna Giovanna's sick son believe would cure him?

Federigo's falcon

Why was Monna Giovanna initially hesitant to ask Federigo for his falcon?

She knew it was his only remaining solace and that she had previously ignored his love.
