Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: What initial mistake does Andreuccio make upon arriving at the Neapolitan market?

He repeatedly draws out his purse of five hundred gold florins in view of everyone to show he "means business," alerting criminals to his wealth.

Q: How does the Sicilian girl convince Andreuccio that she is his long-lost sister?

She uses specific details provided by an old servant, including his father Pietro’s time in Palermo, the names of his kinsfolk, and the claim that her mother was a widow who had an affair with Pietro.

Q: Describe the physical trap that leads to Andreuccio’s first misfortune.

While attempting to use the "stool" (latrine) in a corner of the girl’s house, Andreuccio steps on a plank that has been detached from its joist. He falls through the floor into a foul sewer/alleyway.

Q: How do the thieves ensure Andreuccio enters the Archbishop’s tomb?

When Andreuccio refuses to enter, the thieves threaten him with an iron crowbar, telling him they will beat him until he is dead if he does not comply.

Q: What "insurance" does Andreuccio take while inside the tomb?

Suspecting his companions will abandon him once they have the treasure, he hides the Archbishop's ruby ring on his own finger, handing up the other valuables (the mitre, crosier, and gloves) first while claiming he cannot find the ring.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Archbishop Filippo Minutolo

The Archbishop of Naples whose burial with a ruby ring and fine trappings serves as the final target for the thieves.

Contrive

To devise or plan cleverly; to bring about through ingenuity or schemes.

Evil Hole (Malpertugio)

The disreputable quarter of Naples where the Sicilian girl’s house is located.

Fiammetta

The narrator of this tale, described as "Little Flame" and one of the liveliest storytellers of the *Decameron*.

Florin

A gold coin from Florence, highly trusted in medieval Europe. Andreuccio carries five hundred, a sum sufficient to buy several fine horses.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Guelf

A political faction; the Sicilian girl’s husband is described as a "staunch Guelf" to add legitimacy to her false life story.

Naples

A major commercial city in the 14th century, portrayed as a dangerous and sophisticated environment compared to the provincial Perugia.

Perforce

By necessity; inevitably.

Perugia

A provincial city in Umbria, central Italy, and the home of Andreuccio.

Ruga Catalana

A street in Naples that Andreuccio traverses while attempting to find the sea to wash himself.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Vespers

A sunset prayer service; the time of day when Andreuccio first arrives in Naples and later when the little girl finds him at his inn.
