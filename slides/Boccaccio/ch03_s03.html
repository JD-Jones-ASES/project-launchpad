Tales of the Decameron (Boccaccio)

Chapter 3, Section 3

Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day II, Tale 5

Study Slides

The Transformation of Andreuccio

Initial State

He arrives in Naples from Perugia with five hundred gold florins, foolishly flaunting his wealth in the public marketplace.

The Catalyst

His education occurs through a series of "perilous scapes" involving deception, physical filth, and mortal danger.

Final State

By dawn, having survived three distinct ordeals, he returns to his inn not with horses, but with a ruby ring worth more than his original capital.

The Character of Naples

Labyrinthine and Dark

A place of narrow alleys and "Evil Holes" where a stranger can easily lose their way.

Dangerous

Inhabited by swindlers, prostitutes, bullies, and thieves.

Full of Opportunity

A city where the quick-witted can seize fortune even in the most desperate circumstances.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/4)

Q: Where is Andreuccio from, and what is his profession?

Andreuccio is a young man from Perugia who works as a horse-dealer.

Q: Why does Andreuccio travel to Naples, and how much money does he bring?

He travels to Naples to buy horses, carrying five hundred gold florins in his purse.

Q: How does the Sicilian girl (Fiordaliso) obtain specific information about Andreuccio’s family?

She questions an old Sicilian woman who had previously lived with Andreuccio’s father, Pietro, in both Sicily and Perugia.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/4)

Q: What "cunning stratagem" does the girl use to lure Andreuccio to her house?

She sends a well-trained little girl to tell Andreuccio that a "great lady" wishes to speak with him. She then pretends to be his long-lost sister, claiming to be the illegitimate daughter of his father from his time in Palermo.

Q: How does Andreuccio lose his money at the lady’s house?

He falls through a loose plank in a private "stool" (toilet) into a foul sewer alley. While he is trapped outside, the lady steals his clothes and the money he kept on his person.

Q: Who is Scarabone Buttafuoco?

He is the "bully" or protector of the Sicilian girl who threatens Andreuccio from the window in a "gruff and savage" voice when Andreuccio tries to reclaim his belongings.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/4)

Q: Why do the two thieves want Andreuccio to join them at the Cathedral?

They intend to rob the tomb of Messer Filippo Minutolo, the Archbishop of Naples, who was buried that day with valuable ornaments, including a ruby ring.

Q: What happens when Andreuccio is lowered into the well?

The thieves flee when they see a company of thirsty patrols approaching. The patrols, unaware Andreuccio is on the rope, pull him up to the brink. Terrified by the sight of him, they drop the rope and flee, allowing Andreuccio to climb out.

Q: How do the thieves trap Andreuccio inside the Archbishop’s tomb?

After Andreuccio hands up the Archbishop's trappings (crosier, mitre, and gloves) but claims he cannot find the ring, the thieves—sensing he is trying to cheat them—pull out the prop holding the marble lid, locking him inside.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (4/4)

Q: How does Andreuccio finally escape the tomb?

When a second group of grave robbers (including a priest) opens the tomb, Andreuccio grabs the priest’s leg as he enters. The terrified intruders flee in a panic, leaving the tomb open for Andreuccio to exit.

Key Concepts (1/2)

From which city did Andreuccio di Pietro originate?

Perugia.

What was Andreuccio's profession?

Horse-dealer.

How much gold did Andreuccio bring to Naples to purchase horses?

Five hundred gold florins.

What mistake did Andreuccio make in the Naples marketplace that attracted swindlers?

He repeatedly showed his purse of gold florins to prove he meant business.

How did the Sicilian girl obtain detailed information about Andreuccio's family?

She questioned an old woman who had served Andreuccio's father.

What was the name of Andreuccio's father?

Pietro.

Key Concepts (2/2)

In what Sicilian city did Andreuccio’s father live before returning to Perugia?

Palermo.

What was the name of the dangerous quarter where the Sicilian girl lived?

Evil Hole.

What relationship did the Sicilian girl claim to have with Andreuccio?

She claimed to be his long-lost sister.

According to the girl's story, why did her father leave her in Palermo?

He returned to Perugia and never bestowed another thought on her or her mother.

Why did the Sicilian girl claim she and her husband fled to Naples?

They were Guelfs whose correspondence with King Charles was discovered by King Frederic.

What sensory details in the girl's chamber convinced Andreuccio she was a 'great lady'?

The fragrance of roses and orange blossoms, a sumptuous bed, and many dresses.
