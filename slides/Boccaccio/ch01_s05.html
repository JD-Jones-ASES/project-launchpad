Chapter 1, Section 5

The Project of Boccaccio’s Decameron

Study Slides

Structural Overview: The Cornice

The Setting

The story begins in plague-stricken Florence. To escape the chaos and the breakdown of social order, ten young nobles flee to a series of country villas in the hills.

The Logic of the Frame

The cornice represents an ordered, civilized society set in direct opposition to the death and disorder of the plague.

The Routine

To pass the time, the group establishes a structured routine. Each day, a king or queen is elected to oversee the company and set a specific theme for the stories to be told.

The Mathematical Symmetry

10 storytellers tell 1 tale each per day over 10 days, resulting in exactly 100 tales.

The Thematic Arc of the Ten Days

Day. Theme

Key Characteristics

I. No set theme

Establishes the tone and central concerns of the work.

II. Fortune's reversals

Tales of individuals reaching happiness after unexpected adversity.

III. Cleverness triumphant

Tales of using wit to obtain desires.

IV. Love and tragedy

Tales of love that end unhappily.

V. Love rewarded

Tales of lovers finding happiness after terrible adventures.

VI. Quick wit

Escaping danger or mockery through clever remarks.

VII. Tricks wives play on husbands

Focused on domestic deception and female ingenuity.

The Brigata: The Storytellers (1/2)

Pampinea

The eldest woman; practical and authoritative. She proposes the retreat and serves as the first queen.

Panfilo

His name means "all-loving." He is responsible for telling the very first and the very last tales of the collection.

Filostrato

His name means "vanquished by love." He appropriately serves as king on the day dedicated to tragic love stories.

The Brigata: The Storytellers (2/2)

Neifile

Gravitates toward stories of ingenuity and cleverness.

Fiammetta

Boccaccio’s literary alter ego of his beloved; she focuses on tales of love.

Elissa, Lauretta, and Emilia

Additional members of the company with less sharply defined but distinct preferences.

Glossary of Terms (1/2)

Brigata

The "company" or "brigade"; the group of ten young nobles who flee the plague and tell the tales.

Cornice

Italian for "frame"; the scholarly term for the narrative structure that houses the one hundred tales.

Magnanimity

Greatness of spirit or generosity; the central theme of the final day of storytelling.

Proem

An introduction or preface; specifically, the section where Boccaccio dedicates his work to women.

Pseudonym

A fictitious name; used by the ten storytellers to mask their real identities.

Glossary of Terms (2/2)

Verse

Writing arranged with a metrical rhythm (poetry); the dominant literary form prior to the rise of Boccaccio's prose.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the total number of tales contained in Boccaccio's Decameron?

One hundred tales.

How many storytellers are featured in the Decameron?

Ten storytellers.

In the Decameron, how many of the storytellers are women and how many are men?

Seven women and three men.

From which city do the young people in the Decameron flee to escape the plague?

Florence.

What is the collective name given to the ten storytellers in the Decameron?

The brigata.

Term: Cornice

Definition: The scholarly term for the Decameron's frame narrative involving the ten young people fleeing the plague.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What does the cornice represent in contrast to the chaos of the plague?

An ordered, civilized world.

In the Decameron's structure, how many tales does each storyteller tell per day?

One tale per day.

Which two days in the Decameron have no set theme for their stories?

Days I and IX.

What is the specific theme of Day II in the Decameron?

Fortune's reversals (those who arrive at happiness after adversity).

Which day focuses on tales of those who obtain their desires through wit or cleverness?

Day III.

What characterizes the stories told on Day IV of the Decameron?

Love with unhappy endings (love and tragedy).
