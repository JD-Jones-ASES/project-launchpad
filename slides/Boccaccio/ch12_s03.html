The Narrative Arc: From Chaos to Virtue

The Starting Point

The collection begins in the shadow of the Black Death, the most significant catastrophe in European history. This sets a backdrop of social and moral chaos.

The Progression

The stories move from the free-ranging wit of Day I through the dark tragedies of Day IV, the comic reversals of Days VII and VIII, and finally to the moral elevation of Day X.

The Philosophical Goal

This structure is described by scholar Giuseppe Mazzotta as “the world at play,” mirroring the disorder and reordering of the world outside the garden.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Beffa

A trick or practical joke, often characterized by its audacity or the cleverness of the person performing it.

Brigata

The company of ten young storytellers (seven women and three men) who flee the plague-stricken city of Florence.

Fortuna

Fate or chance, personified as a goddess who unpredictably raises or ruins human plans regardless of their status.

Ingegno

Quick wit, resourcefulness, and the intelligence required to navigate difficult situations or obtain what one desires.

Magnificenza

Magnanimity or nobility of spirit; considered the highest human virtue in medieval culture.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Novella

A short prose narrative; Boccaccio’s refinement of this form became the precursor to the modern short story.
