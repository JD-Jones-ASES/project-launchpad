Boccaccio’s Decameron, Day VI – The Art of the Motto

Study Slides

Sprezzatura: Effortless Mastery

Madonna Oretta (VI.1)

Delivers a devastating social critique with lightness.

Guido Cavalcanti (VI.9)

Torts a group of antagonists while physically vaulting over a tomb.

Fra Cipolla (VI.10)

Improvises an elaborate lie with total spontaneity when his "relic" is compromised.

Metaliterary Reflection

Repetition

Telling the same thing multiple times.

Confusion

Getting names and facts wrong.

Lack of Decorum

A mismatch between the style of the delivery and the subject of the tale.

Summary of the Tales (Day VI, 2–8) (1/2)

VI.2 (Cisti the Baker)

Cisti uses a motto ("The Arno has not yet run dry") to teach a nobleman that gifts of high-quality wine must be offered and received with proper etiquette, not demanded through servants.

VI.3 (Monna Nonna de’ Pulci)

A woman uses a pointed retort to silence the Bishop of Florence after he makes a vulgar jest at her expense.

VI.4 (Chichibio and the Crane)

A servant saves himself from his master’s anger by claiming cranes have only one leg; when proven wrong, his quick retort about "shouting" at the birds rescues him from punishment.

VI.5 (Messer Forese and Giotto)

The famous painter Giotto uses wit to remind a lawyer that true worth is not found in outward appearances, especially when both are bedraggled by a rainstorm.

VI.6 (Michele Scalza)

Uses "mock-logical eloquence" to win a bet by arguing that the ugliest family in Florence is the noblest because they were God’s "first drafts."

Summary of the Tales (Day VI, 2–8) (2/2)

VI.7 (Madonna Filippa)

Caught in adultery, she argues before a podesta that laws made without women’s consent are unjust. Her wit and courage lead to a change in the law.

VI.8 (Fresco da Celatico)

Fresco uses a sharp remark about mirrors to criticize his niece Cesca’s habit of acting disgusted by everyone she sees.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Castiglione, Baldassare

The author of The Book of the Courtier (1528) who defined the concept of sprezzatura.

Gentildonna

A lady of good birth or a gentlewoman; expected to possess subtle and graceful wit.

Giotto di Bondone

A historically significant Italian painter praised by Boccaccio for introducing naturalism and "bringing back to light" the art of painting.

Ingegno

Italian for wit or cleverness; the internal quality that produces a motto.

Metaliterary

Literature that reflects on its own nature or the process of creation.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Motto

A clever, pointed verbal response or witticism delivered at the opportune moment.

Podesta

The chief magistrate of an Italian city-state, typically an outsider hired to ensure impartial justice.

Sprezzatura

The appearance of effortless mastery; performing difficult tasks with nonchalance.

St. Lawrence

A martyr roasted on a gridiron; the patron saint of cooks and a symbol of dark humor.

Key Concepts (1/2)

In the context of Boccaccio's Decameron, what is a 'motto'?

A clever, pointed verbal response delivered at exactly the right moment to rescue the speaker.

Boccaccio describes the motto as the audible form of what mental quality?

Verbal ingegno, or cleverness.

While earlier Days in the Decameron focus on fortune and love, what power does Day VI celebrate?

The power of words.

What is the framing purpose for the storytelling in the Decameron?

To preserve the sanity and humanity of ten young people during the plague.

Which social class does Madonna Oretta represent in her use of wit?

The Florentine upper class (gentildonna).

What characterizes the wit of a 'gentildonna' like Madonna Oretta?

It is expected to be graceful, subtle, and indirect.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Whose wit is characterized as 'dense, allusive, and requiring intellectual effort to decode'?

Guido Cavalcanti.

Fra Cipolla represents wit as what type of social activity?

Popular performance or theatrical showmanship.

Which Renaissance term describes the art of performing difficult tasks with apparent ease and nonchalance?

Sprezzatura.

Who coined the term 'sprezzatura' in the 1528 work 'The Book of the Courtier'?

Baldassare Castiglione.

Why is Madonna Oretta's criticism of the knight considered an example of sprezzatura?

It is a devastating critique delivered with extreme lightness and tact.

In Tale VI.1, how does Madonna Oretta politely tell the knight his storytelling is poor?

She asks to be 'set down' from his story-horse.
