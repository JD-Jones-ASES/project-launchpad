Summary of the Tales of Day VI

Novel. Protagonist

Summary

VI.1. Madonna Oretta

Rebukes a knight’s poor storytelling by asking to be "set down" from his "story-horse."

VI.2. Cisti the Baker

Uses a motto about the Arno river to remind a nobleman that gifts must be offered, not demanded.

VI.3. Monna Nonna de' Pulci

Silences the Bishop of Florence after he makes a vulgar jest at her expense.

VI.4. Chichibio

A cook who escapes punishment for a stolen crane leg by pointing out that cranes only stand on one leg when resting.

VI.5. Giotto

The famous painter retorts to a friend's mockery by noting that true worth is independent of bedraggled appearances.

VI.6. Michele Scalza

Wins a bet by using absurd logic to argue that the "ugly" Baronci family is the noblest, as they were God's "first sketches."

VI.7. Madonna Filippa

Successfully argues against an adultery law by claiming it was made without women's consent and that she merely gave away her "surplus."

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Gentildonna

A gentlewoman or lady of good birth; expected to exhibit grace and indirect wit.

Ingegno

The quality of cleverness or ingenuity; in Day VI, it specifically refers to "verbal ingegno."

Metaliterary

Literature that reflects on its own nature or provides commentary on the process of writing and storytelling.

Motto

A clever, pointed verbal response, quip, or retort delivered at the perfect moment.

Podesta

The chief magistrate of an Italian city-state, usually an outsider brought in to ensure impartial justice.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Sprezzatura

The appearance of effortless mastery; performing difficult tasks (verbal or physical) with nonchalance.

St. Lawrence

A Roman martyr roasted on a gridiron; the patron saint of cooks and the subject of Fra Cipolla’s improvised story about charcoal.
