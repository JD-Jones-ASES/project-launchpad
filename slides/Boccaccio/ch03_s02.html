Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Who succeeds Pampinea as the ruler of the second day?

> **Answer:** Filomena.

Q: What specific theme does the queen establish for the stories of Day II?

> **Answer:** Tales of those who, after various misadventures, achieve a happy ending they had not hoped for (the theme of Fortuna).

Q: What ubiquitous medieval image is used to represent Fortune?

> **Answer:** A goddess turning a great wheel, which raises some mortals up and casts others down.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: To which 6th-century literary work is the medieval personification of Fortune traced?

> **Answer:** Boethius’s *Consolation of Philosophy*.

Q: How does the physical setting of the company change at the start of Day II?

> **Answer:** The company moves from the gardens of the first villa to a new estate.

Q: According to the text, what activities does the company engage in on the morning of the second day before storytelling begins?

> **Answer:** They walk through dewy meadows, cull flowers, weave garlands, eat breakfast in the shade, dance, and sleep until noon.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Who is the first storyteller called upon by the queen on the second day?

> **Answer:** Neifile.
