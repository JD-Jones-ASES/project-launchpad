Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/3)

Q: Who succeeds Pampinea as the ruler for the second day?

> Answer: Filomena.

Q: What is the specific theme prescribed for the stories of Day II?

> Answer: Tales of individuals who, after various misadventures, achieve an unexpected happy ending.

Q: Which historical text is credited with the ubiquitous medieval image of the "Wheel of Fortune"?

> Answer: Boethius's The Consolation of Philosophy (c. 524).

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/3)

Q: How does the physical setting change at the beginning of Day II?

> Answer: The company moves from the first villa to a new estate.

Q: Who is the first person called upon by the Queen to tell a story on the second day?

> Answer: Neifile.

Q: What qualities allow characters in Day II to survive the "misadventures" of Fortune?

> Answer: Luck, wit, or simple endurance.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (3/3)

Q: Describe the narrative style of the tales in Day II compared to Day I.

> Answer: They are among the longest and most elaborately plotted tales, featuring high-adventure elements like shipwrecks and mistaken identities.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Who succeeds Pampinea as the queen for the second day of storytelling?

Filomena

What central theme does Filomena choose for the storytelling on Day II?

Fortune (or Fortuna)

How was Fortune personified in medieval thought?

As a goddess who turned a great wheel.

Which 6th-century work is the primary source for the ubiquitous medieval image of the Wheel of Fortune?

The Consolation of Philosophy

Who is the author of the medieval philosophical work 'The Consolation of Philosophy'?

Boethius

In medieval philosophy, what happens to mortals when the goddess Fortune turns her wheel?

She raises some to prosperity and casts others down.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the general narrative focus of the tales told on Day II?

People who, after various misadventures, achieve an unexpected happy ending.

According to the text, the world of Day II is one of adventure, surprise, and _____.

Reversal

Through what three means do characters in Day II arrive at an unexpected happy ending?

Luck, wit, or simple endurance.

In terms of narrative structure, how do Day II tales compare to the rest of the Decameron?

They are among the longest and most elaborately plotted.

List one frequent plot element mentioned that is characteristic of Day II stories.

Shipwrecks (or disguises, mistaken identities, or midnight escapades).

Where does the company move to at the beginning of Day II?

A new estate (or villa).
