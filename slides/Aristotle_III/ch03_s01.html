The Life of Being (Aristotle III)

Chapter 3, Section 1

Aristotle’s First Philosophy: Being and the Principles of Reality

Study Slides

The Science of Being as Being (on he on)

Definition

The study of things in the capacity of their being, rather than their specific attributes.

Scope

It is a universal inquiry that seeks the most fundamental truths, such as the four causes and the governing principles of reality.

Clarity vs. Vagueness

Unlike specific sciences that gain clarity by narrowing their focus, first philosophy achieves precision by identifying universal truths that apply to every being.

The Primacy of Substance (ousia)

Category. Description

Relationship to Being

Substance. What exists in itself (e.g., Socrates, a horse).

The primary sense of being; the focal point.

Dependent Categories. Qualities, quantities, and relations (e.g., pale, fast, three).

Real only by belonging to or existing through a substance.

The Principle of Non-Contradiction (PNC)

The Formulation

The same attribute cannot at the same time belong and not belong to the same subject in the same respect.

The Defense by Retorsion

The PNC cannot be proven through deduction because it is the presupposition of all proof. Instead, Aristotle defends it by showing that any person attempting to deny it must use meaningful speech. ...

The Principle of Excluded Middle (PEM)

The Formulation

For any statement, there is no middle ground between affirmation and negation. A thing either is or is not; it cannot hover in an indeterminate zone.

Knowledge vs. Reality

The PEM is a claim about the way the world is, not what we know about it. Even if we are ignorant of a fact, the reality of that fact remains determinate.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Being as Being (on he on)

The study of things solely in their capacity as existing entities; the subject of metaphysics or "first philosophy."

Excluded Middle, Principle of

The axiom stating that every meaningful statement is either true or false, with no third possibility; the world is determinate.

Focal Meaning (pros hen equivocity)

A linguistic and metaphysical structure where various related senses of a word all refer back to one primary sense.

Non-Contradiction, Principle of (PNC)

The axiom that it is impossible for the same thing to both be and not be in the same way at the same time.

Ousia

The Greek term for "substance"; that which exists in itself and serves as the primary category of being.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Prote Philosophia

"First philosophy"; the study of the most fundamental principles and the highest beings.

Retorsion

A defensive argument strategy that demonstrates how a position's denial presupposes the very principle it seeks to reject.

Substance

The primary kind of being that does not exist in another; the entity upon which all qualities, quantities, and relations depend.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What does Aristotle mean by the phrase $on\ he\ on$?

The study of "being as being," or things in the capacity of their existence rather than specific attributes.

How does "first philosophy" ($prote\ philosophia$) differ from special sciences like biology or physics?

Special sciences restrict inquiry to a particular kind of thing, while first philosophy has no domain restriction and studies being itself.

Why does Aristotle occasionally refer to the science of being as being as "theology" ($theologike$)?

It culminates in the study of the highest and most perfectly actual beings.

In Aristotle's framework, what is the specific focus of the science of physics?

Physics studies natural beings, specifically bodies in motion.

What is the primary subject of study for the mathematician according to Book IV?

Mathematics focuses on abstract quantities such as numbers and figures.

How does Aristotle respond to the concern that a science of "everything" is hopelessly vague?

He argues that universal truths, such as the four causes and the principle of non-contradiction, are the most precise and fundamental.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What must Aristotle overcome to prove that "being" is a unified concept suitable for a single science?

The objection that "being" is said in many different, unrelated senses.

Concept: Substance ($ousia$)

Definition: The primary category of being that exists in itself rather than in another.

How does a dependent category, such as quality or quantity, differ from a substance?

A dependent category exists only by belonging to or being in a substance.

Aristotle uses the term $pros\ hen$ equivocity, or "focal meaning," to describe what relationship?

The relationship where different senses of "being" all refer back to one primary center, which is substance.

Why is substance considered the "primary" sense of being?

All other kinds of being—such as qualities, quantities, and relations—depend on substances for their existence.

What would happen to qualities and relations if all substances were destroyed?

They would disappear because they have no independent existence outside of substances.
