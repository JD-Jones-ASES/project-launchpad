The Hierarchy of Cognition

Level. Name

Description

1. Sensation (*Aisthesis*)

The most basic form of cognition; grasping particular things in the present moment. Shared by humans and animals.

2. Memory (*Mneme*)

The ability to retain what sensation has experienced.

3. Experience (*Empeiria*)

The accumulation of memories into a practical familiarity with a specific domain. Distinguishes humans from most animals.

4. Art / Craft (*Techne*)

Knowledge of general causes underlying a domain of practice. This level is teachable because it understands the "why" behind the "that."

5. Science (*Episteme*)

Knowledge of necessary truths derived from their causes; grasping truths that could not be otherwise.

6. Wisdom (*Sophia*)

The highest form of theoretical knowledge; understanding the deepest reasons and first causes underlying all being.

Philosophy as a Cooperative Enterprise

Thales

sought the material cause.

Plato

sought the formal cause (though Aristotle critiques Plato for separating "Forms" from physical things).

Anaxagoras

sought the final cause.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Aisthesis

Sensation or perception; the most basic form of cognition.

Aitia

Cause, reason, or explanation; that which is responsible for something being the way it is.

Eidos

Form or shape; the organizing pattern or structure within a thing.

Empeiria

Experience; the accumulation of memories into practical familiarity.

Endoxa

The received opinions of thoughtful people or predecessors.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Episteme

Scientific or demonstrative knowledge; understanding necessary truths.

Eudaimonia

The final cause of human beings; flourishing or living well through virtue.

Hyle

Matter; the potential "stuff" out of which something is made.

Mneme

Memory; the capacity to retain sensory experiences.

Sophia

Wisdom; the highest form of theoretical knowledge regarding first causes and principles.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Techne

Craft, art, or technique; knowledge of the general cause underlying a domain, making it teachable.

Telos

The end, purpose, or goal toward which a thing aims (Final Cause).
