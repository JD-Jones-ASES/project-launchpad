Aristotle’s First Philosophy and the Nature of Wisdom

Study Slides

The Hierarchy of Knowledge

Sensation

The most primitive form of knowledge, common to all animals.

Memory

Arises from sensation in some animals, allowing for intelligence and the ability to be taught (particularly in those that can hear).

Experience

Formed when several memories of the same thing produce a single capacity for judgment. Experience deals with individuals (e.g., knowing that a specific treatment helped Socrates).

Art (Techne)

Arises when a universal judgment is produced from many experiences. Art deals with universals (e.g., knowing that a treatment helps all people of a certain constitution).

Science and Wisdom

The highest levels, focusing on the "why" and the first principles of all things.

The Four-Cause Framework

Cause. Type

Definition

Material Cause. Matter / Substratum

That out of which a thing comes to be (e.g., the bronze of a statue).

Formal Cause. Essence / Substance

The definition or shape that makes a thing what it is (the *ousia*).

Efficient Cause. Source of Change

The agent or process that brings the thing into being (e.g., the sculptor).

Final Cause. Purpose / Good

The *telos*, or the end for which a thing exists (e.g., the reason the statue was made).

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Art (Techne)

A stage of knowledge where universal judgments are formed from multiple experiences; characterized by an understanding of causes and the ability to teach.

Efficient Cause

The source of change or the agent that produces an effect; the third of Aristotle's four causes.

Experience (Empeiria)

Knowledge derived from multiple memories of the same thing, focused on individuals and the "that" rather than the "why."

Final Cause (Telos)

The purpose, end, or "the good" for which a thing is done or exists; the fourth of Aristotle's four causes.

Formal Cause

The essence, definition, or substance (*ousia*) of a thing; the "why" reducible to the definition.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Material Cause

The physical matter or substratum from which a thing is composed.

Sensation

The basic faculty of perception animals are born with; the foundation from which memory and higher knowledge grow.

Substance (Ousia)

The primary category of being; that which exists in itself rather than in another; often linked to the formal cause.

Universal

A judgment or principle that applies to an entire class of objects rather than a single individual.

Wisdom (Sophos)

The highest form of knowledge; the science that investigates the first principles and first causes of all things.
