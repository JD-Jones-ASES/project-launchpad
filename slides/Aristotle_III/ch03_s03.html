Study Slides

The Unity of Being and the Primacy of Substance

The Primary Sense

Substance is the fundamental kind of being upon which all other categories (qualities, accidents, processes) depend.

The Focus of Philosophy

Because all senses of being refer back to substance, the science of being as being is essentially the science of substance.

The Principle of Non-Contradiction (PNC)

Indemonstrability

The PNC cannot be proven through traditional demonstration because there is no more fundamental truth from which to derive it. Attempting to prove everything leads to an infinite regress.

The Law of Excluded Middle (LEM)

Truth and Falsity

To say of what is that it is not, or of what is not that it is, is false. To say of what is that it is, or of what is not that it is not, is true.

Metaphysical Implication

The LEM ensures that reality is determinate. Without it, things would hover in an indeterminate zone, making all statements simultaneously true and false.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

Which Islamic philosopher was known in the Latin West as "The Commentator" for his work on Aristotle?

Averroes (Ibn Rushd)

What title of honor did medieval European scholars give to Aristotle?

"The Philosopher"

In what city in Muslim Spain was the philosopher Averroes born?

Cordoba

Which specific work of Aristotle did Averroes interpret to help reintroduce Aristotelian philosophy to Christian thinkers?

The $Metaphysics$

What is the primary focus of the science that Aristotle describes as investigating "being as being"?

It asks about being itself and the attributes belonging to it solely because it exists.

How does the "first philosophy" differ from "special sciences" like physics or mathematics?

First philosophy is universal to all being, while special sciences restrict themselves to a particular region of reality.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What synonym does Aristotle use for "first philosophy" when discussing the highest beings?

Theology ($prote\ philosophia$)

What phrase did Aristotle use to describe the diverse senses of the word "is"?

"Being is said in many ways" ($to\ on\ legetai\ pollachos$).

According to Aristotle, what is the primary sense of being upon which all other kinds (like quality or quantity) depend?

Substance ($ousia$)

How are qualities, relations, and quantities related to substance in Aristotle’s metaphysics?

They are real only by belonging to, holding between, or being affections of a substance.

The Principle of Non-Contradiction states that the same attribute cannot both belong and not belong to the same subject at the same time and _____.

in the same respect

Why does Aristotle describe the Principle of Non-Contradiction as "non-hypothetical"?

It is a principle that one must already possess to understand anything that exists.
