Aristotle’s Metaphysics: Substance and the Doctrine of Hylomorphism

Study Slides

Hylomorphism: Form and Matter

Element. Description

Function

Matter (Hyle). The physical stuff or substratum.

The "out of which" a thing is made; passive and indeterminate.

Form (Eidos). The defining structure or pattern.

The "what it is to be"; provides identity, knowability, and definition.

Composite (Synolon). The "whole together."

The actual existing individual thing (e.g., a specific bronze statue).

Aristotle vs. Plato on Form

Plato

Forms are separate, eternal, and transcendent realities existing in a realm beyond the physical world.

Aristotle

Forms exist in things. The form of a horse is what makes a specific horse what it is, and this structure exists within the horse itself, not in a separate realm.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Composite (Synolon)

Literally "the whole together"; the individual entity formed by the combination of matter and form.

Essence (To ti en einai)

The "what it is to be" a thing; the formula or definition that states what a thing truly and necessarily is.

Form (Eidos)

The defining structure, pattern, or organizing principle that makes a thing the specific kind of thing it is. It is the primary sense of substance.

Hylomorphism

The philosophical doctrine that all physical substances are composed of both matter (hyle) and form (morphe).

Matter (Hyle)

The physical stuff or substratum out of which things are made; it is passive, indeterminate, and represents pure potentiality.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Prime Matter

Matter in its strictest philosophical sense, considered as pure potentiality with no form or determinate character whatsoever.

Soul

In Aristotelian psychology, the soul is the "form of the body," serving as the organizing principle that makes a living body active and alive.

Substance (Ousia)

The primary kind of being; the underlying reality upon which all other categories of being depend.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle, what is the primary question of metaphysics that has always been and will always be asked?

What is substance?

In Aristotle's categorizations of being, why are qualities and quantities considered secondary to substances?

They depend on substances for their existence.

What is the primary metaphysical task Aristotle pursues throughout Book VII of his work?

Determining the principle or cause of what makes something a substance.

What Greek term is used to denote 'substance' in Aristotle's metaphysics?

$Ousia$

In the investigation of substance, what is the 'substratum' or 'subject'?

The underlying matter that persists through change.

What is the meaning of the Greek phrase $to ti en einai$?

The essence, or the formula stating what a thing truly and necessarily is.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why does Aristotle disqualify universals from being primary substances?

They are properties shared by many things rather than being individual, particular entities.

Which two candidates for substance does Aristotle eventually narrow his debate down to?

Matter and form.

Why does Aristotle argue that matter alone cannot be substance in the primary sense?

Matter is indeterminate and lacks a definable character.

How does Aristotle define $eidos$ (form)?

The defining structure or pattern that makes a thing what it is.

What is the primary role of form in making an object knowable?

It provides the determinate character that can be defined and understood.

What is the definition of $hyle$ (matter)?

The physical stuff or substratum out of which things are made.
