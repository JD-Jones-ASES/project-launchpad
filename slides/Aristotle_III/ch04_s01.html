Hylomorphism: Form and Matter

Element. Description

Function

Matter (*Hyle*). The physical stuff or substratum.

The "out of which" a thing is made; passive and indeterminate.

Form (*Eidos*). The defining structure or pattern.

The "what it is to be"; provides identity, knowability, and definition.

Composite (*Synolon*). The "whole together."

The actual existing individual thing (e.g., a specific bronze statue).

Aristotle vs. Plato on Form

Plato

Forms are separate, eternal, and transcendent realities existing in a realm beyond the physical world.

Aristotle

Forms exist **in** things. The form of a horse is what makes a specific horse what it is, and this structure exists within the horse itself, not in a separate realm.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Composite (*Synolon*)

Literally "the whole together"; the individual entity formed by the combination of matter and form.

Essence (*To ti en einai*)

The "what it is to be" a thing; the formula or definition that states what a thing truly and necessarily is.

Form (*Eidos*)

The defining structure, pattern, or organizing principle that makes a thing the specific kind of thing it is. It is the primary sense of substance.

Hylomorphism

The philosophical doctrine that all physical substances are composed of both matter (*hyle*) and form (*morphe*).

Matter (*Hyle*)

The physical stuff or substratum out of which things are made; it is passive, indeterminate, and represents pure potentiality.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Prime Matter

Matter in its strictest philosophical sense, considered as pure potentiality with no form or determinate character whatsoever.

Soul

In Aristotelian psychology, the soul is the "form of the body," serving as the organizing principle that makes a living body active and alive.

Substance (*Ousia*)

The primary kind of being; the underlying reality upon which all other categories of being depend.
