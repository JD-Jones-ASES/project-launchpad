Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Descartes' Method of Radical Doubt

A systematic process of questioning and stripping away all beliefs that can possibly be doubted to find an indubitable foundation for knowledge.

Cogito Ergo Sum ("I think, therefore I am")

Descartes' foundational certainty, asserting that the very act of doubting or thinking proves the existence of the self as a thinking entity.

Res Cogitans (Thinking Thing)

Descartes' conclusion that the essential nature of the self is not the body, but a non-physical mind or thinking substance.

Wax Argument

An illustration used by Descartes to show that knowledge of the essential nature of things (like the extension of wax) comes from the intellect's grasp, not from mutable sensory perceptions.

Mind-Body Dualism

Descartes' theory that the mind (a thinking, non-extended substance) and the body (an extended, non-thinking substance) are distinct entities.
