The Cartesian Shift: Doubt and the Cogito

The Method of Doubt

Descartes’ procedure involved systematically rejecting any belief that could possibly be doubted. This was not for the sake of permanent skepticism, but to find an indubitable bedrock for knowledge.

The Evil Genius

To push doubt to its limit, Descartes hypothesized a "malicious genius" (evil genius) who might be manipulating his senses and even his mathematical reasoning.

The First Certainty

Descartes discovered that even if he is being deceived, he must exist to be deceived. This led to the fundamental principle: Cogito, ergo sum ("I think, therefore I am").

The Thomistic Synthesis: Reason and Revelation

Sacra Doctrina (Sacred Doctrine)

Aquinas argued that while natural philosophy is valuable, a higher wisdom—revealed by God—is necessary for human salvation.

The Necessity of Revelation

Aquinas maintained that revelation is required because truths reached through unaided reason take too long to discover, are reached only by a few, and are often mixed with error.

The Five Ways

Aquinas utilized Aristotelian metaphysics (specifically the concepts of motion, causation, and potentiality) to provide five logical demonstrations for the existence of God.

The Five Ways of Thomas Aquinas

Way. Argument Source

Summary of Logic

First Way. Motion

Whatever is in motion is put in motion by another; an infinite regress is impossible.

Second Way. Efficient Causation

Every effect has an efficient cause; a thing cannot cause itself; an infinite regress is impossible.

Third Way. Possibility and Necessity

Contingent things (possible not to be) cannot always exist. If everything were contingent, nothing would exist now.

Fourth Way. Gradation

Things are more or less good/true/noble relative to a maximum. The maximum of a genus is the cause of all in that genus.

Fifth Way. Governance (Teleology)

Unintelligent natural bodies act for an end. They must be directed by an intelligent being.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Arche

The principle, beginning, or first cause that Aristotle seeks in the Metaphysics.

Cogito, ergo sum

"I think, therefore I am." Descartes' first certainty, established in Meditation II, proving his existence through the act of thinking.

Efficient Cause

The agent or action that brings something into being; the only type of causation Descartes retained from the Aristotelian framework.

Evil Genius (malin génie)

A hypothetical, supremely powerful deceiver used by Descartes to test the limits of doubt regarding mathematics and reason.

Final Cause

The purpose or "end" for which a thing exists. This was a core part of Aristotle’s system but was rejected by Descartes.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Method of Doubt

Descartes' procedure of systematically rejecting any belief that could possibly be doubted to find a secure starting point for knowledge.

Quinque Viae (Five Ways)

The five logical demonstrations for the existence of God proposed by Thomas Aquinas in the Summa Theologica.

Sacra Doctrina

"Sacred doctrine"; knowledge of God received through divine revelation rather than natural reason alone.

Sed Contra

"On the contrary"; the section of a Summa article where Aquinas states the position he intends to defend, often using an authoritative text.

Respondeo

"I answer that"; the main body of Aquinas's answer in a Summa article, where he provides his detailed philosophical reasoning.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Aristotle's Metaphysics, wisdom is defined as the knowledge of _____.

first causes

How many causes did Aristotle identify as the complete framework for explaining the universe?

Four

What famous opening sentence of Aristotle's Metaphysics expresses his trust in human cognitive capacities?

"All men by nature desire to know."

In what year did René Descartes publish Meditations on First Philosophy?

1641

What was Descartes' primary dissatisfaction with the Aristotelian philosophy he was taught?

It could not certify what was true or arrive at genuine certainty.

According to Descartes, if knowledge is to be more than 'sophisticated opinion,' it must be grounded in what?

Something that cannot possibly be doubted.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Term: Method of Doubt

Definition: Descartes' procedure of systematically rejecting all beliefs that can be doubted to find a foundation of certainty.

In Meditation I, what extreme hypothetical figure does Descartes imagine might be making him wrong about everything?

An evil genius (malin génie).

What are the three categories of belief that Descartes subjects to doubt in the First Meditation?

The physical world, memory, and mathematics.

While Aristotle finds first principles by ascending from experience, Descartes finds them by doing what?

Descending into doubt until hitting bedrock certainty.

What is the 'bedrock' principle Descartes discovers in Meditation II?

The certainty of his own existence as a thinking thing.

The phrase "cogito, ergo sum" is translated as _____.

"I think, therefore I am."
