Important Terms (1/3)

Adventitious

Coming from an external source rather than being inherent; accidental. Used to describe the false authority that the detective's manner lent to the newspaper reports.

Pasquinade

A satire or lampoon, especially one posted in a public place. Nick fears the inquest testimony will be sensationalized.

Surmise

A supposition or guess formed without sufficient evidence. Every "surmise" about Gatsby is directed to Nick after the death.

Superfluous

Unnecessary, more than is needed. Nick calls his written request to Wolfsheim "superfluous," expecting him to come without prompting.

Defiance

Open resistance; bold disobedience. Nick feels a "scornful solidarity" and defiance toward all who abandon Gatsby.

Important Terms (2/3)

Ulster

A long, heavy overcoat originally from Ulster, Ireland. Henry C. Gatz wears one inappropriately in the warm September weather.

Elocution

The skill of clear and expressive speech. Listed in young Gatsby's self-improvement schedule.

Incessant

Never ceasing; constant. Used to describe the faint music and laughter Nick remembers from Gatsby's parties.

Incoherent

Lacking logical or meaningful connection. Nick calls Gatsby's abandoned mansion a "huge incoherent failure of a house."

Commensurate

Corresponding in size, extent, or degree; proportionate. Fitzgerald describes the continent as "something commensurate to his capacity for wonder."

Important Terms (3/3)

Orgastic

Fitzgerald's coinage (often emended to "orgiastic"), suggesting ecstatic or rapturous intensity. Describes the "orgastic future" that forever recedes before us.

Provincial

Relating to a province; unsophisticated. Nick describes his moral discomfort with Tom as "provincial squeamishness."
