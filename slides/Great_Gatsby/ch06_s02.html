Important Terms (1/2)

James Gatz

Gatsby's birth name, reflecting his humble beginnings in North Dakota.

Dan Cody

A self-made millionaire from the Nevada silver fields, Yukon gold rush, and Montana copper. He served as Gatsby's mentor and employer for five years, educating him in the ways of the wealthy.

Lake Superior

The body of water where seventeen-year-old James Gatz first encountered Dan Cody's yacht, marking the beginning of his transformation into Jay Gatsby.

St. Olaf College

The Minnesota college James Gatz briefly attended for two weeks, leaving because he felt it was indifferent to his "destiny" and he despised the janitorial work required to pay his way.

Ella Kaye

The woman who, through legal maneuvering, managed to claim Dan Cody's inheritance, preventing Gatsby from receiving the $25,000 Cody had left him.

Important Terms (2/2)

"Platonic Conception of Himself"

A phrase used by Fitzgerald to describe Gatsby's profound act of self-creation, where he rejected his actual origins to embrace an idealized, self-imagined identity.

Tuolomee

The name of Dan Cody's yacht, aboard which Gatsby spent five years learning about wealth and high society.
