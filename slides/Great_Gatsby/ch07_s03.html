Chapter 7, Section 3: The Hottest Day – Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The End of Gatsby's Trimalchio Persona

Gatsby abruptly stops his lavish parties, signaling a shift from his public pursuit of Daisy through grand displays to a more private, serious stage of their relationship.

Discretion and Secrecy

Gatsby dismisses his old servants to prevent gossip, underscoring the delicate and illicit nature of his renewed relationship with Daisy.

The Oppressive Heat as a Motif

The sweltering summer weather is a dominant presence, creating an atmosphere of discomfort, lethargy, and rising tension, mirroring the emotional climax unfolding between the characters.

The Confrontation at the Buchanan's

The luncheon serves as the setting for an open display of affection between Daisy and Gatsby, forcing Tom to confront the reality of their affair.

Daisy's Conflicted Nature

Daisy's actions—her public kiss, her comment about Gatsby looking "cool," and her struggle with the day's confusion—highlight her wavering loyalty and internal conflict between Gatsby and Tom.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Gatsby's Naivety Regarding Pammy

Gatsby's surprise at Daisy's daughter, Pammy, reveals his idealized vision of Daisy, which struggles to reconcile with her established life as a wife and mother.

"Her Voice Is Full of Money"

Nick's profound realization about Daisy's voice encapsulates her ultimate allure and the deep-seated materialism that defines her and her world.

Tom's Assertiveness and Possessiveness

Tom's attempts to control the situation, question Gatsby, and demand to drive Gatsby's car demonstrate his fierce possessiveness over Daisy and his desire to reassert dominance.

Important Terms (1/2)

Trimalchio

A character from Roman literature known for his extravagant feasts. Nick's reference to Gatsby's "career as Trimalchio" ending signifies the close of his period of ostentatious entertaining.

Caravansary

A large inn or hotel with a courtyard for travelers. Here, it metaphorically refers to Gatsby's bustling, party-filled house.

Dilatory

Slow to act or respond; intended to cause delay. Used to describe the new butler's grudging manner.

Harrowing

Acutely distressing or painful. Nick uses this to describe the intense scene Gatsby had previously outlined.

Shirtwaist

A woman's blouse, typically styled like a man's shirt.

Important Terms (2/2)

Commutation ticket

A ticket for multiple journeys, often for daily travel between home and work.

Gin Rickey

A popular alcoholic cocktail, often consumed in hot weather, made with gin, lime juice, and soda water. Symbolizes the era's decadence and the characters' attempts to cope with the heat.
