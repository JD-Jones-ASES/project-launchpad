Important Terms (2/2)

Ineffable gaudiness

Too great or extreme to be expressed in words, combined with showiness or tasteless extravagance. (Refers to the overwhelming, dream-like splendor of Gatsby's internal world)

Dan Cody

The wealthy copper magnate and yachtsman who became Gatsby's mentor and employer, introducing him to the world of affluence and luxury.

Ella Kaye

A journalist and companion to Dan Cody who, through legal maneuver, inherited Cody's millions, thereby depriving Gatsby of his legacy.

Pioneer debauchee

A dissolute or licentious person from the frontier era. (Describes Dan Cody, symbolizing the rough, unrestrained wealth of the American frontier)

To bring oneself into favor with someone by flattering or trying to please them. (Nick's attempt to please Jordan's aunt)
