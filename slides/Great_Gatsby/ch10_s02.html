Reading a Modern Novel: The Great Gatsby

Chapter 10, Section 2

Chapter 9: Literary Analysis

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• The final chapter lifts the novel from personal tragedy into a meditation on America itself

• Fitzgerald links Gatsby's green light to the Dutch sailors' first sight of Long Island — uniting private dream with national myth

• The green color connects two images: the green light at the end of Daisy's dock and the "fresh, green breast of the new world"

• Gatsby's greatness lies in his capacity for wonder; his doom lies in the nature of the dream itself — projection, idealization, the impossibility of repeating the past

• The final metaphor — boats against the current, borne ceaselessly into the past — universalizes the novel's verdict: we are all Gatsby

• The closing passage functions as an elegy: it mourns Gatsby, the American Dream, and the gap between promise and reality

Key Concepts (2/2)

• The denouement systematically strips away remaining illusion: Tom's guilt-free confession, Jordan's brittle farewell, the obscene word on the mansion steps

• Nick's decision to return to the Midwest is not triumph but admission — a moral education whose lesson is that the dream corrupts

• The novel's final word is "past" — both its subject and its verdict

Key Terms (1/2)

Elegy

A mournful poem or passage lamenting a loss; the novel's final pages function as an elegy for Gatsby, for the American Dream, and for a lost world of possibility

Extended metaphor

A comparison developed at length; the boats-against-the-current metaphor in the closing lines extends across the universal human experience of striving against time

Green light

The light at the end of Daisy's dock that Gatsby reaches toward in Chapter 1; in the closing passage it becomes a symbol of the receding American Dream itself

Dutch sailors

Symbol of the original, uncorrupted moment of American wonder — before the land was claimed, before desire calcified into ownership

Orgastic future

The ecstatic, always-receding quality of the dream; Fitzgerald's coinage captures both the intensity and the futility of the hope that drives Gatsby (and, the novel argues, America)

Key Terms (2/2)

Denouement

The final unraveling of the plot after the climax; Chapter 9's denouement strips away every remaining illusion, from Wolfsheim's cowardly refusal to Tom's guilt-free confession
