Key Terms (1/2)

Class performance

The way characters use dress, speech, gesture, and setting to project social identities that differ from their actual circumstances

Motif

A recurring element — image, object, or idea — that develops thematic meaning across a work; the motif of watching connects Eckleburg's eyes, Wilson's unknowing gaze, and Nick's narration

Irony

A contrast between appearance and reality; the apartment's Versailles tapestry and oversized furniture ironize Myrtle's pretensions to refinement

Juxtaposition

Strident

Loud and harsh; grating — used to describe the arguments that hold Nick in his chair as the evening degrades

Languid

Displaying a disinclination for physical exertion; slow and relaxed — used to describe Mrs. McKee
