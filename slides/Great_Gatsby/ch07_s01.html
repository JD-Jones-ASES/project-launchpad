Reading a Modern Novel: The Great Gatsby

Chapter 7, Section 1

Chapter 6: The Novel

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• Nick reveals Gatsby's true identity: born James Gatz to poor farm people in North Dakota

• At age seventeen, James Gatz reinvented himself as "Jay Gatsby" when he encountered millionaire Dan Cody's yacht on Lake Superior

• Gatsby served Cody for five years as steward, mate, secretary, and jailor — absorbing the manners and ambitions of wealth

• Ella Kaye cheated Gatsby out of his $25,000 inheritance when Cody died

• Tom Buchanan visits Gatsby's estate with Mr. Sloane and a woman; Gatsby is treated with condescension and not-so-subtle exclusion

• Tom and Daisy attend one of Gatsby's parties; Daisy is appalled by West Egg's vulgarity, Tom grows suspicious of Gatsby's background

Key Concepts (2/2)

• After the party, Gatsby reveals his impossible demand: Daisy must tell Tom she never loved him so they can return to Louisville and relive the past

• Nick warns that one cannot repeat the past; Gatsby responds incredulously, "Can't repeat the past? Why of course you can!"

Key Terms

James Gatz

Gatsby's real birth name; he legally changed it to Jay Gatsby at age seventeen

Dan Cody

A wealthy copper magnate and yachtsman who became young James Gatz's mentor; his name evokes the rough self-made wealth of the American frontier

Ella Kaye

A journalist and Dan Cody's companion who, through a legal maneuver, inherited Cody's millions after his death, depriving Gatsby of his $25,000 legacy

laudable

Deserving praise; used to describe the reporter's initiative in seeking Gatsby out

shiftless

Lacking ambition or initiative; how Fitzgerald describes Gatsby's parents to emphasize how completely he rejected his origins
