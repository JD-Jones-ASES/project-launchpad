Reading a Modern Novel: The Great Gatsby

Chapter 6, Section 1

Chapter 5: The Novel

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• Gatsby arranges an elaborate reunion with Daisy at Nick's cottage: flowers, lawn mowing, fine clothes

• The initial meeting is painfully awkward — Gatsby is pale and rigid, nearly knocks over a mantelpiece clock

• After Nick retreats into the rain for half an hour, the atmosphere transforms: Gatsby is radiant, Daisy tearful but joyful

• Gatsby tours Daisy through his mansion, revaluing every possession through her response to it

• Daisy weeps over Gatsby's beautiful shirts — an enigmatic emotional climax

• Gatsby points out the green light across the bay, recognizing that its symbolic power has diminished now that Daisy is physically present

Key Concepts (2/2)

• As Nick departs, a flicker of bewilderment crosses Gatsby's face — a hint that the real Daisy may not match his five-year dream

• Klipspringer, "the boarder," plays piano including the song "Ain't We Got Fun"

Key Terms

defunct mantelpiece clock

The broken clock at Nick's cottage that Gatsby nearly knocks over during the reunion; it symbolizes Gatsby's attempt to reverse or stop time

ecstatic

In a state of overwhelming happiness or rapture; used to describe Gatsby's transformed expression after the reunion succeeds

vestige

A trace or remnant of something disappearing; Nick uses it to describe the last traces of embarrassment vanishing between Gatsby and Daisy

inconceivable

Beyond what can be imagined; used to describe the pitch of intensity at which Gatsby has sustained his dream of Daisy

Klipspringer

Ewing Klipspringer, the "boarder" who has been living at Gatsby's mansion so long he seems to have no other home; plays piano at Gatsby's command
