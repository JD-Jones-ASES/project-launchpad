Key Concepts (2/2)

The Impossibility of Repeating the Past

Gatsby's adamant belief that "of course you can!" repeat the past clashes with Nick's more realistic perspective, underscoring the novel's central theme about the elusive nature of time and memory.

Important Terms (1/3)

Notoriety

The state of being famous or well known, typically for something negative or disreputable.

Insidious

Proceeding in a gradual, subtle way but with harmful effects.

Shiftless

Lacking ambition or purpose; lazy.

Meretricious

Apparently attractive but having in reality no value or integrity; showily but falsely alluring.

Turgid

Swollen and distended; also, of language, pompously inflated.

Important Terms (2/3)

Contingencies

Future events or circumstances that are possible but cannot be predicted with certainty.

Debauchee

A person given to excessive indulgence in sensual pleasures.

Ingratiate

To bring oneself into favor with someone by flattering or trying to please them.

Septic

Infected with harmful bacteria; figuratively, corrupted or poisoned.

Obtrusive

Noticeable in an unwelcome or intrusive way.

Important Terms (3/3)

Dilatory

Slow to act; causing delay.

Incarnation

The embodiment of a quality or idea in physical form.
