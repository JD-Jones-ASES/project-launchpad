Chapter 1: Literary Analysis

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Symbolic geography

East Egg (old wealth, inherited privilege) vs. West Egg (new wealth, lacking social pedigree) establishes the novel's central class tension

Key Terms

Unreliable narrator

A narrator whose credibility is compromised by bias, limited knowledge, or personal involvement — Nick is unreliable not because he lies, but because he selects, interprets, and arranges events

Symbolic geography

The use of physical settings as symbols — here, East Egg and West Egg embody old vs. new wealth and the class divisions of 1920s America

Green light

The recurring symbol of desire and unattainability first glimpsed at the end of Daisy's dock; associated with Gatsby's longing throughout the novel

Feigned

Simulated or pretended; not genuine — used of Nick's deliberate avoidance of unwanted confidences

Supercilious

Behaving as though one is superior to others; disdainfully proud — used to describe Tom Buchanan's manner
