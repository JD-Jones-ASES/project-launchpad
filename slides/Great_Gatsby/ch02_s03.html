Myrtle Wilson's Introduction and Character

Physicality

She is in her "middle thirties," "faintly stout," but carries her "surplus flesh sensuously." She has "no facet or gleam of beauty" in her face but possesses an "immediately perceptible vitality."

Interaction with George

She "walk[s] through her husband as if he were a ghost," demonstrating her disdain and disregard for him. Her voice is "soft, coarse" when speaking to him, and she gives him orders.

Interaction with Tom

She shakes Tom's hand, looking him "flush in the eye," showing an assertiveness and confidence absent with her husband. They quickly arrange a rendezvous in New York.

Tom's Deception

Tom reveals that George believes Myrtle visits her sister in New York, adding, "He's so dumb he doesn't know he's alive." This highlights Tom's casual cruelty and George's oblivious nature.
