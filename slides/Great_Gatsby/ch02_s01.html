Reading a Modern Novel: The Great Gatsby

Chapter 2, Section 1

Chapter 1: The Novel

Study Slides

Key Concepts

• Nick Carraway is the first-person narrator, a Yale-educated Midwesterner who moves to West Egg, Long Island to work in the bond business

• West Egg (new money, less fashionable) and East Egg (old money, established wealth) are separated by a "courtesy bay"

• Gatsby's mansion is a colossal imitation of a French hotel, located next door to Nick's modest bungalow

• Nick visits his cousin Daisy and her husband Tom Buchanan in East Egg, where he also meets Jordan Baker

• Tom has a mistress in New York; a phone call from her disrupts the dinner party

• The chapter ends with Nick glimpsing Gatsby alone on his lawn, arms outstretched toward a single green light across the bay

Key Terms

West Egg

The less fashionable of the two peninsulas on Long Island, home to the newly rich, including Nick and Gatsby

East Egg

The more fashionable peninsula, home to families with established wealth and social pedigree, including Tom and Daisy Buchanan

Green light

A small light at the end of Daisy's dock across the bay, toward which Gatsby reaches in the novel's final image of Chapter 1; associated with longing and unattainable desire

Bond business

The financial profession Nick has moved East to pursue, buying and selling investment securities

Privy

Having secret or private knowledge of something; admitted to a confidence
