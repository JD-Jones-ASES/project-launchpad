Chapter 3, Section 1

Chapter 2: The Novel

Study Slides

Key Concepts

• The valley of ashes is a desolate industrial wasteland between West Egg and New York, where ash-gray workers move through clouds of dust

• The eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg — a faded billboard advertisement for an oculist — brood over the valley like a godlike but indifferent witness

• Tom Buchanan takes Nick to Wilson's garage to meet his mistress, Myrtle Wilson, the wife of the garage owner George Wilson

• At the 158th Street apartment Tom keeps for the affair, a drunken party forms with Myrtle's sister Catherine and the McKees from downstairs

• Myrtle transforms her personality when she changes into a cream-colored chiffon dress, adopting affected mannerisms and airs of upper-class hauteur

• The party ends violently when Tom breaks Myrtle's nose after she repeatedly shouts Daisy's name

Key Terms (1/2)

Valley of ashes

The desolate industrial wasteland between West Egg and New York, symbolizing the dark underside of wealth and prosperity

Eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg

A faded billboard featuring enormous bespectacled eyes that brood over the valley of ashes; functions as a symbol of moral oversight — or its absence

Grotesque

Comically or repulsively ugly or distorted; used to describe the ash formations that take the shapes of houses, chimneys, and men

Vitality

The state of being strong, active, and full of energy; used to describe Myrtle Wilson's forceful presence when she enters

Hauteur

An air of superiority and disdain; the manner Myrtle adopts after changing into her cream-colored dress

Key Terms (2/2)

Contiguous

Sharing a common border; immediately adjacent — used to describe the garage's bleak surroundings
