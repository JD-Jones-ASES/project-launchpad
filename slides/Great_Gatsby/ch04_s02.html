Chapter 3: Literary Analysis

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Jordan Baker's dishonesty

— the moved golf ball, the borrowed car — extends the theme of performance beyond Gatsby; her "cool, insolent smile" is its own carefully maintained facade

Key Terms (1/2)

Performance / self-invention

The central theme of Chapter 3; Gatsby and other characters construct identities through careful selection of words, gestures, dress, and setting

Verisimilitude

The appearance of being real or true; the books in Gatsby's library are real (verisimilitude achieved) but unread (substance absent)

Belasco, David

A Broadway producer famous for obsessively realistic stage sets; the comparison suggests Gatsby's home is an impressive but fundamentally theatrical production

Spectroscopic

Here used figuratively to suggest intense, precise scrutiny — describing the East Egg guests guarding themselves against West Egg's "gayety"

Subterfuge

A deceptive stratagem or trick; Nick concludes Jordan began "dealing in subterfuges when she was very young" to maintain her cool advantage

Key Terms (2/2)

Cardinal virtue

A principal or fundamental virtue; Nick claims honesty as his own, though the novel has already complicated his reliability
