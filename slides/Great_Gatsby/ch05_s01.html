Reading a Modern Novel: The Great Gatsby

Chapter 5, Section 1

Chapter 4: The Novel

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

• Nick catalogs the vast and disreputable array of guests who attend Gatsby's parties

• Gatsby drives Nick to New York and delivers a rehearsed, implausible autobiography

• Physical evidence (a war medal, an Oxford photograph) momentarily convinces Nick of Gatsby's story

• Meyer Wolfshiem is introduced as a gambler who fixed the 1919 World Series — a clue to the criminal origins of Gatsby's wealth

• Jordan Baker reveals the backstory: Gatsby and Daisy fell in love in Louisville in 1917

• Daisy nearly refused to marry Tom Buchanan on their wedding eve but ultimately went through with it

Key Concepts (2/2)

• Gatsby has purchased his West Egg mansion specifically to be across the bay from Daisy

• Gatsby's request of Nick: invite Daisy to tea so Gatsby can "come over" from next door

Key Terms

knickerbocker

A member of the original Dutch settlers of New York; by extension, a person of established New York social standing

rajah

An Indian king or prince; used here to evoke a life of exotic luxury and extravagance

Montenegro

A small kingdom in southeastern Europe; Gatsby's medal from Montenegro is offered as proof of his wartime heroism

denizen

An inhabitant or occupant of a particular place; someone who frequents a specific location

Camp Taylor

A U.S. Army training camp near Louisville, Kentucky, where Gatsby was stationed in 1917
