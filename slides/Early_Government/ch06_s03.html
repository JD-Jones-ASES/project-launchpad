Important Terms (1/2)

governor (GUV-er-ner)

The head of a state's executive branch; like the “president” of the state.

lieutenant governor (loo-TEN-ant)

The second-highest official in the state executive branch; also presides over the Texas Senate.

biennial session (by-EE-nee-ul)

A legislative meeting that happens every two years rather than every year.

judiciary (joo-DISH-ee-air-ee)

The court system; the branch of government that interprets laws and settles disputes.

Attorney General

The state's top lawyer.

Important Terms (2/2)

Comptroller

The state official who manages the state's money.

Texas Senate

The upper chamber of the Texas Legislature, with 31 senators serving four-year terms.

Texas House of Representatives

The lower chamber of the Texas Legislature, with 150 members serving two-year terms.

civil cases

Legal disputes between people or organizations over money, contracts, or rights.

criminal cases

Legal disputes involving crimes against the law.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How are Texas judges selected, differing from federal judges?

Texas judges are elected by voters, while federal judges are appointed by the President.

What is a key difference in the frequency of legislative sessions between the U.S. Congress and the Texas Legislature?

The U.S. Congress meets annually, while the Texas Legislature meets in a biennial session (once every two years).

What is the primary role of the Texas Governor?

To carry out Texas laws and lead the state as the head of the executive branch.

What is the term length for the Governor of Texas?

Four-year terms, and they can be elected more than once.

Name two other significant executive officials in Texas besides the Governor and Lieutenant Governor.
