Key Concepts (2/2)

Texas in the Confederacy (1861–1865)

Texas seceded from the United States to join the Confederate States of America during the Civil War, contributing soldiers and resources to the Southern cause.

Texas as a U.S. State (1845–present)

Texas first joined the U.S. in 1845 and, after the Civil War, was fully readmitted in 1870, remaining one of the largest and most populous states today.

Flag 1: Spain (1519–1821)

Initial Claim

In 1519, Spanish explorer Hernán Cortés claimed Mexico for Spain, beginning a centuries-long Spanish presence in North America.

Expansion into Texas

Over the next 300 years, Spain gradually expanded its control northward into the region known today as Texas.

Establishment of Settlements

New Spain

For roughly three centuries, Texas was an integral part of New Spain, a vast Spanish colony stretching from Mexico to California.

Lasting Impact

Spanish rule deeply influenced Texas culture, leaving a legacy of the Spanish language, the Catholic faith, and Spanish legal traditions.

Flag 2: France (1685–1690)

French Exploration

French explorer René-Robert Cavelier, known as La Salle, aimed to establish a French presence in Texas.

Fort Saint Louis

In 1685, La Salle sailed to the Gulf of Mexico and built a small fort, Fort Saint Louis, on the Texas coast, claiming the land for France.

Short-Lived Colony

The French colony faced numerous difficulties and ultimately failed within a few years. Spain's response to the French intrusion helped solidify its own claims in Texas.

Word Origins: Texas

The word "Texas" (or "Tejas") comes from a Caddo word meaning "friends" or "allies," which Spanish explorers adopted to name the region.

Flag 3: Mexico (1821–1836)

Independence from Spain

In 1821, Mexico successfully gained its independence from Spain, and Texas became part of the new Mexican nation.

Encouraging Settlement

The Mexican government sought to populate its northern territories, including Texas, and offered large grants of land to attract settlers.

Empresarios

Land agents, known as empresarios, were hired to recruit families to move to Texas.

Declaration of Independence

In 1836, Texas declared its independence from Mexico.

Independent Nation

For nine years, Texas operated as an independent country, known as the Republic of Texas.

Flag 5: The Confederate States of America (1861–1865)

Joining the U.S.

In 1845, Texas was annexed by the United States and became the twenty-eighth state.

Secession and Civil War

With the outbreak of the American Civil War in 1861, Texas voted to secede from the Union and joined the Confederate States of America.

Contribution to the Confederacy

Texas provided thousands of soldiers and resources to the Confederate war effort.

Reunion

The Civil War concluded in 1865 with a Union victory, and Texas was subsequently reunited with the United States.

Flag 6: The United States of America (1845–present)

Current Status

Today, Texas remains one of the fifty states in the United States.

Readmission

After the Civil War, Texas met the requirements set by Congress and was fully readmitted to the Union in 1870.

Prominent State

Since then, Texas has grown to become one of the largest and most populous states in the country, maintaining its unique cultural identity shaped by its diverse history.
