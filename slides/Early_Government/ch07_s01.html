Chapter 7, Section 1 Study Guide: What Makes a Law?

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Law vs. Rules vs. Customs

It's essential to understand that not all expectations or guidelines are laws.

Sources of Law

Laws are made by different levels of government, each with its own jurisdiction.

Hierarchy of Law

This principle establishes the order of authority for legal documents, meaning a lower law cannot contradict a higher one. The U.S. Constitution is the supreme law of the land.

Characteristics of a Good Law

For laws to be considered just and fair, they should meet specific standards:

Important Terms

Law

A rule made by a government that everyone in its territory must follow; breaking a law can result in punishment.

Custom

A tradition or habit that many people follow, but which is not enforced by the government.

Statute

A law written down and officially passed by a legislature (can be federal or state).

Ordinance

A law made by a local government (city or county).

Justice

The quality of being fair and righteous; the administration of law in a fair way.
