Important Terms

A leader raised up in times of danger to defend the people. Unlike modern court judges, they were not permanent rulers but served when their community needed guidance or military leadership.

A solemn agreement or promise. In ancient Israelite context, the law given to the people was understood as a covenant, a sacred agreement between the people and God.

king

A hereditary ruler who holds supreme power for life within a kingdom and typically passes this power to a designated heir, usually a child or close relative.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What was the role of a 'judge' in ancient Israel?

A leader raised up in times of danger who led their people when needed, but was not a permanent ruler.

Define 'theocracy'.

A government led by religious figures or based on religious law, where 'God' (theos) 'rules' (kratos).

What is a 'covenant' in the context of ancient Israel?

A solemn agreement or promise. The law in ancient Israel was understood as a covenant between the people and God.

What defines a 'king'?

A hereditary ruler who holds power for life and passes it to an heir.

What was the source of law for the ancient Israelites after settling in Canaan, during the Period of the Judges?

Their law came from the Torah, the first five books of the Hebrew scriptures.

How did the judges of ancient Israel differ from modern court judges?

Ancient Israelite judges were military and spiritual leaders raised by God to defend the people in crises, not permanent legal officials.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Name two famous judges mentioned in the text.

Deborah (led to military victory), Gideon (defeated larger army with 300 warriors), or Samson (known for extraordinary strength).

During the Period of the Judges, how did people govern themselves in 'quiet times'?

They governed themselves through their tribes and families.

Why is Israel during the Period of the Judges considered a theocracy?

God was understood as the true ruler, and laws came from divine command rather than from a human king. Judges carried out God's will.

What request did the elders of Israel make to Samuel, and why?

They requested a king to lead them, 'like all the other nations'.

What specific warnings did Samuel give the people about having a king?

A king would take their sons for the army, daughters for servants, tax their fields and vineyards, and ultimately cause them distress.

Who was the first king of Israel, and who anointed him?

Saul, who was anointed by Samuel.
