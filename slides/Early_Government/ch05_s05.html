Important Terms

checks and balances

The system by which each branch of government has powers that allow it to limit the powers of the other two branches.

override

To defeat a presidential veto. This requires a two-thirds vote in both the House of Representatives and the Senate.

impeach

To formally charge a president, vice president, or federal judge with misconduct while in office. The House of Representatives brings the charges, and the Senate conducts the trial.

confirm

To formally approve. The Senate has the power to confirm the President's nominees for federal courts, Cabinet positions, and other high-level appointments.

judicial review

The power of the Supreme Court and lower federal courts to determine whether a law or government action violates the Constitution. If found unconstitutional, the law or action is nullified.

Real-World Examples of Checks and Balances

War Powers Resolution (1973)

Congress passed this resolution to limit the President's power to deploy military forces without Congressional approval, in response to presidential actions during the Vietnam War.

Supreme Court Decisions

The Supreme Court has, over time, struck down hundreds of federal and state laws that it deemed unconstitutional, impacting areas like civil rights, freedom of speech, and religious liberty.

Senate Rejection of Nominees

The Senate has on various occasions rejected presidential nominees for the Supreme Court, forcing Presidents to select alternative candidates.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of 'checks and balances'?

The system by which each branch of government has powers that allow it to limit the powers of the other two branches.

What does it mean to 'override' a presidential veto?

To defeat a presidential veto, which requires a two-thirds vote of both chambers of Congress.

What does it mean to 'impeach' an official, and which branches are involved?

To formally charge a president, vice president, or federal judge with misconduct while in office. The House impeaches, and the Senate holds the trial.

What does it mean for the Senate to 'confirm' a presidential nominee?

To formally approve the President's nominees for federal courts and Cabinet positions.

What is the primary purpose of the system of checks and balances?

To ensure that no single branch can dominate the others and to prevent tyranny by limiting each branch's power.

How can the Legislative Branch (Congress) check the Executive Branch (President) regarding legislation?

Congress can override a presidential veto with a two-thirds vote of both chambers.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does the Legislative Branch check the Executive Branch's appointments?

The Senate must confirm Cabinet appointments and federal judge nominations made by the President.

What is Congress's most serious check on a President or other federal official accused of misconduct?

Congress can impeach the President, Vice President, and other federal officials.

What exclusive power does Congress have regarding war?

Only Congress can declare war.

How does the Legislative Branch check the Judicial Branch's composition?

The Senate confirms Supreme Court justices and other federal judges nominated by the President.

How can Congress challenge Supreme Court decisions?

Congress can propose constitutional amendments to override court decisions.

What power does Congress have over the structure of the federal judiciary?

Congress creates (and could theoretically eliminate) lower federal courts.
