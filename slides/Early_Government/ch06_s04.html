Key Concepts (2/2)

Multiple Levels of Government

The United States operates with three interconnected levels of government—federal, state, and local—each with specific powers and responsibilities, all working together to serve the public.

Accessibility of Local Government

Local government is the most accessible level for citizens to engage with, offering direct opportunities for participation and influence.

Important Terms

County

A division of a state used to organize local government; Texas has more counties (254) than any other state.

Ordinance

A law made by a city or county government, applying only within that local jurisdiction.

Property Tax

A tax paid by individuals or businesses who own land or buildings, primarily used to fund local services like public schools and other municipal operations.

School Board

An elected group of citizens who volunteer to oversee and govern a public school district, making key decisions for its operation.

Mayor

The elected leader of a city government, often responsible for leading the city council or managing daily operations depending on the city's governmental structure.
