Important Terms (1/2)

President

The head of the executive branch of the U.S. government; the chief executive officer.

Commander-in-Chief

The President's specific role as the supreme military leader of all U.S. armed forces.

Cabinet

A body of presidential advisors, typically consisting of the heads of the major federal executive departments.

Veto

The constitutional power of the President to reject a bill passed by Congress, preventing it from becoming law unless overridden by a supermajority vote in both houses.

Key Powers of the President

Veto Bills

The President can reject legislation passed by both the House of Representatives and the Senate. A presidential veto can be overridden by a two-thirds majority vote in both chambers of Congress.

Pardon Criminals

The President has the power to grant pardons for federal crimes, forgiving individuals of their offenses and restoring their rights.

Nominate Judges

The President selects individuals to serve as federal judges, including all Supreme Court Justices. These nominations, however, require confirmation by the Senate.

George Washington's Enduring Precedent

Unanimous Election

George Washington was the only U.S. President ever to be elected unanimously by the Electoral College.

Voluntary Relinquishment of Power

After serving two terms, Washington voluntarily chose to step down from the presidency and return to his farm. Critically, no law at the time required him to leave office.
