Chapter 9, Section 2

How Citizens Participate

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Citizen Participation

Taking an active role in the community or government, rather than passively observing. A republic cannot function without active citizen involvement.

Informed Citizenry

The necessity for citizens to stay updated on government actions, community issues, and political candidates to make sound decisions, especially when voting.

Voting as a Right and Responsibility

Jury Duty as Civic Service

Serving on a jury is a direct and vital form of civic participation, upholding the justice system and ensuring fair trials, which is a key protection in America.

Obeying Laws and Paying Taxes

These are fundamental duties that ensure a safe, fair, and functional society by funding public services (schools, roads, defense) and protecting everyone's rights and property.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Peaceful Legal Change

While citizens have a duty to obey laws, they also have the right and means to work peacefully and legally (through voting, petitioning, advocacy) to change laws they believe are unjust.

Volunteering and Community Building

Civic participation extends beyond government to non-paid efforts that strengthen communities from the ground up, such as helping neighbors, cleaning parks, or assisting those in need.

Important Terms

participation

Taking an active role in the community or government, not just watching from the sidelines.

informed citizen

A citizen who stays up to date on what government is doing and what issues the community faces.

volunteer

A person who gives time and energy to help others without being paid.

jury duty

The responsibility of an eligible citizen to serve on a jury when called by the court.
