Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the definition of a dictatorship?

A government in which one person seizes and holds complete power, usually by force, without the consent of the people.

Define theocracy.

A government in which religious leaders rule, or in which laws are based on religious commands.

What does 'anarchy' mean?

The absence of government, characterized by no laws, no authority, and no order.

What is a 'coup'?

A sudden seizure of power, usually by a military leader or small group who overthrows the existing government.

How do dictators typically come to power?

They seize power by force or intimidation, rather than being chosen by the people.

What methods do dictators often use to maintain power?

They use propaganda (information designed to control what people think) and fear, and they control or shut down independent courts and free newspapers.

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to Aristotle, how did he define 'tyranny'?

Aristotle defined tyranny as one person ruling for personal gain rather than for the common good.

What is the primary source of law in a theocracy?

Law comes from religious texts and divine command rather than from elected officials.

What is a key characteristic of a strict theocracy regarding law?

There is no separation between religious law and civil law; they are the same thing.

What historical example of a theocracy is mentioned in the text?

Ancient Israel during the period of the judges, where law came from God and leaders were raised by divine calling.

Is anarchy typically a long-lasting or a brief state?

Anarchy is almost never a stable form of government; it is usually a brief, in-between state.

What often happens shortly after a period of anarchy?

The strongest person or group tends to take over by force, which usually leads very quickly to a new government, often a dictatorship.
