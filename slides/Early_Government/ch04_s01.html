Important Terms

colony (KOL-uh-nee)

A territory settled and governed by people from a distant country. The thirteen American colonies were settled by people from England and other European nations.

self-government

When a community makes its own political decisions rather than being governed by an outside power.

representative

A person chosen to speak and act on behalf of others. Colonial assemblies were early forms of representative government.

taxation without representation

Being required to pay taxes to a government in which you have no elected voice. This was a major colonial grievance against Britain and a central cause of the American Revolution.

The Foundations of Colonial Governance

Early Settlement (1607)

English settlers arrived in Virginia, facing the challenge of building a new life and establishing governance thousands of miles from the English king.

Development of Self-Reliance

The vast distance from England encouraged colonists to develop their own systems for making political decisions.

Formation of Thirteen Colonies

Over the next century, thirteen English colonies emerged along the Atlantic coast, from Massachusetts to Georgia.
