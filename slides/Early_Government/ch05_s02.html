Important Terms (1/2)

Congress

The legislative branch of the U.S. federal government, responsible for creating laws. It consists of two chambers: the Senate and the House of Representatives.

Senate

The upper chamber of Congress. Each state is represented by two senators, totaling 100 members.

House of Representatives

The lower chamber of Congress. The number of representatives from each state is determined by its population, totaling 435 members.

Bill

A proposal for a new law. To become law, a bill must pass both the House and the Senate and then be signed by the President.

Bicameral

A legislative body composed of two separate chambers or houses. The U.S. Congress is bicameral.

Important Terms (2/2)

Constituent

A person residing in a particular electoral district and represented by an elected official. Representatives must stay closely connected to the wants of their constituents.

Impeach

To formally charge a federal official with misconduct. The House of Representatives has the power to impeach, while the Senate conducts the trial.

A Bicameral Legislature

Meaning of Bicameral

The term "bicameral" means "two chambers."

Structure of Congress

Congress is divided into two chambers:

Reason for Bicameralism (The Great Compromise)

The House of Representatives

Number of Members

There are 435 members in the House of Representatives.

Basis of Representation

Each state's number of representatives is determined by its population.

Term Length

Representatives serve two-year terms.

Responsiveness to Constituents

Because they face re-election every two years, representatives are typically very responsive to the immediate concerns and desires of their constituents (the people they represent).

The Senate

Number of Members

There are 100 members in the Senate.

Basis of Representation

Each state, regardless of its size or population, sends exactly two senators.

Term Length

Senators serve six-year terms.

Independence and Long-Term Thinking

The longer term length provides senators with greater independence from short-term public opinion, allowing them to focus on long-term policy goals and national interests.

Staggered Elections

Roughly one-third of the Senate faces election every two years, ensuring continuity and preventing a complete turnover of the chamber in a single election cycle.
