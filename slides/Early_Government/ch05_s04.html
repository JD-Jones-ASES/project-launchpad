Important Terms (1/2)

Judicial review

The power of courts to examine laws and government actions and declare them unconstitutional if they violate the Constitution.

Unconstitutional

Going against the rules of the Constitution. An unconstitutional law is invalid and cannot be enforced.

Supreme Court

The highest court in the United States. Its decisions are final on questions of federal law and the Constitution.

Justice

A judge on the Supreme Court.

Chief Justice

The head judge of the Supreme Court.

Important Terms (2/2)

District Courts

The trial courts where most federal cases begin. There are 94 district courts across the country.

Courts of Appeals

Federal courts that hear appeals from district courts. There are 13 courts of appeals; they review whether the law was applied correctly, rather than holding new trials.

The Case

In Marbury v. Madison, Chief Justice John Marshall issued a ruling that forever changed the balance of power in the U.S. government.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is 'judicial review'?

The power of courts to examine laws and government actions and declare them unconstitutional if they violate the Constitution.

Define 'unconstitutional'.

Going against the rules of the Constitution. An unconstitutional law is invalid and cannot be enforced.

What is the Supreme Court?

The highest court in the United States. Its decisions are final on questions of federal law and the Constitution.

Who is a 'justice' on the Supreme Court?

A judge on the Supreme Court. The head judge is called the Chief Justice.

What central question did the young nation face in 1803 regarding a law and the Constitution?

If a law passed by Congress violated the Constitution, which one takes precedence—a regular law or the Constitution?

Which Chief Justice made the landmark decision that gave the judicial branch a new power in 1803?

Chief Justice John Marshall.

Key Concepts (2/2)

List the three layers of the federal court system.

District Courts, Courts of Appeals, and The Supreme Court.

What is the primary function of federal District Courts?

They are trial courts where most federal cases begin. There are 94 district courts across the country.

What is the role of the Courts of Appeals?

They hear appeals from district courts. They do not hold new trials but review whether the law was applied correctly. There are 13 courts of appeals.

How many members are on the Supreme Court, and what are their specific roles?

Nine members: one Chief Justice and eight Associate Justices.

Describe the process for appointing Supreme Court justices.

The President nominates them, and the Senate must confirm them.

Why do Supreme Court justices serve for life (or until they retire or are removed)?

Their lifetime tenure is meant to protect them from political pressure.
