Anacyclosis (Polybius's Cycle of Governments)

Polybius theorized that governments naturally decay and transform in a repeating cycle: monarchy to tyranny, aristocracy to oligarchy, and democracy to mob rule, eventually returning to monarchy.

Important Terms (1/2)

Classify

To sort things into groups based on their similarities. (e.g., Aristotle classified governments).

Aristocracy

Government by the "best and most qualified" citizens, ruling for the common good. (From Greek: *aristos* "best" + *kratos* "rule").

Oligarchy

Government by a small group of wealthy or powerful people, ruling for their own benefit. (From Greek: *oligos* "few" + *arkhein* "to rule").

Tyranny

Rule by one person for their own benefit, typically using fear and force. A tyrant holds power unjustly.

Monarchy

A form of government with a single head of state, usually a king or queen, ruling for the common good.

Important Terms (2/2)

Polity

Aristotle's term for a good form of government where many people rule for the common good, often blending elements of aristocracy and democracy.

Ochlocracy

Mob rule, a corrupt form of democracy where the unthinking crowd governs for its own immediate desires, often at the expense of minorities or justice.

Anacyclosis

Polybius's theory of a "rotating cycle" where governments naturally transition from one form to another, often decaying from good to corrupt forms. (From Greek: *ana-* "again" + *kyklos* "circle").

Mixed Constitution

A governmental system that combines elements of monarchy, aristocracy, and democracy to prevent the concentration of power and stabilize the state, as advocated by Polybius.

How it Works
