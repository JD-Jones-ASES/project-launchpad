Important Terms (1/2)

Independence

Freedom from control by another government. To be independent means to govern yourself.

Natural Rights

Rights that belong to all people simply because they are human. They are not granted by governments; they exist before governments do. Thinker John Locke identified life, liberty, and property as n...

Unalienable

Cannot be taken away or given away. The Declaration calls certain rights "unalienable" — even governments cannot remove them.

Consent of the Governed

The idea that governments get their power from the agreement of the people they rule. If a government loses that consent, the people have the right to change it.

Treason

The crime of betraying one's country, especially by attempting to overthrow its government. Signing the Declaration of Independence was considered treason by the British.

Important Terms (2/2)

Revolutionary War

The armed conflict (1775-1783) between Great Britain and its 13 American colonies, which resulted in the independence of the United States.
