Important Terms (1/2)

Mutual Fund

An investment vehicle that collects money from many investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of securities managed by professionals.

Net Asset Value (NAV)

The value per share of a mutual fund, calculated daily by dividing the fund's total assets minus liabilities by the number of outstanding shares.

Expense Ratio

The annual percentage fee charged by a mutual fund to cover its operating costs, automatically deducted from the fund's returns.

Diversification

The practice of investing in a variety of assets to reduce overall risk, a key benefit of mutual funds.

Professional Management

The oversight and investment decision-making provided by experienced portfolio managers for a mutual fund.

Important Terms (2/2)

Actively Managed Fund

A mutual fund where a fund manager or team makes ongoing decisions about buying and selling securities with the goal of outperforming a market index. These typically have higher expense ratios.

The Critical Cost: Expense Ratios

What is an Expense Ratio?

It is an annual fee, expressed as a percentage of your total investment, that the fund charges. For example, a 1% expense ratio means you pay $1 per year for every $100 invested.

Types of Mutual Funds

Stock Funds

Primarily invest in common stocks, aiming for capital appreciation.

Bond Funds

Focus on investing in various types of bonds, typically seeking income and capital preservation.

Balanced Funds

Maintain a mix of both stocks and bonds, attempting to balance growth potential with income and stability.

Sector Funds

Concentrate investments within a specific industry or economic sector (e.g., technology, healthcare, energy), offering potentially higher growth but also higher risk.

International Funds

Invest in companies based outside the investor's home country, providing exposure to global markets.
