Key Concepts (2/2)

Integrated Financial Plan

Saving and investing are not competing but work together. A healthy plan involves saving for immediate needs and emergencies, and investing for future, long-term goals.

Important Terms (1/2)

Saving

Keeping money in a safe, accessible place like a checking or savings account, where the risk of loss is extremely low.

Investing

Putting money into assets expected to grow in value or generate income over time, such as stocks, bonds, or real estate.

Risk

The possibility that an investment's actual return will differ from its expected return, including the possibility of losing money.

Return

The profit or income generated from an investment or savings account, usually expressed as a percentage of the initial amount.

Time Horizon

The amount of time an individual expects to hold an investment or needs access to saved funds.

Important Terms (2/2)

Assets

Economic resources owned by an individual or company with the expectation that they will provide future benefit. Examples in finance include stocks, bonds, and real estate.

Emergency Fund

A readily available reserve of cash, typically 3-6 months' worth of living expenses, set aside to cover unexpected costs without having to sell investments or go into debt.

The "When Do I Need This Money?" Rule

If you need the money within two years

**SAVE IT.** Keep it in a highly liquid, low-risk account. Short-term market fluctuations can be severe, and you cannot risk needing to withdraw money from investments during a downturn.

If you won't need the money for two years or more

**INVEST IT.** Over longer periods, market downturns are typically smoothed out by subsequent recoveries and long-term growth trends, allowing your money to benefit from higher returns.
