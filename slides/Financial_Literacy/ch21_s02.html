Key Insurance Terms: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Deductible

The initial sum you pay out of your own pocket for covered services before your insurance company starts contributing.

Copay (Copayment)

A fixed, small dollar amount paid for specific services (like a doctor's visit) at the time of service, which may apply even before you meet your deductible.

Coinsurance

A percentage of the cost of covered services that you are responsible for paying *after* your deductible has been met.

Coverage Limit

The maximum dollar amount your insurance company will pay for a specific covered loss or claim. If a claim exceeds this limit, you are responsible for the remainder.

Exclusion

A specific type of situation, condition, or event that your insurance policy explicitly states it will *not* cover.

Important Terms (1/2)

Deductible

The amount of money you must pay yourself before your insurance company will start paying for covered losses or services.

Copay (Copayment)

A predetermined, fixed fee that you pay for certain medical services (e.g., doctor's visit, prescription drug) at the time you receive them.

Coinsurance

The percentage of covered medical expenses you are responsible for paying after you have met your deductible, typically shared with your insurer (e.g., 20% for you, 80% for the insurer).

Out-of-Pocket Maximum (Out-of-Pocket Limit)

The maximum amount of money you are required to pay for covered healthcare services in a policy year. Once this limit is reached, your insurance plan pays 100% of all further covered costs.

Coverage Limit

The highest dollar amount an insurance company will pay for a particular type of loss, claim, or during the lifetime of the policy.

Important Terms (2/2)

Exclusion

A provision in an insurance policy that specifies situations, conditions, or services for which the policy will not provide coverage.

Premium

The amount of money an individual or business pays to an insurance company for coverage, typically paid monthly, quarterly, or annually.

Example Calculation: Health Insurance Out-of-Pocket

You pay

$3,000 (your out-of-pocket maximum).

Insurer pays

$15,000 (total bill) - $3,000 (your payment) = $12,000.
