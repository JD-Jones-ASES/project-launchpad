Chapter 12, Section 3: CDs and Money Market Accounts Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Certificates of Deposit (CDs)

A savings product that pays a fixed interest rate for money deposited for a specified term. The trade-off for higher interest is reduced liquidity due to early withdrawal penalties.

Fixed Interest Rate

With a CD, the interest rate is locked in at the time of opening, providing predictability but meaning you won't benefit if market rates rise.

Fixed Term

CDs have set durations (e.g., 3 months to 5 years). Longer terms generally offer higher rates.

Early Withdrawal Penalty

A significant drawback of CDs, where withdrawing funds before the term matures results in a penalty, often wiping out accrued interest.

FDIC/NCUA Insurance

Both CDs and MMAs at insured institutions are protected up to $250,000, ensuring the safety of your principal.

Key Concepts (2/2)

CD Laddering

A strategy where you split savings across multiple CDs with staggered maturity dates. This balances earning higher CD rates with regular access to portions of your funds as each CD matures.

Money Market Accounts (MMAs)

A type of deposit account offering higher interest rates than regular savings, often requiring a higher minimum balance, and typically imposing limits on monthly transactions.

Flexibility vs. Interest Rate Trade-off

The fundamental principle discussed, where sacrificing immediate access to funds (flexibility) generally leads to higher interest earnings.

A financial strategy involving splitting a sum of money into several Certificates of Deposit with different maturity dates to provide regular access to funds while maximizing interest earnings.

Money Market Account (MMA)

A type of deposit account that typically offers a higher interest rate than a regular savings account, requires a higher minimum balance, and may limit the number of transactions per month.

APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The effective annual rate of return taking into account the effect of compounding interest.
