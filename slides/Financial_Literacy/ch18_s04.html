Important Terms (1/2)

Index Fund

An investment fund that passively tracks a market index by holding all the securities within it.

Market Index

A predefined list of securities that represents a specific part of the market or economy.

S&P 500

A prominent market index tracking 500 of the largest U.S. publicly traded companies.

Expense Ratio

The annual fee charged by a fund, expressed as a percentage of the total assets invested in the fund.

Actively Managed Fund

An investment fund where professional managers try to outperform the market by actively selecting specific securities.

Important Terms (2/2)

Passively Managed Fund

An investment fund (like an index fund) that aims to match the performance of a specific market index rather than trying to beat it.

ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)

An investment fund that trades like a stock on an exchange throughout the day, typically designed to track an index.

NAV (Net Asset Value)

The price per share of a mutual fund, calculated once per day at market close, representing the total value of the fund's assets minus liabilities, divided by the number of shares outstanding.

Diversification

The strategy of investing in a variety of assets to reduce risk.
