Important Terms (1/2)

Principal (P)

The original amount of money borrowed from a lender.

Monthly Payment (M)

The fixed amount of money a borrower pays to the lender each month to repay the loan.

Number of Payments (n)

The total count of monthly payments that will be made over the entire duration of the loan. This is calculated by multiplying the loan term in years by 12.

Total Paid

The sum of all monthly payments made over the life of the loan. It is the full amount returned to the lender.

Total Interest

The extra amount of money paid to the lender beyond the principal amount. It is the cost of borrowing.

Important Terms (2/2)

Interest Rate (APR)

The annual percentage rate, representing the cost of borrowing money over a year, expressed as a percentage of the principal.

Loan Term

The duration or length of time, typically expressed in years or months, over which the borrower agrees to repay the loan.
