Reading a Pay Stub: Understanding Your Earnings and Deductions

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Pay Stub

An itemized statement of an employee's gross pay, deductions, and net pay for a specific pay period. It's a fundamental tool for understanding your income.

Gross Pay

The total amount of money an employee earns before any deductions are taken out. This is your income before taxes and other withholdings.

Net Pay

Also known as "take-home pay," this is the amount of money an employee receives after all required and voluntary deductions have been subtracted from gross pay.

Required Deductions

Payments that employers are legally obligated to withhold from an employee's gross pay. These include federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security tax, and Medicare tax.

Voluntary Deductions

Payments an employee chooses to have withheld from their gross pay for benefits or savings, such as health insurance premiums, retirement contributions, or charitable donations.

Important Terms (1/3)

Pay Stub

A document (paper or electronic) that details an employee's earnings, deductions, and net pay for a specific pay period.

Gross Pay

The total amount of money earned by an employee before any deductions are subtracted.

Net Pay

The amount of pay an employee receives after all deductions have been withheld from their gross pay.

Deductions

Amounts subtracted from an employee's gross pay.

Required Deductions

Deductions mandated by law, such as federal income tax, state income tax, Social Security, and Medicare.

Important Terms (2/3)

Voluntary Deductions

Deductions chosen by the employee, such as health insurance premiums, retirement plan contributions, or union dues.

Federal Income Tax

Tax levied by the U.S. federal government on individual income, withheld by employers based on estimates.

State Income Tax

Tax levied by most state governments on individual income, withheld by employers. (Note: some states have no state income tax.)

Social Security Tax

A federal tax that funds social insurance programs, including retirement, disability, and survivor benefits. It is 6.2% of gross pay, part of FICA.

Medicare Tax

A federal tax that funds health care for the elderly and disabled. It is 1.45% of gross pay, also part of FICA.

Important Terms (3/3)

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)

The U.S. federal law that mandates the Social Security and Medicare taxes.

Health Insurance Premium

The regular payment required to maintain health insurance coverage, often deducted from paychecks.

Pre-tax Deduction

A deduction that is subtracted from gross pay before federal and state income taxes are calculated, thereby reducing taxable income.

Year-to-Date (YTD)

A cumulative total of earnings and deductions from the beginning of the calendar year up to the current pay period.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a pay stub?

A pay stub is a document (paper or electronic) provided by an employer that itemizes exactly how an employee's gross pay was calculated and where every deduc...

Why is it important to read your pay stub regularly?

It is a fundamental money skill. If you never check it, you might miss an error that costs you real money.

What is 'gross pay'?

Gross pay is the total amount an employee earns before any deductions are taken out.

How is gross pay calculated for an hourly worker?

Gross pay is calculated by multiplying the hourly wage by the number of hours worked.

What are 'deductions' on a pay stub?

Deductions are amounts subtracted from an employee's gross pay, leading to their net pay.

What is the primary difference between 'required deductions' and 'voluntary deductions'?

Required deductions are mandated by law (e.g., taxes), while voluntary deductions are chosen by the employee (e.g., health insurance premiums).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is Federal Income Tax on a pay stub?

Federal Income Tax is an estimate of the income tax the federal government requires employers to withhold from an employee's pay.

What factors influence actual Federal Income Tax withholding?

Actual withholding depends on filing status, W-4 elections, and tax bracket calculations.

What is State Income Tax on a pay stub?

State Income Tax is a tax collected by most states based on an employee's income, withheld from their pay.

According to the text, how many states currently do not collect state income tax?

Nine states currently have no state income tax.

What is Social Security tax and what is its current rate, as stated in the text?

Social Security tax is a required deduction, part of FICA, with a tax rate of 6.2% of gross pay.

What is Medicare tax and what is its current rate, as stated in the text?

Medicare tax is a required deduction, also part of FICA, with a tax rate of 1.45% of gross pay.
