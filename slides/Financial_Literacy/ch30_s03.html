Faith-Based Giving

Important Terms (1/2)

Tithe

Derived from the Old English word for "tenth," referring to the practice of giving 10% of one's income, commonly associated with Christian tradition.

Tzedakah

A Hebrew term meaning "righteousness" or "justice," referring to the Jewish practice of charitable giving as a moral imperative.

Zakat

An Arabic term meaning "that which purifies," referring to the obligatory annual charitable contribution in Islam, calculated on a Muslim's accumulated net assets.

Nisab

In Islamic finance, the minimum threshold of wealth that a Muslim must possess for a full lunar year before they are obligated to pay Zakat.

Dāna

A Sanskrit and Pali term in Buddhism referring to the practice of generosity or giving, considered a spiritual path leading to merit and good karma.

Important Terms (2/2)

Philanthropy

From the ancient Greek words *philos* (loving) and *anthropos* (human), literally meaning "love of humanity"; the practice of giving money and time to improve human welfare.
