Chapter 18, Section 1

Understanding Stocks: Ownership in Public Companies

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Stocks as Ownership

A stock represents a fractional ownership stake in a company, making the buyer a shareholder. Companies issue stock to raise money for expansion without incurring debt.

Shareholder Rights

As a shareholder, you are entitled to a proportional share of any dividends paid, voting rights on significant company decisions, and a share of the company's assets if it is ever liquidated.

Two Ways to Profit

Stock Exchanges

Organized marketplaces (like the NYSE or NASDAQ) where buyers and sellers trade stocks, providing transparency, regulation, and liquidity.

Market Capitalization (Market Cap)

The total value of a company's outstanding shares, calculated by multiplying the current stock price by the number of shares outstanding. It indicates a company's size.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Calculating Stock Return

The total profit or loss from a stock investment, which includes both changes in stock price and any dividends received, expressed as a percentage of the initial investment.

Voting Rights

A vote on major company decisions, also proportional to the number of shares owned. This allows shareholders to influence the company's direction.

Asset Claim

A proportional share of the company's assets if the company is ever sold or liquidated.

Stock Exchanges

Definition

A **stock exchange** is an organized marketplace where stocks are bought and sold.

Function

Exchanges connect buyers and sellers, facilitating the trading process.

Benefits

Historical Context

Stock markets have a rich history, with the Amsterdam Stock Exchange, founded in 1602 to trade shares of the Dutch East India Company, often cited as the world's first formal stock market.
