FICA: Social Security and Medicare Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)

The law requiring contributions from workers and employers to fund Social Security and Medicare. These taxes are mandatory for W-2 employees.

Social Security Tax

A 6.2% tax on gross pay (up to an annual wage base) that funds benefits for retirement, disability, and survivors.

Employer Match for Social Security

Employers pay an additional 6.2% on behalf of employees, bringing the total contribution to 12.4% of gross pay.

Social Security Wage Base

The maximum amount of annual earnings subject to the Social Security tax (e.g., $168,600 for 2024). Earnings above this cap are not taxed for Social Security.

Medicare Tax

A 1.45% tax on *all* gross pay (no wage base limit) that funds health insurance for Americans aged 65 and older and those with certain disabilities.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Employer Match for Medicare

Employers pay an additional 1.45% on behalf of employees, making the total contribution 2.9% of gross pay.

Total FICA Deduction for Employees

The combined employee portion of Social Security (6.2%) and Medicare (1.45%) taxes, totaling 7.65% of gross pay.

Self-Employment Tax

Gig workers and self-employed individuals pay both the employee and employer portions of FICA taxes, totaling 15.3% (12.4% for Social Security + 2.9% for Medicare) on their net business income.

Additional Medicare Tax

A 0.9% employee-only tax on earnings above a certain threshold ($200,000 for individuals).

Important Terms (1/2)

FICA (Federal Insurance Contributions Act)

The U.S. federal law that mandates taxation to fund the Social Security and Medicare programs.

Social Security Tax

The portion of FICA taxes, currently 6.2% of gross pay (up to a wage base), dedicated to funding the Social Security program.

Medicare Tax

The portion of FICA taxes, currently 1.45% of gross pay (with no wage base limit), dedicated to funding the Medicare program.

Social Security Wage Base

The maximum amount of a taxpayer's earnings subject to the Social Security tax in a given calendar year.

Employer Match

The additional amount contributed by an employer on behalf of an employee to FICA taxes, matching the employee's contribution.

Important Terms (2/2)

Gross Pay

An employee's total earnings before any deductions are taken out.

Net Pay

An employee's earnings after all deductions (taxes, benefits, etc.) have been taken out; also known as "take-home pay."

W-2 Employee

An employee who receives a W-2 tax form from their employer, indicating that income and taxes have been withheld from their paychecks.

Self-Employment Tax

The FICA taxes (Social Security and Medicare) paid by self-employed individuals, covering both the employee and employer portions.

Additional Medicare Tax

An extra 0.9% Medicare tax imposed on high-income earners above specific thresholds.

Social Security Tax (1/2)

Retired workers

Monthly income after they stop working.

Disabled workers

Financial support if they become unable to work due to a disability.

Surviving family members

Benefits for spouses, children, or parents of deceased workers who contributed to the system.

Medicare Tax

Employee Rate

You, as the employee, pay 1.45% of your gross pay, deducted from your paycheck.

Employer Match

Your employer pays an *additional* 1.45% on your behalf. The total contribution to the Medicare system for you is 2.9% of your gross pay.

No Wage Base Cap

Unlike Social Security, Medicare tax applies to *all* earnings. There is no upper limit or cap on the amount of income subject to the 1.45% Medicare tax.
