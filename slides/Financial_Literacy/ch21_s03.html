Chapter 21, Section 3

Health Insurance Basics

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Financial Protection

Health insurance transforms potentially catastrophic medical bills into manageable out-of-pocket costs, safeguarding personal finances.

Sources of Coverage

Health insurance can be obtained through various channels, including employer-sponsored plans, government marketplaces, government programs like Medicaid and CHIP, or by staying on a parent's plan.

Plan Types

Major health insurance plans (HMO, PPO, HDHP) differ primarily in their network restrictions, referral requirements, and cost structures (premiums vs. deductibles).

COBRA Coverage

Key Concepts (2/2)

Risks of Being Uninsured

Going without health insurance exposes individuals to immense financial risk, as even minor medical emergencies can incur tens of thousands of dollars in debt, often leading to personal bankruptcy.

Important Terms (1/3)

Health Insurance

A type of insurance coverage that pays for medical and surgical expenses incurred by the insured.

Premium

The amount of money an individual or business pays for an insurance policy.

Employer-Sponsored Insurance

Health insurance offered by an employer to its employees, often with the employer covering a significant portion of the premium.

Health Insurance Marketplace (Exchange)

Government-regulated websites where individuals, families, and small businesses can compare and purchase health insurance plans.

Subsidies

Financial assistance provided by the government to help eligible individuals and families afford health insurance premiums and out-of-pocket costs.

Important Terms (2/3)

Open Enrollment Period

A specific window of time each year when individuals can sign up for health insurance or change plans through the Marketplace, or make changes to employer-sponsored plans.

Medicaid

A joint federal and state program that provides health coverage to millions of Americans, including low-income adults, children, pregnant women, elderly adults, and people with disabilities.

CHIP (Children's Health Insurance Program)

A federal-state program that provides low-cost health coverage to children in families who earn too much money to qualify for Medicaid but cannot afford private insurance.

HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)

A type of health insurance plan that limits coverage to care from doctors who work for or contract with the HMO. It generally requires referrals from a primary care physician (PCP) to see specialists.

Important Terms (3/3)

HDHP (High-Deductible Health Plan)

A health insurance plan with a higher deductible than a traditional insurance plan. Lower monthly premiums are typical.

Deductible

The amount of money you must pay out of your own pocket for medical expenses before your health insurance plan starts to pay.

Referral

A written order from a primary care doctor for a patient to see a specialist or get certain medical services.

Health Savings Account (HSA)

A tax-advantaged savings account available to individuals enrolled in a High-Deductible Health Plan, used for qualified medical expenses.

Health Savings Accounts (HSAs)

Rolls Over

Unlike Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs), HSA funds roll over from year to year; you don't lose unused money at the end of the plan year.

Portable

The account belongs to you, not your employer, meaning you keep it even if you change jobs.
