Important Terms (1/2)

Traditional (Brick-and-Mortar) Bank

A financial institution that operates with physical branch locations in addition to offering online and mobile services.

Online-Only Bank

A financial institution that exists solely on the internet and through mobile apps, without any physical branch locations.

Mobile Check Deposit

A feature in banking apps that allows users to deposit checks by taking a photograph of the check with their smartphone.

Credit Union

A nonprofit financial cooperative that is owned by its members (account holders) and provides banking services.

Important Terms (2/2)

NCUA (National Credit Union Administration)

A U.S. government agency that insures deposits at federal credit unions and many state-chartered credit unions, providing the same $250,000 coverage as the FDIC.

APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The effective annual rate of return on an investment or savings account, taking into account the effect of compounding interest.

Overdraft Fees

Fees charged by banks when an account holder spends more money than they have in their account, resulting in a negative balance.

What is FDIC?

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation (FDIC) is a U.S. government agency established to insure deposits at banks that are members of the FDIC.

Credit Unions and NCUA

Credit Unions

These are nonprofit financial cooperatives owned by their members (account holders). They generally offer similar accounts and services to banks, often with lower fees and better interest rates.

NCUA (National Credit Union Administration)

Deposits at credit unions are insured by the NCUA, which provides the same level of coverage as the FDIC: $250,000 per depositor, per credit union, per account ownership category.
