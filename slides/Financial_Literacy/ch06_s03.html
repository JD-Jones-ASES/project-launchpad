How the Progressive Tax System Works

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Progressive Tax System

A tax structure where the tax rate increases as an individual's income increases. This means higher earners pay a larger *percentage* of their income in taxes, not just more money overall.

Tax Brackets

Specific ranges of income levels to which different tax rates apply. In a progressive system, only the income that falls *within* a particular bracket is taxed at that bracket's specific rate.

Important Terms

Progressive Tax

A tax system in which individuals with higher incomes pay a higher percentage of their income in taxes. The U.S. federal income tax is an example.

Tax Bracket

A defined range of income levels to which a specific tax rate applies.

Marginal Tax Rate

The tax rate that applies to the next (or last) dollar of income an individual earns—it's the rate of the highest bracket their income reaches.

Effective Tax Rate

The percentage of an individual's total taxable income that is actually paid in federal taxes, calculated as total tax paid divided by total taxable income.

Marginal Tax Rate vs. Effective Tax Rate

Marginal Tax Rate

This is the rate applied to your *last* dollar of income, corresponding to the highest tax bracket your income reaches. It is the rate you would pay on any *additional* income earned.
