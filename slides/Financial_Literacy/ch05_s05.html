Pre-Tax Deductions and Your Complete Net Pay

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Pre-Tax Deductions

Voluntary deductions from your gross pay that occur before federal (and usually state) income taxes are calculated. They reduce your taxable income, leading to lower income tax payments.

Tax Savings Benefit

Because pre-tax deductions lower your taxable income, you effectively pay less in income tax. This makes the out-of-pocket cost of the benefit lower than its face value.

Common Pre-Tax Benefits

These include health insurance premiums, contributions to traditional 401(k) retirement accounts, Health Savings Accounts (HSAs), and Flexible Spending Accounts (FSAs).

Voluntary but Not Free

While these deductions are voluntary and financially smart due to tax savings, they still reduce your net (take-home) pay. It's essential to factor them into your budget.

Important Terms (1/2)

Pre-Tax Deduction

A voluntary amount subtracted from your gross pay *before* income taxes are calculated, thereby reducing your taxable income.

Taxable Income

The portion of your income that is subject to federal and state income taxes. Pre-tax deductions reduce this amount.

Health Insurance Premiums

The monthly cost for health coverage, often deducted pre-tax from an employee's paycheck.

Traditional 401(k)

A retirement savings plan offered by employers where contributions are made with pre-tax dollars, meaning taxes are deferred until retirement.

Health Savings Account (HSA)

A savings account that allows individuals enrolled in a high-deductible health plan to save pre-tax money for qualified medical expenses. The money can roll over year to year.

Gross Pay

The total amount of money an employee earns before any deductions or taxes are withheld.

Net Pay (Take-Home Pay)

The amount of money an employee receives after all deductions (taxes, insurance, retirement contributions, etc.) have been subtracted from their gross pay.
