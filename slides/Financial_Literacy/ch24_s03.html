Important Terms (1/2)

Coupon

A voucher or code offering a discount on a specific product or service.

Cash-Back Offer

A promotional program that returns a percentage of a purchase price to the consumer after the transaction.

Genuine Savings

A situation where a consumer truly saves money by meeting specific criteria: needing the item, getting the best price, and buying the appropriate quantity.

Coupon Trap

The phenomenon where using a coupon leads a consumer to spend more money overall than they would have without the coupon, often by encouraging unnecessary purchases or bulk buying.

Minimum Payout Threshold

The smallest accumulated amount of cash-back earnings required before a consumer can redeem their rewards.

Important Terms (2/2)

Price Anchoring

A cognitive bias used in marketing where a high initial price (the "anchor") is presented to make a subsequent, lower price seem more appealing, even if the lower price is not an extraordinary deal.

Fair Market Value

The true economic value of a product or service, determined by supply and demand, independent of promotional pricing or artificial discounts.

The Coupon Trap Explained

Scenario

Sarah normally buys one $3 snack per week. A coupon offers $1 off when she buys three.

The Trap

To use the coupon, she buys three packages for $9, gets $1 back, making her net cost $8.

The Reality

If she hadn't seen the coupon, she would have spent only $3 that week. By using the coupon, she spent $8 to "save" $1, making her $5 worse off and tying up money in snacks she won't eat for weeks.

The Rule

A coupon only saves money if it reduces the price of something you intended to buy, in the quantity you genuinely need.

Cash-Back Apps and Programs

Strategic Shopping Timing

Mattresses

Often cheapest in May and November.

TVs

Best deals usually appear around major sporting events (Super Bowl) or holiday sales (Black Friday).

Winter Clothing

Deepest discounts typically come in January–February as stores clear out seasonal inventory.

Cars

Often cheapest at the end of the month or quarter when salespeople are trying to meet quotas.
