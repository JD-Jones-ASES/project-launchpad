Building Your Emergency Fund: How Much to Save and How to Reach Your Goal

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Emergency Fund Purpose

An emergency fund is designed to cover only your most critical, non-negotiable expenses during a financial crisis, such as job loss, a medical emergency, or unexpected major repairs.

Your emergency fund should *not* cover optional or "want" expenses like dining out, entertainment, subscriptions, or new clothing, as these would typically be cut during an emergency.

This is determined by multiplying your total monthly essential expenses by your chosen number of months (N, typically 3 or 6).

Key Concepts (2/2)

Months to Build Calculation

Once you have your target, you can estimate how long it will take to reach it by dividing your Emergency Fund Target by the amount you can save per month.

Starter Emergency Fund

A smaller, more achievable initial goal (typically $500-$1,000). It's designed to cover common, minor financial surprises quickly, serving as a stepping stone to the larger 3-6 month target.

Automation of Savings

Setting up automatic transfers from your checking account to a dedicated savings account is a highly effective strategy to build your emergency fund consistently and without conscious effort.

Important Terms (1/2)

Emergency Fund

A dedicated sum of money set aside specifically to cover essential living expenses in the event of an unexpected financial hardship.

Essential Monthly Expenses

The minimum, non-negotiable costs required to live, including housing, utilities, basic food, necessary transportation, minimum debt payments, and essential insurance/communication.

Discretionary Spending

Optional expenses that are not critical for survival and can be cut back or eliminated during a financial crisis (e.g., entertainment, dining out, subscriptions).

Emergency Fund Target

The specific dollar amount you aim to save for your emergency fund, typically 3 to 6 months of essential expenses.

Starter Emergency Fund

A preliminary, smaller emergency fund, usually $500 to $1,000, intended to quickly cover minor unexpected costs and build saving momentum.

Important Terms (2/2)

Automation (of savings)

The process of setting up recurring, automatic transfers of money from one account (e.g., checking) to another (e.g., savings) without manual intervention.

Essential Expenses Only – Not Total Spending (1/2)

Housing

Rent or mortgage payments.

Utilities

Electric, water, gas/heat.

Basic Groceries

Food necessary for sustenance, not gourmet meals or frequent dining out.

Transportation

Fuel or transit passes for commuting to work or for job searching.

Minimum Required Debt Payments

The absolute minimum payment due on credit cards, loans, etc., to avoid penalties and maintain good standing (not necessarily paying extra).

Essential Expenses Only – Not Total Spending (2/2)

Health Insurance Premiums & Essential Prescriptions

Costs to maintain health coverage and any critical medications.

Phone

An essential service for communication, especially for job searching or emergencies.
