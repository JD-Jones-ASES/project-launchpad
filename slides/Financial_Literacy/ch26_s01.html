Chapter 26, Section 1: The Cost of Moving Out – Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Significant Upfront Costs

Moving into a rental property requires a substantial amount of cash before move-in, including various deposits and initial setup expenses, often equivalent to several months of rent.

Distinction Between Deposits and Rent

A **security deposit** is held by the landlord and returned (minus damages) at move-out, serving as protection against property damage or unpaid rent. It is separate from **first month's rent** and...

Hidden Costs of Furnishing and Moving

Beyond rent and deposits, new renters must budget for essential furnishings (bed, kitchenware, etc.) and the actual costs of moving (truck rental, packing supplies, potentially movers).

Ongoing Monthly Expenses

Rent is only one component of monthly housing costs. A comprehensive budget must also include utilities, renter's insurance, internet, groceries, household supplies, and transportation.

Importance of Saving (Sinking Fund)

Financial preparation is crucial. Creating a dedicated "moving out" sinking fund to save specifically for upfront costs can prevent debt and financial strain.

Key Concepts (2/2)

The 30% Rule of Affordability

A common guideline suggests that housing costs (including rent and utilities) should not exceed 30% of one's gross monthly income to maintain financial stability.

Important Terms (1/2)

Security Deposit

A sum of money, typically one to two months' rent, paid by a tenant to a landlord at the beginning of a lease. It is held to cover any damages beyond normal wear and tear or unpaid rent, and is usu...

First Month's Rent

The initial payment of rent for the first month of tenancy, typically due at or before move-in.

Last Month's Rent

A payment equal to one month's rent, sometimes required upfront by landlords in competitive markets, to cover the final month of tenancy.

Utility Deposits

Payments required by utility companies (electricity, gas, water) from customers without established credit history, held as security against unpaid bills.

Sinking Fund

A dedicated savings account or strategy where money is regularly set aside over time to meet a future large expense, such as moving costs.

Important Terms (2/2)

30% Rule (of Housing Affordability)

A financial guideline stating that a household's total housing costs (rent/mortgage, utilities) should not exceed 30% of its gross monthly income.

Gross Income

An individual's total earnings from all sources before any taxes or deductions are taken out.
