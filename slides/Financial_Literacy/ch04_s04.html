Key Concepts

Entrepreneurship

The process of starting and operating a business, taking on financial risk with the goal of earning a profit by identifying and solving a market need.

Smart Risk Management

Entrepreneurs don't act recklessly; they strategically mitigate risk by starting small, testing ideas, learning from failures, and scaling gradually.

Core Entrepreneurial Activities

A predictable sequence of steps involved in launching any business, including problem identification, solution creation, customer acquisition, value delivery, and financial management.

Important Terms

Entrepreneur

A person who starts and operates a business, taking on financial risk in exchange for the potential to earn a profit. Entrepreneurs identify a need in the market, create a product or service to mee...

Profit

The financial gain, especially the difference between the amount earned and the amount spent in buying, operating, or producing something.

Revenue

The total income generated by a business from its sales of goods or services, before any expenses are deducted.

Expenses

The costs incurred in the process of generating revenue, such as equipment, supplies, and marketing.

Self-Employment Tax

Taxes paid by individuals who work for themselves, covering Social Security and Medicare contributions.

Tips for Starting Small

Leverage existing skills

What are you good at?

Address personal problems

What challenges have you faced that others might also experience?

Meet community needs

What services are lacking in your local area?

Test on a small scale

Before committing significant resources, try out your idea with a small group of customers or in a limited capacity. This minimizes risk.
