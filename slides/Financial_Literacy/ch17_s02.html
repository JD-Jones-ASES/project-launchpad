Important Terms

Return

The profit or loss generated from an investment, typically expressed as a percentage of the initial investment over a specific period.

Volatility

A measure of how much an investment's price or value fluctuates over time. Higher volatility means greater price swings.

Risk-Return Trade-off

The fundamental principle in finance that states that higher potential returns are generally accompanied by higher levels of risk, and vice versa.

Average Annual Return

A simple calculation of an investment's average yearly return by adding up all annual returns and dividing by the number of years.

Rule of 72

A mathematical shortcut used to estimate the number of years required to double an investment at a given annual rate of return.

Historical Performance and the Importance of Averages

Cash/Savings Accounts

Typically offer the lowest returns but also the lowest risk.

Bonds

Generally offer moderate returns with moderate risk.

Stocks

Historically offer the highest returns but also come with the highest risk and volatility.

Understanding Investment Risk

2008-2009 Financial Crisis

The U.S. stock market experienced a dramatic fall of about 50%. This was a terrifying period for many investors.

2012 Recovery

Despite the severe drop, investors who remained in the market saw it recover and reach new highs. By 2020, the market had more than tripled from its 2009 low.

Early 2020 (Pandemic)

The market plummeted roughly 34% in just over a month. However, within six months, it had fully recovered and continued to reach new record highs.
