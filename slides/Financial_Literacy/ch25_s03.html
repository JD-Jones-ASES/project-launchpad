Efficient Graduation

Completing a bachelor's degree in four years or less by taking sufficient course loads, attending summer sessions, and declaring a major early, thereby avoiding extra tuition and living expenses.

Strategic Living Arrangements

Choosing to live off-campus and cook one's own meals, which can be considerably cheaper than on-campus dormitories and meal plans.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Employer Tuition Reimbursement

A benefit offered by some employers to help employees pay for higher education, often covering undergraduate or graduate degrees.

Military Education Benefits

Programs available through military service (active duty or reserves) that can cover significant portions of college tuition and living expenses.

Work-Study and Part-Time Employment

Earning money while enrolled in college to help cover personal expenses, books, and reduce the need for student loans.

Net Price vs. Sticker Price

The actual cost a student pays for college after all grants and scholarships are applied, which is the most important figure to compare, not the advertised "sticker price."

Important Terms (1/2)

Articulation Agreements

Formal agreements between community colleges and four-year universities that guarantee the transferability of credits for specific courses or programs, ensuring credits earned at one institution wi...

Dual Enrollment

Programs that allow high school students to take college-level courses, often at a reduced cost or for free, and earn both high school and college credit simultaneously.

Advanced Placement (AP) Exams

Standardized tests offered by the College Board that, with sufficiently high scores (typically 4 or 5), can earn students college credit or placement into higher-level courses.

College Level Examination Program (CLEP) Tests

Exams administered by the College Board that allow individuals to earn college credit for knowledge acquired outside of a traditional classroom setting, often at a low cost per test.

Net Price

The actual cost a student and their family will pay for one year of college after subtracting any grants, scholarships, and educational benefits from the "sticker price" (total cost of attendance).

Important Terms (2/2)

Sticker Price

The full, advertised cost of attendance for a college or university before any financial aid is applied, including tuition, fees, room, board, and other expenses.
