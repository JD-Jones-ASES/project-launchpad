Chapter 28, Section 1: Your Financial Snapshot Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Financial Snapshot

A clear, immediate picture of your current financial situation, designed to answer "where do I stand financially right now?".

Net Worth

The primary measure of your financial snapshot, representing the difference between your total assets and total liabilities.

Assets

Anything you own that has monetary value.

Liabilities

Any debt or financial obligation you owe to someone else.

Negative Net Worth

A common situation for young adults, especially those with student loans, indicating that total debts exceed total assets. It is not a sign of failure but a starting point.

Important Terms

Financial Snapshot

A summary of your current financial position at a specific point in time.

Net Worth

The value of all your assets minus all your liabilities; a measure of an individual's or company's financial health.

Asset

Any item or resource that an individual owns and that has economic value or future benefit.

Liability

A financial obligation or debt owed to another person or entity.

Defining and Calculating Net Worth

Total Assets

The sum of everything you own that has monetary value.

Total Liabilities

The sum of all your debts and financial obligations.

Understanding Assets (1/2)

Cash

Money you have on hand.

Checking and Savings Accounts

Balances in your bank accounts.

Vehicles

The current market value of your car, motorcycle, etc. (e.g., using Kelley Blue Book or similar valuation tools).

Valuables

Electronics (laptops, phones), musical instruments, jewelry, or other items you could sell.

Investments

Money held in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, or retirement accounts like a 401(k) or IRA.

Understanding Assets (2/2)

Money Owed to You

Any personal loans you've made to others that they are obligated to repay.

Understanding Liabilities

Credit Card Balances

The total amount you currently owe on your credit cards.

Car Loan Balance

The remaining amount you still need to pay on your vehicle loan.

Student Loan Balance

The total amount outstanding on your educational loans.

Personal Loans

Money borrowed from a bank, credit union, or private lender.

Money Owed to Family or Friends

Any informal loans you've taken from people you know.

Example: Alex's Net Worth Calculation

Net Worth = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
