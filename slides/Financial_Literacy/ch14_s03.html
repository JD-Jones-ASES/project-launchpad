Chapter 14, Section 3: Minimum Payments and the Debt Trap

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Minimum Payment Trap

Minimum payments are intentionally structured to keep cardholders in debt for extended periods, allowing credit card companies to maximize interest earned.

High Interest and Low Principal Reduction

When making minimum payments, a large portion of each payment goes towards interest, leaving only a tiny fraction to reduce the actual principal balance owed.

Compounding Effect of Interest

Because the principal reduces so slowly with minimum payments, interest continues to accrue on a relatively high balance, leading to a much larger total cost over time.

The Power of Fixed, Higher Payments

Paying more than the minimum, especially a consistent, higher fixed amount, drastically reduces the payoff time and the total interest paid.

Important Terms (1/2)

Minimum Payment

The smallest amount a credit card holder is required to pay each billing cycle to keep their account in good standing. Often calculated as a small percentage of the balance (e.g., 2%) or a fixed mi...

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The yearly rate of interest charged on outstanding credit card balances. This rate is usually divided by 12 to calculate the monthly interest rate.

Principal

The original amount of money borrowed or charged, not including interest or fees. Reducing the principal is key to paying off debt.

Interest

The cost of borrowing money, calculated as a percentage of the outstanding principal balance.

Debt Trap

A situation where an individual is caught in a cycle of debt, often due to high interest rates and low minimum payments, making it very difficult to pay off the principal.

Important Terms (2/2)

Amortization Calculator

A tool or formula used to determine the exact payment schedule for a loan, showing how much goes to principal and interest over time, and the total cost and time to pay off the debt.

The Reality of Minimum Payments

The Trap Explained

Credit card companies are legally obligated to disclose how long it will take to pay off a balance making only minimum payments. This number is often surprisingly long and expensive.
