Important Terms (1/2)

Revenue

The total income generated from sales of goods or services before any costs are deducted.

Fixed Costs

Business expenses that do not fluctuate with the level of production or sales.

Variable Costs

Business expenses that directly vary with the amount of goods or services produced or sold.

Total Costs

The combined sum of all fixed costs and all variable costs within a given period.

Profit

The financial amount remaining when a business's total costs are subtracted from its total revenue.

Important Terms (2/2)

Break-Even Point

The sales volume (in units or revenue) where a business's total income equals its total expenses, resulting in neither profit nor loss.

Contribution Margin

The per-unit amount that revenue exceeds variable costs, contributing to covering fixed costs and generating profit.

Thinking About Contribution Margin

Before you reach break-even

Each sale's contribution margin goes directly towards reducing your fixed cost deficit. You're slowly "paying off" your fixed expenses with each unit sold.

At break-even

The cumulative contribution margin from all units sold exactly equals your total fixed costs. All fixed costs are now covered.

After break-even

Every additional unit sold generates a profit equal to its contribution margin because the fixed costs have already been fully paid for by previous sales.

Key Concepts (1/2)

Define "revenue."

The total money a business takes in from selling goods or services before subtracting any costs. It's also known as gross sales or top-line income.

What is the formula for calculating revenue?

Revenue = Price per Unit × Units Sold

What are fixed costs, and how do they behave in relation to production volume?

Expenses that remain constant regardless of how much you produce or sell. They do not change with output.

Give two examples of fixed costs for a small business or side hustle.

Monthly website hosting fee, business insurance, equipment payments.

What are variable costs, and how do they behave in relation to production volume?

Expenses that change directly with the amount you produce or sell. More output leads to higher variable costs.

Give two examples of variable costs for a small business or side hustle.

Ingredients for each batch, packaging per item, gas per lawn mowed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the formula for calculating total costs?

Total Costs = Fixed Costs + Variable Costs

What is the fundamental formula for calculating profit?

Profit = Revenue - Total Costs

Define the "break-even point."

The minimum number of units (or sales) needed for revenue to exactly equal costs, meaning you are no longer losing money but have not yet made a profit.

Why is understanding the break-even point crucial for entrepreneurs?

It indicates the sales volume required to cover all costs, marking the transition from loss to potential profitability.

Define "contribution margin" and state its formula.

The amount each unit sold contributes toward covering fixed costs and then generating profit. Formula: Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit.

What is the formula for calculating break-even units?

Break-Even Units = Fixed Costs / (Price per Unit - Variable Cost per Unit)
