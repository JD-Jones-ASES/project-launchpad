Understanding Good Debt vs. Bad Debt

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Core Distinction

Not all debt is equal. The fundamental question to ask is: "Does this borrowing create value greater than its cost?"

Good Debt

Borrowing that is likely to increase your net worth or earning power. The return generated by the asset or skill acquired is expected to exceed the cost of the loan.

Bad Debt

Borrowing for purchases that depreciate (lose value) quickly, or for pure consumption, where you pay interest without gaining any lasting financial benefit.

Important Terms (1/2)

Good Debt

Borrowing used to acquire an asset or skill that is expected to increase in value or generate income greater than the cost of the debt.

Bad Debt

Borrowing used to purchase depreciating assets or for consumption, where the interest cost provides no financial return.

Net Worth

The total value of an individual's assets minus their liabilities (debts). Good debt aims to increase this.

Equity

Depreciate

To decrease in value over time. Assets like cars typically depreciate rapidly.

Important Terms (2/2)

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The annual rate charged for borrowing, expressed as a single percentage number that represents the actual yearly cost of funds over the term of a loan.

Upside Down (on a loan)

A situation where the amount owed on an asset (like a car) is greater than its current market value.
