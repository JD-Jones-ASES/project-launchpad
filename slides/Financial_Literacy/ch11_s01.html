Simple Interest: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Definition of Simple Interest

Simple interest is calculated solely on the original principal amount. The interest earned (or paid) each period remains constant because it doesn't earn interest on previously accumulated interest.

Principal ($P$)

This is the initial sum of money that is either deposited into an account or borrowed as a loan. It's the starting point for all interest calculations.

Interest Rate ($r$)

The percentage charged or earned on the principal per year. It is crucial to convert this percentage to a decimal before using it in any formula (e.g., 6% becomes 0.06).

Time ($t$)

The duration, measured in years, for which the money is deposited or borrowed. If time is given in months, it must be converted to a fraction of a year (e.g., 6 months = 0.5 years).

Simple Interest Formula ($I = Prt$)

This formula calculates the total amount of interest earned or paid over the specified time period.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Total Amount/Final Balance Formula ($A = P + I$ or $A = P(1 + rt)$)

This formula calculates the total value of the investment or loan at the end of the time period, including both the original principal and the accumulated simple interest.

Linear Growth

Simple interest results in a consistent increase in the total balance over time, as the same amount of interest is earned each period.

Important Terms (1/2)

Principal ($P$)

The original amount of money invested or borrowed.

Interest Rate ($r$)

The annual percentage charged on the principal, always converted to a decimal for calculations.

Time ($t$)

The duration for which the principal is invested or borrowed, expressed in years.

Interest ($I$)

The monetary amount earned or paid for the use of the principal.

Final Amount ($A$)

The total value of the principal plus the accumulated interest at the end of the term.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simple Interest

A method of calculating interest where interest is only computed on the initial principal amount.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the fundamental concept behind charging or earning interest?

It is essentially charging for the use of money over time.

Define "Principal ($P$)" in the context of interest.

The original amount of money deposited or borrowed; it is the starting amount before any interest is added.

Define "Interest rate ($r$)" as it applies to simple interest.

The percentage of the principal charged or earned per year. It must be converted to a decimal for use in formulas.

How do you convert a percentage interest rate to a decimal for use in a formula?

Divide the percentage by 100, or move the decimal point two places to the left (e.g., 6% = 0.06).

If an interest rate is 3.5%, what is its decimal equivalent for calculations?

0.035

What does "Time ($t$)" represent in simple interest calculations, and in what units must it be measured?

The length of time the money is deposited or borrowed, measured in years.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do you convert a time period given in months into years for simple interest calculations?

Divide the number of months by 12.

Explain the core characteristic of "simple interest."

Simple interest is calculated only on the original principal amount. The interest earned does not earn interest on itself.

What is the formula for calculating the simple interest earned ($I$)?

$I = P r t$ (Interest = Principal × Rate × Time)

What is the formula for calculating the final amount ($A$) in an account earning simple interest?

$A = P + I$ (Final Amount = Principal + Interest) or $A = P(1 + rt)$

What does the variable $P$ represent in the simple interest formula $I = Prt$?

Principal (the original deposit or loan amount, in dollars).

What does the variable $r$ represent in the simple interest formula $I = Prt$?

The annual interest rate as a decimal.
