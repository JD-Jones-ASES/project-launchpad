Finding and Fixing Spending Leaks: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Spending Leaks

Regular, recurring expenses, often small, that accumulate significantly over months or years, often without conscious awareness. They are distinct from large, obvious purchases.

The Compounding Effect

The principle that small, consistent expenses, when added up over time, result in a substantial total amount. This effect emphasizes the importance of addressing even minor spending habits.

Latte Factor

A term illustrating how small, habitual discretionary purchases (like a daily coffee) can add up to a significant annual sum. The point is not to eliminate pleasures but to make conscious spending ...

Subscription Creep

The gradual accumulation of monthly or annual recurring charges for services, many of which may be unused or forgotten, quietly drawing money from an account.

Impulse Buying

Unplanned purchases triggered by emotional states (e.g., stress, boredom) or external cues (e.g., online sales, social pressure), often leading to acquiring items not truly needed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Friction in Purchasing

Deliberately introducing a delay or barrier to a purchase decision, such as a 24-hour wait rule, to prevent impulse buys and encourage more thoughtful spending.

Annual Cost Evaluation

A critical method for assessing the true impact of spending leaks by multiplying monthly costs by 12, revealing the significant yearly financial burden.

Important Terms (1/2)

Spending Leak

A regular, recurring expense that drains money in small, often unnoticed amounts, accumulating significantly over time.

Latte Factor

A popular term for any small, habitual discretionary purchase whose cumulative cost over time becomes substantial. It emphasizes conscious spending choices.

Subscription Creep

The gradual accumulation of recurring monthly or annual charges for various services, many of which may be rarely or never used.

Impulse Buying

An unplanned purchase made spontaneously, often triggered by emotions or environmental cues, rather than a considered need.

Subscription Audit

The systematic review of bank and credit card statements to identify all recurring charges and evaluate their necessity and usage.

Important Terms (2/2)

Friction

In the context of spending, measures implemented to slow down or prevent impulsive purchasing decisions, such as a waiting period.

Retail Therapy

Shopping used as a temporary coping mechanism to alleviate stress, boredom, or negative emotions.
