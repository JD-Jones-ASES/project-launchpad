Important Terms (1/2)

Estate Planning

The process of preparing for the transfer of a person's assets after their death and for managing their financial and medical decisions if they become incapacitated.

Estate

All of a person's property and possessions, including money, investments, digital assets, and personal belongings.

Beneficiary

The person or entity designated to receive assets or benefits from an account, policy, or will upon the death of the account holder or policy owner.

Important Terms (2/2)

Power of Attorney

A legal document that gives one person (the "agent" or "attorney-in-fact") the power to act on behalf of another person (the "principal") in specified financial or legal matters.

401(k) / IRA

Life Insurance Policy

A contract where an insurer pays a lump sum payment to a beneficiary in return for premium payments, upon the death of the insured person.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What are the key benefits of naming a specific person as your beneficiary on a financial account?

The money passes directly to them, bypassing probate entirely, making the process immediate, private, and free.

In the 'Missing Beneficiary' example, what was the impact of Jordan not naming a beneficiary for his 401(k)?

What happens regarding financial accounts once a young adult turns 18?

Banks cannot legally discuss an 18-year-old's accounts with their parents without the young adult's explicit authorization.

What is the legal implication of not having a Power of Attorney once you turn 18 and become incapacitated?

No one—not even a parent or spouse—has automatic legal authority to manage your financial affairs without a Power of Attorney.
