Purchasing Power

This refers to the actual quantity of goods and services that a specific amount of money can buy. It's the real measure of money's value.

Inflation

A general, sustained increase in the price level of goods and services over time. When inflation occurs, the purchasing power of money decreases, meaning each dollar buys less than before.

Hyperinflation

An extreme, runaway form of inflation where prices rise so rapidly that money loses virtually all its value in a short period, leading to economic collapse and abandonment of the currency.

Important Terms

Legal Tender

Currency that, by federal law, must be accepted as payment for any debt.

Purchasing Power

The quantity of goods and services that a given amount of money can buy.

Inflation

A general, sustained increase in the price level of goods and services in an economy, leading to a decrease in purchasing power.

Hyperinflation

An extreme and rapid increase in the price level, causing money to lose its value at an accelerating rate.

Federal Reserve (The Fed)

The central banking system of the United States, tasked with managing the nation's money supply and monetary policy.

Legal Tender and Government Backing

Federal Law

U.S. currency is designated as legal tender by federal law. This means that if you owe a debt and offer to pay it in U.S. dollars, the creditor is legally required to accept it.

Government Authority

The full authority of the U.S. government—its laws, courts, and institutions—stands behind the currency. This reliability encourages widespread acceptance and use of the dollar.

The Role of Trust

Shared Agreement

Money functions as a form of social contract, where people accept it because they trust that others in society will also accept it for goods and services.

Currency Collapse

If this collective trust erodes, the currency can lose its value, even if legally backed by the government.

Inflation Defined

Purchasing Power Defined

The amount of goods and services a given amount of money can buy.

The Relationship

As inflation rises, the purchasing power of money falls. The same amount of money will buy fewer things than it did before.

Example

If a pizza costs $2.00 in 2010 and $3.50 today, inflation has eroded the dollar's purchasing power for pizza.

When Trust Collapses: Hyperinflation

Cause

Hyperinflation typically occurs when governments lose fiscal discipline and print excessive amounts of money without corresponding economic growth in goods and services.

Consequences

Money loses value almost instantly. Prices skyrocket, often doubling in days or even hours. People rush to spend money as soon as they receive it, leading to economic chaos.
