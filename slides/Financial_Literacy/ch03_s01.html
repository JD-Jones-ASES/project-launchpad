The SMART Goal Framework

An acronym representing five essential elements that make a goal clear, motivating, and actionable. It emerged from decades of research in psychology and business.

Specific (S)

A goal that clearly defines *what* you want to achieve, *how much* it costs, and *why* it matters to you. It answers the questions "What?", "How much?", and "Why?".

Measurable (M)

A goal with a quantifiable amount (dollar amount or quantity) that allows you to track progress and know when you've succeeded. It helps in identifying if adjustments are needed.

Achievable (A)

A goal that is realistic and attainable given your current income, expenses, and resources. It should be a "stretch" that requires effort, but not impossible, to avoid discouragement.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Relevant (R)

A goal that aligns with your personal values, priorities, and genuine desires. Motivation is higher for goals you truly care about, rather than those set by others or perceived obligations.

Time-Bound (T)

A goal that includes a specific deadline. This creates urgency, helps calculate the necessary monthly or weekly contributions, and makes the overall plan concrete and actionable.

Important Terms (1/2)

Financial Goal

A specific, written target for how you will earn, save, spend, or invest money, including a deadline for when you will reach it.

SMART Goal

A financial (or other) goal that is Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound.

Specific

In the context of SMART goals, refers to clearly defining what the goal is, its cost, and its purpose.

Measurable

In the context of SMART goals, refers to having a numerical value attached to the goal for tracking progress.

Achievable

In the context of SMART goals, refers to the goal being realistic and attainable given personal resources and circumstances.

Important Terms (2/2)

Relevant

In the context of SMART goals, refers to the goal aligning with one's personal values and priorities.

Time-bound

In the context of SMART goals, refers to the goal having a specific deadline for completion.

The SMART Goal in Action: From Vague to Concrete (1/2)

Starting Point (Wish)

"I want to save up for something nice."

Step 1 (Specific)

Define "something nice" as a $480 gaming setup.

Step 2 (Measurable)

Determine how to track progress—saving $80 per month.

Step 3 (Achievable)

Verify that saving $80/month is realistic based on income and expenses. (Confirmed as tight but doable).

Step 4 (Relevant)

Confirm that a reliable gaming setup is genuinely important for gaming with friends. (Confirmed relevant).

The SMART Goal in Action: From Vague to Concrete (2/2)

Step 5 (Time-Bound)

Calculate the timeline: $480 / $80/month = 6 months. Set a start date (Jan 1) and end date (June 30).
