Important Terms (1/2)

Business Plan

A written document describing a business's nature, sales and marketing strategy, financial background, and operational plan. For side hustles, it can be a simple, one-page document.

Target Market

The specific group of consumers or businesses most likely to want your product or service, and therefore, the group to whom you should direct your marketing efforts.

Revenue Model

The strategy a business uses to generate income. This defines what products or services will be sold, how they are priced, and how often they are purchased.

Startup Costs

The initial expenses required to establish a business, incurred before the first sale or revenue generation.

Ongoing Costs (Operating Costs)

Expenses that a business incurs regularly as a result of performing its normal operations, such as supplies, utilities, or marketing.

Important Terms (2/2)

Marketing

The process of communicating the value of a product or service to customers, for the purpose of selling that product or service. This includes advertising, selling, and delivering products to consu...

Validation

The process of testing a business idea or product with potential customers to confirm there is a demand and that people are willing to pay for it.

Referral

The act of directing a person to another for information, advice, or service. In business, it's when a satisfied customer recommends your business to others.

Example: Simple Business Plan for a Math Tutoring Service (1/2)

Idea

Math tutoring for middle school students (grades 6–8) struggling with pre-algebra and algebra.

Target Market

50+ families with middle schoolers within 5 miles; only 4–6 regular clients needed.

Revenue Model

$30/hour; minimum 1-hour sessions; 1–2 sessions per client per week.

Startup Costs

$0 (utilizing existing knowledge and client/own home).

Ongoing Costs

Printing/worksheets: $15/month.

Validate Before You Invest

Critical Step

Before purchasing equipment or committing significant resources, try to find your first customer.

The Litmus Test

If someone is willing to pay you, your business idea has market validation. If you can't find a single paying customer before spending money, spending money isn't likely to solve the underlying p...

Action

Talk to potential customers, gauge their interest, and try to secure a commitment or pre-order first.

The Power of Referrals

Most Effective Marketing

For student businesses and side hustles, word-of-mouth marketing through satisfied customers is incredibly powerful and cost-effective.

Strategy for Referrals

Impact

Referrals allow you to grow your client base and revenue without incurring additional marketing costs, as demonstrated by doubling a 5-client business to 10 clients.

Beware of Over-Planning

Common Mistake

A significant pitfall for new entrepreneurs is spending too much time perfecting a detailed business plan without ever interacting with potential customers.

Market Validation is Key

The market's response (i.e., whether customers are willing to pay) is the ultimate determinant of your plan's correctness, not the plan itself.

Action-Oriented Approach

Prioritize getting your first customer as quickly as possible. Use the insights gained from real customer interactions to refine your business plan and offerings iteratively.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of a business plan for a small side hustle or student business?

To provide a clear, concise outline answering six core questions, helping to get started without extensive detail.

How long should a business plan for a small side hustle typically be?

It can be as short as a one-page document.

List the six core questions that a simple business plan should answer.

The Idea, Target Market, Revenue Model, Startup Costs, Ongoing Costs, Marketing.

What critical information does 'The Idea' question seek to clarify in a business plan?

What specific problem the business solves, and for whom (the target beneficiary).

Provide an example of how to make 'The Idea' section of a business plan more specific.

Instead of 'I help people,' specify 'I mow lawns for homeowners who work weekdays and can't do it themselves.'

What does the 'Target Market' question in a business plan help an entrepreneur understand?

Who will buy from the business and the estimated number of potential customers available.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why is estimating the number of potential customers important when defining your target market?

It helps determine if your income goals are realistic and how many customers you need to acquire (e.g., reaching 2% of 500 households).

Explain what the 'Revenue Model' describes in a business plan.

How the business makes money, including the pricing structure (e.g., hourly rate, flat fee, monthly retainer).

Name three common types of revenue models mentioned in the text.

Hourly rate, flat fee per project, monthly retainer.

Define 'Startup Costs' in the context of a business plan.

Purchases or investments required before the business earns its first dollar of revenue.

How do 'Startup Costs' differ from 'Ongoing Costs'?

Startup costs are initial, one-time investments to get started, while ongoing costs are recurring expenses necessary for monthly operation.

What are 'Ongoing Costs' for a business?

Recurring expenses that a business incurs each month to operate, such as supplies, gas, packaging, platform fees, or insurance.
