Key Concepts (1/2)

Charitable Deduction

A reduction in your taxable income allowed by the IRS when you donate money or property to a qualified tax-exempt organization (typically a 501(c)(3)).

Giving as a Values Decision

The primary motivation for charitable giving should be personal values and the desire to support causes, rather than solely for tax benefits. Tax benefits are a potential bonus for those who itemize.

Calculating Tax Savings

If you itemize, your tax savings from a charitable donation are calculated by multiplying the donation amount by your marginal tax bracket rate. This effectively subsidizes a portion of your giving.

Important Terms (1/2)

Charitable Deduction

A specific amount subtracted from your adjusted gross income, reducing your taxable income, for donations made to qualifying charities.

Qualified Tax-Exempt Organization (501(c)(3))

An organization recognized by the IRS as eligible to receive tax-deductible contributions, often referred to by its tax code section.

Itemize Deductions

Standard Deduction

A fixed dollar amount that taxpayers can subtract from their adjusted gross income if they choose not to itemize deductions. The amount varies based on filing status and other factors.

Tax Bracket

A range of incomes taxed at a specific rate. Your marginal tax bracket rate determines the percentage of any additional income (or deduction) that affects your tax bill.

Important Terms (2/2)

Donor-Advised Fund (DAF)

A charitable giving account held at a public charity. You contribute assets to the DAF, receive an immediate tax deduction, and then recommend grants to qualified charities from the fund over time.

Charitable deduction rules and limits are subject to change by Congress. The information provided here is for educational guidance only and should not be considered tax advice.
