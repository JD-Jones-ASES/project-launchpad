Important Terms (1/2)

Gross Income

All income received from wages, salaries, tips, interest, dividends, rental income, business income, etc., before any deductions.

Adjustments (Above-the-Line Deductions)

Deductions that reduce gross income to arrive at AGI, such as student loan interest, traditional IRA contributions, or HSA contributions.

Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)

Gross income minus above-the-line adjustments. A key figure for many tax calculations.

Standard Deduction

A set amount allowed by the IRS that reduces taxable income. Taxpayers can choose this or itemize their deductions.

Itemized Deductions

Specific allowable expenses (like mortgage interest, state and local taxes, charitable contributions, medical expenses) that taxpayers can subtract from their AGI instead of taking the standard ded...

Important Terms (2/2)

Taxable Income

The portion of your income that is subject to federal income tax after all deductions have been applied.

Tax Brackets

The range of income amounts that are taxed at a specific rate. The U.S. tax system is progressive, meaning higher income levels are taxed at higher marginal rates.

Marginal Rate

The tax rate on the next dollar of income earned.

Effective Rate

The actual percentage of your total income paid in taxes.
