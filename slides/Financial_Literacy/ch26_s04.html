Are You Ready to Buy? A Crucial Checklist (1/2)

Stable Income & Job Security

You have a consistent income and confidence in your employment stability.

Good Credit Score

Your credit score is 680 or higher (720+ is even better for more favorable interest rates).

20% Down Payment Saved

You have saved at least 20% of the home's purchase price to minimize your loan amount and avoid Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI). If less than 20%, you have a deliberate plan for managing PMI.

Closing Costs Saved

You have an additional 2-5% of the purchase price specifically saved to cover closing costs.

Fully Funded Emergency Fund

You have a separate emergency fund containing 3-6 months' worth of living expenses, in addition to your down payment and closing cost savings.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What three main factors determine whether renting or buying is the 'right' choice for an individual?

Financial readiness, time horizon in the location, and the local housing market.

Name two advantages of renting over buying a home.

Flexibility (easy to move), no maintenance burden, lower upfront cost, preserved capital, and known monthly cost.

Name two advantages of buying a home over renting.

Builds equity, fixed principal and interest payments (with a fixed-rate mortgage), potential appreciation, freedom to modify, and stability.

What is the primary reason buying a home involves significant transaction costs?

Closing costs when buying, and realtor commission plus closing costs when selling (often 6-10% of the home's value).

What is a commonly used 'break-even estimate' for how long one needs to stay in a home for the advantages of ownership to outweigh transaction costs?

5–7 years.

If you plan to move in 2-3 years, is renting or buying generally the better financial choice?

Renting is usually the better financial choice due to significant transaction costs associated with buying and selling.
