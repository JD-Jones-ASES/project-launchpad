Important Terms (1/2)

Checking Account

A type of deposit account at a bank or credit union that allows for frequent deposits and withdrawals, designed for day-to-day spending.

Balance

The amount of money currently available in your checking account.

Debit Card

A payment card linked directly to your checking account, allowing you to make purchases or withdrawals where money is immediately deducted from your account.

Credit Card

A payment card that allows you to borrow money from a lender up to a certain limit, which you then pay back later, often with interest.

ATM (Automated Teller Machine)

An electronic machine that allows bank customers to complete basic transactions, such as cash withdrawals, deposits, and balance inquiries, without needing a human teller.

Important Terms (2/2)

PIN (Personal Identification Number)

A confidential four-digit code used with a debit card at ATMs and point-of-sale terminals to verify the cardholder's identity.

Routing Number

A nine-digit code that identifies your bank or credit union, typically found at the bottom left of a check.

Account Number

A unique number that identifies your specific checking account at your bank or credit union, typically found at the bottom center of a check.

Direct Deposit

An electronic transfer of funds, most commonly your paycheck, directly into your bank account without a paper check.

Overdraft

Occurs when you spend more money than you have available in your checking account, potentially leading to fees from your bank.

Function

A payment card directly linked to your checking account. When you use it, the purchase amount is taken immediately (or within 1-2 business days) from your available funds.

Debit vs. Credit Card

Key Concepts (2/2)

When writing a check, what is the significance of the 'Amount (words)' field?

The amount written out in words is considered the legal amount, which takes precedence if there is any discrepancy with the numerical amount.

On a paper check, where can you find your bank's ID number and your specific account number?

The routing number (your bank's ID) is the 9 digits at the bottom left, and your specific account number is at the bottom center.

What are the basic steps to withdraw cash using an ATM?

Insert your debit card, enter your PIN, select the amount of cash you want, and the machine dispenses the cash while deducting it from your account.

When might you incur a fee for using an ATM, and who might charge it?

You often incur a fee (typically $2-$5) when using an ATM that belongs to a bank other than your own. Both the ATM owner and your own bank might charge a fee.

What is Direct Deposit?

Direct deposit is an electronic transfer that sends your paycheck directly into your bank account on payday, eliminating the need for a physical paper check.

List two benefits of using direct deposit.

It is faster, safer, and many banks waive fees for accounts with regular direct deposit.
