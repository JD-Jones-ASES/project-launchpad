Pre-tax Contributions

Money contributed to a Traditional 401(k) is deducted from your paycheck before taxes are calculated, reducing your current taxable income.

Tax-Deferred Growth

Investments within a 401(k) grow without being taxed annually, allowing for faster compounding. Taxes are only paid when funds are withdrawn in retirement.

High Contribution Limits

401(k) plans have significantly higher annual contribution limits (e.g., $23,000) compared to IRAs, making them a primary retirement savings tool.

Employer Match

A contribution made by your employer to your 401(k) account, often contingent on your own contributions. This is considered "free money" and offers an immediate, significant return on investment.

Important Terms (1/2)

401(k) Plan

An employer-sponsored retirement savings account that allows employees to contribute a portion of their gross salary on a pre-tax basis, with investments growing tax-deferred until retirement.

Employer Match

Contributions made by an employer to an employee's 401(k) plan, typically a percentage of the employee's own contributions up to a certain limit (e.g., 50% of the first 6% of salary).

Vesting

The legal right an employee earns over time to the employer-contributed funds in their retirement account. It dictates when those funds fully become the employee's property.

Cliff Vesting

A vesting schedule where an employee becomes 100% vested in employer contributions after a specific period of employment (e.g., 3 years), but owns nothing before that period.

Graded Vesting

A vesting schedule where an employee gradually gains ownership of employer contributions over a period of time (e.g., 20% per year for 5 years).

Important Terms (2/2)

Roth 401(k)

A type of 401(k) plan where contributions are made with after-tax dollars, and all qualified withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free.

Vesting: When Employer Money Becomes Yours

The Catch

While your own 401(k) contributions are always 100% yours immediately, employer contributions may be subject to a **vesting schedule**.

Definition

Vesting is the process by which you earn full ownership of the funds your employer contributes.

Impact

If you leave the company before you are fully vested, you may forfeit some or all of the employer's contributions.

Common Vesting Schedules

Important

Always check your employer's vesting schedule before making job changes to avoid losing valuable employer contributions.
