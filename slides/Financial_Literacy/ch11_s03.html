Chapter 11, Section 3

Understanding APY vs. APR

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Annual Percentage Rate (APR)

This is the nominal, or stated, annual interest rate advertised by banks. It does not factor in how frequently interest is compounded within the year.

Annual Percentage Yield (APY)

This is the effective annual rate of return or cost, reflecting the true impact of compounding interest. APY always equals or exceeds the APR, becoming higher as compounding frequency increases.

Compounding Frequency

Refers to how often interest is calculated and added to the principal balance over a year. More frequent compounding (e.g., monthly vs. annually) leads to a greater difference between APY and APR.

Fair Comparison

APY provides a standardized measure for comparing different financial products, especially savings accounts, because it reveals the actual return considering all compounding effects.

APR for Loans

While APR on deposits represents what you earn, APR on loans (like credit cards or mortgages) represents what you pay. For loans, more frequent compounding means borrowers end up paying more interest.

Important Terms

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The stated annual interest rate, expressed as a percentage, *not* accounting for the effect of compounding within the year. Also known as the nominal rate or stated rate.

APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The effective annual rate of return on a deposit account, or the effective cost of a loan, reflecting the actual impact of compounding interest. APY is always equal to or greater than APR.

Nominal Rate

An alternative term for APR, referring to the stated interest rate before considering compounding frequency.

Stated Rate

Another term for APR, highlighting that it is the initial rate quoted by a financial institution.

Compounding Periods per Year (n)

The number of times interest is calculated and added to the principal balance in a single year. For example, monthly compounding means n=12.

APR: The Stated Rate

Definition

APR stands for Annual Percentage Rate. It represents the basic, stated interest rate a financial institution advertises *before* considering the impact of compounding.

Other Names

APR is often referred to as the "nominal rate" or the "stated rate" because it's the initial figure quoted.

What it doesn't show

It doesn't tell you the *actual* amount of interest you'll earn or pay if compounding occurs more than once a year.

APY: The Real Rate

Definition

APY stands for Annual Percentage Yield. This is the more accurate measure as it reflects the *effective* annual rate of return you actually earn on a deposit, or the actual cost you pay on a loan, ...

Relationship to APR

APY is always equal to or greater than APR.

Impact of Compounding Frequency

The more frequently interest is compounded within a year, the larger the difference between the APY and the APR.

Why APY Is the Fair Comparison

Account X

5.00% APR, compounded monthly (`n = 12`)

Account Y

5.05% APR, compounded annually (`n = 1`)
