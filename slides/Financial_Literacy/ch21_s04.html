Auto and Renters/Homeowners Insurance Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Mandatory Insurance

Auto insurance is legally required in most states, and homeowners insurance is typically required by mortgage lenders.

Deductibles

Key Concepts (2/2)

Premiums

The regular payments you make to an insurance company for coverage.

Bundling/Multi-Policy Discounts

Saving money by purchasing multiple insurance policies (e.g., auto and homeowners) from the same company.

Risks of Uninsured Driving

Driving without auto insurance can lead to significant out-of-pocket expenses for damages and injuries, legal penalties, and financial ruin.

Important Terms (1/3)

Auto Insurance

A contract between you and an insurance company that protects you against financial loss in the event of an accident or theft involving your vehicle.

Liability Coverage (Auto)

Insurance that pays for damages and injuries you cause to other people and their property in an accident. It does not cover damage to your own car.

Liability Limits

The maximum amount an insurance company will pay for liability claims, typically expressed as three numbers (e.g., $100/300/100 for per person bodily injury/per accident bodily injury/per accident ...

Collision Coverage

Insurance that pays for damage to your vehicle when it collides with another car or object, regardless of who is at fault.

Comprehensive Coverage

Insurance that pays for damage to your vehicle from events other than a collision, such as theft, vandalism, fire, flooding, hail, or hitting an animal.

Important Terms (2/3)

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM)

Coverage that protects you and your passengers if you are involved in an accident with a driver who has no insurance or not enough insurance to cover your costs.

Rental Reimbursement

Optional auto insurance coverage that pays for the cost of a rental car while your vehicle is being repaired after a covered claim.

Roadside Assistance

Optional auto insurance coverage that provides services like towing, flat tire changes, and lockout assistance.

Renters Insurance

Insurance that protects the personal belongings of a renter, provides liability coverage for incidents in the rented space, and may cover additional living expenses.

Personal Property

Your movable belongings, such as furniture, electronics, clothing, and other possessions.

Important Terms (3/3)

Additional Living Expenses

Coverage in renters or homeowners insurance that pays for temporary housing and food costs if your insured residence becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event.

Homeowners Insurance

Insurance that covers the physical structure of a home, personal property inside it, liability for injuries on the property, and additional living expenses for the homeowner.

Deductible

The amount of money you must pay out of your own pocket before your insurance company starts to pay for a covered loss.

Premium

The amount of money an individual or business must pay for an insurance policy.

Bundling / Multi-policy discount

A discount offered by insurance companies when a customer purchases multiple types of insurance policies (e.g., auto and homeowners) from the same provider.

Additional Coverages (Optional, but often Recommended):

Uninsured/Underinsured Motorist (UM/UIM)

Highly recommended, this coverage protects you and your passengers if you are injured or your vehicle is damaged by a driver who has no insurance or insufficient insurance to cover your costs.

Rental Reimbursement

Covers the cost of a rental car while your vehicle is being repaired after a covered claim, ensuring you have transportation.

Roadside Assistance

Provides help for common roadside issues like towing, flat tire changes, battery jump-starts, and lockout services.

What Renters Insurance Covers:

Personal Property

Protects your belongings (furniture, electronics, clothing, etc.) against damage or theft from covered events such as fire, smoke, burst pipes, windstorms, and vandalism.

Liability

Provides financial protection if someone is injured in your apartment and sues you. It can cover legal costs and any damages awarded.

Additional Living Expenses

If your apartment becomes uninhabitable due to a covered event (like a fire), the policy may cover the costs of a hotel, meals, and other temporary living expenses.

What Homeowners Insurance Covers:

The Structure of Your Home

Protects the physical dwelling and attached structures (like garages) from damage due to covered perils (e.g., fire, storms, vandalism). This is the key difference from renters insurance.

Personal Property

Covers your belongings inside the home, similar to renters insurance.

Liability

Provides protection if someone is injured on your property and you are found legally responsible.

Additional Living Expenses

Covers temporary living costs if your home becomes uninhabitable after a covered event.

Saving on Insurance Premiums (Money Tip)

Bundling

One of the easiest ways to save is by bundling multiple policies (e.g., auto and homeowners insurance) with the same insurance company, often resulting in a 5–15% discount.

Raising Your Deductible

If you can afford to pay a higher deductible out-of-pocket in the event of a claim, your annual premiums can be significantly lower. Always ensure your emergency fund can cover your chosen deductible.
