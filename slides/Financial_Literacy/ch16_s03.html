Key Concepts (1/2)

What critical concept should every car buyer understand before financing a car?

Cars lose value the moment you drive them off the lot.

Define depreciation in the context of assets.

Depreciation is the loss in value of an asset over time.

Name common types of consumer goods that typically depreciate in value.

Most cars, electronics, and consumer goods.

How much value does a new car typically lose in its first year of ownership?

Approximately 20% of its original value.

What causes a brand new car to immediately lose value the instant it leaves the dealership lot?

It is no longer considered 'new' and becomes a 'used' car.

After the first year, at what approximate rate do cars continue to depreciate annually for the next 2-5 years?

Approximately 15% per year of its current market value.

Key Concepts (2/2)

If a new car is purchased for $25,000, what is its estimated value after one year, assuming typical depreciation?

$20,000 (20% loss).

What factors beyond age and use can influence a car's actual depreciation rate?

Make, model, mileage, and overall condition.

What is an 'upside-down loan' (or being 'underwater')?

It occurs when the remaining balance on your loan is greater than the current market value of the asset, meaning you owe more than it's worth.

Explain the 'dangerous gap' mentioned in the context of car financing.

It's the difference that can develop between what you owe on your car loan and the car's actual market value, potentially leading to an upside-down loan.

How can rapid depreciation lead to an upside-down car loan, especially with no down payment?

What is the financial consequence if your car is totaled in an accident while you have an upside-down loan?

Your insurance will only pay the car's market value, which is less than your loan balance, leaving you responsible for paying the difference out of pocket.
