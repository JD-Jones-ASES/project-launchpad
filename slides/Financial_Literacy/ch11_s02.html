Important Terms (1/2)

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on both the original principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Each time interest is added, the new, larger balance becomes the basis for the next interest c...

Simple Interest

Interest calculated only on the original principal amount of a deposit or loan.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money deposited, invested, or borrowed.

Annual Interest Rate (r)

The percentage rate at which interest is earned or charged per year, always expressed as a decimal in financial formulas.

Compounding Periods per Year (n)

The number of times per year that interest is calculated and added to the principal balance. Common frequencies include annually (n=1), monthly (n=12), and daily (n=365).

Important Terms (2/2)

Time in Years (t)

The duration, in years, over which the principal is invested or borrowed.

Final Amount (A)

The total value of the investment or loan at the end of the specified time period, including the original principal and all accumulated interest.

Exponent

A mathematical notation indicating the number of times a base number is to be multiplied by itself. For example, in $x^y$, $y$ is the exponent, meaning $x$ is multiplied by itself $y$ times.

Write down the compound interest formula.

A = P (1 + r/n)^(nt)

In the compound interest formula, what does 'A' represent?

'A' represents the final amount (the original principal plus all accumulated interest).

In the compound interest formula, what does 'P' represent?

'P' represents the principal (the starting amount or initial investment).

In the compound interest formula, what does 'r' represent, and in what format should it be used?

'r' represents the annual interest rate, and it must be expressed as a decimal (e.g., 6% becomes 0.06).

In the compound interest formula, what does 'n' represent, and what are its common values?

'n' represents the number of compounding periods per year. Common values include 1 for annual, 12 for monthly, and 365 for daily.
