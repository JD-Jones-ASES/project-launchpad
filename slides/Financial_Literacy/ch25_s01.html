The True Cost of College: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Key Concepts (1/2)

True Cost of College

This encompasses all direct and indirect expenses associated with attending college, not just tuition. It provides a more realistic financial picture than simply looking at the sticker price.

Direct Costs

Measurable expenses such as tuition and fees, room and board, books and supplies, transportation, and personal expenses.

Important Terms (1/2)

Tuition and Fees

The primary charges for instruction and general services (e.g., student activities, technology, health) levied by an educational institution.

Room and Board

Costs associated with housing (dormitory) and meal plans provided by the college.

Books and Supplies

Expenses for textbooks, course materials, and other academic necessities.

Transportation

Costs related to commuting to and from school, local travel, and any car-related expenses during the academic year.

Personal Expenses

Discretionary spending on items such as clothing, toiletries, entertainment, phone plans, and subscriptions.

Important Terms (2/2)

Opportunity Cost

The potential benefits an individual misses out on when choosing one alternative over another. In education, it's typically the foregone income from not working.

Sticker Price

The publicly listed, total cost of attendance for a college before any financial aid or discounts are applied.

Net Price

The final cost of college for a student after all scholarships, grants, and other aid that does not need to be repaid are deducted from the sticker price.

Endowment

Financial assets or property donated to an institution (like a university) that are invested to provide a permanent stream of income, often used to fund scholarships and financial aid.
