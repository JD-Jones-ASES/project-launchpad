Important Terms (1/2)

Time Value of Money

The principle that a dollar available today is worth more than a dollar available in the future, because today's dollar can be invested and grow over time.

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest from previous periods, leading to exponential growth.

Retirement Account

A special type of savings or investment account created by the federal government to encourage retirement saving by offering significant tax advantages.

Traditional IRA (Individual Retirement Account)

A retirement account offering tax-deferred growth, meaning contributions might be tax-deductible, and taxes are paid when money is withdrawn in retirement.

Roth IRA

A retirement account offering tax-free growth, meaning contributions are made with after-tax money, but qualified withdrawals in retirement are completely tax-free. Often recommended for young people.

The Critical Importance of Early Retirement Saving

Counter-Intuitive Truth

While it might seem logical to delay retirement saving until later in life (e.g., age 40), the financial reality is that starting early (e.g., age 18) is immensely more beneficial.

Dramatic Impact

The difference between starting early and starting later is not slight; it's significant enough to be "life-changing."

The Time Value of Money (Revisited)

Foundation of Growth

The concept of money growing through compound interest is fundamental to retirement savings.

Definition of TVM

Preview: What $6,000/Year Becomes

Example Calculation (Hypothetical)

Contributing $6,000 annually from age 18 to 65 (47 years) with an 8% average annual return yields approximately $2,717,000.

Power of Compounding

Out of the nearly $2.72 million, only $282,000 would be the investor's direct contributions. The vast majority – over $2.43 million – is pure compound growth.

Real World Example: The $1 Million Mistake (Jordan and Sam)

Scenario

Jordan's Outcome

Sam's Outcome
