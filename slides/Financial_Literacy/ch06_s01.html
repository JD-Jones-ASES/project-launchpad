Types of Income: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Income Diversity

Money received comes from various sources and is treated differently by law, especially for taxes.

Earned Income

Compensation received for performing work or services, requiring active effort. It is the most common type of income for students.

Unearned Income

Income received from sources other than work, primarily from assets or investments, and does not require active ongoing effort.

Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

A government benefit designed for lower-income workers, which specifically requires earned income for eligibility. Unearned income does not qualify for EITC.

Important Terms (1/4)

Earned Income

Compensation received for performing work or services; includes wages, salaries, tips, commissions, and net earnings from self-employment.

Wages

Hourly pay received from an employer for work performed.

Salary

A fixed annual amount paid to an employee, typically divided into regular paychecks, regardless of the exact hours worked.

Tips

Extra money received directly from customers for service rendered.

Commissions

A percentage of sales earned by an individual, common in sales professions.

Important Terms (2/4)

Self-employment Income

Profit earned from operating one's own business, freelance work, or gig economy jobs.

Unearned Income

Income received from sources other than active work or employment, such as interest, dividends, rental income, and capital gains.

Interest

Money paid to an individual by a bank or financial institution for keeping money in savings accounts, certificates of deposit, or bonds.

Dividends

Payments made by a company to its shareholders from its profits.

Rental Income

Money received from tenants for the use of property owned by the landlord.

Important Terms (3/4)

Capital Gains

Profit realized from selling an asset (like stocks, real estate, or collectibles) for more than its original purchase price.

Long-term Capital Gains

Profits from selling assets that have been held for more than one year, often taxed at preferential, lower rates.

Ordinary Income Tax Rates

The standard tax rates applied to most types of earned income and some unearned income, structured into various tax brackets.

Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

A refundable tax credit for low-to-moderate-income working individuals and families, requiring earned income for eligibility.

APY (Annual Percentage Yield)

The real rate of return earned on a savings deposit or investment, taking into account the effect of compounding interest.

Important Terms (4/4)

Form 1099-INT

A tax form sent by banks to report interest income earned by an individual to both the taxpayer and the IRS.

Gifts

Money or property transferred from one person to another without expectation of repayment; generally not taxable income to the recipient.

Inheritances

Money or property received upon the death of another person; generally not taxable income to the recipient.

The Importance of Good Record Keeping

Reporting Requirements

Financial institutions like banks and brokerages are legally required to report certain types of income (like interest and dividends) to both you and the IRS.

Accuracy

Your tax return needs to accurately reflect all income reported by these institutions to the IRS to avoid discrepancies and potential issues. Being organized ensures you report everything correctly.
