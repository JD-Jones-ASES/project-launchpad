CHAPTER 27, SECTION 1: THE ENTREPRENEURIAL MINDSET

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Entrepreneurship as a Mindset

More than just having a business idea, it's a distinct way of thinking focused on identifying problems and creating solutions that people value.

Problem-Solving and Value Creation

The core of any successful business lies in recognizing an "unmet need" and then developing an offering that people are willing to pay for because it solves their problem or adds value to their lives.

Calculated Risk Tolerance

Persistence and Learning from Failure

Success rarely happens overnight. Entrepreneurs must be resilient, continuously iterate on their ideas, and view setbacks as valuable lessons rather than reasons to quit.

Resourcefulness

This trait involves making the most of existing assets—personal skills, tools, and social networks—rather than waiting for perfect conditions or significant capital investment.

Important Terms (1/2)

Entrepreneur

An individual who creates a new business, taking on most of the risks and enjoying most of the rewards. They typically identify an unmet need and develop a way to meet it.

Entrepreneurial Mindset

A specific way of thinking that involves being opportunity-focused, innovative, persistent, resourceful, and willing to take calculated risks to create value.

Unmet Need

A desire or problem that people have but for which no satisfactory product or service currently exists, or existing solutions are inadequate. The foundation of every successful business.

Value Creation

The process of producing goods or services that are useful or desirable to others, prompting them to exchange money or other resources for them.

Calculated Risk

A risk that has been thoughtfully evaluated, where potential benefits outweigh potential downsides, and strategies are in place to mitigate negative outcomes. This involves research and testing, no...

Important Terms (2/2)

Persistence

The quality of continuing steadfastly in a course of action, often in spite of difficulties or opposition. Crucial for entrepreneurs facing initial setbacks.

Resourcefulness

The ability to find quick and clever ways to overcome difficulties, often by using what is immediately available (skills, tools, network).

Side Hustle

A secondary job or business undertaken in addition to one's primary employment, often to earn extra income or develop new skills.

Real-World Business Ideas with Low/No Startup Capital (1/2)

Tutoring

Utilizing academic or skill-based knowledge (math, science, music, languages).

Lawn Care / Yard Work

Using basic tools and physical labor for neighborhood services.

Dog Walking and Pet Sitting

Leveraging a love for animals and local demand.

Photography

Using a smartphone or entry-level camera for portraits, events, or real estate.

Social Media Management

Applying existing knowledge of social platforms to help local businesses.

Real-World Business Ideas with Low/No Startup Capital (2/2)

Baked Goods

Starting from a home kitchen (checking local "cottage food laws").

Handmade Goods / Crafts

Creating jewelry, art, or candles to sell online or locally.

Computer Repair and Setup

Using technical skills to assist individuals with tech issues.

Cleaning Services

Providing residential or commercial cleaning.

Errand and Delivery Services

Offering convenience to busy individuals or those needing assistance.
