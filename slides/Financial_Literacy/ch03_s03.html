Real-World Example: Marcus vs. Destiny

The Goal

Both Marcus and Destiny, at age 16 with part-time jobs, wanted to buy a $6,000 used car by age 18 (24 months away).

Marcus's Approach

He procrastinated, thinking two years was a long time. He spent most of his earnings for the first 12 months. With only 12 months left, he realized he needed to save $500/month, which was unmanagea...

Destiny's Approach

She immediately calculated her monthly savings target ($6,000 / 24 months = $250/month). She set up an automatic transfer of $250 each month.

Outcome

Destiny successfully reached her $6,000 goal, buying the car outright with no debt. Marcus fell short and relied on family loans.

Lesson

The difference wasn't income or goal, but immediate planning, consistency, and avoiding procrastination.

Stacking Goals by Time Horizon

How it Works

You can have short-term, medium-term, and long-term goals running concurrently.

Prioritization

While stacking goals, it's crucial to prioritize. An emergency fund, for instance, should come first as it provides a safety net for all other financial efforts.
