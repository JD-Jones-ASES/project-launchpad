Understanding the Core Definitions

What is a Need?

What is a Want?

Maslow's Hierarchy: A Guiding Framework

Practical Rule of Thumb

If going without something would seriously harm your health, safety, or ability to earn income, it's likely a need. If it would be disappointing but not dangerous, it's a want.

The Gray Area Problem: Context is Key

Is a smartphone a need?

Is a car a need?

Is clothing a need?

The Lesson

Honest thinking matters. Continuously ask yourself: "Is this item genuinely required for my health, safety, or ability to function, or do I just really, really want it?"

Real-World Application: Jordan's First Paycheck

Jordan's Decision

She paid her friend back and bought the boots first, addressing true needs and obligations. She then set aside $40 toward the earbuds, recognizing that the jacket and full earbuds would have to wait.

Key Insight

Identifying needs first allows for responsible spending, even when strong wants are present.

The "I Need It" Trap (Want-Creep)

Signs that a "need" might be a disguised want

Money Tip: The 24-Hour Rule

Strategy

Before making any purchase that isn't a clear necessity, wait 24 hours.

Benefit

This pause often causes the initial urge to fade, leading to more thoughtful decisions and preventing impulse buys, potentially saving hundreds of dollars annually.
