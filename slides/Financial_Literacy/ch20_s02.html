The Power of Small Investments: Making Your Money Grow Over Time

Study Slides

Key Concepts

The Power of Starting Early

The single most impactful factor in long-term investing. Starting young, even with small amounts, allows compound interest to work its magic over many decades, leading to exponentially larger sums ...

Compound Interest (or Compounding)

The process where your investment earnings also begin to earn returns, leading to exponential growth over time. The longer your money is invested, the more powerful compounding becomes.

Consistency Over Amount

Emphasizes that regular, consistent contributions (e.g., monthly) are more crucial than the size of each individual contribution, especially when combined with a long investment horizon.

Impact of Annual Return Rate

While time is paramount, higher annual return rates significantly accelerate wealth accumulation. Even small differences in return percentages lead to vastly different future values over decades.

Accessibility of Investing

Debunks the myth that investing is only for the wealthy; even modest daily or weekly savings, redirected into investments, can build substantial wealth.

Important Terms

Future Value (FV)

The value of an investment at a specified date in the future, considering a regular payment (annuity) and a specific rate of return over a period.

Annual Return Rate (r)

The percentage of profit or loss that an investment generates over a one-year period. In these calculations, it's often converted to a monthly rate.

Monthly Contribution (PMT)

A fixed amount of money that an individual regularly invests each month.

Compounding

The process by which an asset's earnings, from either capital gains or interest, are reinvested to generate additional earnings over time. This process causes money to grow at an accelerating rate.
