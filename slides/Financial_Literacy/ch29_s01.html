Important Terms (1/2)

Emergency Fund

A savings account specifically designated to cover 3 to 6 months of living expenses, used to bridge financial gaps during unexpected events like job loss.

Layoff

The temporary or permanent termination of an employee, often due to economic conditions, company restructuring, or downsizing, rather than individual performance.

Unemployment Insurance Benefits

Government payments provided to eligible individuals who lose their jobs through no fault of their own, designed to replace a portion of their previous income while they actively seek new employment.

Hardship Programs

Special arrangements offered by lenders (banks, credit card companies, etc.) to borrowers experiencing financial difficulty, which may include payment deferrals, reduced payments, or modified terms.

Deferment

A temporary postponement of loan payments, often granted during periods of financial hardship, during which interest may or may not accrue depending on the loan type and program.

Important Terms (2/2)

Bridge Income

Temporary income generated to cover living expenses and reduce the drain on an emergency fund while an individual searches for a new, permanent job.

Gig Economy Work

Short-term, flexible jobs or tasks often facilitated by digital platforms, such as food delivery, ride-sharing, or freelance services.

Skills Inventory

A detailed list of an individual's abilities, knowledge, and competencies, often compiled to identify transferable skills and potential areas for quick training or career development.

Your Emergency Fund Is the First Line of Defense

Purpose

The primary reason for having an emergency fund is to provide a buffer during income loss.

Recommended Size

Aim for 3–6 months of living expenses in a separate, easily accessible savings account.

Benefits

Provides time to find new income without incurring debt.

Warning (Retirement Accounts)

Avoid early withdrawal from traditional IRA or 401(k) before age 59½.

Unemployment Benefits

Eligibility

Generally available if you lose your job through no fault of your own (e.g., layoff, company closure).

Function

Government payments intended to replace a portion of your income during your job search.

Key Facts

Important Warning (Taxable Income)

Unemployment benefits are considered taxable income.

Situation

Immediate Actions

Result
