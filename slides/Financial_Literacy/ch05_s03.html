Important Terms (1/2)

Percentage to Decimal Conversion

The mathematical process of changing a percentage value into its decimal form, usually by dividing by 100, which is necessary for calculations involving tax rates.

Progressive Tax System

A tax structure where the tax rate increases as the taxable amount (income) increases.

Tax Bracket

A specific range of taxable income that is subject to a particular tax rate in a progressive tax system.

Marginal Tax Rate

The tax rate that applies to the last dollar of income earned, corresponding to the highest tax bracket an individual's income reaches.

Effective Tax Rate

The overall percentage of an individual's total income that is paid in taxes, calculated as total tax paid divided by total income.

Important Terms (2/2)

Federal Income Tax Withholding

Money that an employer deducts from an employee's gross pay on behalf of the federal government to prepay the employee's income tax liability.

State Income Tax

A tax imposed by a state government on the income of its residents, which can be structured as a flat rate, a progressive system, or not exist in certain states.

Annualize Income

The process of converting income earned over a shorter period (e.g., monthly or weekly) into an equivalent annual amount.

W-4 Form

(Implicitly mentioned) An IRS form employees complete to inform their employer how much federal income tax to withhold from their paychecks based on their personal tax situation.

State Income Tax

Flat-rate states

All income is taxed at a single rate, regardless of the amount.

Progressive states

Use a bracket system similar to the federal model.

No income tax states

As of 2024, nine states do not levy a state income tax: Alaska, Florida, Nevada, New Hampshire, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Washington, and Wyoming.

Withholding Is an Estimate, Not Your Final Tax Bill

Tax Filing

Each year (typically by April 15), you file a tax return to calculate your *actual* tax liability.

Refund vs. Owed

Optimal Withholding

The goal is for your withholding to match your actual tax bill as closely as possible. A large refund means you effectively gave the government an interest-free loan of your money.
