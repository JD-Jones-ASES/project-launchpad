What to Do If Your Identity is Stolen: A Recovery Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Rapid Response

The speed at which you report fraudulent activity significantly impacts your liability and the ease of recovery. Delays can lead to greater financial loss and complicate the resolution process.

Consumer Protections

Federal laws limit your financial liability for fraudulent credit and debit card charges, especially if reported promptly. Credit cards generally offer stronger protections than debit cards.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Thorough Documentation

Maintaining detailed records of all communications, reports, and evidence related to the identity theft is critical for successful dispute resolution and recovery.

Ongoing Monitoring

Identity theft often requires long-term vigilance. Continuously monitoring your credit reports and financial accounts helps detect any lingering or new fraudulent activity.

Step 6: Monitor Your Credit Going Forward

Ongoing Monitoring

For at least 12 months after resolving an incident, closely monitor your credit reports for any new suspicious activity.

Account Alerts

Set up free account alerts with your banks and credit card companies to be notified of any unusual activity.
