Credit Card Fees, Rewards, and Fine Print Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Annual Fees

A yearly charge for having certain credit cards, which can be justified if the card's perks and rewards exceed the fee's cost.

Rewards Programs (Cash Back, Travel Points, Miles)

Incentives offered by credit cards that provide benefits based on spending. They are only genuinely valuable if the full balance is paid every month.

The Golden Rule of Rewards Cards

If you carry any balance, the interest paid will almost always negate and exceed the value of rewards earned, making the program a net loss.

Penalty APR

A significantly higher Annual Percentage Rate (APR) that automatically activates when a cardholder misses a payment or pays late, leading to much higher interest charges.

Introductory 0% APR Offers

Promotional periods where no interest is charged on purchases or balance transfers for a set time. These can be beneficial if the balance is paid off entirely before the period ends, but dangerous ...

Key Concepts (2/2)

Credit Card Wisdom

A set of best practices for using credit cards responsibly, including paying balances in full, avoiding late payments, managing credit utilization, and understanding terms.

Important Terms (1/2)

Annual Fee

A yearly charge assessed by a credit card issuer for the privilege of using the credit card.

Rewards Program

A feature of a credit card that offers incentives (such as cash back, travel points, or miles) for making purchases.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The annual rate of interest charged on outstanding credit card balances.

Penalty APR

A much higher interest rate that a credit card company can impose if a cardholder violates certain terms, such as making a late payment.

Introductory 0% APR Offer

A promotional period during which new cardholders are charged 0% interest on purchases or balance transfers for a specified number of months.

Important Terms (2/2)

Grace Period

The period between the end of a billing cycle and the payment due date, during which no interest is charged if the full balance is paid.

Credit Utilization

The amount of credit you are using compared to your total available credit. Keeping it low is good for your credit score.

Cash Advance

Withdrawing cash using a credit card. These typically come with high fees and immediate interest charges.

Minimum Interest Charge

A small fixed fee charged by some credit card companies even when the calculated interest on a small balance is less than this amount.

Rewards Programs: When They Help and When They Hurt

Types of Rewards

These programs offer incentives like cash back, travel points, or airline miles based on your spending.

The Crucial Condition

Rewards programs are genuinely valuable *only* if you pay your full credit card balance every single month.

The Math: Rewards vs. Interest

The Golden Rule

If you carry *any* balance at all, the interest you pay will always exceed the rewards you earn, making the rewards program a net loss, unless you are under an extremely rare 0% APR promotional offer.
