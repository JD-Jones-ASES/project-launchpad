Renting Basics Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Lease Agreement

A legally binding contract outlining the terms and conditions between a tenant and a landlord for the rental of a property. It defines rent, tenancy length, rules, responsibilities, and consequences.

Tenant and Landlord Responsibilities

Roles and duties of both parties, typically detailed within the lease agreement, covering aspects like repairs, maintenance, and property use.

The 30% Rule for Rent

A widely accepted financial guideline suggesting that monthly rent should not exceed 30% of one's gross (before-tax) monthly income to ensure housing affordability.

Understanding What's Included in Rent

The necessity of clarifying which utilities (water, electricity, gas) and services (parking, internet) are covered by the monthly rent versus those that will be separate, additional expenses.

Renters Insurance

An affordable insurance policy that protects a tenant's personal belongings against damage, loss, or theft, and may also provide liability coverage.

Important Terms (1/2)

Lease

A legal contract outlining the terms under which one party agrees to rent property from another party.

Tenant

The person who rents property from a landlord. Also known as a renter.

Landlord

The owner of a property who leases it to a tenant.

Gross Monthly Income

Your total income before any taxes or deductions are taken out, calculated on a monthly basis.

Security Deposit

A sum of money paid by the tenant to the landlord at the beginning of a lease, held to cover any damages beyond normal wear and tear or unpaid rent. (Mentioned in context of breaking a lease early).

Important Terms (2/2)

Subletting

The act of a tenant renting out their rented property to another person, often requiring landlord approval.

Renters Insurance

An insurance policy that covers a tenant's personal property from perils like fire or theft, and provides liability protection.

The Lease Agreement (1/2)

Monthly Rent Amount and Due Date

Clearly states how much rent is owed and when it must be paid each month.

Length of Tenancy

Defines the duration of the agreement, commonly 12 months, but can vary.

Permitted and Prohibited Activities

Outlines rules regarding pets, smoking, alterations to the property, and whether **subletting** is allowed.

Consequences for Breaking the Lease Early

Details the penalties if you terminate the agreement before its specified end date, which often includes paying 1-2 months' rent and forfeiting your **security deposit**.

Responsibility for Repairs and Maintenance

Specifies who is accountable for different types of repairs and general upkeep of the property.

The Lease Agreement (2/2)

Notice Periods for Move-Out

Dictates how much advance notice (e.g., 30-60 days) you must give before moving out at the end of the lease term.

What's Included in Rent?

Water and Trash

Are these included in the rent?

Electricity and Gas

Are these covered, or will you need to set up accounts and pay separately?

Parking

Is parking provided and included, or is there an additional monthly fee?

Internet

Is internet service part of the rent, or is it a separate utility?

Renters Insurance

Purpose

It covers your personal belongings (furniture, electronics, clothing, etc.) in case of fire, theft, vandalism, or other covered perils. It also typically provides liability coverage if someone is i...

Cost

It is generally inexpensive, typically ranging from $15 to $30 per month.

Requirement

Many landlords now require tenants to carry renters insurance as a condition of the lease.

Value

Given its low cost and the significant protection it offers against potentially devastating losses, renters insurance is almost always worth having.
