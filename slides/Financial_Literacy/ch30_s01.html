Key Concepts (2/2)

Personal Decision

The act of giving — how much, where, and even whether to give — is a deeply personal choice influenced by individual values, beliefs, income, and life stage, rather than a prescriptive requirement.

Important Terms (1/2)

Generosity

The quality of being kind and unselfish, especially in sharing resources like money or time with others.

Community Impact

The positive effect that combined individual actions or contributions have on the well-being and functioning of a local or broader group of people.

Values Alignment

The state where one's actions, including financial decisions, are consistent with and reflect their deeply held beliefs, principles, and priorities.

Legacy

Something that is left or handed down by a predecessor, or the long-lasting impact and contribution an individual makes during their lifetime.

Financial Generosity

The intentional act of sharing one's financial resources, through donations or aid, with others or organizations.

Important Terms (2/2)

Philanthropy

The desire to promote the welfare of others, expressed especially by the generous donation of money to good causes.
