Important Terms (1/2)

Individual Retirement Account (IRA)

A personal retirement savings account that you open and manage yourself, separate from any employer-sponsored plan.

Traditional IRA

An individual retirement account where contributions may be tax-deductible, investments grow tax-deferred, and withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

Tax-Deferred

A tax treatment where taxes on investment gains and sometimes contributions are postponed until a later date, typically when the money is withdrawn from the account.

Tax-Deductible

An amount that can be subtracted from your gross income to reduce your taxable income, potentially lowering your current year's tax bill.

Ordinary Income Tax

The tax rate applied to most types of income, such as wages, salaries, and interest. Traditional IRA withdrawals are taxed at these rates.

Important Terms (2/2)

Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)

The minimum amount of money the IRS requires you to withdraw from a Traditional IRA each year once you reach age 73 to ensure deferred taxes are eventually paid.

Early Withdrawal Penalty

A 10% additional tax imposed by the IRS on withdrawals from retirement accounts (like Traditional IRAs) made before the account holder reaches age 59½, in addition to regular income tax.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Under what condition are Traditional IRA contributions always tax-deductible?

If you do not have a retirement plan at work, you can always deduct Traditional IRA contributions.

When might the Traditional IRA deduction be limited if you have a workplace retirement plan?

If you have a workplace plan (like a 401(k)), the deduction phases out at higher income levels, according to IRS guidelines.

What does 'tax-deferred growth' mean in the context of a Traditional IRA?

Your investments grow without being taxed each year while the money remains in the account.

What is an advantage of tax-deferred growth in a Traditional IRA compared to a regular brokerage account?

You avoid paying taxes annually on dividends and capital gains, which you might owe each year in a regular brokerage account even if you don't sell investments.

Define 'Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)'.

The minimum amount the IRS requires you to withdraw from a Traditional IRA each year once you reach age 73 (age may vary by current IRS rules).

What is the purpose of Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs) from a Traditional IRA?

To ensure that the government eventually collects taxes on money that was contributed tax-deferred.
