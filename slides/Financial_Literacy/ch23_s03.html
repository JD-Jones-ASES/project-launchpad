Power of Attorney and Healthcare Directives

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Estate Planning for Incapacity

Estate planning isn't just about wills; it includes preparing for potential mental or physical incapacitation, ensuring that someone can legally manage your affairs.

Power of Attorney (POA)

A legal document where a person (the "principal") grants another person (the "agent" or "attorney-in-fact") authority to act on their behalf in financial and legal matters.

Durable Power of Attorney

A crucial type of POA that remains effective (or only becomes effective) even if the principal becomes mentally incapacitated, preventing the need for court intervention like conservatorship.

Healthcare Directive (Living Will/Advance Directive)

A legal document that outlines an individual's specific wishes for medical treatment, especially in end-of-life situations where they cannot communicate their desires.

Healthcare Proxy (Healthcare Power of Attorney)

A legal document designating a specific person to make medical decisions on your behalf if you become unable to do so yourself, ensuring a trusted individual advocates for your care.

Important Terms (1/2)

Power of Attorney (POA)

A legal document in which you (the principal) give another person (the agent or attorney-in-fact) the legal authority to act on your behalf in financial and legal matters.

Principal

The individual who grants legal authority to another person through a Power of Attorney.

Agent / Attorney-in-Fact

The person designated in a Power of Attorney to act on behalf of the principal in financial and legal matters.

Durable Power of Attorney

A specific type of power of attorney that remains effective, or only becomes effective, if the principal becomes mentally incapacitated.

General Power of Attorney

A POA that grants broad authority over financial and legal matters but automatically becomes invalid if the principal becomes incapacitated (unless specifically made "durable").

Important Terms (2/2)

Limited (Special) Power of Attorney

A POA that grants authority for a specific task only and expires once that task is completed.

Conservator

A person appointed by a court to manage the financial and/or personal affairs of an individual who is unable to do so themselves. This process is often expensive and time-consuming.

Healthcare Proxy

A legal document (also known as a healthcare power of attorney) designating a specific person to make medical decisions on your behalf if you are unable to do so yourself.

HIPAA Authorization

A legal document that grants permission for designated individuals (e.g., parents) to access your medical records and speak with your doctors, especially important for individuals over 18.

Limited (Special) POA

This grants authority for a very specific task only, such as selling a particular piece of property. It automatically expires once that specific task is completed.

Healthcare Proxy (Healthcare Power of Attorney)

Complete Trustworthiness

They must be someone you implicitly trust to make decisions aligned with your best interests.

Knowledge of Your Values

They should understand your beliefs, values, and specific wishes regarding medical care.

Advocacy Skills

They must be willing and able to advocate for you within a medical setting, often under emotionally challenging circumstances.

Separate from Financial POA (Ideally)

It is generally advisable to name a different person as your healthcare proxy than your financial power of attorney to prevent potential conflicts of interest.

Accidents Happen at Any Age

Car accidents, sports injuries, and sudden illnesses can occur at any point in life, leading to unexpected incapacitation.
