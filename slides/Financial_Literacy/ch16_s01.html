Important Terms (1/2)

Federal Student Loans

Loans issued by the U.S. Department of Education, offering robust borrower protections and benefits.

Private Student Loans

Loans issued by private financial institutions (banks, credit unions), offering fewer protections than federal loans.

Subsidized Loan

A federal loan where the government covers interest during certain periods, typically for students with demonstrated financial need.

Unsubsidized Loan

A federal loan where the borrower is responsible for all accrued interest from the moment of disbursement.

PLUS Loan

A federal loan for graduate students or parents of undergraduate students, requiring a credit check.

Important Terms (2/2)

Interest Capitalization

The addition of unpaid accrued interest to the principal balance of a loan, increasing the amount on which future interest is calculated.

Principal Balance

The original amount of money borrowed, plus any capitalized interest. Interest is calculated on this balance.

Deferment

A temporary postponement of loan payments, often due to enrollment in school or economic hardship.

Grace Period

A short period (e.g., six months) after graduation or leaving school during which loan payments are not required.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The yearly rate of interest charged on a loan, expressed as a percentage.

Federal Interest Rates

Federal student loan interest rates are set annually by Congress and are tied to the yield on 10-year Treasury notes. They are fixed for the life of each loan.

Private Interest Rates

Private student loan rates vary significantly based on the borrower's credit score and the specific lender. They can often be substantially higher than federal rates.
