Loan Basics: Understanding the Foundation of Borrowing

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/3)

Loan Agreement

A formal contract between a borrower and a lender, detailing the terms of repayment for borrowed money.

Core Loan Components

All loans consist of a principal, an interest rate, a loan term, and sometimes collateral.

Principal

The original amount of money borrowed; interest is calculated on the outstanding principal balance.

Interest Rate (APR)

The cost of borrowing, expressed as an annual percentage of the amount owed, representing the fee paid to the lender.

Loan Term

The duration over which the borrower agrees to repay the loan, typically measured in months or years.

Key Concepts (2/3)

Collateral

An asset pledged by the borrower to secure a loan, which the lender can seize if the borrower defaults.

Secured Loan

A loan backed by collateral, generally carrying lower interest rates due to reduced risk for the lender.

Unsecured Loan

A loan not backed by collateral, relying on the borrower's creditworthiness and promise to repay, thus often having higher interest rates.

Common Loan Types

Specific loans for particular purposes, such as mortgages (real estate), auto loans (vehicles), student loans (education), and personal loans (general purpose).

Amortized Loan

A loan repaid through fixed periodic payments that cover both interest and principal, with the proportion of each shifting over time (more interest early, more principal later).

Key Concepts (3/3)

Comparison Shopping

The practice of comparing loan offers from multiple lenders to secure the most favorable interest rate and terms.

Important Terms (1/3)

Principal

The initial sum of money borrowed in a loan.

Interest Rate (APR)

The annual cost of borrowing money, expressed as a percentage of the loan amount, representing the fee paid to the lender.

Loan Term

The predetermined period of time, in months or years, during which a borrower must repay a loan.

Collateral

An asset, such as a car or house, that a borrower pledges as security for a loan; it can be seized by the lender if the borrower fails to make payments.

Secured Loan

A loan for which the borrower pledges an asset (collateral) as security, reducing the lender's risk.

Important Terms (2/3)

Unsecured Loan

A loan that does not require collateral, meaning the lender's only security is the borrower's promise and credit history.

Mortgage

A specific type of loan used to purchase real estate, where the property itself serves as collateral.

Auto Loan

A loan specifically for purchasing a vehicle, with the car serving as collateral.

Student Loan

A loan designed to help finance education expenses.

Personal Loan

A general-purpose loan, often unsecured, used for a variety of personal expenses.

Important Terms (3/3)

Amortized Loan

A loan structure where repayment occurs through a series of equal, regular payments that gradually pay off both the principal and the interest over the loan term.
