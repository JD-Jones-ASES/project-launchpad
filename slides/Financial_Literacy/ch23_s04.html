Important Terms (1/2)

Digital Assets

Property or accounts that exist only in digital form, such as online banking, social media profiles, cryptocurrency, email accounts, and cloud storage.

Cryptocurrency

Digital or virtual currencies secured by cryptography, making them nearly impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. Examples include Bitcoin and Ethereum. Losing the "private key" or "seed phrase"...

Private Key / Seed Phrase

Cryptographic codes essential for accessing and managing cryptocurrency. Their loss results in irreversible loss of the associated digital currency.

Password Manager

Software application that stores and organizes all of an individual's passwords securely, often behind a single "master password."

Emergency Access

A feature in some password managers that allows a designated trusted person to request access to your password vault after a set waiting period, typically for use after an emergency or death.

Important Terms (2/2)

Legacy Contact

A feature offered by some online platforms (e.g., social media) that allows users to designate a trusted person who can manage or delete their account after their death without needing probate.

Probate

The legal process of proving a will in court and executing its terms, including the distribution of assets and settlement of debts. Wills become public records during this process.

Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act (RUFADAA)

A uniform act that provides a legal framework for fiduciaries (like executors or agents) to access and manage a deceased or incapacitated person's digital assets.

What Are Digital Assets? (1/2)

Online Financial Accounts

Bank accounts and investment portfolios accessed purely digitally, often without paper statements.

Cryptocurrency

Digital currencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum, which can have substantial monetary value and are irreversibly lost if the private key or seed phrase is forgotten or not securely transmitted.

Social Media Accounts

Platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and Facebook often contain years of irreplaceable photos, videos, and memories.

Email Accounts

Critical for communication, these often hold important documents, financial records, account recovery information, and sentimental correspondence.

Cloud Storage

Services such as Google Photos, iCloud, and Dropbox contain personal photos, videos, and documents that are often irreplaceable.

What Are Digital Assets? (2/2)

Digital Storefronts and Gaming Accounts

Platforms like Steam or Apple ID, and various gaming accounts, can hold purchased content, valuable in-game items, or significant monetary value.

Online Businesses/Content Creation

YouTube channels, Etsy shops, monetized blogs, and other online ventures can be ongoing sources of income or intellectual property.

Subscription Services

Various online subscriptions (streaming, software, news) incur recurring charges that need to be cancelled to avoid draining the estate.

The Problem: Passwords Die With You

Inaccessible Email Accounts

Families often cannot access email, preventing them from finding critical financial information, cancelling subscriptions, or retrieving important records.

Permanent Loss of Cryptocurrency

Without the private key or seed phrase, cryptocurrency held in personal digital wallets becomes permanently inaccessible, leading to billions of dollars being lost forever.

Difficult Social Media Management

While platforms may offer options to "memorialize" or deactivate accounts, accessing or managing content typically requires complex processes, proof of relationship, and significant effort.

What are digital assets?

Digital assets include any property or account that exists in digital form.

1. Online financial accounts (e.g., online bank accounts, cryptocurrency). 2. Social media and communication accounts (e.g., Instagram, email). 3. Cloud stor...

Why is cryptocurrency a unique concern in digital estate planning?

Cryptocurrency may have real monetary value, but it is lost forever if the private key or seed phrase is lost, making it inaccessible to heirs.

What is the most common problem families face with digital assets after a person's death?

The account passwords are known only to the deceased, making it impossible for families to access essential information or manage accounts.

What is a major financial consequence of lost cryptocurrency access upon death?

Billions of dollars in cryptocurrency have been permanently lost because private keys or seed phrases were inaccessible after the owner's death.

How do social media platforms typically handle accounts after a user's death?

They may 'memorialize' an account or deactivate it, but accessing or deleting the content often requires proof of relationship and significant effort.

Why should you never include actual passwords in your will?

Wills become public records during probate, which would expose your sensitive access information to public view.

What is a 'legacy contact' feature offered by some digital platforms?

A legacy contact is a person you designate who can access, memorialize, or delete your account after your death, according to platform policies.

What is an advantage of using platform legacy contact features?

These designations can be changed anytime, take effect automatically upon verified death, and do not require probate involvement.
