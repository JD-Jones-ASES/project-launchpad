Amortization: How Loan Payments Work

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Monthly Payment Formula

A specific mathematical formula used to calculate the fixed monthly payment ($M$) required to fully pay off an amortized loan over its term.

Variables in the Formula

Understanding what each component represents: $P$ (principal), $r$ (monthly interest rate), and $n$ (total number of monthly payments).

Calculating Monthly Interest Rate ($r$)

Derived by dividing the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) by 12 and expressing it as a decimal.

Calculating Total Number of Payments ($n$)

Found by multiplying the loan term in years by 12.

Exponent Calculation

The use of $(1+r)^n$ in the formula, which requires a scientific calculator's $y^x$ (or equivalent) function.

Important Terms (1/2)

Amortization

The process of paying off a debt with a fixed repayment schedule in regular installments over a period of time, where each payment covers both interest and a portion of the principal.

Monthly Payment (M)

The fixed amount of money paid by the borrower each month to the lender.

Principal (P)

The initial amount of money borrowed from the lender, or the remaining balance of the loan.

Monthly Interest Rate (r)

The interest rate applied to the loan's principal each month, calculated as the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) divided by 12, expressed as a decimal.

Total Number of Monthly Payments (n)

The total count of payments that will be made over the entire life of the loan, calculated as the loan term in years multiplied by 12.

Important Terms (2/2)

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The yearly rate of interest charged on a loan, expressed as a percentage.

Interest

The cost of borrowing money, calculated as a percentage of the principal.

Loan Term

The duration over which a loan is to be repaid, typically expressed in years.
