Financial Literacy for High School

Chapter 7, Section 4

Building Your First Zero-Based Budget

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Zero-Based Budgeting

A budgeting method where every dollar of income is assigned a purpose (spent, saved, or given), ensuring that income minus expenses equals zero. This provides clarity on where all money goes.

Net Income

The actual amount of money an individual takes home after all taxes and deductions have been subtracted from their gross pay. This is the starting point for a budget.

Fixed Expenses

Costs that remain the same each month and are generally non-negotiable, such as rent, insurance premiums, or subscription services. These are typically prioritized in a budget.

Variable Needs

Necessary expenses that fluctuate from month to month, like groceries, transportation fuel, or utility bills. While essential, these often require careful tracking.

Paying Yourself First

The critical principle of prioritizing savings goals (like emergency funds or future investments) before allocating funds to discretionary "wants."

Key Concepts (2/2)

Sinking Funds

Specific savings categories designed to set aside small amounts of money each month to cover larger, irregular expenses that don't occur monthly (e.g., car registration, holiday gifts, annual fees).

Proactive Budgeting

The practice of creating and finalizing a budget *before* the month begins, allowing it to serve as a financial plan rather than just a record of past spending.

Important Terms (1/2)

Zero-Based Budget

A budget where your income minus your expenses and savings equals exactly zero, meaning every dollar has been assigned a job.

Net Pay / Net Income

The amount of money an employee receives after all deductions and taxes have been removed from their gross pay.

Fixed Expenses

Expenses that stay constant from month to month, like rent or car insurance.

Variable Needs

Essential expenses that can change in amount each month, such as groceries or electricity.

Savings Goals

Specific financial objectives for which money is being regularly set aside, like an emergency fund, down payment, or retirement.

Important Terms (2/2)

Sinking Fund

A dedicated savings account or category for a specific, anticipated future expense that is not part of regular monthly bills.

Wants

Discretionary expenses for non-essential items or activities that improve quality of life but are not strictly necessary for survival (e.g., dining out, entertainment).

Giving

Financial contributions made to charities, religious organizations, or individuals in need.

Paying Yourself First

The financial strategy of allocating money to savings or investments as the first priority when income is received, rather than as an afterthought.

The Scenario: Alex's Budget

Income

Alex earns $13.50 per hour, working 40 hours per week.

Net Monthly Income

After taxes and health insurance deductions, Alex's take-home pay is **$1,600 per month**. This is the starting and ending figure for the budget.

Living Situation

Alex shares an apartment with a roommate.

Alex's Monthly Net Income: $1,600

Remaining Income

$199 (after Savings) - $199 (Wants and Giving) = **$0**

Reading the Budget Against Financial Goals

Housing Cost

Aim for **below 30–35% of net income**. Alex's housing is $550 out of $1,600, which is **34.4%**. This is at the upper end of the recommended range.

Savings Rate

Aim to save at least **10% of net income**. Alex saves $275 out of $1,600, which is **17.2%**. This is a strong savings rate, exceeding the minimum recommendation.

Miscellaneous/Buffer Category

Consider adding a small amount ($20–$30) for unexpected minor expenses. Alex currently does not have one, which could lead to budget disruptions for small surprises.

Warning: Forgetting Irregular Expenses

Examples

Annual fees, car registration, back-to-school shopping, holiday gifts, medical copays.

Solution

Utilize **sinking funds** to save a small amount each month for these anticipated irregular expenses. Forgetting them can significantly derail your budget when they inevitably arrive.
