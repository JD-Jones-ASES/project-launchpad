Important Terms (1/2)

Death Benefit

The lump sum amount of money paid to your designated beneficiaries upon your death.

Beneficiary

The person, people, or entity you name in your life insurance policy (or other financial accounts) to receive the death benefit or assets when you die.

Premium

The regular payment you make to the insurance company to keep your life insurance policy active.

Underwriting

The process used by insurance companies to assess an applicant's risk factors (health, age, lifestyle, occupation) to determine eligibility and set the appropriate premium rate for a policy.

Term Life Insurance

A type of life insurance that provides coverage for a specific period (the "term") and pays a death benefit only if the insured dies within that term.

Important Terms (2/2)

Whole Life Insurance (Permanent Life Insurance)

A type of life insurance that provides coverage for the entire lifetime of the insured, as long as premiums are paid, and typically includes a cash value component.

Cash Value

A savings component found in whole life and other permanent life insurance policies that grows over time, tax-deferred, and can be borrowed against or withdrawn by the policyholder.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary purpose of life insurance?

To replace your income and protect your financial dependents if you die.

Who generally needs life insurance?

Individuals with financial dependents (spouses, children, or others) who rely on their earnings.

When might a young adult not need a large life insurance policy?

If they are single and have no financial dependents.

What is the most important insurance-related step a young adult can take right now, according to the text's money tip?

Ensuring existing accounts (bank, retirement, etc.) have properly named beneficiaries.

Define 'term life insurance.'

Life insurance that provides coverage for a specific period of time (a 'term'), paying a death benefit if the insured dies within that term.

What are common terms (durations) for term life insurance policies?

10, 20, or 30 years.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How do term life insurance premiums generally compare to permanent insurance premiums?

They are much lower, especially when purchased young and healthy.

Does term life insurance have an investment component?

No, it is pure protection only, with no investment component.

For whom is term life insurance generally recommended as the best option?

Most people, particularly to cover the years when dependents need income most (e.g., purchasing a 20- or 30-year policy in your 20s or 30s).

What is whole life insurance also known as, and what does it provide?

Also known as permanent life insurance, it provides coverage for your entire lifetime as long as premiums are paid.

What unique component does whole life insurance build over time?

A cash value component that grows slowly and can be borrowed against or withdrawn.

How do whole life insurance premiums compare to term life premiums for the same death benefit?

Premiums can be 5–15 times higher than term life.
