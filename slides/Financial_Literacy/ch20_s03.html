Important Terms (1/2)

PMT (Payment/Monthly Contribution)

The regular, periodic amount of money that needs to be contributed to an investment or savings account to reach a specific future value.

FV (Future Value)

The target amount of money an investment or savings account is projected to be worth at a specific point in the future. In this section, it's typically $1,000,000.

r (Monthly Interest Rate)

The annual interest rate divided by 12, used in calculations to reflect compounding on a monthly basis.

n (Total Months)

The total number of months between the starting point of saving and the target future date (e.g., age 65), calculated as years multiplied by 12.

Roth IRA

An individual retirement account (IRA) that allows qualified withdrawals to be tax-free in retirement. Contributions are made with after-tax dollars.

Solving for Monthly Contribution (PMT)

$PMT$

The monthly contribution you need to make.

$FV$

Your financial goal, the target future value you want to reach (e.g., $1,000,000).

$r$

The monthly interest rate. This is calculated by taking the annual interest rate (e.g., 8%) and dividing it by 12 (e.g., $0.08 / 12 = 0.006667$).

$n$

The total number of months until you reach your goal. This is calculated by taking the number of years until your goal and multiplying it by 12.
