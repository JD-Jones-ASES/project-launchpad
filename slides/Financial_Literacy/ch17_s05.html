Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA): Investing Consistently, Not Perfectly (1/2)

Month 1

Price is $10/share. Your $100 buys 10 shares ($100 / $10).

Month 2

Price is $5/share. Your $100 buys 20 shares ($100 / $5).

Month 3

Price is $8/share. Your $100 buys 12.5 shares ($100 / $8).

Total Invested

$100 + $100 + $100 = $300

Total Shares Owned

10 + 20 + 12.5 = 42.5 shares

Dollar-Cost Averaging (DCA): Investing Consistently, Not Perfectly (2/2)

Average Cost Per Share (DCA)

$300 / 42.5 shares = **$7.06 per share**

Simple Average Market Price

($10 + $5 + $8) / 3 = $23 / 3 = **$7.67 per share**

The Synergistic Power of Diversification and Dollar-Cost Averaging

Diversification

shields your portfolio from the risk of any single investment performing poorly or failing entirely.

Dollar-cost averaging

protects you from the risk of making large investments at "the wrong time," such as right before a market downturn.
