Choosing Between Retirement Accounts: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts

Core Tax Rate Trade-off

The fundamental choice between Traditional (pre-tax) and Roth (after-tax) accounts is a "bet" on whether your tax rate will be higher now or in retirement.

Mathematical Equivalence (Equal Tax Rates)

If your tax rate is the same now as it will be in retirement, the after-tax accumulation in a Roth account and a Traditional account will be identical.

Combination Strategy

It is common and often beneficial to contribute to both a 401(k) and an IRA simultaneously, as their contribution limits are separate.

Employer Match Priority

Always prioritize contributing to a 401(k) at least up to the full employer match, as this is essentially "free money" and an immediate 100% return on that portion of your investment.

Important Terms (1/2)

Traditional IRA (Individual Retirement Account)

A retirement account where contributions are often tax-deductible in the year they are made (pre-tax). Withdrawals in retirement are taxed as ordinary income.

Roth IRA

A retirement account where contributions are made with after-tax money. Qualified withdrawals in retirement are entirely tax-free.

401(k)

An employer-sponsored retirement savings plan. Contributions are typically pre-tax (Traditional 401(k)), though many employers also offer Roth 401(k) options. Often includes an employer matching co...

Pre-tax Contributions

Money contributed to a retirement account before income taxes have been deducted.

After-tax Contributions

Money contributed to a retirement account after income taxes have already been paid.

Important Terms (2/2)

Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)

Mandatory annual withdrawals that account holders must start taking from most Traditional retirement accounts (including Traditional IRAs and 401(k)s) once they reach a certain age (currently 73). ...

Tax Bracket

The range of incomes that are subject to a particular marginal tax rate. For example, income within the 22% tax bracket is taxed at 22%.

Employer Match

A benefit offered by some employers where they contribute a certain amount to an employee's 401(k) or other retirement plan, often based on a percentage of the employee's contribution or salary.
