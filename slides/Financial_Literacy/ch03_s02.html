Important Terms

Short-Term Financial Goal

A financial target planned to be reached within one year or less.

Emergency Fund

A pool of savings set aside specifically for unexpected expenses to avoid disrupting other financial plans.

Reverse Planning

A method of calculating required regular savings by dividing a goal's total amount by its timeline.

SMART Goal

An acronym (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) representing criteria for effective goal setting.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a short-term financial goal?

A target you plan to reach within one year or less.

What are two common characteristics of short-term financial goals?

They typically involve smaller dollar amounts and allow you to see results quickly due to their short timeline.

List three common examples of short-term financial goals for students.

Saving $250 for a new phone case, building a $500 emergency fund, or saving $120 for holiday gifts.

What framework is emphasized when the text notes that short-term goals have a specific dollar amount and a clear deadline?

The SMART goal framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound).

What is the first reason given for focusing on short-term goals, even if long-term goals seem more important?

Quick wins build confidence, proving to yourself that you can achieve financial targets.

How do short-term goals contribute to developing long-term financial habits?

They provide practice in the 'muscle of saving,' making it stronger and preparing you for longer-term goals.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Beyond confidence and habits, what immediate benefit do short-term goals offer?

They solve real, immediate problems like needing school supplies or a bus pass, reducing reliance on others or going without.

According to financial experts, what specific short-term goal should be your first priority?

Building a small emergency fund.

What is an emergency fund?

A dedicated pool of savings set aside exclusively for unexpected expenses (e.g., car repairs, medical bills) to prevent them from derailing other financial p...

What is a realistic starter emergency fund amount for a student with part-time income, and why is this amount recommended?

$500 to $1,000. It covers many common unexpected expenses without being so large it takes years to build, acting as a financial safety net.

What is a recommended strategy for managing an emergency fund and individual goals to avoid accidentally spending the money?

Open a separate savings account specifically for the emergency fund and other individual goals, separate from everyday spending accounts.

What is 'reverse planning' in the context of financial goals?

It's the process of doing the math to determine if a financial goal is achievable, often by calculating the monthly savings needed.
