Building Credit from Scratch: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Credit Catch-22

The dilemma where new borrowers struggle to obtain credit because they lack a credit history, yet they need credit to build that history.

Authorized User

A person added to another's credit card account whose credit report may reflect the primary cardholder's payment history, helping to establish their own credit.

Secured Credit Card

A credit card that requires an upfront cash deposit, which serves as the credit limit and collateral, allowing the cardholder to build credit through regular, on-time payments.

Student Credit Card

Credit cards designed for young adults, often students, with little to no credit history, typically featuring lower limits and more lenient approval standards.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Payment History

The record of a borrower's past payments on debt, which is the most significant factor (35%) in calculating a credit score.

Credit Utilization

The amount of credit you're using compared to your total available credit; keeping this low (ideally under 10-30%) is crucial for a good credit score.

Patience in Credit Building

Establishing a strong credit history is a long-term process, typically requiring 12-24 months of consistent responsible behavior.

Important Terms (1/2)

Authorized User

Someone added to another person's existing credit card account, gaining a card to use, and whose credit report may reflect the account's payment history.

Secured Credit Card

A credit card that requires a cash deposit upfront, which then becomes the cardholder's credit limit and protects the lender.

Primary Cardholder

The main individual responsible for a credit card account, whose credit history is directly tied to the account's performance.

Credit Bureaus

Agencies (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) that collect and maintain credit information on consumers, which is then used to generate credit reports and scores.

Credit Limit

The maximum amount of credit a lender will extend to a borrower on a specific account.

Important Terms (2/2)

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)

The annual rate charged for borrowing, expressed as a percentage of the loan amount, which includes any fees or additional costs associated with the transaction.

Hard Inquiry

A formal request by a lender to review your credit report, typically occurring when you apply for new credit, which can temporarily lower your credit score.

Credit Utilization

The ratio of the amount of credit you are currently using to the total amount of credit you have available.
