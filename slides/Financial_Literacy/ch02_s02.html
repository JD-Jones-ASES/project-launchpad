Important Terms

Values

The beliefs and priorities that guide your decisions and define what matters most to you (e.g., family, security, independence). They are not dollar amounts.

Values-Based Spending

The practice of deliberately directing your money toward the things that matter most to you, ensuring your spending reflects your actual priorities.

Intentional Spending

Making conscious, purposeful decisions about how to use your money, aligning choices with your goals and values.

Mindless Spending

Spending money without conscious thought or consideration of whether the purchase aligns with your values or financial goals.

Did You Know: Experiences vs. Things

Research Finding

Studies consistently indicate that people derive more lasting satisfaction from spending money on experiences (e.g., travel, concerts, dining with friends) than on material possessions.

Implication

This suggests that considering long-term satisfaction is a valuable part of making values-based spending choices.

Money Tip: Identify Spending Values Now

Early Identification

It's ideal to identify your core values before deep-seated spending habits are formed.

Practical Strategy

Building a Habit

You don't need to answer "yes" to this question every time, but consistently asking it will build a powerful habit of conscious spending and transform your relationship with money.

Conclusion: Ongoing Process

Dynamic Values

Personal values are not static; they can evolve as you grow and your life circumstances change.

Continuous Check-in

The habit of regularly asking "Does my spending reflect what I care about?" is a fundamental and powerful tool for lifelong personal financial management.
