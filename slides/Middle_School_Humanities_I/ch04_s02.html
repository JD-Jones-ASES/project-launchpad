The Nuance of "Truth"

Different contexts (historical, dramatic, psychological) require different understandings of what constitutes "truth."

Important Terms (1/3)

Plutarch's *Parallel Lives*

A collection of biographies of famous Greeks and Romans, written by the Greek historian Plutarch, which served as Shakespeare's primary source for *Julius Caesar*.

Thomas North's Translation

The 1579 English translation of Plutarch's *Parallel Lives* that Shakespeare used.

*The Tragedy of Julius Caesar*

William Shakespeare's play, written around 1599, depicting the conspiracy against Julius Caesar, his assassination, and the aftermath.

Historical Accuracy

The extent to which an account faithfully reflects events as they actually occurred, based on available evidence.

Dramatic Plausibility

The believability of events and character actions within the context of a play or story, regardless of strict historical fact.

Important Terms (2/3)

Literary Truth

The deeper insights into human nature, universal themes, or psychological realities conveyed by a work of literature, even if fictionalized.

Rhetoric

The art of effective or persuasive speaking or writing, especially the use of figures of speech and other compositional techniques.

Colossus

A statue of gigantic size; used metaphorically by Cassius to describe Caesar's overwhelming power.

Republican Ideal

The political philosophy supporting a republic, where power is held by the people and their elected representatives, rather than a monarch.

Ides of March

March 15th, the date on which Julius Caesar was assassinated in 44 BC.

Important Terms (3/3)

Stoic

A follower of Stoicism, an ancient Greek philosophy that emphasizes virtue, reason, and emotional fortitude in the face of adversity.

Philippi

The site of two major battles in 42 BC, where the forces of Mark Antony and Octavian defeated Brutus and Cassius, leading to their suicides.
