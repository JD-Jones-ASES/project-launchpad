Important Terms (1/3)

The Venerable Bede (c. AD 673–735)

An English monk and scholar, author of the Ecclesiastical History of the English People.

Ecclesiastical History of the English People (AD 731)

Bede's masterpiece, a primary historical source for early English Christianity and a model of historical scholarship.

AD System (Anno Domini)

Bede's dating system, counting years from the Incarnation of Christ, which became standard throughout the West.

Thucydides (c. 460–400 BC)

Classical Greek historian known for his rigorous historical methodology and focus on human nature as the driver of events.

Providential History

The belief, common in the medieval period, that God is actively guiding and directing human events towards His ultimate purposes.

Important Terms (2/3)

Sparrow in the Mead-Hall

A famous passage from Bede's Ecclesiastical History illustrating the human condition and the appeal of Christianity.

Caedmon

The first named English poet, whose divinely inspired hymn is the oldest surviving poem in Old English.

Old English

The earliest form of the English language, spoken in England from the 5th to the 11th century.

Einhard (c. AD 770–840)

A Frankish scholar and courtier of Charlemagne, author of the Vita Karoli.

Vita Karoli (Life of Charlemagne, c. AD 830)

Einhard's biography of Emperor Charlemagne, modeled on classical Roman biographies.

Important Terms (3/3)

Suetonius' Lives of the Twelve Caesars

A classical Roman biographical work that Einhard consciously imitated.

Carolingian Renaissance

A period of intellectual and cultural revival during the reign of Charlemagne, characterized by renewed interest in classical learning.

Gregory the Great (c. 540–604)

Pope Gregory I, author of Regula Pastoralis.

Regula Pastoralis (Pastoral Care, c. AD 591)

Gregory the Great's handbook for bishops on Christian leadership, spiritual guidance, and ethical conduct.

King Alfred the Great (r. 871–899)

English king who translated Gregory's Pastoral Care into Old English and distributed it to bishops.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What three literary masterpieces are highlighted from this period, and who were their authors?

Bede's Ecclesiastical History, Einhard's Life of Charlemagne, and Gregory the Great's Pastoral Care.

Who was the Venerable Bede, and where did he spend most of his life?

The Venerable Bede (c. AD 673–735) was a monk who spent virtually his entire life in the monastery of Wearmouth-Jarrow in Northumbria.

What is the primary historical significance of Bede's Ecclesiastical History of the English People?

It is the primary historical source for early English Christianity and one of the finest works of historical writing from the medieval period.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the crucial difference in how Bede and Thucydides understood the driving forces of history?

For Thucydides, history was driven by human nature (power, fear, self-interest); for Bede, it was providential, guided by God's purposes.

According to the text, why is a complete historical understanding said to require both Thucydides' and Bede's perspectives?

What is the context of the famous 'sparrow in the mead-hall' passage from Bede's Ecclesiastical History?

It is a reflection offered by a pagan counsellor of King Edwin of Northumbria, debating whether to adopt Christianity.

What universal human longing does the 'sparrow in the mead-hall' passage express, and what does it frame the Gospel as an answer to?

It expresses the universal human longing for meaning and permanence, framing the Gospel as an answer to that longing.

Who was Caedmon, and what is his significance in English literature?

Caedmon was the first named English poet, and his hymn, preserved by Bede, is the oldest surviving poem in Old English.
