Important Terms (1/3)

Pericles (c. 495–429 BC)

A prominent and influential statesman, orator, and general during Athens' Golden Age. He significantly advanced Athenian democracy and spearheaded the city's massive building program.

Pericles' Funeral Oration

A famous speech delivered by Pericles (recorded by Thucydides) during the Peloponnesian War, extolling the virtues of Athenian democracy, its citizens, and its unique cultural and political values.

Peloponnesian War (431–404 BC)

A major ancient Greek war fought between the Delian League (led by Athens) and the Peloponnesian League (led by Sparta). It resulted in Athens' defeat, the end of its hegemony, and a lasting weaken...

Philip II of Macedon (382–336 BC)

King of Macedon and a brilliant military strategist. He developed the **sarissa** and **Macedonian phalanx**, conquering most of Greece and uniting it before his assassination.

Important Terms (2/3)

Sarissa

An exceptionally long (up to 18 feet) pike used by the Macedonian army, invented by Philip II, which gave the **Macedonian phalanx** a significant advantage in battle.

Macedonian Phalanx

A highly effective and fearsome military formation developed by Philip II, consisting of tightly packed infantry wielding sarissas, which proved instrumental in his conquests.

Alexander the Great (356–323 BC)

Son of Philip II, he created one of the largest empires of the ancient world, stretching from Greece to India. His conquests were crucial in initiating the **Hellenistic** era.

Macedonia and Alexander the Great

Rise of Philip II

The weakened Greek city-states created a power vacuum. **Philip II of Macedon** (382–336 BC) seized this opportunity.

Alexander's Conquests

Philip's son, **Alexander the Great** (356–323 BC), tutored by **Aristotle**, inherited his father's powerful army and embarked on one of history's most ambitious military campaigns.

The Age of Hellenism

While Alexander's political empire quickly dissolved, his conquests had an immense and lasting cultural impact, initiating the period of **Hellenism**.
