Important Terms (1/2)

Pax Romana

Latin for "Roman peace." A period of relative political stability and absence of major wars throughout the Roman Empire, from Augustus (27 BC) to Marcus Aurelius (AD 180). Its conditions (safe road...

Messiah

Hebrew for "anointed one," a promised deliverer expected by the Jewish people, traditionally a conquering king.

Koine Greek

The common spoken dialect of Greek, spread by Alexander's conquests, that served as the *lingua franca* (common language) of the eastern Roman Empire, enabling widespread communication of the Gospel.

Incarnation

The Christian doctrine that God became flesh in the person of Jesus of Nazareth.

Apostles

The twelve chief disciples of Jesus, chosen to be the inner circle of his new covenant community and primary witnesses of his life, ministry, and resurrection.

Important Terms (2/2)

Pentecost

A major Jewish festival, fifty days after Passover. For Christians, it marks the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the apostles, signifying the birth of the Church.

Martyr

A person who willingly suffers death rather than renounce their religion or cause. The courage of early Christian martyrs was a powerful witness.

Pharisee

A member of an ancient Jewish sect, distinguished by strict observance of the traditional and written law. Paul was a Pharisee.

The World Into Which Jesus Was Born

Roman Hegemony

Jesus was born at the height of the Roman Empire's power, under Augustus Caesar (27 BC–AD 14), who initiated the **Pax Romana**.

Judea's Context

The Roman province of Judea was governed by a client king, Herod the Great (d. 4 BC), known for rebuilding the Jerusalem Temple and his cruelty.

"The Fullness of Time"

Early Christians saw God's providential hand in these historical circumstances (Galatians 4:4).
