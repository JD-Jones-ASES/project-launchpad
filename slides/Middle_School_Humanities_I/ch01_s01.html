Important Terms (1/3)

Aegean Sea

The arm of the Mediterranean Sea located between the Greek mainland to the west and Asia Minor (modern Turkey) to the east; served as the central highway of ancient Greek civilization.

Asia Minor

The western coastal region of modern Turkey, home to many ancient Greek-speaking communities that were culturally and economically central to the Greek world.

Polis (plural: poleis)

The Greek term for a city-state, an independent political and cultural unit comprising a city and its surrounding territory.

Arable

Land suitable for farming and cultivation.

Acropolis

The defensible hilltop or citadel within a Greek city-state, often housing the city's most important temples and serving as a last-resort fortress (from *akra* = "high" + *polis* = "city").

Important Terms (2/3)

Agora

The open marketplace and civic center located at the foot of the acropolis, where citizens gathered for trade, political discussion, religious ceremonies, and judicial proceedings.

Limen

The Greek word for "harbor"; a vital economic lifeline for coastal city-states.

Viticulture

The cultivation of grapevines, particularly for wine production.

Amphorae (singular: amphora)

Large ceramic jars, typically with two handles and a narrow neck, used by the Greeks for storing and transporting liquids like olive oil and wine.

Metropolis

The "mother city" from which a new Greek colony originated, maintaining cultural and religious ties with its offspring.

Important Terms (3/3)

Drachma

Athens' famous silver coin, widely trusted and circulated as an international currency in the ancient Mediterranean.

Laurium

The rich silver mines located in southern Attica, which provided the silver for Athens' drachma coins.

The Invention of Coinage and Cultural Exchange

Need for Standard Exchange

Widespread commerce required a standard medium of exchange.

Origin

First metal coins minted in Lydia (western Asia Minor) around 600 BC, quickly adopted by Greeks.

Ubiquity

By the 5th century BC, almost every significant polis minted its own coins.

Athens' Drachma

Famous silver coin, featuring Athena and her owl, minted from Laurium silver. Widely trusted and circulated, becoming a de facto international currency.
