Key Concepts (1/2)

What was the Romans' greatest technological innovation in construction?

Opus caementicium, or Roman concrete, combined with their mastery of the arch.

What unique ingredient made Roman concrete different from modern concrete?

Roman concrete (opus caementicium) used volcanic ash called pozzolana, mixed with lime and seawater.

What were two key properties of Roman concrete?

It hardened underwater and grew stronger with age.

How did the Roman arch differ from Greek post-and-lintel construction in transferring weight?

The arch transferred weight sideways into supporting columns, redirecting forces through compression, while post-and-lintel relied on a horizontal beam under...

What significant advantage did the Roman arch offer over Greek post-and-lintel methods?

The arch allowed for much larger spans and the construction of massive structures that Greek methods could not achieve, enabling the scale of the Roman Empire.

Name three major Roman structures built using the arch and concrete technology.

The Pantheon's dome, the Colosseum, and hundreds of aqueducts.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is an aqueduct?

A channel or pipe system for carrying water, typically over long distances, often running on stone arches at a precise, gentle downhill gradient using gravity.

By the late Republic, what proportion of Italy's population was estimated to be enslaved?

Name four significant roles played by slaves in the Roman economy and society.
