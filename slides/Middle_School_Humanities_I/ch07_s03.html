Gregory the Great (c. AD 540–604) (1/2)

First Monk-Pope

Elected Pope in AD 590, he was a highly influential figure in medieval Church history.

Pragmatic Leadership

Organized Rome's charitable activities during severe crises like plague, floods, and Lombard invasions, effectively becoming the civil as well as spiritual governor of Rome and much of Italy.

Missionary Zeal

Sent Augustine of Canterbury to England in AD 596 to convert the Anglo-Saxons.

*Pastoral Care*

His manual for bishops became the standard for clerical conduct throughout the medieval period.

Extensive Correspondence

Over 850 letters survive, demonstrating his active management of Church affairs across the Mediterranean.

Gregory the Great (c. AD 540–604) (2/2)

"Last Roman and First Medieval Pope"

This title reflects his bridging of the ancient Roman world (with its administrative challenges) and the emerging medieval world (with its focus on monasticism and papal authority).
