Gregory the Great (c. AD 540–604) (1/2)

First Monk-Pope

Elected Pope in AD 590, he was a highly influential figure in medieval Church history.

Pragmatic Leadership

Organized Rome's charitable activities during severe crises like plague, floods, and Lombard invasions, effectively becoming the civil as well as spiritual governor of Rome and much of Italy.

Missionary Zeal

Sent Augustine of Canterbury to England in AD 596 to convert the Anglo-Saxons.

Pastoral Care

His manual for bishops became the standard for clerical conduct throughout the medieval period.

Extensive Correspondence

Over 850 letters survive, demonstrating his active management of Church affairs across the Mediterranean.

Gregory the Great (c. AD 540–604) (2/2)

"Last Roman and First Medieval Pope"

This title reflects his bridging of the ancient Roman world (with its administrative challenges) and the emerging medieval world (with its focus on monasticism and papal authority).

Key Concepts (1/2)

How did Western Europe's economy recover after the collapse of the Roman trade system?

Through the development of a new, more self-sufficient economic and social order at the local level, less dependent on distant markets.

What three key aspects characterized the new economic and social order in post-Roman Western Europe?

Feudal agriculture, the monastic economy, and Charlemagne's efforts at standardization and education.

How did the territorial extent of Charlemagne's Carolingian Empire compare to the Western Roman Empire?

By Charlemagne's death in AD 814, the Carolingian Empire covered roughly the same territory as the Western Roman Empire west of the Rhine and Danube rivers.

Who crowned Charlemagne 'Emperor of the Romans,' and when did this significant event occur?

Pope Leo III crowned Charlemagne on Christmas Day, AD 800.

What was the primary agricultural innovation of the early medieval period, replacing the Roman two-field system?

The three-field system.

Key Concepts (2/2)

List three significant advantages of the three-field system over the two-field system.

It increased total production by one-third, improved soil fertility (especially with legumes), spread agricultural risk, and provided more protein in the diet.

What principle guided the economic life of Benedictine monasteries, and what does it mean?

'Ora et labora' ('pray and work'), which required monks to support themselves through their own labor.

Beyond spiritual life, what economic expertise did Benedictine monasteries develop?

Expertise in agriculture, craft production, and meticulous record-keeping.

How did monasteries contribute to technological diffusion in the early medieval West?

They introduced innovations like water mills, improved crop rotation techniques, and systematic drainage into regions that lacked them.
