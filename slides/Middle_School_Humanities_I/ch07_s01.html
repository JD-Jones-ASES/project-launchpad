The Recovery of Western Europe

Context of Collapse

Western Europe faced the collapse of the Roman trade economy, a decline in long-distance markets, and the cessation of the slave supply.

New Order

Recovery was slow, leading to the development of a new, localized, and self-sufficient economic and social order.

Key Aspects

This section focuses on three pillars of this new order: feudal agriculture, the monastic economy, and Charlemagne's efforts at standardization and education.

The Carolingian Minuscule

Consequential Legacy

The development of lowercase letters (minuscule script), which we still use today.

Problem

Before the Carolingian era, Latin manuscripts used diverse, often difficult-to-read regional scripts, leading to copying errors and corrupt texts over generations.

Alcuin of York

Working at Charlemagne's court, he played a key role in developing or standardizing the Carolingian minuscule—a clear, uniform, rounded script legible throughout the empire.

Direct Lineage

The lowercase letters in modern textbooks and writing systems descend directly from the Carolingian minuscule.
