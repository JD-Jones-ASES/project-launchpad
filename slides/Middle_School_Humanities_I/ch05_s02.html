Important Terms (1/2)

Judaea

The southern region of ancient Israel, where Jerusalem was located.

Galilee

The northern region of ancient Israel, where Nazareth was located.

Samaria

The region situated between Judaea and Galilee, characterized by historical hostility between Jews and Samaritans.

Pax Romana

A period of relative peace and stability across the Roman Empire, lasting for roughly 200 years (27 BC – AD 180), which fostered safe travel and communication.

Roman Citizenship

A privileged legal status within the Roman Empire that granted specific rights and protections, including the right to a fair trial and appeal to the Emperor.

Important Terms (2/2)

Jewish Diaspora

The dispersion of Jewish people from their ancestral homeland to communities across the globe, particularly throughout the Roman Empire.

Synagogue Network

The system of Jewish places of worship established by the Diaspora communities in virtually every major city of the Roman Empire.

God-fearers

Gentiles (non-Jews) who were attracted to Jewish monotheism and ethical practices, attending synagogues and worshipping the God of Abraham without fully converting to Judaism.

Koine Greek

The common, everyday form of the Greek language spoken throughout the Hellenistic world and the Roman Empire, distinct from classical Athenian Greek.

Martyrs

Individuals who endure suffering or death for their beliefs, serving as powerful witnesses to their faith, as exemplified by early Christians.

The Geographic Context of Early Christianity

Small Scale of Ministry

Jesus's entire ministry took place in a geographically small area (e.g., Nazareth to Jerusalem is only about 90 miles), smaller than the state of New Jersey.

Key Locations

Bethlehem (south of Jerusalem), Nazareth (in Galilee, the north), Jerusalem (in Judaea, the south), and the Jordan River (site of John's baptisms).

Samaria's Significance

The region of Samaria separated Galilee and Judaea, underscoring the social tension and hostility between Jews and Samaritans, making parables like the "Good Samaritan" especially impactful.
