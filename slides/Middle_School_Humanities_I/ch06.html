Important Terms (1/6)

Crisis of the Third Century (AD 235–284)

Fifty years of extreme instability: 50+ emperors (most murdered), civil war, frontier collapse, trade breakdown, and currency debasement. Set in motion the long decline ending in 476.

Currency Debasement

Reducing the precious metal content of coins while keeping their face value. Roman emperors did this when tax revenues fell short, causing inflation as merchants raised prices. By mid-third century...

Diocletian (r. AD 284–305)

Emperor who stabilized the Empire through drastic reforms: doubled the army, reorganized provinces, divided the Empire between East and West (the Tetrarchy). Also launched the Great Persecution of ...

Tetrarchy

"Rule of four" — Diocletian's system of two senior emperors (Augusti) and two junior emperors (Caesars) ruling the two halves of the Empire simultaneously.

Constantine I (r. AD 306–337)

Eventually reunited the Empire after the Tetrarchy's civil war. Founded Constantinople (330). Issued the Edict of Milan (313). Called the Council of Nicaea (325). Reviewed in Chapter 5.
