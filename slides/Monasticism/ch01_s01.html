Important Terms (2/3)

Poverty (Evangelical Counsel)

The promise to give up personal property and possessions, holding all things in common with the community, and depending on God rather than wealth for security.

Chastity (Evangelical Counsel)

The promise to renounce marriage and family life, dedicating one's heart entirely to God with an undivided love.

Obedience (Evangelical Counsel)

The promise to submit one's own will to the authority of a superior (abbot or abbess) and to the rule of the community. It is often considered the most difficult counsel.

Abbot

From the Aramaic abba, meaning "father." The abbot is the elected head of a monastery, who governs the community according to its monastic rule.

Cell

The monk's private room, typically small and sparsely furnished. From the Latin cella, meaning "a small room or storeroom."

Habit

The distinctive clothing worn by members of a religious order, serving as a visible sign of their consecration to God. From the Latin habitus, meaning "condition" or "dress."

Tonsure

The ritual shaving of part or all of the head as a sign of religious dedication, symbolizing the crown of thorns in the Western tradition and marking a departure from the world.
