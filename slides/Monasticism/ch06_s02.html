Important Terms (1/2)

Cenobitic Community

A monastic community where monks live together under a common rule and an abbot, as prescribed by Saint Benedict.

Regular Monastery

A monastery governed by a *regula* (rule), such as the Rule of Saint Benedict, which dictates the daily life and spiritual practices of the monks.

Canonical Election

The process by which a religious community (like monks electing an abbot) chooses its leader in accordance with canon law and without undue external interference.

Opus Dei

Latin for "the work of God," referring to the communal prayer of the Divine Office, which Saint Benedict deemed the most important activity of a monk's day.

Canonical Hours

The eight prayer offices (Matins, Lauds, Prime, Terce, Sext, None, Vespers, Compline) that structure the monastic day around communal prayer, derived from ancient Jewish and early Christian prayer ...

Important Terms (2/2)

Psalter

The Book of Psalms, comprising 150 psalms. In monastic life, the psalter is chanted as part of the Divine Office, often over a weekly or even daily cycle.

Suffrages

Intercessory prayers offered on behalf of the dead, rooted in the Catholic belief that the prayers of the living can assist souls in purgatory.

Ordo Cluniacensis

The Cluniac customary, or book of customs, which meticulously described how each liturgical celebration was to be performed within the Cluniac order, including specific rubrics and gestures.

Lay Interference

The control or influence exerted by secular lords, nobles, or non-clerical individuals over church appointments, property, or affairs.

Episcopal Interference

The control or influence exerted by local bishops over monasteries, often regarding the appointment of abbots or the administration of monastic property.
