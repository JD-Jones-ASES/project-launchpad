Key Concepts (1/2)

Where did monasticism originally begin, as noted in Chapter 1?

Monasticism was born in the East.

Describe Mount Athos: its location and unique administrative status.

Mount Athos is a self-governing monastic republic located on a rocky peninsula in northeastern Greece.

What is the rule regarding women on Mount Athos, and since when has it been in effect?

No women are permitted to enter Mount Athos, a rule dating back to the eleventh century.

What is a 'diamonitirion'?

A diamonitirion is the special permit required for male visitors to enter Mount Athos.

What liturgical tradition do the monks of Mount Athos follow, and in what languages do they chant the Divine Office?

They follow the Byzantine liturgical tradition, chanting the Divine Office in Greek and Church Slavonic.

What is 'Church Slavonic'?

Church Slavonic is the liturgical language of the Slavic Orthodox churches, developed by Saints Cyril and Methodius in the ninth century.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the Eastern equivalent of the Latin Mass?

The Eastern equivalent of the Latin Mass is the Divine Liturgy.

Define Hesychasm and its core practices.

Hesychasm (from Greek hesychia, 'stillness') is a contemplative tradition in Eastern Orthodoxy centered on inner silence, the Jesus Prayer, and the pursuit o...

Which saint was a significant champion of the hesychast tradition?

Saint Gregory Palamas (1296–1359) championed the hesychast tradition.

What is the 'Jesus Prayer'?

The Jesus Prayer is: 'Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.'

Name two historical challenges Mount Athos survived.

Mount Athos survived the fall of Constantinople (1453), centuries of Ottoman rule, and the turmoil of the modern era.

How did communist regimes in the 20th century impact Eastern monasticism?

Communist regimes suppressed monasteries, confiscated property, and imprisoned or executed monks and nuns, closing major centers like the Kiev Caves Monastery.
