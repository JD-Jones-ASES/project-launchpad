Important Terms (1/3)

Navigatio Sancti Brendani Abbatis

"The Voyage of Saint Brendan the Abbot," a popular 9th-century Latin text detailing Saint Brendan's seven-year journey across the Atlantic. It is primarily a work of theology, not geography.

Paschal

Pertaining to Easter. The Paschal feast refers to the celebration of Easter.

Solemnity

A day of particular religious significance, celebrated with special liturgical rites, such as Easter.

Holy Sabbath

In the context of the *Navigatio*, this refers specifically to Holy Saturday, the day before Easter Sunday, a day of quiet contemplation before the resurrection celebration.

Pillar (Crystal Pillar)

A miraculous, towering structure rising from the sea, interpreted variously as an iceberg, a sign of God's power, or a symbol of the crystalline heavens, showcasing the wonders of creation.

Important Terms (2/3)

Firmament

The vault of heaven; the visible sky, regarded in medieval cosmology as a solid dome or sphere.

Cubits

An ancient unit of measure, roughly eighteen inches, based on the length from the elbow to the tip of the middle finger.

Fog (symbolism)

A recurring motif in the *Navigatio*, often symbolizing the boundary or threshold between the ordinary world and sacred, hidden lands or divine revelations.

Precious stones

Pilgrimage

In a Christian context, a journey to a sacred place. Here, it also refers metaphorically to the journey of earthly life itself, understood as a path toward the heavenly homeland.

Important Terms (3/3)

St. Columbanus (c. 540–615)

A prominent Irish missionary monk who brought the rigorous discipline of Irish monasticism to continental Europe, particularly Gaul, in the late 6th century.

Luxeuil

A monastery founded by St. Columbanus in the Vosges Mountains (modern-day France) around 590, which became a significant center for Irish monasticism on the continent.

Jonas of Bobbio (c. 600–659)

An Italian monk and author of the *Life of Columbanus*, written around 642, providing an important early biography of the saint based on direct testimony.

Saint Columbanus and Monastic Discipline (1/2)

Shift in Focus

From miraculous voyages to the practical discipline of monastic life.

St. Columbanus's Significance

The "greatest of the Irish missionary monks."

Historical Context (c. 590)

Arrived in Gaul (Frankish territories) where the local church was in considerable disorder.

Impact

Brought the "fierce discipline of Irish monasticism" to the continent, significantly influencing European monasticism.

Founding of Luxeuil

Established this monastery in the Vosges Mountains.
