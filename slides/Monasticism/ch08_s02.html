Important Terms (1/2)

Bernard of Clairvaux

A prominent French abbot and a major leader in the Cistercian monastic order, known for his writings on spiritual life and theology.

*On Loving God (De Diligendo Deo)

*: Bernard's most systematic theological presentation of divine love, addressed to Cardinal Haimeric, explaining why and how God should be loved.

Cardinal Haimeric

The Cardinal Chancellor of Rome, to whom Bernard's treatise *On Loving God* was addressed, having requested Bernard's explanation of divine love.

Dignity (as a gift)

Refers to humanity's free-will, which elevates man above other earthly creatures and grants him dominion.

Wisdom (as a gift)

The capacity to perceive one's inherent dignity and understand that it, along with all other good qualities, is a gift from God, not self-acquired.

Important Terms (2/2)

Virtue (as a gift)

The spiritual drive that leads man to eagerly seek and steadfastly cling to God, the ultimate Source of his being and blessings.

Vain-glory

The false pride of taking credit for gifts or accomplishments that one has received from another (God), rather than acknowledging their true source.

Carnal Love

The first of Bernard's four degrees of love; it is natural self-love, where an individual loves himself primarily for his own needs and desires.

Four Degrees of Love

Bernard's influential mystical framework that describes the soul's progressive journey from self-love to a transformative, selfless love of God.
