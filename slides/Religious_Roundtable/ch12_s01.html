Important Terms (1/2)

Ren (仁)

The central Confucian virtue meaning benevolence, humaneness, goodness, or love. It embodies the ideal of being fully human-in-relation to others.

Li (禮)

Rituals, rites, etiquette, or propriety. These are the established norms, customs, and ceremonies that guide social interactions and cultivate respect and order.

Analects

A collection of sayings and ideas attributed to Confucius and compiled by his students after his death. It is the primary source of Confucian thought.

Self-Cultivation (修身)

The ongoing process of moral, intellectual, and spiritual development that an individual undertakes to become a virtuous and genuinely human person.

Reciprocity (恕 - shu)

The principle of mutual exchange and understanding. In Confucianism, it is most famously expressed as the Golden Rule, "What you do not want done to yourself, do not do to others."

In Confucian thought, what constitutes the "substance" of the self?

The substance of the self is constituted by one's relationships (e.g., son/daughter, student, friend, sibling, citizen), which are not external accessories.

What is the central virtue in Confucian thought, and what are its common translations?

The central virtue is ren (仁), often translated as benevolence, humaneness, goodness, or love.

Describe the literal composition of the Chinese character for ren and explain its significance.

The character for ren (仁) is composed of "person" and "two," signifying that humaneness is inherently relational and arises "between people."

Can ren be practiced in isolation according to Confucianism? Explain.

What does Confucianism imply about the nature of one's humanity – is it a fixed possession or something else?

One's humanity is not a fixed, private possession. It is something one either realizes or fails to realize through their actual conduct and treatment of others.

Key Concepts (2/2)

According to Confucius, how does a person achieve genuine humaneness, regardless of education or circumstances?

A person achieves genuine humaneness by consistently showing warmth, loyalty, and care to those around them, rather than merely knowing "the right things."

What concept is essential in Confucianism because ren does not come naturally or easily?

The concept of self-cultivation is essential, as becoming genuinely human requires deliberate effort.
