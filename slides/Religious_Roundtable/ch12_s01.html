Important Terms (1/2)

Ren (仁)

The central Confucian virtue meaning benevolence, humaneness, goodness, or love. It embodies the ideal of being fully human-in-relation to others.

Li (禮)

Rituals, rites, etiquette, or propriety. These are the established norms, customs, and ceremonies that guide social interactions and cultivate respect and order.

Analects

A collection of sayings and ideas attributed to Confucius and compiled by his students after his death. It is the primary source of Confucian thought.

Self-Cultivation (修身)

The ongoing process of moral, intellectual, and spiritual development that an individual undertakes to become a virtuous and genuinely human person.

Important Terms (2/2)

Reciprocity (恕 - shu)

The principle of mutual exchange and understanding. In Confucianism, it is most famously expressed as the Golden Rule, "What you do not want done to yourself, do not do to others."
