Islam: Submission, Faith, and the Oneness of God

Revelations sent to humanity.

Prophets

Messengers who deliver God's word.

The Day of Judgment

The ultimate accountability of the soul.

The Six Articles of Faith (Iman) (2/2)

Divine Decree (Qadar)

God's ultimate will and control.

The Shahada: The Declaration of Faith

Negation

"There is no god but God." This clears the field of false idols, such as wealth, power, or ideology.

Affirmation

"Muhammad is the messenger of God." This affirms that there is a genuine path and revelation to follow.

Revelation and the Quran

The Quran

Understood as the direct, verbatim speech of God transmitted to Muhammad via the angel Jibril (Gabriel). It is considered final, perfect, and uncorrupted.

Previous Scriptures

The Torah and the Gospel are recognized as original revelations but are believed to have been partially corrupted over time through alteration or careless transmission.

Recitation

The word "Quran" means "recitation." Because it is the direct word of God, reciting it in Arabic is considered speaking God's own words.

Quiz Question 1

In the context of Islamic tradition, how is the term 'islam' (surrender) qualitatively distinguished from the surrender of defeat?

A. It is a passive acceptance of one's predestined fate without personal agency.

B. It is a willing and active alignment with a reality greater than oneself.

C. It is a ritualistic submission performed primarily to avoid social consequences.

D. It is an intellectual acknowledgement of God's existence without emotional engagement.

Hint: Consider the difference between being forced to give up and choosing to join something greater.

Quiz Question 2

A. Faith remains static once the Shahada is sincerely recited.

B. Faith is an internal conviction that is entirely independent of external actions.

C. Faith increases through acts of obedience and decreases through sin.

D. Faith is only measurable through the intellectual study of divine books.

Hint: Think about whether faith is a fixed point or something that can grow or shrink.

Hint: What are the common 'false gods' or priorities humans often place above the divine?

Quiz Question 4

D. It allows for the delegation of divine power to secondary spiritual entities.

Quiz Question 5
