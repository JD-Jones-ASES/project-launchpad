The Six Articles of Faith (Iman) (2/2)

Divine Decree (Qadar)

God's ultimate will and control.

The Shahada: The Declaration of Faith

Negation

"There is no god but God." This clears the field of false idols, such as wealth, power, or ideology.

Affirmation

"Muhammad is the messenger of God." This affirms that there is a genuine path and revelation to follow.

Revelation and the Quran

The Quran

Understood as the direct, verbatim speech of God transmitted to Muhammad via the angel Jibril (Gabriel). It is considered final, perfect, and uncorrupted.

Previous Scriptures

The Torah and the Gospel are recognized as original revelations but are believed to have been partially corrupted over time through alteration or careless transmission.

Recitation

The word "Quran" means "recitation." Because it is the direct word of God, reciting it in Arabic is considered speaking God's own words.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the meaning of the Arabic word “islam”?

“Islam” means “surrender” – specifically, a willing, active surrender to a greater reality, chosen for alignment rather than defeat.

What does the Arabic word “muslim” signify?

A “muslim” means “one who surrenders” (to God).

In Islam, what does the Arabic word “iman” mean?

“Iman” means faith, trust, and conviction; it is the interior state that transforms surrender into liberation.

What six core beliefs are involved in “iman” (faith) in Islam?

Iman involves belief in God (Allah), angels, divine books, prophets, the Day of Judgment, and divine decree (qadar).

How is “iman” understood as more than just intellectual assent?

Iman is a state of the whole person – heart, mind, and action – oriented toward God, not merely an intellectual acceptance of beliefs.

According to classical Islamic scholars, how does faith (iman) change?

Faith (iman) increases with obedience and decreases with sin.
