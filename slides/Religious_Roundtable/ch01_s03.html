Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Apocrypha (Deuterocanon)

Additional books included in the Catholic Old Testament canon that are not found in the Protestant canon.

Canon

The authoritative collection of texts recognized by a religious community as sacred scripture.

Codex Sinaiticus

Dating to approximately 330–360, it is the oldest complete manuscript of the New Testament in Greek.

Epistle

A formal letter; the New Testament contains many epistles written by Paul and other leaders to early Christian communities.

Gospel

One of the four accounts (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John) that narrate the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Magisterium

The teaching authority of the Roman Catholic Church hierarchy.

Sola Scriptura

The Protestant principle that Scripture alone is the ultimate authority for Christian belief and practice.

Tanakh

The term used in Judaism for the Hebrew Bible, which Christians refer to as the Old Testament.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How is the Christian Bible best understood, rather than as a single book?

As a library of 66 (Protestant count) or 73 (Catholic count) books.

Over what approximate period of time were the books of the Bible written?

Roughly a thousand years.

What three original languages was the Bible written in?

Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek.

Name three types of literary forms found within the Bible.

Law, history, poetry, prophecy, biography, letter-writing, or apocalyptic vision.

According to Christians, what single subject unifies the diverse books of the Bible?

The relationship between God and humanity, moving from creation through fall, redemption, and restoration.

What are the two major sections into which the Bible is divided?

The Old Testament and the New Testament.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What do Jews call the Old Testament?

The Tanakh, or Hebrew Bible.

Define "canon" in the context of sacred texts.

An authoritative collection of texts recognized by a religious community as sacred scripture.

How many books are in the Old Testament according to the Protestant canon, and how many in the Catholic canon?

39 books in the Protestant canon and 46 books in the Catholic canon.

What are the Deuterocanon or Apocrypha?

Additional works included in the Catholic Old Testament canon, but generally not in the Protestant canon.

What overarching narrative do the books of the Old Testament record?

God's relationship with the people of Israel, including creation, stories of patriarchs and prophets, psalms, and their history as a chosen people.

How many books are in the New Testament, and when were they written?

27 books, written in the first century by followers of Jesus.
