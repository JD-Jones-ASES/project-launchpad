The Four Vedas

Key Philosophical Contribution

Core Focus

The nature of ultimate reality (**Brahman**) and the individual self (**atman**).

Paradoxical Nature

The texts describe Brahman as simultaneously smaller than a grain of rice and larger than the entire universe.

Purpose

They challenge readers to wrestle with ideas that exceed normal categories of thought, moving beyond passive reading to active intellectual engagement.

Overview of the Sacred Library (2/3)

The Dilemma

Arjuna is paralyzed by the prospect of fighting his kinsmen.

The Teaching

Krishna addresses the human condition, the immortality of the self (which is transformed rather than killed), and the importance of performing one’s duty (*dharma*).

Nishkama Karma

The central ethical principle of "desireless action"—performing one's duty without attachment to the results or "fruits" of those actions.

Mahabharata

At approximately 200,000 verses, it is the longest poem ever written. It contains the Bhagavad Gita.

Ramayana

The story of King Rama, his wife Sita, and the struggle between good and evil. These stories are central to the cultural life of the Indian subcontinent and are frequently performed or televised.

Overview of the Sacred Library (3/3)

Puranas

Ancient stories focusing on gods, cosmology, and genealogy.

Dharmashastra

Texts dedicated to law and social order.
