Chapter 4, Section 4

Diversity in Hindu Practice

Study Slides

The Metaphor of the Mountain

Many Paths

There are numerous routes up the mountain.

Different Perspectives

Practitioners at different stages or on different paths may not see one another.

One Summit

All paths, when followed with sincerity, lead to the same ultimate destination.

Henotheism: A Unique Theological Framework

Definition

The worship of a single supreme god while simultaneously acknowledging the existence and reality of other deities.

Comparison

Major Devotional Streams

Tradition. Central Figure(s)

Key Characteristics & Practices

Vaishnavism. Vishnu (the sustainer and preserver) and his avatars (Rama, Krishna).

Focuses on bhakti (devotional love) as the path to moksha. Emphasizes surrender to God and seeing the divine in every human. It is the largest stream worldwide.

Shaivism. Shiva (the destroyer and regenerator).

Focuses on the paradox of Shiva: ascetic and householder, god of death and yoga. Emphasizes yogic discipline, meditation, and finding the divine within.

Shaktism. The Divine Feminine (Shakti), including forms like Devi, Durga, Kali, Lakshmi, and Saraswati.

Views the ultimate reality as a dynamic, maternal energy. Shakti is the creative, active force of the universe.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Avatars

Divine descents where a deity takes human or animal form to restore cosmic order (e.g., Rama and Krishna).

Bhakti

Devotional love; the primary path to spiritual liberation in the Vaishnava tradition.

Devi / Durga / Kali / Lakshmi / Saraswati

Various aspects and forms of the single divine power known as Shakti.

Diwali

The "Festival of Lights," a multi-faith celebration with varying meanings across different traditions.

Henotheism

A religious system that adheres to one supreme god without denying the existence of others.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Moksha

Spiritual liberation or the release from the cycle of rebirth.

Shakti

The creative, energizing, and active force of the universe, personified as the Divine Feminine.

Vishnu

The member of the Hindu trinity responsible for the preservation and sustenance of the universe.

Shiva

A complex deity representing both destruction and renewal, essential for the recreation of the universe.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is a common misconception about Hinduism regarding its deities?

A common misconception is that Hinduism has one pantheon of gods and that Hindus worship all of them equally.

How do Hindu thinkers describe the diversity of spiritual paths within the tradition?

They use the metaphor of a mountain with many paths, all leading to the same summit if followed sincerely.

Which major stream of Hindu devotional practice is centered on Vishnu?

Vaishnavism is centered on Vishnu, the sustainer and preserver of the universe.

What are 'avatars' in the context of Vaishnavism, and who are two beloved examples?

Avatars are divine descents into human or animal form to restore cosmic order. Rama and Krishna are two beloved examples.

What is 'bhakti' and which Hindu stream emphasizes it as the primary path to moksha?

Bhakti is devotional love, and Vaishnavism emphasizes it as the primary path to moksha.

Which stream of Hinduism is currently the largest worldwide?

Vaishnavism is currently the largest stream of Hinduism worldwide.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe the complex nature of Shiva, the central deity in Shaivism.

Shiva is a paradoxical figure, simultaneously destroyer and regenerator, ascetic and householder, and the god of death and yoga.

What fundamental truth does Shiva embody for Shaivas regarding creation?

Shiva embodies the truth that destruction is necessary for renewal: the universe must be dissolved so it can be recreated.

What do Shaivas tend to emphasize in their spiritual practice?

Shaivas tend to emphasize ascetic practice, yogic discipline, and the recognition that the divine is within, accessible through meditation.

What is Shaktism centered on, and what does it consider the ultimate reality?

Shaktism is centered on the Divine Feminine (the goddess in her many forms), considering the ultimate reality to be Shakti, a dynamic, maternal divine energy.

Name some prominent forms or aspects of the Divine Feminine in Shaktism.

Devi, Durga, Kali, Lakshmi, and Saraswati are aspects of a single divine power (Shakti).

How is the image of Kali, dancing on the corpse of Shiva, interpreted in Shaktism?

It is interpreted not as a horror, but as a meditation on the power that drives transformation.
