Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Abolition Movement

A historical movement to end slavery, cited as being deeply influenced by religious thought.

Civic Necessity

The requirement to understand others' beliefs to function effectively as a citizen and neighbor in a diverse world.

Cultural Literacy

The ability to understand and participate fluently in a culture; requires knowledge of religious context to comprehend art, music, and literature.

Historical Literacy

The understanding of past events and civilizations, which is impossible without recognizing the religious threads woven through them.

Philosophical Necessity

The drive to confront fundamental questions of existence, purpose, and justice that science cannot address.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Religiously Unaffiliated

Individuals who do not identify with a specific religious group, though they may still maintain spiritual practices.

Strong Understanding

The ability to momentarily adopt another's worldview and treat their commitments with seriousness, distinguished from mere agreement or weak tolerance.
