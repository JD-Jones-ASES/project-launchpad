Key Concepts (1/2)

Who was Muhammad ibn Abdullah, and in what city was he born?

Muhammad was the Prophet of Islam, born around 570 CE in the city of Mecca in the Arabian Peninsula.

What was Muhammad's early reputation as a merchant, and what nickname did he earn?

He earned a reputation for honesty and reliability, which led to him being known as “Al-Amin”—the trustworthy.

Who was Khadijah, and what was her significance to Muhammad's prophetic message?

Khadijah was Muhammad's wife and the first person to believe his prophetic message after his initial revelation, comforting him.

When and where did Muhammad experience his first divine revelation?

He experienced it in 610 CE, in a cave on Mount Hira outside Mecca.

Which angel delivered the divine revelations to Muhammad, and what was the initial command?

The angel Gabriel (Jibril in Arabic) appeared and commanded Muhammad to 'recite' (iqra'), the root of the word Quran.

How long did the divine revelations to Muhammad continue?

The revelations continued for twenty-three years, from 610 CE until Muhammad's death in 632 CE.

Why did the wealthy, polytheistic merchants of Mecca find Muhammad's message threatening?

His message of monotheism, human equality, and social justice challenged their established religious practices and socio-economic hierarchy.

Describe the 'first Muslim diaspora' and its context.

In 615 CE, a group of early Muslim followers fled persecution in Mecca to Christian Ethiopia, where the Negus (king) granted them protection.

How did Muhammad's role evolve in Medina compared to his time in Mecca?

In Medina, Muhammad transitioned from being solely a preacher to also becoming a statesman and lawgiver, establishing the first Muslim community.

Define 'Ummah' as it was established in Medina.

The Ummah is the community of all Muslim believers, understood as a single spiritual family transcending tribe, race, and nationality.
