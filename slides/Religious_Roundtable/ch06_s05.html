The Influence and Legacy of Taoism: A Study Guide

Study Slides

Traditional Chinese Medicine and Cosmology

Acupuncture and Herbalism

Medical practices rooted in Taoist thought.

Qi

The concept of "vital energy" that flows through the human body.

Arts and Physical Practices

Feng Shui

The art of arranging spaces to achieve harmony with natural forces.

Internal Martial Arts

Disciplines such as **tai chi**, **bagua**, and **xinyi** are considered physical embodiments of the principle of *wu wei*.

Aesthetics

Chinese painting and poetry emphasize Taoist values of emptiness, suggestion, and naturalness.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: According to the text, what is the core Taoist concept behind the practice of acupuncture?

> **Answer:** Acupuncture is rooted in Taoist cosmology and the concept of *qi*, or the vital energy that flows through the body.

Q: List three internal martial arts that serve as embodiments of *wu wei*.

> **Answer:** Tai chi, bagua, and xinyi.

Q: How did Taoism influence the development of Zen Buddhism?

> **Answer:** Taoism contributed an emphasis on direct experience over doctrine, a distrust of elaborate systems, and the practice of sitting quietly with reality.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What is the Taoist view on the relationship between humanity and the natural world?

> **Answer:** Humans are not masters of nature or the "summit of creation." Instead, they are participants in a natural system where the Tao flows through everything equally.

Q: How does the text define the practical application of *wu wei* in the modern world?

> **Answer:** It is defined not as laziness, but as the recognition that not all problems are solved by doing more. It involves stopping, observing, and allowing natural movements to reveal themselves.

C. The pursuit of physical immortality through external alchemy.

Hint: Recall how Taoist thinkers viewed complex intellectual systems and doctrines.
