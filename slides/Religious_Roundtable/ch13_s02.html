Important Terms (1/3)

Shruti

"That which is heard"; directly revealed texts.

Smriti

"That which is remembered"; humanly composed, inspired texts.

Guru

A spiritual teacher.

Parampara

The unbroken chain of teacher-to-student transmission of sacred knowledge.

Rishis

Ancient seers or sages who "heard" the divine truths comprising the Shruti texts.

Important Terms (2/3)

Vedas

The oldest and most authoritative collection of Shruti texts, consisting of hymns, rituals, and philosophical speculation.

Upanishads

Later Shruti texts focusing on philosophical and spiritual concepts, often embedded within the Vedas.

Mahabharata

One of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India, classified as Smriti. The Bhagavad Gita is a part of it.

Ramayana

The other major Sanskrit epic, also classified as Smriti.

Puranas

A genre of Smriti texts containing narratives about Hindu deities, cosmology, genealogies, and religious practices.

Important Terms (3/3)

Bhagavad Gita

A pivotal Hindu scripture within the Mahabharata, a dialogue between Krishna and Arjuna on dharma, duty, and spiritual paths.

Krishna

The divine charioteer and teacher in the Bhagavad Gita, revered as an avatar of Vishnu.

Arjuna

The Pandava prince and warrior in the Mahabharata and Bhagavad Gita, whose moral dilemma initiates Krishna's teachings.

Dharma

Righteous conduct, moral duty, cosmic order, or law. A central theme in the Bhagavad Gita.

Maya

Divine power of illusion or manifestation, referenced by Krishna in the Gita when describing his divine appearances.
