Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the approximate global Muslim population and its proportion of humanity?

Approximately 1.8 billion adherents, representing about one in four human beings.

What is Islam's ranking among the world's religions by number of adherents?

Islam is the second-largest religion on Earth.

Is Islam primarily a Middle Eastern religion? Explain.

No, it is not. The countries with the largest Muslim populations are Indonesia, Pakistan, Bangladesh, and India, and Muslims live on every continent.

What language serves as the language of prayer and scripture for all Muslims globally?

Arabic.

What historical period is often referred to as the 'Islamic Golden Age'?

Roughly between 800 and 1300 CE.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Name three areas of scholarship where Islamic civilization made immense contributions during its Golden Age.

Philosophy (e.g., preserving Aristotle), mathematics (e.g., from Greece and India), and medicine (e.g., preserving Galen).

From what Arabic term does the English word 'algebra' derive, and who was the 9th-century mathematician associated with it?

'Algebra' comes from the Arabic 'al-jabr,' from a treatise by al-Khwarizmi.

How did modern numerals, often called Hindu-Arabic numerals, reach Europe?

They came to Europe through Islamic scholarship.

Who was Avicenna (Ibn Sina), and what was his significant medical contribution?

Avicenna (Ibn Sina) was an Islamic scholar who produced a medical encyclopedia used in European universities for centuries.

What was the potential impact of Islamic scholars on the European Renaissance?

Without Islamic scholars preserving and extending ancient knowledge, the European Renaissance might have had far less to rediscover.

What type of artistic and architectural elements characterize the Alhambra in Granada, Spain?

Intricate geometric tilework and calligraphic poetry on its walls, exemplifying beauty in service of the sacred.
