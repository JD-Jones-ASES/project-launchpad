Class consciousness

Awareness by members of a class (especially the working class) of their common interests and common exploitation. For Marx, class consciousness is the prerequisite for collective action.

Proletariat (proh-lih-TAIR-ee-at)

The class of workers who do not own the means of production and must sell their labor to survive. For Marx, the proletariat is the agent of historical transformation.

Bourgeoisie (boor-zhwah-ZEE)

The class that owns the means of production (factories, land, capital) in capitalist society. Marx argues that bourgeois morality serves bourgeois interests.

Solidarity

In Marxist ethics, the bond of mutual obligation and shared interest among members of the working class. The supreme social virtue.
