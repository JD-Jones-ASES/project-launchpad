The Nature of Jewish Eschatology

Worldly Transformation, Not Escape

The prophetic vision does not describe souls ascending to a heavenly paradise but rather the repair and perfection of the actual world—a world of nations, harvests, cities, and human interactions.

Prophetic Visions

Tikkun Olam

This concept encapsulates the idea of a world that is "broken" and needs to be "repaired." It is the central idea behind humanity's role in this transformation.

Tikkun Olam: Repairing the World

Definition

Tikkun olam is a Hebrew phrase meaning "repair of the world."

Human Obligation

It represents the human duty to actively work towards the healing and improvement of the world. This is not passive waiting for divine intervention but active partnership with God.

Methods of Repair

This obligation is fulfilled through acts of justice (tzedakah), compassion (gemilut hasadim), and ethical behavior in all aspects of life.

Underlying Belief

The concept acknowledges that the world, in its current state, is imperfect and requires human effort to bring it closer to its ideal, God-given state.
