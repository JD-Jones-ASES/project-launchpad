Oral Torah

The interpretive tradition that, according to rabbinic teaching, was given to Moses alongside the Written Torah at Mount Sinai. It was transmitted orally for centuries before being written down in ...

Mishnah (MISH-nah)

The first major written collection of Jewish oral traditions, forming the core of the Oral Torah, compiled around 200 CE.

Talmud (TAL-mud)

A vast body of Jewish civil and ceremonial law and legend, comprising the Mishnah and rabbinic commentaries on it (Gemara). It is a central text of rabbinic Judaism.

Lectio Continua (LEK-tee-oh con-TIN-yoo-ah)

Latin for "continuous reading." The synagogue custom of publicly reading the entire Written Torah scroll from beginning to end over an annual cycle.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simchat Torah (sim-KHAHT tor-AH)

Hebrew for "Rejoicing in the Torah." A Jewish holiday that marks the completion of the annual cycle of public Torah readings and the immediate beginning of the new cycle.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the narrow definition of 'Torah' in Judaism?

In its narrowest sense, the Torah refers to the five books of Moses (Genesis through Deuteronomy).

What is the broader meaning of 'Torah'?

In a broader sense, 'Torah' refers to the entire body of divine teaching given to Israel.

What does the Hebrew word 'Torah' primarily mean?

'Torah' in Hebrew means 'instruction' or 'teaching.'

What were the two main texts into which the Oral Torah was eventually written down and expanded?

The Oral Torah was first written down as the Mishnah (c. 200 CE) and later expanded and debated into the Talmud.

What is the Mishnah?

The Mishnah is the first written compilation of the Oral Torah, codified around 200 CE, which records rabbinic discussions on Jewish law.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the Talmud?

The Talmud is a vast body of Jewish civil and ceremonial law and legend, comprising the Mishnah and rabbinical commentaries (Gemara) on it.

What is 'lectio continua' in the context of Jewish practice?

'Lectio continua,' meaning 'continuous reading,' is the synagogue practice of publicly reading the entire Torah from beginning to end over the course of a year.

Beyond being merely scripture, what multiple roles does the Torah serve for Judaism?

The Torah is Judaism's constitution, history, law code, poetry, and love story with God all at once.

How does the text describe a devout Jew's experience of reading Torah?

How frequently does the word 'Torah' appear in the Hebrew Bible?

The word 'Torah' appears more than two hundred times in the Hebrew Bible, making it the most common word for scripture.

What is notable about Psalm 119 regarding the Torah?

Psalm 119 is the longest chapter in the Bible (176 verses), and every single verse is a meditation on the goodness and importance of Torah.
