Important Terms (1/2)

Torah (tor-AH)

Hebrew for "instruction" or "teaching." In its narrowest sense, it refers to the Five Books of Moses (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy). In a broader sense, it encompasses the entir...

Oral Torah

The interpretive tradition that, according to rabbinic teaching, was given to Moses alongside the Written Torah at Mount Sinai. It was transmitted orally for centuries before being written down in ...

Mishnah (MISH-nah)

The first major written collection of Jewish oral traditions, forming the core of the Oral Torah, compiled around 200 CE.

Talmud (TAL-mud)

A vast body of Jewish civil and ceremonial law and legend, comprising the Mishnah and rabbinic commentaries on it (Gemara). It is a central text of rabbinic Judaism.

Lectio Continua (LEK-tee-oh con-TIN-yoo-ah)

Latin for "continuous reading." The synagogue custom of publicly reading the entire Written Torah scroll from beginning to end over an annual cycle.

Important Terms (2/2)

Simchat Torah (sim-KHAHT tor-AH)

Hebrew for "Rejoicing in the Torah." A Jewish holiday that marks the completion of the annual cycle of public Torah readings and the immediate beginning of the new cycle.

Meaning

*Lectio continua* means "continuous reading" in Latin.

Function

This synagogue practice ensures that the Written Torah remains at the very heart of Jewish communal worship and identity, having done so for over two millennia.

Annual Cycle

The entire Torah is read publicly, from beginning to end, over the course of each year.
