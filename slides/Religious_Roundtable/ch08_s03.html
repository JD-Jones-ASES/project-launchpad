Key Concepts (1/2)

What is unique about the authorship of surviving Norse sacred texts compared to many other traditions?

They were written by Christians who aimed to preserve cultural heritage, not by the pagan practitioners themselves.

What are the main themes of the Völuspá poem found in the Poetic Edda?

Known as 'The Seeress's Prophecy,' it describes the creation of the world and the coming of Ragnarök.

What kind of content does the Hávamál offer, and to whom is it attributed?

It contains practical wisdom, accounts of Odin's sacrifices, and rune-lore, attributed to Odin ('Sayings of the High One').

What is the Prose Edda?

A handbook for aspiring skalds (poets) written by the Icelandic scholar Snorri Sturluson around 1220.

Who was Snorri Sturluson?

An Icelandic scholar who authored the Prose Edda, a key source for understanding Norse mythology and poetry.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the primary purpose of Snorri Sturluson's Prose Edda?

To serve as a handbook for aspiring skalds, explaining Norse mythology in narrative form and cataloging poetic techniques like kennings.

Name the three main sections of the Prose Edda.

Gylfaginning ('The Fooling of Gylfi'), Skáldskaparmál ('The Language of Poetry'), and Háttatal ('List of Verse Forms').

What is contained within the Gylfaginning section of the Prose Edda?

It retells Norse mythology in narrative form, providing a comprehensive overview of the myths.

Beyond a mere writing system, what additional significance did runes hold for the Norse people?

Runes carried magical and sacred significance; the very word 'rune' means 'secret' or 'mystery' in Old Norse.

What was the Elder Futhark?

The earliest runic alphabet, consisting of 24 characters, widely used for inscriptions.

Where were runic inscriptions typically found?

On weapons, grave-markers, jewelry, and stone monuments throughout the Germanic and Scandinavian world.
