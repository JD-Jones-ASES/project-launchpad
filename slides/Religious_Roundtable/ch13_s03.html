Important Terms (1/3)

Poetic Edda

The older collection of Old Norse mythological and heroic poems, compiled in Iceland around 1270.

Prose Edda

A 13th-century handbook for poets, written by Snorri Sturluson, which retells Norse myths and explains poetic forms.

Snorri Sturluson (1179–1241)

A Christian Icelandic chieftain, poet, and historian who authored the Prose Edda.

Kenning

A distinctive compound poetic metaphor used in Norse skaldic verse (e.g., "whale-road" for the sea).

Völva

A Norse female seer or prophetess who practiced divination and magic (sei).

Important Terms (2/3)

Yggdrasil

The World Tree in Norse cosmology, connecting all the realms, on which Odin hung himself to gain knowledge.

Runes

Sacred symbols believed to contain hidden knowledge, which Odin acquired through his self-sacrifice.

Ragnarök

The prophesied ultimate battle of the gods and the end of the world in Norse mythology.

Hávamál ("Sayings of the High One")

A poem within the Poetic Edda that records Odin's own account of his acquisition of wisdom and the runes.

Völuspá ("The Seeress's Prophecy")

A prominent poem in the Poetic Edda where a völva prophesies the creation of the world, its destruction, and rebirth.

Important Terms (3/3)

Skald

An Old Norse court poet, known for composing complex verse often featuring kennings.

Ásatrú

A modern religious movement that revives and practices Norse paganism, grappling with the source texts preserved by Christian hands.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the Poetic Edda?

The older of the two Eddas, a collection of anonymous Old Norse poems compiled in Iceland around 1270, notably in the manuscript known as the Codex Regius.

Name some significant poems found within the Poetic Edda.

Prominent poems include the Völuspá, Hávamál, and Grímnismál, among other mythological and heroic verses.

Why is the Poetic Edda considered a crucial source for Norse mythology?

It is our earliest direct source for Norse mythology.

Who wrote the Prose Edda and when?

Snorri Sturluson, a Christian Icelandic chieftain, wrote the Prose Edda between 1179 and 1241.

What was Snorri Sturluson's primary purpose in writing the Prose Edda?

He wrote it as a handbook for aspiring skalds, intending to retell Norse mythology in narrative form and catalogue traditional kennings and verse-forms.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the significance of kennings in the Norse oral tradition?

Kennings made the oral tradition denser and more resistant to casual paraphrase, requiring memorization and proper understanding to be effective.

Who is a Völva in Norse tradition?

A Völva is a Norse female seer or prophetess, capable of performing sei (a form of divination and magic).

What is the role of the Völva in the poem Völuspá?

The speaker of the Völuspá ('The Seeress's Prophecy') is a Völva whom Odin has raised from the dead to learn the secrets of creation and Ragnarök.

According to the Hávamál, how did Odin obtain sacred knowledge?

Odin hung himself on Yggdrasil, the World Tree, wounded by a spear, for nine nights without food or water, sacrificing himself to himself.

What specific knowledge did Odin gain through his suffering on Yggdrasil?

He gained the runes—sacred symbols containing hidden knowledge.
