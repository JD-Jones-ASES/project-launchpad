The Global Impact and Contemporary Relevance of Buddhism

Study Slides

Key Concepts Overview

Glossary of Important Terms

Ahimsa

The principle of non-harm or non-violence, a core ethical tenet shared with Hinduism and Jainism that has influenced global movements for peace.

Engaged Buddhism

A movement associated with Thich Nhat Hanh that applies Buddhist teachings directly to contemporary social, political, and environmental issues.

Interdependence

The foundational ecological insight in Buddhism that all things are connected; it serves as a basis for Buddhist environmental ethics.

Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy (MBCT)

A therapeutic approach developed in the 1990s that integrates Buddhist meditation practices to address mental health conditions such as anxiety and depression.

Phenomenology

A branch of philosophy that, along with neuroscience, engages with Buddhist analysis of consciousness and perception.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How many adherents does Buddhism claim worldwide, and what is its ranking among religions?

Buddhism claims approximately 500 million practitioners worldwide, making it the fourth largest religion by number of adherents.

Beyond its direct adherents, where has Buddhist influence been increasingly seen in the late 20th and early 21st centuries?

Buddhist ideas, particularly mindfulness meditation, have moved into hospitals, schools, corporate offices, and psychological clinics.

What is considered one of the most intellectually fertile conversations happening today involving Buddhism?

The relationship between Buddhism and modern psychology.

What is MBCT, and what is its connection to Buddhist practices?

MBCT stands for Mindfulness-Based Cognitive Therapy, developed in the 1990s to treat depression and anxiety, drawing directly on Buddhist meditation practices.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the principle of ahimsa, and which other religions share it with Buddhism?

Ahimsa is the principle of non-harm or non-violence, shared with Hinduism and Jainism.

How has the principle of ahimsa influenced social movements?

It has shaped movements for peace and social justice.

What is 'Thich Nhat Hanh's Engaged Buddhism'?

It is an approach that applies the Buddha's teaching directly to contemporary issues such as war, poverty, and environmental destruction.
