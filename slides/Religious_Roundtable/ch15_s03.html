Important Terms (1/2)

Sangha (SANG-hah)

The community of Buddhist practitioners. In Theravada tradition, it often refers to the monastic community; in Mahayana, it includes all practitioners, monastic and lay. It is one of the Three Jewels.

Three Jewels / Three Refuges / Triple Gem

The fundamental objects of Buddhist veneration and commitment: the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha. Taking "refuge" in these three signifies one's commitment to the Buddhist path.

Vinaya (vi-NAH-yah)

The comprehensive code of monastic discipline that governs the daily life, conduct, and interactions of Buddhist monks and nuns.

Bhikkhu (BIK-koo)

A fully ordained Buddhist monk. The feminine equivalent is **Bhikkhuni**. Monks and nuns live according to the Vinaya and form the core of the traditional monastic Sangha.

Important Terms (2/2)

Dharma

The teachings of the Buddha; the path to liberation. One of the Three Jewels.

Buddha

The awakened teacher, Siddhartha Gautama, whose teachings form the basis of Buddhism. One of the Three Jewels.

Anatta

The Buddhist doctrine of "non-self" or "no-soul," teaching that there is no permanent, unchanging self or essence to an individual.

The Traditional Monastic Sangha

Simple Life

Monastics live a life devoid of possessions, focused on simplicity.

The Vinaya

Their communal life is meticulously governed by the Vinaya, a detailed code of monastic discipline. This code covers every aspect of their existence, from eating and speaking to resolving conflicts.
