Important Terms (1/2)

Sangha (SANG-hah)

The community of Buddhist practitioners. In Theravada tradition, it often refers to the monastic community; in Mahayana, it includes all practitioners, monastic and lay. It is one of the Three Jewels.

Three Jewels / Three Refuges / Triple Gem

The fundamental objects of Buddhist veneration and commitment: the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha. Taking "refuge" in these three signifies one's commitment to the Buddhist path.

Vinaya (vi-NAH-yah)

The comprehensive code of monastic discipline that governs the daily life, conduct, and interactions of Buddhist monks and nuns.

Bhikkhu (BIK-koo)

A fully ordained Buddhist monk. The feminine equivalent is Bhikkhuni. Monks and nuns live according to the Vinaya and form the core of the traditional monastic Sangha.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the Sangha in Buddhism?

The community of Buddhist practitioners, and one of the Three Jewels of Buddhism.

How does the understanding of Sangha typically differ between Theravada and Mahayana traditions?

In Theravada, it typically refers to the monastic community; in Mahayana, it extends to all practitioners (monastic and lay).

Name the Three Jewels (or Three Refuges/Triple Gem) of Buddhism.

The Buddha (the awakened teacher), the Dharma (the teaching), and the Sangha (the community).

What is the 'foundational act' of becoming a Buddhist?

Taking refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha.

Why is the Sangha considered one of the three most essential things in Buddhist life?

It is inseparable from the Buddha and the Dharma; the community is the medium in which the spiritual life happens, not a 'bonus feature'.

What is the Vinaya?

The code of monastic discipline for Buddhist monks and nuns, governing every aspect of communal life in the monastery.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Who is a Bhikkhu?

A fully ordained Buddhist monk. The feminine form is bhikkhuni.

What is the primary role of bhikkhus and bhikkhunis in the Sangha?

They follow the Vinaya and form the core of the monastic Sangha, dedicating themselves full-time to the Buddha's path.

What is a Bodhisattva in Mahayana Buddhism?

A being who has vowed to attain enlightenment not just for themselves but for the benefit of all sentient beings.

According to tradition, how did the Sangha begin after the Buddha's enlightenment?

The god Brahma urged the Buddha to teach, and his decision to teach (rather than remain in solitary awakening) was the founding act of the Buddhist community.

Describe the characteristics of the original monastic Sangha.

Monks (bhikkhus) and nuns (bhikkhunis) who left ordinary life, lived without possessions in simplicity, and were governed by the Vinaya.

How is a Buddhist monastery designed to aid in awakening?

Its routines, relationships, and rules are arranged to reduce conditions fostering ego and distraction, while increasing clarity and compassion.
