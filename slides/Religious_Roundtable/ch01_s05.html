Glossary of Key Terms and Figures

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the approximate number of adherents to Christianity worldwide, making it the world's largest religion?

Approximately 2.4 billion adherents, or nearly one in three people on Earth.

Beyond raw numbers, what is the 'deeper claim' regarding Christianity's global significance?

Christianity fundamentally shaped the world, particularly the civilizations of Europe and the Americas, which have had enormous global influence.

What foundational concept of modern law and ethics has deep roots in Christian teaching?

The concept of universal human dignity, which states that every person possesses inherent worth regardless of status or ability.

What Christian teaching is the basis for the concept of universal human dignity?

The teaching that every human being is made in the image of God (imago Dei).

Name two Christian thinkers mentioned who contributed to the development of the natural rights tradition underpinning modern democracy.

Thomas Aquinas and John Locke.

List three major social movements in significant part described as 'theological projects' driven by Christian teaching.

The abolitionist movement, the early labor movement, and the American civil rights movement.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the theological motivation behind these social movements, according to the text?

People read the Gospels and concluded that slavery and oppression were incompatible with Christian teaching.

How is Western art through the nineteenth century related to Christian theology?

The overwhelming majority of Western art through the nineteenth century is unintelligible without understanding Christian theology.

Name two examples of influential Western artworks or artists mentioned that reflect Christian theological influence.

Dante's Divine Comedy, Bach's cantatas, Michelangelo's Sistine Chapel ceiling, or Dostoevsky's novels.

How did early modern scientists like Copernicus, Mendel, and Newton view their scientific work in relation to their Christian faith?

They were devout Christians who understood their scientific work as studying the order God had built into creation.

Name three founders of modern science disciplines mentioned who were devout Christians.

Copernicus (astronomy), Mendel (genetics), and Newton (physics).

In what contemporary debates is Christianity's influence still evident, even in societies that consider themselves secular?

Debates about bioethics, social justice, and human rights.
