Chapter 9, Section 4

Communism as a Structural Religion

Study Slides

Structural Parallels Between Communism and Religion

Religious Component. Communist Equivalent

Description

Cosmology (Cosmic Story). Historical Materialism

A "creation-fall-redemption" narrative: starts with primitive communism, falls with the emergence of private property, and moves toward the revolution.

Hamartiology (The Problem). Alienation and Exploitation

The world is "wrong" due to class exploitation and the corruption of human nature by capitalist conditions.

Soteriology (Salvation). Revolution

Collective, this-worldly liberation from structural oppression; communism in history rather than heaven after death.

Ethics (Moral Demands). Solidarity and Sacrifice

Rejection of bourgeois individualism; willingness to sacrifice personal comfort for the cause and the working class.

Canon (Sacred Texts). The Writings of Marx and Lenin

Foundational texts like The Communist Manifesto, Das Kapital, and the Paris Manuscripts, later supplemented by Lenin and Mao.

Ecclesiology (Community). The Party

The "community of the faithful" organized to carry out the revolutionary mission.

Eschatology (Hope). The Classless Society

The vision of a stateless, earthly paradise where alienation is overcome and history reaches its fulfillment.

Glossary of Terms (1/3)

Alienation

The state of human beings not living as they were "meant to live" due to capitalist conditions and class exploitation.

Apostasy

In a Communist context, the act of defecting from the Party or the cause.

Bourgeois Individualism

A set of values rejected by Communist ethics in favor of collective solidarity and sacrifice.

Canon

A collection of sacred or authoritative texts; for Communism, this includes the works of Marx, Engels, Lenin, and Mao.

Cosmology

An account of the origin and development of the universe/world.

Glossary of Terms (2/3)

Ecclesiology

The study or structure of a religious community or "church."

Eschatology

The study of "last things" or the ultimate hope/end of history; in Communism, this is the classless, stateless society.

Hamartiology

The study of "sin" or what is fundamentally wrong with the world.

Heresy

In a Communist context, the act of criticizing the leader or deviating from established Party doctrine.

Historical Materialism

The Marxist theory of history that views economic and material conditions as the primary forces of change.

Glossary of Terms (3/3)

Proletariat

The working class, identified as a "sacred agent" in the history of liberation.

Soteriology

The study of salvation; in Communism, this refers to this-worldly liberation through revolution.
