At the Roundtable: Comparing Creation in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Taoism

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Brahman (Hinduism)

The undifferentiated, ultimate ground of being from which all things emerge and into which they dissolve in vast, pulsating cycles. It is not a personal creator god, but the primordial source.

Nasadiya Sukta (Hinduism)

A hymn from the Rig Veda that expresses intellectual humility regarding the ultimate truth of creation, suggesting that even the gods may not fully know the origin of the cosmos.

Pratityasamutpada (Buddhism)

The Tao (Taoism)

The nameless, formless, and ineffable source of all reality, prior to all distinctions and descriptions. The cosmos flows naturally and effortlessly from the Tao, without design or decision.

Liberation (Buddhism)

The central goal of Buddhist practice, focusing on escaping the cycle of suffering (dukkha) by understanding and eliminating its causes, rather than contemplating cosmic origins.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Alignment (Taoism)

The appropriate human response to the Tao, involving learning to flow with the natural grain of reality and the universe, rather than striving against it or attempting to control it.

Non-Arbitrary Cosmos (Shared Idea)

Despite their differences in explaining *how* the cosmos came to be, all three traditions agree that the universe is not mere chaos, but possesses an inherent character and order worth understanding.

In Hinduism, the supreme cosmic spirit or ultimate reality of the universe; the fundamental source of all existence.

Pratityasamutpada

The Buddhist concept of "dependent arising" or "interdependent origination," stating that all phenomena arise in dependence upon other phenomena, without a single first cause.

A Spectrum of Creation and Meaning

Divine Origin (Hinduism)

A cosmos pulsing with life and spiritual meaning, emerging from an ultimate ground of being (Brahman), with room for personal deities and intellectual humility.

Self-Arising Cosmos (Buddhism)

A cosmos without a first cause, where everything arises from interdependence, with the focus shifted to overcoming suffering.

Unnamed, Wordless Source (Taoism)

A cosmos flowing from an ineffable Tao without design, inviting human alignment with its natural rhythm.
