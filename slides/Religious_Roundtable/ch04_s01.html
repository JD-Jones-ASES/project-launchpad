Chapter 4, Section 1

The Origins and Evolution of Hinduism

Study Slides

The Indus Valley Civilization (2500–1900 BCE)

Cultural Indicators

Advanced drainage, granaries, and ritual bathing pools.

Legacy

The emphasis on ritual bathing suggests a concern with purity that remains central to Hindu practice. Figurines found in these sites suggest early forms of deities, such as Shiva.

The Aryan Migration and Vedic Religion (c. 1500 BCE)

Vedic Religion

Centered on yajnas (fire sacrifices) performed by Brahmins (priests).

Scripture

The Philosophical Turn: The Upanishads (800–200 BCE)

Focus

Questioning the nature of reality, the self, and the universe.

Significance

These texts became the philosophical heart of the tradition, occurring simultaneously with the rise of Greek philosophy and the Hebrew prophets.

The Construction of "Hinduism"

Etymology

"Hindu" was originally a Persian word for the people of the Indus river region.

Colonial Influence

British colonial officials used the label "Hindu" to group the diverse religious practices of the subcontinent that were not Islamic, Christian, or Buddhist.

Pluralism

Many practitioners identify as Vaishnavas, Shaivas, or Shaktas rather than using the generic label.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Agni

The Vedic god of fire.

Aryans

A cattle-herding people from Central Asia who migrated to India around 1500 BCE, bringing the Vedic religion.

Axial Age

A period (roughly 800–200 BCE) characterized by a profound philosophical turn in multiple global civilizations.

Bhakti

Devotional movements that rose in prominence during the first millennium CE.

Brahmins

Specialized priests responsible for performing ritual sacrifices in the Vedic tradition.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Indus Valley Civilization

An ancient urban civilization (2500–1900 BCE) known for sophisticated cities like Mohenjo-daro and Harappa.

Rishis

"Seers" or sages who composed the philosophical Upanishads.

Sanskrit

An ancient, precisely constructed language of the Vedas and the ancestor of many South Asian and Indo-European languages.

Upanishads

Philosophical texts composed between 800–200 BCE that explored the nature of the self and reality.

Vedas

The earliest layer of Hindu scripture, containing hymns and ritual knowledge.

Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Yajnas

Fire sacrifices performed to honor and propitiate nature gods in the Vedic tradition.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is unique about Hinduism's origin compared to many other religions?

Hinduism does not have a single founding moment, prophet, or decisive event; it is the accumulated religious life of a civilization stretching back more than...

What is the estimated timeframe for the flourishing of the Indus Valley Civilization?

Around 2500–1900 BCE.

Which two major cities are associated with the Indus Valley Civilization?

Mohenjo-daro and Harappa.

What archaeological evidence from the Indus Valley Civilization suggests connections to later Hindu practices?

Advanced drainage systems, granaries, ritual bathing pools (suggesting a concern with purity), and figurines/symbols (possibly an early form of the god Shiva).

Who were the Aryans, and when did they migrate into the Indian subcontinent?

A cattle-herding people who migrated into the Indian subcontinent from Central Asia around 1500 BCE.

What significant cultural contributions did the Aryans bring with them?

A rich oral tradition of hymns, ritual knowledge, and the Sanskrit language.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was the religious culture introduced by the Aryans called?

The Vedic religion.

What was the central practice of the Vedic religion, and who performed it?

Fire sacrifices (yajnas) performed by specialized priests (Brahmins).

Name three important nature gods propitiated in the Vedic religion.

Indra (god of thunder and war), Varuna (god of cosmic order), and Agni (god of fire).

What are the Vedas, and what do they preserve?

The earliest layer of Hindu scripture, preserving the hymns and ritual knowledge of the Vedic religion.

What profound philosophical texts emerged during the period of 800–200 BCE, and who composed them?

The Upanishads, composed by thinkers called rishis (seers or sages).

What kind of questions did the Upanishads explore, differentiating them from earlier Vedic concerns?

Deeper philosophical questions like 'What is reality?', 'What is the self?', and 'What is the relationship between the individual and the universe?'
