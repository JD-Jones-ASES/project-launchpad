Important Terms (1/3)

Confucius

(551–479 BCE) The foundational figure of Confucianism, lived during a time of political fragmentation and societal collapse in China.

Spring and Autumn period

The historical era in which Confucius lived, characterized by warfare between states and a breakdown of traditional order.

Zhou dynasty

An ancient Chinese dynasty whose early period was considered by Confucius a time of ritual order and harmonious society.

Da Tong (Great Harmony / Great Commonwealth)

The Confucian vision of an ideal, perfectly harmonious society, where people live together in peace, virtue, and mutual care.

Book of Rites (Li Ji)

One of the Five Classics of Confucianism, a text compiled by Confucian scholars that contains descriptions of rites and the famous passage on Da Tong.

Important Terms (2/3)

ren (benevolence)

A fundamental Confucian virtue encompassing humanity, compassion, and altruism; central to a harmonious society.

yi (righteousness)

A Confucian virtue representing moral uprightness, justice, and doing what is appropriate.

li (ritual propriety / ritual)

The code of behavior, etiquette, and ritual practices that Confucius believed were essential for maintaining social order and cultivating virtue.

xing shan (goodness of human nature)

Mencius's central teaching that human beings are born with an innate capacity for goodness, like seeds of virtue.

Mencius

(372–289 BCE) The most influential inheritor of Confucius's tradition, known for his teaching on the goodness of human nature.

Important Terms (3/3)

Xunzi

An alternative Confucian thinker who held that human nature tends toward selfishness and requires external shaping through ritual and education.

Neo-Confucian tradition

A resurgence and reinterpretation of Confucianism during the Song dynasty (960–1279 CE), which integrated Buddhist and Taoist metaphysical ideas with classical Confucian ethics.

Kang Youwei

(1858–1927) A reformist Confucian thinker who reimagined Da Tong in his utopian work, Book of the Great Unity, advocating for global governance and equality.

Sun Yat-sen

The founder of modern China, who frequently quoted the Da Tong passage in his political speeches, highlighting its enduring influence.

hexie shehui (harmonious society)

A modern political slogan used by the Communist Party of China, reflecting the deep cultural resonance of the Confucian vision of harmony.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Describe how care for children and elders functions in the Great Harmony.

All people share in caring for one another's children and elders as if they were their own.

How does society support its members, both strong and weak, within the Da Tong vision?

The able are employed for the good of all, and the weak are cared for and supported, not abandoned.

What dual nature does Da Tong possess regarding time?

It is both a description of a legendary past age and a vision for a possible future society.
