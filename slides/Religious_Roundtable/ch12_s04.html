Chapter 12, Section 4: At the Roundtable: Comparing the Human Person

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Confucian Definition of Humanity

Humans are constituted by their relationships, and their humanity (*ren*) is a practice realized through how they treat others.

Norse Pagan Definition of Humanity

Humans are material creatures defined by their origin and fate, embedded in a cosmic web of destiny.

Communist Definition of Humanity

Humans are distinctively defined by their capacity for free, conscious, creative labor and production.

Contrasting Loci of Problem

Norse Paganism locates the problem in the inherent cosmic order (fate, mortality), while Communism locates it in specific, changeable human arrangements (the capitalist system).

Opposing Solutions

Due to their differing diagnoses, Norse tradition calls for courage in the face of the unchangeable, while Communism calls for revolution to change what seems fixed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Confucianism's Middle Ground

It focuses on internal cultivation and relational virtue, distinct from cosmic fate or economic structures. Its solution involves disciplined personal growth within daily relationships.

Important Terms

Ren (Confucianism)

The core concept of humanity or benevolence; not a private possession but a practice realized through ethical conduct in relationships.

Norns (Norse Paganism)

Deities who weave the web of fate, determining the destinies of gods and humans alike.

Alienation (Communism)

Cultivation (Confucianism)

The disciplined effort to develop virtues and improve oneself ethically, particularly in relation to others.

Capitalism

An economic system, seen by Communism as the root cause of human alienation due to its structure of private ownership and wage labor.
