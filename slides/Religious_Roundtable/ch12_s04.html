Chapter 12, Section 4: At the Roundtable: Comparing the Human Person

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Confucian Definition of Humanity

Humans are constituted by their relationships, and their humanity (ren) is a practice realized through how they treat others.

Norse Pagan Definition of Humanity

Humans are material creatures defined by their origin and fate, embedded in a cosmic web of destiny.

Communist Definition of Humanity

Humans are distinctively defined by their capacity for free, conscious, creative labor and production.

Contrasting Loci of Problem

Norse Paganism locates the problem in the inherent cosmic order (fate, mortality), while Communism locates it in specific, changeable human arrangements (the capitalist system).

Opposing Solutions

Due to their differing diagnoses, Norse tradition calls for courage in the face of the unchangeable, while Communism calls for revolution to change what seems fixed.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Confucianism's Middle Ground

It focuses on internal cultivation and relational virtue, distinct from cosmic fate or economic structures. Its solution involves disciplined personal growth within daily relationships.

Important Terms

Ren (Confucianism)

The core concept of humanity or benevolence; not a private possession but a practice realized through ethical conduct in relationships.

Norns (Norse Paganism)

Deities who weave the web of fate, determining the destinies of gods and humans alike.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Confucianism, how is the human person fundamentally defined?

The human person is defined through relationships; we are not isolated individuals but are constituted by our relationships from the beginning.

What is 'ren' in Confucian thought, and how is it realized?

'Ren' is humanity, understood not as a private possession but as a practice, realized or failed in the way we actually treat the people around us.

What does Confucianism identify as the core problem or 'what has gone wrong' with the human person?

The problem is a failure of cultivation: we have not worked hard enough to develop the virtues that make genuinely human life possible.

What are the primary solutions proposed by Confucianism for fixing the human problem?

Education, self-reflection, ritual practice, and the guidance of wise teachers.

How does Norse Paganism define the human person?

The human person is defined through origin and fate, as material creatures (trees given breath, sense, and warmth by three gods) embedded from birth in a web...

What is the primary nature or calling of human beings according to Norse Paganism?

Humans are earthly beings, not primarily spiritual, called to face their fated lives with courage and character.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the fundamental problem or difficulty of existence in Norse Paganism?

The inescapable difficulty of existence in a cosmos that does not promise safety or survival, even to the gods.

What is the Norse Pagan solution to the challenges of human existence?

Living in such a way that you leave behind deeds worth remembering, rather than seeking escape or revolution.

According to Communism, what distinctly defines the human person?

What makes us distinctively human is our capacity for free, conscious, creative production (labor).

Where does Communism locate the fundamental problem of the human person?

The problem is in the economic system, specifically Capitalism, not in the soul or a failure of virtue.

Name the four forms of alienation caused by Capitalism, according to Communism.

Capitalism alienates workers from the products of their labor, from the act of laboring, from their species-being, and from each other.

What is the Communist solution to the problem of alienation?

Outward revolution: the transformation of the economic structures that produce alienation.
