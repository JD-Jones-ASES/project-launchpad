Origins of Confucianism: The Life and Legacy of Master Kong

Study Slides

The Early Life of Kong Qiu

Birth

Kong Qiu (later known as Confucius) was born in 551 BCE in the state of Lu, located in the modern-day Shandong Province of China.

Social Standing

He was born into a family of modest means that had lost its former aristocratic status. This background allowed him to receive a classical education while remaining acutely aware of the social and ...

Motivation

His primary life goal was to bridge the gap between the chaotic reality of his society and the high moral standards described in ancient texts.

A World in Crisis

Political Instability

Rival states competed for dominance, and wars were constant.

Moral Decay

Political leaders frequently ignored or murdered advisors when their counsel was inconvenient.

Empty Ritualism

The ancient rituals intended to maintain social cohesion were either abandoned or performed as "empty theater," lacking their original meaning.

The Root Cause

Confucius concluded that the crisis was fundamentally moral rather than political. He argued that society had lost the "inner virtues" that external rituals were originally designed to reinforce.

Educational Innovation

Inclusivity

He opened his school to any student willing to learn, regardless of their social rank or hereditary privilege.

Curriculum

Beyond studying classical texts, his curriculum focused on character development, aiming to create individuals who could reform government and society from within.

The Search for a Wise Ruler

Conflicting Priorities

His search was largely unsuccessful because the rulers of the time prioritized military strategy over moral philosophy.

Return to Lu

In his old age, he returned to the state of Lu to focus on teaching and editing classical texts. He died in 479 BCE, believing his mission to reform society had failed.

The Merit-Based Civil Service

Function

A rigorous series of tests based on the Confucian classics used to determine eligibility for government office.

Impact

It replaced purely hereditary systems with a merit-based approach, though it still favored those wealthy enough to afford years of study.

Duration

This system remained the foundation of Chinese governance until it was abolished in 1905 CE.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was Confucius's primary occupation for most of his life?

He worked most of his life as a teacher.

What was the goal of Confucius's teachings for his students?

He taught them not only classical texts but also how to become the kind of people who could reform government and society from the inside.

How did Confucius attempt to directly apply his philosophical ideas in government during his lifetime?

He spent thirteen years wandering from state to state, seeking a wise ruler who would give him a position to put his ideas into practice.

He never found a ruler willing to adopt his philosophical guidance, as they preferred military advisors over philosophers.

His students kept his teachings alive, and those students taught students of their own, ensuring the propagation of his ideas.
