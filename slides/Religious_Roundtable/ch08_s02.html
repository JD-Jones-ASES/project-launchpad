Inhabitants

The tree is populated by various beings, including eagles in its branches and serpents gnawing at its roots. The equilibrium of the entire world depends upon the survival of Yggdrasil.

The Afterlife Realms

Valhalla

Odin's great hall in Ásgarðr. Half of those who die in battle are chosen by Valkyries to feast and fight here until they join the gods at Ragnarök.

Fólkvangr

The hall of the goddess Freyja, which receives the other half of those who die in battle.

Hel

A dim, shadowy realm ruled by the goddess Hel. This is the destination for those who die of illness or old age. It is described as a place of quiet, pale existence rather than punishment.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Æsir

The primary family of gods (including Odin and Thor) who reside in Ásgarðr; they are mortal and destined to die at Ragnarök.

Fólkvangr

The hall of the goddess Freyja where half of those slain in battle reside.

Hel

Both the name of the shadowy afterlife realm for those who die of old age or illness, and the name of the goddess who rules it.

Norns

The three weavers of fate: Urðr, Verðandi, and Skuld.

Ragnarök

The prophesied end of the cosmos involving a great battle, the death of the gods, and the eventual renewal of the world.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Valhalla

Odin’s hall in Ásgarðr for the battle-slain who will fight alongside the gods during Ragnarök.

Valkyries

Warrior women who serve Odin and choose which of the battle-dead are taken to Valhalla.

Vanir

The divine family associated with fertility, the sea, and the earth (including Freyr and Freyja).

Wyrd (or Örlög)

The Norse concept of fate; a pattern of destiny woven by the Norns.

Yggdrasil

The immense ash tree (World Tree) that acts as the structural foundation for the nine realms of the Norse universe.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is Yggdrasil in Norse cosmology?

Yggdrasil is an immense ash tree that serves as the structural principle of the Norse universe, holding together the nine distinct realms.

What does the name 'Yggdrasil' mean?

Yggdrasil means 'Odin's horse,' a reference to the gallows upon which Odin 'rode' during his self-sacrifice.

Which three important realms do the roots of Yggdrasil reach into?

The three roots of Yggdrasil reach into Ásgarðr (realm of the Æsir gods), Jötunheimr (realm of giants), and Niflheimr (realm of ice and death).

How is the Norse world structured beyond a simple heaven and earth?

The Norse world is a layered, interconnected web of nine distinct realms, supported by Yggdrasil.

Who are the Æsir, and where do they dwell?

The Æsir are the primary family of Norse gods, including figures like Odin, Thor, Frigg, and Tyr, and they dwell in Ásgarðr.

Name three prominent Æsir gods.

Three prominent Æsir gods are Odin (wisdom, war), Þórr/Thor (thunder, protector), and Frigg (marriage, foresight).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is a distinctive characteristic of the Æsir gods regarding their mortality?

Unlike gods in many other traditions, the Æsir are mortal in a cosmic sense; they are prophesied to die at Ragnarök.

Who are the Vanir, and what are their associated domains?

The Vanir are a second divine lineage, associated with earth, fertility, and the sea, including gods like Freyr, Freyja, and Njörðr.

How did the relationship between the Æsir and Vanir evolve?

After a great war, the Æsir and Vanir made peace and exchanged hostages, leading them to live together in the cosmos while remaining distinct.

Who are the Norns, and what is their role in Norse belief?

What is the Norse concept of 'fate' (wyrd)?

Norse fate is not simple predestination but rather a pattern that unfolds through one's choices and encounters, inescapable even for the gods.

What is Ragnarök?

Ragnarök is the prophesied destruction of the Norse cosmos, a great battle leading to the death of most gods and giants, followed by the world's renewal.
