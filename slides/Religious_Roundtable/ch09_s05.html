Geopolitical Scale

At its peak, approximately one-third of the global population lived under Communist rule. This influence extended across the Soviet Union, China, Latin America, Africa, and Asia.

The Cold War

A defining global conflict from roughly 1947 to 1991, characterized by the struggle between the capitalist United States and the Communist Soviet Union.

Philosophical Inquiry

Communism functions as a worldview that attempts to answer fundamental human questions regarding suffering, the direction of history, and the nature of human life.

Entity/Event. Timeframe

Significance

Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR). 1922–1991

Encompassed fifteen republics across Russia, Central Asia, and Eastern Europe.

People's Republic of China. 1949–Present

The world's most populous country; remains governed by the Chinese Communist Party.

A global contest for supremacy between the United States and the Soviet Union.

Anti-Colonial Movements. 20th Century

Achievements and Contributions

Industrialization

Achieved rapid industrial growth in several nations.

Social Equality

Pursued genuine commitments to gender equality in certain contexts.

Decolonization

Led or supported movements that effectively ended European imperialism.

Human Rights Catastrophes

The Soviet Union

Under Stalin, purges and the Gulag labor camp system resulted in the deaths of millions.

China

Mao Zedong’s Great Leap Forward (1958–1962) led to a famine that killed an estimated 15 to 55 million people.

Cambodia

The Khmer Rouge, led by Pol Pot, systematically murdered roughly 25 percent of the country’s population during the 1970s.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Alienation

A philosophical concept engaged by Marx regarding the dehumanization of workers within a capitalist system.

Cold War

The global struggle between the capitalist United States and the Communist Soviet Union (1947–1991).

Commodity

A term used in the critique of capitalism to describe how human creativity and effort are treated as marketable goods.

Exploitation

A central concept in the Marxist critique of the relationship between capital and labor.

Great Leap Forward

A forced industrialization and collectivization campaign in China (1958–1962) that resulted in a massive famine.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Gulag

The system of forced labor camps in the Soviet Union used during Stalin’s purges.

Khmer Rouge

The followers of the Communist Party of Kampuchea in Cambodia, led by Pol Pot, responsible for mass killings in the 1970s.

Purges

Systematic efforts by leaders like Stalin to eliminate perceived political enemies, resulting in millions of deaths.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How significantly did Communism shape the 20th century?

It shaped it more dramatically than any other ideology, influencing wars, revolutions, social movements, and geopolitics for the last 175 years.

What was the peak global reach of governments that called themselves Communist?

At its peak, roughly one-third of the world's population lived under governments that called themselves Communist.

Name two major Communist states that existed or continue to exist, as mentioned in the text.

The Union of Soviet Socialist Republics (USSR) and the People's Republic of China.

In what types of global movements did Communism play a significant role during the 20th century?

Revolutions, anti-colonial independence movements, and labor organizing across Latin America, Africa, and Asia.

What was the defining global conflict from 1947 to 1991, and what ideologies did it pit against each other?

The Cold War, a contest between the capitalist United States and the Communist Soviet Union.

What are some positive achievements attributed to 20th-century Communist governments?

Rapid industrialization in some countries, genuine commitments to gender equality in certain contexts, and leadership or support for anti-colonial movements ...

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is the significant negative legacy of 20th-century Communist governance?

They were responsible for some of the worst human rights catastrophes in history.

What human rights catastrophes are associated with Joseph Stalin's rule?

Stalin's purges and the Gulag system, which killed millions.

What was Mao Zedong's 'Great Leap Forward,' and what was its impact?

It was a forced industrialization and collectivization campaign (1958–1962) that caused a famine killing an estimated 15 to 55 million people in China.

What was the impact of the Cambodian Khmer Rouge regime under Pol Pot?

They systematically murdered approximately 25 percent of their own country's population in the 1970s.

What is the ongoing debate regarding the atrocities committed under Communist regimes?

Historians and political philosophers debate how much is attributable to Marx's theory versus the specific decisions of leaders and historical circumstances.
