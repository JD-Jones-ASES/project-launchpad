Overview of Key Concepts

Geopolitical Scale

At its peak, approximately one-third of the global population lived under Communist rule. This influence extended across the Soviet Union, China, Latin America, Africa, and Asia.

The Cold War

A defining global conflict from roughly 1947 to 1991, characterized by the struggle between the capitalist United States and the Communist Soviet Union.

Philosophical Inquiry

Communism functions as a worldview that attempts to answer fundamental human questions regarding suffering, the direction of history, and the nature of human life.

Entity/Event. Timeframe

Achievements and Contributions

Industrialization

Achieved rapid industrial growth in several nations.

Social Equality

Pursued genuine commitments to gender equality in certain contexts.

Decolonization

Led or supported movements that effectively ended European imperialism.

Human Rights Catastrophes

The Soviet Union

Under Stalin, purges and the **Gulag** labor camp system resulted in the deaths of millions.

China

Mao Zedong’s **Great Leap Forward** (1958–1962) led to a famine that killed an estimated 15 to 55 million people.

Cambodia

The Khmer Rouge, led by Pol Pot, systematically murdered roughly 25 percent of the country’s population during the 1970s.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Alienation

A philosophical concept engaged by Marx regarding the dehumanization of workers within a capitalist system.

Cold War

The global struggle between the capitalist United States and the Communist Soviet Union (1947–1991).

Commodity

A term used in the critique of capitalism to describe how human creativity and effort are treated as marketable goods.

Exploitation

A central concept in the Marxist critique of the relationship between capital and labor.

Great Leap Forward

A forced industrialization and collectivization campaign in China (1958–1962) that resulted in a massive famine.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Gulag

The system of forced labor camps in the Soviet Union used during Stalin’s purges.

Khmer Rouge

The followers of the Communist Party of Kampuchea in Cambodia, led by Pol Pot, responsible for mass killings in the 1970s.

Purges

Systematic efforts by leaders like Stalin to eliminate perceived political enemies, resulting in millions of deaths.
