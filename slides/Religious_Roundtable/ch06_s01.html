Key Concepts (1/2)

What does the name "Laozi" mean in Chinese?

"Old Master."

According to tradition, when is Laozi believed to have lived?

In the 6th century BCE, roughly contemporary with Confucius.

What was Laozi's legendary role at the royal court of Zhou?

He was an archivist.

According to legend, what prompted Laozi to leave the Zhou court?

Weariness of the court's corruption.

How was the Tao Te Ching created, according to the legend of Laozi?

A border guard, recognizing Laozi's wisdom as he rode west, begged him to write something down before he disappeared.

What is the Tao Te Ching?

A foundational Taoist text attributed to Laozi, consisting of eighty-one short chapters on the nature of the Way.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Why does the historical uncertainty surrounding Laozi's existence align with Taoist philosophy?

It fits a tradition that distrusts rigid definitions and fixed identities, emphasizing the text's importance over biography.

When did Zhuangzi live, and what is notable about his historical certainty compared to Laozi?

He lived around 369–286 BCE, and his historical existence is far more certain than Laozi's.

How does Zhuangzi's writing style contrast with that of the Tao Te Ching?

Where the Tao Te Ching is compressed and cryptic, Zhuangzi's work is exuberant, full of parables, jokes, paradoxes, and flights of imagination.

Describe Zhuangzi's famous "butterfly dream" parable.

He dreams he is a butterfly, then wakes and wonders if he is a man who dreamed he was a butterfly, or a butterfly now dreaming he is a man.

What central Taoist idea does the "butterfly dream" parable illustrate?

It illustrates Zhuangzi's gift for dissolving the boundaries and fixed identities we often take for granted.

What is "Philosophical Taoism" rooted in?

The ideas and writings of Laozi (Tao Te Ching) and Zhuangzi.
