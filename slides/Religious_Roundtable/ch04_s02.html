Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to the Upanishads, what is the one ultimate reality beneath all changing appearances?

Brahman.

Define Brahman in Hinduism.

Brahman is the ultimate, infinite, impersonal reality that underlies and pervades all existence; the ground of all being and the source from which everything...

How does Hinduism describe Brahman in contrast to a conventional 'god'?

Brahman is not a god among gods or a person with feelings to pray to; it is the impersonal essence, like an ocean of which everything is a wave.

What is 'atman'?

Atman is the individual soul or self; the innermost self of every human being.

What is the core philosophical connection between atman and Brahman?

At the deepest level, the atman is ultimately identical with Brahman; the individual self is not separate from the universal reality.

What is the significance of the Upanishadic phrase "Tat tvam asi"?

It means "That art thou," signifying that the vast, infinite reality (Brahman) is ultimately identical with your deepest self (atman).

Key Concepts (2/2)

What is 'maya' in Hindu philosophy?

Maya is the misidentification of the self with the individual personality rather than with Brahman; it is often translated as 'illusion' or 'the veil'.

According to Hindu belief, what is the root of all human suffering?

The misidentification of the self with the individual personality (maya), rather than with Brahman.

Define 'dharma' in its cosmic sense.

Dharma is the cosmic and moral order that holds the universe together.

Define 'dharma' in its personal sense.

Dharma refers to the specific duties and responsibilities that belong to each being according to their nature, stage of life, and social position.

How does acting in accordance with dharma affect one's karma?

Acting in accordance with dharma produces good karma, as it aligns oneself with the structure of reality.

What is 'karma'?

Karma is the law of moral cause and effect; every intentional action generates consequences that will be experienced in this life or future lives.
