Agape (ah-GAH-pay)

The Greek word for the unconditional, self-giving love that is the cornerstone of Christian ethics. It is distinct from friendship love (philia) or romantic love (eros).

Justification

The theological concept, particularly important in Protestantism, referring to God declaring a person righteous in His sight through faith in Christ, not through their own moral achievements or works.

Sanctification

The process by which a Christian grows in holiness after conversion—the ongoing work of the Holy Spirit to conform the believer to the character of Christ.

Sermon on the Mount

Jesus's extended ethical teaching recorded in Matthew 5–7, widely considered the most concentrated summary of Christian moral vision in the entire New Testament.
