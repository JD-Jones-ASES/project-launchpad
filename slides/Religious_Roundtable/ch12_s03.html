Describe 'alienation from the product' under capitalism.

The worker produces things that immediately become the property of the capitalist; the product of their labor confronts them as something foreign and hostile.

Describe 'alienation from the process' of labor under capitalism.

The act of working itself is not free and self-directed but compelled by the need to survive; it exhausts rather than fulfills the worker.

How does capitalism lead to 'alienation from species-being'?

The worker is cut off from the free, creative, conscious labor that defines genuine humanity and is reduced to working like an animal—merely to stay alive.

Explain 'alienation from other human beings' in Marx's analysis.

Under capitalist conditions, everyone is in competition, which stunts the natural human capacity for solidarity and cooperation.
