Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Alienation (Entfremdung)

The estrangement of workers from their labor, products, humanity, and each other under capitalism.

Bourgeoisie

The class that controls the means of production in a capitalist society.

Class Struggle

The constant opposition between social classes with opposing economic interests (e.g., lord and serf, oppressor and oppressed).

Communism

The final stage of social evolution; a classless, stateless society where private property is abolished.

Historical Materialism

The theory that the economic organization of society determines its social, legal, and political structures.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Proletariat

The working class who must sell their labor to the bourgeoisie.

Socialism

A transitional stage of society in which the working class controls the state and the means of production, but classes and the state still exist.

Species-Being (Gattungswesen)

The essential human essence characterized by free, conscious, and purposive creative activity.

Superstructure

The legal, religious, and political institutions built upon the economic base of society.

Surplus Value

The value produced by workers that exceeds their wages, which is captured by capitalists as profit.
