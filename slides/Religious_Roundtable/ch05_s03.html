I. Historical Overview and Early Life

Birth and Lineage

Siddhartha was born around 563 BCE in Lumbini (located in modern-day southern Nepal). He was the son of a ruler of the Shakya clan.

The Prophecy

At his birth, a sage prophesied that Siddhartha would follow one of two paths: becoming a great king or becoming a great spiritual teacher.

III. Awakening and the Buddha

Bodhi (Awakening)

After a night of deep meditation, at approximately age 35, Siddhartha attained enlightenment. He gained a profound understanding of the nature of suffering, its causes, and the path to its cessation.

The Buddha

Upon his awakening, he became the Buddha, a title meaning "The Awakened One."

The First Sermon

The Buddha delivered his first sermon at the Deer Park near Sarnath to five former companions. This event is referred to as "the turning of the wheel of Dhamma."

IV. The Spread of Buddhism

The Sangha

A community of practitioners—including monks, nuns, and laypeople—formed around his teachings.

Death

The Buddha died around 483 BCE at the age of 80.
