Important Terms (1/3)

Transcendence

The quality of the divine being utterly beyond, above, and independent of the material universe, existing outside the limits of human experience and understanding.

Immanence

The quality of the divine being present, active, and inherent within the material world, accessible in human experience and everyday reality.

Tawhid

(Islam) The absolute oneness and uniqueness of God (Allah), emphasizing that nothing is comparable to Him. It underscores divine transcendence.

Shirk

(Islam) The gravest sin in Islam, involving associating anything with God, whether through idolatry or attributing divine qualities to creation. It directly contradicts tawhid.

Qurb

(Islam, Sufism) A mystical term meaning "nearness" to God, pursued through spiritual practices and leading to a felt, experiential intimacy with the divine.

Important Terms (2/3)

Nirguna Brahman

(Hinduism, Advaita) The transcendent, formless, quality-less absolute reality, beyond all attributes and distinctions.

Saguna Brahman

(Hinduism, Advaita) The immanent, personal aspect of the absolute reality, conceived with names, forms, and attributes, making it accessible for worship and devotion.

Atman

(Hinduism, Advaita) The individual soul or self, which, in Advaita philosophy, is ultimately identical with Brahman.

Maya

(Hinduism, Advaita) Illusion; the cosmic power that creates the appearance of the material world and the individual self as separate from Brahman, obscuring the ultimate unity of reality.

Tao

(Taoism) The ultimate, ineffable principle that is the source, pattern, and substance of everything that exists. It is prior to all distinctions and categories, flowing through all things.

Define theological transcendence.

The idea that God is utterly beyond the world—infinite, uncaused, unlike anything we can name, existing outside of and independent from creation.

Define theological immanence.

The idea that God is genuinely near, present in our experience, within the world, responsive to us, and available in everyday reality.

How does Islam typically navigate the transcendence-immanence tension?

Islam holds both poles simultaneously and refuses to let go of either, insisting on both sides based on revealed scripture rather than philosophical argument.

What is 'Shirk' in Islam and why is it considered the gravest error?

Shirk is associating anything with God. It is the gravest error because it violates Tawhid, the principle of Allah's absolute oneness and transcendence.
