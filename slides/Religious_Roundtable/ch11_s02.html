Anicca: Impermanence on a Cosmic Scale

Universal Change

Everything, without exception, is subject to change, decay, and cessation.

Cosmic Cycles

Buddhist cosmology describes immense world-systems that arise, evolve, and dissolve over vast stretches of time. Our current universe is just one of many, and it, too, will eventually pass away.

No Eternal Existence

Stars, galaxies, and even the gods within Buddhist cosmological frameworks are impermanent. There is nothing, at any scale, that possesses a permanent, unchanging existence.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What was the Buddha's famous response when asked metaphysical questions about the universe's origin and extent?

He refused to answer, sitting in silence, a response sometimes called the 'Buddha's noble silence'.

Why is the 'Buddha's noble silence' considered philosophically significant?

It represents a deliberate choice to prioritize practical liberation from suffering over speculative metaphysical inquiries.

What parable did the Buddha use to explain his refusal to answer complex metaphysical questions?

What is the Sanskrit term for 'dependent origination' or 'dependent arising'?

pratityasamutpada

Define 'pratityasamutpada' as one of the Buddha's most important teachings.

It holds that all phenomena arise in dependence on conditions; nothing exists independently, on its own, as a self-sufficient entity.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does 'pratityasamutpada' explain the existence and cessation of phenomena?

When the conditions are present, the phenomenon arises; when the conditions cease, the phenomenon ceases.

According to Buddhism's 'pratityasamutpada', what is its view on a 'first cause' or 'uncaused creator'?

There is no first cause, no uncaused creator; only an endless, beginningless web of interdependence.
