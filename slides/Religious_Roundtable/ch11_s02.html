Pratityasamutpada: The Buddhist Alternative to Creation

Core Principle

All phenomena, without exception, arise in dependence on conditions. Nothing exists in isolation, independently, or as a self-sufficient entity.

Causality

When specific conditions are present, a phenomenon arises. When those conditions cease, the phenomenon ceases. This is a dynamic, interconnected process.

Buddhism's Practical Goal: A Map Out of Suffering

Focus on Suffering

The core of Buddhist teaching addresses the reality of suffering, its causes, the possibility of its cessation, and the path leading to that cessation (the Four Noble Truths).

Urgency over Speculation

The universe's precise origins or ultimate fate may remain unknown, but the suffering of sentient beings is real and immediate. Buddhism's practical orientation prioritizes understanding and resolv...

Anicca: Impermanence on a Cosmic Scale

Universal Change

Everything, without exception, is subject to change, decay, and cessation.

Cosmic Cycles

Buddhist cosmology describes immense world-systems that arise, evolve, and dissolve over vast stretches of time. Our current universe is just one of many, and it, too, will eventually pass away.

No Eternal Existence

Stars, galaxies, and even the gods within Buddhist cosmological frameworks are impermanent. There is nothing, at any scale, that possesses a permanent, unchanging existence.
