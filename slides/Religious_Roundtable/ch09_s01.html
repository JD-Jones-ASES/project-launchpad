Chapter 9, Section 1

The Origins of Marxism and the Development of Communist Theory

Study Slides

Historical Context: Industrial Europe

Urbanization

Millions of people transitioned from agricultural life on farms to industrial labor in cities.

Labor Exploitation

Factories employed children as young as six for fourteen-hour shifts.

Hazardous Conditions

Workers faced dangerous environments, such as collapsing coal mine shafts and machinery that caused frequent injury. Injured workers were typically replaced without compensation.

Wealth Disparity

While factory owners (those who supplied capital) accumulated wealth at unprecedented speeds, the working class suffered from extreme poverty, illness, and low life expectancy.

Global Implementation and Practice

Revolution. Leader

Context and Adaptation

Russian Revolution (1917). Vladimir Lenin

Adapted Marx's theory for a peasant society that lacked full industrialization.

Chinese Revolution (1949). Mao Zedong

Established Communism in the world's most populous nation.

Cuban Revolution (1959). Fidel Castro

Created the only Communist state in the Western Hemisphere to survive into the 21st century.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Bourgeoisie

The class that owns the means of production (factories, land, machinery) and employs workers but does not perform physical labor themselves.

Das Kapital

Marx’s major work (1867) on economics; the later volumes were edited and published by Engels after Marx's death.

Historical Materialism

The theory that history is driven by material economic conditions and production organization rather than ideas or spiritual forces.

Means of Production

The physical assets required to produce goods, such as land, factories, and machinery.

Proletariat

The working class who own no means of production and must sell their labor to the bourgeoisie to survive.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Superstructure

The social institutions (law, politics, religion) that Marx argued are built upon and shaped by the economic base of society.

The Communist Manifesto

An 1848 document authored by Marx and Engels outlining the history of class struggle and the inevitable revolution of the proletariat.
