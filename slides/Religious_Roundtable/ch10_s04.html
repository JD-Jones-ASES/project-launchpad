Faith and Revelation in the Abrahamic Traditions

Study Slides

The Centers of Revelation

Christianity (The Person)

Revelation is centered on the Incarnation. God did not just send a message; God became a human being. Faith is oriented toward the person of Jesus Christ.

Judaism (The Practice)

Revelation is centered on the Torah given at Sinai. It serves as instruction for living as a covenant people. Revelation is viewed as an ongoing process of rabbinic interpretation and wrestling wit...

Islam (The Recitation)

Revelation is centered on the Quran. It is regarded as the direct, verbatim, and final speech of God, preserved perfectly in Arabic and transmitted through the prophet Muhammad.

Nature of Faith and Response

Trust (Pistis)

In Christianity, faith is personal and relational. It is an act of trust that precedes full intellectual understanding.

Doing/Embodying

In Judaism, the appropriate response to revelation is the embodiment of the Torah through the rhythms of daily life and the practice of the covenant.

Submission/Discipline (Iman)

In Islam, faith is expressed through a structured, disciplined life oriented toward God, beginning with the Shahada and manifested in the Five Pillars.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the primary focus of God's revelation in Christianity, according to the text?

God's revelation in Christianity centers on a person, Jesus Christ, with God becoming a human being.

How is 'pistis' (faith) described in Christianity?

'Pistis' is personal, relational, oriented toward Jesus Christ, and it precedes full understanding rather than waiting for it.

What is the supreme act of faith for a Christian?

Trusting in the event of God becoming human and in the person (Jesus Christ) through whom it happened.

What is the primary focus of God's revelation in Judaism?

God's revelation in Judaism centers on a practice: the Torah given at Sinai as instruction for how to live as God's covenant people.

How is the appropriate response to revelation expressed in Judaism?

By doing the Torah and embodying it in the rhythms of daily life, rather than simply believing it.

How does revelation continue in Judaism beyond Sinai?

It continues in every generation of rabbinic interpretation, argument, and wrestling with the text.

God's revelation in Islam centers on a recitation: the Quran, which is God's direct, verbatim, and final speech to humanity.

It was transmitted through the prophet Muhammad and preserved perfectly in Arabic.
