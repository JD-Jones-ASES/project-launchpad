Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: According to the text, which two authors were significantly shaped by their study of Norse literature and language?

> J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis.

Q: What is the D’vergatal, and how did it influence J.R.R. Tolkien?

> The D’vergatal is the "Catalogue of Dwarves" found in the Völuspá (part of the Poetic Edda). Tolkien used it to source names for his characters, including Thorin, Fili, Kili, and Gandalf.

Q: How does the Norse view of the future differ from traditions like Buddhism or Taoism?

> Unlike other traditions that offer a form of ultimate hope (like nirvana or the enduring Tao), Norse tradition centers on Ragnarök—the inevitable end of the gods and the world.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is one of the most visible linguistic legacies of Norse religion in the English-speaking world?

Four days of the week (Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday) are named after Norse divine names.

How does the modern calendar reflect a fusion of ancient cultures?

The calendar reflects a centuries-long fusion of Roman (months), Christian, and Germanic (Norse gods for days) religious culture.

Name two influential authors significantly shaped by Norse literature and mythology.

J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis.

How did J.R.R. Tolkien's academic background influence his famous works?

As a professional scholar of Old Norse and Old English, Tolkien drew deeply on Norse mythology, language, and cosmology for works like The Lord of the Rings.

List some common Norse mythological elements found in modern fantasy fiction.

The Nine Worlds, the World Tree, the heroic warrior code, and a shadowy afterlife.

From which Norse text did J.R.R. Tolkien borrow many names for his dwarves and Gandalf in The Hobbit?

The Dvergatal ('Catalogue of Dwarves') found within the Völuspá, a part of the Poetic Edda.

Key Concepts (2/2)

What was Tolkien's intention when using names from the Poetic Edda in his own stories?

He was paying homage to a tradition he loved and studied deeply, rather than stealing.

What unique philosophical concept distinguishes Norse religion from most other traditions discussed in the textbook?

It is the only tradition that places the death of the gods themselves (Ragnarök) at the center of its worldview.

How does the Norse concept of 'ultimate hope' compare to traditions like Christianity, Buddhism, or Communism?

Unlike traditions offering heaven, nirvana, moksha, an enduring Tao, or a classless society, Norse tradition offers Ragnarök—the end of the gods and the worl...

What is Ragnarök in Norse mythology?

Ragnarök is the prophesied end of the gods and the world, followed by a renewal. The gods currently living will not survive it.

In the face of the gods' inevitable demise, how did the Norse advise people to live?

To live as if it mattered: keep faith with community, develop skills, courage, and generosity, die well, and hope one's deeds are remembered.

What is the Hávamál, and what does it represent?

The Hávamál, or 'Sayings of the High One' (Odin), offers an earthy, practical, and honest vision of life emphasizing the importance of one's deeds.
