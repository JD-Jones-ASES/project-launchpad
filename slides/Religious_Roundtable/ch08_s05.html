Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: According to the text, which two authors were significantly shaped by their study of Norse literature and language?

> J.R.R. Tolkien and C.S. Lewis.

Q: What is the *D’vergatal*, and how did it influence J.R.R. Tolkien?

> The *D’vergatal* is the "Catalogue of Dwarves" found in the *Völuspá* (part of the *Poetic Edda*). Tolkien used it to source names for his characters, including Thorin, Fili, Kili, and Gandalf.

Q: How does the Norse view of the future differ from traditions like Buddhism or Taoism?

> Unlike other traditions that offer a form of ultimate hope (like nirvana or the enduring Tao), Norse tradition centers on Ragnarök—the inevitable end of the gods and the world.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What are the three specific traits the Norse encouraged individuals to develop in the face of inevitable failure?

> Courage, skill, and generosity.

Q: According to the *Hávamál*, what is the one thing that never dies?

> The fame of a dead man’s deeds.
