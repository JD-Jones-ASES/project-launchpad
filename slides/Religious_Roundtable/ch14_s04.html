Christianity: Grounding Morality in Love

Source of Obligation

God's command and example, specifically embodied in the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus. It's a relational grounding: one owes love because they have first been loved by God.

Nature of Morality

Not primarily about following external rules, but about internal transformation. The Beatitudes illustrate the character and state of being of a person formed by love for God and neighbor.

Ethical Demand

To be transformed by love, reflecting God's nature. This goes beyond mere compliance to a deep internal change.

Taoism: Grounding Morality in Nature

Source of Obligation

The Tao, the fundamental, deep pattern of reality that precedes and underlies all human constructs and conventions.

Nature of Morality

The good life is achieved not through more effort or rules, but by removing obstacles to one's natural state and aligning with the Tao.

Ethical Demand

To achieve "naturalness," to "be what it is" without force. The concept of "wu wei" (effortless action) describes the sage who acts in perfect harmony with the Tao, not by conscious compliance but ...

Communism: Grounding Morality in History

Source of Obligation

The objective interests of the working class, who produce all value in society but are exploited. Morality is a product of actual historical and material conditions, not eternal truths.

Nature of Morality

Arises from conditions of exploitation and the possibility of liberation.

Ethical Demand

Solidarity with the working class and collective action to achieve the good life that capitalism has denied. Moral obligation is historical and material.
