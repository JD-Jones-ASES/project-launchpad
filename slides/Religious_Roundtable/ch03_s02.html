The Rightly Guided Caliphs

This term refers to the first four leaders after Muhammad: Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali. Their era is considered the "golden age" of Islamic governance.

Internal Diversity

Sunni Islam includes four major schools of legal interpretation known as **madhabs**. While they agree on core tenets, they differ on specific details of practice.

Concept of Leadership

Shias believe leadership should have remained within Muhammad’s immediate family, specifically his cousin and son-in-law, Ali ibn Abi Talib.

The Imams

Shias are led by Imams—descendants of Ali who possess divinely guided authority to interpret revelation. While they do not receive new prophecy, they are seen as spiritual guides.

Major Traditions and Streams (2/2)

The Battle of Karbala (680)

A pivotal tragedy where Ali’s son, Husayn, was murdered. This event is commemorated annually during **Ashura**, a period of intense mourning and reflection.

Demographics

Shias form majorities in Iran, Iraq, Bahrain, and Lebanon.

Core Objective

Sufism focuses on inward practice, devotion, and contemplation to achieve a direct experience of God’s presence, often referred to as **qurb** (nearness).

Historical Impact

Sufism was a primary vehicle for the peaceful spread of Islam into Africa, Central Asia, and South Asia.
