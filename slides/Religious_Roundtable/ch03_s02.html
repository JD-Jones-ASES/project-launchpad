The Five Pillars of Islam

Pillar. Description

Purpose and Significance

Shahada. The Declaration of Faith: "I bear witness that there is no god but God, and I bear witness that Muhammad is the messenger of God."

Sincere recitation makes one a Muslim; it marks the beginning and end of life.

Salat. Daily prayer performed five times (dawn, midday, afternoon, sunset, and night).

Muslims orient toward the Ka'ba in Mecca, using prayer to sanctify the day.

Zakat. Almsgiving of 2.5% of annual savings to those in need.

Not mere charity, but a mandatory "purification of wealth" for those above a wealth threshold.

Sawm. Fasting from food, drink, and physical pleasures during daylight hours of Ramadan.

Commemorates the first Quranic revelation; fosters physical discipline and solidarity with the hungry.

Hajj. The pilgrimage to Mecca, required once in a lifetime for those physically and financially able.

Involves rituals like circling the Ka'ba; symbolizes human equality and the Day of Judgment.
