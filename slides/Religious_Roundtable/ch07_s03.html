Sacred Texts of Confucianism

Study Slides

The Four Books (Sishu)

Text. Title in Chinese

Key Focus and Characteristics

The Analects. Lunyu

A collection of sayings and conversations between Confucius and his students. It is the most direct source of Confucius's voice and is characterized by a "deceptively simple" format of brief exchanges.

The Great Learning. Daxue

Describes the cultivation of virtue through a concentric vision of ethics. It outlines "eight steps" for expanding virtue from the individual to the family, the state, and eventually the world.

The Doctrine of the Mean. Zhongyong

A philosophical exploration of moderation, balance, and sincerity. It links human virtue to the metaphysical order of the cosmos.

Mencius. Mengzi

Collected sayings of Mencius, the most important disciple of Confucius. It argues that human nature is fundamentally good, containing "seeds" of virtue like compassion and a sense of right and wrong.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (1/2)

Q: How did Confucius describe his relationship to the wisdom he taught?

Confucius described himself as a transmitter rather than a creator, stating, “I transmit but do not create. I believe in and love the ancients.”

Q: Who was responsible for compiling the Four Books into the core of the imperial examination system?

The Neo-Confucian philosopher Zhu Xi (1130–1200 CE).

Q: What is the central argument regarding human nature found in the Mencius?

Mencius argued that human nature is fundamentally good, and that virtues like compassion and a sense of right and wrong are present in every person from birth, requiring only proper cultivation.

Short-Answer Practice Questions (2/2)

Q: What is the "concentric vision of ethics" described in The Great Learning?

It is the idea that the cultivation of virtue begins with the individual and expands outward to the family, the state, and finally to "all under heaven."

Q: Why did Confucius consider the Book of Songs essential for a person's development?

He believed the poetry within the book was essential for cultivating sensitivity, moral imagination, and proper feeling.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Analects (Lunyu)

Meaning "selected conversations," this is the primary source of Confucius's own thoughts and conversations, compiled by his students.

Concentric Ethics

The Confucian model of virtue starting with individual self-cultivation and expanding to the family, state, and world.

Eight Steps

The specific process outlined in The Great Learning for achieving peace "under heaven," beginning with the investigation of things.

Mencius (Meng Ke)

The most significant disciple of Confucius, known for the doctrine that human nature is inherently good.

Neo-Confucianism

The movement associated with Zhu Xi that reorganized the Confucian canon and revitalized the tradition's philosophical depth.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Spring and Autumn Annals (Chunqiu)

A historical chronicle of the state of Lu, traditionally attributed to Confucius, used to teach moral lessons through history.

Zhu Xi

The 12th-century philosopher who selected and commented on the Four Books, creating the standard for Confucian education for centuries.

Key Concepts (1/2)

How did Confucius view his relationship with the ancient texts he studied?

Confucius saw himself as a transmitter of ancient wisdom, not its author. He stated, 'I transmit but do not create.'

What are the two main collections of sacred texts in Confucianism?

The Four Books and the Five Classics.

Who compiled and commented on the Four Books, and what was their significance in later Confucian tradition?

The Neo-Confucian philosopher Zhu Xi compiled and commented on them. They became the core curriculum of the imperial examination system.
