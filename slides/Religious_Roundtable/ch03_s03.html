The Nature of the Quran

Etymology

The word "Quran" means "recitation."

Revelation

The text was revealed to Muhammad by God through the angel Gabriel between the years 610 and 632.

Language and Translation

Preservation and Compilation

Oral Tradition

From its inception, the community memorized the revelations. A person who has committed the entire Quran to memory is honored with the title hafiz ("one who preserves").

Early Records

During Muhammad’s life, fragments were recorded on various materials, including flat stones, bones, and palm leaves.

Codification

Structural Organization

Ordering

Rather than being chronological or thematic, the suras are arranged roughly from longest to shortest.

Exception

The first chapter, Al-Fatiha, is a short opening chapter.

Scale

The text contains approximately 77,000 words and covers topics ranging from theology and ethics to law, history, and the nature of the afterlife.

The Hadith and Sunnah

Hadith

Sunnah

This refers to the "practice" or the way of life of the Prophet Muhammad. It serves as the model for Muslim behavior and is preserved through the Hadith.

Sharia (Islamic Law)

Literal Meaning

The term translates to "the path to the watering hole."

Purpose

It is intended not as a restrictive system, but as a "road to life" providing guidance in all areas of human conduct.

Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is the fundamental Muslim belief about the Quran's origin and nature?

Muslims believe the Quran is the direct, verbatim word of God, revealed to Prophet Muhammad through the angel Gabriel.

What is the literal meaning of the Arabic word "Quran"?

The word "Quran" means "recitation."

How is the Quran considered unique compared to other religious scriptures in how it was 'written'?

Muslims believe God spoke the Quran, and Muhammad merely recited it, rather than Muhammad composing it under divine inspiration or writing it himself.

Why is the Quran considered 'untranslatable' in a strict sense?

The original Arabic text is considered the Quran itself. Translations are seen as interpretations or meanings, not the Quran itself.

What is significant about the Arabic language of the Quran?

Its Arabic is held to be miraculous in its beauty and precision.

What is a 'hafiz' in Islam?

A hafiz is a Muslim who has memorized the entire Quran, which is a cherished honor.

Key Concepts (2/2)

How was the Quran initially preserved during the lifetime of Prophet Muhammad?

It was memorized by the community and also written down on various available materials like bones, flat stones, and palm leaves.

Who initiated the compilation of the Quran into a single document after Prophet Muhammad's death?

The first caliph, Abu Bakr, ordered the fragments compiled.

Who produced the authoritative written text of the Quran that Muslims use today, and approximately when?

The third caliph, Uthman ibn Affan, produced the authoritative text around 650 CE, sending standardized copies to major cities.

How is the Quran organized structurally?

It is organized into 114 chapters called suras, arranged roughly from longest to shortest, with the exception of the opening chapter, Al-Fatiha.

Name three broad categories of topics addressed in the Quran.

The Quran addresses theology, ethics, law, history, stories of earlier prophets, the nature of Paradise and Hell, and the duties of the believer.

Why is the Quran meant to be heard as much as it is read?

Its rhythms, sound patterns, and repeated refrains are an integral part of its impact and spiritual message.
