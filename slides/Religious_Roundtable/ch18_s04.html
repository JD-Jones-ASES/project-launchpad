Important Terms (1/2)

Eschatology

The part of theology concerned with death, judgment, and the final destiny of the soul and humankind; beliefs about the "end times" or the ultimate future of history.

Supernatural

Attributed to some force beyond scientific understanding or the laws of nature; in this context, divine action that transcends normal historical processes.

Tikkun Olam

A Hebrew phrase meaning "repair of the world" or "healing the world." In Jewish thought, it refers to the human obligation to engage in acts of kindness, justice, and social action to perfect and t...

Messiah/Messianic Future

In Abrahamic religions, a divinely appointed leader or savior; the "messianic future" refers to an anticipated era of peace, justice, and redemption brought about by or associated with the Messiah.

Great Harmony

In Confucianism, an ideal social and political state characterized by peace, order, moral excellence, and societal flourishing, achieved through human virtue and ethical governance.

Jewish Eschatology: Hope in Human-Divine Partnership

Source of Hope

Judaism envisions a messianic future that emerges from a dynamic partnership between human action and divine faithfulness. The repair of the world is understood as occurring within history.

Mechanism of Change

This future is not an abrupt, supernatural intervention, but the culmination of a continuous process. Humanity plays a crucial role.

Confucian Eschatology: Hope in Human Effort Alone

Source of Hope

Confucianism maintains that humanity possesses the inherent capacity, guided by wisdom and virtue, to create an ideal society known as the "Great Harmony." This vision relies solely on human effort.

Mechanism of Change

The path to the Great Harmony involves cultivating virtue, adhering to proper rituals, and being guided by the wisdom of ancient sages.

Absence of the Divine

Crucially, there is no divine promise, supernatural guarantee, or messianic figure in Confucian eschatology. It is a purely humanist vision.

The Central Tension: Divine Dependence vs. Human Responsibility

• Does hope gain power from its dependence on God, or does this dependence risk fostering passivity?

• Is human optimism about progress realistic given humanity's historical track record?

Eschatology's Revelatory Power

• Who or what drives historical change.

• The nature and role of God (or lack thereof).

• The inherent nature and potential of human beings.

• The very meaning and source of hope itself.
