The Global Impact and Philosophical Foundations of Hinduism

Study Slides

Historical and Demographic Significance

Longevity

Hinduism is recognized as the world’s oldest living religious tradition, having been practiced, refined, and debated for a duration exceeding that of most other civilizations.

Scale

With approximately 1.2 billion practitioners, it is the third-largest religion globally.

Psychological Insights

The *Bhagavad Gita* offers an analysis of human suffering rooted in attachment and identifies detachment as a path to freedom, anticipating several Western psychological insights.

The Darshanas

These are the great schools of Indian philosophy characterized by systematic and vigorous debate on topics such as consciousness, time, and the nature of reality.

Reform Movements

Major figures such as Swami Vivekananda, B. R. Ambedkar, and Mahatma Gandhi fiercely challenged caste-based discrimination.

While the Indian constitution officially bans caste discrimination, the practice persists in various forms, requiring an honest examination of both the tradition's resources and its failures.
