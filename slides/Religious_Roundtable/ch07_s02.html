Confucianism—Virtue, Ritual, and the Shape of Society

The Primary Virtues

Virtue. Meaning

Function

Ren. Benevolence, humaneness, goodness

The inner quality of genuinely caring for others; the source of social harmony.

Li. Ritual propriety

The proper forms of conduct and ceremony that express and cultivate inner virtue.

Yi. Righteousness

The practical wisdom to discern and act on what is right, even at personal cost.

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Li

Ritual propriety; the proper forms of conduct, ceremony, and social interaction. It is the outward expression of inner virtue.

Ren

Benevolence, humaneness, or compassion; the supreme virtue from which all proper human relationships flow.

Xin

Faithfulness; the primary virtue governing the relationship between friends.

Xiao

Filial piety; the devotion, care, and respect shown by children toward their parents and ancestors.

Yi

Righteousness or moral rightness; the virtue of discerning and doing what is genuinely right in a specific situation.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Zhengming

“The rectification of names”; the insistence that words must be used accurately to ensure clarity of thought and stability in society.
