Important Terms (1/2)

Tao (道)

Literally "The Way" or "The Path." In Taoism, it refers to the ultimate, indescribable, and nameless source, principle, and natural order of the entire universe.

`Wu Wei` (無為)

Chinese for "non-action" or "effortless action." It describes a state of spontaneous, natural, and unforced action that is in perfect harmony with the flow of the Tao.

Laozi (老子)

The legendary "Old Master" traditionally credited as the author of the Tao Te Ching and the founder of Taoism. His historical existence is debated among scholars.

Tao Te Ching (道德經)

The foundational sacred text of Taoism, an 81-chapter work attributed to Laozi, which presents the core philosophical concepts of the Tao, wu wei, and living in harmony with nature.
