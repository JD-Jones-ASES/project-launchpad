Sacred Texts and Traditions of Judaism

Study Slides

I. The Sacred Library: The Tanakh

Section. Name Translation

Description

Torah. The Law

The innermost core of the faith, believed to be revealed by God to Moses at Sinai.

Nevi'im. The Prophets

Historical accounts and the writings of prophets who interpreted history and called for faithfulness.

Ketuvim. The Writings

A collection of diverse literature including poetry, wisdom, and narrative.

II. The Oral Torah and the Talmudic Tradition

Living Tradition

The principle "These and these are the words of the living God" illustrates that even minority or unadopted opinions are preserved as sacred.

Timelessness

In Jewish academies (yeshivas), students engage with these ancient debates as if the rabbis—some of whom lived hundreds of years apart—are in the same room.

III. Worship and the Siddur

The Siddur

The prayer book used to organize the three daily services (morning, afternoon, evening) as well as liturgies for Shabbat and holy days.

The Minyan

A quorum of ten adults required for communal prayer.

Collective Orientation

Jewish prayers are focused on the collective rather than the individual. They use "our God" rather than "my God" and focus on the restoration of Israel and humanity rather than personal salvation.

VI. Glossary of Important Terms (1/3)

Gemara

The rabbinic commentary on the Mishnah; part of the Talmud.

Ketuvim

"The Writings"; the third section of the Tanakh.

Minyan

A quorum of ten adults required for certain communal prayers.

Mishnah

The first written collection of the Oral Torah, compiled around 200 CE.

Nevi'im

"The Prophets"; the second section of the Tanakh.

VI. Glossary of Important Terms (2/3)

Oral Torah

The interpretations and applications of the law believed to have been given to Moses alongside the Written Torah.

Rabbi

A teacher and interpreter of the Torah; the central figure of Jewish life following the destruction of the Temple. The word means "my master" or "my teacher."

Siddur

The Jewish prayer book.

Talmud

The multi-volume compilation of Jewish law, ethics, and theology comprising the Mishnah and the Gemara.

Tanakh

The Hebrew Bible, consisting of the Torah, Nevi'im, and Ketuvim.

VI. Glossary of Important Terms (3/3)

Torah

"The Law" or the Five Books of Moses; the innermost core of the Tanakh.

Yeshiva

A Jewish academy of study where students engage with sacred texts and rabbinic debates.
