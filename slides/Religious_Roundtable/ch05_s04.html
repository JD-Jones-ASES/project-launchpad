Key Concepts (1/2)

What are the three major traditions, also called "vehicles" or "yanas," in Buddhism?

Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana.

What core aspect of Buddhism remains central across all traditions, even as it spread and changed?

The Four Noble Truths.

Which of the Three Vehicles is the oldest surviving tradition?

Theravada.

What does 'Theravada' mean?

'The Teaching of the Elders.'

In which regions is Theravada Buddhism dominant?

Southeast Asia, including Sri Lanka, Thailand, Myanmar (Burma), Laos, and Cambodia.

What is the primary path to liberation emphasized in Theravada Buddhism?

Monastic life, adhering closely to the Pali Canon.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Who is the ideal figure in Theravada Buddhism?

The arhat.

Define an arhat in Theravada Buddhism.

An individual who, through rigorous practice, achieves nirvana for herself.

How do Theravadans respond to critics who describe their tradition as 'the narrow vehicle'?

They reject this framing, asserting that their path is precise, demanding, and genuinely transforming.

When and where did Mahayana Buddhism emerge?

Roughly five centuries after the Buddha in India.

What does 'Mahayana' mean?

'The Great Vehicle.'

In which regions did Mahayana Buddhism primarily spread?

North and east into China, Japan, Korea, and Vietnam.
