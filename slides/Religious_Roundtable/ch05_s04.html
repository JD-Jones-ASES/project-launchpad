Chapter 5, Section 4

Sacred Texts of Buddhism: The Pali Canon and Beyond

Study Slides

I. The Pali Canon (Tipitaka)

Basket. Name

Content and Purpose

First Basket. Vinaya Pitaka

Contains the rules governing monastic life. It dictates how monks and nuns should eat, dress, speak, settle disputes, and conduct ceremonies.

Second Basket. Sutta Pitaka

Comprises thousands of discourses (teachings) attributed to the Buddha, recorded by his disciples after his death.

Third Basket. Abhidhamma Pitaka

A technical philosophical analysis of mind, matter, and reality. It was developed by later scholars to systematize the Buddha's teachings.

II. Mahayana and Tibetan Scriptural Traditions

Heart Sutra

A concise meditation focused on the concept of emptiness, recited daily in millions of monasteries.

Lotus Sutra

A text that expands the vision of who is capable of attaining enlightenment.

Diamond Sutra

A philosophical dialogue concerning the nature of perception and the practice of non-attachment.

Terma

Known as "hidden treasure" texts, these are believed to have been revealed by realized masters.

Bardo Thodol (Tibetan Book of the Dead)

A guide for navigating the various states of consciousness between death and rebirth, emphasizing the mind's continuity beyond a single lifetime.

V. Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Abhidhamma Pitaka

The third "basket" of the Tipitaka; a technical, systematized philosophical analysis of reality and the mind.

Bardo Thodol

Also known as the *Tibetan Book of the Dead*; a guide to the states of consciousness experienced between death and rebirth.

Dhammapada

A collection of 423 poetic verses found in the Sutta Pitaka that distills the Buddha's teachings.

Pali

A language closely related to Sanskrit, used for the oldest complete collection of Buddhist scriptures.

Pali Canon

The oldest complete collection of Buddhist scriptures, preserved by the Theravada tradition.

V. Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Sutta / Sutra

A discourse or teaching attributed to the Buddha. *Sutta* is the Pali form; *Sutra* is the Sanskrit form.

Sutta Pitaka

The second "basket" of the Tipitaka; contains thousands of recorded discourses of the Buddha.

Terma

"Hidden treasure" texts in Tibetan Buddhism, revealed by realized masters.

Tipitaka

Meaning "Three Baskets"; the collective name for the three parts of the Pali Canon.

Vinaya Pitaka

The first "basket" of the Tipitaka; contains the practical rules for monastic life.
