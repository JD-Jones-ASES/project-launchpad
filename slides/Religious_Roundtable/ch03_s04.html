Sunni Islam

The Rightly Guided Caliphs

This term refers to the first four leaders after Muhammad: Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali. Their era is considered the "golden age" of Islamic governance.

Internal Diversity

Sunni Islam includes four major schools of legal interpretation known as madhabs. While they agree on core tenets, they differ on specific details of practice.

Shia Islam

Concept of Leadership

Shias believe leadership should have remained within Muhammad’s immediate family, specifically his cousin and son-in-law, Ali ibn Abi Talib.

The Imams

Shias are led by Imams—descendants of Ali who possess divinely guided authority to interpret revelation. While they do not receive new prophecy, they are seen as spiritual guides.

The Battle of Karbala (680)

A pivotal tragedy where Ali’s son, Husayn, was murdered. This event is commemorated annually during Ashura, a period of intense mourning and reflection.

Demographics

Shias form majorities in Iran, Iraq, Bahrain, and Lebanon.

Sufi Islam

Core Objective

Sufism focuses on inward practice, devotion, and contemplation to achieve a direct experience of God’s presence, often referred to as qurb (nearness).

Key Practices

Historical Impact

Sufism was a primary vehicle for the peaceful spread of Islam into Africa, Central Asia, and South Asia.

Commonalities and Unity

• Recitation of the same Quran.

• Performing the same daily prayers.

• Fasting during the month of Ramadan.

• Facing the Ka'ba during prayer.

• Adherence to the theological principle of the unity of the ummah.

Glossary of Key Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

What fundamental question led to the most significant and lasting division in Islamic history?

The question of who had legitimate authority to guide the Muslim community (ummah) after Muhammad's death in 632.

What percentage of Muslims worldwide adhere to Sunni Islam?

Roughly 85–90%.

In Sunni Islam, what is a 'caliph' and how is one chosen?

A 'caliph' (meaning 'successor' or 'deputy') is a qualified leader chosen by community consensus to govern Muslim affairs and protect the faith.

Who are the four Rightly Guided Caliphs in Sunni Islam?

Abu Bakr, Umar, Uthman, and Ali.

What era is regarded by Sunnis as the 'golden age' of Islamic governance?

The era of the first four Rightly Guided Caliphs.

Are all Sunni Muslims unified in legal interpretation? Explain.

No, Sunni Islam contains four major schools of legal interpretation (madhabs) that differ on details of practice.
