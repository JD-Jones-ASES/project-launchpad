Recommended Starting Points

Christianity. *The Holy Bible* (King James Version)

Gospel of Mark; Paul’s Letter to the Romans

Judaism. *The Torah* (Five Books of Moses)

Exodus (Exodus narrative and the Law at Sinai)

Islam. *The Quran* (Abdel Haleem translation)

Sura 1 (Al-Fatiha); Sura 2 (Al-Baqara)

Hinduism. *Bhagavad Gita* (Barbara Stoler Miller translation)

Chapter 2 (Philosophical heart of the text)

Buddhism. *Dhammapada* (F. Max Müller translation)

The entire collection of verse sayings

Taoism. *Tao Te Ching* (Laozi); *Chuang Tzu*

*Tao Te Ching* (All 81 chapters); *Chuang Tzu* (Chapter 3: "The Cook and the Ox")

Confucianism. *The Analects* (D. C. Lau translation)

Books 1, 2, and 4

Glossary of Key Terms (1/3)

Alienation

A philosophical concept developed by Karl Marx, specifically discussed in his *Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844*.

Al-Baqara (Sura 2)

A significant chapter of the Quran providing theological foundations for Islam.

Al-Fatiha (Sura 1)

The opening chapter of the Quran.

The Analects

The primary aphoristic text of Confucianism, attributed to Confucius.

Bhagavad Gita

A central primary text of Hinduism; Chapter 2 contains its philosophical heart.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/3)

Dhammapada

A collection of the Buddha’s sayings in verse.

Eightfold Path

A core Buddhist teaching introduced in Thich Nhat Hanh’s secondary literature.

Four Noble Truths

Fundamental Buddhist principles covered in introductory secondary sources.

Gylfaginning

A section of the *Prose Edda* detailing the Norse cosmos.

Hávamál

A section of the *Poetic Edda* containing the practical wisdom of Odin.

Glossary of Key Terms (3/3)

The Law at Sinai

The legal framework given to the Israelites, found within the book of Exodus in the Torah.

Tao Te Ching

The 81-chapter primary text of Taoism, traditionally attributed to Laozi.

Torah

The Five Books of Moses, serving as the primary text of Judaism.

Völuspá

A section of the *Poetic Edda* concerning the creation and doom of the world.
