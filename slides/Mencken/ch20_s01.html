Chapter 20, Section 1

H.L. Mencken’s “The Will to Peace” (Notes on Democracy)

Study Slides

Liberty vs. Safety

Safety

The primary desire of the common man.

The Herd

The "warm, reassuring smell" of collective security.

Protection

The presence of a "policeman" to guard him from his own impulses and others.

The Philosophical Foundation

Friedrich Nietzsche

Influenced Mencken with concepts of the “will to power” and “herd morality.”

Arthur Schopenhauer

Influenced Mencken with his pessimism and the concept of the “will to live” as the fundamental human drive.

Rhetorical Strategy: The "Weary Diagnostician"

Resigned Authority

He treats the "case" of democracy as closed.

Savage Amusement

Moments of contempt expressed through grotesque comedy.

Flattery of the Reader

He addresses the "educated skeptic," making the reader feel part of an intellectual "elect."

The Penitentiary Analogy

Forfeiture of Liberty

Like a convict, the citizen has traded freedom for lack of conflict.

Obedience over Merit

Privileges are earned through compliance, not character.

Efficiency over Justice

A "well-managed" government is one that runs smoothly, regardless of whether it is just.
