Quiz Question 2

According to the text, what is a primary intellectual characteristic of democracy regarding the concept of 'the insoluble'?

A. It becomes unfashionable or even infamous to suggest a problem cannot be solved.

B. It is embraced as a necessary reality of the human condition.

C. It is relegated to the realm of private faith rather than public policy.

D. It is used as a justification for the maintenance of the status quo.

Hint: Think about how democratic society views a person who identifies a problem but offers no solution.

Quiz Question 3

How does Mencken characterize the difference between ancient despotisms and modern democracy regarding human 'sin' and 'poverty'?

A. Ancient systems accepted these as immutable realities, while democracy views them as curable by law.

B. Ancient systems utilized these evils to maintain power, while democracy seeks to ignore their existence.

C. Ancient systems viewed these as moral failings, while democracy views them as economic inefficiencies.

D. There is no functional difference; both systems use mystical emanations to control the mob.

Hint: Focus on which system believes in the 'immutability' of worldly evils versus which one attempts to 'repeal the laws of nature'.

Quiz Question 4

What paradox does Mencken identify in the philosophy of democracy regarding the relationship between 'John Doe' and 'Richard Roe'?

A. It provides a means to rise above others while simultaneously removing the benefits of doing so by declaring everyone equal.

B. It forces individuals to join groups for protection while demanding they act as independent agents.

C. It encourages the pursuit of wealth while morally condemning those who achieve it.

D. It creates a government from the mob that then acquires the power to destroy the mob.

Hint: Look for the section discussing 'pretty logical gymnastics' and the history of democratic states.
