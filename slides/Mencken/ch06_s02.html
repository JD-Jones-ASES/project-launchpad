Chapter 6, Section 2

The Biological Basis of Democratic Man

Study Slides

I. Key Concepts and Themes

Inability to Conceptualize Universal Good

He cannot formulate a concept of "good" that is not his own private advantage.

Cruelty and Cowardice

His congenital fear is easily translated into cruelty, manifesting as a desire to suppress heretics and radicals.

Hostility Toward Superiors

While charitable to his own kind, he is described as "wolfish" when dealing with those he perceives as "betters."

The Urban Proletariat

Regarded as slightly superior to the peasant because they had the "wit to escape" rural life. They may be "run amok by ideas," even if those ideas are senseless.

The Yokel (Peasant)

Described as the "lowest caste among civilized men" and the "bulwark of all democratic states." His mind is characterized by primitive animism, superstition, and an absolute intolerance for dissent...
