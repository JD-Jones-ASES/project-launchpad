Glossary of Important Terms

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Mencken's 'The New Psychology', what primary force governs the mind of the common man?

Fear

What rhetorical strategy does Mencken employ in Chapter 4 to build upon the zoological classification used in Chapter 3?

A mock-scientific approach using experimental psychology.

Which psychologist and founder of behaviorism does Mencken cite to lend authority to his polemic?

Dr. John B. Watson

How does Mencken use the observations of Dr. Eleanor R. Wembridge?

To highlight the limits of verbal comprehension in lower-class populations.

What was the dominant school of American psychology in the 1920s?

Behaviorism

Behaviorism rejected the traditional method of _____ in favor of observing external behavior.

Introspection

Key Concepts (2/2)

How does behaviorism explain human behavior according to John B. Watson?

As observable stimulus-response patterns.

What specific aspect of Watson's behaviorism does Mencken ignore to support his own arguments?

Environmentalism

Mencken's prose style in this section combines the mock-scientist persona with that of the _____.

Entertainer

What is the rhetorical purpose of the comic 'vaudeville' set pieces in Mencken's clinical analysis?

To keep the reader amused and demonstrate that emotions govern even the educated.

How does Mencken describe the relationship between reason and emotion in his own readers?

He appeals to their sense of humor to prove they are governed by feeling rather than logic.

Why does Mencken occasionally use 'tactical retreats' or hedges in his arguments?

To create the impression of being a fair-minded investigator while dismissing competing views.
