Key Concepts and Themes

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Blackguard

A villain or scoundrel. Mencken uses this archaic term to describe the Apostle Peter.

Cumulative Sentence

A sentence that starts with a main clause followed by successive phrases or clauses that elaborate on the initial point.

Hosannas

Shouts of praise; used ironically by Mencken to describe the mob’s celebration of tyrants.

Improvident

Lacking foresight; failing to provide for the future.

Litotes

A figure of speech featuring understatement through the use of negation (e.g., "moved an inch" to describe stagnation).

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Nether

Lower or situated below; often carries overtones of the underworld or hell.

Peroration

The concluding part of a speech or argument, intended to inspire emotion and summarize key themes.

Stammvater

A German term for ancestor, progenitor, or patriarch.

Tricolon

A rhetorical device involving a series of three parallel words, phrases, or clauses.
