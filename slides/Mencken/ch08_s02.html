The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 8, Section 2

Liberty and Democratic Man

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Themes (1/2)

Courage

The willingness to fight and potentially shed blood for freedom.

Self-reliance

The capacity to stand alone and be "precarious" in one's own territory.

Resolution and Enterprise

The strength to endure the "dreadful loneliness" and responsibilities associated with being the master of one's own world.

The Capacity for Doing Without

The ability to prioritize independence over comfort.

Country Gentlemen

Figures like Thomas Jefferson who formulated the underlying philosophies of modern states.

Key Concepts and Themes (2/2)

Intellectuals

Poets and philosophers who aided in articulating these concepts.

Absolute Monarchs

Paradoxically, the text notes that figures like Frederick the Great provided the first legal support for concepts such as free speech.

Hint: Focus on the psychological qualities Mencken associates with 'the free man' as a 'sort of god in his little world.'
