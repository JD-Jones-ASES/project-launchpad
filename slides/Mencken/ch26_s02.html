The Rhetorical Toolkit

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Aristotle

Greek philosopher (384–322 BCE) whose Rhetoric is the foundational treatise on persuasion; Book III specifically addresses prose style.

Bathos

A rhetorical shift where the writer moves from a high, serious tone to a trivial or absurd one to create a specific effect.

Diction

The specific choice of words; Mencken often used a mix of "learned" (aesthetics) and "crude" (barnyard) diction to create contrast.

Homo boobiens

Mencken’s hyperbolic term for the democratic citizen, used to illustrate his contempt for "the mob."

Peroration

The final part of an argument, designed to inspire or provide a forceful conclusion.

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Semicolon as Fulcrum

A structural technique where a semicolon balances two opposing halves of an antithetical construction.

Sibilance

A sound pattern involving "s" sounds that can create a specific mood, such as the "sinister" tone noted in Mencken’s "sweet, soft music."

Style as Argument

The principle that linguistic texture—rhythm, irony, and diction—communicates authority and intelligence before the literal argument is processed.
