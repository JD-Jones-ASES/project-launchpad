Key Concepts and Central Arguments

The Transformation of Values

In *ressentiment*, the envious person declares the thing they cannot have as "evil" and their own deprivation as "virtue."

The Sour Grapes Metaphor

Similar to the fox who declares unreachable grapes sour, the democratic moralist declares unattainable pleasures sinful to justify their own lack of access to them.

Master vs. Slave Morality

The Iowa Farmer

Represents raw, violent envy. He views the "sinful" pleasures of the city from his desolate farm and uses the government (via Prohibition or blue laws) to suppress those pleasures.

The City Proletarian

Represents managed and sublimated envy. This figure is kept docile through the "welfare state"—a system of social services Mencken likens to *panem et circenses* (bread and circuses).
