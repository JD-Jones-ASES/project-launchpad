Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Antinomianism

A rejection of socially established morality; in the text, it refers to the perceived moral or sexual freedom of urban women.

Glabella

The smooth part of the forehead between the eyebrows; the rural man "feels along his glabella" for the onset of a headache after drinking crude stimulants.

Hind / Yokel / Husbandman

Terms used by Mencken to describe the rural or agricultural worker, often characterized as inferior or driven by resentment.

Mann Act

A federal statute used as an example of democratic moralism fueled by the rural man's resentment of urban sexual freedom.

Panem et Circenses

"Bread and circuses"; a metaphor for the superficial means of gratification used by a government to appease the masses.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Plutocracy

The wealthy class that controls a state; Mencken describes them as experts in "dealing with the proletarian."

Prohibition

The 18th Amendment banning alcohol; Mencken cites it as the ultimate example of "democratic moralism" and rural envy.

Puritanism

Defined by Mencken as the "twin" of democracy; the impulse to regulate the private behavior of others through legislation.

Sackbuts

Ancient wind instruments; used here to illustrate the romantic, musical atmosphere of the city cabaret that the rural man envies.

Yellow-backs

Cheap, sensationalist novels or pamphlets that described urban vice, fueling rural indignation.
