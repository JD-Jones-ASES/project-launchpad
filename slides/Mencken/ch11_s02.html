The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 11, Section 2

The Two Kinds of Democracy

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Core Themes

Mass Formulation is Impossible

Large groups cannot formulate complex measures *en masse*.

Historical Precedent

Even lauded institutions like the New England town-meeting were dominated by a few men of initiative—often demagogues and fanatics—rather than the collective wisdom of the folk.

Inherent Limitations

The general public remains "congenitally unable" to comprehend complex problems or consider them in an unbiased manner.
