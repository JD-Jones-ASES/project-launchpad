Chapter 11, Section 2

The Two Kinds of Democracy

Study Slides

The Failure of "Purer" Democracy

Mass Formulation is Impossible

Large groups cannot formulate complex measures en masse.

Historical Precedent

Even lauded institutions like the New England town-meeting were dominated by a few men of initiative—often demagogues and fanatics—rather than the collective wisdom of the folk.

Inherent Limitations

The general public remains "congenitally unable" to comprehend complex problems or consider them in an unbiased manner.

Key Concepts (1/2)

What is Mencken's central question in Part II of Notes on Democracy?

He asks whether the distinction between direct and representative democracy truly matters.

What is Mencken's direct answer to his central question about direct vs. representative democracy?

He answers that the distinction is "simply rhetoric," setting up the argument for the rest of Part II.

What primary rhetorical strategy does Mencken use to demolish the distinction between direct and representative democracy?

He uses analogy, comparing political theory to more familiar situations to make his point vivid and concrete.
