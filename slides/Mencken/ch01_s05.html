H.L. Mencken and the American Mercury

Study Slides

I. Key Concepts and Historical Context

Target Audience

College students, young professionals, and those who wished to distance themselves from small-town values and traditional "pieties."

Editorial Stance

The magazine provided readers with "permission to feel superior" to the masses. Mencken used his monthly editorials to attack a consistent set of targets, including:

Legislative Shifts

The 18th Amendment (Prohibition) and the 19th Amendment (Women’s Suffrage).

Social Conflict

The massive revival of the Ku Klux Klan and the 1925 Scopes Trial, which highlighted the clash between science and fundamentalism.

Technological Change

The rapid adoption of radio, cinema, and the automobile.
