The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 4, Section 2

Mencken’s "The New Psychology" and the Democratic Mind

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Summary (1/2)

The Ascent

Every soul attempts to climb toward "gaudy and seductive flags" representing success or enlightenment.

The Majority

Most people find the climb too agonizing and give up early, settling for easier comforts like "Golf," "Rotary," or "Fundamentalism."

Governance

In an aristocratic society, those at the top of the poles govern. In a democratic society, government is the function of those who have remained near the ground.

Bitterness

Directed toward genuine superiority.

Admiration

Directed toward fraud or superficial success.

Key Concepts and Summary (2/2)

Phylogenic Origin

Fear is an inherited race experience. Dr. John B. Watson suggests it may stem from "arboreal man’s" ancestral hazards, such as falling from trees.

Evolution of Fear

While infants fear loud noises or loss of support, adults develop fears of "strange men," "strange ideas," and "powers and principalities of the air."
