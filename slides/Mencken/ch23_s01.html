Chapter 23, Section 1

Corruption Under Democracy (Analysis of H.L. Mencken)

Study Slides

Democracy as "Wholesale Bribery"

Official Bribery

Buying labor leaders with offices.

Legislative Bribery

Buying the followers of those leaders with favorable legislation.

Special Interest Trading

Providing specific benefits to manufacturers, religious groups (Methodists and Catholics), and farmers to maintain power.

The Industrialization of Public Opinion

The Source

It originates as the "mob's fears."

The Process

It is "piped" to central factories where it is "flavored and colored."

The Result

It is "put into cans," representing a standardized, manufactured product designed to ensure compliance rather than reflect genuine self-government.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Anaphora

The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of successive clauses or sentences; used by Mencken to create a drumbeat effect of accumulation.

Conies

An archaic term for rabbits; used by extension to mean dupes or "gulls" who are easily cheated.

Coup de grâce

A final, decisive blow that ends suffering; used to describe Mencken’s final metaphor of the "canning" of public opinion.

Friedrich von Gentz

A German diplomat and secretary to Prince Metternich; cited by Mencken as a historical example of someone who became disillusioned with democratic governance.

Immemorial

Existing beyond the reach of memory; ancient. Mencken uses this to describe the mob's fears as a permanent feature of human nature.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Prehensile

Capable of grasping or wrapping around; used to describe the greedy nature of minority groups in a democracy.

Rooked

A colloquial term meaning cheated or swindled.

Tergiversations

Evasions or shifts of allegiance; a Latinate term used to describe political dishonesty in a scholarly tone.
