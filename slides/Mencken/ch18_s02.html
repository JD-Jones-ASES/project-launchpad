The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 18, Section 2

The Rewards of Virtue in Democratic Politics

Study Slides

Regional Differences in Political Integrity

Established States

In states where party fidelity is historically strong, such as Georgia or Vermont, men of genuine dignity and ability are effectively barred from public life.

Border and Newer States

Honest men have a slightly better chance in these regions, though they still face "incredible bitterness" and opposition from all political factions.

The Role of the Press

• Praises fraudulent men and ideas while denouncing those respected by "their betters."

• Treats honest political or social combat as a "sham battle" for publicity.

• Views any editor who displays courage or honesty with "incredulous hostility."

The "Cult of Incompetence"

• Law-making becomes a series of "panics" or "orgies."

• Public service is a "refuge for prehensile morons" focused on personal gain.

• Mastering a job makes a public servant an enemy to their less capable peers.

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Anti-Saloon League

A powerful Prohibitionist organization that used its payroll to influence judges, attorneys-general, and other high officers.

Comstocks

A group or movement described as engaging in "sinister and dishonest censorship of literature."

Katzenjammer

A term used to describe the post-war state of confusion or distress (specifically the "post-war Katzenjammer" in Missouri).

Medical Trust

A mythical entity (including the Mayo brothers and Johns Hopkins) that the author was accused of being paid by when he exposed "quackeries" such as osteopathy and chiropractic.

Prehensile Morons

A derogatory term for those in public service who are focused solely on "getting theirs" (personal gain).

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Rectitude

Morally correct behavior or thinking; righteousness.

The Cult of Incompetence

A concept by Emile Faguet describing a system that favors the unqualified and treats law-making as a series of panics.

Trimmer

A person who adapts their views to the prevailing political climate for personal or professional survival; a flatterer.

Venality

The state of being open to bribery or corruption.

Whiskey Ring / Beer Trust

"Bugaboos" or labels used by Prohibitionists to accuse their opponents of being in the pay of the alcohol industry.

Key Concepts (1/2)

According to Mencken, what happens to an honest, intelligent man who enters public life under a democracy?

Virtue is not rewarded but punished because the mob cannot comprehend disinterested public service and assumes all public men are corrupt.

Why does an honest man suffer more from the mob's assumptions than an actual crook?

The honest man suffers more because the crook at least understands how to 'play the game' of assumed corruption.

What rhetorical purpose does Mencken's autobiographical turn serve in this section?

It transforms the argument from abstract political theory into personal witness, adding a layer of direct testimony to his critique of democracy.
