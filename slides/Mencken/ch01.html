Important Terms (1/3)

Irony

Saying one thing while meaning another, or the opposite. **Verbal irony** is a gap between what is said and what is meant; **situational irony** is a gap between expectation and reality. Mencken fr...

Hyperbole

Deliberate exaggeration for emphasis or effect, not meant to be taken literally. Mencken used hyperbole to shock readers and highlight the absurdity of his targets.

Antithesis

The placement of contrasting ideas in parallel grammatical structures to sharpen an argument by emphasizing the opposition between two concepts.

Periodic Sentence

A sentence that builds suspense by withholding its main idea until the end, typically through subordinate clauses and modifying phrases preceding the main clause.

Cumulative Sentence

A sentence that begins with its main idea and then adds details through a series of modifying phrases and clauses. It's the opposite of a periodic sentence and often creates an effect of accumulation.

The Genteel Tradition

A term coined by George Santayana describing the polite, moralistic, and often lifeless literary culture prevalent in late 19th-century America. Mencken actively opposed it.

Important Terms (3/3)

The American Language

H.L. Mencken's monumental 1919 study documenting the divergence of American English from British English, showcasing his erudition.

Prejudices

A series of six volumes (1919–1927) collecting Mencken's literary and cultural essays, provocatively titled to reflect his openly biased and confident judgments.

The Uplift

Mencken's broad label for the collection of moralistic and puritanical forces he opposed, including Puritanism, Prohibition, evangelical Christianity, political demagogues, and popular sentimentality.

Primary Objective

This book's central aim is to teach students *how* Mencken writes, focusing on his rhetorical techniques and linguistic strategies, rather than debating the validity of his political or social views.

Core Text for Analysis

The study will primarily use Mencken's *Notes on Democracy*, examining every sentence to deconstruct his argumentative methods.
