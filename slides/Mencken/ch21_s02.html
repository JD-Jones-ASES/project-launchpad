The "Anthropoid Good Man" and Puritan Legislation

Stated Motive

Puritan legislation (Prohibition, Comstockery, vice crusading) is often defended as altruistic or evangelical—an effort to benefit others against their will.

Actual Motive

The text argues the true motives are punitive: to punish others for having a better time and to reduce them to a lower, unhappier level.

The Judicial System and the "Virtuoso of Virtue"

District Attorneys

Use high-profile prosecutions of "men above the general" (such as rich or intelligent individuals) to gain favor with the mob and advance their political careers.

The Mob

Delights in seeing superior men "manhandled and humiliated."

The Result

The criminal law acquires a "flavour of fraud," and judges often prioritize their jobs over professional dignity.
