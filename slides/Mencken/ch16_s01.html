The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 16, Section 1

Analysis of H.L. Mencken’s "The Occasional Exception"

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Themes (1/2)

English Oligarchy

He invokes an idealized vision of English governance (Oxford, Cambridge, Westminster) where scholarship and "breeding" temper popular opinion.

Feudalism

He uses the medieval hierarchy to illustrate how modern democracy has merely redirected servitude from a lord to "the mob."

Prussian Aristocracy

Through anecdotes of Frederick the Great and Wilhelm I, Mencken highlights a code of honor, dignity, and rectitude that he believes is absent in democratic systems.

The Persona

An aristocratic outsider who is amused, contemptuous, unbribable, and detached.

The Reality

A working journalist in Baltimore who was deeply engaged in the political process and maintained diverse friendships.

Key Concepts and Themes (2/2)

The Purpose

This gap allows Mencken to make provocative statements that a more literal or "openly positioned" writer could not.

Honor

Integrity, rectitude, dignity, self-respect.

Degradation

Compromised, crawled, broken to the goose-step, "male Magdalens."

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Amour Propre

(French) Self-love or self-regard. Mencken uses this term to imply vanity, suggesting politicians defend a cheap self-image rather than true honor.

Charlatanry

The practice of a charlatan; pretending to possess knowledge or skill one does not have. Mencken applies this to veteran politicians who have practiced deception so long it has become second nature.

Exemplum

A brief narrative or anecdote drawn from history used to illustrate a general moral or principle (e.g., the Frederick the Great anecdote).

Male Magdalens

A metaphor applying the language of sexual shame and prostitution to political compromise. It suggests politicians have sold their "most intimate" possessions and live in a state of perpetual, yet ...

Persona

The constructed character or voice through which an author speaks, distinct from the author's real-life personality.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Suzerain

A feudal lord to whom a vassal owes allegiance.

Tactical Concession

A rhetorical maneuver where an arguer acknowledges a valid point from the opposing side to strengthen their own overall case.
