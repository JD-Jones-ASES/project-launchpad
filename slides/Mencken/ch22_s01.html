Key Concepts (1/2)

How does Mencken argue the law has been 'weaponized' under Puritanical influence?

It is used against unpopular citizens, with courts abandoning impartiality, especially when 'heresy' is perceived to be at stake.

According to Mencken, what happens to constitutional rights and common law rules when someone is accused of 'heresy'?

They are swept away as invalid; rules against double jeopardy and hearsay are suspended in order to secure a conviction.

What does Mencken claim is the consequence of the 'enormous multiplicity of sumptuary and inquisitorial statutes'?

It makes it easy for authorities to find a law an unpopular person can be accused of breaking, even after being singled out for persecution.

What specific constitutional rights does Mencken accuse the Volstead Act of destroying or habitually violating?

The constitutional right to a jury trial, the prohibition of unreasonable searches and seizures, and the rule against double jeopardy.
