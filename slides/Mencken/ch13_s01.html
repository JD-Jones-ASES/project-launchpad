Rhetorical Techniques and Stylistic Devices (1/2)

Technical vs. Colloquial

He mixes constitutional terminology (e.g., "proportional representation") with "barnyard contempt" (e.g., "yokels," "bucolic imbecilities").

Reductio ad absurdum

Mencken extends the logic of popular sovereignty to its most ridiculous conclusions to demonstrate its inherent flaws.

Rhetorical Techniques and Stylistic Devices (2/2)

Irony and Paradox

The central irony of the text is Mencken's attempt to prove the people are sovereign only to demonstrate that their sovereignty is ultimately worthless or contemptible.
