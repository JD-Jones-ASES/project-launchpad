Chapter 3, Section 1

Analysis of Mencken’s "Varieties of Homo Sapiens"

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Rhetorical Analysis

The Mock-Scientist Persona

He adopts the tone of a naturalist or laboratory observer. By categorizing humans as "specimens," he strips them of their status as citizens with rights and treats them as objects of biological study.

Targeting the "Democrat"

Mencken defines a "democrat" not by political affiliation, but as anyone who maintains a sentimental faith in the fundamental competence and equality of the common man.

Scientific Veneer

By using the language and methods of natural science, Mencken implies his conclusions are "findings" rather than mere opinions.

Linguistic and Stylistic Features

Glossary of Key Terms (1/2)

Antithesis

A rhetorical device where two opposite ideas are put together in a sentence to achieve a contrasting effect (e.g., comparing "superior equipment" to "insensate sweating").

Extended Metaphor

A comparison between two unlike things that continues throughout a series of sentences in a paragraph or even an entire work.

Homo stultus

Mencken's mock-Latin classification for the "foolish man."

Insensate

Lacking physical sensation, power of mind, or feeling; used by Mencken to describe the "thinking" of the masses.

Linnaean System

The biological system of naming and classifying organisms; Mencken borrows this structure to categorize political "specimens."

Glossary of Key Terms (2/2)

Mock-Scientist

A rhetorical persona that adopts the language and detached tone of a researcher to present subjective or satirical views as objective findings.

Pedagogue

A teacher; in Mencken’s usage, a symbol of the democratic belief in universal education, which he views with contempt.

Ratiocination

Methodical, logical reasoning; considered by Mencken to be a rare ability limited to the "superior" mind.

Register Mixing

The practice of using different levels of formality or specialized language (e.g., scientific Latin vs. crude similes) within the same context to create a specific rhetorical effect.
