Politics Under Democracy: A Comprehensive Study Guide

Study Slides

Key Concepts and Themes

Rejection of Imagination

Leaders with genuine imagination and ability are "shining marks" and easily targeted by demagogues.

Election of Mediocrities

"Colourless mediocrities" are preferred by the mob because they do not inspire fear.

Substitution of Fear

To enter World War I, leaders had to replace the fear of danger with a more powerful fear: the threat of a victorious, vengeful Germany.

Official Propaganda

Government agencies and newspapers were used to spread "dreadful surmises," such as attributing industrial accidents to foreign agents and emphasizing the danger to American women and infants.

The Heroic Mood

The implementation of the draft allowed those not serving (the "John Does") to develop an "altruistic eagerness" for war, as they could appear bold and heroic without personal risk.

Historical "Bugaboos" in American History

Glossary of Important Terms (1/2)

Bolshevism

A radical revolutionary ideology mentioned as one of the "imaginary monsters" pursued in American history.

Bugaboo

An imaginary object of fear; a source of needless alarm used by politicians to distract or motivate the public.

Demagogue

A political leader who gains power by appealing to popular desires and prejudices rather than by reasoned argument; a "professor of mob psychology."

Hyphenates

A term used in the text to describe a group (likely Americans of dual nationality) targeted as a "bugaboo" during times of national tension.

Mob Psychology

The study or exploitation of the collective emotions and prejudices of the general public, used by demagogues to maintain control.

Glossary of Important Terms (2/2)

Plutocracy

Government by the wealthy; rule by a rich elite.

Scotching

To put an end to something; used in the text to describe the act of "defeating" a manufactured political threat.

Venal

Susceptible to bribery; corrupt.

Witch-hunter

A metaphor for a democratic statesman who maintains power by identifying and pursuing perceived internal or external enemies.
