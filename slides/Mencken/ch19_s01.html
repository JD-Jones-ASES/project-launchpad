The "Footnote" as a Rhetorical Device

False Modesty

By suggesting the material is an incidental postscript, Mencken implies that the examples are so obvious they hardly require formal analysis.

Structural Disguise

The "footnote" framing masks what is actually a sustained historical narrative and a closing argument against democratic politics.

Tonal Range and Bathos

Technique. Definition

Example in Text

Bathos. A calculated drop from the grand to the ridiculous to deflate pomposity.

Shifting from the gravity of a presidential candidate’s death to the "leaky copper roof" of the White House.

Hyperbole. Extravagant exaggeration used for rhetorical effect rather than literal truth.

Claiming William Jennings Bryan did more damage to the South than General Sherman’s army.

Metaphor. Describing a subject through a comparative lens to assign specific traits.

Labeling Bryan an "ambulent boil," transforming him from a person into a walking disease.
