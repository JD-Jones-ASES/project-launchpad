Glossary of Terms (1/2)

Congeries

A rhetorical device involving the relentless accumulation of examples or words to create a rhythmic, overwhelming effect.

Democratic Moralism

The impulse within a democracy to regulate private behavior and enforce public virtue through laws such as Prohibition or censorship.

Homo boobiens / Homo vulgaris

Mock-scientific terms coined by Mencken to categorize the average citizen as a zoological specimen rather than a political entity.

Inferior Man

Mencken’s Nietzschean term for the average citizen, characterized by intellectual limitations, credulity, and hostility toward excellence.

Libertarian

In Mencken’s specific usage, a temperamental individualist who regards the state as the natural enemy of personal freedom and opposes all government interference in private life.

Glossary of Terms (2/2)

Nietzschean

Referring to the philosophical influence of Friedrich Nietzsche, particularly regarding the distinction between the "inferior man" and the "able man."

Register Mixing

A rhetorical tool where the author combines different levels of language (e.g., scientific jargon mixed with slang) to create a satirical effect.

Zeugma

A rhetorical figure of speech used by Mencken in Part II to enhance the prosecutorial weight of his sentences.
