The Semicolon Hinge

Mencken uses parallel clauses separated by semicolons to create sharp contrasts, such as his description of William Jennings Bryan’s "cross-of-gold speech" versus his "cow State political economy."

In more complex passages, such as those regarding World War I, Mencken uses dashes to create parenthetical escalations, mimicking the way propaganda adds layers of alarm to a narrative.
