The Sage of Baltimore (Mencken)

Chapter 22, Section 2

Analysis of Mencken’s "Where Puritanism Fails"

Study Slides

The Persecution of Minority Thought

Targeting

An unpopular individual is singled out.

Fabrication

Police, prosecutors, and courts collaborate to find a statute the individual might have broken, aided by a multiplicity of "sumptuary and inquisitorial" laws.

Escalation

A case may begin with a vague charge like "disturbing the peace" and end with crimes carrying "staggering penalties."

The Erosion of Constitutional Protections

Common Law Rules

Protections against double jeopardy and hearsay are frequently suspended to ensure the conviction of "heretics."

Specific Legislation

The **Volstead Act** is cited as a prime example of legislation that destroys the right to a jury trial and violates prohibitions against unreasonable searches and seizures.

Inquisitorial Statutes

Certain states, notably California, possess laws that essentially criminalize "unpopular thoughts."
