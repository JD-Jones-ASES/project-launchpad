Important Terms (1/2)

Cynicism

An attitude of distrust toward human sincerity or integrity; Gurov's initial worldview regarding women and relationships.

Restraint (Chekhov's)

The author's deliberate choice not to offer explicit moral judgments or editorial comments on characters, allowing readers to draw their own conclusions.

Resort Town (in fiction)

A setting that functions as a temporary space where characters are freed from their ordinary lives and social conventions, often leading to actions or relationships that would not occur elsewhere.

Isolation

The state of being alone or apart from others; in the story, Gurov experiences a profound emotional isolation when his deepest feelings cannot be shared or understood by his social circle.

The Significance of Setting: Yalta, Moscow, and the Provincial Town

Yalta

Represents warmth, openness, possibility, and a temporary escape from societal constraints.

Moscow

A cold, enclosed city, full of obligations and social noise, where Gurov's newfound feelings have no place or audience.

Anna's provincial town

Portrayed as gray, dull, and cage-like, symbolizing the suffocating life Anna endures and the restrictive societal structures that entrap both characters.
