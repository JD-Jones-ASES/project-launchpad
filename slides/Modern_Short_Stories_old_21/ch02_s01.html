Chekhov's "The Lady with the Dog" - Questions and Discussion

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

Character Transformation

The gradual or sudden shift in a character's beliefs, values, or understanding, particularly exemplified by Gurov's evolving perception of women and love.

Authorial Irony and Intent

Chekhov's deliberate use of contrasting elements (like Gurov's initial misogyny versus his eventual transformation) to highlight thematic ideas or challenge reader expectations.

Symbolism and Representation

How characters, objects, or settings can stand for larger ideas or abstract concepts beyond their literal meaning (e.g., Anna's husband representing societal constraints).

Thematic Ambiguity and Open Endings

The practice of concluding a story without resolving all conflicts, leaving room for reader interpretation and emphasizing the ongoing complexity of life.

Setting as a Narrative Device

The use of physical locations to mirror, contrast, or influence characters' emotional states and thematic development.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Point of View and Perspective

The impact of experiencing a story primarily through one character's consciousness and the insights gained by considering alternative perspectives.

The Invisible Interior Experience

The profound isolation felt when an individual's deep personal experiences or transformations are unseen, misunderstood, or dismissed by their social environment.

Important Terms (1/2)

Irony

A literary device where the actual meaning is different from the apparent meaning. In this context, often refers to *situational irony*, where an outcome is contrary to what was expected.

Contradiction

A combination of statements, ideas, or features that are opposed to one another; an inconsistency.

Cynicism

An inclination to believe that people are motivated purely by self-interest; distrustful of human sincerity or integrity. Gurov initially displays this trait.

Villain

A character whose evil actions or motives are important to the plot; an antagonist. The questions prompt consideration of whether Anna's husband fits this narrow definition.

Consciousness

A person's awareness or perception of something; their subjective experience of the world.

Important Terms (2/2)

Setting

The place or type of surroundings where something is positioned or where an event takes place; more than just a backdrop, it can reflect mood or theme.

Narrative Perspective/Point of View

The angle from which a story is told, influencing what information the reader receives and how it is interpreted.
