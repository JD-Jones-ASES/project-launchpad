James Joyce's "The Dead" - Reading Part One

Study Slides

Key Concepts (1/2)

The Morkan Household as a Microcosm

The party and the Morkan sisters represent an unchanging, ritualistic Dublin, stubbornly resistant to the outside world and serving as a preserved "world in amber."

Domestic Ceremony as Cultural Insight

Joyce employs everyday social gatherings to reveal the deeper spiritual and cultural condition of an entire society, highlighting collective decisions to cling to the past.

Gabriel's Self-Conscious Anxiety

Gabriel Conroy is presented as a character prone to nervousness, self-doubt, and a tendency to "rehearse" his interactions, often failing to connect genuinely with others.

Dubliners

The collection of short stories by James Joyce, published in 1914, from which "The Dead" is the final and longest story. It is a portrait of life in Dublin at the turn of the 20th century.

Calcified

To become hardened, rigid, or fixed, like calcium deposits. In the text, it describes how the party's rituals have become unchangeable traditions.

Effusive

Expressing feelings of gratitude, pleasure, or approval in an unrestrained or heartfelt manner. Used to describe Freddy Malins's praise for Aunt Julia's singing.
