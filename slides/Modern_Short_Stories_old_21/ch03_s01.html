Key Concepts (1/2)

Character Analysis

Understanding how authors reveal character through dialogue, internal monologue, actions, and interactions with others. This includes distinguishing between a character's likability and their abili...

Thematic Development

Identifying the main ideas or messages that begin to emerge in a story, such as cultural identity, social anxiety, disconnection, and the nature of human relationships.

Literary Detail and Symbolism

Recognizing how specific descriptions (e.g., food, setting, clothing) contribute to atmosphere, characterization, and deeper symbolic meaning beyond mere background.

Public vs. Private Self

Exploring the discrepancy between how a character presents themselves to the world and their inner thoughts, feelings, and true motivations. This gap often reveals psychological complexity.

Comparative Literature

Analyzing and contrasting elements (characters, themes, narrative techniques) between different literary works to gain new perspectives on each text.

Key Concepts (2/2)

Formulating Interpretive Predictions

Developing initial hypotheses about a story's central meaning or direction based on early sections, a practice that highlights the evolving nature of literary understanding.

Important Terms (1/2)

Sympathetic

Eliciting pity or compassion from the reader. A character might be flawed but still evoke understanding or concern.

Likable

Easy to like; pleasant or agreeable in a way that makes a reader feel positively towards them.

Foil Character

A character who contrasts with another character, usually the protagonist, to highlight particular qualities of the other character. (e.g., Lily and Miss Ivors could be seen as foils to Gabriel).

Disconnection

A state of being separate, detached, or isolated from others, a culture, or a specific place.

Social Anxiety

A significant and often irrational fear of social situations, leading to discomfort, self-consciousness, and avoidance.

Important Terms (2/2)

Public Self

The persona, image, or outward behavior a character displays to society or to other characters.

Private Self

The innermost thoughts, feelings, beliefs, and true identity of a character, often hidden from others.

Theme

The central topic, subject, or message within a narrative.

Motif

A recurring element, idea, or image that has symbolic significance in a story.

Atmosphere/Mood

The emotional feeling or sense of a story, often created by descriptions of setting, weather, and character interactions.
